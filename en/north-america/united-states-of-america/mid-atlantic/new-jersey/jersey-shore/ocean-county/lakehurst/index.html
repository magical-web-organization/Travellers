Introduction to Lakehurst

Located in Ocean County within the Jersey Shore region of New Jersey, Lakehurst is a quaint borough that has something to offer everyone. From its rich history and culture to its picturesque natural beauty and outdoor adventures, Lakehurst is a must-visit destination.

List of Places to Visit in Lakehurst

Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or just looking for a relaxing day out, Lakehurst has plenty of attractions to keep you entertained. Here are some of the top places to visit in Lakehurst:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lakehurst Historical Society Museum: This small museum is housed in a historic train station and features exhibits that showcase the history of Lakehurst and the surrounding area.
  • Hindenburg Crash Site: This historic site is where the Hindenburg airship crashed in 1937. Visitors can see a memorial to the victims and learn about the disaster that changed the course of air travel.
  • St. John’s Church: This historic church was built in 1872 and features stunning stained-glass windows and intricate woodwork.

Natural Attractions of Lakehurst: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Horicon: This peaceful lake is a popular spot for fishing, boating, and picnicking. Visitors can also walk along the scenic trails that surround the lake.
  • JB MDL Lakehurst: This military installation is home to a variety of natural attractions, including hiking trails, fishing ponds, and a bird sanctuary.
  • Popcorn Park Zoo: This unique sanctuary is home to a variety of rescued animals, including tigers, bears, and primates.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lakehurst

Lakehurst is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with a variety of hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views and a chance to explore the borough’s natural beauty. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Horicon-Wells Mills Trail: This 7.7-mile trail runs through a state forest and offers a challenging hike with beautiful views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Colliers Mills Wildlife Management Area: This 12,000-acre wildlife preserve features a variety of hiking and biking trails, as well as opportunities for fishing and hunting.
  • Lakehurst Loop Trail: This 6.6-mile loop trail is a great option for both hikers and bikers, with a mix of paved and unpaved paths and beautiful views of Lake Horicon.

Best Things to Do in Lakehurst: Activities and Tours

In addition to its natural and cultural attractions, Lakehurst offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors looking to experience the borough in a unique way. Here are some of the best things to do in Lakehurst:

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Take a thrilling hot air balloon ride over Lakehurst and the surrounding area, for breathtaking views and an unforgettable experience.
  • Brewery Tour: Visit one of the local breweries in Lakehurst and learn about the beer-making process while sampling some of the delicious local brews.
  • Ghost Tour: Take a spooky tour of Lakehurst and learn about the borough’s haunted history and the paranormal activity that still haunts the town today.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lakehurst

Day 1: Arrive in Lakehurst and check into your hotel. Spend the day exploring the Lakehurst Historical Society Museum and St. John’s Church.

Day 2: Take a hot air balloon ride over Lakehurst for a breathtaking view of the borough from above. Spend the rest of the day relaxing by Lake Horicon and enjoying the natural beauty of the area.

Day 3: Explore the Hindenburg Crash Site and learn about the historic disaster that happened here. In the afternoon, take a brewery tour of one of the local breweries in Lakehurst.

Day 4: Spend the day hiking in the Colliers Mills Wildlife Management Area, and enjoy the beautiful scenery and wildlife in this natural sanctuary.

Day 5: Visit the Popcorn Park Zoo and see the rescued wildlife up close. In the evening, take a ghost tour of Lakehurst and learn about the paranormal activity that still haunts the borough today.

Day 6: Spend the day kayaking or fishing on Lake Horicon, and enjoy the peaceful serenity of the lake.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy your final day in Lakehurst, wandering along the scenic trails or visiting local shops and restaurants for souvenirs and local treats.

Food and Drink Scene of Lakehurst

Lakehurst’s food and drink scene is a reflection of the borough’s diverse culture and history. From classic American dishes to authentic Italian and Mexican cuisine, there’s something to suit every taste. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tomato Pie: This unique pizza-like dish is a New Jersey specialty, with a thick crust topped with sweet tomato sauce and grated cheese.
  • Pork Roll and Cheese Sandwich: This classic breakfast sandwich is made with pork roll, a type of cured ham that’s unique to New Jersey, and melted cheese on a Kaiser roll.
  • Linguine with Clams: This Italian dish features linguine pasta with clams, garlic, and white wine, for a delicious and flavorful meal.

Local Beverages

  • Trenton Thunderbolt: This cocktail is made with blueberry vodka and lemonade, and is a refreshing drink to cool down on a hot summer day.
  • Jersey Blue: A local craft beer made with blueberries, for a unique and flavorful twist on a classic brew.
