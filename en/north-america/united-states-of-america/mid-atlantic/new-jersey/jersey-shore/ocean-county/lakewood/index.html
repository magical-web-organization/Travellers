Introduction to Lakewood

Nestled in Ocean County, New Jersey, Lakewood is a town of hidden gems and natural beauty that make it a must-visit destination for travelers. The town features a balance of cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and exciting activities that cater to visitors of all ages. Whether you’re interested in learning about Lakewood’s rich history or exploring its scenic natural parks, you’ll find something to suit your interests in this charming town.

List of Places to Visit in Lakewood

Exploring Lakewood’s hidden treasures is one of the best experiences you can have while in town. Here are some must-visit attractions you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Strand Theater: The historic and beautifully preserved theater offers an experience like no other and a lively calendar of events, from movies to plays, concerts, and more.
  2. The Lakewood Heritage Museum: This museum houses an extensive collection of exhibits that celebrate the town’s rich history and culture.
  3. The Lakewood Piners National Reserve: This historic and nature-filled reserve boasts miles of trails filled with beautiful flora and fauna, and its Forest Interpretive Center educates visitors on the history and ecology of Lakewood.

Natural Attractions of Lakewood: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Allaire State Park: A sprawling green space that offers campsites, trails, and picnic areas, and it’s home to a fascinating reenactment of iron production at the park’s historic blast furnace.
  2. Lake Carasaljo: A picturesque lake that spans over 80 acres and is perfect for fishing, kayaking, and bird-watching.
  3. Ocean County Park: Offering manicured gardens, picnic areas, sports fields, and walking paths, this park is perfect for outdoor lovers of all ages.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lakewood

Lakewood offers some fantastic spots for biking and hiking enthusiasts. Below are a few places that should top your list:

  1. The Forge Pond Loop: This two-mile hiking and biking trail runs around scenic lakes, historic mills and buildings, and wetlands, making it ideal for wildlife enthusiasts.
  2. Allaire State Park: The park is home to nearly 20 miles of various hiking and biking trails, including easy paths for beginners and challenging routes for seasoned pros. The forest setting makes for a peaceful and tranquil atmosphere.
  3. Lake Shenandoah County Park: This beautiful, 2.4-mile trail loops around the lake, offering stunning views of the water and natural forest surroundings.

Best Things to Do in Lakewood: Activities and Tours

Lakewood’s activities and tours cater to visitors of all tastes and interests. Here are a few must-do activities in town:

  1. Visit some of the town’s farm-based businesses, like the Wemrock Orchard or Happy Day Farm.
  2. Take in a show at The Strand, which hosts events throughout the year, including comedy, music, and theater.
  3. Explore the Pine Barrens via kayak, with over 1.1 million acres of natural waterways to explore, including the Cedar Creek and Mullica River.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lakewood

Here’s a sample itinerary for those looking to make the most out of their stay in Lakewood:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Lakewood Heritage Museum, followed by lunch at the Pine Belt Market
2 Spend the day in Allaire State Park and visit historic homes and artisan shops
3 Take a guided horseback tour through the lush nature of the Pineland National Reserve
4 Explore the Pine Barrens by ATV with Pine Barrens Adventure
5 Visit the Jersey Shore Premium Outlets mall for shopping
6 Hike the Forge Pond Loop and settle in for a picnic
7 Experience a history and ghost tour of Lakewood, followed by drinks at Klee’s Bar and Grill.

Food and Drink Scene of Lakewood

Lakewood boasts a diverse culinary scene that offers an impressive range of dining options, from classic American cuisine to authentic Middle Eastern and Jewish fare.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. BBQ pulled pork at the Franklin BBQ Smokehouse
  2. Lamb shawarma at Cafe Sheli
  3. Kosher-style hot dogs or burgers at Oak Street Eatery

Local Beverages

  1. Ocean County Cranberry Wine from Laurita Winery
  2. Beer from Icarus Brewing Company, one of the best craft breweries in the state.

Where to Stay in Lakewood: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lakewood offers a wide variety of accommodating options to suit all types of travelers. Here are some popular options:

  1. Four Points by Sheraton Lakewood: A luxurious hotel that’s perfect for business travelers and large groups.
  2. Bluegreen Vacations at Atlantic Palace: A high-end guesthouse that offers breathtaking oceanfront views.
  3. The Lakewood Courtyard by Marriott: A family-friendly hotel that features an outdoor pool, a fitness center, and free breakfast.

What to Buy in Lakewood: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Lakewood boasts an array of unique and locally made souvenirs. Here are a few recommendations:

  1. Pine Barrens pottery at Woodland Wonders
  2. Subs from Mike’s Giant Submarines
  3. Chocolates and sweets from The Chocolate Belles

Local Culture and Customs of Lakewood

The culture and customs of Lakewood are diverse and vibrant. With a mix of Jewish, Hispanic, and Asian-American influences, the town boasts a unique and welcoming cultural scene that provides an opportunity for visitors to immerse themselves in a completely different environment.

Best Time to Visit Lakewood

The best time to visit Lakewood is late spring, from May to June, or in fall between September and early November. The weather is pleasant, and there are fewer crowds. If you’re after a beach vacation, the summer months are also a popular time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lakewood

Do:

  1. Respect Lakewood’s unique culture and customs.
  2. Take a guided nature hike to gain a better understanding of the area’s natural landscape.
  3. Support local businesses and shops while you’re in town.

Don’t:

  1. Go off-trail while hiking, as many of the area’s parks and reserves have fragile ecosystems.
  2. Litter or leave trash in outdoor spaces.
  3. Take photos without asking permission first.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lakewood

Lakewood is considered to be one of the safest towns in New Jersey. Its low crime rates make it a relatively secure place to visit. Nonetheless, it’s still wise to keep an eye on your personal belongings and avoid isolated areas, just in case.

Health in Lakewood

There are several medical centers and hospitals in Lakewood, including Ocean Medical Center, Community Medical Center, and Lakewood Urgent Care. It’s helpful to have an understanding of your personal health insurance and what healthcare resources are available in case of an emergency.

Getting to Lakewood: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lakewood

  1. Newark Liberty International Airport; 56 miles from Lakewood
  2. Atlantic City International Airport; 55 miles from Lakewood

Bus Stations Near Lakewood

  1. Lakewood Bus Terminal
  2. Toms River Bus Terminal

Other Options

The town is about 70 miles south of New York City, making it an easy road trip from the tri-state area. Lakewood is accessible via car from the Garden State Parkway.

How to Get Around in Lakewood

Lakewood offers several transportation options:

  1. Car rentals or ride-sharing services like Lyft and Uber
  2. Local buses
  3. Bicycles can be rented from Pedego Electric Bikes in Toms River

Where to Go Next from Lakewood: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lakewood’s location is strategic and makes it a perfect jumping-off point for exploring nearby destinations. Here are a few to add to your list:

  1. Toms River: Only a 30-minute drive away, you’ll find the plentiful recreational opportunities, including the Toms River Seaport Society and Maritime Museum.
  2. Six Flags Great Adventure in Jackson offers an amusement park, safari park, and an outdoor concert venue.
  3. Philadelphia: Drive an hour and a half to the city of brotherly love and explore the Liberty Bell, Independence Hall, and Museum of Art.

Conclusion

Lakewood has hidden gems and natural beauty that make it a must-visit destination for travelers. From its cultural attractions and museums to its natural beauty, biking and hiking trails, and exciting activities, Lakewood is a perfect place to visit anytime of year.
