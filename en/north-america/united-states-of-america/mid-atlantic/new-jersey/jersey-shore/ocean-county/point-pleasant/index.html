Introduction to Point Pleasant

Named after the beautiful and serene point of land that extends into the Manasquan River, Point Pleasant is a charming and tranquil borough nestled in the heart of Ocean County, in Jersey Shore, New Jersey. It offers an abundance of natural beauty, exciting water sports, a rich history, and a vibrant food scene, making it a coveted destination for visitors looking to get away from the hubbub of the city.

List of Places to Visit in Point Pleasant

A wide variety of attractions awaits visitors to Point Pleasant. Below are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Point Pleasant Historical Society Museum: Featuring artifacts dating back to the 1800s, including old photographs, documents, furniture, and clothing, this museum offers a glimpse into the borough’s rich history.
  • Seaport Museum: A maritime museum with various exhibits that preserve the rich boating culture of the Jersey Shore.
  • The Channel Club Tower: A historic landmark built in the 1920s, offering a fascinating insight into the borough’s architecture and history.

Natural Attractions of Point Pleasant: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Point Pleasant Beach: This clean and well-maintained beach is perfect for sunbathing, swimming, surfing, and relaxing in the sun.
  • Manasquan Inlet: This picturesque inlet offers a fantastic opportunity for fishing, crabbing, and bird watching.
  • Riverfront Park: Located on the Manasquan River, this park is ideal for jogging, picnicking, and strolling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Point Pleasant

The borough offers several scenic trails for biking and hiking enthusiasts, including:

  • Point Pleasant Canal Trails: A 2.5-mile trail with incredible views of the Manasquan River and the peninsula.
  • Manasquan River Greenway Trail: A 4.5-mile trail from the Wall Township border to the tip of the peninsula.

Best Things to Do in Point Pleasant: Activities and Tours

  • Whale and Dolphin Watching Tour: Witness these majestic sea creatures up close, led by an experienced guide.
  • Fishing Charter Tour: Catch a variety of fish in the Atlantic Ocean with a guided fishing tour.
  • Escape Room: An exciting mental challenge that requires players to solve a series of puzzles to escape the room before time runs out.

Food and Drink Scene of Point Pleasant

Point Pleasant’s food scene features a mix of classic boardwalk bites and high-end restaurants that serve fresh regional seafood.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Clams: Point Pleasant is famous for the freshest, most succulent clams.
  • Pork Roll: Also known as Taylor ham, Pork Roll is a favorite local snack in Point Pleasant.
  • Saltwater Taffy: A classic Jersey Shore candy that comes in a variety of flavors.

Local Beverages

  • Jersey Devil Vodka: A locally made vodka with hints of pepper and berries and served with herbs like rosemary and thyme.
  • Beach Haus Brewery: A local brewery with a variety of beers that reflect the local flavor.

Where to Stay in Point Pleasant: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From waterfront hotels to bed and breakfasts, Point Pleasant offers accommodation options for every budget. Some of the top choices include:

  • The White Sands Oceanfront Resort and Spa: A waterfront resort with direct access to the beach.
  • The Tower Cottage: A beautifully restored Victorian bed and breakfast that offers luxury suites and cottages.
  • The Pelican Point Motel: An affordable family-owned option within walking distance of the beach.

What to Buy in Point Pleasant: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can take a bit of Point Pleasant home with them by purchasing souvenirs such as t-shirts, caps, and postcards. You can also take home the locally made Beach Haus beer and Jersey Devil Vodka.

Local Culture and Customs of Point Pleasant

The people of Point Pleasant are friendly, generous, and welcoming. It is customary to show appreciation by leaving tips at restaurants, cafes, and bars.

Best Time to Visit Point Pleasant

The best time to visit Point Pleasant is during the summer months (June to August), when the weather is warm, and visitors can enjoy the beach, outdoor activities, and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Point Pleasant

Do:

  • Enjoy the Jersey Shore’s great outdoors.
  • Try local seafood, especially the clams.
  • Respect local culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beach or streets.
  • Engage in dangerous or illegal activities.
  • Be rude to fellow visitors or locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Point Pleasant

Point Pleasant is generally a safe borough. Visitors should take reasonable precautions such as locking their cars and hotel rooms, being aware of their surroundings, and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Point Pleasant

Point Pleasant has several medical facilities, pharmacies, and doctors’ offices that can provide medical care to visitors in case of any emergency.

Getting to Point Pleasant: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Point Pleasant

The nearest airport is Newark Liberty International Airport, which is approximately a 1-hour drive from Point Pleasant.

Bus Stations Near Point Pleasant

The closest bus station is the Point Pleasant Bus Terminal, which is served by NJ Transit and Greyhound.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a taxi or Uber ride from the airport or bus station.

How to Get Around in Point Pleasant

Visitors can rent a car, bike, or scooter to get around Point Pleasant. Walking is also a great option for those exploring the historic district and downtown.

Where to Go Next from Point Pleasant: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Asbury Park: A popular summer destination that is famous for its live music scene.
  • Cape May: A charming and historic seaside town with stunning Victorian architecture.
  • Atlantic City: A bustling coastal city with many casinos, restaurants, and shows.

Conclusion

Point Pleasant offers a perfect combination of outdoor adventure, cultural exploration, relaxation, and fun. We hope this comprehensive guide helps you plan a memorable trip to this beautiful borough.
