Introduction to Cape May County

Cape May County is a beautiful coastal county located on the southern tip of New Jersey. It is famously known for its stunning beaches, rich culture, historic landmarks, and vivid wildlife. From taking a leisurely walk on the beach to going on a bird-watching expedition, Cape May County has something for everyone.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cape May County

Day 1: Visit the Cape May Lighthouse and Naval Air Station Wildwood Aviation Museum

Day 2: Explore the Historic Cold Spring Village and Emlen Physick Estate

Day 3: Go to Cape May Point State Park and The Wetlands Institute

Day 4: Visit Sunset Beach and Stone Harbor Bird Sanctuary

Day 5: Go on a bike ride on the Wildwood Boardwalk Bike Path and Cape May County Bike Route

Day 6: Enjoy the beach and water activities, such as parasailing and jet skiing

Day 7: Go on a fishing charter and end the day with a relaxing trolley tour

Local Culture and Customs of Cape May County

Cape May County’s rich culture and history are reflected in its architecture, art, and festivals. Some notable events to attend are:

Best Time to Visit Cape May County

The best time to visit Cape May County is from May to September when the weather is pleasant, and the beaches are open for swimming, sunbathing, and water activities. However, the fall season is also a great time to visit, with its vibrant autumn foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cape May County

Do:

  • Respect the environment and wildlife
  • Visit the local farmers’ markets and festivals
  • Explore the historic landmarks and museums
  • Try the local seafood and craft beer

Don’t:

  • Litter and pollute the beaches and waterways
  • Disturb the wildlife and their habitats
  • Ignore local customs and traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Cape May County

Cape May County is a safe and friendly destination for tourists. However, visitors should take precautions and follow the usual safety measures, such as keeping valuables in a secure place, staying alert in crowded areas, and following beach safety guidelines.

Health in Cape May County

Visitors should also take into account the health precautions necessary during their visit. Sunscreen is essential during the summer months, and visitors should be aware of ticks and the potential for Lyme disease when hiking or exploring outdoor areas.

Getting to Cape May County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cape May County

  • Atlantic City International Airport
  • Philadelphia International Airport

Bus Stations Near Cape May County

  • Greyhound Bus Station in Atlantic City

Other Options

  • Driving: Cape May County is accessible via car from major cities along the east coast such as New York City and Philadelphia.
  • Ferry: The Cape May - Lewes ferry connects Cape May County to Lewes, DE.

How to Get Around in Cape May County

The most convenient way to travel around Cape May County is by car, although visitors can also rent bicycles or scooters. Taxis, Uber, and Lyft are also available.
