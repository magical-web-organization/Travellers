Introduction to Stone Harbor

Located in Cape May County, New Jersey, Stone Harbor is a picturesque coastal town known for its dramatic dunes, white sandy beaches, and stunning sunset views over the bay. It is perfect for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and soak up the laid-back coastal atmosphere.

This charming town has a mix of attractions, from historical sites and museums to natural wonders, and a bustling marine life that promises to keep tourists entertained throughout their stay.

Week-Long Itinerary for Stone Harbor

Day Itinerary
Day 1 Visit the Wetlands Institute, explore the Stone Harbor Museum, and enjoy the sunset at Sunset Beach
Day 2 Go bird watching at Stone Harbor Bird Sanctuary, relax at the Stone Harbor Point and go surf fishing; take a romantic carriage ride
Day 3 Experience the outdoors at Wildwood’s Beaches and Boardwalks, The Cape May Seashore Lines, and the Southern Cape May County Bike Path
Day 4 Take a hike on the Nature Trail at the Wetlands Institute, visit the Cape May County Park & Zoo, and grab a drink at the Hawk Haven Vineyard & Winery
Day 5 Explore the Historic Cold Spring Village, take a sunset sail on the bay, and watch the Cape May Lighthouse in action
Day 6 Go boating at Miss Chris Marina, take a horse-drawn carriage ride with Only Yesterday Carriages, and explore the Seaport Aquarium
Day 7 Relax on the beach with a good book and take in the relaxing coastal atmosphere

Local Culture and Customs of Stone Harbor

  • Stone Harbor’s warm, friendly locals embrace visitors from all over the world and always go the extra mile to make their guests feel welcome.
  • Visitors are expected to respect local customs and traditions, including wearing appropriate attire in public places and following local customs when eating or dining out.
  • Smoking on the beach and sea walls is not allowed.

Best Time to Visit Stone Harbor

The best time to visit Stone Harbor is from May through September, when the weather is pleasant and the beaches are at their best. The peak season is during June, July, and August, so be prepared for crowded beaches and streets during these months. September and October are great months for tourists to take seasonal tours or hikes, as the leaves will begin to change colors and the scenery is sometimes quite striking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Stone Harbor

Do:

  • Respect the locals
  • Follow local customs
  • Try local cuisines and drinks
  • Pick up after yourself and keep the beaches clean and litter-free

Don’t:

  • Smoke on the beaches or sea walls
  • Ignore safety protocols
  • Disturb the wildlife or toss any trash around casually

Safety and Health

Safety in Stone Harbor

Stone Harbor has a low crime rate, and visitors should exercise the usual safety precautions that they would take in any tourist destination. Bear in mind the seas can be rough sometimes, especially in stormy weather.

Health in Stone Harbor

Stone Harbor is a generally healthy place, and visitors should seek medical attention if they are experiencing any symptoms of illness. It is also recommended to carry a first aid kit and plenty of sunscreen to protect against sunburns.
