Introduction to Ocean City

Located in Jersey Shore, New Jersey, Ocean City is a popular destination for tourists looking to enjoy a beach vacation. The town offers a plethora of activities for all ages, from outdoor adventures to cultural attractions. In this travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at the must-visit places and experiences in Ocean City.

List of Places to Visit in Ocean City

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ocean City Historical Museum: This museum showcases the history and heritage of Ocean City, with exhibits and artifacts dating back to the early days of the town.

  • Lucy the Elephant: This unique attraction is a six-story elephant-shaped building that has been standing in Margate, just a few miles from Ocean City, for over 100 years. Visitors can take a guided tour of the interior and learn about the history of Lucy the Elephant.

  • Somers Mansion: Built in the mid-1700s, this historic mansion is one of the oldest buildings in New Jersey. It’s open to the public for tours and features period furnishings and architecture.

Natural Attractions of Ocean City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ocean City Beach and Boardwalk: The beach and boardwalk are the main attractions in Ocean City, with miles of golden sand and endless entertainment options. Visitors can enjoy swimming, sunbathing, walking, jogging, and biking along the boardwalk.

  • Cape May County Park and Zoo: Located just a few miles from Ocean City, this park and zoo is a must-visit for families. It features over 85 species of animals, including lions, tigers, and giraffes, and offers educational programs and tours.

  • Corson’s Inlet State Park: This state park offers stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean and is a popular spot for fishing, bird watching, and hiking. Visitors can choose from several trails, including one that leads to a secluded beach.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ocean City

  • Ocean City Boardwalk: The boardwalk is a popular spot for walking, jogging, and biking. It stretches for 2.5 miles and offers stunning views of the beach and ocean.

  • Ocean City Bike Paths: Ocean City has several bike paths that run throughout the town, including the 3.1-mile Gardens Parkway Bike Path, which winds through a scenic residential area and offers views of the bay.

Best Things to Do in Ocean City: Activities and Tours

  • Pirate Adventures: This interactive pirate experience is perfect for families with young children. Visitors can climb aboard a replica pirate ship and participate in treasure hunts, water cannon battles, and more.

  • Ripley’s Believe It or Not! Museum: This quirky museum features exhibits and artifacts from around the world and includes a shrunken head, a two-headed calf, and more.

  • Ocean City Parasail: Visitors can take a thrilling ride high above the ocean with Ocean City Parasail. The company offers single and tandem parasailing experiences.

Food and Drink Scene of Ocean City

Must-Try Dishes

  • Salt Water Taffy: Ocean City is famous for its salt water taffy, a chewy candy made with saltwater and molasses. Visitors can find it at several candy shops along the boardwalk.

  • John’s Pizza: This local pizza joint has been serving up delicious pies for over 50 years. Visitors can try the classic cheese pizza or opt for one of the specialty pies.

  • Shriver’s Gelato: This family-owned ice cream shop offers over 70 flavors of gelato, including classic options like chocolate and strawberry and unique flavors like lavender and cinnamon.

Local Beverages

  • Cape May Brewing Company: Located in nearby Cape May, this popular brewery serves up a variety of craft beers and offers tours and tastings.

  • Tuckahoe Brewing Company: This local brewery is located in nearby Egg Harbor Township and offers a variety of craft beers, as well as live music and food trucks.

Where to Stay in Ocean City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Chateau Bleu Inn: This family-friendly inn is located just a block from the beach and offers a variety of rooms and suites.

  • Impala Island Inn Bed and Breakfast: This charming bed and breakfast is located just a short walk from the beach and boardwalk and offers cozy rooms and a homemade breakfast.

  • Ocean City Mansion Bed and Breakfast: This luxurious bed and breakfast features Victorian-style rooms and suites and a gourmet breakfast each morning.

What to Buy in Ocean City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Salt Water Taffy: As mentioned earlier, salt water taffy is a must-try treat in Ocean City. Visitors can buy it at several candy shops along the boardwalk.

  • Boardwalk Art: The boardwalk is lined with vendors selling unique artwork and crafts, including paintings, sculptures, and jewelry.

  • Ocean City Apparel: Visitors can show their love for Ocean City with T-shirts, hats, and other apparel available at several local shops and boutiques.

Local Culture and Customs of Ocean City

Ocean City is known for its family-friendly atmosphere and is a “dry” town, meaning alcohol is not sold in restaurants or bars. The town also has strict rules when it comes to beach etiquette, including no smoking and no littering.

Best Time to Visit Ocean City

The best time to visit Ocean City is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and the beach and boardwalk are bustling with activity.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ocean City

Do:

  • Do follow all beach rules and regulations, including no smoking and no littering.
  • Do try the salt water taffy and other local foods and drinks.
  • Do bring sun protection, including sunscreen and hats.

Don’t:

  • Don’t bring alcohol to the beach or boardwalk.
  • Don’t litter or leave trash on the beach or boardwalk.
  • Don’t ignore beach warning signs or swim in restricted areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ocean City

Ocean City is a relatively safe town, with low crime rates and a strong police presence. However, visitors should always take appropriate safety precautions, such as locking their vehicles and securing their valuables.

Health in Ocean City

Ocean City has several medical facilities, including a hospital and urgent care centers. Visitors should also take appropriate health precautions, such as using sunscreen and staying hydrated in the summer heat.

Getting to Ocean City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ocean City

The nearest airport to Ocean City is Atlantic City International Airport, located about 20 miles away. Philadelphia International Airport is also a viable option, located about 65 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Ocean City

Greyhound and New Jersey Transit both have bus stops in nearby Atlantic City, which is about 10 miles away from Ocean City.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Ocean City, as the town is located just off the Garden State Parkway.

How to Get Around in Ocean City

Ocean City is a relatively small town, and most attractions are located within walking or biking distance of each other. Visitors can also use their vehicles to get around, though traffic can be heavy during peak summer months.

Where to Go Next from Ocean City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Atlantic City: Located just a few miles from Ocean City, Atlantic City is known for its casinos, nightlife, and beach boardwalk.

  • Cape May: This historic town is located about 20 miles south of Ocean City and is known for its Victorian architecture and charming downtown area.

Conclusion

Ocean City, New Jersey offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. From the beach and boardwalk to the wildlife sanctuary and museums, there’s something for everyone in this charming town. Plan your next vacation to Ocean City today and experience the best of what the Jersey Shore has to offer.
