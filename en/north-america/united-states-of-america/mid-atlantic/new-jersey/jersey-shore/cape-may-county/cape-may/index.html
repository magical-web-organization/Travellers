Introduction to Cape May

Tucked away in the southernmost part of the Garden State is the gorgeous coastal town of Cape May. Known for its stunning Victorian architecture, breathtaking beaches, and vibrant culture, Cape May is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking a perfect blend of sun, sand, and culture.

Local Culture and Customs of Cape May

Cape May is a friendly and welcoming town that embraces tourists with open arms. Visitors are always greeted with a smile and a warm hello. Dress is casual, but smart casual is preferred at most restaurants. Smoking is not allowed in any of the public places.

Best Time to Visit Cape May

The best time to visit Cape May is between May to September, when the weather is sunny and warm, and perfect for outdoor activities. Cape May’s peak tourist season is during the summer months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cape May

Do:

  • Tip at least 15% at restaurants
  • Walk or bike around town
  • Take a dolphin or whale watching tour
  • Follow beach rules and respect the wildlife
  • Visit Cape May Zoo
  • Shop at the Washington Street Mall

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beaches
  • Swim in unauthorized areas
  • Feed the seagulls
  • Enter restricted areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Cape May

Cape May is generally a safe destination for tourists. However, travelers should take normal safety precautions like keeping an eye on valuables and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Cape May

Cape May has several medical facilities, hospitals, and clinics that offer quality healthcare services.

How to Get Around in Cape May

The easiest and most convenient way to get around Cape May is by walking and biking. Bikes are available for rent throughout town, and taxis and car rentals are also available.
