Introduction to Avalon

Located in Cape May County on Jersey Shore, Avalon is a small town that covers just over four square miles of beautiful coastline. The town is known for being family-friendly and offering a quieter, more relaxed alternative to nearby bustling destinations like Atlantic City. With a plethora of activities, scenic views, and natural attractions, Avalon is a great choice for vacationers who want to enjoy some peace and quiet while still having plenty to do.

List of Places to Visit in Avalon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Cape May County Museum is the perfect place to start your cultural exploration of Avalon. Located in nearby Cape May Court House, this museum has a wide variety of exhibits showcasing the rich history of Cape May County and its surrounding areas. The museum is open year-round and offers visitors a glimpse into the county’s past, including its Native American heritage, colonial history, and even exhibits on a Revolutionary War shipwreck off the coast.

Natural Attractions of Avalon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Avalon is known for its beautiful beaches, and they are the town’s biggest attraction. Whether you want to swim, sunbathe, or take a walk along the shore, Avalon’s beaches are an excellent place to relax and unwind. The town’s beaches offer a variety of amenities, including restrooms, showers, and lifeguards, ensuring visitors can safely enjoy their time on the shore.

One of the most popular areas for outdoor activities in Avalon is the vast network of hiking and biking trails that wind through the town. From easy-going nature walks to challenging rocky paths, Avalon boasts a trail for every level of outdoor enthusiast. Visitors can also explore other natural attractions in the region, such as the Edwin B. Forsythe National Wildlife Refuge and the Cape May County Park and Zoo.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Avalon

Avalon offers miles of scenic trails that are perfect for hikers and bikers. The best place to start your excursion is along the town’s boardwalk that stretches from 21st Street to 32nd Street. From there, you can explore the Dune Trail that winds through the dunes and offers stunning views of the ocean and the town.

Another popular route is the 7.5-mile Avalon Boulevard Bike Path. This path takes riders through the heart of Avalon and offers plenty of scenic stops along the way. If you’re looking for a more challenging route, the 20-mile Coastline Trail runs the length of five barrier islands, offering some of the best views of the coastline in the region.

Best Things to Do in Avalon: Activities and Tours

Avalon has something to offer visitors of all ages and interests. Whether you’re interested in water sports, shopping, or cultural events, there’s always something to do in this vibrant beach town.

Some popular activities include surfing, paddleboarding, and kayaking, which can be arranged with local rental companies. Visitors can also take sunset cruises, fishing charters, and whale watching tours from nearby marinas.

Week-Long Itinerary for Avalon

Day Activity
1 Explore Avalon’s beaches, including the iconic Seven Mile Beach. Enjoy lunch at the Avalon Brew Pub.
2 Take a guided tour of the Cape May County Zoo, one of the best in the country. Spend the afternoon exploring nearby state parks and wildlife refuges.
3 Visit the Wetlands Institute, a nonprofit organization dedicated to conservation and education. Afterward, take in a show at the nearby Cape May Opera House.
4 Try your hand at fishing with one of the many charter companies in Avalon. In the evening, enjoy drinks and live music at the Concord Cafe.
5 Rent a bike and explore some of the many scenic trails in the area. Enjoy lunch at one of the town’s many outdoor cafes or seafood restaurants.
6 Take a day trip to nearby Cape May, known for its historic downtown and beautiful Victorian architecture. Don’t forget to sample some of the town’s famous fudge!
7 Relax on the beach and soak up the sun. In the evening, attend a free outdoor concert at the town’s band shell in Avalon Community Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Avalon

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Clams and Oysters: Avalon is famous for its seafood, particularly clams and oysters. Try them raw, fried, or in a chowder at one of the town’s many seafood restaurants.

  2. Salt Water Taffy: A classic Jersey Shore sweet treat, saltwater taffy is a must-try when in Avalon. Stop by one of the many candy stores in town to sample some of their unique flavors.

Local Beverages

  1. Craft Beer: Avalon has a thriving craft beer scene, with several microbreweries in town. Don’t miss the opportunity to sample some of the area’s best brews.

  2. Local Wines: The nearby Cape May region is home to some of New Jersey’s best wineries. Take a day trip wine tasting to sample some of the area’s top vintages.

Where to Stay in Avalon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Avalon has plenty of options for accommodations, from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxury hotels. For those looking for a more authentic experience, consider staying in a bed and breakfast. Many are located in historic homes or buildings, adding to the town’s charm and character.

What to Buy in Avalon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some great souvenirs to bring back from Avalon include:

  1. T-Shirts and Beach Apparel: Show off your love for Avalon with some beach-themed apparel from one of the town’s many surf shops.

  2. Salt Water Taffy: Bring home a taste of Avalon with some of the town’s famous saltwater taffy.

Local Culture and Customs of Avalon

Avalon is known for its welcoming and friendly locals. Visitors should make an effort to be respectful of local customs, such as following beach rules and avoiding excessive noise after dark.

Best Time to Visit Avalon

The best time to visit Avalon is from late spring through early fall when the weather is warm and pleasant. However, visitors looking to beat the crowds should consider visiting in the shoulder seasons of September and October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Avalon

Do:

  1. Respect the local wildlife and ecosystem.

  2. Follow beach rules and regulations.

Don’t:

  1. Make excessive noise after dark.

  2. Leave trash on the beach.

Safety and Health

Safety in Avalon

Avalon is a relatively safe town, but visitors should still take precautions to avoid incidents. Make sure to lock up valuables and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Avalon

Avalon has several medical facilities, including an urgent care center. Visitors should make sure to have health insurance and take necessary precautions, such as applying sunscreen and staying hydrated.

Getting to Avalon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Avalon

The closest airport to Avalon is Atlantic City International Airport, approximately 45 minutes away. Philadelphia International Airport is another viable option, located about 2 hours away.

Bus Stations Near Avalon

New Jersey Transit operates several bus routes that stop in nearby towns like Wildwood and Cape May.

Other Options

Driving to Avalon is also a popular option, with several major highways in close proximity to the town. Visitors can also take a train to nearby Atlantic City and take a shuttle or taxi to Avalon from there.

How to Get Around in Avalon

Visitors can easily get around Avalon on foot or by bike. The town also has several taxi companies for those needing transportation between different areas of the town.

Where to Go Next from Avalon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cape May

Located just 20 minutes south of Avalon, Cape May is a National Historic Landmark known for its beautifully preserved Victorian architecture and charming downtown.

Wildwood

Known for its Wildwood Boardwalk, which stretches for over 2 miles and is one of the top destinations for family fun on the East Coast.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking for a relaxing beach vacation or are interested in exploring the cultural and natural attractions of the Jersey Shore, Avalon has something for everyone. With its beautiful beaches, scenic trails, and welcoming community, this small town is a hidden gem that shouldn’t be missed.
