Introduction to Atlantic City

Located in Atlantic County, New Jersey Shore, Atlantic City is one of the most popular resort cities in the United States. It is known for its stunning beaches, iconic boardwalk, exciting nightlife, and rich cultural heritage. Atlantic City has something for everyone, from families looking for a relaxing beach vacation to solo travelers seeking adventure and entertainment. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take a closer look at the top attractions, activities, accommodations, and local tips to help you plan the perfect trip to Atlantic City.

List of Places to Visit in Atlantic City

There are plenty of things to see and do in Atlantic City, ranging from historical attractions to natural wonders. Here are some of the must-visit places in the city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Atlantic City Boardwalk: This 4-mile boardwalk is the heart of the city and features numerous shops, restaurants, and attractions.
  • Absecon Lighthouse: Climb to the top of the tallest lighthouse in New Jersey for panoramic views of the city.
  • The Steel Pier: This iconic amusement park has been a favorite for over 100 years and features rides, games, and live entertainment.
  • The Atlantic City Experience: This museum highlights the city’s rich history, including its role in Prohibition.
  • Gardner’s Basin: A picturesque marina featuring shops, restaurants, and a small aquarium.

Natural Attractions of Atlantic City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Atlantic City Beach: Relax on the soft sand and soak up the sun at this beautiful beach.
  • The Wharton State Forest: This expansive nature reserve offers hiking trails, camping opportunities, and scenic vistas.
  • The Lucy the Elephant: A unique and quirky attraction, this six-story elephant-shaped building offers guided tours and stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Atlantic City

If you’re looking to explore the city on foot or bike, Atlantic City has plenty of options. The Atlantic City Boardwalk is a popular place for walkers and runners, while the Absecon Lighthouse is a great spot to climb up and take in the city’s views. For more adventurous hikers and bikers, the Wharton State Forest offers miles of trails for all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Atlantic City: Activities and Tours

Aside from the attractions mentioned above, Atlantic City offers a variety of activities and tours for all types of travelers. Here are some of the best things to do in the city:

  • Try your luck at a casino: Atlantic City is known for its many world-class casinos, including the Borgata, Harrah’s Resort, and Tropicana Casino.
  • Take a surf lesson: Learn to ride the waves with professional instructors at Atlantic City’s beaches.
  • Take a sunset cruise: Enjoy a relaxing sunset cruise on the Atlantic Ocean with dinner, drinks, and live entertainment.
  • Explore the city by Segway: See the sights of Atlantic City in a unique and fun way on a Segway tour.
  • Visit the Atlantic City Aquarium: Get up close with a variety of marine life, including sharks, stingrays, and sea turtles.

Week-Long Itinerary for Atlantic City

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Atlantic City:

Day 1 Arrival and check-in at hotel, explore the Atlantic City Boardwalk

Food and Drink Scene of Atlantic City

Atlantic City offers a diverse and delicious food and drink scene, with plenty of options for all tastes and budgets. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Saltwater Taffy: A classic Atlantic City treat, this chewy candy comes in a variety of flavors.
  • White House Sub Shop: This family-run business has been serving legendary sandwiches since 1946.
  • Seafood: Fresh seafood is a staple in Atlantic City, with plenty of restaurants offering everything from crab cakes to lobster rolls.

Local Beverages

  • The Orange Crush: This refreshing cocktail is made with fresh orange juice, vodka, triple sec, and Sprite.
  • Atlantic City Beer: Try a local beer from one of the city’s many craft breweries, such as Tun Tavern Brewery or Little Water Distillery.

Where to Stay in Atlantic City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Atlantic City, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some top picks:

  • The Water Club at Borgata: This luxury hotel features elegant rooms, a spa, and its own casino.
  • Flagship Resort: A budget-friendly option with ocean views and an on-site restaurant.
  • The Chelsea: This boutique hotel offers chic rooms, a rooftop bar, and easy access to the beach.

What to Buy in Atlantic City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect souvenir or gift from your trip to Atlantic City? Here are some ideas:

  • Saltwater taffy: Bring home a box of this sweet treat to share with family and friends.
  • Boardwalk artwork: Browse the many artists selling their work on the boardwalk and pick up a unique piece to decorate your home.
  • Atlantic City Beer: Grab a six-pack of locally-brewed beer to enjoy at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Atlantic City

Atlantic City has a rich cultural heritage, with influences from Native American, African American, and European American communities. Visitors to the city should be respectful of local customs and traditions, including dress codes at casinos and beach etiquette. Tipping is also expected at restaurants, bars, and hotels.

Best Time to Visit Atlantic City

The best time to visit Atlantic City depends on your preferences. Summer is the most popular time, with warm weather, beach days, and plenty of events and activities. However, prices can be high and crowds can be thick. Spring and fall offer milder weather and smaller crowds, but some businesses may not be open year-round. Winter is the quietest season, with lower prices and fewer tourists, but colder temperatures and limited activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Atlantic City

Here are some general do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Atlantic City:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine: Atlantic City is known for its delicious seafood, so don’t miss out on the fresh catch of the day.
  • Explore the boardwalk: This iconic feature of Atlantic City offers plenty of shopping, dining, and entertainment options.
  • Take a stroll on the beach: The soft sand and ocean views are a must-see on any trip to Atlantic City.

Don’t:

  • Overlook the historical sites: Atlantic City has a rich heritage that is worth exploring, from the lighthouse to the underground tunnels.
  • Forget to tip: Service industry workers rely on tips as a significant portion of their income, so be sure to tip generously at restaurants, bars, and hotels.
  • Ignore safety precautions: Like any city, Atlantic City has its fair share of crime and safety risks, so be sure to take appropriate precautions when exploring.

Safety and Health

Like any city, Atlantic City has safety and health concerns that visitors should be aware of. Here are some important considerations:

Safety in Atlantic City

  • Watch your belongings: Pickpocketing and theft can occur in any city, so be sure to keep a close eye on your belongings and lock them up when possible.
  • Be aware of your surroundings: Avoid walking alone at night or in secluded areas, and stay in well-lit areas when possible.
  • Follow casino rules: Casinos have strict rules regarding alcohol consumption, smoking, and gambling, so be sure to follow them to avoid any issues with security or law enforcement.

Health in Atlantic City

  • Wear sunscreen: The sun can be intense on the beach, so be sure to wear sunscreen with at least SPF 30 and reapply regularly.
  • Stay hydrated: Drink plenty of water, especially if you’re spending time outdoors or walking long distances.
  • Be aware of allergens: Some restaurants and cafes may use peanuts or other common allergens in their dishes, so be sure to ask about ingredients if you have any allergies.

Getting to Atlantic City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Atlantic City is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Atlantic City

  • Atlantic City International Airport: Located just outside of the city, this airport serves several major airlines.

Bus Stations Near Atlantic City

  • Atlantic City Bus Terminal: This bus station is located in the heart of the city and is served by several major bus companies, including Greyhound and Peter Pan.

Other Options

  • Driving: Atlantic City is accessible via several major highways, including the Atlantic City Expressway and the Garden State Parkway.
  • Train: While there is no Amtrak service to Atlantic City, NJ Transit offers trains to nearby Absecon Station, where you can transfer to a bus or taxi to reach the city.

How to Get Around in Atlantic City

The most popular way to get around Atlantic City is by walking, biking, or using public transportation. The Jitney mini-bus service is a popular option for getting around the city, with stops at all major attractions. Taxis and rideshare services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Atlantic City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of the Mid-Atlantic region, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to choose from. Here are some top picks:

  • Cape May: This charming seaside town is known for its Victorian architecture, lovely beaches, and fine dining.
  • Philadelphia: Just a short drive or train ride away, Philadelphia has plenty of historical attractions, art museums, and urban parks.
  • New York City: A longer excursion but worth the trip, the Big Apple is a world-class metropolis with endless entertainment and cultural offerings.

Conclusion

Atlantic City is a vibrant and exciting city that offers plenty of opportunities for fun and relaxation. With this comprehensive guide, you’re well-equipped to plan the perfect trip, from exploring the boardwalk to trying out local cuisine. Remember to be respectful of local customs and take appropriate safety and health precautions to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip.
