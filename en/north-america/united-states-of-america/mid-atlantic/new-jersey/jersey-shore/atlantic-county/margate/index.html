Introduction to Margate

Margate is a beach town located in Atlantic County in New Jersey, USA. Popularly known as “America’s Playground,” Margate has something to offer every traveler who visits this charming town. Whether you are looking for a relaxing beach vacation, a little bit of adventure, or an escape into nature, Margate won’t disappoint. Here’s the ultimate travel guide to help you plan your next trip to Margate.

List of Places to Visit in Margate

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lucy the Elephant: One of the most popular and iconic landmarks in Margate, Lucy the Elephant is a six-story building shaped like an elephant. Built in 1881, Lucy is now a National Historic Landmark and a must-visit destination for every traveler.
  • Margate City Historical Society Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving and showcasing the local history of Margate City. The museum features exhibits on the city’s growth, development, and famous residents.

Natural Attractions of Margate: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Margate Beach: Margate’s beach is a mile-long stretch of pristine sand and blue waters, perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and relaxing.
  • Jersey Shore Alpacas: Visitors to Margate can get up close and personal with alpacas at this family-owned and operated farm. Visitors can pet and feed the alpacas as well as learn about their history and habitat.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Margate

Margate is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts looking to explore the area on foot or bike. Some of the best hiking/biking routes in Margate are:

  • Margate Boardwalk: Take a stroll or bike ride along the scenic Margate Boardwalk, which stretches for miles along the coast.
  • Atlantic County Bikeway: This 7-mile paved bike trail winds through the region’s scenic countryside and offers views of the surrounding forests, wetlands, and farmlands.

Best Things to Do in Margate: Activities and Tours

  • Jet Ski Rentals: Rent a jet ski and hit the waves for an adrenaline-filled adventure.
  • Fishing Charters: Take a fishing charter and try your luck at catching some fish in the Atlantic Ocean.
  • Atlantic City Casinos: Margate is just a short drive away from the famous Atlantic City casinos and boardwalk.

Week-Long Itinerary for Margate

Day 1: Explore Margate’s boardwalk and beach. Day 2: Visit Lucy the Elephant and the Margate City Historical Society Museum. Day 3: Go fishing or rent a jet ski for a fun and adventurous day out. Day 4: Take a day trip to Atlantic City and explore its famous casinos and boardwalk. Day 5: Go hiking or biking on the Atlantic County Bikeway. Day 6: Visit Jersey Shore Alpacas and learn about these adorable animals. Day 7: Relax on the beach and enjoy the sun and sand.

Food and Drink Scene of Margate

Must-Try Dishes

  • Saltwater Taffy: This classic local treat is a must-try for anyone visiting Margate.
  • Crab Cakes: Fresh crab cakes are a staple of the Jersey Shore and can be found at many local restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Jersey Devil Vodka: This locally produced vodka is made from New Jersey-grown sweet potatoes.
  • Cape May Brewing Company Beer: This award-winning brewery is located just a short drive from Margate and offers a variety of delicious beers.

Where to Stay in Margate: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Margate offers a variety of accommodation options, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses.

  • The Margate Resort: This elegant waterfront hotel offers stunning views of Lake Winnipesaukee and features an indoor pool, fitness center, and spa.
  • The Bayberry Inn: This charming bed and breakfast is located just a short walk from the beach and offers cozy rooms and delicious breakfasts.

What to Buy in Margate: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Saltwater Taffy: This iconic candy is a great gift to bring back home for friends and family.
  • Beachwear: Margate offers a great selection of beachwear, including swimsuits, hats, and sunglasses.

Local Culture and Customs of Margate

Margate has a laid-back and friendly culture, with a focus on beach life, seafood, and outdoor activities. Visitors should be respectful of the environment and take care not to litter or damage fragile ecosystems.

Best Time to Visit Margate

The best times to visit Margate are in the spring and fall when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. However, if you want to experience the full beach town experience, the summer months are the busiest and offer the most activities and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Margate

Do:

  • Respect the environment and natural habitats
  • Try local seafood and cuisine
  • Visit Lucy the Elephant

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the beaches and natural habitats
  • Swim in areas designated as unsafe for swimming
  • Ignore beach rules and regulations

Safety and Health

Safety in Margate

Margate is a relatively safe destination for travelers, but visitors should exercise caution when swimming in the ocean and be aware of rip currents and other potential hazards.

Health in Margate

Visitors to Margate should be aware of the risk of sunburn and take appropriate precautions, such as wearing sunscreen and staying hydrated.

Getting to Margate: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Margate

  • Atlantic City International Airport: Located just 15 miles from Margate, this airport offers daily flights to and from major cities across the United States.

Bus Stations Near Margate

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: This bus company offers bus service to and from Atlantic City, which is a short drive from Margate.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car at the airport and drive to Margate.
  • Taxi Service: Taxis are available from the airport to Margate.

How to Get Around in Margate

Margate is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance. Visitors can also rent bikes or take a taxi to get around.

Where to Go Next from Margate: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Atlantic City: Just a short drive from Margate, Atlantic City offers a variety of attractions, including casinos, beaches, and boardwalks.
  • Cape May: This charming seaside town is located about an hour’s drive from Margate and is known for its historic Victorian architecture and beautiful beaches.

Conclusion

Margate is a charming and inviting destination for anyone looking for a beach town getaway. From the famous Lucy the Elephant to the scenic Margate Boardwalk, there’s something for everyone in this New Jersey gem. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re all set to plan your next trip to Margate!
