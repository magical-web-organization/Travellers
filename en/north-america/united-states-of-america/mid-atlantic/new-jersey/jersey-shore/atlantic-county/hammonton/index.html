A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Hammonton, New Jersey

Hammonton is a charming town located in Atlantic County, New Jersey, known for its stunning scenery, cultural attractions, vibrant food scene, and rich history. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your next trip to this hidden gem of the Garden State, where you will find plenty of things to see and do, delicious food to try, and memories to last a lifetime.

Introduction to Hammonton

Located in the heart of South Jersey’s Pinelands, Hammonton is a town of approximately 14,000 residents, 30 miles from Philadelphia and 60 miles from Atlantic City. Known as the “Blueberry Capital of the World,” it is home to numerous blueberry farms and festivals.

List of Places to Visit in Hammonton

Whether you are interested in history, culture, or nature, Hammonton has something for everyone.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hammonton Historical Society Museum: located in the historic train station, this museum has a vast collection of artifacts and archives that tell the story of the town’s rich history.

  • Wharton State Forest: the largest forest in New Jersey, this natural wonder offers visitors acres of preserved wilderness perfect for hiking, camping, bird watching, and bike riding.

Natural Attractions of Hammonton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Atsion Lake: a serene lake perfect for swimming, fishing, and boating, surrounded by beautiful pine trees and picturesque trails.

  • Batsto Village: once a bustling community in the 19th century, now a preserved historic site with guided tours, a visitor center, and numerous trails for hiking and biking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hammonton

If a leisurely stroll or a challenging hike is your thing, Hammonton has plenty of options for both.

  • Kirkwood Preserve: a 150-acre nature preserve with several trails, including the Kirkwood Trail, a 2.5-mile hike through stunning pine forests and swamps.

  • Wharton State Forest: with over 100,000 acres of pristine wilderness, you will find numerous trails for hiking, biking, and camping.

Best Things to Do in Hammonton: Activities and Tours

While visiting Hammonton, you will never run out of things to do. Here are some of our favorites:

  • Blueberry Picking: visit one of the many blueberry farms in town and pick your own fresh, juicy berries.

  • Wine Tasting: Hammonton is known for its growing selection of wineries, and many offer tours and tastings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hammonton

If you have a week to explore Hammonton, here is a suggested itinerary:

Day Activities
Day 1 Hammonton Historical Society Museum
Day 2 Wharton State Forest
Day 3 Fruit Picking and Farm Markets
Day 4 Batsto Village
Day 5 Wine Tasting
Day 6 Kirkwood Preserve
Day 7 Atsion Lake

Food and Drink Scene of Hammonton

Hammonton is a hub for fresh, local, and delicious food. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Blueberry Pie: made with fresh, juicy, locally grown blueberries, this iconic dessert is sure to satisfy your sweet tooth.

  • Pork Roll: a Jersey classic, this breakfast meat is a must-try for any visitor to the state.

Local Beverages

  • Blueberry Wine: made with the town’s signature fruit, this wine comes in many varieties and is the perfect way to celebrate your visit to Hammonton

  • Craft Beer: Hammonton is home to several breweries, including Tomfoolery, where you can enjoy a locally brewed pint.

Where to Stay in Hammonton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you are looking for a luxurious bed and breakfast or a cozy guesthouse, Hammonton has many options to choose from.

  • The Inn at Sugar Hill: a historic bed and breakfast located in the heart of Hammonton, close to all of the town’s attractions.

  • La Campagnola: a charming guesthouse with beautifully appointed rooms, close to the town’s restaurants and shopping.

What to Buy in Hammonton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking for unique gifts and souvenirs to bring back home, Hammonton has plenty of options.

  • Blueberry Products: from jams to pies, from wine to ice cream, you can find all sorts of blueberry-inspired products throughout the town.

  • Artisanal Products: Hammonton is home to many talented artisans who create unique ceramics, jewelry, and other unique creations.

Local Culture and Customs of Hammonton

Hammonton prides itself on its rich cultural heritage and celebrates it through annual festivals such as the Red, White, and Blueberry Festival. Residents are friendly and welcoming, and the town is safe and easy to navigate.

Best Time to Visit Hammonton

The best time to visit Hammonton is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the blueberry harvest is in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hammonton

Here are some helpful tips to help you make the most of your visit:

Do:

  • Try Local Cuisine: Hammonton is known for its delicious food, so make sure to try out some of the local specialties.

  • Explore the Great Outdoors: with the Pinelands and the Atsion Lake as its backdrop, there are plenty of scenic hiking and biking trails to explore.

Don’t:

  • Litter: it is essential to take care of the town’s beautiful surroundings by disposing of trash properly.

  • Ignore the Speed Limit Signs: Hammonton is a relatively small town, so make sure to abide by the speed limit signs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hammonton

Hammonton is generally a safe town, but it is always essential to take usual safety precautions, such as locking your car doors and not leaving valuables in plain sight.

Health in Hammonton

Being a small town, you will have limited options in healthcare. There is a nearby hospital in Atlantic City and several clinics in the town open during business hours.

Getting to Hammonton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Hammonton is easily accessible by all forms of transportation, including:

Airports Near Hammonton

  • Atlantic City International Airport: located 26 miles away from Hammonton and offers regular flights from major cities in the U.S.

Bus Stations Near Hammonton

  • New Jersey Transit: provides regular bus services from New York City, Philadelphia, and other neighboring towns.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals: several car rental companies and taxis are available in the town.

How to Get Around Hammonton

Hammonton is pedestrian-friendly, and most of its attractions are walkable. If you do not want to walk, car rentals or taxis are available.

Where to Go Next from Hammonton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time to explore more of New Jersey, here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Atlantic City: known as the gambling capital of the East Coast, Atlantic City offers world-class casinos, nightlife, and a famous boardwalk.

  • Cape May: a quaint and historic seaside town known for its Victorian architecture, pristine beaches, and natural beauty.

Conclusion

Hammonton, New Jersey, is a charming town with a rich history, cultural attractions, and beautiful scenery. It is the perfect destination for a weekend or a week-long trip. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your next adventure to this hidden gem of the Garden State, and let Hammonton show you what it has in store for you.
