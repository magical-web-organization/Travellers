Introduction to Brigantine

Located in Atlantic County, New Jersey, Brigantine is a charming seaside community tucked away on the Jersey Shore, offering visitors an array of activities, attractions, and miles of beautiful coastline to explore. Originally inhabited by the Lenni-Lenape Native American tribe, Brigantine has a rich history dating back to the 1600s. Today, it’s a popular destination for vacationers from all over the world, known for its pristine beaches, stunning scenery, and warm hospitality. This ultimate travel guide to Brigantine will help you plan your next trip to this idyllic destination.

List of Places to Visit in Brigantine

Whether you’re looking for a relaxing beach day or an action-packed adventure, Brigantine has plenty of attractions to suit every kind of traveler. Here are some of the best places to visit in Brigantine:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Brigantine Beach Historical Museum: Located on the corner of 26th street and Brigantine Avenue, this museum tells the story of Brigantine’s rich history and showcases artifacts from its past.
  • Lighthouse Challenge of New Jersey: Visit the lighthouses of New Jersey and climb to the top of the Absecon Lighthouse for panoramic views of Brigantine.
  • Edwin B. Forsythe National Wildlife Refuge: The refuge is located on the Atlantic Flyway and provides a home for thousands of migratory birds, making it a great spot for bird watchers.

Natural Attractions of Brigantine: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Brigantine Beach: The six-mile stretch of Brigantine beach is perfect for sunbathing, swimming and surfing.
  • Brigantine Sea Wall: The Brigantine Sea wall is the perfect place to take a stroll, bike ride or jog with views of the ocean and the bay.
  • North Brigantine State Natural Area: This 526-acre nature preserve is home to an array of wildlife, such as birds and foxes, and offers visitors picturesque views of the marshlands.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Brigantine

Brigantine offers numerous hiking and biking routes for travelers who love the great outdoors. Here are some of the best trails to check out during your visit:

  • Brigantine North-End Trail: Located in the North Brigantine State Natural Area, this trail is a four-miles-long and offers stunning views of the marshland and wildlife.
  • Brigantine Beach Trail: Take a walk along the shoreline in Brigantine that offers visitors a peaceful respite from the crowds.

Best Things to Do in Brigantine: Activities and Tours

Brigantine offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors of all ages. Here are some of the most popular ones:

  • Surfing Lessons: Take a lesson at Brigantine beach and experience the thrill of catching a wave.
  • Fishing and Boat Tours: Rent a boat and explore the waterways around Brigantine.
  • Golf Courses: Brigantine has several golf courses for visitors who enjoy the sport.

Here’s a week-long itinerary to make the most of your visit to Brigantine:

Food and Drink Scene of Brigantine

Brigantine offers an array of dining options, from seafood restaurants to cafes and bistros. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Brigantine:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood: Brigantine is known for its fresh seafood, which can be enjoyed at restaurants such as Crabby’s Suds and Seafood, Hooper’s Crab House, and The Cellar 32.
  • Pizza: Brigantine has some of the best pizza joints, including Ernesto’s Ristorante and Tony’s Baltimore Grill.
  • Saltwater Taffy: Take home some saltwater taffy from the iconic James’ Candy Company, a staple in Brigantine since 1880!

Local Beverages

  • Cocktail: Head over to one of Brigantine’s popular Beach bars like Laguna Grill & Rum Bar, The Cove or Sammy’s Beach Bar to enjoy some of the best drinks and light bites.
  • Beer: Check out the crazy selection of beer at the Brigantine Brewery or the Ship Bottom Brewery, both of which are located on the mainland.

Where to Stay in Brigantine: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Brigantine has a range of accommodation options for visitors, from affordable hotels and motels to luxurious beachfront resorts. Some of the popular hotels include:

  • Harbour Inn and Marina: This family-friendly hotel overlooks the marina and is just a stone’s throw from the beach.
  • Legacy Vacation Resort: A beachfront resort with excellent amenities, this hotel is perfect for families.
  • Brigantine Beach Club: Located right on the beach south end, Brigantine Beach Club offers stylish accommodation with beach-side amenities.

What to Buy in Brigantine: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • T-shirts or hats from any of the beachfront gift shops;

Local Culture and Customs of Brigantine

Brigantine offers a warm and welcoming atmosphere, with locals always eager to help visitors make the most of their stay. Here are a few cultural customs to keep in mind:

  • Bring your beach gear, and be prepared to relax all day long.
  • If you’re surfing or fishing, make sure to follow the rules of the beach for the safety of yourself and others.
  • Respect the environment and marine life.

Best Time to Visit Brigantine

Brigantine is a year-round destination, although the best time to visit is from May to September when the weather is warm, and the beach and surrounding attractions are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Brigantine

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Brigantine.

Do:

  • Always tip servers and bartenders.
  • Take time to explore North Redington Beach for serene views and experiences.
  • Learn about Brigantine’s history at The Brigantine Historical Museum.

Don’t:

  • Don’t wear your shoes on the sand, take them off when stepping onto the beach.
  • Don’t leave trash or litter on the beach.
  • Don’t rent a board or boat nearby the jetty.

Safety and Health

Brigantine is a safe and healthy destination, but it’s still important to take precautions to ensure a worry-free trip.

Safety in Brigantine

  • Always keep a watchful eye on young children near water.
  • Remember to reapply sunscreen frequently.
  • Always lock doors and windows before leaving your hotel room or rental property.

Health in Brigantine

  • If you’re planning to spend the day at the beach or engaging in outdoor activities, bring plenty of water and stay hydrated.
  • Protect yourself from bites and bugs by wearing protective clothing and insect repellent.

Getting to Brigantine: Transportation Options and Logistics

Brigantine is easily accessible by car and public transportation. Here are some of the transportation options available:

Airports Near Brigantine

  • Atlantic City International Airport: Located just fifteen minutes away from Brigantine, this airport offers direct flights to and from several major cities in the USA.

Bus Stations Near Brigantine

  • Greyhound: Greyhound Bus Lines operates a station in Atlantic City, which is about 10 miles from Brigantine.

Other Options:

  • Travel by Car: Brigantine is easily accessible by car. Visitors can take the AC Expressway East and turn off on North Route 30.
  • Ride-Sharing Services: Visitors can also book an Uber or Lyft for a more convenient way to travel.

How to Get Around in Brigantine

Brigantine is a relatively small town, which means it’s possible to get around on foot or by bike. There are also several car rental companies available for visitors who prefer to have their transportation.

Where to Go Next from Brigantine: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore some nearby destinations, here are a few ideas:

  • Princeton, New Jersey: Home to Princeton University, this charming college town is just a two-hour drive from Brigantine.
  • Camden Waterfront: Located just an hour away, the Camden Waterfront offers stunning views of the Delaware River and several popular attractions, including the Adventure Aquarium and Battleship New Jersey.
  • Atlantic City: The bustling resort town is a 15 minutes drive from Brigantine and offers a lively nightlife, casinos, shopping, and entertainment.

Conclusion

Brigantine is the perfect destination for travelers looking for a relaxed beach vacation with plenty of activities and sights to explore. With its stunning coastline, outdoor adventures, and rich cultural heritage, there is something for everyone in Brigantine. Whether you’re traveling solo, with family, or with a group of friends, this ultimate travel guide will help you make the most of your trip.
