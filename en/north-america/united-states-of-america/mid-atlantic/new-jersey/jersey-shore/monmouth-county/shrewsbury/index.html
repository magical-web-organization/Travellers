Introduction to Shrewsbury

Located on the Jersey Shore in Monmouth County, New Jersey, Shrewsbury offers visitors a mix of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and delicious dining options. Known for its scenic beauty and rich history, the town attracts visitors from across the region. This comprehensive travel guide to Shrewsbury will help you plan your visit, including the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay.

List of Places to Visit in Shrewsbury

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Shrewsbury Historic District - This 18th-century district is home to numerous historic homes, including the Allen House and Christ Church. Visitors will get a glimpse of Shrewsbury’s past as they walk along the district’s charming streets.
  • Monmouth County Historical Association Museum - The museum showcases the history of Monmouth County, with exhibits on the area’s Native American history, Revolutionary War battles, and agricultural past.
  • Twin Lights Historic Site - Just a short drive away, the Twin Lights Historic Site offers visitors stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean from its iconic lighthouses. Also located on the site is the Museum of Twin Lights, which explores the history of the lighthouses and their importance to the region’s shipping industry.

Natural Attractions of Shrewsbury: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Seven Presidents Oceanfront Park - This beachfront park is a popular spot for swimming, sunbathing, and fishing. With bike paths, volleyball courts, and playgrounds, it’s an ideal place for families.
  • Hartshorne Woods Park - With over 700 acres of wilderness, Hartshorne Woods Park is a haven for hikers and mountain bikers. The park features over 16 miles of trails, offering visitors stunning views of the Navesink River and Atlantic Ocean.
  • Shrewsbury Riverfront - Visitors can rent kayaks and paddleboards to explore the tranquil waters of the Shrewsbury River. Bird watchers will also appreciate the diverse birdlife that calls the river home.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shrewsbury

With its beautiful natural landscapes, Shrewsbury is a great place for hiking and biking. Some of the best trails to explore include:

  • The Henry Hudson Trail - This 24-mile rail-trail runs from Freehold to Highlands, passing through numerous parks and scenic towns. The trail can be accessed from a number of points in Shrewsbury.
  • Huber Woods Park - This park offers several hiking trails that range in difficulty, but all offer scenic views of the forest and river. The park is also known for its bird watching opportunities.
  • Cedar Ridge Preserve - This preserve is a great spot for mountain biking, with a variety of trails of varying difficulty levels. Visitors will also enjoy the picturesque views of the surrounding farmland.

Best Things to Do in Shrewsbury: Activities and Tours

  • Sailboat Charter - Experience the beauty of the Atlantic Ocean by renting a sailboat with Blue Water Sailing.
  • Escape Room - Test your puzzle-solving skills at Shrewsbury’s Escape Room.
  • Fishing Charters - Enjoy a day fishing with Cindi Hahn’s Custom Fishing Charters.

Week-Long Itinerary for Shrewsbury

Day 1: Take a walking tour of the Shrewsbury Historic District and explore the Allen House. Day 2: Hike the trails at Hartshorne Woods Park and enjoy a picnic lunch. Day 3: Spend the day at the beach at Seven Presidents Oceanfront Park. Day 4: Visit the Monmouth County Historical Association Museum and learn about the region’s past. Day 5: Rent kayaks and explore the Shrewsbury River. Day 6: Take a fishing charter with Cindi Hahn’s Custom Fishing Charters. Day 7: Take a day trip to the Twin Lights Historic Site and the Museum of Twin Lights.

Food and Drink Scene of Shrewsbury

Must-Try Dishes

  • Schneider’s Bakery - Don’t leave Shrewsbury without trying one of Schneider’s famous crumb cakes.
  • Lil Cutie Pops - These artisanal popsicles come in unique flavors like honey lavender and espresso.
  • Duck Donuts - These made-to-order donuts are sure to satisfy your sweet tooth.

Local Beverages

  • Red Tank Cider - This farm winery offers a range of hard ciders made from locally sourced ingredients.
  • Wine Tasting at 4JGs Orchards & Vineyards - 4JGs is a family-owned winery that offers tastings of its award-winning wines.

Where to Stay in Shrewsbury: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Tower Cottage Bed & Breakfast - This Victorian-style B&B offers luxurious rooms and a gourmet breakfast.
  • Courtyard by Marriott - Located minutes from the beach, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and a range of amenities.
  • Extended Stay America - This budget-friendly hotel offers spacious rooms with kitchenettes, making it a great choice for families or long-term stays.

What to Buy in Shrewsbury: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Schneider’s Bakery Crumb Cakes - Bring home a taste of Shrewsbury with one of Schneider’s famous crumb cakes.
  • Red Tank Cider - Take home a bottle of hard cider made from locally sourced ingredients.

Local Culture and Customs of Shrewsbury

Shrewsbury is a quaint, historic town with a focus on community. Visitors should be respectful of the town’s history and take time to appreciate the unique architecture and natural beauty of the area.

Best Time to Visit Shrewsbury

The best time to visit Shrewsbury is during the summer months of June through August when the weather is warm and sunny, and the beaches are open.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shrewsbury

Do:

  • Take a walking tour of the Shrewsbury Historic District.
  • Hike the trails at Hartshorne Woods Park.
  • Visit the Monmouth County Historical Association Museum.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beaches or in the parks.
  • Trespass on private property.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shrewsbury

Shrewsbury is a safe town to visit but, like any other place, visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take precautions to avoid theft or other crimes.

Health in Shrewsbury

Visitors should take care of their skin when spending time outside in the sun and protect themselves from tick bites when hiking in the woods.

Getting to Shrewsbury: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shrewsbury

The closest airports to Shrewsbury are Newark Liberty International Airport (EWR) in Newark, NJ and John F. Kennedy International Airport (JFK) in New York City.

Bus Stations Near Shrewsbury

The closest bus station to Shrewsbury is the Red Bank Bus Station, which offers connections to New York City and other destinations in New Jersey.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Shrewsbury, take a train to the nearby Red Bank station, or hire a car service to take them to their destination.

How to Get Around in Shrewsbury

The best way to get around Shrewsbury is by car, but visitors can also rent bikes or use a taxi service.

Where to Go Next from Shrewsbury: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Red Bank - This nearby town is known for its vibrant arts scene and bustling downtown area.
  • Long Branch - Just a short drive away, Long Branch offers visitors a beautiful beach, charming downtown, and boardwalk.
  • Asbury Park - Known for its boardwalk and lively music scene, Asbury Park is a great place to spend the day.

Conclusion

Shrewsbury offers visitors a unique blend of history, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can plan their perfect trip, whether they’re interested in hiking, beach-going, or simply exploring the town’s charming streets.
