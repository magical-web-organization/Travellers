Introduction to Allentown

Located in Monmouth County, in the Jersey Shore region of New Jersey, the cozy borough of Allentown offers scenic countryside views, cultural landmarks, and a rich history. It is part of Mid-Atlantic in the United States of America, in North America.

Allentown was established in the 18th century and has managed to preserve its architectural and cultural heritage throughout the years. The borough is famous for being the site of the United States’ first recorded flag-raising ceremony. It is also home to several historical and cultural landmarks that attract tourists from all over the world.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore all the aspects of this charming town, including must-see attractions, outdoor adventures, dining options, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Allentown

No visit to Allentown is complete without exploring its iconic landmarks and scenic locations. Here are some not-to-be-missed places to visit in Allentown:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Old Presbyterian Church of Allentown: Established in the 18th century, the Old Presbyterian Church of Allentown is a historical landmark that offers a glimpse into the rich history of the town. The church features beautiful stained glass windows, antique pews, and a cemetery where local heroes are buried.

  • Allentown Public Library: The Allentown Public Library houses a vast collection of books, magazines, and historical artifacts. The library frequently hosts cultural events, such as lectures, workshops, and book clubs, that are open to the public.

  • Site of the First Flag Raising: This historic site marks the location where the United States’ first recorded flag-raising ceremony took place. The celebration occurred in 1777, and a marker now commemorates this important event.

Natural Attractions of Allentown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Assunpink Wildlife Management Area: The Assunpink Wildlife Management Area is a natural reserve that covers over 6,000 acres of land and provides excellent opportunities for hiking, bird-watching, and fishing. It is home to various species of plants and wildlife, such as bears, deer, and bald eagles.

  • Stone Tavern Farm: This family-owned farm is located in the heart of Allentown and offers visitors a chance to experience rural life. Visitors can participate in fun activities like hayrides, pumpkin picking, and feeding the farm animals.

  • Mill Pond Nature Preserve: This stunning nature preserve covers 14 acres of land, including a pond that provides ample opportunities for fishing, boating, and kayaking. The park also features walking trails, picnic areas, and a playground.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Allentown

Exploring Allentown’s natural beauty is an experience that nature lovers should not miss. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Allentown:

  • Assunpink Wildlife Management Area Hiking Trails: The Assunpink Wildlife Management Area features several hiking trails that vary in length and difficulty. Visitors can explore the lush woods, fields, and wetlands of the reserve and observe local wildlife in their natural habitat.

  • D&L Trail: The D&L Trail is a 165-mile-long trail that passes through Allentown and offers beautiful views of the countryside. The trail is perfect for cycling, hiking, and horseback riding and is accessible to people of all ages and skill levels.

  • Mill Pond Nature Preserve Trails: The Mill Pond Nature Preserve features a variety of easy-to-moderately-difficult hiking trails that offer stunning views of the pond and the surrounding woods. Visitors can explore wildlife habitats, bogs, and wetlands while enjoying the peaceful scenery.

Best Things to Do in Allentown: Activities and Tours

Allentown offers visitors a wide range of activities and tours that cater to all interests and age groups. Here are some of the best things to do in Allentown:

  • Allentown Artisans’ Gallery and Gifts: This quaint shop offers visitors a chance to browse and purchase unique, handmade arts and crafts. The gallery features local artists’ works, including pottery, jewelry, paintings, and sculptures.

  • Historic Walking Tours: Visitors can join a historic walking tour of Allentown and learn about the town’s rich history and architectural style. The tours are led by knowledgeable guides who share interesting stories and anecdotes about the town’s past.

  • Wine Tasting Tours: Allentown is home to several wineries that offer wine-tasting tours and scenic vineyard views. Visitors can enjoy a relaxing day out and learn about the winemaking process while sampling delicious wines.

Week-Long Itinerary for Allentown

To fully explore all that Allentown has to offer, visitors can follow this week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Visit the Old Presbyterian Church of Allentown, the Site of the First Flag Raising, and the Allentown Public Library.

Day 2: Explore Assunpink Wildlife Management Area and enjoy hiking trails and fishing.

Day 3: Experience rural life at Stone Tavern Farm and participate in farm activities.

Day 4: Visit the Mill Pond Nature Reserve and enjoy hiking, boating, and relaxing in natural surroundings.

Day 5: Join a historic walking tour of Allentown and learn about the town’s rich history.

Day 6: Visit wineries for wine-tasting tours and enjoy the scenic countryside views.

Day 7: Spend a day exploring local artisan shops and galleries, and enjoy local cuisines.

Food and Drink Scene of Allentown

Allentown is a hidden gem in New Jersey’s restaurant scene that boasts a diverse culinary experience for visitors. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Allentown:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jersey Crabcake: Made with fresh blue crab meat and seasoned with Old Bay spice, this dish is a local delicacy that seafood lovers should not miss.

  • Pork Roll: Also known as Taylor ham, this delicious dish is made by grilling or frying thin slices of seasoned pork roll. It is served on a soft Kaiser roll with egg and cheese, making it a perfect breakfast sandwich.

  • Salt Water Taffy: This famous candy is a must-try when exploring the Jersey shore. Saltwater taffy is a chewy and soft candy that comes in a variety of flavors, including vanilla, chocolate, and mint.

Local Beverages

  • Jersey Fresh Wine: Allentown is home to several wineries that produce delicious wines from locally grown grapes. Visitors can enjoy wine-tasting tours and scenic vineyard views.

  • Craft Beer: Allentown has a growing craft beer scene, with several microbreweries crafting unique and delicious beers. Visitors can sample local beer on tap at various breweries and brewpubs.

Where to Stay in Allentown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Allentown offers a wide range of accommodation options for visitors, from luxury hotels to cozy bed and breakfasts. Here are some of the best places to stay in Allentown:

  • The Inn at Millrace Pond: This charming bed and breakfast offers comfortable rooms with scenic views of the pond and the surrounding countryside. Guests can enjoy a hot breakfast and relax in the beautiful garden.

  • Crystal Springs Resort: This luxury resort is located near Allentown and offers deluxe accommodations, fine dining options, and world-class amenities. Guests can enjoy golf courses, spa services, and swimming pools.

  • Molly Pitcher Inn: This waterside hotel overlooks the Navesink River and boasts comfortable rooms, scenic views, and excellent dining options.

What to Buy in Allentown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Culture and Customs of Allentown

Best Time to Visit Allentown

The best time to visit Allentown is during the spring and fall seasons, when the weather is mild, and the scenery is beautiful. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and snowy.

Getting to Allentown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Allentown is easily accessible by car or public transportation. Visitors can choose from several transportation options to get to Allentown.

  • Newark Liberty International Airport: Located about 47 miles from Allentown, this airport is one of the busiest airports in the country and offers domestic and international flights.

  • Philadelphia International Airport: Located about 50 miles from Allentown, this airport offers domestic and international flights and is a popular option for visitors to Allentown.

Bus Stations Near Allentown

  • NJ Transit: This regional transit system operates several bus routes that connect Allentown to the nearby towns and cities.

  • Greyhound: This national bus service offers routes that connect Allentown to major cities across the country.

Other Options
