Introduction to Howell

Howell is a township located in Monmouth County, New Jersey, which is part of the Jersey Shore. It is a perfect destination for travelers who want to relax and enjoy a peaceful environment away from crowded cities. Howell offers a unique blend of outdoor adventure, scenic beauty, and cultural attractions that make it an ideal place to explore. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your visit to Howell, New Jersey, and make the most of your vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Howell

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Howell is a township rich in history and culture. Explore the following museums and historical places that reflect the township’s past:

  • Allaire State Park: This park was once an iron-producing village in the 19th century but is now a historic site that features a museum, a blacksmith shop, and a carpentry shop. Visitors can also enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking, camping, and fishing.
  • Davy’s House: This historical home in Howell was built in the 1800s and was the site of a local Civil War skirmish.
  • The Barnegat Bay Decoy and Baymen’s Museum: This museum has an enormous collection of decoys, antique boats, and other artifacts related to duck hunting in the Barnegat Bay.
  • Howell Works: Take a tour of this historic distillery and production facility at Striker’s Square.

Natural Attractions of Howell: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Howell is surrounded by natural beauty and offers a range of outdoor activities. Here are the best natural attractions to enjoy in Howell:

  • Echo Lake Park: This stunning park is the perfect spot for a picnic or a hike. It features a lake, extensive trails, and beautiful scenery.
  • Manasquan Reservoir: This beautiful reservoir has over 1,200 acres of water and is a popular spot for fishing, birdwatching, and hiking.
  • Turkey Swamp Park: This park has over 2,300 acres of land and offers activities such as biking, hiking, and fishing. It also has campgrounds for those who want to spend the night.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Howell

Howell has several hiking and biking trails for visitors to explore. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Patriots’ Path Trail: This is a 35-mile trail that connects various parks and historical sites in New Jersey. The section that runs through Howell is approximately 5.5 miles long.
  • Manasquan Reservoir Perimeter Trail: This is a 7-mile trail that follows the perimeter of the Manasquan Reservoir and offers stunning views and a relaxing hike.
  • Allaire State Park Trail: This is a 16-mile trail that winds through Allaire State Park and offers the opportunity to explore both the park’s natural beauty and history.

Best Things to Do in Howell: Activities and Tours

Howell has a range of activities and tours for visitors. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Wild West City: This attraction is a theme park that re-creates the Old West with cowboy shows, stagecoach rides, and more.
  • Belford Brewing Company: Take a tour of Howell’s local brewery and enjoy a cold beer.
  • The Jumping Brook Country Club: This country club offers golf, swimming, and tennis as well as a great place for a meal and a drink.

Food and Drink Scene of Howell

Must-Try Dishes

Howell has several great restaurants that serve delicious cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes that you need to try on your visit:

  • Pork Roll: This is a popular breakfast dish in New Jersey. It is a type of sausage made from pork shoulder that is popular in sandwiches and as a side dish.
  • Taylor Ham: This is another type of breakfast meat that is popular in New Jersey. It is similar to pork roll but has a slightly different flavor.
  • Clam Chowder: Howell is located near the coast and offers fresh seafood. Clam chowder is a popular dish that you should try while in Howell.

Local Beverages

Howell has several breweries and wineries where visitors can enjoy locally produced beverages. Here are the best places to enjoy these local drinks:

  • Belford Brewing Company: This is a great place to enjoy a locally brewed beer and learn about the brewing process.
  • Four JGs Orchards and Vineyards: This farm produces wines made from apples, pears, and other fruits grown on the farm.

Where to Stay in Howell: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Howell offers several lodging options for visitors. Here are some of the best places to stay in Howell:

  • Hilton Garden Inn: This hotel is located near the Garden State Parkway and offers comfortable rooms, a pool, and a fitness center.
  • The Inn at Colts Neck: This bed and breakfast is located in the heart of Howell and offers a great place to relax and unwind.
  • Crystal Inn: This affordable hotel is located near the Freehold Raceway Mall and offers comfortable rooms and free breakfast.

What to Buy in Howell: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Howell offers several great souvenirs and local products for visitors to bring home. Here are some of the best things to buy in Howell:

  • Handmade soap: Visit local shops that sell handmade soap made from natural ingredients and essential oils.
  • Furniture: Howell has several antique shops that sell unique and beautiful furniture and home decor items.
  • Local wine and cider: Purchase wines and ciders made from locally grown fruits as a souvenir or gift for friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Howell

Howell has a rich and unique culture. Here are some of the customs and traditions of Howell that visitors should know:

  • Respecting nature: Howell is a town surrounded by natural beauty, and locals take great pride in preserving the environment. Visitors should respect nature and avoid littering or damaging the natural resources.
  • Friendliness: Howell is a friendly town, and visitors will find locals to be welcoming and accommodating.

Best Time to Visit Howell

The best time to visit Howell is during the summer months, from June to August. The weather is warm, and visitors can enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking and swimming. Fall is also a great time to visit, as the foliage is beautiful, and the weather is mild.

Safety and Health

Safety in Howell

Howell is a safe town, and visitors can explore without fear. Visitors should exercise standard safety precautions like locking car doors and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Howell

Howell has several medical facilities that provide high-quality healthcare. Visitors can access hospitals and clinics for any medical problems.

Getting to Howell: Transportation Options and Logistics

Howell is easily accessible by car and public transportation. Here are the best ways to get to Howell:

Airports Near Howell

  • Newark Liberty International Airport: This airport is located approximately 50 miles from Howell and is the closest international airport.

Bus Stations Near Howell

  • New Jersey Transit: Visitors can take a bus from the Port Authority Bus Terminal in New York City to Howell.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Visitors can rent a car from any major car rental company and drive to Howell.

How to Get Around in Howell

Howell is a small town and can easily be explored on foot or by bike. Visitors can also drive or rent a car to get around town.

Where to Go Next from Howell: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Howell is surrounded by several other towns and attractions that are worth a visit. Here are some nearby destinations:

  • Asbury Park: This town is located near the coast and is famous for its boardwalk, music, and nightlife.
  • Six Flags Great Adventure: This amusement park is located 30 miles from Howell and offers thrilling rides and attractions.
  • Princeton: This town is home to Princeton University and is famous for its art museums and beautiful architecture.
