Introduction to Holmdel

Welcome to Holmdel, a charming township sitting in Monmouth County, New Jersey. Being a part of the Jersey Shore, Holmdel offers stunning beaches, natural landscapes, outdoor recreation, cultural treasures, and excellent dining experiences. This travel guide will help you plan a memorable trip to Holmdel by covering everything you need to know about the town.

List of Places to Visit in Holmdel

Below are the top attractions and must-visit places in Holmdel:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Holmdel Museum - Explore the town’s cultural and historical exhibits, including photographs, artifacts, and documents that showcase Holmdel’s past.
  • Monmouth Battlefield State Park - Learn about Revolutionary War history at the site of one of the most significant battles of the war.
  • Dorbrook Recreation Area - Visit the park that houses the Holmes-Hendrickson House, which is believed to have been built in the 17th century.

Natural Attractions of Holmdel: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Holmdel Park - This park provides breathtaking views, playgrounds, tennis courts, soccer fields, hiking and biking trails, ice-skating ponds, and picnic areas.
  • Bayonet Farm - Take a leisurely walk and observe its beautiful orchards, fields, and hillsides.
  • Thompson Park - A perfect place for families with kids that features a zoo, playgrounds, and hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Holmdel

Holmdel has many walking and biking routes that will take you through scenic county landscapes, passing lakes, state parks, estates, and vast nature preserves. Below are a few routes where you can enjoy Holmdel’s natural beauty by hiking and biking:

  • Holmdel Park Trail - Located in the Holmdel Park, it’s a hikers’ delight, circling around 10-acre pond of Big Brook and adorned with beautiful wildflowers, migratory birds, and river otters
  • Bayshore Waterfront Park Trail - Bike through 234 acres of glowing waterfront parks, saltwater marshes, and wetlands, including several fishing spots.
  • Henry Hudson Trail - Spanning around 24 miles of flat, easy-to-navigate terrain, it follows the route of the former Henry Hudson Railway.

Best Things to Do in Holmdel: Activities and Tours

Holmdel has endless options for visitors to enjoy, from water activities to scenic parks and thrilling experiences. Below are the top activities and tours to do in the area:

  • Beaches of the Jersey Shore - Go swimming, sunbathing, surfing, or just relax on the golden sands of the beaches.
  • Holmdel Performing Arts Center - A state-of-the-art amphitheater with seating for nearly 17,000 guests for live performances, concerts, and festivals.
  • The Holmdel Community Farmers Market - Spend a morning at the farmers market with an array of local produce, baked goods, and crafts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Holmdel

Here is a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Holmdel:

1 Explore the Holmdel Museum and Monmouth Battlefield State Park

Food and Drink Scene of Holmdel

No trip is complete without experiencing local food and drink. Holmdel offers a mix of restaurants, bars, pubs, and cafes with delicacies that’ll delight your taste buds. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jersey Tomatoes - Fresh ripe jersey tomatoes available from mid-June to October. Made into delicious salads, sandwiches, and dishes.
  • Boardwalk Fries - A classic beachside treat with a crispy exterior and fluffy interior, served with vinegar and salt. A must-try at any nearby boardwalk.
  • Newark-style hot dogs - A must-try street food with a unique style of preparation, featuring savory toppings like onions, mustard, and chili sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Hometown beer - New Jersey has a robust craft beer scene, and Holmdel’s local breweries are a must-visit for beer lovers.
  • Blueberry Wine - Award-winning blueberries grown in New Jersey make a great wine, served at many of the local wineries.
  • Apple cider - Enjoy fresh, sweet, and tangy cider made by the town’s local cider mills.

Where to Stay in Holmdel: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many options to choose from, depending on your budget and preferences. Some of the best accommodations in Holmdel include:

  • Courtyard Lincroft Red Bank - It’s an affordable hotel with standard amenities like free Wi-Fi, fitness center, and pool.
  • The Oyster Point Hotel - A beautiful luxury hotel with stunning waterfront views and amenities like an in-house restaurant, pool, and fitness center.
  • The Molly Pitcher Inn - A glamorous hotel equipped with spa, kayaking, and shopping and dining experiences.

What to Buy in Holmdel: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Holmdel has many local shops and markets that offer authentic, handcrafted, and unique products to remember your trip. Some of the best items to buy from here include:

  • Jersey Peach Preserves - Made with fresh, juicy, and sweet Jersey peaches.
  • Holmdel Honey - A unique experience to savor the taste of honey from locally grown wildflowers and clover.
  • Handmade Pottery - Handcrafted pottery by local artists that reflects the town’s culture, architecture, and landscape.

Local Culture and Customs of Holmdel

Holmdel is a friendly, welcoming, and safe town with rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its many art galleries, live music, theatre, and film festivals. Some of the local customs to keep in mind while visiting include:

  • Respecting the town’s history and architecture.
  • Dressing appropriately while visiting religious and cultural sites.
  • Taking photographs responsibly, respecting the privacy of others and avoiding sensitive areas.

Best Time to Visit Holmdel

The best time to visit Holmdel is from March to November when the weather is mild, and the town comes alive with outdoor activities, festivals, and events. The summer months offer perfect weather for beaches and water sports, while spring and fall season are best for hiking and biking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Holmdel

Here are some basic guidelines to follow while visiting Holmdel:

Do:

  • Respect the town’s culture and customs.
  • Make arrangements for transportation in advance.
  • Keep the town clean and litter-free.

Don’t:

  • Purchase illegal drugs from unknown sources.
  • Engage in public intoxication or disturbance.
  • Do not violate traffic laws, especially while biking on public roads.

Safety and Health

Holmdel is generally a safe and healthy place to visit. However, tourists must follow standard safety precautions as they would in any new place to ensure a comfortable and hassle-free trip.

Safety in Holmdel

  • Always keep your belongings safe and secure.
  • If traveling with children, ensure their safety by keeping a watchful eye on them.
  • Stay in well-lit areas while walking during the night.

Health in Holmdel

  • Stay hydrated and carry plenty of water while participating in outdoor activities.
  • Be aware of any allergies or health conditions before consuming local food or drink.
  • Follow regularly prescribed medications and sunblock if spending time outdoors.

Getting to Holmdel: Transportation Options and Logistics

Holmdel is accessible through various transportation options for tourists.

Airports Near Holmdel

  • Newark Liberty International Airport is the nearest major airport to Holmdel.
  • Philadelphia International Airport is another option, located approximately 85 miles southwest of Holmdel.

Bus Stations Near Holmdel

  • NJ Transit has bus service from New York City to the town of Holmdel. The bus station is located at the County Seat Plaza and offers various buses for hire.

Other Options

  • The nearest train station is in Red Bank, which is around three and a half miles from Holmdel.
  • Taxis and rental cars are available.

How to Get Around in Holmdel

Holmdel is a relatively small town, and tourists can explore most of it on foot or bike. However, different modes of transportation options are available, including public transportation, taxis, and rental cars.

Where to Go Next from Holmdel: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations that visitors can explore while in Holmdel:

  • Atlantic City - You can try your luck at the city’s famous casinos, stroll down the Atlantic City Boardwalk, or visit the Absecon lighthouse.
  • Asbury Park - Enjoy the beautiful beaches, boardwalk, shops, and restaurants, and catch a live show at the Stone Pony, a music venue that helped launch the careers of Springsteen and Bon Jovi.
  • Jersey City and Hoboken - Visit Jersey City’s waterfront, Liberty State Park, and Hoboken’s historical sites and take a walk along the popular “Cake Boss” bakery.

Conclusion

Holmdel has something for everyone, from natural scenery to cultural heritage, outdoor adventures to fine dining and accommodation options. We hope that this comprehensive travel guide helps you plan your trip and optimize your time exploring this stunning town tucked away in New Jersey’s countryside.
