Introduction to Colts Neck

Located in Monmouth County, in Jersey Shore, in New Jersey, Colts Neck is a small town that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, rich history, and small-town charm. Surrounded by rolling hills, lush forests, and serene lakes, Colts Neck is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts who seek to soak in the beauty of nature.

The town is also home to several historical landmarks and museums, allowing visitors to delve into the rich history of the region. In this comprehensive travel guide to Colts Neck, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in this charming town.

List of Places to Visit in Colts Neck

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Historic Longstreet Farm – A beautifully preserved farm that dates back to the 1890s, offering visitors a glimpse of rural life in early 20th-century New Jersey.
  • Hominy Hill Golf Course – A 27-hole golf course that has been lauded as one of the best public golf courses in New Jersey.
  • Holmdel Park – A sprawling park that offers visitors a chance to enjoy the beauty of nature while exploring the park’s hiking trails, fishing ponds, and picnic areas.
  • Colts Neck Reformed Church – A stunningly beautiful church that dates back to the early 19th century, featuring beautiful stained-glass windows and intricate wood carvings.

Natural Attractions of Colts Neck: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Big Brook Park – A serene park that is perfect for hiking, biking, and picnicking, with beautiful views of Big Brook Lake.
  • Dorbrook Recreation Area – A popular destination for hiking, fishing, kayaking, and more, with plenty of outdoor activities for visitors of all ages.
  • Thompson Park – A stunning park that is home to several hiking trails, picnic areas, and fishing spots, with beautiful views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Colts Neck

  • Big Brook Park to Dorbrook Recreation Area Trail - This 3.5-mile trail connects two of Colts Neck’s most beautiful parks, offering visitors a chance to explore the region’s natural beauty on foot or bicycle.
  • Holleman Preserve Trail - A 3-mile trail that winds through a stunningly beautiful nature preserve, with beautiful views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Thompson Park Trail - This 2.5-mile trail takes visitors on a leisurely hike through the park’s forests and fields, with plenty of beautiful views along the way.

Best Things to Do in Colts Neck: Activities and Tours

  • Horseback Riding at Dapple Hill – A family-owned and operated horseback riding stable that offers guided trail rides through some of Colts Neck’s most stunning landscapes.
  • Wagon and Sleigh Rides at Happy Trails Farm – A fun-filled activity that takes visitors on a scenic wagon or sleigh ride around the beautiful landscapes of Colts Neck.
  • Wine Tasting at Cream Ridge Winery – A charming winery that produces some of New Jersey’s best wines, with regular tastings and events for visitors to enjoy.

Food and Drink Scene of Colts Neck

Must-Try Dishes

  • Philly Cheesesteak – A classic sandwich that has been perfected by Jersey Shore’s local restaurants and food trucks.
  • Boardwalk Pizza – Thin-crust pizza that is the quintessential snack while walking on the boardwalk.
  • Italian Sub – A hearty sandwich that is loaded with meats, cheese, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Central Jersey Beer – Brewed from locally sourced ingredients, these beers are a favorite among locals.
  • Jersey Tomato Juice – Made from fresh Jersey tomatoes, this juice is a classic Jersey Shore drink.
  • Cranberry Wine – Made from locally grown cranberries, this wine is a delicious and unique treat.

Where to Stay in Colts Neck: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Country Inn – A cozy bed and breakfast that offers personalized service and a warm and inviting atmosphere.
  • The Courtyard Marriott – A modern hotel that features spacious rooms, a fitness center, and an indoor pool.

Local Culture and Customs of Colts Neck

  • Colts Neck is a charming small town with friendly locals who are proud of their heritage and always willing to share their knowledge and love of the area.
  • Visitors are encouraged to explore the town’s many cultural and historical landmarks, and to participate in the many local events that are held throughout the year.
  • Colts Neck has a strong agricultural tradition, and visitors are encouraged to sample the many local foods and products that are made using locally sourced ingredients.

Best Time to Visit Colts Neck

  • Colts Neck is a year-round destination, with plenty to see and do no matter what the season.
  • However, visitors who want to explore the area’s many outdoor attractions should plan their trip for spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the foliage is at its most beautiful.

Safety and Health

Safety in Colts Neck

  • Colts Neck is a safe and welcoming community, with a low crime rate and friendly locals.
  • Visitors should take normal precautions when exploring the area’s outdoor attractions, such as wearing appropriate clothing and footwear and carrying plenty of water and sunscreen.

Health in Colts Neck

  • Visitors should be aware of the potential for tick-borne illnesses when spending time outdoors in Colts Neck, and should take appropriate precautions, such as wearing bug spray and checking for ticks after spending time in wooded areas.
  • There are several excellent medical facilities in and around Colts Neck should visitors require medical attention during their stay.

Getting to Colts Neck: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Colts Neck

  • Newark Liberty International Airport – Located approximately 1 hour and 15 minutes from Colts Neck.
  • Philadelphia International Airport – Located approximately 1 hour and 30 minutes from Colts Neck.

Bus Stations Near Colts Neck

  • Asbury Park Bus Terminal – Located approximately 20 minutes from Colts Neck.
  • Red Bank Bus Terminal – Located approximately 25 minutes from Colts Neck.

Other Options

  • Visitors can also reach Colts Neck by car, with several major highways offering easy access to the town.
  • Visitors who prefer to travel by train can take the NJ Transit North Jersey Coast Line to the nearby Red Bank Station, which is located approximately 20 minutes from Colts Neck.

How to Get Around in Colts Neck

  • The best way to get around Colts Neck is by car, as the town is spread out and there are few public transportation options available.
