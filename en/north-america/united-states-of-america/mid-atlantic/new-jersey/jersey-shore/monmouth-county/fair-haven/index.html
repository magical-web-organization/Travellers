Introduction to Fair Haven

Fair Haven is a charming borough located in Monmouth County, in Jersey Shore, in New Jersey, in Mid-Atlantic, in the United States of America, in North America. With a population of just over 6,000 people, this small town offers visitors a relaxing and peaceful respite from the bustling city life of nearby New York and Philadelphia. Here, you will find beautiful parks, scenic waterfronts, and a rich history that dates back to the Revolutionary War era. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will help you plan your perfect trip to Fair Haven.

List of Places to Visit in Fair Haven

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Parker Homestead-1665: This historical site is one of the oldest homes in America and was built by one of the first settlers of the area, John Parker. Visitors can take a guided tour of the house and learn more about the history of Fair Haven.

  • The Navesink Twin Lights: This iconic lighthouse sits atop the Navesink Highlands and offers breathtaking views of the Atlantic Ocean. Visitors can climb to the top of the lighthouse for even more stunning views.

  • The Monmouth County Historical Association: This museum houses a collection of artifacts, documents, and photos that tell the story of Monmouth County’s past. Exhibits range from the Revolutionary War to modern times.

Natural Attractions of Fair Haven: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Fair Haven Fields Natural Area: This 111-acre park is home to hiking trails, fishing ponds, picnic areas, and more. It’s the perfect place to spend a day in the great outdoors.

  • McCarter Pond: This scenic pond is a popular spot for fishing, kayaking, and paddleboarding. It’s also home to a resident population of swans.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fair Haven

  • Fair Haven Fields Natural Area: This park offers a variety of hiking trails that range in difficulty from easy to moderate. The trails wind through the woods and offer scenic views of the surrounding area.

  • Henry Hudson Trail: This 24-mile trail begins in Atlantic Highlands, New Jersey, and runs through Fair Haven. It’s a popular spot for bikers, walkers, and joggers.

Best Things to Do in Fair Haven: Activities and Tours

  • Kayak Launch: Launch your kayak into the Navesink River and explore the surrounding waterfront. Rentals are available from nearby vendors.

  • Riverfront Boardwalk: Stroll along the boardwalk and enjoy stunning views of the Navesink River. Stop at one of the many benches along the way to take in the scenery.

Food and Drink Scene of Fair Haven

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pizza from Denino’s Pizzeria: This family-owned pizzeria has been serving delicious pies for over 80 years.

  • Lobster Rolls from the Navesink Fishery: This seafood restaurant is known for its fresh seafood and, in particular, its mouthwatering lobster rolls.

Local Beverages

  • Beer from Red Bank Brewery: This local brewery offers a variety of beers, including IPAs, stouts, and lagers.

  • Wine from Sickles Market: This local market offers a variety of wines from New Jersey and around the world.

Where to Stay in Fair Haven: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Molly Pitcher Inn: This waterfront hotel offers stunning views of the Navesink River and is located just a short drive from the center of Fair Haven.

  • TownePlace Suites by Marriott: This comfortable hotel is great for families and offers spacious suites with kitchens.

What to Buy in Fair Haven: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Jersey Shore-themed souvenirs from the Fair Haven General Store: This charming store offers a variety of souvenirs and local products that make great gifts.

  • Fresh produce from the Fair Haven Farmers Market: This weekly market features fresh produce from local farmers.

Local Culture and Customs of Fair Haven

Fair Haven is a small, close-knit community that values tradition and family. Visitors should respect the local customs and be mindful of noise levels, especially in residential areas. The people of Fair Haven are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are sure to feel at home.

Best Time to Visit Fair Haven

The best time to visit Fair Haven is in the summer months, from June to September. The weather is warm and pleasant, and there are plenty of outdoor activities and events to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fair Haven

Do:

  • Take a guided tour of the Parker Homestead-1665.
  • Visit the Monmouth County Historical Association.
  • Rent a kayak and explore the Navesink River.
  • Enjoy a lobster roll from the Navesink Fishery.

Don’t:

  • Be loud or disruptive in residential areas.
  • Park in a no-parking zone.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fair Haven

Fair Haven is a safe and friendly community, and visitors are unlikely to encounter any major safety concerns. As with any place, visitors should take normal safety precautions, such as locking their car and securing their valuables.

Health in Fair Haven

There are several medical facilities located in and around Fair Haven, including Riverview Medical Center and Meridian Health Village. Visitors should have no trouble finding quality medical care if needed.

Getting to Fair Haven: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Fair Haven

  • Newark Liberty International Airport (EWR)
  • LaGuardia Airport (LGA)
  • John F. Kennedy International Airport (JFK)

Bus Stations Near Fair Haven

  • Red Bank Bus Station
  • Middletown Train Station

Other Options

  • Uber and Lyft are both available in the area.
  • Rental cars are available from several major car rental companies.

How to Get Around in Fair Haven

Fair Haven is a small town, and many of the attractions are within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also rent a bike or take a car service to get around.

Where to Go Next from Fair Haven: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Sandy Hook: This beautiful beach is located just a short drive from Fair Haven and offers stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean.

  • Asbury Park: This trendy beach town is located just a few miles south of Fair Haven and offers a vibrant nightlife and entertainment scene.

Conclusion

Fair Haven is a hidden gem in the heart of New Jersey. With its small-town charm, natural beauty, and rich history, Fair Haven is a must-visit for anyone looking to escape the hustle and bustle of metropolitan life.
