Introduction to Long Branch

Nestled in Monmouth County, Long Branch is a charming seaside city located in the heart of Jersey Shore, New Jersey. The city boasts a rich history, beautiful beaches, scenic views, and a vibrant cultural scene. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing escape from the hustle and bustle of city life or an action-packed adventure, Long Branch has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Long Branch

Here are the must-visit attractions of Long Branch:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Long Branch Historical Museum Association: This museum showcases the history of Long Branch city with a collection of historic photographs, artifacts, and memorabilia.
  • Seven Presidents Oceanfront Park: Named after the United States Presidents who visited Long Branch, this park has beautiful trails and paths for jogging, hiking and cycling.
  • Monmouth Park Racetrack: This famous racetrack is a popular destination for horse racing enthusiasts.

Natural Attractions of Long Branch: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pier Village: This outdoor shopping and dining complex is lined with palm trees and fountains and overlooks the ocean. There are also beach clubs, rooftop lounges and spas in the complex.
  • Long Branch Beaches: The city boasts several beaches with miles of white sand and crystal clear waters including Pier Village Beach, Seven Presidents Beach, and North Beach.
  • Ocean Boulevard: This scenic roadway offers breathtaking views of the Atlantic Ocean and is perfect for a leisurely drive.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Long Branch

There are several hiking and biking trails in Long Branch, perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to take:

  • Loantaka Brook Reservation Trail: This 8-mile trail is perfect for running, hiking, and cycling. It has scenic views, a few hills, and a loop around the park.
  • Henry Hudson Trail: This 22-mile trail runs through several towns in New Jersey and offers beautiful scenic views, natural habitats, and historic sites.
  • Sandy Hook Multi-Use Pathway: This 8-mile trail runs through a scenic barrier island in New Jersey and is ideal for walking, jogging, and cycling.

Best Things to Do in Long Branch: Activities and Tours

From beach activities to art galleries, Long Branch has plenty of options to keep visitors entertained. Here are some of the best things to do in Long Branch:

  • Beach Activities: Visitors can enjoy swimming, sunbathing, and surfing at the various beaches in Long Branch.
  • Monmouth County Arts Council: This council hosts art exhibits, concerts, and theater productions at various locations throughout the city.
  • Luxury Boat Tours: Visitors can take a luxury boat tour of the area, which offers amazing views of the city, the beaches, and the ocean.

Week-Long Itinerary for Long Branch

Day 1: Visit Long Branch Historical Museum Association and Seven Presidents Oceanfront Park. Day 2: Explore the Long Branch beaches and Pier Village. Day 3: Take a scenic drive on Ocean Boulevard and visit Taylor Avenue Waterfront. Day 4: Hiking at Henry Hudson Trail or Sandy Hook Multi-Use Pathway. Day 5: Monmouth Park Racetrack and sample the local cuisine. Day 6: Visit Monmouth County Arts Council for an evening event. Day 7: Take a luxury boat tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Long Branch

Long Branch has a diverse food scene that reflects the city’s cultural diversity. Visitors can enjoy cuisine from around the globe. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pork Roll: A regional specialty, it is a type of breakfast meat that is generally sliced and grilled.
  • New York-style Pizza: Thin-crust pizza, New York-style is a classic choice in Long Branch.
  • Salt Water Taffy: A sugary sweet candy that is a shore staple.

Local Beverages

  • Carton Brewing: Known for its off-centered and creative brews. Visitors can stop by the brewery’s tasting room and sample some of their unique beers.

Where to Stay in Long Branch: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Long Branch has a variety of accommodations options, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Ocean Place Resort and Spa: This beachfront resort has several restaurants, a full-service spa, and comfortable rooms with ocean views.
  • Bungalow Hotel: This hip, boutique hotel located in the heart of Pier Village offers stylish rooms and a chic atmosphere.
  • Blue Bay Inn: This budget-friendly hotel has clean rooms, friendly staff, and a central location.

What to Buy in Long Branch: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can take home a piece of Long Branch with them by purchasing some local souvenirs or products. Here are some popular choices:

  • Beach-Themed Home Decor: Visitors can find a variety of beach-themed home decor items at local shops and boutiques.
  • Jersey Shore T-Shirts: T-shirts that say “Jersey Shore” are a popular souvenir choice.
  • Local Art: Visitors can purchase unique art pieces created by local artists at several art galleries around the city.

Local Culture and Customs of Long Branch

Long Branch has several customs and traditions that visitors should be aware of. Here are some tips:

  • Beach Etiquette: Visitors should be respectful of others when using the beach and follow all posted rules and regulations.
  • Tipping: Tipping is customary in Long Branch and should be done at restaurants, bars, and for other services.
  • Casual Dress: Long Branch is a casual town, and visitors should dress accordingly.

Best Time to Visit Long Branch

The best time to visit Long Branch is in the summer, between June and August when the weather is perfect for outdoor activities on the beaches. The city also hosts several events and festivals during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Long Branch

Do:

  • Do Explore Beaches: Long Branch has some of the best beaches in New Jersey. Visitors should spend some time exploring them.
  • Do Try the Local Cuisine: Long Branch has an excellent food scene that visitors should not miss.
  • Do Shop Local: Visitors should support local businesses when shopping for souvenirs and other items.

Don’t:

  • Don’t Litter: Visitors should not litter anywhere in the city.
  • Don’t Be Disrespectful: Visitors should be respectful of the local customs and culture.
  • Don’t Underestimate Sun Exposure: Visitors should always wear sunscreen when spending time outdoors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Long Branch

Long Branch is a generally safe city and the crime rate is considerably low. However, visitors should still take precautions like they would in any other city. Here are some tips:

  • Be Aware of Your Surroundings: Visitors should be aware of their surroundings and alert at all times.
  • Stay in Well-Lit Areas: Visitors should stick to well-lit areas and avoid walking alone at night.
  • Keep Valuables Out of Sight: Visitors should keep their valuable belongings out of sight in public places.

Health in Long Branch

Long Branch has several medical facilities, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. Visitors should remember to carry their prescriptions and necessary medications with them.

Getting to Long Branch: Transportation Options and Logistics

Long Branch is easily accessible by road, air, and bus. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Long Branch

  • Newark Liberty International Airport: Located 53 miles from Long Branch.
  • John F. Kennedy International Airport: Located 61 miles from Long Branch.

Bus Stations Near Long Branch

  • Newark Penn Station: Located 42 miles from Long Branch.
  • New York Port Authority Bus Terminal: Located 53 miles from Long Branch.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals: Visitors can rent a car at any of the nearby airports.
  • Taxis and Uber: Taxis and Uber are readily available in Long Branch.

How to Get Around in Long Branch

Long Branch is a small city that is easy to navigate on foot, but visitors can also take advantage of the following transportation options:

  • Trolley: The Long Branch Trolley runs from Pier Village to the train station.
  • Buses: The New Jersey Transit bus system provides service to Long Branch and surrounding areas.
  • Taxis and Ridesharing: Taxis and ridesharing services like Uber and Lyft are available in Long Branch.

Where to Go Next from Long Branch: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors who want to explore New Jersey beyond Long Branch can visit the following nearby destinations:

  • Asbury Park: This seaside city is known for its vibrant music scene and has several restaurants, bars, and shops.
  • Red Bank: This quaint town on the banks of the Navesink River has a thriving arts and culture scene and great dining options.
  • Atlantic City: This world-famous beach destination has several casinos, bars, restaurants, and shops.

Conclusion

Long Branch is a charming seaside city with a lot to offer visitors. Whether you want to spend your time relaxing on the beach, exploring the local culture, or taking in the scenic views, Long Branch has something for you. With this comprehensive guide, you’ll have everything you need to plan your perfect Long Beach Getaway.
