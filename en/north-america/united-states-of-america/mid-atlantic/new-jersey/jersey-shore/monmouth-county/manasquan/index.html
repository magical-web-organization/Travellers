Introduction to Manasquan

Nestled in the charming Monmouth County in the Jersey Shore region of New Jersey, Manasquan is a perfect getaway for those seeking sun-soaked adventures and scenic beauty. With its pristine beaches, vibrant nightlife, and a rich cultural history, this quiet town has everything for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Manasquan

Manasquan has an abundance of cultural and natural attractions for visitors to explore. Here are some of the must-visit attractions in Manasquan:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sea Girt Lighthouse: This historic lighthouse, which opened in 1896, still functions to guide boats into Manasquan Inlet. Visitors can take a guided tour to learn about its history and enjoy the panoramic views from atop.
  • National Guard Militia Museum of New Jersey: History buffs will love to explore this museum, which tells the story of New Jersey’s militia and national guard from colonial times to the present day.

Natural Attractions of Manasquan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Manasquan Reservoir: This beautiful reservoir offers a range of outdoor activities, such as hiking, biking, fishing, and boating. The reservoir is also a popular spot for bird watching.
  • Fisherman’s Cove Conservation Area: This park offers spectacular views of the Manasquan Inlet and the Atlantic Ocean. Visitors can hike on the trails, go fishing, or simply relax on the beach.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Manasquan

Manasquan offers several scenic trails for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Allaire State Park: This park has over 20 miles of trails, ranging from easy to challenging, with lovely views of wetlands, forests, and streams.
  • Spring Lake Boardwalk: This historic boardwalk is perfect for a leisurely walk or bike ride, with stunning views of the ocean and the Spring Lake beach.

Best Things to Do in Manasquan: Activities and Tours

  • Surfing Lessons: Manasquan’s beaches are considered some of the best on the East Coast for surfing. Take a lesson with a local surf school and ride the waves!
  • Manasquan Inlet Fishing Charters: Join one of the fishing charters to explore the rich waters of the Atlantic and catch some of New Jersey’s finest fish.
  • Brewery Tour: Visit the local breweries in Manasquan and learn about their craft beer production. Take a tour and enjoy a tasting of local brews.

Week-Long Itinerary for Manasquan

Day 1: Visit Sea Girt Lighthouse and National Guard Militia Museum of New Jersey Day 2: Spend a day at Manasquan Reservoir for hiking, biking, and fishing. Day 3: Join a surfing lesson at one of the local surf schools. Day 4: Go on a fishing charter tour at Manasquan Inlet. Day 5: Take a leisurely walk or bike ride on the Spring Lake Boardwalk. Day 6: Visit the local breweries and enjoy a tasting of local brews. Day 7: Relax on Fisherman’s Cove Conservation Area beach and watch the sunset.

Food and Drink Scene of Manasquan

Manasquan has a diverse food scene with plenty of options for all types of travelers. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: A classic seafood dish, featuring succulent chunks of lobster on a buttered roll.
  • Pork Roll, Egg, and Cheese Sandwich: A breakfast classic in New Jersey featuring pork roll (a regional delicacy made from pork shoulder), egg, and cheese on a roll.

Local Beverages

  • Beach Haus Brewery Beer: Try the local craft beer by Beach Haus Brewery, which offers a range of brews with unique flavors that capture the essence of the shore.
  • Old Causeway Oyster Stout: This innovative beer is brewed with oysters, giving it a distinctive briny taste derived from the sea.

Where to Stay in Manasquan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Manasquan has a range of options for accommodation, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses and hostels. Some of the top-rated options include:

  • The Asbury Hotel: This chic and trendy hotel in Asbury Park is just a short drive from Manasquan and offers stylish rooms, a rooftop bar, and live music concerts.
  • The Hoffman House Bed & Breakfast: Located in the heart of Manasquan, this charming bed and breakfast offers beautiful rooms, outdoor seating areas, and a relaxing atmosphere.
  • Blue Star Motel: A budget-friendly option, this motel offers simple yet clean rooms and is located right on Route 35, just a short drive from the beach.

What to Buy in Manasquan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Beach Souvenirs: Purchase souvenirs such as t-shirts, beach towels, and hats to remind you of your days on the beach.
  • Salt Water Taffy: A classic candy of the Jersey Shore, salt water taffy comes in a variety of flavors and makes for a perfect gift to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Manasquan

Manasquan is a laid-back town that cherishes its roots and traditions. Visitors are expected to respect the local customs, such as keeping the beaches clean and following beach regulations.

Best Time to Visit Manasquan

The best time to visit Manasquan is from May to September when the weather is sunny and warm, and the town comes alive with beachgoers and tourists. The town’s festivals and events, such as the Manasquan Seafood Festival and the Manasquan Fireman Fair, are also held during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Manasquan

Do:

  • Respect the beach regulations and keep the beaches clean.
  • Try the local cuisine and craft beer.
  • Join a surfing lesson or fishing charter tour.

Don’t:

  • Leave your garbage on the beach.
  • Ignore beach regulations such as no alcohol or smoking on the beach.
  • Disrespect the local culture and customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Manasquan

Manasquan is generally a safe town, but visitors should take basic safety precautions such as locking their cars and securing their belongings. The beaches can have strong currents, so it is essential to follow beach regulations and stay within the designated swimming areas.

Health in Manasquan

Visitors should be careful when spending time in the sun and apply sunscreen regularly. If you need medical assistance, there are several hospitals and clinics in the town.

Getting to Manasquan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Manasquan

The closest airport to Manasquan is Newark Liberty International Airport, which is located approximately 55 miles away. Another option is Philadelphia International Airport, which is located approximately 85 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Manasquan

New Jersey Transit operates several bus routes that connect Manasquan to neighboring towns, including the #830, #831, and #832 buses.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Manasquan using the Garden State Parkway or the New Jersey Turnpike.

How to Get Around in Manasquan

The best way to get around Manasquan is by car or bike. Many of the attractions and restaurants are within a short drive or bike ride from each other. There are also several car rental companies in the town.

Where to Go Next from Manasquan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Asbury Park: This vibrant town is known for its lively music scene, eclectic restaurants, and beautiful beaches. It is just a short drive from Manasquan.
  • Point Pleasant Beach: This charming beach town is located just a few miles north of Manasquan and offers beautiful beaches, a bustling boardwalk, and a range of restaurants and activities.

Conclusion

Manasquan is a small town with big attractions. Whether you’re looking to soak up the sun, explore the town’s history and culture, or simply relax on the beach, Manasquan has everything for all types of travelers. So plan your visit, pack your bags, and get ready to experience the best of the Jersey Shore!
