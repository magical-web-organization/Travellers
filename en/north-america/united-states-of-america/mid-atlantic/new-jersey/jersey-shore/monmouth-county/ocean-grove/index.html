Introduction to Ocean Grove

Nestled in Monmouth County, New Jersey, Ocean Grove is a charming seaside town on the Jersey Shore. Established in 1869 as a religious resort community, Ocean Grove boasts a rich history and a unique character that sets it apart from other coastal destinations. Visitors to Ocean Grove can enjoy a peaceful atmosphere and plenty of natural beauty along with a range of cultural and recreational activities. Whether you’re interested in museums, hiking, or sampling the local cuisine, Ocean Grove has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Ocean Grove

If you’re planning a trip to Ocean Grove, here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ocean Grove Historical Museum: Learn about the town’s history and architecture from the 19th century to present day.

  • Great Auditorium: Attend a concert or lecture at this historic venue, which dates back to 1894 and is famous for its unique acoustics.

  • The Tent City: Explore the charming collection of Victorian-era tents arranged in a quaint community, a unique living experience for all tourists

Natural Attractions of Ocean Grove: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ocean Pathway: Take a stroll down the charming wooden boardwalk that runs parallel to Ocean Avenue.

  • Fletcher Lake: The lake located in North End is a great place to enjoy a peaceful sunset and observe different wildlife.

  • Beach and Oceanfront: Sunbathe or surf at one of the many popular beaches in Ocean Grove.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ocean Grove

  • Ocean Grove to Asbury Park Boardwalk: A one-mile walk that spans two boardwalks and provides scenic views of both towns.

  • Circuit Trail: A 3.2-mile moderate loop trail that offers scenic views of the park and the surrounding scenery.

  • Ocean Grove to Great Lawn and Beach: A scenic and flat bike ride through the town which ends at the beautiful Great Lawn and Beach

Best Things to Do in Ocean Grove: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing or Kayaking: Try your hand at fishing or kayaking in the beautiful Atlantic Ocean and learn new skills while enjoying the charming town scenery.

  • Ghost Tours: Take a tour of the town’s haunted past and experience the spooky side of Ocean Grove

  • Yoga on the Beach: Perfect your yoga moves and breathe fresh sea air while enjoying spectacular sunrise and morning breeze at the beach

Week-Long Itinerary for Ocean Grove

Day 1: Explore the town and architecture of Ocean Grove

Day 2: Rent a bike and ride the scenic route along the beach from Ocean Grove to Asbury Park

Day 3: Take a Ghost Tour

Day 4: Relax at the beach and take part in beach activities

Day 5: Learn to surf with an experienced instructor

Day 6: Visit the Ocean Grove Historical Museum

Day 7: Watch the sunset at Fletcher Lake

Food and Drink Scene of Ocean Grove

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood: Sample fresh seafood dishes made with locally sourced ingredients.

  • Tacos: Enjoy made-to-order tacos with unique flavors and fresh ingredients from many food trucks

  • Ice Cream: Try the homemade ice cream and walk around the town with a cone in hand

Local Beverages

  • House-made crafty beers around the town engineered for every taste from hoppy to fruity

  • Sparkling Wine: Enjoy champagne in the beachy, charismatic Ocean Grove

  • Coffee and Tea: Sip a cup of flavorful coffee or tea made by many small cafes around the town

Where to Stay in Ocean Grove: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options available in Ocean Grove, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some options to consider:

  • Ocean Plaza Hotel: A Victorian-style hotel with charming rooms and stunning ocean views.

  • The Sunburst Motel: A budget-friendly option with comfortable rooms and a convenient location.

  • The Twisted Gull Guest House: A cozy guesthouse within walking distance to the beach with long history in the town

Local Culture and Customs of Ocean Grove

The local culture in Ocean Grove reflects the town’s unique history and religious background. Visitors should be respectful of local customs, such as quiet hours from 10 pm to 8 am, as well as dress codes in churches and other religious establishments. Sunday morning worship is a big happening throughout the town. The local lifestyle is easygoing and the diverse local community is welcoming to tourists from all walks of life.

Best Time to Visit Ocean Grove

The best time to visit Ocean Grove is during the summer months from June to August when the weather is warm and the beach is accessible. However, visitors should expect crowds and higher prices during this time. Late spring and early fall are quieter but still pleasant times to visit with many activities and events still ongoing. Winter festival in early February is an interesting time to visit the town where there are ice sculptures and seasonal events all around the streets

Do’s and Don’ts of Ocean Grove

Do:

  • Respect the dress codes in churches and other religious establishments

  • Follow the town’s noise ordinances

  • Take care of the town’s natural environment and oceanfront

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash on the beaches

  • Ignore the rules of the local establishments

Safety and Health

Safety in Ocean Grove

Ocean Grove is generally a safe town. However, visitors should take the same precautions that they would in any other tourist area, such as being aware of their surroundings and keeping their belongings close.

Health in Ocean Grove

There are several medical facilities available in the town for general health concerns. Visitors should also be aware of sun safety during summer months and take proper precautions against the heat and sunburn.

Getting to Ocean Grove: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ocean Grove

  • Newark Liberty International Airport: The closest major airport to Ocean Grove, located 48 miles north.

  • Philadelphia International Airport: Located 82 miles southwest of Ocean Grove, these airports offer various transportation options.

Bus Stations Near Ocean Grove

  • Route 33 and 71/66 intersection: The closest major bus stop to Ocean Grove, located 1 mile north.

  • Asbury Park Transportation Center: The closest intercity bus station for connections across the area.

Other Options

  • Car Rentals: Visitors can rent a car from several rental companies located near the airports.

  • Train: Take train that runs on NJ Transit’s North Jersey Coast Line and make a stop in Asbury Park

How to Get Around in Ocean Grove

The easiest way to get around Ocean Grove is by foot or bike. Many of the town’s attractions are within walking distance or accessible via boardwalk. Visitors can also rent bikes in the town center. For those who prefer to travel by car, there are several parking options throughout the town. There are also several shuttle companies that introduce a range of vehicles and options to choose from to suit your transport preference around the town.

Where to Go Next from Ocean Grove: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursions to consider after visiting Ocean Grove, including:

  • Asbury Park: Visit the popular boardwalk and beach town, famous for its music scene and colorful murals.

  • Sandy Hook: Enjoy the unique history and outdoor activities in the nearby national park, which features a lighthouse and historic military fort.

  • Cape May: Visit the historic Victorian town and enjoy the beach, wineries, and charming shops.

Conclusion

Ocean Grove is a hidden gem on the Jersey Shore, offering a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in exploring museums, hiking trails, or simply relaxing on the beach, Ocean Grove has something to offer. Follow our guide to discover the best places to visit, food to eat, and activities to enjoy, to ensure a memorable getaway that you will cherish forever.
