Introduction to Englishtown

Englishtown is a small town located in Monmouth County in the state of New Jersey, United States of America. Known for its rich cultural history, natural beauty, and laid-back charm, Englishtown is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking a unique and authentic experience. Its central location makes it easily accessible by various modes of transportation including air, bus, and car. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll cover all the top attractions, best things to do, where to stay, local cuisine, safety tips, and more to help you plan your perfect visit to Englishtown.

List of Places to Visit in Englishtown

From culturally significant museums and historical sites to natural outdoor adventures, Englishtown has something to offer for everyone. Here is a list of must-visit places in Englishtown:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Englishtown Auction Sales - One of the largest flea markets in the tri-state area, Englishtown Auction Sales has been in operation for over 90 years. Visitors can browse through thousands of vendors selling antiques, collectibles, clothing, jewelry, and more.

  • Old Scots Burying Ground - A historic cemetery founded in 1685, the Old Scots Burying Ground is a reminder of Englishtown’s Scottish heritage. The tombstones include many famous figures from New Jersey’s past.

  • Monmouth County Historical Association Museum - This museum features exhibits on the history of Monmouth County, including artifacts related to the Revolutionary War, colonial life, and Native American culture. Guided tours are available.

Natural Attractions of Englishtown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Six Flags Great Adventure - The world’s largest theme park, Six Flags Great Adventure, is just a short drive away in Jackson, New Jersey. It features over 100 rides and attractions, including the Kingda Ka coaster, the world’s tallest and fastest roller coaster.

  • Turkey Swamp Park - A 2,200-acre park with forests, nature trails, fishing, kayaking, and camping facilities. Visitors can also rent boats, enjoy a picnic, and play sports in the park’s open fields.

  • The Pine Barrens - Located just a short drive away, the Pine Barrens is a vast natural expanse of hills, swamps, and forests. It is a paradise for hikers, campers, and anyone looking to explore the natural beauty of New Jersey.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Englishtown

Englishtown and the surrounding areas boast of several scenic hiking and biking trails. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes:

  • Manasquan Reservoir - This 1,200-acre park has a 5.2-mile hiking and biking trail that encircles the reservoir. Visitors can enjoy the scenic views of the water and wildlife along the way.

  • Monmouth Battlefield State Park - This historic park which served as a battleground for the Revolutionary War has several hiking trails ranging from 1 to 4 miles. The trails feature interpretive signs and stunning views of the countryside.

  • Lake Topanemus Park - Located in nearby Freehold, New Jersey, this park has a 2.4-mile hiking and biking trail around the lake. Visitors can enjoy fishing, canoeing, and kayaking in the lake.

Best Things to Do in Englishtown: Activities and Tours

Besides the museums, theme park, and hiking trails, Englishtown also offers several unique and exciting things to do. Here are some of the best:

  • Helicopter Tours - Experience the beauty of New Jersey’s shore from above with a helicopter tour. Several companies offer tours in and around Englishtown with routes that cover everything from the coastline to the mountains.

  • Englishtown Raceway Park - If you’re a thrill-seeker, don’t miss Englishtown Raceway Park. This renowned drag racing track hosts a variety of events and races throughout the year, including the iconic NHRA Summernationals.

  • Indoor Rock Climbing - Located in nearby Freehold, New Jersey, Inner Wall Rock Climbing Gym offers indoor rock-climbing for all skill levels. It’s a great activity for a rainy day or to escape the summer heat.

Week-Long Itinerary for Englishtown

If you have a week to spare, here’s a suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your trip to Englishtown:

Day Plan
Day 1 Visit the Monmouth County Historical Association Museum and Old Scots Burying Ground.
Day 2 Spend the day at Six Flags Great Adventure.
Day 3 Take a helicopter tour of the area.
Day 4 Go hiking in Monmouth Battlefield State Park and end the day with a visit to Englishtown Auction Sales.
Day 5 Enjoy a day of outdoor activities at Turkey Swamp Park.
Day 6 Visit Lake Topanemus Park for fishing and kayaking and end the day with indoor rock climbing.
Day 7 Explore the Pine Barrens and end the trip with a visit to Englishtown Raceway Park for an evening race.

Food and Drink Scene of Englishtown

Englishtown has a diverse culinary scene that features everything from classic American cuisine to international flavors. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages you can’t miss:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pork Roll Sandwich - A classic New Jersey breakfast sandwich made with pork roll, egg, and cheese on a roll.

  • Porketta Sandwich - A popular Italian sandwich made with roasted pork, garlic, and Italian spices.

  • Taylor Ham Egg and Cheese - Another classic New Jersey sandwich made with Taylor ham, egg, and cheese on a roll.

Local Beverages

  • Jersey Devil Vodka - A handcrafted vodka made by the Jersey Devil Distillery in nearby Fairfield, New Jersey.

  • Cape May Brewing Company Beer - This popular brewery offers a variety of beers that are brewed onsite and available for tasting.

  • Tomasello Winery Wines - A family-owned winery based in Hammonton, New Jersey, Tomasello Winery produces award-winning wines using locally grown grapes.

Local Culture and Customs of Englishtown

Englishtown is a diverse and inclusive community that respects and celebrates different cultures and traditions. Visitors are expected to adhere to common courtesy and respect local customs when visiting public places or interacting with locals. Englishtown also has a thriving arts and music scene, with concerts, festivals, and events throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Englishtown

The best time to visit Englishtown is during the Spring and Fall when the weather is pleasant, and the foliage is at its peak. The Summer months can be crowded with tourists, and temperatures can get hot and humid, while the Winter months can get cold and snowy. Englishtown has several indoor and outdoor attractions that can be enjoyed year-round, so plan your visit accordingly.

Safety and Health

Safety in Englishtown

Englishtown is generally a safe and friendly community. However, visitors should exercise caution when leaving valuables in cars or when walking alone at night. Traffic can also be congested, so follow traffic laws and be cautious when driving.

Health in Englishtown

Englishtown has several medical and emergency services located throughout the town and nearby areas. Visitors are encouraged to practice basic hygiene and follow safety guidelines when participating in outdoor activities. In case of emergency, call 911.
