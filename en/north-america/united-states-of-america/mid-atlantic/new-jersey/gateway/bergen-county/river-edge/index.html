Introduction to River Edge

River Edge is a borough located in Bergen County, New Jersey, just a few miles away from the bustling New York City. The town has a small population of around 12,000, making it an excellent choice for people looking for a peaceful retreat from the hectic city life. With its beautiful natural landscapes and fascinating cultural and historical attractions, River Edge has a lot to offer.

List of Places to Visit in River Edge

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Old Stone House

A visit to Old Stone House will transport you back in time. Built-in 1765, this stone house is now a museum that showcases the history of Bergen County. The house has a display of period rooms, antique furniture, and artwork that will give you a glimpse of life in the past.

Bergen County Zoo

Bergen County Zoo, situated on Van Saun County Park, is the perfect place to go for a family-friendly day out. The park covers an area of 146 acres and features over 80 species of animals from various countries worldwide. You can also enjoy a picnic, visit the train ride and the carousel, and explore the zoo’s many educational programs.

Natural Attractions of River Edge: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Van Saun County Park

Van Saun County Park is a vast open space with a lot of fun activities for everyone. It features a beautiful lake where you can go boating or fishing, numerous walking and bike trails, a playground, and BBQ areas where you can have a picnic with your loved ones.

Overpeck County Park

Overpeck County Park is a vast green space covering over 800 acres. The park has several beautiful scenic views of the surrounding areas, perfect for taking photos. You can also go fishing, boating, or kayaking on the Overpeck Lake, which is open to both residents and visitors.

Hiking/Biking Routes of River Edge

Kinderkamack Trail

Kinderkamack Trail is an eleven-mile trail that starts in River Edge and passes through different towns, including Emerson, Harrington Park, and Haworth. The trail provides a scenic and peaceful environment for hiking and biking, with many beautiful views to enjoy.

Best Things to Do in River Edge: Activities and Tours

Wine Tasting at Ventimiglia Vineyard

Ventimiglia Vineyard is located in Warren County, New Jersey, but it is worth the one-hour drive from River Edge. The vineyard features an incredible view of the surrounding hills where you can go on a guided tour, taste different wines, and enjoy a picnic.

Garden State Plaza

Garden State Plaza is one of New Jersey’s largest malls, located just a few miles away from River Edge. The mall features over 320 stores, a food court, and a cinema where you can enjoy the latest blockbuster movies.

Week-Long Itinerary for River Edge

If you’re planning a week-long visit to River Edge, here’s an itinerary to help you plan your trip.

Day 1: Visit Old Stone House

Learn about the history of Bergen County and explore the house’s period rooms and antique furniture.

Day 2: Explore Van Saun County Park

Enjoy different park activities such as boating, fishing, bike riding, and picnicking.

Day 3: Go on a Wine Tasting Tour at Ventimiglia Vineyard

Experience a guided tour and wine tasting of local wines at Ventimiglia Vineyard.

Day 4: Visit Overpeck County Park

Enjoy stunning views of Overpeck Lake while fishing, kayaking, or hiking through the park.

Day 5: Explore Bergen County Zoo

Experience a wide variety of animals from different regions all over the world and even participate in educational programs.

Day 6: Shop at Garden State Plaza

Indulge in shopping from over 320 stores, dine-in, and catch the latest blockbuster movie.

Day 7: Relax and rejuvenate

Take the day off to relax and rejuvenate with a spa or salon appointment or a yoga session.

Food and Drink Scene of River Edge

Must-Try Dishes

Cronuts from Dumont Donuts

Cronuts are a hybrid of croissants and doughnuts and are a must-try dish from Dumont Donuts in River Edge. It is a delicious pastry with a unique and crispy texture.

Local Beverages

Craft Beer at River Edge Restaurant

The River Edge Restaurant has a unique flavor of craft beers that you should try. The restaurant has a fascinating outdoor seating area and a romantic ambiance to complete your experience.

Where to Stay in River Edge: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Red Carpet Inn

The Red Carpet Inn is a budget-friendly motel located along the route 4 highway. It has an outdoor pool and comfortable rooms with free Wi-Fi.

What to Buy in River Edge: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Gifts and Decor from Opus Terrarum

Opus Terrarum is a gift shop that has a wide range of quality gifts, including decorative arts, sculptures, and hand-crafted jewelry. It is a perfect store for purchasing souvenirs or gifts for your loved ones.

Local Culture and Customs of River Edge

The residents of River Edge are welcoming and friendly, and they appreciate visitors who respect their culture and customs. River Edge is a community that values education, community service, and environmental preservation.

Best Time to Visit River Edge

The best time to visit River Edge is during the fall season, from September to November. The weather is pleasant, and there are many autumn festivals and events to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of River Edge

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Make an effort to learn about the history of River Edge.

Don’t:

  • Cause damage to the natural environments
  • Disrespect the locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in River Edge

River Edge is a relatively safe town, but you should take basic precautions such as locking your doors and not leaving valuable items in plain sight.

Health in River Edge

River Edge has reputable hospitals and clinics where you can receive medical attention if needed.

Getting to River Edge: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near River Edge

The nearest major airport is Newark Liberty International Airport, located approximately 25 miles from River Edge. Other airports options include Teterboro Airport, which is closer but mainly for charter flights.

Bus Stations Near River Edge

Coach USA Lines provide daily services from River Edge to the Port Authority Bus Terminal in New York City.

How to Get Around in River Edge

Getting around River Edge is easy as there are local transport services available, including buses and taxis. You can also explore the town on foot or rent a bike and get around at ease.

Where to Go Next from River Edge: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

New York City

River Edge, being a few miles away from New York City, there are plenty of places to visit within the city that you can explore on a day trip.

Hoboken

Hoboken is a city located across the Hudson River, and it’s a popular destination for shopping, dining, and nightlife.

Conclusion

River Edge is a gem of a town with a lot to offer. From history to natural beauty, the town has everything that an adventurer could want. Whether you love outdoor activities or cultural and historical attractions, River Edge is a destination worth exploring.
