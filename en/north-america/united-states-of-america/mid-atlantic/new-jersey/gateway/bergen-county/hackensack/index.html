Introduction to Hackensack

Located in Bergen County, New Jersey, Hackensack is a city of over 45,000 residents and is known for its cultural, historical, and natural attractions. The city has several museums, theaters, parks, hiking trails, and more.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll introduce you to the best things to see and do in Hackensack, from cultural landmarks and natural attractions to food and drink scenes and local customs.

Local Culture and Customs of Hackensack

The locals in Hackensack are friendly and hospitable. Some customs to note include:

  • Greeting with a handshake is common.
  • Tipping around 20% is expected at restaurants and bars.
  • Dress code is usually business casual.

Best Time to Visit Hackensack

The best time to visit Hackensack is during the summer months, between June and August. The weather is warm, and there are many outdoor activities to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hackensack

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Hackensack:

Do:

  • Smile and greet locals with a friendly hello.
  • Enjoy the outdoors and scenic views.
  • Embrace the food and drink scene.

Don’t:

  • Be loud or disruptive in public places.
  • Litter or damage the natural attractions.
  • Neglect to tip at restaurants and bars.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hackensack

Hackensack is a relatively safe city, but visitors should take some precautions to avoid any issues. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid walking alone at night.
  • Keep belongings safe and secure.
  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash.

Health in Hackensack

Hackensack has several hospitals and medical facilities, providing excellent medical care to any visitors to the city.
