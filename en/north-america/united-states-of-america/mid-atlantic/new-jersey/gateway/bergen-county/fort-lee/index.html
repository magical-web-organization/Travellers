Fort Lee Travel Guide

Fort Lee is a small town located in Bergen County, New Jersey, in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States. The town serves as a gateway to New York City and offers a perfect blend of urban and natural attractions. It’s a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and foodies. Whether you’re planning a week-long trip or just a short visit, our comprehensive guide will help you make the most of your time in Fort Lee.

List of Places to Visit in Fort Lee

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Lee Historic Park - Located on the site of an old American Revolutionary War encampment, the park has a hiking trail, picnic areas, and stunning views of the Hudson River.
  • Museum of Early Trades and Crafts - The museum exhibits artifacts from America’s early history, including tools, weapons, and household items.
  • Fort Lee Museum - The museum is home to historical artifacts and information relating to Fort Lee and its role in the Revolutionary War.

Natural Attractions of Fort Lee: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Palisades Interstate Park - A 30-mile stretch of parkland on the Hudson River with stunning views of the Palisades cliffs.
  • The Long Path - A 358-mile hiking trail that runs from the George Washington Bridge to Albany, New York, and passes through Fort Lee.
  • Overpeck County Park - A 811-acre park with a lake, trails, and picnic areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fort Lee

Fort Lee offers some of the best hiking and biking routes in the Mid-Atlantic region.

  • The Long Path - A challenging hiking trail that offers stunning views of the Hudson River and the Palisades.
  • Fort Lee Historic Park Trail - A moderate hiking trail that follows the path of the Revolutionary War encampment.
  • Henry Hudson Drive - A scenic drive with several lookout points that showcases the beauty of the Palisades cliffs.

Best Things to Do in Fort Lee: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking on the Hudson River - Experience the beauty of the Hudson River from the water with a guided kayak tour.
  • Food Tours - Discover the diverse food scene of Fort Lee with a guided walking tour of the town’s best restaurants and cafes.
  • Historic Tours - Learn about Fort Lee’s role in the Revolutionary War with a guided tour of the historic sites in the town.

Food and Drink Scene of Fort Lee

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pork roll sandwiches - A breakfast sandwich made of sliced pork roll, cheese, and egg on a sesame seed bun.
  • Bagels and lox - Salmon served with a cream cheese spread and toasted bagels.
  • Tomato pie - A savory pastry made of pizza dough topped with a chunky tomato sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Local craft beer - Try a beer from Brix City Brewing or Alementary Brewing Company.
  • Fresh juices - Stop off at Fort Lee Farmers Market, for freshly squeezed juices.

Local Culture and Customs of Fort Lee

Fort Lee is a vibrant town with a diverse population. The town celebrates its cultural diversity with various festivals and events throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Fort Lee

The best time to visit Fort Lee is during the fall when the colorful leaves make for a scenic view.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fort Lee

Do:

  • Be respectful of the town’s diverse population.
  • Take time to explore the town’s natural beauty.

Don’t:

  • Litter or vandalize public areas.
  • Disrespect local customs or beliefs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fort Lee

Fort Lee is generally a safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors should still take precautions when traveling.

Health in Fort Lee

Fort Lee has several medical facilities located throughout the town, including urgent care centers and hospitals. Make sure you have all necessary medical insurance or travel insurance coverage before traveling to Fort Lee.

Getting to Fort Lee: Transportation Options and Logistics

Other Options

Visitors can also get to Fort Lee by car, train, and ferry.

How to Get Around in Fort Lee

Visitors can explore Fort Lee by car or foot. The town is small enough to walk around, but a car would be more convenient for longer distances.

Where to Go Next from Fort Lee: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • New York City - Just across the George Washington Bridge, visitors can explore the Big Apple.
  • Cedar Grove - A charming town in Essex County with beautiful parks and historic landmarks.

Conclusion

Fort Lee, New Jersey, offers a unique blend of urban and natural attractions. From the hiking trails to the diverse food scene, there’s something for everyone. Whether you’re planning a week-long itinerary or just a short visit, our travel guide has everything you need to know to make the most of your time in Fort Lee.
