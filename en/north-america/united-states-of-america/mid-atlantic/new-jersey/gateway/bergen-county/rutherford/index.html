Introduction to Rutherford

Rutherford is a small borough in Bergen County, New Jersey. It is located in the Gateway region of the state and is part of the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States of America. Rutherford is known for its rich history, scenic beauty, and vibrant community. Visitors will find a plethora of cultural and natural attractions, hiking and biking routes, food and drink scene, and accommodations suitable for all budget ranges.

List of Places to Visit in Rutherford

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Meadowlands Museum: This museum showcases the history of the Meadowlands region, including Rutherford. Visitors can learn about the Native American culture, early Dutch settlers, and the contribution of the region to the Industrial Revolution.
  • Felician University Art Gallery: The gallery features a collection of contemporary art and hosts rotating exhibits and events throughout the year.
  • Rutherford Civil War Memorial: A prominent landmark in Rutherford, this monument pays tribute to the soldiers who fought in the American Civil War.

Natural Attractions of Rutherford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kingsland Point Park: This waterfront park offers stunning views of the Manhattan skyline, picnic areas, and a playground for children.
  • Rutherford Nature Preserve: This 35-acre nature preserve has a 1.1-mile trail that takes visitors through wooded areas and along the Passaic River.
  • Rutherford Pool: Visitors can cool off during the summer months at this public pool that features an Olympic-sized swimming pool, diving boards, and a kiddie pool.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rutherford

  • Rutherford Riverfront Trail: This 1.5-mile trail is an easy and scenic walk or bike ride along the Passaic River.
  • Bergen County Park System: The county park system is home to various trails suitable for different skill levels. Some popular trails include the Saddle River County Park and the Overpeck County Park.

Best Things to Do in Rutherford: Activities and Tours

  • Rutherford Farmers’ Market: The farmers’ market is open from June to November and features locally grown produce, baked goods, and crafts.
  • Rutherford Pancake House: This popular breakfast spot is known for its fluffy pancakes and decadent omelets.
  • Rutherford Hall: This historic mansion can be rented for events and weddings and offers guided tours of its stunning architecture and beautiful gardens.

Food and Drink Scene of Rutherford

Must-Try Dishes

  • Disco Fries: A New Jersey staple, these fries are covered in gravy and melted cheese.
  • Taylor Ham and Cheese Sandwich: Also known as a “pork roll” sandwich, this breakfast sandwich is undoubtedly a must-try.
  • Cannolis: This Italian dessert is often filled with ricotta cheese and chocolate chips.

Local Beverages

  • Rutherford Red Ale: A beer brewed locally by the Rutherford Brewing Company.
  • Lemonade: Visitors can enjoy refreshing lemonade, often sold by local farmers and vendors.

Where to Stay in Rutherford: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Extended Stay America: A budget-friendly option located near the Rutherford train station.
  • Renaissance Meadowlands Hotel: This hotel offers luxurious accommodations and easy access to various attractions and transportation options.
  • Airbnb: Visitors can find various apartments, homes, and private rooms listed on Airbnb suitable for different budget ranges.

What to Buy in Rutherford: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Rutherford T-Shirt: Visitors can purchase a T-shirt with Rutherford’s logo as a souvenir.
  • Local Honey: Harvested by local beekeepers, visitors can buy jars of honey as a unique and natural souvenir to bring back home.
  • Handmade Crafts: Visitors can find various handmade crafts and products at the Rutherford Farmers’ Market, including candles, jewelry, and pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of Rutherford

  • Community-Oriented: Rutherford is known for its strong sense of community and volunteerism. Visitors can participate in various community events and activities throughout the year.
  • Patriotic: Rutherford residents take pride in their town’s contribution to the American Revolutionary War and other significant historical events in the country’s history.

Best Time to Visit Rutherford

The best time to visit Rutherford is during the spring months (April to June) and the fall months (September to November). During these months, visitors can expect mild weather and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rutherford

Do:

  • Do visit the local farmers’ market to support local businesses and farmers.
  • Do try the local food, including the Disco Fries and Taylor Ham and Cheese Sandwich.
  • Do take in the scenic views of the Manhattan skyline from Kingsland Point Park.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in the parks and natural areas.
  • Don’t forget to tip the waitstaff at restaurants.
  • Don’t forget to bring sunscreen and insect repellent for outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rutherford

Rutherford is a safe borough, with a low crime rate compared to other towns and cities in New Jersey. However, visitors should still take general safety precautions, such as locking car doors and securing valuables.

Health in Rutherford

Visitors should take precautions against tick bites and Lyme disease when engaging in outdoor activities. They should also stay hydrated during warm weather and take precautions against sunburn and heatstroke.

Getting to Rutherford: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Rutherford

  • Newark Liberty International Airport: Newark Liberty is the closest airport to Rutherford, located approximately 18 miles southeast of the borough.
  • LaGuardia Airport: LaGuardia is another option, located approximately 20 miles east of Rutherford.

Bus Stations Near Rutherford

  • Rutherford Station: New Jersey Transit buses operate from Rutherford Station, providing connections to other towns and cities in New Jersey and New York City.

Other Options

  • Train: Rutherford is serviced by New Jersey Transit trains, providing connections to other towns and cities in the region.
  • Car: Visitors can rent a car or drive their own to explore Rutherford and the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Rutherford

Visitors can get around Rutherford on foot or bike, using public transportation, such as buses and trains, or renting a car. Taxis and ride-sharing services, such as Uber and Lyft, are also available.

Where to Go Next from Rutherford: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • New York City: Rutherford is located just 12 miles from the heart of New York City. Visitors can easily commute to the city and explore its many attractions.
  • Paterson Great Falls National Historical Park: This park, located approximately 8 miles northwest of Rutherford, is home to a 77-foot waterfall and various historical sites.
  • Liberty State Park: Located approximately 14 miles southeast of Rutherford, this park offers stunning views of the Manhattan skyline and the Statue of Liberty.

Conclusion

Rutherford is a hidden gem in Bergen County, New Jersey. Visitors can explore its rich history, scenic beauty, and vibrant community by visiting its cultural and natural attractions, hiking and biking routes, food and drink scene, and accommodations. The town’s proximity to New York City and other nearby attractions make it an ideal destination for a weekend getaway or a week-long vacation. Be sure to add it to your travel bucket list and experience it for yourself!
