Introduction to Bergen County

Bergen County is a diverse and dynamic region located in the heart of the Mid-Atlantic region of the US. It is a place where you can find a mix of vibrant cities and charming towns, beautiful natural scenery, rich history, and diverse cultural attractions. Known as the Gateway to New Jersey, it is an ideal destination for those who want to explore the best of New Jersey and Mid-Atlantic.

List of Places to Visit in Bergen County

Bergen County boasts a fantastic mix of cultural, historical, and natural attractions, including:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Meadowlands Museum: This museum explores the history of the Meadowlands, where Bergen County is located, and features exhibits on the region’s Native American tribes, explorers, and settlers.
  • The Aviation Hall of Fame of New Jersey: This museum is dedicated to preserving and honoring the aviation heritage of New Jersey and features exhibits on the state’s notable aviators and aircraft.
  • The Hermitage Museum: This historic house museum showcases the life and times of the Rosencrantz family, who owned the Hermitage estate and played a significant role in the American Revolution.
  • The New Jersey Naval Museum: This museum features exhibits on the history of the US Navy in New Jersey and includes displays of historic military artifacts and memorabilia.

Natural Attractions of Bergen County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ramapo Valley County Reservation: This picturesque park features hiking trails, picnic areas, fishing ponds, and scenic views of the Ramapo Mountains.
  • Palisades Interstate Park: This park offers scenic views of the Hudson River and the New York City skyline and features hiking trails, picnic areas, and historic sites like the Women’s Federation Monument.
  • Bergen County Zoological Park: This zoo is home to more than 100 species of animals from around the world, including tigers, bears, and primates.
  • Flat Rock Brook Nature Center: This nature center features hiking trails, wildlife viewing opportunities, and educational displays on the local flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bergen County

Bergen County is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with numerous trails and routes to suit every level of experience. Some of the best hiking/biking trails in Bergen County include:

  • Ramapo Valley County Reservation Trails: The trails at Ramapo Valley County Reservation offer hikers and bikers the chance to explore the park’s rolling hills and scenic views of the Ramapo Mountains.
  • Saddle River County Park Trail: This trail runs through Saddle River County Park and offers hikers and bikers the chance to see a variety of wildlife and enjoy scenic views of the Saddle River.
  • Oradell Reservoir Loop Trail: This 6.5-mile trail offers hikers and bikers the chance to explore the Oradell Reservoir and enjoy scenic views of the surrounding woods and countryside.
  • Palisades Interstate Park Trail: This trail runs through the Palisades Interstate Park and offers hikers and bikers the chance to see stunning views of the Hudson River and New York City skyline.

Best Things to Do in Bergen County: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or family-friendly fun, Bergen County has something for everyone. Some of the best things to do in Bergen County include:

  • Take a hot air balloon ride over the Meadowlands
  • Enjoy a scenic cruise on the Hudson River
  • Visit a local winery or brewery and taste the best of the region’s wine and beer
  • Take a guided tour of the historic Hermitage Museum or Aviation Hall of Fame
  • Explore the natural beauty of Ramapo Valley County Reservation or Palisades Interstate Park
  • Take the kids to Bergen County Zoological Park or Legoland Discovery Center
  • Catch a live performance at Bergen Performing Arts Center or Symphony Hall

Food and Drink Scene of Bergen County

Bergen County is home to a diverse and vibrant food and drink scene, with something to suit every taste and preference. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages to sample while in Bergen County:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Taylor ham, egg, and cheese sandwich: A New Jersey classic, this breakfast sandwich features a slice of Taylor ham, a fried egg, and melted cheese on a roll.
  • Pizza: New Jersey is known for its delicious pizza, and Bergen County has some of the best pizzerias in the state.
  • Bagels: Another New Jersey staple, Bergen County is home to numerous bagel shops that serve fresh, chewy bagels with a variety of toppings and spreads.
  • Korean barbecue: Bergen County has a vibrant Korean community, and its restaurants serve some of the best Korean barbecue in the state.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer: Bergen County is home to numerous craft breweries, serving up a variety of locally brewed beer styles.
  • Wine: The region also has several wineries that produce delicious local wines, including reds, whites, and fruit wines.
  • Coffee: Bergen County has many cozy coffee shops that serve up delicious coffee and espresso drinks made with locally roasted beans.

Where to Stay in Bergen County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bergen County has plenty of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Some of the best places to stay in Bergen County include:

  • The Hilton Hasbrouck Heights/Meadowlands: This hotel is located just a short drive from the Meadowlands and offers comfortable rooms, on-site dining, and an indoor pool.
  • The Saddle Brook Marriott: This hotel is located in the heart of Bergen County and features modern guest rooms, a fitness center, and an on-site restaurant.
  • The Hostel at Bergen Community College: For budget-conscious travelers, this hostel offers affordable dorm-style rooms and easy access to the region’s popular attractions.
  • The St. Leonard’s Court Guest House: This charming guest house is located in an historic home in Englewood and offers comfortable rooms and personalized service.

What to Buy in Bergen County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home from Bergen County, here are some ideas:

  • Taylor ham: Bring home a package of the delicious New Jersey pork product known as Taylor ham, which is a staple of breakfast sandwiches in the region.
  • Local wine or beer: Pick up a bottle or six-pack of locally brewed beer or wine from one of Bergen County’s many craft breweries or wineries.
  • Bergen County memorabilia: Many local shops and boutiques sell t-shirts, hats, and other souvenirs featuring the Bergen County logo or designs that celebrate the region’s history and culture.

Local Culture and Customs of Bergen County

Bergen County is a diverse and welcoming community that celebrates its history and culture in many ways. Some of the unique customs and traditions you may encounter in Bergen County include:

  • The Hackensack Music Festival: This annual event celebrates the rich history of music in Bergen County and features live performances by local musicians and bands.
  • The Bergen County Irish Festival: This festival celebrates the Irish heritage of Bergen County and features live music, Irish step dancing, and traditional Irish food and drink.
  • The Bergen County Farmers Market: This weekly market features fresh, locally grown produce, baked goods, and other artisanal products.

Best Time to Visit Bergen County

Bergen County is a year-round destination, with something to offer visitors in every season. However, if you want to enjoy the region’s outdoor attractions and scenic views, the best time to visit is during the spring or fall. July and August can be hot and humid, and winters can be cold and snowy, but there are plenty of indoor attractions and activities to enjoy during any time of year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bergen County

To help you make the most of your visit to Bergen County, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Explore the natural beauty of the region, including its parks and hiking trails.
  • Sample the local cuisine and beverages, including Taylor ham, pizza, and craft beer.
  • Take the time to learn about the history and culture of Bergen County by visiting museums and cultural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Speed on the region’s highways and roads, as the police strictly enforce speed limits and other traffic laws.
  • Ignore the signs and regulations in the region’s parks and natural areas, as they are in place to protect the environment and wildlife.

Safety and Health

Health and safety are always a concern when traveling, and Bergen County is no exception. Here are some things to keep in mind:

Safety in Bergen County

  • Be aware of your surroundings when traveling in urban areas, especially at night.
  • Keep valuables and important documents in a secure location.
  • Follow traffic laws and regulations when driving or cycling.

Health in Bergen County

  • Stay hydrated during the hot summer months, especially if you are spending time outdoors.
  • Take precautions to prevent tick bites when hiking or spending time in wooded areas.
  • Stay up-to-date on routine vaccinations and consult with your doctor if you have any health concerns before traveling.

Getting to Bergen County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bergen County is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Bergen County

  • Newark Liberty International Airport: Located approximately 25 miles south of Bergen County, Newark Liberty is the nearest major airport.
  • LaGuardia Airport: Located approximately 20 miles east of Bergen County, LaGuardia is another viable airport option.
  • John F. Kennedy International Airport: Located approximately 40 miles east of Bergen County, JFK is a larger airport that offers flights to a wider range of destinations.

Bus Stations Near Bergen County

  • Port Authority Bus Terminal: This major bus terminal in New York City offers numerous bus routes to points throughout New Jersey and the Mid-Atlantic region.
  • George Washington Bridge Bus Station: Located in Fort Lee, NJ, this bus station offers a variety of bus routes to destinations throughout New Jersey and the New York metropolitan area.

Other Options

  • Car rental: If you prefer to drive, you can rent a car at Newark Liberty or LaGuardia airports.
  • Train: Amtrak and NJ Transit offer train service to Bergen County via the Secaucus Junction station.

How to Get Around in Bergen County

Bergen County offers numerous transportation options for travelers, including buses, trains, and taxis. If you plan on exploring the region’s parks and natural areas, you may also want to rent a car or bike. Most of Bergen County’s main attractions are located within a short distance of one another, so it is easy to explore the region on foot or by bike.

Where to Go Next from Bergen County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip beyond Bergen County, there are several nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • New York City: Just across the Hudson River from Bergen County, New York City offers endless attractions and experiences, from Broadway shows and world-class museums to iconic landmarks like the Statue of Liberty and Empire State Building.
  • The Jersey Shore: Located approximately two hours south of Bergen County, the Jersey Shore is a popular beach destination that offers sun, sand, and surf.
  • The Delaware Water Gap: Located approximately two hours west of Bergen County, the Delaware Water Gap is a stunning natural area that offers hiking, fishing, and scenic views of the Delaware River.

Conclusion

Bergen County is a dynamic and exciting destination that offers visitors a wealth of attractions, natural beauty, and cultural experiences. From hiking and biking to sampling the region’s delicious cuisine and beverages, there is something for everyone in this picturesque region of New Jersey.
