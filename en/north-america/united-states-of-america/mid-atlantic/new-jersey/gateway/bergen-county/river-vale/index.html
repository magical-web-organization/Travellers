Introduction to River Vale

Nestled in Bergen County, New Jersey, River Vale is a charming township that has something for everyone. Whether you’re looking to explore cultural sites, enjoy scenic views, go hiking and biking or simply relax and indulge in local cuisine, River Vale has it all.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the best things to do in River Vale, including cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, hiking and biking routes, local food and drink, accommodations, and more. So sit back, relax, and let’s explore this hidden gem of New Jersey.

List of Places to Visit in River Vale

River Vale offers a diverse range of attractions that cater to all interests. Here’s a list of places to add to your itinerary when visiting River Vale.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Historic New Bridge Landing - A perfect spot for history buffs, this historical park features a museum, visitor center, and a collection of restored buildings showcasing different periods of American history.
  2. Westervelt-Thomas Barn - This barn-turned-museum is a must-visit for anyone interested in local agricultural history and the evolution of farming in New Jersey.
  3. Belskie Museum of Art and Science - This museum showcases a collection of art and science exhibits featuring works from local artists and scientists.

Natural Attractions of River Vale: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Pascack Brook County Park - This sprawling park offers a variety of trails for hiking and biking, as well as picnic areas and playgrounds.
  2. Bear Swamp Natural Area - A peaceful nature reserve in the heart of River Vale, this area is known for its marshlands, hiking trails, and striking views of the surrounding landscape.
  3. Fifth Street Park - A serene park located near the town center, Fifth Street Park offers plenty of greenery and a picturesque pond that’s perfect for a relaxing afternoon stroll.

Hiking/Biking Routes of River Vale

  1. Pascack Valley Line Trail - A scenic 11-mile trail that runs from Ridgewood to Hawthorne, offering plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking, and taking in stunning views along the way.
  2. Saddle River County Park - A beautiful 6-mile trail that runs through five towns, connecting you to some of the best natural and cultural attractions in the area.
  3. Oradell Reservoir Loop - A 5.8-mile trail located just minutes from River Vale, known for its scenic views of the reservoir and surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in River Vale: Activities and Tours

  1. Bike Tour - Join a guided bike tour that takes you through some of the most scenic trails and sights in River Vale.
  2. Wine Tour - Explore local wineries and vineyards with a guided tour that includes tasting sessions and an introduction to the art of wine-making.
  3. Food Tour - Immerse yourself in the local cuisine with a food tour that takes you through some of the best restaurants and cafes in River Vale.

Week-Long Itinerary for River Vale

Day 1: Explore River Vale’s cultural landmarks, including Historic New Bridge Landing, Westervelt-Thomas Barn, and Belskie Museum of Art and Science.

Day 2: Enjoy the local art scene at the Puffin Cultural Forum, an independent arts organization that hosts exhibitions, concerts, and workshops.

Day 3: Spend the day outdoors at Pascack Brook County Park, the Bear Swamp Natural Area, and Fifth Street Park.

Day 4: Take a bike tour of River Vale’s picturesque trails and stop for lunch at one of the town’s many cafes and restaurants.

Day 5: Take a guided wine tour of local vineyards and wineries.

Day 6: Explore nearby attractions in Bergen County, including the Bergen County Zoological Park and the Bergen Performing Arts Center.

Day 7: Indulge in the local cuisine with a food tour that takes you through River Vale’s best restaurants and cafes.

Food and Drink Scene of River Vale

No trip to River Vale is complete without indulging in the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to add to your list.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Pizza - River Vale is known for its delicious brick oven pizzas, made with fresh, local ingredients.
  2. Bagels - Bagel shops in River Vale are famous for their traditional New York-style bagels, crispy on the outside and soft on the inside.
  3. Seafood - River Vale’s coastal location means there’s no shortage of fresh seafood, from shrimp and lobster to scallops and clams.

Local Beverages

  1. Beer - River Vale is home to several craft breweries, with options ranging from hoppy IPAs to malty stouts.
  2. Wine - A trip to River Vale isn’t complete without sampling some of the region’s finest wines, made with locally-grown grapes.
  3. Coffee - Local cafes serve up some of the best coffee in the area, made with locally-roasted beans and served in charming, cozy environments.

Where to Stay in River Vale: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

River Vale offers plenty of accommodation options to suit all tastes and budgets. Here are some of our top picks:

  1. Courtyard by Marriott Montvale - A stylish, modern hotel located near the town center, with spacious rooms and a range of amenities.

  2. Tappan Motel - A cozy, family-owned motel with affordable rates and a friendly, welcoming atmosphere.

  3. The Castle at Skylands Manor - If you’re looking for a unique, romantic getaway, this historic Castle-turned-bed and breakfast is the perfect choice.

What to Buy in River Vale: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Fresh Produce - Stop by one of River Vale’s many farmers’ markets to pick up some locally-grown fruits and vegetables.

  2. Artisanal Products - River Vale is home to many talented artisans, including potters, painters, and jewelry makers, whose products make the perfect souvenirs.

  3. Local Wine - Bring home a bottle of locally-produced wine, made with grapes grown right here in River Vale.

Local Culture and Customs of River Vale

River Vale is a welcoming community that takes pride in its rich history and diverse culture. Visitors are encouraged to explore the town and engage with locals, who are always happy to share their knowledge and experience. Be sure to attend one of the many local festivals and events, such as River Vale Day or the Annual Street Fair, to truly experience the local culture.

Best Time to Visit River Vale

The best time to visit River Vale is during the spring and fall months, when the weather is mild and the fall foliage is at its peak. Summer is also a popular time to visit, thanks to outdoor activities and events like the Farmers’ Markets and concert series. Winter is the quietest time of year in River Vale, but still offers plenty of opportunities to explore local museums and enjoy a cozy retreat in one of the town’s many bed and breakfasts.

Do’s and Don’ts of River Vale

Here are some helpful tips to keep in mind when visiting River Vale:

Do:

  • Take the time to explore the town’s natural beauty and rich history.
  • Visit local farmers’ markets and try some of the fresh, locally-grown produce.
  • Attend one of the many local festivals and events to truly experience the local culture.

Don’t:

  • Forget to pack comfortable shoes for hiking and biking.
  • Miss out on the opportunity to try the local cuisine, including pizza, bagels, and seafood.
  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions of River Vale.

Safety and Health

Safety in River Vale

River Vale is a safe town, with low crime rates and a friendly, welcoming atmosphere. However, visitors should always exercise common sense and take precautions to stay safe, especially when exploring natural areas.

Health in River Vale

River Vale offers many healthcare facilities and services, including hospitals, urgent care centers, and dental clinics. Visitors should also take precautions to prevent tick bites, as Lyme disease is prevalent in the area.

Getting to River Vale: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near River Vale

  1. Newark Liberty International Airport - Located 35 miles from River Vale, Newark is the closest major airport, with a range of domestic and international flights.

  2. LaGuardia Airport - Located 30 miles from River Vale, LaGuardia offers a variety of domestic and international flights, with a focus on regional travel.

Bus Stations Near River Vale

  1. The Port Authority Bus Terminal - Located 20 miles from River Vale, The Port Authority Bus Terminal offers a range of bus and coach services, including Greyhound and New Jersey Transit.

  2. Coach USA - Located 15 miles from River Vale, Coach USA provides local and regional bus services throughout the tri-state area.

Other Options

River Vale is easily accessible by car, with the Garden State Parkway and New Jersey Turnpike both within close proximity. Additionally, local taxi and rideshare services are available for transportation around town.

How to Get Around in River Vale

River Vale is a small town, easily navigable on foot or by car. For those looking to explore the surrounding area, bike rentals and guided tours are also available.

Where to Go Next from River Vale: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. New York City - Just a short drive or train ride from River Vale, New York City offers a wide range of cultural and entertainment options, from world-class museums to Broadway shows.

  2. Harriman State Park - Located just a short drive from River Vale, Harriman State Park offers miles of hiking trails, stunning views, and an array of outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in exploring New Jersey’s rich history and culture, enjoying the great outdoors, or indulging in some of the best food and drink in the region, River Vale has it all. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has inspired you to book your next trip to this hidden gem of the Garden State.
