Introduction to Paterson

Located in Passaic County, in the Gateway region of New Jersey, Paterson is an industrial city with a rich history and plenty of cultural attractions. The city sits alongside the Passaic River and offers stunning views of its Great Falls, which played a significant role in the Industrial Revolution. Visitors will definitely enjoy exploring various attractions in Paterson, including well-maintained parks, historical landmarks, and excellent museums. Here is a comprehensive breaking-down tour guide of things to do in Paterson.

Local Culture and Customs of Paterson

In the years of its existence, Paterson has grown from a small town to a metropolis, but it has retained its traditions and unique local customs. Several cultural events throughout the year showcase Paterson’s rich heritage, which includes vibrant African American, Hispanic, and Italian communities.

Best Time to Visit Paterson

The best time to visit Paterson is during the fall, when the leaves turn brilliant colors and the crisp air provides perfect hiking weather. In addition, late spring and early summer offer perfect weather with less crowds and lower prices.

Safety and Health

For visitors who follow the safety guidelines, Paterson is a safe city to explore. Health-wise, you should avoid getting too close to the Great Falls, as the mist can cause respiratory issues.

Safety in Paterson

  1. Stick to well-lit, high-traffic areas at night.
  2. Keep your belongings in front of you and in a secure place.
  3. Avoid traveling alone at night.

Health in Paterson

  1. Wear comfortable shoes and clothing.
  2. Drink plenty of water.
  3. Avoid getting too close to the Great Falls mist.
