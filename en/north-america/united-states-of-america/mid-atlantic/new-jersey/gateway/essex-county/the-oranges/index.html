Introduction to The Oranges

Located in Essex County, in the Gateway region of New Jersey, The Oranges consists of four towns: East Orange, Orange, South Orange, and West Orange. The region is known for its tree-lined streets, historic homes, cultural attractions, and scenic views. The Oranges has something to offer for everyone, whether you are looking for outdoor adventures, museum hopping, or gastronomic delights.

List of Places to Visit in The Oranges

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Thomas Edison National Historical Park: This museum and park are dedicated to the life and work of Thomas Edison, who lived in West Orange. Visitors can tour his labs, see his inventions, and visit his home.
  • Montclair Art Museum: This museum showcases American and Native American art, including works by Georgia O’Keeffe and Edward Hopper.
  • Zimmerli Art Museum at Rutgers: This museum has a collection of more than 60,000 works of art, including American, European, and Asian art.

Natural Attractions of The Oranges: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • South Mountain Reservation: This 2,000-acre park is a popular spot for hiking, biking, and picnicking. It offers scenic views of New York City and the surrounding landscape.
  • Eagle Rock Reservation: This park has hiking trails, picnic areas, and a 9/11 memorial. It also offers panoramic views of Manhattan.
  • Turtle Back Zoo: This zoo has more than 200 species of animals, including a sea lion exhibit and a bird aviary.

Hiking/Biking Routes of The Oranges

  • The Lenape Trail: This 34-mile trail runs through The Oranges, as well as other towns in Essex County. It is a great option for hiking, running, or biking. It offers scenic views of the Watchung Mountains and the New York City skyline.
  • South Mountain Reservation: This park has several hiking trails, ranging from easy to difficult. The trails offer views of waterfalls and streams.

Best Things to Do in The Oranges: Activities and Tours

  • Heritage Tours: These guided tours will take you around West Orange and South Orange to see historic homes, buildings, and landmarks.
  • Escape Rooms: The Oranges has several escape rooms, including The Puzzle Parlour, where you can solve puzzles and riddles to escape a room and The Escape Squad, where you can play games with friends or colleagues.
  • Luna Stage Theatre Company: This non-profit theatre company produces contemporary plays and new works by emerging playwrights.

Food and Drink Scene of The Oranges

Must-Try Dishes

  • Clinton Lunch: This diner in East Orange is known for its breakfast platters, burgers, and milkshakes.
  • SuzyQue’s BBQ and Bar: This restaurant in West Orange serves smoked meats, southern sides, and craft beer.
  • Ricalton’s Village Tavern: This local pub in South Orange has a menu of classic American dishes, including wings, burgers, and sandwiches.

Local Beverages

  • Gaslight Brewery & Restaurant: This brewery in South Orange has a selection of craft beers on tap, as well as a menu of pub grub.
  • Hat City Kitchen: This bar in West Orange has live music and a large selection of cocktails and wine.

Where to Stay in The Oranges: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Wilshire Grand Hotel: This hotel in West Orange has spacious rooms, a fitness center, and an on-site restaurant.
  • The Avenue Suites: This hotel in South Orange has suite-style rooms, a fitness center, and a indoor pool.
  • The Inn at Montclair: This bed and breakfast in Montclair has cozy rooms, a garden, and a complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in The Oranges: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Irv’s Luggage: This store in South Orange has a selection of luggage, travel accessories, and backpacks.
  • Rock Paper Scissors: This store in Montclair has a selection of greeting cards, stationery, and gifts.
  • The Fringe: This store in South Orange has a selection of jewelry, handbags, and accessories.

Local Culture and Customs of The Oranges

The Oranges is a diverse and welcoming community with a rich history and cultural heritage. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions. The Oranges also have a strong arts scene, and visitors can attend live performances, exhibits, and shows.

Best Time to Visit The Oranges

The best time to visit The Oranges is during the spring or fall, when the temperatures are mild and the foliage is colorful. The summers can be hot and humid, and the winters can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of The Oranges

Do:

  • Do explore the parks and outdoor spaces.
  • Do try the local food and drink.
  • Do attend local cultural events and performances.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage historic landmarks.
  • Don’t leave valuables in your car.
  • Don’t park illegally.

Safety and Health

Safety in The Oranges

The Oranges are generally safe for visitors, but caution should be exercised in unfamiliar areas and at night. Visitors should also be aware of traffic and obey traffic signals.

Health in The Oranges

Visitors to The Oranges should be up-to-date on routine vaccinations and be aware of tick-borne illnesses, such as Lyme disease. Visitors should also wear sunscreen and drink plenty of water during the hot summer months.

Getting to The Oranges: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near The Oranges

  • Newark Liberty International Airport: This airport is about 15 miles from The Oranges and has domestic and international flights.
  • LaGuardia Airport: This airport is about 40 miles from The Oranges and has domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near The Oranges

  • Greyhound Bus Terminal in Newark: This bus terminal is about 10 miles from The Oranges and has connections to major cities in the Northeast.
  • NJ Transit Bus Terminal in South Orange: This bus terminal is in The Oranges and has connections to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

  • Train: The Oranges are served by NJ Transit’s Morris & Essex Line, with stops in East Orange, Orange, South Orange, and West Orange.
  • Car: The Oranges are easily accessible by car and are located near major highways, such as I-280 and the Garden State Parkway.

How to Get Around in The Oranges

The Oranges are best explored on foot or by bike, especially if you are visiting the parks and outdoor spaces. There are also several public transportation options, including buses and trains.

Where to Go Next from The Oranges: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Montclair: This town is known for its arts scene, fine dining, and shopping.
  • Jersey City: This city is located across the Hudson River and has a vibrant downtown, waterfront parks, and Italian neighborhoods.
  • New York City: The Big Apple is only a short train or bus ride away from The Oranges.

Conclusion

The Oranges, New Jersey is a hidden gem that offers a mix of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and gastronomic delights. Whether you are traveling solo, with family, or with friends, The Oranges has something to offer for everyone. So pack your bags, grab a map, and explore this beautiful region in the Garden State.
