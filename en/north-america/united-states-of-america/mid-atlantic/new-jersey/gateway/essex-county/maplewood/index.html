Introduction to Maplewood

Located in Essex County and part of the Gateway region of New Jersey, Maplewood is a charming town that boasts a rich history, diverse cultures, and natural beauty. It is conveniently situated just 20 miles west of New York City, making it an ideal destination for a day trip or weekend getaway.

List of Places to Visit in Maplewood

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or nature, Maplewood has something for everyone. Here are some of the top attractions to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Durand-Hedden House & Garden: A historic house museum that showcases the town’s early history and architecture.
  • The Woodland: A historic mansion that has been converted into a performance space and art gallery.
  • Geralyn’s Art Studio: A contemporary art gallery that features the works of local artists.

Natural Attractions of Maplewood: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • South Mountain Reservation: A large park with hiking and biking trails, picnic areas, and scenic views.
  • Maplewood Memorial Park: A small park that features a pond and walking paths.
  • Maplewood Golf Course: A public golf course that offers beautiful views of the surrounding hills.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Maplewood

Maplewood is renowned for its scenic hiking and biking routes. Here are some top picks:

  • South Mountain Reservation: The park offers over 2,000 acres of hiking and biking trails.
  • Maplewood Greenway: A paved path that runs through the town and is perfect for leisurely bike rides.

Best Things to Do in Maplewood: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and biking, there are many other exciting activities to enjoy in Maplewood:

  • Art Walk: A self-guided tour of Maplewood’s art galleries and studios.
  • Farmers’ Market: A weekly market that features local produce, artisanal goods, and live music.
  • Maplewoodstock: An annual music festival that features local and national acts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Maplewood

If you have a week to explore Maplewood, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore the town’s history at Durand-Hedden House & Garden and The Woodland, followed by dinner at Lorena’s.
  • Day 2: Hike the trails at South Mountain Reservation and have a picnic lunch.
  • Day 3: Take a self-guided art walk and enjoy lunch at Arturo’s Osteria & Pizzeria.
  • Day 4: Play a round of golf at Maplewood Golf Course and have lunch at Verjus.
  • Day 5: Visit the Maplewood Memorial Park and have an early dinner at The Cassidy.
  • Day 6: Take a cooking class at Cooking Gems and enjoy dinner at The Maplewood Grille.
  • Day 7: Visit the farmers’ market for breakfast and end the day with a concert at The Woodland.

Food and Drink Scene of Maplewood

Maplewood boasts a vibrant food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maplewood Burger at St. James’s Gate Publick House
  • Chicken Shish Kabob at Arturo’s Osteria & Pizzeria
  • Pork Belly Banh Mi at The Cassidy

Local Beverages

  • Village Vintner Wine Boutique & Tasting Room
  • Kobrick Coffee Co.
  • Able Baker Brewing Company

Where to Stay in Maplewood: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Maplewood offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some top picks:

  • The Maplewood Hotel: A charming Victorian bed and breakfast.
  • The Madison Hotel: A luxurious hotel located in nearby Morristown.
  • Wyndham Hamilton Park Hotel & Conference Center: A contemporary hotel with a spa and golf course.

What to Buy in Maplewood: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to unique souvenirs and local products, Maplewood has plenty to offer. Here are some top picks:

  • South Mountain Creamery: Locally-sourced and organic dairy products.
  • Cedar Ridge Farm: Locally-grown produce, flowers, and herbs.
  • Wine from Village Vintner Wine Boutique & Tasting Room.

Local Culture and Customs of Maplewood

Maplewood is a diverse community with a rich cultural heritage. Here are some tips to help you navigate the local culture and customs:

  • Respect religious and cultural practices.
  • Tip at restaurants and bars.
  • Use respectful language when addressing others.

Best Time to Visit Maplewood

The best time to visit Maplewood is during the spring or fall when the weather is mild and the scenery is beautiful. The summers can be hot and humid, while the winters can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Maplewood

To make the most of your visit to Maplewood, here are some essential do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Explore the parks and natural attractions.
  • Show respect for the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage public property.
  • Use offensive language or behave inappropriately.
  • Violate traffic rules or laws.

Safety and Health

Safety in Maplewood

Overall, Maplewood is a safe destination for travelers. However, it’s always a good idea to take basic precautions, such as locking doors and windows, staying alert at night, and avoiding unsafe areas.

Health in Maplewood

Maplewood has several medical facilities and hospitals in case of emergencies. It’s also important to have medical insurance that covers travel-related illnesses and injuries.

Getting to Maplewood: Transportation Options and Logistics

Maplewood is easily accessible by various modes of transportation:

Airports Near Maplewood

  • Newark Liberty International Airport: 14 miles away.
  • John F. Kennedy International Airport: 32 miles away.
  • LaGuardia Airport: 34 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Maplewood

  • Newark Penn Station: 5 miles away.
  • Port Authority Bus Terminal: 20 miles away.

Other Options

  • Train: NJ Transit’s Morristown Line stops in Maplewood.
  • Car: Maplewood is easily accessible via the Garden State Parkway and Interstate 78.

How to Get Around in Maplewood

Maplewood is a pedestrian-friendly town with walkable neighborhoods and convenient public transportation options. NJ Transit buses and trains connect Maplewood to nearby communities and New York City.

Where to Go Next from Maplewood: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to explore from Maplewood:

  • Morristown: A historic town with museums, restaurants, and parks.
  • South Orange: A charming town with a vibrant arts scene.
  • New York City: The Big Apple is just a short train ride away.

Conclusion

Maplewood is a hidden gem in New Jersey that offers something for everyone, from history buffs and outdoor enthusiasts to foodies and art lovers. Whether you’re visiting for a day or a week, this comprehensive tour guide will help you plan your perfect trip to Maplewood.
