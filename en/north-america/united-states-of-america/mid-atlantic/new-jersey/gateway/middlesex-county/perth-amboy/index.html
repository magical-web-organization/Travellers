Introduction to Perth Amboy

Perth Amboy is a historic city located in Middlesex County, New Jersey, known for its diverse culture, scenic riverfront, and rich history. The city was founded in 1683 and served as the capital of East Jersey, one of the two original provinces of New Jersey, from 1686 to 1702. Its strategic location on the Raritan Bay has played an important role in its rich history, attracting traders, settlers, and industrialists over the centuries.

Today, Perth Amboy is a vibrant and welcoming community that offers visitors a wealth of attractions and activities, including historical landmarks, cultural centers, outdoor adventures, and a thriving food scene.

List of Places to Visit in Perth Amboy

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Perth Amboy is home to many historical landmarks and museums, showcasing the city’s rich and diverse history.

  • The Proprietary House: Built in the 1760s as the residence of New Jersey’s last royal governor, William Franklin, and his wife, Ann Smith Franklin. Today it serves as a museum that offers visitors a glimpse into Colonial-era life in New Jersey.
  • The Perth Amboy Ferry Slip: The Perth Amboy Ferry Slip is a historic maritime landmark that served as an important transportation hub for Manhattan-bound ferries in the 19th and early 20th centuries. Today, it is a museum and visitor center that provides a window into the city’s rich naval history.
  • The Kearny Cottage: A well-preserved example of a Federal-style house built in 1781. It served as the residence of the Kearny family, one of the most prominent families of the Revolutionary War era, including General Philip Kearny.
  • The Jewish Renaissance Foundation: A cultural and educational center that showcases Jewish culture, history, and community involvement through art exhibitions, live performances, and other events.

Natural Attractions of Perth Amboy: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Perth Amboy’s location on the Raritan Bay provides plenty of opportunities for outdoor recreation and scenic views.

  • Caledonia State Park: A popular recreation area that offers hiking trails, biking trails, camping sites, and fishing spots.
  • The Waterfront: Perth Amboy’s 2-mile waterfront promenade offers scenic views of the Raritan Bay and the New York City skyline. The promenade also features a designated bike lane and several parks, including the Bayview Park, Harbortown Park, and Washington Park.
  • The Patriot’s Observation Deck: Located at the top of the Perth Amboy Waterfront, the Patriot’s Observation Deck offers panoramic views of the skyline of New York City and the Raritan Bay.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Perth Amboy

Perth Amboy has several hiking and biking routes that are perfect for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. These routes offer scenic views of the city’s natural beauty and rich history.

  • The Historic Perth Amboy Loop: A 2.8-mile biking and hiking route that passes through several historic landmarks, including the Proprietary House, Kearny Cottage, and the Perth Amboy Ferry Slip.
  • The Waterfront Trail: A 2.5-mile biking and hiking route that follows the Perth Amboy Waterfront, offering scenic views of the Raritan Bay and the New York City skyline.
  • Cheesequake State Park: A popular hiking and wildlife observation area located adjacent to Perth Amboy which offers miles of hiking trails, including easy, scenic hikes like the Green Trail, to more challenging trails like the Blue Trail, which clocks in at nearly 6 miles long.

Best Things to Do in Perth Amboy: Activities and Tours

Perth Amboy has many activities and tours for tourists and families, ranging from historic attractions to family-friendly events throughout the year, including:

  • Perth Amboy Art Walk: An annual art festival in September showcasing local artists through the city’s galleries, shops, and restaurants.
  • Pedaling History Bike Tours: A bike tour of the city’s major historic sites, including the Proprietary House, Kearny Cottage, and the Perth Amboy Ferry Slip.
  • Waterfront Arts Festival: An annual waterfront event featuring live music, art, local vendors, and food trucks.

Week-Long Itinerary for Perth Amboy

Perth Amboy is a destination that deserves more than just a day trip. We recommend a weeklong itinerary that includes:

  • Day 1: Visit the Proprietary House, Kearny Cottage, and the Perth Amboy Ferry Slip, all accessible from the Historic Perth Amboy Loop.
  • Day 2: Explore Perth Amboy’s outdoor attractions by hiking through Cheesequake State Park and enjoying the scenic views of the waterfront trail.
  • Day 3: Head to Caledonia State Park for camping and fishing.
  • Day 4: Take a bike tour of the city’s major historic sites, followed by a visit to the Perth Amboy Art Walk in the evening.
  • Day 5: Explore Perth Amboy’s cultural institutions, such as the Jewish Renaissance Foundation, and enjoy a scenic walk along the waterfront.
  • Day 6: Visit Patriot’s Observation Deck and enjoy panoramic views of the New York City skyline.
  • Day 7: Visit the Waterfront Arts Festival and enjoy live music, art, and local food.

Food and Drink Scene of Perth Amboy

Perth Amboy offers visitors a wide range of dining options, from casual cafes and diners to upscale restaurants. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jibarito: A Puerto Rican sandwich made with flattened and fried plantains instead of bread.
  • Peruvian Chicken: Roasted chicken marinated with Peruvian spices and served with rice and beans.
  • Portuguese-style Octopus: Braised in garlic, olive oil, onions, and tomatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Cape May Brewing Company: The brewery offers a wide range of craft beers on tap, including the popular Cape May IPA and Coastal Evacuation Double IPA.
  • Vale Portugal: A specialty wine and liquor store that carries Portuguese wines, ports, and liquors.

Perth Amboy offers a range of accommodations that cater to different budgets and needs. Here are some recommendations:

  • The Heldrich Hotel & Conference Center: A modern hotel and conference center located in downtown New Brunswick, about a 15-minute drive from Perth Amboy.
  • Hampton Inn Woodbridge: A comfortable and modern hotel located in the heart of Woodbridge, just a 10-minute drive from Perth Amboy.
  • The Atrium: A cozy and affordable bed and breakfast located in the heart of Perth Amboy, offering easy access to major attractions and activities.

What to Buy in Perth Amboy: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Perth Amboy has several shops and markets that offer unique souvenirs and local products.

  • The Perth Amboy Farmers Market: A weekly farmers market that showcases local produce, baked goods, honey, and jams.
  • The Proprietary House Gift Shop: A shop located in the Proprietary House that sells Colonial-style souvenirs, jewelry, and pottery.
  • El Barrio Market: An indoor marketplace that offers a wide variety of goods, including clothing, beauty supplies, fresh produce, and cultural souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Perth Amboy

Perth Amboy is a diverse and inclusive community that values cultural traditions and customs. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions, including:

  • Festivals and Celebrations: Perth Amboy is home to several festivals and celebrations throughout the year that reflect the city’s cultural diversity, including the Puerto Rican Festival, the Portuguese Feast, and the Ecuadorian Festival.
  • Languages Spoken: The official language of Perth Amboy is English, but many of its residents also speak Spanish, Portuguese, and other languages.
  • Respect for Historic Sites: Perth Amboy has several historic sites that are important to the local community and should be respected by visitors.

Best Time to Visit Perth Amboy

The best time to visit Perth Amboy is during the spring or fall when the weather is temperate and many outdoor activities and festivals are available. The summer months can be hot and humid, while winter can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Perth Amboy

Visitors should be mindful of the following do’s and don’ts when visiting Perth Amboy:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Explore the city’s historic sites and natural attractions.
  • Attend one of the city’s many festivals and cultural celebrations.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural landmarks and attractions.
  • Disrespect the locals, their culture, or iconography.
  • Traffic and/or park illegally.

Getting to Perth Amboy: Transportation Options and Logistics

Perth Amboy is located about 35 miles south of Manhattan and is easily accessible by car, train, bus, or plane.

Airports Near Perth Amboy

  • Newark Liberty International Airport: Located 17 miles north of Perth Amboy and served by major airlines.
  • John F. Kennedy International Airport: Located 38 miles north of Perth Amboy and served by major airlines.
  • LaGuardia Airport: Located 34 miles north of Perth Amboy and served by major airlines.

Bus Stations Near Perth Amboy

  • Perth Amboy Bus Terminal: Located in downtown Perth Amboy and served by NJ Transit buses to New York City and other nearby destinations.

Other Options

  • Train: The New Jersey Transit train station is located on the northeast side of town. A direct train to New York Penn Station takes 40 minutes.
  • Rideshare Services: Car rental and rideshare services are also available in Perth Amboy.

How to Get Around in Perth Amboy

Perth Amboy is a relatively small city that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. There are also several taxi, rideshare, and car rental services available for those who prefer to travel by car.

Where to Go Next from Perth Amboy: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Perth Amboy is located near several other destinations in New Jersey and the surrounding area, including:

  • New York City: Just a 35-mile drive from Perth Amboy, offering a wide range of cultural attractions, shopping, and dining.
  • Atlantic City: Located about 120 miles south of Perth Amboy, offering world-class casinos, beaches, and nightlife.
  • Philadelphia: Located about 80 miles southwest of Perth Amboy, offering a wealth of American history and culture, including Independence Hall and the Liberty Bell.

Conclusion

Perth Amboy is a hidden gem in Middlesex County, New Jersey, offering visitors a rich mix of history, culture, outdoor recreation, and dining. Whether you are looking for a day trip or a week-long vacation, Perth Amboy has something for everyone. Plan your trip in advance, explore the city’s diverse neighborhoods and attractions, and experience the charm and hospitality of this historic American city.
