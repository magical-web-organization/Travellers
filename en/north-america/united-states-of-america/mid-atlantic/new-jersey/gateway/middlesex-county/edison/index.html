Introduction to Edison

Edison is a vibrant township located in Middlesex County, in the Gateway region of New Jersey. With a population of over 100,000 residents, Edison is the fifth-largest municipality in New Jersey. This diverse and multicultural town offers visitors an array of attractions, ranging from cultural and historical landmarks to natural parks and outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Edison

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Edison Tower - This 131-foot tower is a tribute to Thomas Edison, who had his laboratory in Menlo Park. The tower provides a scenic view of the Raritan River and is located in Edison State Park.

  2. Thomas Edison Center - The center commemorates the famous inventor and displays some of his inventions, including early phonographs and light bulbs. This is a must-visit for anyone interested in technology and history.

  3. Roosevelt Park - The park is home to the beautiful Menlo Park Museum, which displays Edison’s early inventions and letters. In addition, the park provides visitors with bike trails, fishing, and picnic areas.

Natural Attractions of Edison: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Dismal Swamp Preserve - A 650-acre preserve that offers hiking, fishing, bird-watching and nature trails. The preserve is home to a range of flora and fauna and is an excellent escape from the hustle and bustle of the city.

  2. Merrill Park - The Park has an abundance of walking paths, playgrounds, and nature trails. With a lake, a picnic area, and expansive lawns, this park has something for everyone.

  3. Raritan River Greenway - The Greenway is a 600-acre natural oasis that offers walking, biking, fishing, and bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Edison

  1. Edison Greenway - A 3.6-mile path that winds through the township, offering scenic views of natural parks and commercial landmarks.

  2. Rutgers Ecological Preserve - The Preserve has over 300 acres of pristine forests, fields, and wetlands. The preserve offers a range of hiking and biking trails that offer unique views of the natural surroundings.

  3. Metuchen Greenway - The greenway passes through Metuchen and Edison. The 3.5-mile long path is popular among bird-watchers and hikers.

Best Things to Do in Edison: Activities and Tours

  1. Escape Rooms - Edison is famous for its numerous escape rooms. Visitors can try their luck at trivia and puzzles and challenge their mental abilities.

  2. Color Me Mine - The pottery studio allows visitors to unleash their artistic abilities and create one-of-a-kind ceramic pieces.

  3. Speed Raceway- The thrilling activity center features high-speed go-kart racing and arcade games that are sure to get your adrenaline pumping.

Food and Drink Scene of Edison

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Italian Hot Dogs - A cross between a hot dog and a sandwich, this is a must-try dish that Edison is famous for.

  2. Thali - A meal consisting of rice, curry, and other delicacies that are a traditional favorite in Indian cuisine.

  3. Boba Tea - A popular Taiwanese drink that consists of tea, milk, and tapioca pearls. This refreshing drink can be found in several shops around Edison.

Local Beverages

  1. Carton Brewing - This local brewery is famous for its craft beers and offers an array of unique flavors.

  2. Cypress Brewing Company - A local brewery that offers an extensive selection of seasonal beers and chili-infused flavors.

  3. JR’s Bar & Grill - Known for its extensive drink menu and live music, this is an excellent spot for nightlife aficionados.

Where to Stay in Edison: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Courtyard Edison Woodbridge - The hotel offers well-appointed rooms, an indoor pool, and a fitness center.

  2. Fairfield Inn & Suites Edison-South Plainfield - The hotel provides guests with spacious rooms and a friendly staff.

  3. Hilton Garden Inn Edison/Raritan Center - The hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms, a pool, gym, and an on-site restaurant.

What to Buy in Edison: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Indian jewelry and clothing - Edison is home to numerous Indian stores, offering traditional and contemporary clothing and jewelry.

  2. Homemade candles - Local artisans make handmade candles with a range of scents and designs.

  3. Toll House Cookie Mix - The Toll House chocolate chip cookie mix originated in the town of Whitman and is a popular souvenir among tourists visiting Edison.

Local Culture and Customs of Edison

Edison is a township with a diverse and multicultural population, shaped by various ethnic backgrounds. Visitors are likely to experience a rich tapestry of traditions and customs, which combine to create a unique and vibrant culture.

Best Time to Visit Edison

The best time to visit Edison is during the summer months, between June and August, when the weather is sunny, and outdoor activities can be enjoyed.

Safety and Health

Safety in Edison

Edison is generally a safe township, but it is always advisable to take precautions when traveling. Visitors are advised not to travel alone at night and to be aware of pickpockets and other petty crimes.

Health in Edison

Edison is home to several hospitals and clinics that provide medical care to residents and visitors.

Getting to Edison: Transportation Options and Logistics

Where to Go Next from Edison: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  2. Jersey Shore - The beautiful beaches of the Jersey Shore are located about an hour’s drive from Edison.

  3. Atlantic City - Visitors can enjoy the excitement and glamour of Atlantic City, located about two hours’ drive from Edison.

Conclusion

Edison is a vibrant township in New Jersey that offers visitors an array of attractions, cultural landmarks, natural parks, and outdoor activities. With its diverse and multicultural community, unique cuisine, and friendly hospitality, Edison is an excellent destination for anyone visiting the Mid-Atlantic region.
