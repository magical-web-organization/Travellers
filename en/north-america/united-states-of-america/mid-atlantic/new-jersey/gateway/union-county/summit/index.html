Introduction to Summit

Summit, located in Union County, is a charming city situated in the Gateway Region of New Jersey. This small town boasts a welcoming community with top-rated schools, quaint neighborhoods, and a bustling downtown area filled with shops, restaurants, and historical landmarks.

List of Places to Visit in Summit

Summit is a delightful destination for tourists who want to explore its culture, history, and natural wonders. Here are some of the top places to visit in Summit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Summit Playhouse

Established in 1918, the Summit Playhouse is a beautifully restored historic building that has been an active community theatre group for more than nine decades. Visitors can enjoy live performances of classic plays and musicals throughout the year.

Reeves-Reed Arboretum

The Reeves-Reed Arboretum is a stunning 13.5-acre oasis of rolling lawns, woods, and colorful gardens. It features more than 700 types of trees and plants, and visitors can take a relaxed hike or stroll around the property, enjoy a picnic, or attend events and programs.

Summit Historical Society

The Summit Historical Society, located in the former home of the city’s founding family, is dedicated to preserving the history of Summit and the surrounding area. Visitors can tour the Society’s museum, which features exhibits on Summit’s past, present, and future.

Natural Attractions of Summit: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Watchung Reservation

The Watchung Reservation is a 2,000-acre park that spans several townships in Union County. This beautiful wilderness area features miles of trails for hiking, biking, and horseback riding, as well as picnic areas, playgrounds, and wildlife observation points.

Briant Park

Briant Park is a lovely park in Summit, stretching across 96 acres and featuring a beautiful pond, a lake, and a playground area. Visitors can hike, bike or picnic in the park, or enjoy outdoor concerts and various events held throughout the year.

Hidden Valley Park

Hidden Valley Park is a 38-acre park located in nearby New Providence. This tranquil spot offers visitors the chance to hike, bike, or simply meditate in the peaceful surroundings. The park also features a small pond perfect for fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Summit

Summit is a popular destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the top hiking and biking routes in Summit include:

  • The Lenape Trail: This 34-mile trail is ideal for those looking for a more challenging hiking experience.
  • The Overlook Trail: This 10-mile trail features breathtaking views of the surrounding area.
  • The Watchung Reservation Trail: This trail is perfect for a leisurely walk or bike ride, offering scenic views of the forest and wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Summit: Activities and Tours

Summit offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors, including:

  • The Summit Farmers Market: Held every Sunday in the summer months, this market features locally sourced produce, crafts, and other handmade goods.
  • The Summit Downtown Walking Tour: Visitors can take a guided tour of Summit’s downtown area and learn about the city’s history and architecture.
  • The Summit Wine and Food Festival: This annual event showcases the best local wines and cuisine, featuring culinary demonstrations and tastings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Summit

If you have a week to spend in Summit, here is a suggested itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Summit

Summit’s food and drink scene is not to be missed. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Summit Diner: This classic American diner offers a wide range of dishes, including the classic burger and fries, Philly Cheesesteak, and Greek salad.
  • Winberie’s Restaurant and Bar: This local institution serves up delicious comfort food, such as chicken pot pie, buffalo wings, and fish and chips.

Local Beverages

  • Devil’s Juice Hard Cider: This refreshing cider is made from locally grown apples and is a must-try for cider enthusiasts.
  • Tito’s Handmade Vodka: This award-winning vodka is locally produced in Summit and is a staple at many local bars and restaurants.

Where to Stay in Summit: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Summit offers a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and taste. Some of the best places to stay in Summit include:

  • The Grand Summit Hotel: Built in 1929, this charming hotel offers guests a taste of old-world luxury with modern amenities.
  • DeBary Inn: This elegant bed and breakfast is housed in a historic mansion and offers cozy and spacious rooms.
  • Extended Stay America: This affordable hotel offers comfortable and well-equipped rooms for longer stays.

What to Buy in Summit: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Summit is a great place to find unique souvenirs and locally made products. Some of the best items to look for include:

  • Handmade jewelry from local artisans at the Summit Farmers Market
  • Summit t-shirts or hats from local shops and boutiques
  • Locally sourced honey and maple syrup from farmers markets and specialty stores

Local Culture and Customs of Summit

Summit is a welcoming and friendly community with a strong sense of local pride. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and to engage with the community.

Best Time to Visit Summit

The best time to visit Summit is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and the city is buzzing with activity. Fall can also be a great time to visit, as the leaves change colors and the weather cools down.

Do’s and Don’ts of Summit

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Summit:

  • Explore the parks and natural attractions
  • Attend local events and festivals
  • Try local cuisine and beverages

  • Disrespect the local customs and culture

Safety and Health

Summit is a relatively safe city, with low crime rates and a welcoming community. Visitors should still take precautions such as staying aware of their surroundings and not leaving valuables in plain sight.

Safety in Summit

  • Avoid walking alone at night in dimly lit or deserted areas
  • Keep an eye on your belongings in public places

Health in Summit

  • Always pack necessary prescription medicines
  • Stay hydrated and wear sunscreen when participating in outdoor activities during summer months.

Getting to Summit: Transportation Options and Logistics

Summit is easily accessible by several modes of transportation.

Airports Near Summit

  • Newark Liberty International Airport: This airport is located 14 miles east of Summit.

Bus Stations Near Summit

  • Summit Train Station: located in the downtown area, offers easy commuting options within Summit and NYC

Other Options

  • Summit is conveniently located near Interstate 78, making it easy to reach by car.

How to Get Around in Summit

Summit offers several options for getting around, including buses, taxis, bikes, and on foot. Visitors can use the Summit Green Shuttle Service, Uber, or Lyft for convenient and affordable transportation.

Where to Go Next from Summit: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Summit is located in close proximity to several other exciting destinations in the region, including:

  • New York City: Located just 30 miles east of Summit and can be reached by car or train in less than an hour.
  • Jersey Shore: With only a 90-minute drive, visitors can spend a weekend away, lounging on the beach, and enjoying fresh seafood.

Conclusion

Summit is a welcoming and charming city with a rich history, stunning natural beauty, and a vibrant local culture. This comprehensive travel guide provides all the information you need to plan your trip and make the most of your time in Summit. With so much to see and do, Summit is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking a unique and memorable experience.
