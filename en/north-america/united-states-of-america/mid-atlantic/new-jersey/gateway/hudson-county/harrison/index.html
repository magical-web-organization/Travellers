Introduction to Harrison

Located in Hudson County, New Jersey, Harrison is a small town with a lot of charm. This place has a rich history that is reflected in its museums and landmarks. Harrison is also home to some of the best outdoor adventures, scenic views, and delicious cuisines. Whether you are a history buff, an outdoors enthusiast, or a foodie, Harrison has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Harrison

Here is a list of top places that you can visit in Harrison:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Harrison & East Newark Heritage Center: This museum showcases the history and culture of Harrison and East Newark through artifacts, documents, and photographs.
  • Newark Museum of Art: Located just a few miles away from Harrison, this museum is home to a vast collection of American, Asian, African, and Classical art.

Natural Attractions of Harrison: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Frank E. Rodgers Boulevard Parks: This park offers a wide range of recreational activities in its five-acre space, including basketball courts, playgrounds, and a picnic area.
  • Branch Brook Park: This park is located in the neighboring town of Newark and is home to the largest collection of cherry blossom trees in the United States.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Harrison

  • Riverfront Walkway: This trail spans more than 22 miles and runs through Harrison, providing picturesque views of Manhattan’s skyline.
  • Harrison Heritage Trail: This trail is a perfect way to explore Harrison’s history and culture while enjoying a scenic walk.

Best Things to Do in Harrison: Activities and Tours

  • Red Bull Arena: This arena is home to the New York Red Bulls and is a must-visit for soccer enthusiasts.
  • Cape Liberty Cruise Port: If you’re in Harrison for a short time, a cruise ship tour from Cape Liberty Cruise Port is a great way to spend your day.

Week-Long Itinerary for Harrison

Day 1: Visit the Harrison & East Newark Heritage Center, followed by a walk on the Harrison Heritage Trail.

Day 2: Take a short trip to Newark and explore the Newark Museum of Art.

Day 3: Have a picnic at Frank E. Rodgers Boulevard Parks and enjoy a sunset view on the Riverfront Walkway.

Day 4: Embark on a cherry blossom picnic at Branch Brook Park.

Day 5: Head to Red Bull Arena to catch a soccer game.

Day 6: Indulge in a food tour around Harrison and enjoy some local delicacies.

Day 7: Relax at Cape Liberty Cruise Port and take a scenic tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Harrison

Must-Try Dishes

  • Taylor Ham Sandwich: A signature dish of Jersey. It is a pork roll served with egg and cheese on a bagel or roll.
  • Pizza: Grab a slice of this New Jersey staple at your local pizzeria. Harrison is famous for its delicious pizzas.

Local Beverages

  • Apple Cider: New Jersey is known for its apple orchards. Harrison’s local apple cider is a must-have.
  • Craft Beer: Harrison is dotted with some of the best breweries in New Jersey that provide a range of craft beers.

Where to Stay in Harrison: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some of the best places to stay in Harrison.

  • Element Harrison/Newark: A premium hotel; perfect for a luxury stay.
  • Harrison ApartaHotel: An affordable yet comfortable hotel with essential amenities.

What to Buy in Harrison: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Jersey T-Shirts and Hats: Bring home a classic souvenir from your trip to Harrison.
  • Local crafts: Purchase some local crafts at the Harrison & East Newark Heritage Center.

Local Culture and Customs of Harrison

Harrison is a diverse community with a mix of cultures. The locals are friendly and welcoming. The best way to immerse yourself in Harrison’s culture is to take a food tour, explore the museums, and meet the locals.

Best Time to Visit Harrison

The best time to visit Harrison is during the fall and spring months. The cherry blossom season at Branch Brook Park is an excellent time to visit Harrison.

Do’s and Don’ts of Harrison

Do:

  • Have a slice of local pizza.
  • Enjoy a Red Bulls soccer game.
  • Visit the Harrison & East Newark Heritage Center.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the parks and open spaces.
  • Enter into restricted areas without prior permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Harrison

Harrison is a safe town with low crime rates. However, it is advisable to take some precautions while travelling, such as keeping your belongings safe and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Harrison

It is essential to have travel health insurance when travelling to Harrison. If you need medical assistance, the nearby cities of Newark and New York have some of the best medical facilities.

Getting to Harrison: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Harrison

  • Newark Liberty International Airport: This airport is located ten miles away from Harrison.
  • John F. Kennedy International Airport: This airport is located 21 miles away from Harrison.

Bus Stations Near Harrison

  • Harrison PATH Station: This station offers convenient transport to the nearby cities of Newark, Jersey City, and New York City.
  • Newark Penn Station: This station has Amtrak, NJ Transit, and PATH trains that run to various locations.

Other Options

  • Car rental: You can rent a car from the nearby car rental agencies and explore Harrison and nearby places at your own pace.
  • Taxi or rideshare services: Uber, Lyft, and airport taxis are readily available in Harrison.

How to Get Around in Harrison

Harrison is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. If you prefer to drive, car rental services are available.

Where to Go Next from Harrison: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Newark: This bustling city is a 15-minute drive away and home to some of the best museums, restaurants, and shopping destinations.
  • New York City: The city that never sleeps is just a 20-minute train ride away from Harrison. Explore the iconic landmarks, museums, and cultural events.

Conclusion

Harrison offers a mix of history, culture, and outdoor adventures. With this comprehensive tour guide, you can explore the town’s best places, cuisines, and traditions. So, pack your bags and get ready for an unforgettable trip to Harrison!
