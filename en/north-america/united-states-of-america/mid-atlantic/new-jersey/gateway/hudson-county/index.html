Introduction to Hudson County

Located in the heart of Gateway, Hudson County is a charming destination that offers a mix of historical landmarks, outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and vibrant nightlife. The county comprises several cities and towns, including Bayonne, Jersey City, Hoboken, Union City, West New York, and North Bergen, each with its own unique character and charm.

With its proximity to New York City, Hudson County is a popular destination for travelers seeking a quieter, more affordable, and less crowded alternative to the Big Apple. Whether you’re interested in exploring the county’s rich history, admiring stunning waterfront views, or indulging in delicious cuisine, Hudson County has something for everyone.

In this travel guide, we’ll provide you with a comprehensive list of the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and practical travel information to help you plan your trip to Hudson County.

List of Places to Visit in Hudson County

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hudson County is steeped in history and culture, and there are plenty of museums and landmarks that showcase the county’s rich heritage. Here are some of the must-visit cultural attractions in Hudson County:

  • The Statue of Liberty National Monument: Located on Liberty Island, the Statue of Liberty is one of the most iconic landmarks in America and a symbol of freedom and democracy. Visitors can take a ferry to Liberty Island to see the statue up close and explore the museum and observation deck.

  • Ellis Island National Museum of Immigration: Located next to the Statue of Liberty, Ellis Island is a museum that tells the story of America’s immigrant history. Visitors can explore the exhibits, take a guided tour, and trace their family’s ancestry.

  • Central Railroad of New Jersey Terminal: Located in Jersey City, the Central Railroad of New Jersey Terminal is a historic train station that served as a major point of entry for immigrants coming to America in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. Today, the terminal is a museum that showcases the station’s history and features exhibits on immigration and transportation.

  • Liberty Science Center: Located in Jersey City, the Liberty Science Center is a hands-on museum that focuses on technology, science, and innovation. Visitors can participate in interactive exhibits, see live demonstrations, and watch 3D movies.

  • The Morris Canal Greenway: Stretching across several cities in Hudson County, the Morris Canal Greenway is a trail that runs along the historic Morris Canal and showcases some of the county’s most scenic views. Visitors can take a leisurely walk, jog, or bike ride and admire the natural beauty of the area.

Natural Attractions of Hudson County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hudson County also boasts several natural attractions that offer outdoor adventures and breathtaking views. Here are some of the best natural attractions in Hudson County:

  • Liberty State Park: Located in Jersey City, Liberty State Park is a vast public park that offers stunning views of the Statue of Liberty and the Manhattan skyline. Visitors can rent a bike, take a walk along the waterfront, have a picnic, or take a boat tour to the Statue of Liberty.

  • Hudson River Waterfront Walkway: Stretching along the Hudson River from Bayonne to the George Washington Bridge, the Hudson River Waterfront Walkway is a pedestrian trail that offers panoramic views of the Manhattan skyline and the Hudson River.

  • Weehawken Waterfront Park and Recreation Center: Located in Weehawken, the Weehawken Waterfront Park and Recreation Center is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The park features a fishing pier, picnic areas, walking paths, and stunning views of the Hudson River and the Manhattan skyline.

  • Hackensack RiverWalk: Stretching along the Hackensack River from Jersey City to Kearny, the Hackensack RiverWalk is a scenic pedestrian and bike path that offers stunning views of the river and the surrounding wetlands.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hudson County

Hudson County offers several hiking and biking routes that allow visitors to explore the county’s natural beauty. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Hudson County:

  • The Morris Canal Greenway: As mentioned earlier, the Morris Canal Greenway is a scenic trail that showcases some of Hudson County’s most stunning views. The trail is suitable for both hiking and biking.

  • The Hudson River Waterfront Walkway: The Hudson River Waterfront Walkway is a pedestrian trail that offers several options for hiking and biking. Visitors can choose to walk or bike the entire route or pick a segment that suits their fitness level.

  • Palisades Interstate Park: Located on the New Jersey side of the Hudson River, Palisades Interstate Park is a vast park that offers several hiking and biking trails with stunning views of the Hudson River and the Manhattan skyline.

Best Things to Do in Hudson County: Activities and Tours

In addition to cultural attractions and natural wonders, there are plenty of things to do in Hudson County. Here are some of the best activities and tours to try:

  • Food Tours: Hudson County boasts a vibrant food scene that reflects the county’s diverse cultural heritage. Visitors can take a food tour to sample some of the county’s best dishes and learn about the local cuisine.

  • Kayaking and Canoeing: With its many waterways, Hudson County is an excellent place for kayaking and canoeing. Visitors can rent a kayak or canoe and explore the Hudson River and the county’s many wetlands.

  • Art Galleries and Studios: Hudson County is home to many talented artists and creative spaces. Visitors can explore the county’s art galleries, studios, and art centers to view and purchase art from local artists.

Food and Drink Scene of Hudson County

Hudson County boasts a diverse culinary scene that reflects the county’s cultural and ethnic diversity. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Hudson County:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pizza: Hudson County is known for its delicious, thin-crust pizza. Some of the best pizza places in the county include Razza Pizza Artigianale, Grimaldi’s Pizzeria, and Lombardi’s Coal Oven Pizza.

  • Hot Dogs: Hudson County is also famous for its hot dogs. Visitors can try local favorites like the classic hot dog with sauerkraut and mustard at Hot Grill in Clifton, or the chili dog at Johnny and Hanges in Paterson.

  • Seafood: With its proximity to the Hudson River and the Atlantic Ocean, Hudson County is an excellent place to sample fresh seafood. Visitors can try dishes like crab cakes, lobster rolls, and clam chowder at seafood restaurants like Blue Water Seafood, Dino & Harry’s, and Ed’s Lobster Bar.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Hudson County is home to several craft breweries, including Departed Soles Brewing Co., Hoboken Brewing Company, and Jersey City Brewing Company.

  • Coffee: Visitors can sample artisanal coffee at local coffee shops like Hidden Grounds, Bwe Kafe, and Modcup Coffee.

Where to Stay in Hudson County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hudson County offers a range of accommodation options to suit all tastes and budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay in Hudson County:

  • Westin Jersey City Newport: Located in Jersey City, the Westin Jersey City Newport is a luxury hotel with stunning views of the Manhattan skyline, comfortable rooms, and excellent amenities like a spa, fitness center, and on-site restaurant.

  • Hyatt Regency Jersey City: Also located in Jersey City, the Hyatt Regency Jersey City is another excellent option for travelers seeking luxury accommodations. The hotel features spacious rooms, a spa, a rooftop bar, and panoramic views of the Hudson River and the Manhattan skyline.

  • The Holland Hotel: Located in Jersey City, The Holland Hotel is a budget-friendly option that offers clean and comfortable rooms, free Wi-Fi, and a continental breakfast.

  • Jersey City Hostel: Situated in Jersey City, the Jersey City Hostel is a hostel that offers affordable dormitory-style accommodations, free Wi-Fi, a shared kitchen, and a common area.

What to Buy in Hudson County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Hudson County offers plenty of souvenirs and local products that make excellent gifts for friends and family back home. Here are some of the best things to buy in Hudson County:

  • Local Art: Hudson County is home to many talented artists, and visitors can purchase artwork, prints, and crafts from local galleries and markets.

  • Hot Sauce: Many restaurants in Hudson County make their own hot sauce, which makes an excellent souvenir or gift.

  • Craft Beer: Hudson County is home to several craft breweries that produce some of the best beer in the region. Visitors can purchase craft beer from local breweries like Departed Soles Brewing Co. and Jersey City Brewing Company.

Local Culture and Customs of Hudson County

Hudson County has a rich cultural heritage that reflects the county’s diverse population. From food to music to art, visitors can experience a blend of different cultures and customs in Hudson County. Some of the cultural events and festivals in Hudson County include the Jersey City Puerto Rican Day Parade, Hoboken Arts and Music Festival, and West New York Cuban Day Parade.

Best Time to Visit Hudson County

The best time to visit Hudson County is from late spring to early fall when the weather is mild and pleasant. During this time, visitors can enjoy outdoor activities, festivals, and events. However, the peak season can also be crowded, so visitors should book their accommodations and activities in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hudson County

Do:

  • Try Local Cuisine: Hudson County offers some of the best food in the region. Visitors should take advantage of the diverse culinary scene and try local dishes and drinks.

  • Take a Walking Tour: Hudson County has plenty of landmarks and historical sites that are best explored on foot. Visitors can take a walking tour to learn about the county’s history and architecture.

  • Be Respectful: Hudson County has a diverse population, and visitors should be respectful of the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Visitors should be mindful of the environment and dispose of their trash properly.

  • Disrespect Historic Sites: Hudson County has several historic sites and landmarks that should be treated with respect. Visitors should not deface or damage these sites.

  • Overpack: Visitors should pack light and only bring essential items to avoid carrying heavy luggage.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hudson County

Hudson County is generally safe for visitors, but like any urban area, visitors should be mindful of their surroundings and take precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. Visitors should avoid walking alone at night in secluded areas and should keep their valuables out of sight.

Health in Hudson County

Visitors to Hudson County should take precautions to protect themselves from common illnesses like the flu and the coronavirus. Visitors should wash their hands regularly, wear a mask in public places, and avoid close contact with sick people.

Getting to Hudson County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Hudson County is easily accessible from New York City and other parts of the region. Here are some transportation options to get to and around Hudson County:

Airports Near Hudson County

  • Newark Liberty International Airport: Located in Newark, NJ, Newark Liberty International Airport is the closest airport to Hudson County.

  • LaGuardia Airport: Located in Queens, NY, LaGuardia Airport is another convenient option for travelers heading to Hudson County.

  • John F. Kennedy International Airport: Located in Queens, NY, John F. Kennedy International Airport is a larger airport that’s farther from Hudson County.

Bus Stations Near Hudson County

  • Port Authority Bus Terminal: Located in Manhattan, the Port Authority Bus Terminal serves several bus companies that offer service to and from Hudson County.

  • Journal Square Transportation Center: Located in Jersey City, the Journal Square Transportation Center serves several bus routes that connect to other parts of Hudson County.

Other Options

  • Train: Hudson County is served by several train stations, including Hoboken Terminal, Exchange Place Station, and Journal Square Station.

  • Ferry: Visitors can take a ferry from Manhattan or other parts of the region to several destinations in Hudson County, including Liberty Island, Ellis Island, and Jersey City.

How to Get Around in Hudson County

Hudson County is easily navigable by public transportation. Here are some transportation options to get around Hudson County:

  • Light Rail: Hudson County is served by the Hudson-Bergen Light Rail, which connects several cities and towns in the county.

  • Bus: Hudson County is served by several bus routes that connect to other parts of the region.

  • Taxi and Ride-Share: Visitors can use taxi or ride-share services like Uber and Lyft to get around Hudson County.

Where to Go Next from Hudson County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hudson County is a great base for exploring other parts of New Jersey and the surrounding areas. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Jersey Shore: Located less than two hours from Hudson County, the Jersey Shore is a popular destination for beachgoers and sun-seekers. Visitors can enjoy swimming, sunbathing, and water sports activities.

  • The Catskills: Located about three hours from Hudson County, the Catskills offer stunning natural beauty and outdoor adventures like hiking, skiing, and camping.

  • Philadelphia: Located about two hours from Hudson County, Philadelphia is a vibrant city that’s famous for its history, culture, and food scene. Visitors can explore popular attractions like the Liberty Bell, Independence Hall, and the Philadelphia Museum of Art.
