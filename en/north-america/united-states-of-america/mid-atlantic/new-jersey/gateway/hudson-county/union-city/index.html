Introduction to Union City

Union City, located in Hudson County, Gateway, is a hidden gem in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States. With a rich cultural heritage and surrounded by stunning natural beauty, this small New Jersey city is a perfect destination for travelers seeking a unique experience. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take a closer look at the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Union City.

List of Places to Visit in Union City

Whether your interests lie in cultural attractions, natural beauty, or outdoor adventures, Union City has something for everyone. Here are some of the must-visit places in Union City:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Union City Museum: Exhibiting the city’s rich history since its inception.
  • Hoboken Historical Museum: Located nearby, features exhibitions about the city and the historical events that happened in the area.
  • Ellis Island National Museum of Immigration: The museum houses in memory of those who have been through Ellis Island Gate and the millions of immigrants that came through it.

Natural Attractions of Union City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Palisades Interstate Park: A popular natural attraction and the perfect place for a hike or picnic.
  • Weehawken Waterfront Park and Recreation Center: This park features stunning views of the New York City skyline and is a great place for a leisurely stroll or jog.
  • Kennedy Boulevard: A scenic route on which one can see the views of the city all the way from southern to northern Hudson County.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Union City

  • North Hudson Braddock Park: Part of the Palisades Interstate Park system, where visitors can bike around a lake, primarily flat with a few climbs.
  • Union City Reservoir: A hidden gem of a lake, has mostly flat trails around it. Great for an early morning jog or a quiet picnic with stellar scenic views around.

Best Things to Do in Union City: Activities and Tours

  • Explore Bergenline Ave: One of the busiest streets in New Jersey, it is a great way to experience the city. Full of little shops and restaurants that will take your breath away, make sure to walk around and eat.
  • Walking tour of Weehawken Waterfront: Offers scenic views of the New York City skyline, the Hudson River, and the tranquil Hudson County Park.
  • Galaxy Towers: A high-rise luxury apartment complex that offers stunning Manhattan views and an outdoor pool.

Week-Long Itinerary for Union City

For those looking to spend a week in Union City, here is a sample itinerary:

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore Bergenline Ave
Day 2 Visit Palisades Interstate Park
Day 3 Visit Union City Museum
Day 4 Take a walking tour of Weehawken Waterfront
Day 5 Explore Ellis Island National Museum of Immigration
Day 6 Relax at Union City Reservoir
Day 7 Head to Galaxy Towers for views and a swim

Food and Drink Scene of Union City

One of the best ways to experience a new place is through the local cuisine. Union City is no exception, with plenty of options to tantalize your taste buds. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Union City:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Colombian-style empanadas
  • Peruvian-style rotisserie chicken with fresh green sauce
  • Cuban-style pork dish.

Local Beverages

  • Mate, a traditional Argentinian beverage made from steeping the dried leaves of the yerba mate plant.
  • Café con leche, a Cuban-originated coffee beverage that has a shot of espresso mixed with milk.

Where to Stay in Union City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Sheraton Lincoln Harbor Hotel, offers stunning scenic views and deluxe amenities.
  • Marriott Courtyard Jersey City Newport, Recently renovated, this trendy hotel has panoramic views of Manhattan.

What to Buy in Union City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • A traditional ‘guitar’ with local paint and detailed iconography that tells a story.
  • Salsa music CDs from local artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Union City

Union City is a melting pot of different cultures, and it’s reflected in the city’s customs and traditions. The city celebrates several festivals throughout the year, including the Hispanic Heritage Festival, as well as Puerto Rican and Ecuadorian Street Fairs.

Best Time to Visit Union City

The best time to visit Union City is during the spring and fall months, when the weather is mild, and the city is not overwhelmed with tourists. During this time, you can enjoy the natural beauty of the city without having to battle the crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Union City

Here are some of the do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Union City:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine
  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Visit the city’s museums and historical attractions

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the city’s traditions and customs
  • Litter and damage the natural beauty of the city.

Safety and Health

Safety in Union City

Union City is generally a safe destination for tourists. However, as with any city, it is always important to be aware of your surroundings and exercise caution when walking alone at night.

Health in Union City

The city has an extensive medical network with major hospitals and medical facilities readily available throughout the city.

Getting to Union City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Union City

Newark Liberty International Airport is the nearest international airport to Union City, located just a few miles away.

Bus Stations Near Union City

  • George Washington Bridge Bus Station, operates buses to points throughout New York State, New Jersey, and the Northeast.
  • Port Authority Bus Terminal, offers service to destinations throughout the Tri-State area.

Other Options

  • Taxis and car services are readily available throughout the city.

How to Get Around Union City

Walking and biking are the best ways to get around Union City, as the city is compact and easy to navigate. If you need to travel further afield, taxis and car rental services are available.

Where to Go Next from Union City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • New York City: Located just across the Hudson River, New York City is a must-visit destination for travelers to the area.
  • Ocean Grove: A peaceful seaside town just a short drive to the south, featuring boutique shops and quaint restaurants.

Conclusion

Union City is a unique and vibrant travel destination that offers something for everyone. From its rich cultural heritage to its stunning natural beauty, this New Jersey city is a perfect destination for travelers seeking a unique experience. With so much to see and do, there has never been a better time to plan a trip to Union City.
