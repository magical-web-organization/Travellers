Introduction to Allegany County (New York)

Allegany County is a hidden gem in Southern Tier, New York. It is a perfect destination for those who want to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and explore nature and history. It is known for its scenic beauty, diverse wildlife, and rich history.

In this travel guide, we will highlight all the must-visit places in Allegany County and provide useful information to help you plan your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Allegany County (New York)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Cuba Historical Society

Located in Cuba, this museum offers insight into the county’s rich history through exhibits that feature artifacts, photographs, and storytelling.

Allegany County History Center

This museum in Belmont provides an in-depth look into the county’s history and features a wide range of artifacts, including military memorabilia and antique toys.

Natural Attractions of Allegany County (New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Allegheny River

If you love water adventures, the Allegheny River is a must-visit destination. You can enjoy kayaking, fishing, or boating on the river, which features stunning views of the surrounding hills and forests.

Letchworth State Park

Known as the “Grand Canyon of the East,” Letchworth State Park is one of the most popular natural attractions in New York. The park boasts three magnificent waterfalls and offers opportunities for hiking, kayaking, and birdwatching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Allegany County (New York)

Genesee Valley Greenway

This 90-mile trail connects Allegany County to Monroe County and offers breathtaking views of the Genesee River Valley.

Pfeiffer Nature Center

The Pfeiffer Nature Center features over 600 acres of natural beauty, including forests, waterfalls, and wetlands. It has several trails for hiking and biking.

Best Things to Do in Allegany County (New York): Activities and Tours

Balloons Over Letchworth

Take a relaxing hot air balloon ride over Letchworth State Park and enjoy the stunning views of the park from above.

Southern Tier Balloon Tours

Southern Tier Balloon Tours offers hot air balloon rides over Allegany County. You can take a sunrise or sunset flight and admire the scenic views of the countryside.

Food and Drink Scene of Allegany County (New York)

Must-Try Dishes

Beef on Weck

This sandwich originated in Western New York and is made with roast beef on a kummelweck roll. It is a favorite among locals and visitors alike.

Bison Burger

Allegany County is known for its bison farms, and a bison burger is a must-try dish. It is a lean and flavorful alternative to traditional beef burgers.

Local Beverages

Craft Beer

Allegany County is home to several breweries that offer a wide range of craft beers. The most popular ones include Four Mile Brewing and Ellicottville Brewing Company.

Apple Cider

Allegany County is known for its apple orchards, and the fresh apple cider is a must-try beverage. You can find it at the local farmers’ markets and cider mills.

Where to Stay in Allegany County (New York): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

River Spring Lodge

This luxury lodge in Darien provides an upscale and cozy experience. It features spacious rooms, a fireplace lounge, and an outdoor patio.

The Palmer House

This bed and breakfast in Alfred offers a charming and cozy experience. It features comfortable rooms, a well-manicured garden, and homemade breakfast.

What to Buy in Allegany County (New York): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Maple Syrup

Allegany County is known for its high-quality maple syrup. You can find it at local farmers’ markets and maple farms.

Handmade Crafts

Allegany County is home to many talented artisans, and you can find unique handmade crafts at local shops and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Allegany County (New York)

The local culture in Allegany County is deeply rooted in its history, agriculture, and nature. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and they take pride in their community.

Best Time to Visit Allegany County (New York)

The best time to visit Allegany County is during early fall, from September to October, when the foliage is at its peak. Spring and summer are also great seasons to visit, with mild temperatures and clear skies.

Do’s and Don’ts of Allegany County (New York)

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities and scenic views.
  • Try the local food and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Trespass or damage any natural areas.
  • Disrespect the locals or their culture.
  • Litter or damage any historical or cultural sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Allegany County (New York)

Allegany County is a safe destination overall. However, it is still important to take precautions, like locking your car and not leaving valuables in plain sight.

Health in Allegany County (New York)

Healthcare facilities are easily accessible in Allegany County, with several hospitals and clinics in the area. Make sure to bring any necessary medication and check with your doctor before traveling.

Getting to Allegany County (New York): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Allegany County (New York)

The closest major airports to Allegany County are Buffalo Niagara International Airport and Greater Rochester International Airport, both of which are about a two-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Allegany County (New York)

Greyhound and Trailways both have bus stations in Belmont, providing easy access to and from the county.

Other Options

Renting a car is the best option for exploring Allegany County and the surrounding areas. Taxi and ride-sharing services are also available in some areas.

How to Get Around in Allegany County (New York)

Driving is the best option for getting around Allegany County, as public transportation is limited. There are bike rentals available in some areas for those who prefer to explore on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Allegany County (New York): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Finger Lakes

The Finger Lakes region is just a short drive away from Allegany County and is known for its stunning lakes, wineries, and historic towns.

Niagara Falls

Niagara Falls is about a two-hour drive from Allegany County, and it is a must-visit destination for any New York traveler.

Conclusion

Allegany County is a hidden gem that offers a unique blend of nature, history, and culture. It is a perfect destination for those who want to escape the city and explore the countryside. With this guide, you can plan a trip to Allegany County with ease and make the most out of your visit.
