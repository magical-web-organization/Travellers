Introduction to Alfred (New York)

Located in Allegany County of New York’s Southern Tier, Alfred is a charming village that’s home to Alfred University and Alfred State College. With a population of fewer than 2,000 people, the village has a small-town feel with plenty of big-city amenities. Alfred boasts a rich history, a thriving arts scene, and beautiful natural surroundings. This guide will take you through all the essential information you need to know to plan a memorable trip to Alfred, New York.

List of Places to Visit in Alfred (New York)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Alfred Ceramic Art Museum

Located on the Alfred University campus, the Alfred Ceramic Art Museum is a must-visit for art lovers. This museum features an extensive collection of ceramics from renowned artists, including internationally acclaimed artists like Robert Arneson, Betty Woodman, and Adrian Saxe. The museum has over 8,000 objects on display, including contemporary ceramic art, historical pieces, and ancient pottery.

2. Scholes Library

Scholes Library is another highlight of the Alfred University campus. The library was built in 1965 and features a unique, mid-century modern design. It’s an excellent spot for anyone who loves to read or explore stunning architecture. In addition to its vast collection of books, Scholes Library also has a sculpture garden that’s worth checking out.

3. Almond Historical Society

The Almond Historical Society is located nearby Alfred and celebrates the history of the surrounding area. The society maintains a museum that showcases the history and culture of the village’s early settlers. Visitors can explore the museum’s exhibits and artifacts that transport them back to the 19th century.

Natural Attractions of Alfred (New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Alfred Farmers Market

The Alfred Farmers Market is an outdoor market that takes place every Sunday from June to October. Visitors can explore the various stalls that sell locally grown produce, homemade baked goods, artisanal crafts, and more. It’s an excellent opportunity to experience the local flavor and get a taste of the region’s agricultural heritage.

2. Kanakadea Park

Located just outside Alfred, Kanakadea Park is a popular spot for picnics, hiking, and taking in the scenic beauty of the area. The park has several hiking trails that range in difficulty levels, making it accessible to all visitors. The park also has a swimming area, playgrounds, and picnic tables.

3. Allen Lake State Forest

Allen Lake State Forest is a dense forest located just minutes away from Alfred. The forest features a beautiful lake that’s perfect for fishing and boating, as well as several hiking trails that offer breathtaking views of the natural scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alfred (New York)

The Alfred area boasts several hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • Finger Lakes Trail: This is a 580-mile hiking trail that runs from the Allegheny Mountains to the Catskill Mountains. A section of the trail runs through Alfred and offers hikers breathtaking views of the local scenery.

  • Genesee Valley Greenway: This is a 90-mile trail that runs through New York’s Southern Tier, including Alfred. The trail follows an abandoned railroad bed and offers a peaceful, scenic route for hikers and bikers.

Best Things to Do in Alfred (New York): Activities and Tours

There are plenty of fun activities and tours to choose from in Alfred, New York. Below are some of the best options:

  • Alfred Art Walk: This walking tour takes visitors through Alfred University’s campus and the village’s streets to view local artwork and architecture. It’s an excellent way to explore the town’s aesthetic side and learn about local culture.

  • Alfred Bike Rental: Visitors can rent bikes and explore the town’s surrounding areas’ natural beauty at their own pace. Rental shops have several bike options and can provide trail recommendations.

  • Hot Glass Show: Corning Museum of Glass’ Hot Glass Show is just a short drive from Alfred and is an absolute must-see for glass enthusiasts. Visitors can watch master glassblowers create stunning pieces while learning about the history and science of glassblowing.

Week-Long Itinerary for Alfred (New York)

Day 1: Arrival and check-in at the hotel. Day 2: Explore the Alfred Ceramic Art Museum and the Scholes Library on Alfred University’s campus. Day 3: Enjoy a scenic hike or bike ride along the Finger Lakes Trail. Day 4: Take a day trip to the Corning Museum of Glass and enjoy the Hot Glass Show. Day 5: Visit the Almond Historical Society and explore the village’s early settler’s history and culture. Day 6: Shop for local produce at the Alfred Farmers Market and have a picnic at Kanakadea Park. Day 7: Departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Alfred (New York)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Alfred State Truckstop Pie: This is a chocolate cream pie that’s made with graham cracker crust, whipped cream, and chocolate chips. It’s a specialty dessert that’s popular in the area.

  • The Jerk House’s Jerk Chicken: The Jerk House is a restaurant that serves Caribbean-style cuisine, with their Jerk Chicken being the standout dish. It’s a flavorful and spicy dish that’s perfect for anyone who loves bold flavors.

Local Beverages

  • Alfred Coffee: The area’s coffee shops serve a unique blend of coffee made with locally-sourced beans.

  • Local Wine: There are several vineyards in the region that produce high-quality wine varieties.

Where to Stay in Alfred (New York): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Alfred has several accommodation options that cater to different types of travelers. Here are some of the best options:

  • Alfred State College Guesthouse: A comfortable guest house that’s an ideal choice for budget travelers.

  • Alfred University’s Herrick Guest Suites: Herrick Guest Suites offer visitors a charming and comfortable stay in the heart of Alfred.

  • Microtel Inn & Suites by Wyndham: A three-star hotel that’s centrally located and offers comfortable rooms and amenities.

What to Buy in Alfred (New York): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Pottery: Alfred is known for its rich ceramic history, so it’s no surprise that pottery is a popular souvenir. Visitors can purchase locally-made ceramic pieces to bring home.

  • Local Honey and Maple Syrup: Both honey and maple syrup are some of the area’s top agricultural products, and visitors can purchase them from local farmers markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Alfred (New York)

Alfred is an artistically rich town with a thriving arts scene. Visitors should respect local creativity and treat the town’s artwork with care and reverence. Alfred University and Alfred State College are significant academic institutions, and visitors should give priority to students and staff when navigating around campus areas.

Best Time to Visit Alfred (New York)

The best time to visit Alfred is between May and October when the weather is mild, and the local produce is abundant. The fall months, in particular, are a breathtaking time to visit when the foliage is at its most beautiful. Winters can be harsh and frigid, with snow often accumulating significantly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alfred (New York)

Do:

  • Explore the local arts scene and appreciate the town’s creativity.

  • Respect the town’s educational institutions and the students and staff who attend them.

Don’t:

  • Litter, damage, or deface the town’s public property or artwork.

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Alfred (New York)

Alfred is a relatively safe town with a friendly and welcoming community. Visitors should take standard precautions and avoid leaving valuables unattended or walking alone late at night.

Health in Alfred (New York)

Alfred has several medical facilities, including Alfred State College Health Center, Alfred University Health and Wellness Services, and St. James Mercy Hospital. Visitors can seek medical assistance in case of emergencies.
