Introduction to Franklinville

Franklinville, a hamlet in the Cattaraugus County, located in the Southern Tier region of New York, is the perfect destination for nature lovers and history enthusiasts. Surrounded by lush green hills and serene waterways, the town has a rich cultural heritage which can be experienced through its quaint museums, historic landmarks, and local festivals.

In this guide, we will take you through the best places to visit, top things to do, local culture, and more, to help you plan your trip to Franklinville.

List of Places to Visit in Franklinville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Franklinville has a rich history, and there are plenty of museums and historical places to visit in the town to explore that history. Some of the top attractions include:

  • Franklinville Historical Society Museum: The Franklinville Historical Society Museum, located on Pine Street, lets visitors explore the town’s heritage through an amazing collection of artifacts, furniture, and photographs.
  • The Salamanca Rail Museum: The Salamanca Rail Museum is a must-visit place for history enthusiasts. Located just 20 minutes away from Franklinville, the museum showcases the rich history of railroads in the area.
  • Griffis Sculpture Park: The ArtPark is located in East Otto, 15 minutes from Franklinville. The sculpture park is an oasis of beauty and creativity and features a collection of contemporary sculptures from around the world set amidst nature and wildlife.
  • Frank Lloyd Wright’s Graycliff Estate: The Graycliff Estate is a 30-minute drive from Franklinville and is a National Historic Landmark designed by Frank Lloyd Wright. The estate offers breathtaking views of Lake Erie and is undoubtedly a must-visit attraction in the area.

Natural Attractions of Franklinville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Franklinville is surrounded by stunning natural beauty, and there is no shortage of scenic views, hiking trails, and outdoor activities. Outdoor enthusiasts can try:

  • Rock City Park: Located in Olean, 30 minutes from Franklinville, this natural wonder features immense rock formations and scenic trails.
  • Allegany State Park: Allegany State Park, located just 35 minutes from Franklinville, offers visitors 65,000 acres of outdoor adventure, including hiking, camping, fishing, and more. Don’t forget to visit Thunder Rocks for some incredible rock formations.
  • The Enchanted Mountains: The Enchanted Mountains are a breathtaking range of hills and mountains that are just as magical as their name suggests. Hiking, skiing, camping, and other outdoor activities are all available here.
  • Cattaraugus Creek: Fishers and nature enthusiasts will enjoy exploring Cattaraugus Creek, which is the perfect spot to fish for trout and take in scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Franklinville

If you love hiking or biking, you’ll be pleased to know that there are plenty of trails for you to explore in Franklinville. Some popular options include:

  • The Wild Turkey Trail: This route is perfect for hikers of all skill levels and offers scenic views of the rolling hills and countryside.
  • The Allegheny River Valley Trail: This trail runs along the Allegheny River and provides stunning views of the waterway and wildlife.
  • The Art Roscoe Cross-Country Ski and Mountain Bike Area: Also known as Allegany State Park, this 15-mile trail is open year-round and is perfect for mountain biking, hiking, and snowshoeing.

Best Things to Do in Franklinville: Activities and Tours

Besides exploring the town’s cultural and natural sites, there are plenty of other things to do in Franklinville, including:

  • Attend a local festival: Franklinville hosts several festivals throughout the year, including the Franklinville-Farmersville Fall Festival in September, and the Summer Art Festival in July. These events give visitors a taste of the community’s culture and allow them to experience local art, music, and food.
  • Go skiing at Holiday Valley Ski Resort: During the winter months, visitors can hit the slopes at Holiday Valley Ski Resort, located just a short drive away from Franklinville.
  • Take a brewery tour: Rising Storm Brewing Company, located just 15 minutes from Franklinville, offers tours of their brewery facilities and tastings of their delicious craft beers.

Food and Drink Scene of Franklinville

Must-Try Dishes

Franklinville is known for its delicious local cuisine, and there are several dishes you must try during your visit, including:

  • Beef on Weck: This sandwich features thinly sliced roast beef served on a kummelweck roll, a type of roll covered in caraway seeds and salt.
  • Lake Erie Perch: If you’re a seafood lover, you’ll enjoy the freshwater fish from Lake Erie, which can be prepared in various ways, including fried, grilled, or blackened.
  • Apple Cider Donuts: These fried doughnuts are made with fresh apple cider and are a perfect treat during the fall months.

Local Beverages

Craft beer and wine enthusiasts will enjoy exploring the local beverages of Franklinville, with breweries and wineries to explore, such as:

  • Four Mile Brewing Company: This brewery creates original craft beers brewed on site, with a tasting room available for visitors.
  • Wooden Nickel Buffalo Farm and Winery: Specializing in small-batch wines made on their farm, the Wooden Nickel in nearby Edinburg, is renowned for its award-winning vino.

Where to Stay in Franklinville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

While Franklinville is a small town, there are still plenty of options for accommodation, including:

  • The Franklinville Inn: This locally owned and operated inn provides cozy rooms and top-notch service, plus it’s within walking distance of area attractions.
  • The Wingate by Wyndham Ellicottville: Just a short drive from Franklinville, this modern hotel offers comfortable rooms and close proximity to the area’s top attractions.
  • Airbnb: Franklinville is home to many hospitable locals who offer rooms, apartments, cabins, and guesthouses for rent on Airbnb.

What to Buy in Franklinville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for something to bring back home? Here are a few suggestions for souvenirs and local products to look out for:

  • Maple Syrup: Western New York is known for its sugar maples and fresh maple syrup, which makes an excellent gift or addition to any pantry.
  • Hand-Crafted Pottery: Several local artists have studios in the area, and their pottery showcases their creativity and craftsmanship.
  • Cattaraugus County Snowbelt: Many outdoor outfitters and souvenir stores sell Cattaraugus County Snowbelt gear, which includes t-shirts, fleece jackets, and hats suited for cold weather.

Local Culture and Customs of Franklinville

While visiting Franklinville, there are several things to keep in mind to respect local culture and customs.

  • Hunting and Fishing: Hunting and fishing are a significant part of life for many residents of Franklinville. Be sure to ask for permission before hunting or fishing on private property, and be respectful of hunting/fishing laws in the area.
  • Tipping: Tipping is customary at bars and restaurants in Franklinville. In the US, it is customary to tip servers at least 15-20% of the total bill.
  • Smoking: Smoking is not allowed in many public areas, including restaurants, bars, and hotels. Be sure to familiarize yourself with the local smoking laws before lighting up.

Best Time to Visit Franklinville

The best time to visit Franklinville is between April and October, as this period has the most temperate weather and allows for outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, fishing, and attending festivals. However, the autumn season provides breathtaking fall colors, making it a popular time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Franklinville

Do:

  • Respect wildlife and their natural habitat.
  • Observe hunting and fishing laws.
  • Participate in local festivals and events.
  • Leave no trace when exploring natural sites.

Don’t:

  • Trespass or hunt on private property without permission.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Park in unauthorized areas.
  • Ignore the smoking laws in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Franklinville

Franklinville is generally considered safe, but it’s still important to take precautions while visiting. Here are some tips for staying safe:

  • Lock your belongings in a safe or secure location.
  • Be aware of your surroundings, particularly when in unfamiliar areas or at night.
  • Keep your car doors locked while driving and parked.
  • Know the emergency phone numbers and locations before you need them.

Health in Franklinville

It’s comforting to know that the states of New York and the USA have implemented high safety and health standards throughout the area, including Franklinville. For travelers, it is customary to have sufficient medical insurance covering emergency medical care.

Getting to Franklinville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Franklinville

  • Buffalo Niagara International Airport (BUF): The airport is located approximately 69 km (43 miles) northwest of Franklinville and offers a variety of domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Franklinville

  • Olean Transit Services Center: Olean Transit Services is the primary bus terminal that serves the Cattaraugus County area, which includes Franklinville. Phone: 716-376-5671.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Car rental services are available both at the airport and within Franklinville.
  • Taxis and Ridesharing Services: Taxis and ridesharing services such as Uber and Lyft are available in Buffalo, which is approximately 69 km (43 miles) from Franklinville.

How to Get Around in Franklinville

Franklinville is small enough to be easily explored on foot. Still, many visitors may wish to rent a car to explore the surrounding area and have greater freedom in traveling.

Where to Go Next from Franklinville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re ready to explore nearby areas, here are some destinations to consider:

  • Ellicottville: This quaint village is known for skiing and snowboarding during the winter months and golfing, mountain biking, and hiking during the summer months.
  • Chautauqua Institution: The institution is a perfect destination for families, with plenty of recreational and educational activities available, including hiking, swimming, and world-class performances.
  • Niagara Falls: Located just two hours north of Franklinville, Niagara Falls is a must-visit destination for anyone exploring the area.
