Introduction to Chautauqua County

Located in the southern part of New York, Chautauqua County is surrounded by natural beauty and rich in cultural heritage. The county spans over 1,500 square miles and is home to a diverse population of around 127,939 people. There are many attractions in Chautauqua County that are perfect for tourists of all ages, interests and budget. In this guide, we will cover everything you need to know about exploring Chautauqua County.

List of Places to Visit in Chautauqua County

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Chautauqua County has a rich cultural heritage that has shaped its identity. Some of the must-visit museums and historical places include:

National Comedy Center

The National Comedy Center is not just a museum, it is an experience! It showcases the evolution of comedy and its impact on American culture. There are interactive exhibits, rare memorabilia, and thousands of comedy artifacts that will make you laugh out loud.

Lucille Ball Desi Arnaz Museum

The Lucille Ball Desi Arnaz Museum is dedicated to Hollywood legend Lucille Ball’s life, her work and unparalleled comedy career. The museum has an impressive collection of Lucille Ball’s personal artifacts and a replica of her Hollywood makeup room. It is a must-visit for all Lucy fans!

Chautauqua Institution

The Chautauqua Institution is a historic cultural center renowned for its educational and recreational activities. Here you can enjoy visual and performing arts, lectures, concerts, and other cultural events.

Natural Attractions of Chautauqua County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Chautauqua County has some of the most diverse and breathtaking landscapes that offer outdoor adventures and scenic views to explore. Here are some of the natural attractions you should visit:

Chautauqua Lake

Stretching over 17 miles, Chautauqua Lake is a popular tourist destination for water sports and fishing. During summers, visitors can swim, kayak, paddleboard, or take a leisurely boat ride across the serene waters of the lake. A lakeside walkway is perfect for a stroll by the lake.

Panama Rocks Scenic Park

Panama Rocks Scenic Park is one of the most unique geologic wonders of the country, with massive rocks and boulders winding through the forests. Visitors can go for a hike, take photos, and explore the park’s twisty, narrow trails.

Lily Dale Assembly

Lily Dale Assembly is a place of spiritual significance and is home to the world’s largest center for the science, philosophy, and religion of spiritualism. Visitors can go on a guided tour, attend seminars, meditate and obtain spiritual guidance.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chautauqua County

Chautauqua County has an extensive list of hiking and biking trails for adventure lovers. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Rails to Trails, connecting many charming towns in Chautauqua County
  • Long Point State Park, offering tranquil and beautiful routes of varying difficulty
  • Panama Rocks Scenic Park, for a short and challenging route amidst rocks and boulders

Best Things to Do in Chautauqua County: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to see and experience in Chautauqua County. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Attend performances and plays at the Chautauqua Institution
  • Take a guided boat tour of Chautauqua Lake
  • Go for wine tasting at any of the 25+ wineries in the region
  • Attend the annual Lucille Ball Comedy Festival

Week-Long Itinerary for Chautauqua County

Day 1: Check-in and explore the historic city of Jamestown

Day 2: Visit the Chautauqua Institution and attend cultural events

Day 3: Hike at Panama Rocks Scenic Park and have lunch at a local cafe

Day 4: Explore the National Comedy Center and visit the Lucille Ball Desi Arnaz Museum

Day 5: Take a guided boat tour of Chautauqua Lake and do some fishing

Day 6: Attend a seminar at Lily Dale Assembly and get a spiritual reading

Day 7: Visit wineries and enjoy a wine tasting tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Chautauqua County

Must-Try Dishes

Chautauqua County is known for its regional cuisine, here are some local favorites:

  • Beef on Weck: A traditional sandwich made with slow-cooked beef, horseradish sauce served on top of a Kummelweck rolls

  • Grape Pie: A sweet and unique dessert made from concord grapes and sugar, served with whipped cream.

  • White Clam Pie: A New England-style pizza with a flavored oil base, sliced clams as toppings, and grated cheese.

Local Beverages

Chautauqua County has a burgeoning beverage scene, try some of these drink staples:

  • Wine: Chautauqua County has over 25 wineries where you can enjoy a wine tasting tour and sample locally made wines.

  • Craft Beer: The county has a booming craft beer industry with several breweries producing high-quality beer.

Where to Stay in Chautauqua County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chautauqua County offers a wide range of accommodation options for every budget. Here are some of our favorites:

  • The Spencer Hotel & Spa: A historically significant hotel in downtown Chautauqua that offers luxury spa services.

  • Lake Erie Lodge Westfield: A romantic bed and breakfast with a beautiful view of Lake Erie.

  • Peek’n Peak Resort: An upscale resort with luxurious amenities and gorgeous views of the surrounding mountains.

What to Buy in Chautauqua County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to take a piece of Chautauqua County with you, consider picking a unique souvenir from a local shop. Here are some ideas:

  • Grape seed oil, made from the region’s famous grapes, is a popular choice.

  • Hand-carved wooden products from Cherry Creek.

  • Chautauqua Institution logoed apparel, hats, and other merchandise.

Local Culture and Customs of Chautauqua County

Chautauqua County is known for its cultural heritage, spiritualism, and love of comedy. Visitors should respect local customs and traditions, such as:

  • Dress appropriately and respectfully in spiritual places like Lily Dale Assembly.

  • Always offer a generous tip as a token of appreciation for excellent service.

  • Visitors should take note that Chautauqua is a dry county, and alcohol sales are prohibited except at certified wineries or breweries.

Best Time to Visit Chautauqua County

Chautauqua County is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is from early summer through Fall. The temperature is conducive for outdoor activities and the fall foliage is incredibly magical.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chautauqua County

Here are some things to keep in mind while visiting Chautauqua County:

Do:

  • Respect private property and follow regulations of hiking trails and parks.

  • Have backup plans for outdoor activities as weather can be unpredictable.

  • Make reservations in advance at popular local restaurants and cafes.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave any garbage behind while exploring nature.

  • Don’t rely completely on GPS while navigating as mobile coverage can be limited in some areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chautauqua County

Chautauqua County is a generally safe place for tourists, but here are some safety tips:

  • Carry a first aid kit while exploring nature parks and hiking trails.

  • If you’re going to swim, stick to the designated swimming areas and follow safety guidelines.

  • Drive responsibly, and respect the speed limit, as rural roads might not have any streetlights.

Health in Chautauqua County

Chautauqua County has excellent health care facilities. In case of emergencies, visitors can dial 911 for assistance. It is advisable to bring any medication you might require before traveling to the region.

Getting to Chautauqua County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chautauqua County

Chautauqua County is served by two airports:

  • Chautauqua County-Jamestown Airport

  • Buffalo Niagara International Airport

Bus Stations Near Chautauqua County

The Greyhound Bus has a terminal in downtown Jamestown, which is connected to different cities in the region.

Other Options

Renting a car can be a great way to travel in Chautauqua County, as it offers mobility and flexibility to visitors navigating the area.

How to Get Around in Chautauqua County:

The best way to get around in Chautauqua County is by renting a car. Visitors can also use ride-hailing services and Local WRTA buses from major cities in the region.

Where to Go Next from Chautauqua County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Chautauqua County is surrounded by many scenic routes and nearby destinations. Some popular excursions include:

  • Niagara Falls.

  • Letchworth State Park.

  • Lake Ontario.

Conclusion

Chautauqua County is rich in history, natural beauty, and culture. From hiking and biking to wine tasting, this county has everything for everyone. So, what are you waiting for? Pack your bags and head to Chautauqua County for your next adventure vacation.
