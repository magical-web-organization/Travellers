Introduction to Binghamton

Binghamton is a charming city located in Broome County, in the Southern Tier region of New York. It is known for its rich history, scenic views, wonderful architecture, and vibrant arts and culture scene. Binghamton is home to many cultural and recreational attractions that appeal to all types of travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Binghamton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Binghamton has a rich history, which is evident in its numerous historical landmarks and museums. The following are some of the top cultural attractions:

  • Roberson Museum and Science Center: This museum features exhibits related to regional history, science, and art.

  • Phelps Mansion Museum: This is a restored Victorian mansion that now serves as a museum and cultural center.

  • Bundy Museum of History and Art: This museum is housed in the historic Harlow E. Bundy house and features exhibits on local history.

Natural Attractions of Binghamton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Binghamton is home to several natural attractions, including state parks, hiking trails, and nature preserves. Some of the most popular ones are:

  • Chenango Valley State Park: This park offers a range of recreational activities, including fishing, hiking, camping, and swimming.

  • Otsiningo Park: This park features hiking trails, picnic areas, and beautiful views of the Susquehanna River.

  • Waterman Conservation Education Center: This is a nature preserve and wildlife sanctuary that offers hiking trails, bird watching, and educational programs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Binghamton

Binghamton is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts and offers many hiking and biking trails. Some of the must-try routes are:

  • Chenango Valley State Park Nature Trails: These trails offer breathtaking views of the park’s woodlands, lakes, and streams.

  • Vestal Rail Trail: This is a paved trail that runs through the heart of the city and offers scenic views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Oakley Corners State Forest: This forest offers miles of hiking trails through picturesque woodlands, streams, and waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Binghamton: Activities and Tours

Binghamton has a plethora of activities and tours that offer visitors a chance to experience the best of the city. Some of the must-do activities are:

  • Binghamton Zoo at Ross Park: This is the fifth oldest zoo in the country and offers up-close views of over 100 different animal species.

  • Spiedie Fest and Balloon Rally: This is an annual event that features hot air balloon rides, spiedie eating contests, and live music.

  • The Forum Theater: This historic theater hosts Broadway shows, concerts, and other cultural events.

Week-Long Itinerary for Binghamton

Planning a week-long trip to Binghamton can be a great way to explore the city in depth. Here is an itinerary that covers some of the top attractions:

Day 1-2: Visit cultural sites such as Roberson Museum and Science Center, Phelps Mansion Museum, and Bundy Museum of History and Art.

Day 3: Explore one of the many natural attractions, such as Chenango Valley State Park, Otsiningo Park, or Waterman Conservation Education Center.

Day 4: Go hiking or biking on one of Binghamton’s scenic routes.

Day 5: Take a tour of Binghamton Zoo at Ross Park.

Day 6: Attend one of the many cultural events hosted at The Forum Theater.

Day 7: Shop for souvenirs at local markets and boutiques and enjoy the city’s food and drink scene.

Food and Drink Scene of Binghamton

Binghamton is known for its diverse food and drink scene, which offers something for everyone. Some of the must-try dishes and local beverages are:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Spiedies: This is a local specialty, which is essentially marinated meat skewered and grilled to perfection.

  • Tomato Pie: This is a savory, pizza-like dish featuring a thick crust and a sweet tomato sauce.

  • Chicken Riggies: This is a pasta dish that features chicken, rigatoni pasta, and a spicy tomato cream sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Coffee: Binghamton has many coffee shops that serve delicious coffee and espresso drinks, including Strange Brew, La Tourelle, and Little Venice.

  • Beer: The city is home to several breweries, including Binghamton Brewing Company and Water St. Brewing Co., which offer a variety of craft beers.

Where to Stay in Binghamton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Binghamton offers a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Some of the top choices are:

  • DoubleTree by Hilton Binghamton: This hotel is conveniently located in downtown Binghamton and features modern amenities and comfortable rooms.

  • Traditions at the Glen Resort and Hotel: This is a luxury hotel that features a spa, golf course, and beautiful views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Red Roof Inn and Suites Binghamton: This is a budget-friendly hotel that offers comfortable rooms and basic amenities.

What to Buy in Binghamton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Binghamton has many unique shops and markets that offer a range of souvenirs and locally-made products. Some of the best places to shop are:

  • Kilmer Market: This market offers locally-grown produce, baked goods, and crafts.

  • Old Barn Hollow: This shop sells all-natural, handcrafted skincare products made from locally-sourced ingredients.

  • Hand of Man Museum: This museum has a shop that sells handcrafted items made by local artisans, such as pottery, jewelry, and woodwork.

Local Culture and Customs of Binghamton

Binghamton has a rich culture and customs that are worth experiencing. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Binghamton is home to many festivals and events throughout the year, such as First Friday Art Walks, Spiedie Fest and Balloon Rally, and LUMA Projection Arts Festival.

  • The city has a strong Italian-American community, which is evident in its food, festivals, and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Binghamton

The best time to visit Binghamton is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm and sunny, and several events and festivals take place. However, the city is beautiful year-round, and each season has its own charm.

Do’s and Don’ts of Binghamton

Do:

  • Explore the city’s cultural and historical sites, natural attractions, and outdoor recreation opportunities.

  • Try the local cuisine, including spiedies, tomato pie, and chicken riggies.

  • Attend one of the many festivals and events hosted in the city throughout the year.

Don’t:

  • Ignore Binghamton’s rich history, architecture, and culture.

  • Leave the city without trying a spiedie or visiting one of the local breweries.

  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions, such as Italian-American culture and cuisine.

Safety and Health

Safety in Binghamton

Binghamton is a safe city to visit; however, visitors should still take the usual precautions to ensure their safety, such as locking car doors and avoiding deserted areas at night.

Health in Binghamton

Binghamton has several hospitals and medical clinics, and visitors can easily access healthcare services if needed.

Getting to Binghamton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Binghamton is easily accessible by car, bus, and plane.

Airports Near Binghamton

  • Greater Binghamton Airport

Bus Stations Near Binghamton

  • Greyhound Bus Station

Other Options

  • Visitors can also take a train to Albany or Syracuse and then take a bus or car to Binghamton.

How to Get Around in Binghamton

Binghamton is a walkable city, and visitors can easily explore downtown on foot. Public transportation is also available, including buses and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Binghamton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can explore after visiting Binghamton. Some of the top choices are:

  • Finger Lakes Region: This region is known for its beautiful lakes, wineries, and scenic drives.

  • Corning: This charming town is home to the Corning Museum of Glass, which showcases the history and art of glassmaking.

  • Cooperstown: This town is home to the Baseball Hall of Fame and offers picturesque views of Otsego Lake.

Conclusion

Binghamton is a wonderful city that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and outdoor recreation. Its natural beauty and cultural attractions make it a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to New York.
