Introduction to Tioga County (New York)

Located in the Southern Tier region of New York, Tioga County is a charming destination with something to offer for everyone. The county is home to lush forests, rolling hills, and scenic views that make it an ideal spot for outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and fishing. Visitors can also explore the cultural and historical attractions in the county, including museums, galleries, and historical sites. With a rich array of dining and lodging options, Tioga County promises an unforgettable experience for any traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Tioga County (New York)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tioga County Historical Society Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of Tioga County through a variety of exhibits, displays, and events. Visitors can learn about the Native American settlements, colonial era, and the county’s contribution to the American Revolution.

  • The Creek and Valley Cultural Center: Located in the charming village of Candor, this cultural center hosts art exhibits, musical concerts, and theatrical performances throughout the year.

  • The Tioga Downs Casino Resort: This complex features over 900 slot machines, as well as a hotel, restaurants, and even a horse racing track.

Natural Attractions of Tioga County (New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Glen State Park: This state park is ideal for hiking, camping, and recreational activities. Visitors can enjoy the scenic views of the park’s five waterfalls.

  • Greenway Trail: This 22-mile trail starts in Owego and winds its way through the county, showcasing the natural beauty of Tioga County.

  • Hickories Park: A picturesque park situated by the Susquehanna River, featuring sports fields, picnic areas, and hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tioga County (New York)

  • The Finger Lakes Trail: This trail stretches from the Southern Tier to the Catskills, offering over 900 miles of hiking opportunities. Tioga County is home to a section of this trail, ranging from challenging hikes to easy walks.

  • Catharine Valley Trail: This 12-mile trail stretches from Watkins Glen to Horseheads, offering scenic views and excellent biking opportunities.

Best Things to Do in Tioga County (New York): Activities and Tours

  • Fishing: Tioga County is known for its excellent fishing opportunities, with plenty of streams and lakes stocked with trout and other species.

  • Kayaking and Rafting: Visitors can enjoy paddling down the Susquehanna River and taking in the scenic views of the county.

  • Meadows Golf Center: This 18-hole golf course is perfect for golfers of all levels.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tioga County (New York)

Day 1: Explore the museums and historical sites of downtown Owego, including the Tioga County Historical Society Museum and Gallery 41.

Day 2: Hike the Finger Lakes Trail, enjoying the scenic views of the county.

Day 3: Visit Glen State Park and explore the beauty of its five waterfalls.

Day 4: Take a biking tour on the Catharine Valley Trail, exploring the scenic views of the county.

Day 5: Enjoy fishing and kayaking on the Susquehanna River.

Day 6: Play a round of golf at the Meadows Golf Center.

Food and Drink Scene of Tioga County (New York)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Spiedies: This delicious sandwich features marinated meat skewers, commonly made with chicken or pork, served on a roll.

  • Cornell Chicken: This locally famous recipe involves marinating chicken in a flavorful sauce and grilling it to perfection.

  • Apple Cider Doughnuts: Perfect for fall, these doughnuts are a local treat made with fresh apple cider.

Local Beverages

  • Grist Iron Brewing Company: Located in Burdett, this brewery offers an array of craft beers and ciders for visitors to try.

  • Thirsty Owl Wine Company: Visitors can enjoy a glass of wine and a vineyard tour at this award-winning vineyard in Cayuta.

Where to Stay in Tioga County (New York): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Owego Treadway Inn: This hotel offers comfortable rooms, high-quality dining options, and is located conveniently to downtown Owego.

  • The Red Roof Inn: With affordable rates and a central location, this hotel is perfect for travelers on a budget.

  • The River Edge Mansion: This quaint and charming guesthouse offers scenic views of the Susquehanna River.

What to Buy in Tioga County (New York): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maple Syrup: Tioga County is home to several maple syrup farms, where visitors can buy this locally produced treat.

  • Handmade Jewelry: Visitors can browse local jewelry shops or visit a craft fair to find unique handmade pieces.

  • Local Art: Galleries and art centers showcase the work of local artists, making these the perfect places to find a souvenir or gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Tioga County (New York)

Tioga County has a strong commitment to the arts and culture. Many of the small towns in the area host music festivals, craft fairs, and other community events throughout the year. Additionally, visitors can enjoy the local traditions of agriculture, winemaking, and maple syrup production.

Best Time to Visit Tioga County (New York)

The best time to visit Tioga County is in the spring, summer, and fall when the weather is mild and the outdoor activities are plentiful. Farmers markets and outdoor festivals are also popular during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tioga County (New York)

Do:

  • Explore the county’s museums and historical sites.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and fishing.
  • Sample the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural beauty of the county.
  • Overlook the smaller towns and villages in the area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tioga County (New York)

Tioga County is a safe and welcoming destination for visitors. However, visitors should practice caution when participating in outdoor activities such as hiking and kayaking, and follow proper safety protocols.

Health in Tioga County (New York)

Visitors can access medical services at Tioga County public health centers or hospitals in neighboring cities. However, visitors should pack essential medications and preventive measures such as bug spray and sun protection.

Getting to Tioga County (New York): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tioga County (New York)

The Elmira/Corning Regional Airport is the closest airport to Tioga County, located roughly 20 miles southeast of the county.

Bus Stations Near Tioga County (New York)

The Greyhound Bus Station in Binghamton is the closest station to Tioga County, located roughly 30 miles northeast of the county.

Other Options

Visitors can access Tioga County via car, with several major highways including Interstate 86 and the New York State Thruway providing access.

How to Get Around in Tioga County (New York)

Visitors can rent a car to explore Tioga County, or use a taxi or ridesharing service to get around. Biking and walking are also popular options in the smaller towns.

Where to Go Next from Tioga County (New York): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ithaca: Located roughly 30 miles to the southeast of Tioga County, Ithaca is a vibrant college town with a thriving culinary and cultural scene.

  • Finger Lakes: This popular vacation destination is located to the northeast of Tioga County, featuring stunning lakes, wineries, and charming small towns.

  • Niagara Falls: This world-famous attraction is located roughly 3 hours to the northwest of Tioga County, making it an ideal day trip or weekend excursion.

Conclusion

Tioga County offers an abundance of outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and local traditions that make it a must-visit destination in New York State. From hiking the Finger Lakes Trail to enjoying wine and cheese on a vineyard tour, there is something for everyone. This comprehensive tour guide will help visitors make the most of their trip to Tioga County.
