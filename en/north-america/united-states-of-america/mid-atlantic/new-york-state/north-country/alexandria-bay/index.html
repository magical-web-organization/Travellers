Introduction to Alexandria Bay

Alexandria Bay is a beautiful village located in the heart of the Thousand Islands region, in North Country, New York. Situated on the shores of the St. Lawrence River, this charming town is a popular tourist destination famous for its natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we have compiled a list of the best places to visit, things to do, and experiences not to be missed in Alexandria Bay. Whether you’re planning a day trip, a weekend getaway, or a longer vacation, we have everything you need to know to make your trip to Alexandria Bay unforgettable.

List of Places to Visit in Alexandria Bay

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Boldt Castle: Built by millionaire George Boldt as a tribute to his wife, Boldt Castle is a stunning example of Gilded Age architecture. Visitors can explore the castle’s 120 rooms, picturesque gardens, and unique tunnels and tunnels. Don’t miss the heartwarming love story behind its construction.
  • Singer Castle: Nicknamed the “Castle on the Island,” Singer Castle was built in 1905 as a private residence. Today, visitors can take a guided tour to discover its secret passageways and hidden rooms, as well as admire the views of the St. Lawrence Seaway from its towers.
  • Thousand Islands Arts Center: The Thousand Islands Arts Center features exhibitions and galleries showcasing the work of local artists. It also offers classes and workshops for artists of all levels.

Natural Attractions of Alexandria Bay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Thousand Islands: Comprising over 1,800 islands dotted along the St. Lawrence River, the Thousand Islands offer spectacular views and endless opportunities for outdoor adventures, such as boating, swimming, and kayaking. take a scenic boat tour to discover its breathtaking beauty.
  • Wellesley Island State Park: The largest state park in the Thousand Islands region, Wellesley Island State Park is home to numerous hiking and biking trails, as well as sandy beaches and campgrounds. Don’t miss the Minna Anthony Common Nature Center, a showcase for the region’s flora and fauna.
  • Coyote Moon Vineyards: Enjoy a taste of the Thousand Islands region with a visit to Coyote Moon Vineyards. Its award-winning wines are made from grapes grown on the shores of Lake Ontario, and the vineyard also hosts concerts and events throughout the year.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alexandria Bay

Alexandria Bay is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with many hiking and biking trails offering stunning views of the St. Lawrence River and its surrounding landscapes. Some of the best routes to explore in Alexandria Bay include:

  • Wellesley Island State Park Nature Trails: This 5-mile loop trail takes visitors through dense forests, over streams, and past scenic vistas. Keep an eye out for the park’s abundant wildlife, including deer, porcupines, and bald eagles.
  • Thousand Islands Park Heritage Trail: This 2.5-mile trail takes visitors through the historic Thousand Islands Park, known for its Victorian-era homes and cottages dating back to the early 1900s.
  • Clayton Riverwalk: This paved path follows the banks of the St. Lawrence River and offers stunning views of the river and its islands. It’s a popular spot for jogging, cycling, and walking with furry friends.

Best Things to Do in Alexandria Bay: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tours: One of the best ways to experience the natural beauty of Alexandria Bay is by taking a boat tour. Choose from a variety of options, including scenic sightseeing tours, sunset dinner cruises, and fishing trips.
  • Scuba Diving: Discover the underwater world of the St. Lawrence River with a scuba diving excursion. Explore old shipwrecks, deep caves, and colorful aquatic life with the help of local dive shops and experienced guides.
  • Skiing and Snowboarding: In the winter months, Alexandria Bay turns into a winter wonderland with opportunities for skiing and snowboarding. MacDonald’s resort has a ski hill for the winter enthusiasts.
  • Fishing: Fishing has been a part of the Alexandria Bay lifestyle forever. Take a guide and fish the waters of the St. Lawrence River or the fishing grounds around the islands.

Food and Drink Scene of Alexandria Bay

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish Fry: Enjoy a classic fish fry at one of the many local restaurants, featuring fresh fish from the St. Lawrence River.
  • Poutine: This Canadian classic is a must-try when visiting Alexandria Bay. Crispy fries topped with gravy and cheese curds, it’s the ultimate comfort food.
  • Beer-battered Onion Rings: Another delicious appetizer from the St. Lawrence River region. Try them with a local microbrew.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: The Thousand Islands region is home to numerous wineries producing award-winning wines using locally grown grapes. Don’t miss a chance to try the region’s best wines at Coyote Moon Vineyards or Thousand Islands Winery.
  • Beer: The region has seen a recent surge in microbrews. With delicious flavors like Dockside Amber Ale, St. Lawrence IPA, and Helles On Ice, you can’t go wrong.

Where to Stay in Alexandria Bay: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Riveredge Resort Hotel: This luxurious hotel overlooks the St. Lawrence River and offers upscale dining and spa services. It’s the largest hotel in the Thousand Islands region.
  • Bonnie Castle Resort & Marina: Located in the heart of Alexandria Bay and on the water, this resort has its own marina and offers great views of the Thousand Islands.
  • Kring Point State Park: This campground located on the waterfront of the St. Lawrence River has sites for tents and trailers.

What to Buy in Alexandria Bay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Thousand Islands Sauce: Known as the condiment that made the Thousand Islands famous, this pink-hued dressing is delicious on everything from salads to burgers.
  • Local Art and Crafts: The Thousand Islands region is home to many talented artists, and you can find their work at galleries and shops throughout Alexandria Bay.
  • Antiques and Collectibles: While in Alexandria Bay, check out the many antique shops to find unique treasures from the past.

Local Culture and Customs of Alexandria Bay

Alexandria Bay and the Thousand Islands region have a rich history and unique culture. Be sure to take time to learn about the region’s heritage through its museums, historical sites, and art galleries.

Best Time to Visit Alexandria Bay

The best time to visit Alexandria Bay is from May to October, when the weather is mild and the outdoor activities are in full swing. Summers are busy and filled with visitors, so if you prefer fewer crowds, opt for the shoulder seasons of spring and fall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alexandria Bay

Do:

  • Take a boat tour of the Thousand Islands: A boat tour is the best way to see the region’s natural beauty, learn about its history, and spot wildlife like bald eagles and herons.
  • Try the local cuisine: The St. Lawrence River region is known for its fresh seafood. Don’t miss a chance to try local specialties like fish fry, poutine, and beer-battered onion rings.
  • Explore Boldt Castle: This iconic castle is one of the main attractions of Alexandria Bay. Take a tour to learn about its fascinating history and admire its unique architecture.

Don’t:

  • Litter: The Thousand Islands region is a precious natural resource. Please do your part to keep it clean and beautiful.
  • Trespass on private property: Many islands and properties along the St. Lawrence River are privately owned. Respect others’ property and stay on designated trails and roads.
  • Take any plants or animals from the park: The Thousand Islands region is rich in flora and fauna, and some species are endangered. Don’t take anything from the parks or natural areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Alexandria Bay

Alexandria Bay is a safe destination for tourists, with low crime rates and a friendly community. Visitors should still take proper precautions while exploring the outdoors, such as wearing sunscreen, staying hydrated, and dressing appropriately for the weather and activity.

Health in Alexandria Bay

Visitors to Alexandria Bay should be up to date on routine vaccinations such as the yearly flu shot, as well as any travel-specific vaccinations recommended by their doctor. Be sure to pack sunscreen, insect repellent, and any necessary medications for your travels.

Getting to Alexandria Bay: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Alexandria Bay

The nearest major airports to Alexandria Bay are Ottawa-Macdonald-Cartier International Airport (YOW) in Canada, and Syracuse Hancock International Airport (SYR) in New York. Both airports are approximately 2-3 hours away from Alexandria Bay by car.

Bus Stations Near Alexandria Bay

The closest bus stations to Alexandria Bay are located in Watertown (30 minutes away) and Syracuse (2 hours away). Greyhound, Jefferson Lines, and Adirondack Trailways all offer service to these stations.

Other Options

Many visitors to Alexandria Bay arrive by car, as it’s easily accessible from major highways such as I-81. Additionally, many tourists arrive by boat, as Alexandria Bay is home to several marinas and yacht clubs.

How to Get Around in Alexandria Bay

While in Alexandria Bay, visitors can get around by car, bike, foot, or boat. Many local attractions are located within walking distance of the downtown area. Several local companies offer bike and boat rentals for those looking for more adventure.

Where to Go Next from Alexandria Bay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The Thousand Islands region is full of great destinations waiting to be explored. Some nearby excursions to consider include:

  • Cape Vincent: A charming village known for its lighthouses and beautiful waterfront parks.
  • Sackets Harbor: A historic village that was once a naval base during the War of 1812. It’s a great place to learn about local history, enjoy a picnic in the park or explore the harbor.
  • Kingston, Ontario: Just across the border in Canada, Kingston is a beautiful historic city situated on the shores of Lake Ontario.

Conclusion

Alexandria Bay is a destination that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re exploring its historic castles, hiking and biking its trails or just soaking up the sun on its sandy beaches, Alexandria Bay is sure to steal your heart. Plan your trip and experience the stunning beauty and rich culture that awaits you in Alexandria Bay.
