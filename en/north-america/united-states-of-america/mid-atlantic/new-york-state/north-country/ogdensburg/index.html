Introduction to Ogdensburg

Nestled in North Country, Ogdensburg, New York, is a charming and historic city on the edge of the St. Lawrence River. Established in 1749, Ogdensburg is one of the oldest settlements in the United States and has a rich history that dates back over 250 years. It is a perfect destination for those who want to explore a hidden gem that is off the beaten path.

Whether you are interested in history, outdoor adventures, culture or simply relaxing, Ogdensburg has something to offer to everyone. In this comprehensive guide, we will explore everything Ogdensburg has to offer, from its cultural landmarks and natural attractions to its culinary delights and bustling markets.

So, pack your bags, and let’s get started on a journey to this unique and beautiful corner of New York State.

List of Places to Visit in Ogdensburg

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ogdensburg has a rich cultural and historical heritage, which can be seen in its museums, historical landmarks, and festivals. Here are some of the most important places to visit in Ogdensburg:

  • Frederic Remington Art Museum: This museum is dedicated to the works of Frederic Remington, a legendary American artist who specialized in depicting the American West. The museum houses a collection of his paintings, sculptures, and writings.

  • The Ogdensburg Sculpture Garden: This garden is home to a collection of outdoor sculptures that celebrate Ogdensburg’s natural and cultural heritage.

  • St. Lawrence County Historical Association and Museum: This museum houses exhibits that tell the story of Ogdensburg’s rich past, including displays on Native American culture, colonial life, and the city’s history as a major transportation hub.

  • Ogdensburg Public Library: The Ogdensburg Public Library is a historic building that dates back to 1904. Its architecture is a blend of Beaux-Arts and classical styles, and it houses a collection of over 28,000 volumes.

Natural Attractions of Ogdensburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ogdensburg is located in one of the most beautiful parts of New York State, and there are plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic hikes. Here are some of the best places to explore in Ogdensburg:

  • Ogdensburg-Prescott International Bridge: This bridge spans the St. Lawrence River and connects Ogdensburg with Prescott, Canada. It offers stunning views of the river and the surrounding countryside.

  • Spectacle Lake Trail: This 2.5-mile hike takes you through a peaceful forest and around the shore of Spectacle Lake. It is a great place to see local wildlife, including birds and deer.

  • Eel Weir State Park: Eel Weir State Park is a beautiful park that offers hiking trails, fishing spots, picnicking areas, and camping facilities. The park is located on the banks of the St. Lawrence River and offers stunning views of the water.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ogdensburg

If you love hiking or biking, Ogdensburg has plenty of trails to explore. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in and around Ogdensburg:

  • Spectacle Lake Trail: This 2.5-mile trail is perfect for hiking and offers a beautiful view of Spectacle Lake.

  • Oswegatchie River Trail: This 12-mile trail follows the Oswegatchie River from Ogdensburg to Heuvelton. It is suitable for both hiking and biking.

  • Wellesley Island State Park: Located just south of Ogdensburg, Wellesley Island State Park offers miles of hiking and biking trails through dense forests and along the shoreline of the St. Lawrence River.

Best Things to Do in Ogdensburg: Activities and Tours

Ogdensburg is a great place to explore, whether you are interested in history, culture, or nature. Here are some of the best things to do in Ogdensburg:

  • Visit the Frederic Remington Art Museum: The Frederic Remington Art Museum is a must-visit for those interested in American art and culture.

  • Take a River Cruise: A river cruise along the St. Lawrence River is a great way to explore the area and see the sights from a different perspective.

  • Explore the Downtown District: Ogdensburg’s downtown district is home to a variety of shops, restaurants, and cultural landmarks, making it a great place to explore on foot.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ogdensburg

If you’re planning a trip to Ogdensburg, here’s a week-long itinerary to help you make the most of your time in the area:

Day 1: Visit the Frederic Remington Art Museum and explore the downtown district.

Day 2: Take a river cruise along the St. Lawrence River and enjoy the views from the water.

Day 3: Head to Eel Weir State Park and spend the day hiking and picnicking in the great outdoors.

Day 4: Explore the St. Lawrence County Historical Association and Museum to learn about Ogdensburg’s rich history.

Day 5: Rent a bike and cycle along the Oswegatchie River trail for a scenic ride through the countryside.

Day 6: Visit the Ogdensburg Public Library and marvel at its historic architecture and collection of books.

Day 7: Take a day trip to one of the nearby towns, such as Massena or Canton, to experience more of North Country’s charm.

Food and Drink Scene of Ogdensburg

Ogdensburg offers a variety of local foods and drinks, ranging from hearty meat dishes to sweet desserts. Here are some of the must-try dishes and drinks in Ogdensburg:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Beef on Weck: This sandwich features thinly sliced roast beef, seasoned with caraway, and served on a kummelweck roll.

  • Fish Fry: A classic dish in Ogdensburg, the Fish Fry is a breaded fish filet served with fries and coleslaw.

  • Poutine: This Canadian classic is also popular in Ogdensburg. It consists of a bed of French fries, topped with cheese curds and gravy.

Local Beverages

  • Maple Syrup: Ogdensburg and the surrounding area is known for producing some of the best maple syrup in the country. Try it drizzled over pancakes or waffles.

  • Craft Beer: Local breweries, such as Wood Boat Brewery in Clayton, offer a variety of craft beers, perfect for sampling on a sunny afternoon.

Best Time to Visit Ogdensburg

The best time to visit Ogdensburg is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and the days are long. This is also the time when many local festivals and events take place, such as the Ogdensburg Seaway Festival and the Ogdensburg International Shad Fishing Derby.

Getting to Ogdensburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

If you’re planning a trip to Ogdensburg, here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Ogdensburg

The closest major airport to Ogdensburg is Ottawa Macdonald-Cartier International Airport, which is located about 75 miles north of Ogdensburg.

Bus Stations Near Ogdensburg

Ogdensburg is served by Greyhound Bus Lines, which operates a station in the downtown area.

Other Options

If you prefer to drive, Ogdensburg is located near Interstate 81, making it easily accessible by car.

How to Get Around in Ogdensburg

Ogdensburg is a small city that is easily explored on foot, but there are also a few transportation options available, including:

  • Taxis: Taxis are available in Ogdensburg and can be called through local taxi companies.

Where to Go Next from Ogdensburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
