Champlain and Rouses Point are small towns located in North Country, New York, which attracts travelers looking for a peaceful, picturesque getaway. Situated on the shore of the beautiful Lake Champlain, these towns offer visitors a combination of outdoor adventure, historical and cultural attractions, and a laid-back ambience. Here’s our comprehensive travel guide to Champlain and Rouses Point – your one-stop destination resource with all the must-see attractions, dining, and accommodation options.

Introduction to Champlain and Rouses Point

Champlain and Rouses Point are located in the northwestern corner of New York state and are known for their stunning lakes, beautiful parks, and majestic mansions. With over a century of history to discover, you will be transported back in time when you visit these charming towns which were once important sites during the French and Indian War.

List of Places to Visit in Champlain and Rouses Point

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Montgomery State Historic Site: This British fort has been beautifully restored, and visitors can learn about the history of the French and Indian War and how the battles unfolded.
  • Samuel de Champlain History Center: This interactive museum offers a glimpse into the life and adventures of the French explorer, the history of the Champlain Valley, and the building of the Champlain Canal.
  • Rouses Point Train Station Museum: This museum is housed in a historic train station and features exhibits on the history of the Delaware and Hudson Railroad, as well as the customs and border patrol history.

Natural Attractions of Champlain and Rouses Point: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cumberland Bay State Park: Located on the shores of Lake Champlain, this park offers hiking trails, fishing, picnic facilities, and a public boat launch.
  • Alburg Dunes State Park: North of Rouses Point, this park offers some of the best sandy beaches and freshwater swimming options in the area and breathtaking views of the Green Mountains of Vermont.
  • St. Albans Bay Park: This beautiful waterfront park is perfect for swimming, kayaking, and picnicking, making it a great place to relax and soak in the natural beauty of the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Champlain and Rouses Point

  • Rail Trail: This trail is perfect for hiking, walking, and cycling enthusiasts, running 10 miles south from Rouses Point through Champlain and Chazy, and is part of the larger Champlain Valley Trail system.
  • Montgomery’s Point Preserve: This nature preserve covers 80 acres and features several trails and docks, offering visitors the chance to see a variety of wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Champlain and Rouses Point: Activities and Tours

  • Noyes House Museum Tour: Guided tours of this beautiful historic house offer visitors an insight into the life of the Noyes family and the social norms of the 19th century.
  • Champlain Valley Transportation Museum Tour: This museum offers a unique glimpse into the history and evolution of transportation in the region, with vintage automobiles and railcars on display.
  • Champlain Valley Ghost Tours: Explore the haunted side of Champlain and Rouses Point on these spooky walking tours, led by expert guides who share ghost stories and folklore of the local area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Champlain and Rouses Point

Day 1: Visit Fort Montgomery State Historic Site and Cumberland Bay State Park

Day 2: Tour the Samuel de Champlain History Center, hike Rail Trail

Day 3: Visit the Noyes House Museum, take a stroll along the waterfront

Day 4: Visit Alburg Dunes State Park, explore the Champlain Valley Transportation Museum

Day 5: Experience a Champlain Valley Ghost Tour, visit Rouses Point Train Station Museum

Day 6: Explore St. Albans Bay Park, hike Montgomery’s Point Preserve

Day 7: Enjoy a leisurely day trip to Vermont or Montreal, Canada.

Food and Drink Scene of Champlain and Rouses Point

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lake Champlain Walleye: This freshwater fish is a regional specialty and must-try dish.
  • Maple Syrup: This area is famous for its maple syrup, and you can find it on almost every menu
  • Vermont Cheese: Champlain and Rouses Point are located close to Vermont, which is known for its delicious cheese, making local markets and farm stands great places for cheese sampling.

Local Beverages

  • Lake Champlain Chocolates: No visit to Champlain is complete without indulging in some of the best chocolates in the region, made with pure, local ingredients.
  • The Fainting Goat Island Inn: Offering craft beers and a wide selection of wines, this is a great place to unwind and relax.
  • Apple Cider: If you’re visiting in the fall, you can’t miss out on the local apple cider, which is a must-try drink.

Where to Stay in Champlain and Rouses Point: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Fainting Goat Island Inn: This cozy inn has lovely boutique rooms and offers breathtaking views of Lake Champlain.
  • Champlain House Bed & Breakfast: This historic bed and breakfast offers four beautiful guest rooms and a gourmet breakfast
  • Le Bocage Country Inn and Restaurant: This country-style inn was built in 1907 and has lovely rooms, each with a distinct theme, along with in-house restaurant with a delightful food scene

What to Buy in Champlain and Rouses Point: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maple Syrup: Local made maple syrup is a great souvenir and makes for a delicious gift for family or friends.
  • Handmade Crafts: Local artisans make handmade crafts, such as quilts and pottery, which make great souvenirs or gifts.
  • Benton’s Sugar Shack & Market: This local market has locally sourced everyday groceries along with souvenirs and various maple products to choose from.

Local Culture and Customs of Champlain and Rouses Point

Champlain and Rouses Point have a unique culture, with a distinct focus on history, nature, and outdoor living. People in the area are friendly and relaxed, often welcoming visitors with open arms. Visitors are encouraged to take the time to get to know the locals, who are always helpful when it comes to finding the best spots to visit or the most delicious local foods to try.

Best Time to Visit Champlain and Rouses Point

The best time to visit is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and the parks and outdoor attractions are open for visitors. However, the fall season (September - November) with the foliage and maple harvests is just as beautiful and not as crowded, while the spring (April to May) offers fewer crowds, comfortable temperatures, and a chance to see the region come back to life.

Do’s and Don’ts of Champlain and Rouses Point

Do:

  • Be friendly and polite to the locals.
  • Take the time to explore the nature parks and outdoor attractions.
  • Enjoy the local dining and drink scene, including regional beers, wine, and maple syrup.
  • Shop for local souvenirs at local shops and markets.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places.
  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.
  • Disturb the wildlife in the area.
  • Swim in areas that are unsafe or prohibited.

Safety and Health

Safety in Champlain and Rouses Point

Champlain and Rouses Point are both safe places to visit. Visitors should still take the usual precautions, such as staying vigilant, locking cars, and not leaving valuables in plain sight.

Health in Champlain and Rouses Point

Regarding health concerns, it is always advisable to carry basic first-aid kits, anti-inflammatory medication, and sunscreen while out and about in the region. Also, visitors must practice caution while hiking and exploring the wilder sections of the parks and natural reserves.

Getting to Champlain and Rouses Point: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Champlain and Rouses Point

Burlington International Airport (VT) is the nearest airport, located approximately 45 minutes away from Champlain and Rouses Point.

Bus Stations Near Champlain and Rouses Point

There are frequent bus services running between New York City and Rouses Point, stopping at several locations in between. Greyhound Bus company offers daily trips between the two destinations.

Other Options

Car rentals and private taxi services are readily available for visitors who prefer the convenience of personal transportation.

How to Get Around in Champlain and Rouses Point

Visitors can hire bicycles, rent cars or use taxis to explore the area. There are also state park shuttles that can transport visitors around Cumberland Bay State Park.

Where to Go Next from Champlain and Rouses Point: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Montreal, Canada: Located just one hour away, Montreal is a charming city with a vibrant French culture and cuisine that offers a perfect day trip from Champlain and Rouses Point.
  • Lake Placid: A popular tourist destination, home of two winter Olympics games, Lake Placid is a scenic hour and half drive with the stunning Adirondack mountains all along the way.

Conclusion

Champlain and Rouses Point have something for everyone, from nature lovers and history buffs to culinary enthusiasts and families in search of a relaxing retreat. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip and make the most of your visit. With breath-taking natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and delicious local food and drink scenes, it’s no surprise that Champlain and Rouses Point has something for everyone.
