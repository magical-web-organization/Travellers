Introduction to Lowville

Lowville, a small town in North Country, New York is a hidden gem for those who love exploring historical sites, outdoor adventures, and scenic views. Located in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States, Lowville is surrounded by the Adirondack Mountains and the Black River Valley. This guide will help you plan your trip to Lowville, and explore its many attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Lowville

Here’s a list of must-visit places in Lowville:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lowville Farmers Co-Op - Established in 1947, the Lowville Farmers Co-Op is a great place to learn about farming practices and the importance of local agriculture.
  • Maple Ridge Center - This 150-acre property includes a farm and forest museum, a maple syrup museum, and demonstration trails to showcase the life of the country people of the 1800s.
  • Lewis County Historical Society Museum - The museum features artifacts, exhibits, and interactive displays on Lewis County history from 1805 to the present day.

Natural Attractions of Lowville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tug Hill State Forest - This 130,000-acre forest offers hiking, biking, skiing, snowmobiling, and fishing opportunities.
  • Whetstone Gulf State Park - This park features a scenic canyon with three waterfalls and a nature trail.
  • Black River Trail - This 8-mile trail along the Black River is perfect for a relaxing stroll or a bike ride.
  • Croghan Island Mill Site - Located on the Black River, this historic site was once home to a sawmill and a grist mill.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lowville

Lowville is a great place for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the popular routes:

  • Tug Hill Trail - This 46-mile route takes you through pine forests and scenic vistas in the Tug Hill region.
  • Whetstone Gulf Trail - This 6.5-mile trail takes you through Whetstone Gulf State Park and offers beautiful views.
  • Otter Creek Trail - This 5-mile trail along Otter Creek offers scenic views and birdwatching opportunities.

Best Things to Do in Lowville: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Lowville:

  • Explore the Lewis County Fairgrounds - The annual county fair is held here every summer.
  • Go fishing on the Black River - The river is known for its brown trout, rainbow trout, and smallmouth bass.
  • Attend the Lowville Cream Cheese Festival – A festival dedicated to Lowville’s famous cream cheese.
  • Go apple picking at Burrville Cider Mill - This family-owned cider mill offers apple picking, donut making, and cider tasting events.

Food and Drink Scene of Lowville

Experience Lowville’s unique cuisine scene, including must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lowville Cream Cheese - A locally produced cream cheese with a soft, creamy texture and tangy flavor.
  • Coney Island Hot Dogs - A Lowville staple, these hot dogs are topped with mustard, onions, and meat sauce.
  • Panning Pancakes - A traditional breakfast dish made with cornmeal.

Local Beverages

  • Burrville Cider - Made from locally grown apples, this cider has a sweet, crisp taste.
  • Old Tug Brewery - Local brewery that produces a variety of craft beers, including IPAs, stouts, and lagers.

Local Culture and Customs of Lowville

Lowville has a rich cultural heritage. The town prides itself on its farming community and agriculture heritage. The town holds festivals and fairs to celebrate its local culture.

Best Time to Visit Lowville

The best time to visit Lowville is from May to October when the weather is temperate and perfect for outdoor activities. However, winter offers excellent opportunities for snowmobiling and skiing.
