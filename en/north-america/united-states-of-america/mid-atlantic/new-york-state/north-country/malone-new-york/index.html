Introduction to Malone (New York)

Located in the North Country region of New York, Malone is a town that is rich in history and natural beauty. It is a quiet escape from the hustle and bustle of city life, where visitors can enjoy a range of outdoor activities, explore the local culture and customs, and indulge in the town’s food and drink scene. In this travel guide, we will provide you with everything you need to know to plan your trip to Malone (New York).

List of Places to Visit in Malone (New York)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Malone is home to several culturally important attractions, including museums, historical sites, and galleries. As you explore Malone, be sure to check out:

  • Franklin County Historical and Museum Society: Featuring exhibits and artifacts from the history of Malone and Franklin County
  • Malone Railroad Depot Museum: A restored depot museum showcasing the heritage of the railroad in Northern New York
  • North Star Underground Railroad Museum: Dedicated to the history of the Underground Railroad in the North Country Region
  • The Art Works Gallery: A local gallery showcasing the works of local artists

Natural Attractions of Malone (New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Malone’s natural beauty offers visitors the opportunity to partake in a range of outdoor adventures and experience breathtaking scenic views. Some of the best natural attractions to explore in Malone include:

  • Adirondack Park: A vast park featuring six million acres of public and private land, perfect for hiking, camping, and exploring.
  • Titus Mountain Family Ski Center: A family-owned ski resort that offers skiing, snowboarding, and tubing during the winter months.
  • Salmon River Falls: A picturesque waterfall and gorge offering a scenic hike and picnic area.
  • Jennings Park: A local park featuring a beach, hiking trails, and picnic facilities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Malone (New York)

Malone is an excellent destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. There are several popular hiking and biking routes in the area, including:

  • The Malone Recreational Trail: A 12-mile paved trail for hikers, bikers, and joggers.
  • Lyon Mountain Trail: A challenging hike with stunning vistas of the Adirondacks at the top.
  • Coney Mountain Trail: A moderate hike with beautiful views of Tupper Lake and the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Malone (New York): Activities and Tours

In addition to exploring the local attractions and enjoying the natural beauty of Malone, there are several other activities and tours to experience during your visit, including:

  • Malone Auto Museum: Featuring a collection of vintage cars and memorabilia.
  • Raquette River Outfitters: Offering guided fishing trips and canoe rentals to explore the rivers and lakes of the Adirondacks.
  • Tour de Malone: A cycling event held annually that explores the best routes and scenery of the area.
  • Golfing: Malone is home to several golf courses like Malone Golf Club and it is a great destination for golf enthusiasts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Malone (New York)

Day 1: Visit the Adirondack Park and hike or camp at one of the many beautiful natural areas.

Day 2: Explore Malone’s museums and galleries, including the Franklin County Historical and Museum Society and Art Works Gallery.

Day 3: Experience the area’s natural beauty by taking a guided fishing trip or canoeing down the Raquette River with Raquette River Outfitters.

Day 4: Go skiing or snowboarding at Titus Mountain Family Ski Center during the winter season.

Day 5: Take a break and enjoy a round of golfing at Malone’s Golf Courses.

Day 6: Explore the area’s hiking and biking trails, such as the Malone Recreational Trail and Lyon Mountain Trail.

Day 7: Enjoy one of the town’s many activities or tours, such as Tour de Malone or a visit to the Malone Auto Museum.

Food and Drink Scene of Malone (New York)

Must-Try Dishes

While visiting Malone, be sure to try some of the local specialties, including:

  • Poutine: A classic Northern New York dish featuring fries, cheese curds, and gravy.
  • Maple Syrup: A local specialty produced in nearby Adirondack forests.
  • Trout: Fresh-caught from the area’s many rivers and lakes.

Local Beverages

Malone is home to several breweries, wineries, and distilleries. Some of the popular local beverages to try include:

  • Great Adirondack Brewing Company: A local brewery offering a range of craft beers.
  • Hardscrabble Cider: A family-owned cidery producing hard apple cider in the nearby Adirondack Mountains.
  • Northern Lights Chocolate Factory: A local chocolate shop that also features wines and spirits.

Where to Stay in Malone (New York): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Malone offers a range of accommodation options for every budget, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the top options include:

  • Hotel Saranac: A historic hotel located in nearby Saranac Lake that offers grand accommodations and dining.
  • Red Roof Inn & Suites: Affordable hotel with modern amenities and good location.
  • The Porcupine Inn: A charming guesthouse featuring antique decor and homemade breakfasts.

What to Buy in Malone (New York): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are several souvenirs and local products to bring back home to remember your time in Malone, including:

  • Maple Syrup: Produced in nearby Adirondack forests.
  • Adirondack Handcrafted Furniture: The town is home to several artisans producing handmade furniture.
  • Adirondack Trivet Placemats: A beautiful and functional souvenir that is produced locally.

Local Culture and Customs of Malone (New York)

Malone’s local culture is heavily influenced by its proximity to the Adirondack Mountains and its agricultural heritage. The area’s slow pace of life and strong community values are also reflected in its culture and customs.

Best Time to Visit Malone (New York)

The best time to visit Malone is during the summer months when you can take full advantage of the outdoor activities and enjoy a range of festivals and events. The fall season is also beautiful with colorful foliage in the area.

Do’s and Don’ts of Malone (New York)

Do:

  • Do spend time exploring the natural beauty of Adirondacks.
  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages, including poutine, maple syrup, and hard apple cider.
  • Do attend one of the town’s many festivals and events, such as the famous Radio Auction or Malone’s Annual Poutine Fest.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to check the weather before heading out into the wilderness or embarking on any outdoor adventures.
  • Don’t visit during the winter months if you’re not a fan of extreme snow and cold.
  • Don’t miss the opportunity to learn about the area’s rich cultural and historical heritage in the area’s museums and galleries.

Safety and Health

Safety in Malone (New York)

Malone is a safe town, but visitors should always take necessary precautions when exploring the wilderness or taking part in outdoor activities. Make sure to familiarize with local safety requirements.

Health in Malone (New York)

It is recommended that visitors ensure they have necessary medical and travel insurance coverage. The area does not have many large medical facilities but you will find small local health centers and pharmacies.

Getting to Malone (New York): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Malone (New York)

The nearest airport to Malone is the Adirondack Regional Airport located in Saranac Lake, which operates regional flights. Major international airports like Albany and Montreal are also nearby.

Bus Stations Near Malone (New York)

The nearest bus station to Malone is in nearby Plattsburgh. Greyhound runs scheduled services connecting Plattsburgh with most other cities in the US.

Other Options

Depending on where you’re coming from, driving can be a great option. Major highways like Interstate-87 and Interstate-81 allow visitors to reach Malone from various nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Malone (New York)

Renting a car is the best way to get around in Malone as local public transport options are limited. Alternatively, you can choose to walk, bike, or hike.

Where to Go Next from Malone (New York): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Malone’s central location makes it an excellent base to explore the Adirondacks and other nearby destinations. Some of the places worth visiting nearby are:

  • Lake Placid: A famous Olympic village with stunning scenery and exciting outdoor activities.
  • Thousand Islands: A beautiful destination in the St. Lawrence River with numerous islands and waterways.
  • Montreal: A vibrant city with a unique fusion of French and English culture.

Conclusion

Malone is a hidden gem of North New York State full of natural beauty, local culture, and friendly people. Enjoy the scenic beauty, outdoor activities and indulge in local cuisine, and make unforgettable memories.
