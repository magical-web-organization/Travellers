Introduction to Clayton (New York)

Clayton is a charming village located in North Country, New York. Situated on the banks of the St. Lawrence River and the Thousand Islands region, Clayton is a popular tourist destination that offers a perfect mix of natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and cultural experiences. This quaint village is home to several museums, art galleries, gardens, and historical sites that pay homage to the region’s rich cultural heritage.

Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or simply looking for a relaxing getaway, Clayton has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ve compiled all the information you need to plan the perfect trip to Clayton. From must-visit attractions to hiking and biking routes, activities and tours to food and drink scene, where to stay to local culture and customs, safety and health to transportation options and nearby destinations, we’ve got you covered.

List of Places to Visit in Clayton (New York)

Here’s a list of the top places to visit in Clayton:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Antique Boat Museum: This museum houses one of the largest and most diverse collections of antique boats in the world, showcasing the rich maritime history of the St. Lawrence River and the Thousand Islands region.
  • Boldt Castle: This historic castle, located on Heart Island, was built by millionaire George Boldt as a tribute to his beloved wife, Louise. The castle is now open to the public and offers guided tours, scenic views, and beautiful gardens.
  • Clayton Island Tours: Take a narrated boat tour of the Thousand Islands region and learn about the history and culture of the area.
  • Thousand Islands Arts Center: This art center features a gallery, hands-on arts and crafts classes, and a gift shop selling local handmade crafts and artwork.
  • Thousand Islands Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of the Thousand Islands region through exhibits, artifacts, and photographs.

Natural Attractions of Clayton (New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Thousand Islands Bridge: This 8-mile bridge provides scenic views of the St. Lawrence River and connects Clayton to Canada.
  • Grass Point State Park: This beautiful park offers hiking trails, picnic areas, fishing, and swimming.
  • Wellesley Island State Park: This state park features miles of hiking trails, camping facilities, swimming, and boating.
  • Thousand Islands National Park: This park features over 20 islands accessible by boat, as well as hiking trails, kayaking, and camping.
  • Clayton Municipal Harbor: This harbor offers boat rentals and scenic views of the St. Lawrence River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Clayton (New York)

Clayton offers several hiking and biking trails that allow visitors to explore the natural beauty of the region. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Clayton Marina Trail: This 2-mile trail winds along the St. Lawrence River and offers scenic views of the harbor and surrounding islands.
  • The Frink Memorial Trail: This 6.2-mile trail features scenic views of the St. Lawrence River and the Thousand Islands region.
  • Minna Anthony Common Nature Center: This nature center offers several hiking trails that allow visitors to observe wildlife and explore the natural beauty of the area.
  • Wellesley Island State Park Trails: This state park features over 12 miles of hiking and biking trails that offer scenic views of the St. Lawrence River and the surrounding islands.

Best Things to Do in Clayton (New York): Activities and Tours

There are many activities and tours to enjoy in Clayton, from boat tours to food tours. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Clayton Island Tours: Take a narrated boat tour of the Thousand Islands region and learn about the history and culture of the area.
  • Uncle Sam Boat Tours: This boat tour offers several options, including a 2-Nation Tour, a Sunset Cruise, and a Boldt Castle Tour.
  • Clayton Food Tours: This walking tour takes visitors on a culinary journey through Clayton, exploring the local food scene and sampling local delicacies.
  • Clayton Distillery: This distillery offers tours and tastings of their award-winning spirits.

Week-Long Itinerary for Clayton (New York)

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for Clayton:

  • Day 1: Explore the Antique Boat Museum and take a stroll along the Clayton Marina Trail.
  • Day 2: Take a boat tour of the Thousand Islands region and visit Boldt Castle.
  • Day 3: Go hiking in the Minna Anthony Common Nature Center and explore the Thousand Islands Arts Center.
  • Day 4: Take a day trip to visit nearby Alexandria Bay and the Thousand Islands.
  • Day 5: Enjoy a sunset cruise on the St. Lawrence River.
  • Day 6: Visit the Clayton Distillery, and go fishing or swimming at Grass Point State Park.
  • Day 7: Spend the day at Wellesley Island State Park, hiking and exploring the beauty of the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Clayton (New York)

Clayton offers a range of dining options, from chic waterfront restaurants to casual cafes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish Fry: Deep-fried fish served with french fries, coleslaw, and tartar sauce.
  • Poutine: French fries with cheese curds and gravy, a Canadian classic.
  • Lobster Roll: Fresh lobster meat served on a roll with lettuce and mayo.

Local Beverages

  • Thousand Islands Winery: This winery offers hand-crafted wines from grapes grown in the region.
  • Dark Island Spirits: This distillery offers a range of spirits made from locally sourced ingredients, including vodka, gin, and whiskey.
  • Clayton Distillery: This distillery offers tours and tastings of their award-winning spirits.

Where to Stay in Clayton (New York): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Clayton offers a range of accommodation options, from cozy guesthouses to luxury resorts. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • The Inn at Harbor Hill Marina: This cozy inn offers waterfront rooms and suites, as well as a private beach and dock.

Local Culture and Customs of Clayton (New York)

Clayton has a rich cultural heritage, reflected in its architecture, museums, and institutions. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, especially when visiting cultural attractions. Locals are friendly and welcoming, and visitors should reciprocate this hospitality.

Best Time to Visit Clayton (New York)

The best time to visit Clayton is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and most attractions are open. The fall months of September and October also offer beautiful foliage and cooler temperatures, perfect for hiking and outdoor activities. The winter months can be cold and snowy but offer a unique beauty and opportunities for winter sports.

Safety and Health

Safety in Clayton (New York)

Clayton is a safe village, but visitors should take standard precautions, such as locking cars and securing valuables. Local emergency services are available.

Health in Clayton (New York)

Visitors should drink plenty of water and apply sunscreen when spending time outdoors. Medical facilities are available in nearby communities.

Getting to Clayton (New York): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Clayton (New York)

The closest airport to Clayton is Watertown International Airport, located approximately 20 miles away. Other nearby airports include Syracuse Hancock International Airport and Ottawa Macdonald-Cartier International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Clayton (New York)

Greyhound and Trailways offer bus service to nearby cities such as Watertown.

Other Options

Driving is the best option for getting around Clayton and the surrounding area. Rental cars are available at nearby airports and in downtown Clayton.

How to Get Around in Clayton (New York)

Visitors can easily explore Clayton on foot or by bike. Car rentals are available for those who want to travel further afield.

Where to Go Next from Clayton (New York): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many nearby destinations and excursions to enjoy from Clayton. Here are some ideas:

  • Alexandria Bay: This town is located just 7 miles from Clayton and offers boat tours, shops, and restaurants.
  • Sackets Harbor: This historic village is located approximately 30 miles from Clayton and offers museums, historic sites, and scenic views of Lake Ontario.
  • Kingston, Ontario: This Canadian city is located across the St. Lawrence River and offers a range of cultural and historical attractions.
