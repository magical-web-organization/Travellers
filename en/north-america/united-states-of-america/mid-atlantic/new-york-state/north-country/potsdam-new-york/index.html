Potsdam, located in the North Country region of New York, is a charming town that offers visitors a unique experience that blends history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in exploring museums and historical landmarks or outdoor adventures and scenic views, Potsdam has something for everyone. In this guide, we’ll take you through some of the best attractions, activities, and experiences that Potsdam has to offer.

Introduction to Potsdam (New York)

Potsdam is a town in St. Lawrence County that boasts a population of approximately 17,000 residents. The town is home to two colleges - SUNY Potsdam and Clarkson University, which contributes to the vibrant and dynamic atmosphere of the town. Potsdam’s history dates back to the late 18th century when it was founded by a group of settlers from New England. Today, Potsdam offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Potsdam (New York)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. The Frederic Remington Art Museum

The Frederic Remington Art Museum is a must-visit for art lovers. The museum is dedicated to showcasing the works of Frederic Remington, who was famous for his depictions of the American West. The museum also houses exhibits on other artists and their work.

2. The Potsdam Public Museum

The Potsdam Public Museum is a small, but informative museum that details the history of the town and its people. Museum exhibits include photographs, documents, and artifacts from the town’s early days to present times.

3. The St. Lawrence Power and Equipment Museum

The St. Lawrence Power and Equipment Museum is a unique museum that showcases antique tractors, engines, and other equipment. Visitors can witness a piece of history come to life as they explore the exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Potsdam (New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Clarkson Outdoor Recreation Park

The Clarkson Outdoor Recreation Park is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The park offers miles of hiking and biking trails, fishing opportunities, and a 9-hole golf course.

2. Higley Flow State Park

Higley Flow State Park is another popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors can enjoy hiking, fishing, boating, and camping on the park’s 1,400-acre property.

3. Raquette River

Raquette River is a scenic river that winds through Potsdam. Visitors can take a canoe or kayak down the river for a relaxing and picturesque experience.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Potsdam (New York)

1. Stone Valley Trail

The Stone Valley Trail is a 6.5-mile trail that offers hikers stunning views of the Stone Valley Creek and a series of waterfalls, natural pools, and rocky cliffs.

2. Red Sandstone Trail

The Red Sandstone Trail is a 7-mile trail that takes visitors through the picturesque forests and scenic views around the Raquette River.

3. Clarkson Trails

Clarkson Trails consist of over 20 miles of trails that are perfect for hiking, biking, and cross-country skiing. These trails offer visitors a unique experience that combines outdoor adventure and scenic views.

Best Things to Do in Potsdam (New York): Activities and Tours

1. Potsdam Farmers’ Market

The Potsdam Farmers’ Market is a local farmer’s market that takes place every Saturday. Visitors can enjoy fresh produce, handmade crafts, and live music.

2. Potsdam Specialty Paper Tour

Potsdam Specialty Paper Tour is a unique tour that takes visitors behind the scenes of Potsdam’s famous paper mill. Visitors can witness the paper-making process up close and personal.

3. The Potsdam Public Library

The Potsdam Public Library is a beautiful building that dates back to the 19th century. Visitors can explore the library’s vast collections of books and other materials or attend lectures and workshops.

Week-Long Itinerary for Potsdam (New York)

Day 1: Explore the Frederic Remington Art Museum and the Potsdam Public Museum. Day 2: Hike the Stone Valley Trail and enjoy the scenic views along the way. Day 3: Take a canoe or kayak down the Raquette River. Day 4: Visit the St. Lawrence Power and Equipment Museum. Day 5: Enjoy the Potsdam Farmers’ Market and attend a workshop at the Potsdam Public Library. Day 6: Hike or bike the Red Sandstone Trail. Day 7: Play a round of golf at Clarkson Outdoor Recreation Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Potsdam (New York)

Must-Try Dishes

1. Potsdam Bunny

Potsdam Bunny is a local dish that features a combination of chicken, vegetables, and potatoes, smothered in gravy and topped with a biscuit.

2. Apple Cider Donuts

Apple Cider Donuts are a popular treat in Potsdam. The donuts are made from locally sourced apples and are a local favorite.

Local Beverages

1. North Country Brewing Company

The North Country Brewing Company is a local brewery that offers a variety of craft beers and local ciders.

2. Jake’s on the Water

Jake’s on the Water is a cozy bar that overlooks the Raquette River. The bar offers a variety of local wines and craft beers.

Where to Stay in Potsdam (New York): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

1. The Clarkson Inn

The Clarkson Inn is a historic hotel that dates back to the 19th century. The hotel offers visitors a unique experience that combines old-world charm and modern amenities.

2. Brambles Inn and Gardens

Brambles Inn and Gardens is a bed and breakfast that offers guests a peaceful and relaxing experience. The Inn is surrounded by lush gardens and offers a variety of amenities, including a spa and sauna.

What to Buy in Potsdam (New York): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Pure Maple Syrup

Pure Maple Syrup is a local favorite in Potsdam. Visitors can purchase this tasty treat at any of the local farmers’ markets or specialty stores.

2. Handmade Crafts

Potsdam is home to a variety of talented artisans. Visitors can purchase locally made pottery, jewelry, and other handmade crafts at any of the local craft fairs or specialty shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Potsdam (New York)

Potsdam is a town that prides itself on its diversity and inclusivity. Visitors can expect to find a town that is welcoming and open. It’s important to be respectful of the local culture and customs, including dressing modestly and being mindful of your surroundings.

Best Time to Visit Potsdam (New York)

The best time to visit Potsdam is during the summer and fall months. The weather is mild, and visitors can enjoy outdoor activities without worrying about extreme temperatures.

Safety and Health

Potsdam is a relatively safe town with a low crime rate. Visitors should take the usual precautions, such as locking their cars and securing their belongings.

Health in Potsdam (New York)

Potsdam has a variety of health care options, including clinics and hospitals. Visitors should be aware of the local health care options and bring any necessary medications with them.

Getting to Potsdam (New York): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Potsdam (New York)

The closest airport to Potsdam is the Ogdensburg International Airport, which is approximately 25 miles away. Other nearby airports include the Watertown International Airport and the Syracuse Hancock International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Potsdam (New York)

The Potsdam bus station is located in the center of town and is served by several bus companies, including Greyhound and Trailways.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Potsdam or take a train to the nearby city of Albany.

How to Get Around Potsdam (New York)

Potsdam is a small town, and visitors can easily get around on foot or by bike. Taxis and car rentals are also available.

Where to Go Next from Potsdam (New York): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

2. Ottawa, Canada

Ottawa, Canada is a nearby city that offers visitors a unique experience that blends Canadian and European culture. Ottawa is just a few hours’ drive from Potsdam.
