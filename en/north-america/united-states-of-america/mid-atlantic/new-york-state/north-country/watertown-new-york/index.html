Introduction to Watertown (New York)

Located in the North Country region of upstate New York, Watertown is a charming city with a rich history and diverse culture. It is known for its beautiful natural scenery, outdoor activities, and cultural attractions. The city is situated on the banks of the Black River and offers visitors a unique blend of small-town charm and big-city amenities.

List of Places to Visit in Watertown (New York)

If you’re planning a trip to Watertown, these are the top places you don’t want to miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jefferson County Historical Society - This museum features exhibits and collections that tell the story of Watertown and its surrounding communities.
  • Roswell P. Flower Memorial Library - Visit this historic library to admire its stunning architecture and beautiful gardens.
  • New York State Zoo at Thompson Park - This zoo offers a fun and educational experience for the whole family, with over 100 different species of animals.

Natural Attractions of Watertown (New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Thompson Park - This park offers stunning views of the Black River and is home to a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, and picnic areas.
  • Black River Trail - This trail is ideal for hiking and biking, offering stunning views of the Black River and surrounding scenery.
  • Whetstone Gulf State Park - This park is known for its rugged beauty, with deep gorges, waterfalls, and breathtaking views. It offers a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Watertown (New York)

Watertown is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes to explore:

  • Black River Trail - This 4-mile trail is ideal for hiking and biking and offers some of the best views of the Black River in the area.
  • Whetstone Gulf State Park Trails - These trails offer a range of difficulty levels, from easy to challenging, and take visitors through some of the most beautiful parts of the park.
  • Dry Hill Ski Area Trails - During the summer months, this ski area transforms into a mountain biking haven, with over 6 miles of challenging trails to explore.

Best Things to Do in Watertown (New York): Activities and Tours

Watertown has plenty of activities and tours to keep visitors entertained. Some of the best options include:

  • Boat Tour of the Black River - Take a scenic ride down the Black River and admire the stunning scenery from a unique perspective.
  • Watertown Escape Room - This interactive experience challenges visitors to solve puzzles and escape from a locked room within a designated time frame.
  • Riverquest Kayak Adventure - This popular tour takes visitors on a guided kayak trip down the Black River, offering a unique and exciting adventure.

Week-Long Itinerary for Watertown (New York)

If you have a week to spend in Watertown, here is a suggested itinerary to make the most of your time:

Day 1: Arrive and explore downtown Watertown, visit Roswell P. Flower Memorial Library.

Day 2: Visit Jefferson County Historical Society, enjoy a hike in Thompson Park.

Day 3: Take a boat tour down the Black River, explore Whetstone Gulf State Park.

Day 4: Visit New York State Zoo at Thompson Park, try a local restaurant for lunch or dinner.

Day 5: Discover Dry Hill Ski Area Trails, take a kayaking tour with Riverquest.

Day 6: Spend the day outdoors in Black River Trail, enjoy a picnic in the park.

Day 7: Visit any of the places you’ve missed and buy souvenirs from local stores.

Food and Drink Scene of Watertown (New York)

No trip to Watertown is complete without trying the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken Riggies - A local specialty, this dish features chicken and rigatoni pasta cooked in a spicy tomato-cream sauce.
  • Fish Fry - This classic dish features beer-battered fried fish and French fries, served with tartar sauce and lemon.
  • Poutine - This Canadian classic has made its way to Watertown and consists of French fries, cheese curds, and gravy.

Local Beverages

  • Thousand Islands Winery - This award-winning winery offers a variety of wines, including Chardonnay, Cabernet, and Riesling.
  • Sackets Harbor Brewing Company - Visit this local brewery to try their signature craft beers, including the War of 1812 Amber Ale and the 1812 Stout.
  • Black River Distillery - This distillery produces a variety of spirits, including vodka, gin, and bourbon.

Where to Stay in Watertown (New York): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Watertown offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Some of the best options include:

  • Hilton Garden Inn Watertown - This hotel offers modern amenities and comfortable rooms in a central location.
  • Best Western Watertown/Fort Drum - This hotel is conveniently located near Fort Drum and offers a range of amenities, including a fitness center and indoor pool.
  • Watertown International Hostel - This hostel offers affordable accommodation in shared dormitories, as well as private rooms.

What to Buy in Watertown (New York): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home, these are the best options in Watertown:

  • Thousand Islands Winery Wine - Bring home a bottle of award-winning wine from this local winery.
  • Maple Syrup - Maple syrup is a popular product in upstate New York, and Watertown is no exception.
  • Black River Distillery Spirits - If you’re a fan of spirits, bring home a bottle of vodka, gin, or bourbon from Black River Distillery.

Local Culture and Customs of Watertown (New York)

Watertown has a rich history and a diverse population, with a strong military presence due to the nearby Fort Drum. Visitors are encouraged to explore the local attractions and interact with the friendly locals to get a true sense of the city’s unique culture.

Best Time to Visit Watertown (New York)

The best time to visit Watertown is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and ideal for outdoor activities. However, the fall months of September and October also offer stunning scenery with the changing leaves, and winter sports enthusiasts will enjoy the skiing and snowboarding opportunities in the area.

Do’s and Don’ts of Watertown (New York)

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Watertown:

Do:

  • Explore the great outdoors and take advantage of the natural attractions in the area.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages, including Thousand Islands Wine and poutine.
  • Take a boat tour down the Black River for a unique perspective of the scenery.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the rich history and culture of Watertown by only focusing on the outdoor activities.
  • Miss out on the chance to try some of the local beer and spirits, including Sackets Harbor Brewing and Black River Distillery.
  • Forget to bring a camera to capture the stunning scenery and memorable moments.

Safety and Health

Safety in Watertown (New York)

Watertown is a safe city, but as with any travel destination, visitors should take precautions to stay safe. Always keep valuables out of sight and be aware of your surroundings, especially at night.

Health in Watertown (New York)

Watertown has excellent healthcare facilities, including Samaritan Medical Center and Carthage Area Hospital. Visitors should ensure they have proper medical insurance coverage before traveling.

Getting to Watertown (New York): Transportation Options and Logistics

Watertown is easily accessible by various transportation options. Here are the best ways to get there:

Airports Near Watertown (New York)

  • Watertown International Airport - Located just 8 miles from downtown Watertown, this airport offers domestic flights to and from major cities such as Albany, Boston, and Washington, D.C.

Bus Stations Near Watertown (New York)

  • Greyhound Bus Terminal - Located in downtown Watertown, this bus station offers both regional and national bus services.

Other Options

  • Car Rental - Renting a car is a convenient option for visitors, as it allows for flexibility and easy access to the surrounding areas.
  • Train - There are several Amtrak train stations within a short drive of Watertown, offering services to and from major cities such as New York and Toronto.

How to Get Around in Watertown (New York)

Getting around Watertown is easy and convenient, with a variety of transportation options available. These include:

  • Car Rental - Renting a car is the most convenient option, as it allows for easy access to the surrounding areas.
  • Public Transportation - Jefferson County Public Transportation offers bus services throughout the city and surrounding areas.
  • Biking/Walking - Watertown is a bike- and pedestrian-friendly city, with several bike trails and walking paths throughout the area.

Where to Go Next from Watertown (New York): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Watertown is ideally located near several other destinations and excursions that visitors may want to explore:

  • Thousand Islands - Located just a short drive away, the Thousand Islands offer stunning scenery and a range of outdoor activities, including boating and fishing.
  • Adirondack Mountains - This popular destination is known for its stunning natural beauty, outdoor activities, and charming small towns.
  • Ottawa, Canada - Just a few hours’ drive from Watertown, Ottawa is a beautiful city with a rich history and diverse culture.

Conclusion

Watertown is a hidden gem in upstate New York, offering visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, outdoor activities, and cultural attractions. Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or foodie, there is something for everyone in this charming city. Plan your trip today and discover all that Watertown has to offer!
