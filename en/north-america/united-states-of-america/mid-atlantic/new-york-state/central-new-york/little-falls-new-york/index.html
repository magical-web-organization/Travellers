Little Falls (New York) Travel Guide

Welcome to Little Falls, a charming city in Central New York that is known for its rich history, natural beauty, and cultural significance. Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or foodie, this city has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll help you plan your trip to Little Falls with an extensive list of top attractions, things to do, where to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Little Falls (New York)

Here’s a list of the top attractions and places to visit in Little Falls:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Herkimer Home State Historic Site
  • Little Falls Historical Society Museum
  • Little Falls Antique Center
  • Mohawk Valley Center for the Arts

Natural Attractions of Little Falls (New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Moss Island
  • Lock 17 Canal Park
  • Rock City Park
  • Little Falls Diamond Mines
  • Erie Canalway National Heritage Corridor

Hiking/Biking Routes of Little Falls (New York)

Little Falls has several scenic hiking and biking trails that offer a great opportunity to explore the city’s natural beauty. Here are a few:

  • Canalway Trail
  • Moss Island Trail
  • Rock City Trail
  • Little Falls Canal Harbor Trail

Best Things to Do in Little Falls (New York): Activities and Tours

  • Take a scenic boat tour along the Erie Canal
  • Visiting the Little Falls Farmers Market
  • Attend the Mohawk Valley Garlic and Herb Festival
  • Check out the local brewpubs and wineries
  • Explore the city’s arts scene with the Mohawk Valley Center for the Arts

Week-Long Itinerary for Little Falls (New York)

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for your trip to Little Falls:

Day 1: Visit Herkimer Home State Historic Site and Little Falls Historical Society Museum

Day 2: Take a scenic boat tour along the Erie Canal, afterward check out the local brewpubs and wineries

Day 3: Go hiking or biking on one of Little Falls’ many trails, such as Moss Island Trail or Little Falls Canal Harbor Trail. Afterward visiting Little Falls Farmers Market

Day 4: Check out the Little Falls Antique Center

Day 5: Attend the Mohawk Valley Garlic and Herb Festival

Day 6: Explore the city’s arts scene with the Mohawk Valley Center for the Arts

Day 7: Visit Lock 17 Canal Park and Rock City Park

Must-Try Dishes

Local Beverages

  • Utica Club beer

Where to Stay in Little Falls (New York): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some great options for staying in Little Falls:

  • Canal Side Inn - a luxurious bed and breakfast located on the Erie Canal
  • Canal Harbor Inn - another great Bed and Breakfast option located on the canal overlooking the park
  • Knights Inn Little Falls - great budget hotel option located close to many of the city’s attractions

What to Buy in Little Falls (New York): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some great souvenirs and local products to bring back from Little Falls:

  • Half-moon cookies from Hemstrought’s Bakery
  • Beekman 1802 skincare and home goods
  • Local artisan products from the Little Falls Farmers Market

Local Culture and Customs of Little Falls (New York)

Little Falls is a friendly, welcoming community with a rich history and thriving arts scene. Visitors are encouraged to explore the city’s unique culture and to engage with locals.

Best Time to Visit Little Falls (New York)

The best time to visit Little Falls is during the warm months of summer and fall when the weather is pleasant and outdoor activities, such as boating or hiking, are at their best. The Mohawk Valley Garlic and Herb Festival in September is another great reason to plan a trip to Little Falls.

Do’s and Don’ts of Little Falls (New York)

Do:

  • Do take a scenic boat tour along the Erie Canal
  • Do visit the local brewpubs and wineries
  • Do explore the city’s arts scene
  • Do try the local cuisine

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in the city’s natural areas or along the canal
  • Don’t disrespect the city’s historic sites

Safety and Health

Safety in Little Falls (New York)

Little Falls is a relatively safe city with a low crime rate. However, visitors should exercise caution when exploring natural areas or walking alone at night.

Health in Little Falls (New York)

Visitors should take precautions to stay hydrated during hot summer months and should wear protective clothing and use bug spray when hiking or exploring wooded areas.

How to Get Around in Little Falls (New York)

Little Falls is a small city that can be easily navigated by walking or driving.
