Cortland, located in Central New York, offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. Known for its scenic views, Cortland is an ideal destination for those seeking peace and tranquility amidst nature. Whether you are a nature lover, a history buff, or an adventure enthusiast, Cortland has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through the best things to do, places to visit, and local insights that will help you explore the beauty of Cortland.

List of Places to Visit in Cortland

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Cortland has a rich history, evident in its numerous museums and historical landmarks. Visit the Cortland County Historical Society to learn about the region’s past or explore the 1890 House Museum, a beautiful Victorian mansion that provides a glimpse into the lifestyle of the wealthy in the late 19th century. Another must-see is the Lime Hollow Nature Center, which offers educational programs, events, and exhibits on the local flora and fauna.

Natural Attractions of Cortland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Cortland is home to picturesque parks, nature trails, and vast expanses of serene natural beauty. The Finger Lakes Trail is a long-distance hiking trail that stretches through New York’s southern counties, including Cortland, while the Tinker Falls Trail takes hikers through a scenic gorge to the 50-foot Tinker Falls. Cortland also boasts of the Greek Peak Mountain Resort, where you can go skiing or snowboarding in the winter or zip-lining and hiking in the summer.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cortland

Cortland is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts and offers several hiking/biking trails. The Hoxie Gorge Trail is a favorite among locals and a relatively easy hike to take in stunning views of the nearby river. Robert H. Treman State Park is another popular hiking destination with several trails, including the Rim Trail, which offers panoramic views of the park’s waterfalls and gorge. Bike enthusiasts can enjoy the 22-mile long Black Diamond Trail, an unpaved path that stretches between Ithaca and Trumansburg.

Best Things to Do in Cortland: Activities and Tours

Cortland hosts several events and activities year-round, offering something for everyone. Attend the Great Cortland Pumpkinfest, held every October, to see giant pumpkins, carnival rides, and games. The Cortland Arts and Wine Festival, held each August, is another event that shouldn’t be missed. For a unique experience, take a Hot Air Balloon ride over the scenic countryside near Cortland.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cortland

Day 1: Explore the local history and heritage by visiting the Cortland County Historical Society and the 1890 House Museum.

Day 2: Take a hiking trail at the nature center and explore the scenic views of the Lime Hollow Nature Center.

Day 3-4: Go skiing or snowboarding at the Greek Peak Mountain Resort (winter-only).

Day 5: Visit the nearby city of Ithaca and hike the trails around Taughannock Falls State Park.

Day 6: Attend a festival, like the Cortland Arts and Wine Festival or the Great Cortland Pumpkinfest.

Day 7: Take a relaxing hot air balloon ride to see the countryside from a different perspective or go fishing at a nearby lake.

Food and Drink Scene of Cortland

Cortland’s food and drink scene are worth exploring. The local farms provide fresh ingredients, resulting in a farm-to-table experience that is hard to miss. Try the local specialty, salt potatoes, which are boiled and coated with butter and salt and served hot. For a more upscale dining experience, visit The Summit, a rooftop restaurant that offers panoramic views of Cortland and exceptional food. The craft beer scene is also thriving, with several local breweries like the Cortland Beer Company, who offer tastings and tours.

Where to Stay in Cortland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cortland offers several options to stay, ranging from budget-friendly hotels to luxurious guesthouses. For an exceptional experience, consider staying at Hope Lake Lodge, located at Greek Peak Mountain Resort. The lodge offers spacious suites, a spa, and a water park. There are also several bed and breakfasts in the area that offer a more intimate and personalized experience.

What to Buy in Cortland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Cortland back home with you by getting handmade soaps from Cortland Soapworks or local crafts from the Purple Monkey. If you are looking for a traditional souvenir, head to the Cortland line, located at the intersection of Route 81 and 13, where you can get Cortland-specific apparel and gear.

Local Culture and Customs of Cortland

The residents of Cortland are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are expected to reciprocate the same. The locals take pride in their natural surroundings, so it’s important to follow the “Leave No Trace” principles when exploring the trails or parks. Additionally, Cortland’s diverse community hosts several cultural events and festivals throughout the year that celebrate art, music, and food.

Best Time to Visit Cortland

The best time to visit Cortland is between late May and early October when the weather is mild, and the region is bustling with outdoor activities and festivals. Winter months are perfect for skiing and snowboarding activities. Cortland and its surrounding areas also enjoy distinct fall foliage, making the autumn season a perfect time to explore the natural beauty of the region.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cortland

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and observe local conservation efforts.
  • Say “Hi” and “Thank You” to the locals, who are friendly and welcoming.
  • Take the time to explore the region’s beautiful landscapes and rich history.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage a trail.
  • Disrespect the town’s conservative culture.
  • Ignore the “Leave No Trace” principles when hiking or camping.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cortland

Cortland is a safe city, and it’s unlikely to encounter any significant safety concerns. However, it’s good to take precautions, especially when exploring the trails and parks. Carry a map and inform someone of your plans before heading out. Be aware of any wildlife in the area and maintain a safe distance.

Health in Cortland

The area has several medical centers and hospitals, should you need any medical attention. Always carry your medical insurance documents, especially when hiking or engaging in outdoor activities.

Getting to Cortland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Cortland is easily accessible through various modes of transportation.

Airports Near Cortland

Syracuse Hancock International Airport is the nearest airport, located about 30 miles away, and offers regular flights to many major cities in the United States.

Bus Stations Near Cortland

The Cortland Transit Center serves as the main bus station providing transportation to surrounding areas.

Other Options

Cortland is connected to major highways, including US Route 81 and NY State Route 13, making it an easy drive from major cities like New York City and Albany. A car rental is recommended if you plan to explore the region.

How to Get Around in Cortland

The best way to get around Cortland is by car, although there is also a public transit system that offers regular service around Cortland and the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Cortland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cortland is situated in a region of New York State known for its rich history and natural beauty. Nearby destinations worth exploring include Ithaca, home to Cornell University and the picturesque Finger Lakes. Syracuse, known for its art and cultural attractions, is just a short drive away. The stunning Watkins Glen State Park, an awe-inspiring gorge, is also within reach of a day trip.

Conclusion

Cortland’s natural beauty, culture, and adventurous outdoor activities will leave you mesmerized. Whether you plan to explore the trails, indulge in the local food scene or take in Cortland’s rich history, there is always something to discover in this beautiful city. Start planning your trip to Cortland today and prepare to fall in love with this hidden gem in Central New York.
