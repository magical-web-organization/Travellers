Introduction to Oswego

Oswego is a beautiful city located in Central New York, midway between Rochester and Syracuse, and on the southeastern shore of Lake Ontario. This charming city is a popular destination for travelers looking to explore the rich history, scenic beauty, and vibrant culture of Upstate New York.

Oswego boasts a plethora of attractions, from fascinating museums and historic sites to breathtaking natural wonders, hiking trails, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, there’s something for everyone in Oswego. Let’s dive into the top places to visit, things to do, and hidden gems of this vibrant city.

List of Places to Visit in Oswego

Here’s a quick overview of the best attractions and places to visit in Oswego:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Ontario State Historic Site: Explore the rich history and military heritage of Oswego at this iconic fort, which served as a military post since the early 18th century.
  • Safe Haven Museum and Education Center: Discover the inspiring story of the 982 refugees who fled Europe and found sanctuary in Oswego during World War II.
  • H. Lee White Maritime Museum: Learn about the rich maritime history of Oswego and Lake Ontario, including shipwrecks, navigation, and fishing.

Natural Attractions of Oswego: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Breitbeck Park and Beach: Enjoy stunning views of Lake Ontario, go for a refreshing swim, have a picnic, or explore the park’s walking trails.
  • Salmon River Falls: Hike the picturesque trails of this stunning waterfall, which is home to a variety of wildlife, including bald eagles and beavers.
  • Rice Creek Field Station: Discover the stunning natural beauty of the Rice Creek Field Station, including forests, wetlands, and wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Oswego

  • Oswego Riverwalk Trail: This 1.5-mile trail follows the Oswego River and offers stunning views of the city, harbor, and Lake Ontario.
  • Green Lakes State Park Trail: Enjoy hiking, biking, or birdwatching while gazing at the brilliantly turquoise waters of the two glacial lakes in this park.
  • Hooper Trail: This 5.5-mile scenic trail is perfect for hiking and horseback riding, with picturesque views of the Oswego River and surrounding forests.

Best Things to Do in Oswego: Activities and Tours

  • Oswego Speedway: Catch a thrilling race at this iconic Speedway, which has been a fixture of Oswego’s sporting and cultural scene since 1951.
  • Riverboat Tours: Take a relaxing tour of the historic Oswego River on a classic riverboat, and enjoy stunning views of the city, harbor, and Lake Ontario.
  • Oswego Farmers Market: Get a taste of Oswego’s vibrant local culture by visiting its bustling farmers market, where you can enjoy fresh produce, artisanal goods, and live music.

Food and Drink Scene of Oswego

Oswego has a vibrant food and drink scene, with a plethora of local specialties and hidden gems to explore.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken Riggies: This iconic Upstate New York dish features chicken and rigatoni in a spicy tomato cream sauce, and is a must-try in Oswego.
  • Salt Potatoes: These delicious bite-sized potatoes are boiled in heavily salted water and served with melted butter, and are a savory local delicacy.
  • Creamy Tomato Soup: Made with fresh tomatoes and cream, this delicious soup is a classic comfort food in Oswego and perfect for a cold winter day.

Local Beverages

  • Oswego Brewing Company: This local microbrewery produces a wide range of craft beers, including IPAs, stouts, and lagers.
  • Ontario Orchards: This local orchard produces delicious apple cider and other fruit-based drinks, perfect for sipping on a hot summer day.
  • Oswego Tea Company: This local tea company creates a wide range of loose-leaf and bagged teas, including herbal blends and traditional varieties.

Where to Stay in Oswego: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Oswego, ranging from budget-friendly guesthouses and hostels to luxurious hotels and resorts.

Some of the best options include:

  • The Beacon Hotel: A charming boutique hotel located in the heart of downtown Oswego, offering stylish rooms and suites and easy access to top attractions.
  • Oswego Inn: A budget-friendly hotel located on the outskirts of Oswego, with comfortable rooms and amenities.
  • The Eis House Bed and Breakfast: A quaint bed and breakfast located in the nearby town of Mexico, offering comfortable rooms and delicious breakfasts.

What to Buy in Oswego: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring back home, Oswego has plenty of options.

Some of the best products to buy include:

  • Local honey: Oswego County is home to many honey producers, and their honey is a sweet and delicious souvenir.
  • Artisanal cheeses: Oswego is home to several local cheese makers, and their cheese is a delicious treat.
  • Handmade crafts: Oswego has a vibrant arts and crafts scene, and local artisans create everything from pottery and jewelry to paintings and sculptures.

Local Culture and Customs of Oswego

Oswego is a friendly and welcoming city, with a strong sense of community and a deep appreciation for its rich history and traditions. Visitors to Oswego can expect to encounter warm and welcoming locals, delicious local cuisine, and a vibrant arts and culture scene.

Best Time to Visit Oswego

The best time to visit Oswego is during the summer and fall months, from June to October, when the weather is warm and sunny, and most of the outdoor attractions are open.

Do’s and Don’ts of Oswego

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Oswego:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, including chicken riggies, salt potatoes, and creamy tomato soup.
  • Explore the city’s vibrant arts and culture scene, including its museums, galleries, and theaters.
  • Enjoy the amazing natural beauty of the region, from scenic hiking trails to picturesque waterfalls.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural beauty of Oswego’s parks and trails.
  • Don’t disrespect the local culture and customs of Oswego, including its history and traditions.
  • Don’t forget to tip your servers and bartenders for their hard work and exceptional service.

Safety and Health

Oswego is a safe and welcoming city, with a low crime rate and a strong sense of community. Visitors should take normal precautions, such as locking their cars and keeping an eye on their belongings, but can generally feel safe and secure during their visit.

Safety in Oswego

  • Avoid walking alone at night in secluded areas
  • Always lock your car doors and keep valuable items out of sight

Health in Oswego

  • Tap water is safe to drink
  • Medical facilities including hospitals and pharmacies are available in Oswego

Getting to Oswego: Transportation Options and Logistics

Oswego is easy to get to, with several transportation options available.

Airports Near Oswego

The closest airport to Oswego is Hancock International Airport in Syracuse, which is located about an hour’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near Oswego

Grayhound stations are located in Syracuse and Fulton, which are both nearby to Oswego.

Other Options

There are plenty of car rental agencies available in the region which can make getting around Oswego and beyond more convenient.

How to Get Around in Oswego

One of the best ways to get around Oswego is by car as many of the attractions are spread out across the city. Additionally, buses and taxicabs are available as well.

Where to Go Next from Oswego: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursions to explore after visiting Oswego. Here are some options:

  • Syracuse: Located an hour’s drive south of Oswego, Syracuse is a bustling city with a thriving arts and culture scene, delicious food, and plenty of attractions and activities.
  • Adirondack Mountains: Located a few hours drive east of Oswego, the Adirondack Mountains offer pristine wilderness, stunning hiking trails, and breathtaking views.
  • Lake Placid: Located a few hours drive northeast of Oswego, Lake Placid is a charming resort town famous for hosting the 1932 and 1980 Winter Olympics.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking to explore the rich history, natural beauty, or vibrant culture of Upstate New York, Oswego has something for everyone. With its wide range of top attractions, delicious cuisine, and warm and welcoming locals, Oswego is a must-visit destination in the Mid-Atlantic region.
