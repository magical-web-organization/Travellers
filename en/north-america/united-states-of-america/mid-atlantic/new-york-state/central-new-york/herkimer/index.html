Introduction to Herkimer

Nestled in the heart of Central New York, Herkimer is a charming town that offers a mix of history, culture, and outdoor adventure. The town is home to a number of historical landmarks, including the Herkimer Home State Historic Site, which once served as the revolutionary war headquarters of General Nicholas Herkimer. In addition to its cultural heritage, Herkimer also boasts spectacular natural beauty, with scenic views of the Mohawk River Valley, the Adirondack Mountains, and the nearby Herkimer Diamond Mines - a popular tourist attraction.

List of Places to Visit in Herkimer

There are a number of interesting places to visit in Herkimer, ranging from cultural landmarks to scenic nature parks. Here are some must-visit spots in Herkimer:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Herkimer Home State Historic Site: This historic site dates back to Revolutionary times and served as a crucial headquarters during the Battle of Oriskany. Visitors can tour the Georgian-style mansion, learn about the history of the site, and enjoy beautiful gardens and landscapes.
  • Herkimer County Historical Society: Located in a beautiful Victorian-era mansion, this museum offers a fascinating look into the history of the region through interactive exhibits and rotating displays.
  • Erie Canal Village: Explore the rich history of the Erie Canal system at this living history museum. Experience the sights, sounds, and smells of 19th-century life as you stroll through a recreated canal village.

Natural Attractions of Herkimer: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Herkimer Diamond Mines: This popular attraction is a must-visit for rockhounds and outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors can search for and keep their own unique Herkimer diamonds - double-terminated quartz crystals that are only found in this region.
  • Moss Island: This beautifully preserved island park offers stunning views of the Mohawk River and is a popular spot for fishing, kayaking, and picnicking.
  • Adirondack Park: An outdoor adventurer’s paradise, the Adirondack Park is just a short drive from Herkimer and offers over 6 million acres of hiking, fishing, and hunting opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Herkimer

For those interested in exploring the great outdoors, Herkimer offers a variety of hiking and biking trails. Here are some recommended routes:

  • The Herkimer Rail Trail: This 9-mile trail runs from the Village of Herkimer to the Village of Little Falls. It offers beautiful views of the Mohawk River and is ideal for a leisurely bike ride or a scenic walk.
  • West Canada Lakes Wilderness: One of the most beautiful wilderness areas in the state, West Canada Lakes offers over 15 miles of hiking trails that wind through serene forests and stunning mountain landscapes.
  • Monroe State Forest: This park features over 10 miles of hiking trails that offer beautiful views of the Adirondack Mountains and the Mohawk Valley.

Best Things to Do in Herkimer: Activities and Tours

Apart from the above-mentioned attractions, Herkimer also offers a plethora of fun activities and tours to indulge in. Here are some of the best things to do in Herkimer:

  • Take a scenic boat ride on the Erie Canal or Mohawk River.
  • Go fishing on the Mohawk River or one of the nearby lakes.
  • Take a guided tour of the Herkimer Diamond Mines or a local brewery.
  • Attend one of the many annual festivals held in Herkimer, such as the County Fair or the Garlic Festival.

Week-Long Itinerary for Herkimer

For those planning a weeklong visit to Herkimer, here’s a sample itinerary that covers all the essentials:

Day 1 Arrive in Herkimer, check into hotel, take a leisurely stroll around town, dine at a local restaurant
Day 2 Visit the Herkimer Home State Historic Site, explore nearby landmarks

Day 5 Go fishing on the Mohawk River or nearby lake, visit a local brewery

Food and Drink Scene of Herkimer

Herkimer is a foodie’s paradise with a diverse culinary scene that ranges from classic American comfort food to authentic international cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes to savor in Herkimer:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tomato pie: Herkimer’s signature dish, this savory pastry is made with fresh tomato sauce, Parmesan cheese, and a crispy crust.
  • Utica greens: A tangy mixture of escarole, hot peppers, and prosciutto, Utica greens are a popular dish that originated in the nearby town of Utica.
  • Chicken Riggies: A zesty mix of rigatoni pasta, tender chicken, and hot peppers. Native to the Mohawk Valley Region, Chicken Riggies is a must-try dish for those who love spicy food.

Local Beverages

Herkimer also has a thriving craft beer scene, with several microbreweries that offer a range of unique brews. Here are some notable places to enjoy local beers:

  • Herkimer Brew House: A popular brewery with a large selection of beers on tap and an outdoor patio.
  • Woodland Farm Brewery: Nestled in the beautiful Adirondack Mountains, this brewery offers stunning views and delicious beers made from locally sourced ingredients.

What to Buy in Herkimer: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

From handmade jewelry to locally crafted beer, Herkimer offers plenty of unique souvenirs and products to take back home. Here are some great places to shop in Herkimer:

  • Herkimer Diamond Mines Gift Shop: A perfect place to buy souvenirs, gems, and jewelry from the Herkimer Diamond Mines.
  • Mohawk Valley Trading Company: This local shop offers a variety of homemade artisanal products, including maple syrup, honey, and beeswax candles.
  • Gems Along the Mohawk: A family-owned business that offers unique gifts, local crafts, and souvenirs that showcase the region’s natural beauty.

Local Culture and Customs of Herkimer

Herkimer’s culture and customs are deeply rooted in the region’s history and scenic landscapes. The town is known for its love of outdoor adventure, homegrown foods, local crafts, and festivals that celebrate the town’s heritage. Visitors are encouraged to explore the rich cultural heritage of the region, meet the friendly locals, and experience the region on a deeper level.

Best Time to Visit Herkimer

The best time to visit Herkimer is during the summer months (June-August), when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities. Visitors can also enjoy the town’s many festivals and cultural events during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Herkimer

  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages.

Safety and Health

Safety in Herkimer

Herkimer is a safe town to visit with low crime rates. However, visitors should exercise normal safety precautions, such as locking their cars and hotel rooms and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Herkimer

Visitors to Herkimer should take precautions to avoid tick bites and Lyme disease, which is common in the region. It is also important to stay hydrated and wear appropriate clothing and sunscreen when spending time outdoors.

Getting to Herkimer: Transportation Options and Logistics

Herkimer can be easily accessed by car or public transport. Here are some transportation options for reaching Herkimer:

Airports Near Herkimer

  • Albany International Airport (ALB): 1 hr 20 min drive
  • Syracuse Hancock International Airport (SYR): 1 hr 10 min drive

Bus Stations Near Herkimer

  • Trailways Bus Station (Utica): 20 min drive
  • Greyhound Bus Station (Little Falls): 10 min drive

Other Options

  • Amtrak Train: Utica Union Station is 20 min drive from Herkimer.
  • Car Rental: Several car rental companies, including Hertz, Avis, and Budget, operate in Herkimer.

How to Get Around in Herkimer

The best way to get around Herkimer is by car, although the town is also easily navigable on foot or by bike. Taxis and ride-hailing services are also available in the area.

Where to Go Next from Herkimer: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Herkimer is a great starting point for exploring the surrounding areas. Here are some nearby destinations worth exploring:

  • Cooperstown: Home of the Baseball Hall of Fame and a charming town filled with quaint shops and eateries.
  • Utica: A vibrant city that offers great dining, shopping, and cultural attractions like the Munson-Williams-Proctor Arts Institute.
  • Old Forge: A picturesque mountain town that sits at the southwestern end of the Adirondack Park and offers stunning views, hiking trails, and several lakes.

Conclusion

Herkimer is a fascinating mix of history, culture, and natural beauty that offers something for everyone. Explore the town’s landmarks, enjoy the scenic beauty of the outdoor preserves and explore the delicious food scene. With our comprehensive tour guide on hand, your visit to Herkimer is sure to be unforgettable.
