Introduction to Cortlandt

Cortlandt, located in Westchester County, is a hidden gem in New York’s Mid-Atlantic region. This charming town offers a unique mix of cultural and natural attractions, making it an ideal destination for both history buffs and outdoor enthusiasts.

With an abundance of hiking and biking trails, Cortlandt is the perfect destination for nature lovers. The town’s rich history is also reflected in its museums and historical sites, making it a prime location for those who want to immerse themselves in local culture.

In this comprehensive guide, we will explore Cortlandt’s top attractions, hiking and biking routes, best things to do, food and drink scene, and local customs. We will also provide information on the best time to visit, how to get there, and where to stay.

List of Places to Visit in Cortlandt

Here are the top places to visit in Cortlandt:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Van Cortlandt Manor - Built in the 18th century, Van Cortlandt Manor is a historic home that provides a fascinating glimpse into life in colonial times. Visitors can take a tour of the manor, watch costumed interpreters demonstrate traditional crafts, and participate in educational programs.

  2. Hudson Valley Center for Contemporary Art - One of the most prominent art museums in the region, the Hudson Valley Center for Contemporary Art showcases the work of local and international artists. The museum’s rotating exhibits cover a variety of media, including painting, sculpture, photography, and video.

  3. Boscobel House and Gardens - Situated on a bluff overlooking the Hudson River, Boscobel House and Gardens is a neoclassical mansion built in the early 19th century. The house has been fully restored to its original state and features period furnishings and décor. Visitors can stroll through the picturesque gardens and enjoy stunning views of the river.

Natural Attractions of Cortlandt: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Blue Mountain Reservation - With over 1,500 acres of woodlands, lakes, and streams, Blue Mountain Reservation is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts. The park offers an extensive network of hiking trails, as well as opportunities for fishing, picnicking, and birdwatching.

  2. Bear Mountain State Park - Located just a short drive from Cortlandt, Bear Mountain State Park is a sprawling wilderness area that features rugged mountains, pristine lakes, and panoramic views of the Hudson River. The park offers a wide range of recreational activities, including hiking, biking, fishing, and boating.

  3. Croton Gorge Park - Situated along the Croton River, Croton Gorge Park is a tranquil retreat that is ideal for a peaceful day out in nature. The park’s main attraction is the Croton Dam, which offers stunning views of the river and the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cortlandt

Cortlandt provides a plethora of hiking and biking routes for nature enthusiasts. Here are the top three routes to explore:

  1. Old Croton Aqueduct Trail - The Old Croton Aqueduct Trail offers a scenic, 26.2-mile hike or bike ride through Cortlandt’s picturesque countryside. The trail runs along the historic aqueduct and is popular with both locals and visitors.

  2. Teatown Lake Reservation Trail - Located in the heart of Cortlandt, the Teatown Lake Reservation Trail is a 14-mile trail that winds through the park’s lush forests and scenic wetlands. The trail offers hikers and bikers a chance to experience the region’s natural beauty up close.

  3. Bear Mountain State Park Trails - Bear Mountain State Park boasts an extensive network of hiking and biking trails that crisscross the park’s rugged mountains and pristine lakes. The trails range from easy to strenuous and offer something for hikers and bikers of all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Cortlandt: Activities and Tours

Here are the top things to do in Cortlandt:

  1. Hudson River Cruises - Take a relaxing cruise along the Hudson River and enjoy stunning views of the region’s scenic coastline and charming towns. There are several tour operators that offer cruises, including Hudson Cruises and River Rose Cruises.

  2. Teatown Lake Reservation Nature Center - The Teatown Lake Reservation Nature Center is a great place to learn about the region’s wildlife and natural history. The center offers educational programs, guided hikes, and exhibits on local flora and fauna.

  3. Birdwatching - Cortlandt provides a wide range of birdwatching opportunities, with several parks and preserves that are home to a variety of species. Some of the best places to spot birds include Blue Mountain Reservation, Teatown Lake Reservation, and Croton Point Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Cortlandt

Here are some of the top dishes and local beverages to try in Cortlandt:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Hudson Valley Foie Gras - This local specialty features duck liver that has been cooked and seasoned to perfection. Hudson Valley Foie Gras is a delicacy that is highly sought after by foodies.

  2. Apple Cider Donuts - Made with locally sourced apples and freshly ground cinnamon, Apple Cider Donuts are a sweet treat that is synonymous with the region’s fall festivals.

  3. Spiedies - Originating from nearby Binghamton, Spiedies are a regional specialty that features marinated cubes of meat, typically chicken, pork or lamb, that are skewered and grilled to perfection.

Local Beverages

  1. Hudson Valley Wine - With over 40 wineries in the region, the Hudson Valley is known for its excellent wines, particularly its crisp whites and robust reds.

  2. Craft Beer - There are several local breweries in Cortlandt and the surrounding area that offer a wide range of craft beers. Some of the best include Peekskill Brewery, Captain Lawrence Brewing Company, and River Outpost Brewing Co.

Where to Stay in Cortlandt: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are the top places to stay in Cortlandt:

  1. Holiday Inn Express & Suites Peekskill-Lower Hudson Valley - Located in the heart of Peekskill, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and modern amenities at an affordable price.

  2. Cromwell Manor Inn - Built in the 1820s, the Cromwell Manor Inn is a historic guesthouse that offers a touch of old-world charm. Located just a short drive from Cortlandt, the inn provides a peaceful retreat from the hustle and bustle of city life.

  3. The Inn on the Hudson - Situated in nearby Cold Spring, the Inn on the Hudson is a picturesque hotel that overlooks the river. The hotel’s elegant rooms and gourmet restaurant make it a popular choice among couples and honeymooners.

What to Buy in Cortlandt: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home from Cortlandt:

  2. Hudson Valley Art - There are several art galleries in Cortlandt and the surrounding area that feature the work of local artists. Buying a piece of art is a great way to support the local community and bring a piece of the region’s culture home with you.

  3. Handmade Crafts - The region is home to several artisans who specialize in handmade crafts such as pottery, jewelry, and textiles. These unique and beautiful items make great souvenirs and gifts.

Best Time to Visit Cortlandt

The best time to visit Cortlandt is during the fall, when the region’s foliage is at its peak and there are several festivals and events taking place. Spring and summer are also popular times to visit, with mild temperatures and plenty of outdoor activities.

Getting to Cortlandt: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are the various transportation options to get to Cortlandt:

Airports Near Cortlandt

  • Westchester County Airport (HPN) - Located just 15 miles from Cortlandt, Westchester County Airport offers flights to several major cities in the northeast.

  • LaGuardia Airport (LGA) - Located approximately 35 miles from Cortlandt, LaGuardia Airport offers flights to several national and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Cortlandt

  • Peekskill Train Station - The Peekskill Train Station is a major transportation hub that offers commuter rail service to New York City and other destinations in the region.

  • Greyhound Bus Station - The Greyhound Bus Station is located in nearby White Plains and offers bus service to several destinations throughout the northeast.

Other Options

  • Car Rental - There are several car rental agencies located in the region, making it easy to rent a car and explore Cortlandt and the surrounding area at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Cortlandt

Here are some of the best ways to get around Cortlandt:

  • Walking - Cortlandt is a pedestrian-friendly town with several walkable areas and trails.

  • Biking - With its extensive network of bike trails, Cortlandt is also a great town for biking enthusiasts.

  • Public transportation - The town is served by the MTA Metro-North Railroad, which provides commuter rail service to New York City and other destinations in the region.

Where to Go Next from Cortlandt: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider after your visit to Cortlandt:

  1. Cold Spring - Located just a short drive from Cortlandt, Cold Spring is a picturesque town that is known for its beautiful architecture and charming shops and cafes.

  2. Storm King Art Center - Situated on 500 acres of rolling hills, the Storm King Art Center is one of the country’s premier outdoor sculpture parks. Visitors can explore the park’s winding trails and view its impressive collection of contemporary art.

  3. Bear Mountain State Park - As mentioned earlier, Bear Mountain State Park is a nearby wilderness area that features rugged mountains, pristine lakes, and panoramic views of the Hudson River. It’s the perfect destination for a day trip or weekend getaway.
