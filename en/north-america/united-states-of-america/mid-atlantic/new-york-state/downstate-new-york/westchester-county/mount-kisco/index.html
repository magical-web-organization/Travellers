Introduction to Mount Kisco

Mount Kisco is a vibrant, suburban village located in Westchester County, New York. It boasts a rich history, a thriving downtown area, and is surrounded by beautiful natural landscapes that make it a popular destination for tourists and locals alike. Whether you’re looking to explore cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, or simply soak up the local flavor, Mount Kisco has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Mount Kisco

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Mount Kisco is steeped in rich history, and there are plenty of museums and historical sites worth visiting. Some of the must-see attractions include:

  • The Bedford Historical Society Museum, which showcases the history of the town and features both permanent and rotating exhibits.
  • The John Jay Homestead, a National Historic Landmark that was once the home of Founding Father John Jay and his family.
  • The Caramoor Center for Music and the Arts, a sprawling estate that hosts concerts, festivals, and exhibitions throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Mount Kisco: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature enthusiasts will find plenty to see and do in and around Mount Kisco. Some of the top outdoor attractions include:

  • The Pound Ridge Reservation, a 4,700-acre nature reserve with hiking trails, fishing spots, and stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • The Mianus River Gorge Preserve, a 1,000-acre nature preserve with scenic trails, birdwatching opportunities, and a wide variety of wildlife.
  • The Rockefeller State Park Preserve, a 1,400-acre park with walking trails, gardens, and scenic overlooks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mount Kisco

Mount Kisco is a paradise for hikers and cyclists, with miles of trails and scenic routes to explore. Some of the best hiking and biking trails in the area include:

  • The North County Trailway, a 22-mile paved trail that runs from Mount Kisco to Baldwin Place.
  • The Leatherman’s Loop, a 10k trail that winds through some of the most scenic areas around Mount Kisco.
  • The Westmoreland Sanctuary, a 640-acre nature preserve with over 7 miles of scenic hiking trails.

Best Things to Do in Mount Kisco: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures or cultural attractions, Mount Kisco offers plenty of things to see and do. Some of the top activities and tours in the area include:

  • Visiting the Mount Kisco Farmers’ Market, which features fresh, locally-sourced produce, handmade crafts, and live music.
  • Taking a walking tour of downtown Mount Kisco, which includes stops at historic landmarks and local businesses.
  • Joining a food tour of the area, which showcases the best of Mount Kisco’s culinary scene.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mount Kisco

For those looking to stay in Mount Kisco for an extended period, there are plenty of activities to fill a week-long itinerary. Here’s a sample itinerary to help you plan your trip:

  • Day 1: Visit the John Jay Homestead and explore the nearby Mianus River Gorge Preserve.
  • Day 2: Enjoy a day in downtown Mount Kisco, shopping, dining, and visiting local art galleries and boutique stores.
  • Day 3: Spend a day at the Westmoreland Sanctuary, hiking and enjoying the beautiful scenery.
  • Day 4: Take a food tour of the area, sampling the best local cuisine.
  • Day 5: Visit the Caramoor Center for Music and the Arts, and attend a concert or exhibition.
  • Day 6: Explore the surrounding towns, including Bedford and Chappaqua.
  • Day 7: Spend the day at the Pound Ridge Reservation, hiking and picnicking.

Food and Drink Scene of Mount Kisco

Must-Try Dishes

Mount Kisco is a foodie’s paradise, with plenty of locally-sourced ingredients and innovative chefs. Some of the must-try dishes in the area include:

  • The lobster roll at The Boathouse Restaurant, featuring fresh Maine lobster and house-made aioli.
  • The handmade pasta dishes at Via Vanti!, which are made with fresh ingredients sourced from local farms.
  • The artisanal chocolates at Chocolations, which are crafted with the finest cocoa beans and local ingredients.

Local Beverages

Mount Kisco is home to several local breweries, wineries, and distilleries, offering visitors plenty of opportunities to sample local libations. Some of the top local beverages include:

  • The craft beer at Captain Lawrence Brewery, which includes a wide variety of styles and flavors.
  • The award-winning wines at Mill River Winery, which are made with locally-grown grapes.
  • The small-batch spirits at StilltheOne Distillery, including gin, rum, and vodka.

Where to Stay in Mount Kisco: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors to Mount Kisco will find a variety of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the top options include:

  • The Holiday Inn Mount Kisco, which offers comfortable rooms, an on-site restaurant, and a fitness center.
  • The Hidden Pond Farm Bed and Breakfast, a charming guesthouse with cozy rooms and a farm-to-table breakfast.
  • The Crabtree’s Kittle House, a historic inn and restaurant that dates back to the 1700s.

What to Buy in Mount Kisco: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts to bring back home, there are plenty of locally-made products to choose from. Some of the top options include:

  • Artwork from local galleries, such as the Northern Westchester Artists Guild.
  • Handmade ceramics and pottery at the Katonah Clay Art Center.
  • Locally-made cheese and other artisanal products at nearby farms, such as Hemlock Hill Farm.

Local Culture and Customs of Mount Kisco

Mount Kisco is a friendly and welcoming community, and visitors are encouraged to explore and engage with the local culture. Here are a few tips to help you get the most out of your visit:

  • Respect local customs and traditions, such as tipping your waitstaff or removing your shoes before entering a home.
  • Take the time to chat with locals and learn more about their experiences living in the area.
  • Be mindful of the natural surroundings, including picking up after yourself and staying on designated trails.

Best Time to Visit Mount Kisco

Mount Kisco is a year-round destination, with something to offer visitors in every season. The best time to visit depends on your preferences and interests. Some of the top times to visit include:

  • Spring, when the weather is mild and the flowers are in bloom.
  • Summer, when you can enjoy outdoor activities like hiking and swimming.
  • Fall, when the foliage turns vibrant shades of red and orange.
  • Winter, when you can enjoy winter sports like skiing and ice skating.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mount Kisco

Do:

  • Explore the local food and drink scene, trying new restaurants, breweries, and wineries.
  • Take advantage of the natural beauty of the area, including hiking, biking, and enjoying scenic views.
  • Chat with the locals and learn more about the area’s history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Make loud noises late at night, disturbing the peace of the community.
  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mount Kisco

Mount Kisco is a safe and welcoming community, but visitors should always take precautions to ensure their safety. Some tips for staying safe in the area include:

  • Locking your doors and windows at night.
  • Being aware of your surroundings, especially when walking alone at night.
  • Following local traffic laws and signals.

Health in Mount Kisco

Mount Kisco has excellent healthcare facilities, including Northern Westchester Hospital and CareMount Medical. If you require medical attention during your visit, these facilities offer you the care and treatment you need.

Getting to Mount Kisco: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mount Kisco

The nearest airports to Mount Kisco are Westchester County Airport and Stewart International Airport in Newburgh, NY.

Bus Stations Near Mount Kisco

The nearest bus station to Mount Kisco is the Katonah Metro-North Station, which offers service to New York City and other local areas.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Mount Kisco, as it is easily accessible from major highways such as I-684 and the Taconic State Parkway.

How to Get Around in Mount Kisco

Visitors to Mount Kisco can easily get around on foot, by bike, or by car. The downtown area is easily walkable, and there are plenty of bike trails and bike-friendly roads. If you prefer to drive, there are plenty of parking options in and around the village.

Where to Go Next from Mount Kisco: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mount Kisco is situated in the heart of Westchester County, making it an ideal base for exploring other nearby towns and attractions. Some of the top nearby destinations include:

  • The historic town of Bedford, with its charming downtown area and rich history.
  • The Hudson Valley Wine Trail, a scenic drive with stops at local wineries and vineyards.
  • The Kensico Dam Plaza, a beautiful park with walking trails, picnic areas, and stunning views of the surrounding area.

Conclusion

Mount Kisco is a hidden gem in the heart of Westchester County, offering visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re looking to explore the local food scene, enjoy outdoor adventures, or simply soak up the local flavor, Mount Kisco has something for everyone. Plan your visit today and discover all that this vibrant community has to offer!
