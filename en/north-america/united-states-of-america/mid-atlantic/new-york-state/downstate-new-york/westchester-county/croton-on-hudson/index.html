Introduction to Croton-on-Hudson

Croton-on-Hudson is a charming village located in Westchester County, in Downstate New York, in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States. Nestled in the Hudson River Valley, Croton-on-Hudson is known for its natural beauty, historic landmarks, and vibrant arts scene. Whether you’re looking to explore the great outdoors, delve into local history and culture, or savor delicious food and drink, Croton-on-Hudson has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Croton-on-Hudson

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Van Cortlandt Manor - Step back in time and experience life in colonial America at this beautifully restored 18th-century manor.
  • Croton Point Park - This picturesque park features a beach, hiking trails, camping facilities, and stunning views of the Hudson River.
  • Hendrick Hudson Free Library - Immerse yourself in local history and culture with a visit to this impressive library, which houses a range of rare books, manuscripts, and artifacts.
  • Teatown Lake Reservation - This nature preserve offers over 1,000 acres of protected land, including miles of trails, educational exhibits, and a wildlife rehabilitation center.
  • Old Croton Aqueduct State Historic Park - Take a leisurely stroll along this historic aqueduct, which once provided drinking water to New York City and is now a scenic walking trail.

Natural Attractions of Croton-on-Hudson: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Croton Gorge Park - This stunning park boasts breathtaking views of the Croton Dam and Reservoir, as well as hiking trails, picnic areas, and fishing spots.
  • Blue Mountain Reservation - With over 1,500 acres of forested land, this park offers miles of hiking and mountain biking trails, picnic areas, and scenic vistas.
  • Harmon Park and Nature Preserve - Nestled along the Hudson River, this park boasts beautiful views of the water and surrounding mountains, as well as hiking trails and picnic facilities.
  • Sprout Creek Farm - This serene, 200-acre farm features hiking trails, an animal sanctuary, and a wide variety of farm-fresh produce, cheese, and other handmade goods.
  • Kitchawan Preserve - This 208-acre nature reserve is home to a diverse range of ecosystems, including woodlands, wetlands, and meadows, as well as hiking trails and birdwatching opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Croton-on-Hudson

Croton-on-Hudson is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, boasting a wide range of hiking and biking routes to suit all levels of ability. Some popular trails include:

  • The Croton Aqueduct Trail - This scenic, 26-mile trail follows the path of the historic Croton Aqueduct from Croton-on-Hudson to the Bronx, offering stunning views of the Hudson River and New York City skyline along the way.

  • The Teatown-Kitchawan Trail - This beautiful, 14-mile trail winds through the woodlands and wetlands of Teatown Lake Reservation, Kitchawan Preserve, and other nearby nature reserves.

  • The Old Croton Trail - This 26.2-mile trail follows the route of the historic Old Croton Aqueduct, offering spectacular views of the Hudson River, scenic vistas, and historic sites.

Best Things to Do in Croton-on-Hudson: Activities and Tours

  • Croton-Harmon Train Station - Take a ride on the historic Metro-North Hudson Line train, which runs along the scenic Hudson River, offering stunning views of the surrounding countryside and the Manhattan skyline.

  • Kayaking and Canoeing - Explore the calm waters of the Hudson River with a guided kayaking or canoeing tour, which offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape and wildlife.

  • Shopping and Dining - Croton-on-Hudson offers a wide range of unique shops, boutiques, and restaurants, serving up delicious food and drink from both local and international cuisines.

  • Dancing and Music - Croton-on-Hudson is known for its vibrant arts scene, with a range of live music, dance performances, and theatrical productions taking place throughout the year.

Week-Long Itinerary for Croton-on-Hudson

Day 1: Start your trip with a visit to Van Cortlandt Manor, followed by a scenic hike in Blue Mountain Reservation.

Day 2: Take a leisurely stroll along the Old Croton Aqueduct, and then spend the afternoon exploring the Hendrick Hudson Free Library and nearby downtown Croton-on-Hudson shops and restaurants.

Day 3: Visit Teatown Lake Reservation, followed by a relaxing picnic lunch at Harmon Park and Nature Preserve.

Day 4: Spend the day at Croton Point Park, enjoying the beach, hiking trails, and birdwatching opportunities.

Day 5: Head to Sprout Creek Farm for a guided hike and sampling of locally made cheeses and other products.

Day 6: Experience the beauty of the Hudson River up close with a kayaking or canoeing tour, followed by a delicious dinner at a local restaurant.

Day 7: Wrap up your trip with a visit to the historic Croton-Harmon Train Station and a scenic train ride along the Hudson River.

Food and Drink Scene of Croton-on-Hudson

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hudson Valley Foie Gras - A local delicacy, Hudson Valley Foie Gras is a rich and flavorful goose liver pate served with crispy baguette.

  • Croton Creek Steakhouse and Wine Bar - This locally owned and operated steak house offers an array of mouth-watering cuts of steak, accompanied by an extensive selection of New York State wines.

  • Harvest on Hudson - With breathtaking views of the Hudson River, Harvest on Hudson offers an upscale dining experience that features fresh, locally sourced ingredients and an extensive Italian wine list.

  • Black Cow Coffee Company - A must-visit for specialty coffee lovers, Black Cow offers a range of expertly crafted espresso drinks, pour-overs, and other signature coffee blends.

  • Anton’s Dumplings & Bakery - This cozy bakery and restaurant specializes in handmade dumplings, baked goods, and other traditional Russian fare.

Local Beverages

  • Captain Lawrence Brewing Company - This award-winning craft brewery offers a rotating selection of unique, locally brewed beers, including IPAs, stouts, and seasonal specialties.

  • Taconic Distillery - The first farm distillery in New York State, Taconic Distillery produces small-batch whiskey, gin, and other spirits using locally sourced grains and natural ingredients.

  • Croton Tapsmith - This family-owned and operated taproom offers a range of locally brewed beers, wines, and ciders, as well as an extensive food menu featuring locally sourced ingredients.

Where to Stay in Croton-on-Hudson: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Inn at Croton-on-Hudson - This charming, historic inn features eight guest rooms, each uniquely decorated and appointed with modern amenities, as well as a cozy lounge area and outdoor spaces.

  • The Time Nyack - This stylish boutique hotel is located just a short drive from Croton-on-Hudson, offering chic, modern guest rooms, an onsite restaurant and bar, and a rooftop lounge with stunning views of the Hudson River.

  • Riverfront Motel - Situated just steps from the Hudson River, this cozy motel offers comfortable guest rooms with scenic views, as well as a complimentary continental breakfast and outdoor seating area.

What to Buy in Croton-on-Hudson: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Crafts and Gifts - Croton-on-Hudson is known for its vibrant arts scene, with a range of local artisans offering one-of-a-kind handmade crafts, jewelry, and gifts.

  • Local Produce and Products - Visit a local farmers market or specialty shop to sample locally made jams, honey, cheese, and other products made from fresh, locally sourced ingredients.

  • Historic Memorabilia - Browse gift shops and antique stores to find unique souvenirs and historic memorabilia, including photos, postcards, and other items related to Croton-on-Hudson’s rich history and culture.

Local Culture and Customs of Croton-on-Hudson

Croton-on-Hudson is a friendly, welcoming community that values its natural heritage, local history, and vibrant arts scene. Visitors are encouraged to explore the village’s many attractions, sample locally made food and drink, and immerse themselves in the local culture and customs.

Best Time to Visit Croton-on-Hudson

Croton-on-Hudson is beautiful year-round, but the best time to visit depends on your interests. The summer months (June-August) are ideal for outdoor activities like hiking, biking, and kayaking, while the fall (September-November) offers stunning autumn foliage and seasonal harvest festivals. Winter (December-February) is ideal for cross-country skiing, snowshoeing, and other winter sports, while the spring (March-May) brings lush greenery and the blooming of local flowers and trees.

Safety and Health

Safety in Croton-on-Hudson

Croton-on-Hudson is generally a safe and welcoming community, but visitors should still take standard precautions to keep themselves and their belongings secure. Some tips to keep in mind include:

Getting to Croton-on-Hudson: Transportation Options and Logistics

Croton-on-Hudson is easily accessible by car, train, or bus. Some common transportation options include:

Airports Near Croton-on-Hudson

  • Westchester County Airport - Located just 10 miles away in White Plains, NY, this regional airport offers flights to many major U.S. cities.
  • Stewart International Airport - Located about 35 miles away in Newburgh, NY, this mid-sized airport offers flights to several destinations across the U.S.

Bus Stations Near Croton-on-Hudson

  • Greyhound Bus Lines - The nearest Greyhound bus station is located in White Plains, about 20 minutes away by car.

Other Options

  • Metro-North Hudson Line - This scenic commuter train runs from Grand Central Terminal in New York City to Croton-on-Hudson, with several stops along the way.
  • Car Rentals - Several car rental companies operate in nearby Westchester County, offering a range of vehicle options for visitors who prefer to drive during their stay.

How to Get Around in Croton-on-Hudson

Croton-on-Hudson is a walkable, bikeable village, with several pedestrian-friendly pathways and bike trails. There are also several local taxi and shuttle services available, as well as car rental options for visitors who want to explore the surrounding area. Some popular transportation options include:

  • Croton Trolley - This convenient shuttle service offers transportation to and from many popular local attractions, including parks, museums, and restaurants.

  • E-Bike Rentals - Several local rental businesses offer electric bike rentals, allowing visitors to explore the village and surrounding area with ease.

  • Walking and Biking Trails - Croton-on-Hudson features several scenic walking and biking trails, including the Croton Aqueduct Trail and the Old Croton Trail.

Where to Go Next from Croton-on-Hudson: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Croton-on-Hudson is surrounded by many other charming villages and natural attractions in the Hudson River Valley region. Some nearby destinations worth exploring include:

  • Sleepy Hollow - This historic village is located just a short drive from Croton-on-Hudson, and boasts several famous landmarks, including the Sleepy Hollow Cemetery and the Headless Horseman Bridge.

  • Hudson Valley Wine Region - The Hudson Valley is home to several world-renowned wineries and vineyards, offering visitors a chance to sample local wines and enjoy scenic views.

  • Bear Mountain State Park - Located just 20 miles away from Croton-on-Hudson, this expansive park offers stunning views of the Hudson River, as well as hiking trails, picnic areas, and a zoo.
