Introduction to Lewisboro

Located in Westchester County in Downstate New York, Lewisboro is a hidden gem in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States of America. The town is known for its stunning natural surroundings, rich cultural heritage, and wide range of outdoor activities. If you are a nature lover, Lewisboro is the perfect place to explore the great outdoors, enjoy scenic views, and take part in exciting adventure activities. In addition to its natural beauty, Lewisboro also offers a range of cultural attractions and historical sites that are worth visiting.

List of Places to Visit in Lewisboro

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Lewisboro Town House Museum: This museum tells the story of the town’s rich history through interactive exhibits, fascinating artifacts, and guided tours.
  • The South Salem Presbyterian Church: Built in the 18th century, this beautiful church is a great example of colonial architecture and a must-visit for history buffs.
  • The Katonah Museum of Art: Located just a short drive from Lewisboro, the Katonah Museum of Art is a world-class cultural institution that features some of the best contemporary artwork in the region.

Natural Attractions of Lewisboro: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ward Pound Ridge Reservation: This beautiful park is spread over 4,315 acres and is the largest park in Westchester County. It offers a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, horseback riding, and cross-country skiing in the winter.
  • Leon Levy Preserve: This scenic nature preserve is the perfect place to explore the great outdoors and enjoy stunning views of the countryside.
  • Lake Truesdale: This beautiful lake is a popular spot for swimming, fishing, and boating in the summer months. There is also a small beach area where you can relax and soak up the sun.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lewisboro

Lewisboro is a hiker’s paradise, with miles of trails that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside. The following are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • The Leatherman’s Loop Trail: This challenging 10k trail takes you through rugged terrain and offers stunning views of the countryside.
  • The Angle Fly Preserve: This 654-acre nature reserve is ideal for a leisurely hike or a peaceful bike ride.
  • The Lewisboro Trailway: This 15-mile trail connects the town to its neighboring communities and offers a range of hiking and biking options.

Best Things to Do in Lewisboro: Activities and Tours

  • Take a guided tour of the Lewisboro Town House Museum to learn about the town’s rich history and culture.
  • Go hiking in Ward Pound Ridge Reservation and enjoy some of the most breathtaking views in the region.
  • Take a leisurely bike ride through the Angle Fly Preserve and soak up the natural beauty of the area.
  • Go swimming, fishing, or boating at Lake Truesdale during the summer months.
  • Attend one of the many cultural festivals and events that take place in the town throughout the year.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lewisboro

When planning your trip to Lewisboro, consider the following itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive in Lewisboro and check in at your hotel or guesthouse. Explore the town center and visit the Lewisboro Town House Museum.

Day 2: Go hiking in Ward Pound Ridge Reservation or take a leisurely bike ride through the Angle Fly Preserve.

Day 3: Visit the South Salem Presbyterian Church and enjoy a picnic lunch at Lake Truesdale.

Day 4: Take a day trip to the nearby Katonah Museum of Art and explore the town’s charming shops and restaurants.

Day 5: Enjoy a scenic drive through the countryside and visit some of the town’s picturesque villages and hamlets.

Day 6: Go horseback riding in Ward Pound Ridge Reservation or take a yoga class at a local studio.

Day 7: Depart Lewisboro and head to your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Lewisboro

Must-Try Dishes

  • Apple Cider Doughnuts: Made with fresh, locally sourced apples, these delicious doughnuts are a Lewisboro specialty.
  • Maple Syrup: The town is known for its rich, delicious maple syrup, which is sourced from local maple trees.
  • Lobster Roll: A popular dish in the area, the lobster roll is a must-try for seafood lovers.

Local Beverages

  • Hard Apple Cider: Made with locally grown apples, Lewisboro’s hard apple cider is a refreshing and delicious drink.
  • Wine: The area is home to some of the best wineries in the region, and a wine-tasting tour is a great way to explore the local vineyards.
  • Craft Beer: The town has a thriving craft beer scene, with a range of local breweries offering delicious brews.

Where to Stay in Lewisboro: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Ethan Allen Hotel: This elegant hotel is located just a short drive from Lewisboro and offers luxurious rooms, a swimming pool, and an on-site restaurant.
  • The Redding Roadhouse Inn: This charming bed and breakfast is located in the heart of the town and offers comfortable rooms, a cozy fireplace, and delicious breakfasts.
  • The Inn at South Salem: This historic inn is located just a short walk from the South Salem Presbyterian Church and offers cozy rooms and a welcoming atmosphere.

What to Buy in Lewisboro: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maple Syrup: A sweet and delicious souvenir that is sure to remind you of your Lewisboro adventure.
  • Local Artwork: The area is home to many talented artists, and there are a range of galleries and shops where you can buy beautiful paintings, sculptures, and other artwork.
  • Handcrafted Jewelry: The town has a thriving craft scene, and you can find a range of unique and beautiful jewelry made by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Lewisboro

  • The town has a rich history and culture, and visitors are encouraged to learn about the area’s fascinating past through guided tours and exhibits.
  • Lewisboro is known for its hospitality, and visitors are sure to feel welcome and at home in this charming town.
  • The area has a strong sense of community, and locals are proud to share their traditions and customs with visitors from around the world.

Best Time to Visit Lewisboro

The best time to visit Lewisboro is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny and there are a range of outdoor activities to enjoy. However, the town is beautiful year-round, and visitors can enjoy the stunning fall foliage in the autumn months and the winter wonderland scenery in the winter.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lewisboro

Do:

  • Take a guided tour of the Lewisboro Town House Museum to learn about the town’s rich history and culture.
  • Explore the great outdoors and enjoy the scenic views and exciting adventure activities.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages, including the hard apple cider, maple syrup, and lobster roll.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the natural surroundings and beauty of the area.
  • Be disrespectful to the local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lewisboro

Lewisboro is a safe and welcoming town, but visitors should always take basic precautions to ensure their safety, such as locking their doors and windows at night and avoiding poorly lit areas after dark.

Health in Lewisboro

Visitors to Lewisboro should ensure that they have adequate health insurance coverage before traveling to the area. It is also important to take basic precautions to protect against insect bites and other potential health hazards.

Getting to Lewisboro: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lewisboro

The nearest airports to Lewisboro are Westchester County Airport, LaGuardia Airport, and JFK International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Lewisboro

The nearest bus stations to Lewisboro are the Katonah train station and the Mount Kisco train station.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Lewisboro or take a taxi or rideshare service from nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Lewisboro

The best way to get around Lewisboro is by car, as the town is spread out and there is limited public transportation available. Visitors can also rent bicycles or arrange for a guided tour of the area.

Where to Go Next from Lewisboro: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • New York City: Located just a short drive or train ride from Lewisboro, New York City is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to the area.
  • The Hudson Valley: This beautiful region is home to some of the most stunning natural scenery in the state, as well as a range of cultural attractions and outdoor activities.
  • The Berkshires: Located just across the border in Massachusetts, the Berkshires offer stunning natural beauty, charming towns, and a range of activities and attractions.

Conclusion

Lewisboro is a beautiful and welcoming town that offers a range of outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and delicious cuisine. Whether you are a nature lover, history buff, or foodie, there is something for everyone in this charming town. Plan your trip today and discover all that Lewisboro has to offer!
