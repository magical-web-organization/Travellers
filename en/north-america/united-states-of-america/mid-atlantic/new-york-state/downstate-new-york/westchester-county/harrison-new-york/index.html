Introduction to Harrison (New York)

Harrison is a suburban town located in Westchester County, New York, with a population of over 28,000 residents. It is located just 22 miles northeast of Manhattan, making it an ideal place to visit for those who want to escape the hustle and bustle of the city while still being close to it. Harrison is a beautiful and peaceful destination that attracts visitors with its rich history, cultural attractions, outdoor activities, and charming restaurants.

List of Places to Visit in Harrison (New York)

If you’re planning a visit to Harrison, you won’t want to miss these top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Harrison Historical Society: The society is dedicated to preserving and promoting the history of Harrison, offering exhibits and events throughout the year.

  • St. Paul’s Church National Historic Site: This historic stone church, built in 1765, served as a hospital during the Revolutionary War. Today, it is a museum that offers tours and special exhibits.

  • Rye Playland: This amusement park has been in operation for over 80 years, offering thrilling rides, games, and attractions for all ages.

Natural Attractions of Harrison (New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Saxon Woods Park: This park is known for its beautiful hiking trails, picnic areas, and sports fields. There is also an 18-hole golf course and a small lake for fishing.

  • Playland Beach: This beach offers a great place to relax and soak up the sun, while also providing opportunities for swimming and water sports.

  • Kensico Dam Plaza: This park offers stunning views of the Kensico Reservoir, as well as walking paths, playgrounds, and picnic areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Harrison (New York)

Harrison offers many great hiking and biking routes for outdoor enthusiasts. Some popular options include:

  • Colonial Greenway: This trail connects multiple historic sites and parks, offering a peaceful and scenic route for walkers, runners, and cyclists.

  • Westchester County Trailway: This trail spans over 22 miles, connecting multiple towns throughout Westchester County and offering beautiful views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Harrison (New York): Activities and Tours

  • Harrison Farmers’ Market: This market, open on Saturdays from May through November, offers fresh produce, baked goods, and other local products.

  • Blind Brook Club: This exclusive club offers golf, tennis, swimming, and dining for members and their guests.

  • Westchester Wine School: This school offers classes and tastings for wine enthusiasts, as well as private events and consulting services.

Week-Long Itinerary for Harrison (New York)

Day 1: Visit the Harrison Historical Society and the Rye Playland.

Day 2: Explore Saxon Woods Park, followed by a picnic lunch.

Day 3: Take a guided tour of St. Paul’s Church National Historic Site and enjoy lunch at a local restaurant.

Day 4: Go for a leisurely bike ride along the Colonial Greenway, stopping at historic sites along the way.

Day 5: Spend the day at Playland Beach, soaking up the sun and enjoying water sports.

Day 6: Go wine tasting at the Westchester Wine School, followed by a gourmet dinner at a local restaurant.

Day 7: Play golf or tennis at the Blind Brook Club, followed by a farewell dinner at a local hotspot.

Food and Drink Scene of Harrison (New York)

  • Chicken Parmesan: This classic dish is a favorite at many Italian restaurants in Harrison.

  • Lobster Roll: Made with fresh lobster meat, this sandwich is a summertime favorite.

  • Thin-Crust Pizza: Harrison is known for its delicious pizza, with many restaurants using local ingredients and traditional methods to create their pies.

  • Craft Beer: Harrison is home to several breweries that offer locally made craft beer, often using ingredients from the surrounding area.

  • Wine: Westchester County is known for its vineyards, and Harrison is no exception. Many local restaurants offer wine lists featuring wines from the area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Harrison (New York)

Harrison is a safe and peaceful community, with a low crime rate. Visitors should take normal precautions, such as locking car doors and securing valuables.
