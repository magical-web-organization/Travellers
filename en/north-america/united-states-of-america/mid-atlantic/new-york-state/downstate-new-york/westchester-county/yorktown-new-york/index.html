Introduction to Yorktown (New York)

Yorktown is a town located in Westchester County, Downstate New York, Mid-Atlantic, United States of America. It is situated in the northern part of the county, approximately 33 miles from New York City. Known for its great outdoor activities, Yorktown offers a plethora of opportunities for hiking, biking, and exploring the great outdoors. In addition to its natural beauty, Yorktown is also home to a rich cultural history, reflected in its many museums, historical sites, and attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Yorktown (New York)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Yorktown Museum: The Yorktown Museum showcases the town’s history, from its earliest days to the present. With exhibits ranging from Native American artifacts to the Revolutionary War, this museum is a must-see for history buffs.
  • John Jay Homestead: This historic site was once the home of John Jay, one of America’s founding fathers and the first Chief Justice of the United States. Now a museum, it offers a glimpse into 18th-century life, with beautiful gardens and a working farm.
  • Pines Bridge Monument: A landmark in Yorktown, the Pines Bridge Monument commemorates the Revolutionary War battle that took place here, in which American troops successfully defended a strategic bridge against British forces.

Natural Attractions of Yorktown (New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Turkey Mountain Nature Preserve: Turkey Mountain Nature Preserve is Yorktown’s largest park, offering over 830 acres of trails for hiking and biking, as well as picturesque lakes and streams.
  • Kitchawan Preserve: The Kitchawan Preserve is a 200-acre protected wildlife sanctuary that is open to the public. With trails that wind through forests, meadows, and wetlands, it offers a unique opportunity to see a variety of wildlife up close.
  • Lasdon Park and Arboretum: Lasdon Park and Arboretum, located on the Croton Reservoir, offers over 228 acres of natural beauty to explore. With walking trails, a conservatory, and a horticultural library, it is a great place to spend a day.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yorktown (New York)

Yorktown is a mecca for outdoor enthusiasts, with miles of hiking and biking trails just waiting to be explored. Some of the best trails in the area include:

  • North County Trailway: This 22-mile trailway runs through Yorktown and is perfect for hiking, jogging, and biking. With views of the Hudson River and the surrounding countryside, it’s a scenic route you won’t want to miss.
  • Croton Gorge Park: Located just a few miles outside of Yorktown, Croton Gorge Park features hiking trails that wind through beautiful woods and along the Croton River.
  • Blue Mountain Reservation: Blue Mountain Reservation boasts over 1,500 acres of woods, lakes, and streams to explore. With miles of well-marked trails for hikers and bikers, it’s a great place to spend a day outdoors.

Best Things to Do in Yorktown (New York): Activities and Tours

  • Horseback Riding: If you’re looking for a unique way to experience the beauty of Yorktown, why not try a horseback riding tour? There are several stables in the area that offer guided trail rides.
  • Golfing: Yorktown is home to several golf courses, including the Mohansic Golf Course and Hollow Brook Golf Club. Whether you’re a seasoned pro or a beginner, there’s a course for you to enjoy.
  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: For a truly unforgettable experience, take a hot air balloon ride over Yorktown and the surrounding countryside. Several companies offer tours from May through October.

Week-Long Itinerary for Yorktown (New York)

Day 1: Visit the Yorktown Museum and learn about the town’s history.

Day 2: Spend the day hiking in Turkey Mountain Nature Preserve.

Day 3: Check out the John Jay Homestead and enjoy the beautiful gardens.

Day 4: Take a horseback riding tour through the Kitchawan Preserve.

Day 5: Golf at one of Yorktown’s beautiful courses.

Day 6: Take a hot air balloon tour and see Yorktown from a new perspective.

Day 7: Relax at Lasdon Park and Arboretum, with a picnic and some birdwatching.

Food and Drink Scene of Yorktown (New York)

Yorktown boasts a variety of dining options, from upscale restaurants to casual cafes. Some must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Yorktown Sandwich: This local favorite is a hearty sandwich made with beef, chicken, or vegetables and topped with melted cheese and crispy onions.
  • Hudson Valley Duck Breast: Another local favorite, this dish features succulent duck breast served with seasonal vegetables and a sweet glaze.
  • Apple Cider Donuts: Made with locally sourced apples, these donuts are a perfect fall treat.

Local Beverages

  • Hudson Valley Wine: The Hudson Valley is home to several wineries, producing award-winning wines.
  • Captain Lawrence Brewing Company: This local brewery has won numerous awards for its craft beers, including the popular Liquid Gold.

Where to Stay in Yorktown (New York): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Yorktown has a variety of options for accommodation, ranging from budget-friendly to upscale. Some of the best options in the area include:

  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites: Conveniently located near major highways, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and a complimentary breakfast.
  • Cromwell Manor Historic Inn: For a unique experience, stay at this historic inn, which dates back to 1820. With just four guest rooms, it offers a cozy, personalized atmosphere.
  • Inn on the Hudson: This upscale hotel offers beautiful views of the Hudson River, as well as amenities such as a fitness center and indoor pool.

What to Buy in Yorktown (New York): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or want to take home a taste of Yorktown, consider purchasing:

  • Hudson Valley Wine: Bring home a bottle of local wine to enjoy.
  • Homemade Pies: Several local bakeries offer delicious homemade pies, made with fresh, seasonal ingredients.
  • Handmade Jewelry: Several local artisans sell unique, handmade jewelry featuring local gemstones and materials.

Local Culture and Customs of Yorktown (New York)

Yorktown is proud of its rich cultural heritage, which is reflected in its many museums, historic sites, and attractions. The town has a strong sense of community and is known for its friendly, welcoming atmosphere.

Best Time to Visit Yorktown (New York)

The best time to visit Yorktown is in the fall, when the leaves are changing colors and the weather is mild. Spring is also a great time to visit, as the flowers are in bloom and the weather is comfortable.

Safety and Health

Safety in Yorktown (New York)

Yorktown is a safe town, with low crime rates. However, it’s always important to take basic safety precautions, such as locking doors and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Yorktown (New York)

Yorktown is home to several hospitals and medical facilities, including Hudson Valley Hospital Center and NewYork-Presbyterian/Hudson Valley Hospital. It’s important to have medical insurance and bring any necessary medications with you when traveling.

Getting to Yorktown (New York): Transportation Options and Logistics

Yorktown is easily accessible by both car and public transportation. Some of the best options for getting to Yorktown include:

  • Westchester County Airport: Located just 19 miles from Yorktown, Westchester County Airport offers both domestic and international flights.
  • Metro-North Railroad: The Metro-North Railroad offers service to the nearby towns of Croton-Harmon and Peekskill, from where you can take a taxi or bus to Yorktown.
  • Car Rental: Renting a car is a great option for those who want to explore the surrounding area at their own pace.

Airports Near Yorktown (New York)

  • Westchester County Airport: Located just 19 miles from Yorktown, Westchester County Airport offers both domestic and international flights.
  • John F. Kennedy International Airport: JFK is located approximately 50 miles from Yorktown.

Bus Stations Near Yorktown (New York)

  • MBTA: The MBTA offers bus service to New York City and other nearby cities.
  • Bee-Line Bus System: The Bee-Line Bus System offers local bus service in Westchester County.

Other Options

  • Taxi Service: Taxi service is available in Yorktown and the surrounding area.
  • Uber/Lyft: Uber and Lyft also operate in the area.

How to Get Around in Yorktown (New York)

Driving is the most convenient way to get around Yorktown and the surrounding area. However, for those without a car, there are several options for public transportation, including buses and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Yorktown (New York): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore the area surrounding Yorktown, consider visiting:

  • The Hudson Valley: With its beautiful landscapes, historic sites, and charming towns, the Hudson Valley is a must-see destination.
  • New York City: Just a short drive or train ride away, New York City offers endless opportunities for sightseeing, shopping, and entertainment.
  • Bear Mountain State Park: Located just over 20 miles from Yorktown, Bear Mountain State Park offers beautiful scenery and a variety of outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Yorktown is a beautiful town with a rich cultural heritage and a wealth of outdoor activities to enjoy. Whether you’re interested in history, hiking, or just relaxing in the great outdoors, Yorktown has something to offer everyone. So if you’re looking for a new destination to explore in the Mid-Atlantic region, consider a trip to Yorktown!
