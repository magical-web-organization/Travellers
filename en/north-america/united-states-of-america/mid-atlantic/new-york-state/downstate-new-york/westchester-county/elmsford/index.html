Introduction to Elmsford

Elmsford is a charming village in Westchester County, in Downstate New York. It is located within the town of Greenburgh and is surrounded by lush foliage and natural beauty. Despite being a small village, Elmsford boasts a vibrant cultural and recreational scene, which makes it an ideal destination for all kinds of travelers. In this travel guide, we will explore the top attractions, activities, and hidden gems of Elmsford to help you plan your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Elmsford

Elmsford is home to a variety of must-see places, including museums, parks, and historical attractions. Here are some of the top places that you should include in your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Greenburgh Nature Center: This non-profit nature preserve spans 33 acres and features live animals, nature trails, a pond, and a greenhouse. It is a great spot to learn about wildlife, ecology, and sustainability.
  • Lyndhurst Mansion: This Gothic Revival mansion is a National Historic Landmark and was once owned by railroad magnate Jay Gould. Visitors can take guided tours of the property, enjoy stunning Hudson River views, and explore the meticulously landscaped grounds.
  • Philipsburg Manor: This 18th-century Colonial estate offers a glimpse into the life of wealthy Dutch landowners and their enslaved African workers. Visitors can tour the restored manor house, gristmill, and farm, and participate in interactive educational programs.

Natural Attractions of Elmsford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rockefeller State Park Preserve: This 1,700-acre park offers a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, birdwatching, fishing, and horseback riding. It also has several scenic spots, such as Swan Lake and the Pocantico River.
  • Kensico Dam Plaza: This park features a massive dam and reservoir, as well as picnic areas, playgrounds, and a beautiful fountain. It is a popular spot for jogging, cycling, and relaxing with family and friends.
  • Tarrytown Lakes: This tranquil spot is a favorite among locals for fishing, hiking, and ice skating. It consists of two connected lakes surrounded by wooded trails and peaceful landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Elmsford

Elmsford offers several picturesque hiking and biking trails for outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • South County Trailway: This 14.1-mile trail follows the path of the former Putnam Division of the New York Central Railroad. It goes through Elmsford and connects several other Westchester towns, offering a scenic and relatively easy ride.
  • Rockefeller State Park Preserve Trails: This park has over 40 miles of carriage roads and footpaths that allow visitors to explore the different habitats and ecosystems of the area. Some of the most popular trails include the Brothers Path, the Eagle Hill Trail, and the Rockwood Hall Trail.
  • Old Croton Aqueduct State Historic Park: This park offers a unique hike along the historic aqueduct that supplied water to New York City in the 1800s. The trail is approximately 26 miles long and passes through Elmsford, offering beautiful views of the Hudson River and the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Elmsford: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and biking, Elmsford has plenty of other activities and tours to offer. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Horseback Riding at Pocantico Hills Stable: This family-owned stable offers guided trail rides through the beautiful Rockefeller State Park Preserve. It is a fun and unique way to experience the park and its natural beauty.
  • Escape Room Game at Hour to Exit: This escape room facility offers a thrilling and challenging experience for groups of friends or families. Participants have to solve puzzles and riddles to unlock a series of clues in order to escape a locked room before the time runs out.
  • Wine Tasting at Tarrytown Wine & Spirits: This wine store and tasting room offers a wide selection of wines, beers, and spirits from around the world. Visitors can enjoy a guided wine tasting experience, or simply browse the store for their favorite drink.

Week-Long Itinerary for Elmsford

To help you make the most of your trip to Elmsford, here is a suggested itinerary for a one-week stay:

Day 1: Visit the Greenburgh Nature Center and hike the nearby trails. Day 2: Take a guided tour of the Lyndhurst Mansion and then sample some local cuisine at Chutney Masala Indian Bistro. Day 3: Spend the day exploring the Rockefeller State Park Preserve, enjoying a picnic lunch by the lake, and taking a horseback ride. Day 4: Visit Philipsburg Manor and learn about the area’s rich history, followed by an early dinner at the RiverMarket Bar and Kitchen. Day 5: Go on a wine tasting tour at Tarrytown Wine & Spirits and then catch a movie at the Alamo Drafthouse Cinema. Day 6: Explore the Old Croton Aqueduct State Historic Park and have a picnic lunch at one of the scenic spots along the way. Day 7: Relax at the Kensico Dam Plaza, enjoy a bike ride along the South County Trailway, and have dinner at the Vintage Kitchen.

Food and Drink Scene of Elmsford

Elmsford has a diverse and exciting food and drink scene, with options ranging from international cuisine to local favorites. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Burger at The Beehive: This charming gastropub serves delicious burgers made with grass-fed beef and locally sourced ingredients. They also have a great selection of craft beers and cocktails.
  • Falafel at Turquoise: This Mediterranean restaurant offers authentic and flavorful dishes, including their signature falafel made with fresh herbs and spices.
  • Taco at Salsa Picante: This family-owned Mexican restaurant serves some of the best tacos in town. Their homemade tortillas and salsas are a must-try.

Local Beverages

  • Beer at Captain Lawrence Brewing Company: This award-winning craft brewery is located in nearby Pleasantville and offers a range of creative and tasty beers. Visitors can take a brewery tour and sample some of their brews in the tasting room.
  • Cider at Doc’s Draft Hard Cider: This local cidery produces a variety of handmade ciders using fresh, locally sourced apples. Visitors can tour the facility, taste their ciders, and even take home a bottle or two.
  • Wine at Sleepy Hollow Wine & Spirits: This boutique wine and spirits store offers a curated selection of wines from around the world, with a focus on local and organic producers. Visitors can attend wine tastings and events, or simply browse the store for their favorite bottle.

Where to Stay in Elmsford: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Elmsford has a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Hampton Inn White Plains/Tarrytown: This modern hotel offers comfortable rooms and suites, complimentary breakfast, and a fitness center. It is located close to several attractions and restaurants.
  • Westchester Marriott: This upscale hotel features spacious and elegant rooms, an indoor pool, and a restaurant serving American cuisine. It is located near the Westchester County Center and other local landmarks.
  • Elmsford Motel: This budget-friendly motel offers simple and clean rooms with basic amenities, such as a TV, refrigerator, and free Wi-Fi. It is conveniently located near the highway and several fast-food restaurants.

What to Buy in Elmsford: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking for unique and local souvenirs to bring home from Elmsford, here are some ideas:

  • Handmade Crafts at Greenburgh Nature Center Gift Shop: This gift shop offers a range of eco-friendly and handmade items, including jewelry, toys, and home decor. Proceeds from sales support the nature center’s educational programs and animal care.
  • Local Honey at Apiary: The Local Honey Co.: This small and artisanal honey producer offers a variety of honey products made from locally harvested honey. Visitors can sample different flavors and learn about the benefits of raw and unfiltered honey.
  • Books at The Voracious Reader: This independent bookstore specializes in children’s and young adult books, as well as toys and gifts. They have a knowledgeable and friendly staff, and often host author events and book clubs.

Local Culture and Customs of Elmsford

Elmsford has a rich cultural heritage, blending different influences and traditions. The area has a strong agricultural and industrial history, which can be seen in the local landmarks and museums. The community is also known for its environmental and sustainability efforts, with several organizations and initiatives dedicated to preserving the natural resources and wildlife of the area.

Best Time to Visit Elmsford

Elmsford can be visited year-round, but the best time to go depends on your interests and preferences. Spring and fall are ideal for outdoor activities, such as hiking, biking, and horseback riding, as the weather is mild and the foliage is beautiful. Summer is a great time for outdoor concerts, festivals, and markets, with plenty of events happening throughout the region. Winter can be cold and snowy, but it is a good time to enjoy indoor activities, such as museums, theaters, and restaurants.

Do’s and Don’ts of Elmsford

To make the most of your trip to Elmsford, here are some dos and don’ts:

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, including farm-to-table restaurants and artisanal food producers.
  • Do explore the natural and cultural landmarks of the area, such as the Rockefeller State Park Preserve and Lyndhurst Mansion.
  • Do support local businesses, such as independent bookstores and gift shops.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment, especially the parks and trails.
  • Don’t ignore the history and culture of the area, including the role of slavery and Indigenous peoples in shaping the region.
  • Don’t overlook the nearby towns and attractions, such as Sleepy Hollow, Tarrytown, and White Plains.

Safety and Health

Elmsford is generally a safe and low-crime area, but visitors should take standard precautions, such as locking their car and not leaving valuables in plain sight. The local hospitals and medical facilities are well-equipped and offer quality care, but visitors should have travel insurance in case of emergencies.

Safety in Elmsford

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially when walking or biking at night.
  • Avoid leaving your valuables in your car or backpack, and keep them secure at all times.
  • Follow the rules and guidelines of the parks and attractions, especially when hiking or swimming.

Health in Elmsford

  • Drink plenty of water and stay hydrated, especially during the summer months.
  • Wear sunscreen and protective clothing, as the sun can be strong and damaging.
  • Take precautions against ticks and other insects, such as wearing long pants and using insect repellent.

Getting to Elmsford: Transportation Options and Logistics

Elmsford is easily accessible by car, train, or bus. Here are some of the best ways to get there:

  • By Car: Elmsford is located near several major highways, including the New York State Thruway, the Saw Mill River Parkway, and the Taconic State Parkway. It is approximately 30 miles from New York City.
  • By Train: The nearest train station is in Tarrytown, which is served by Metro-North Railroad on the Hudson Line. From there, visitors can take a taxi or bus to Elmsford, which is about 4 miles away.
  • By Bus: Several bus companies, including Greyhound and Coach USA, operate routes to and from Elmsford. The nearest bus station is in White Plains, which is about 6 miles away.

Airports Near Elmsford

  • Westchester County Airport (HPN): This airport is located in nearby White Plains and offers domestic flights from major airlines, such as Delta, American, and United.
  • LaGuardia Airport (LGA): This airport is located in Queens, New York City, and offers domestic and international flights from major airlines, such as Delta, American, and JetBlue.
  • John F. Kennedy International Airport (JFK): This airport is located in Queens, New York City, and offers international flights from major airlines, such as Delta, American, and Emirates.

Bus Stations Near Elmsford

  • White Plains Bus Terminal: This bus terminal is located in downtown White Plains and offers routes to and from several destinations, including New York City, Boston, and Washington, DC.
  • Tarrytown Train Station: This train station also serves as a bus stop for several companies, including Greyhound and Coach USA.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car at any of the nearby airports or in downtown White Plains. This is a convenient option for those who want to explore the area on their own schedule.
  • Taxi or Uber: Visitors can also take a taxi or Uber from the airport or train station to Elmsford. This can be a more expensive option, but it offers flexibility and convenience.

How to Get Around in Elmsford

The best way to get around Elmsford is by car, as many of the attractions and activities are spread out and not easily accessible by public transportation. Taxis and Ubers are also available, but they can be more expensive and less convenient. If you are staying in the area and plan to do a lot of sightseeing, renting a car is the most practical and cost-effective option.

Where to Go Next from Elmsford: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Elmsford is located near several other popular destinations and attractions in the Hudson Valley and New York City area. Here are some ideas for where to go next:

  • Sleepy Hollow: This nearby town is famous for its spooky legends and historic landmarks, including the Sleepy Hollow Cemetery and the Old Dutch Church.
  • Tarrytown: Known for its charming Main Street and Hudson River views, Tarrytown offers plenty of shops, restaurants, and cultural events, such as the Tarrytown Music Hall.
  • White Plains: This bustling city is the commercial hub of Westchester County and offers shopping, dining, and entertainment options for all tastes and budgets.
  • New York City: Just a short train or car ride away, New York City needs no introduction. From the Empire State Building to Central Park, the city offers endless attractions and experiences for visitors of all ages.

Conclusion

Elmsford is a hidden gem in the Hudson Valley, offering a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural richness, and outdoor adventure. Whether you are interested in history, nature, or food, there is something for everyone in this charming village.
