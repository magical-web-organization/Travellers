Introduction to Yonkers

Located in Westchester County in Downstate New York, Yonkers is a vibrant city known for its rich history, natural beauty, and diverse cultural attractions. The city is home to more than 200,000 residents and offers a unique blend of urban and suburban living. With easy access to New York City and major attractions, Yonkers is the perfect destination for a quick weekend getaway or a longer vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Yonkers

Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, history buff, or culture lover, Yonkers has plenty to offer. Here’s a list of must-visit places in Yonkers:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hudson River Museum: This museum is a must-visit in Yonkers. Located in a beautiful park on the banks of the Hudson River, the museum features art, science, and history exhibits, as well as a planetarium and outdoor amphitheater.
  • Philipse Manor Hall State Historic Site: Step back in time and explore the history of Yonkers at this 18th-century historic house museum. The museum features exhibits on the Philipse family, who were some of the wealthiest landowners in Colonial America.
  • Science Barge: This unique attraction is a floating urban farm and environmental education center that showcases sustainable agriculture and renewable energy practices. Visitors can learn about hydroponics, solar power, wind energy, and more.

Natural Attractions of Yonkers: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tibbetts Brook Park: This 161-acre park is a popular destination for picnic, fishing, hiking, and swimming in the summer months. The park also features an 18-hole golf course and a children’s playground.
  • John F. Kennedy Marina: Located on the Hudson River, this marina offers stunning views of the Manhattan skyline. Visitors can rent kayaks, canoes, and paddleboards to explore the river or enjoy a meal at the marina’s waterfront restaurant.
  • Untermyer Gardens: This 43-acre park is a hidden gem that features a stunning Persian garden, a classical temple, a rock garden, and a waterfall. The park is perfect for a peaceful stroll or a relaxing picnic.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yonkers

Yonkers offers a variety of hiking and biking trails for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Old Croton Aqueduct Trail: This 26.2-mile trail spans from Yonkers to Croton-on-Hudson and offers beautiful views of the Hudson River, historic landmarks, and charming towns. The trail is perfect for biking or hiking.
  • Tibbetts Brook Park Trail: This 2.2-mile trail is located within Tibbetts Brook Park and offers scenic views of the park’s lake, forest, and waterfall. The trail is ideal for a leisurely stroll or a family bike ride.
  • Saxon Woods Park Trail: This 2.5-mile trail winds through the scenic Saxon Woods Park and features rolling hills, lush forests, and a lake. The trail is perfect for hiking, jogging, or a quick bike ride.

Best Things to Do in Yonkers: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re traveling solo or with family, Yonkers has plenty of fun activities and tours to keep you entertained. Here are some of the best things to do in Yonkers:

  • Visit the Empire City Casino: If you’re feeling lucky, head to the Empire City Casino for some gambling fun. The casino features over 5,000 slot machines and electronic table games, as well as several dining options and live entertainment.
  • Take a Hudson River Cruise: Enjoy stunning views of the Hudson River and the Manhattan skyline on a scenic cruise. Several tour operators offer different types of cruises, including sightseeing tours, dinner cruises, and party boats.
  • Explore Street Art: Yonkers is known for its vibrant street art scene, with several murals and graffiti paintings adorning the city’s buildings. Take a street art walking tour to discover some of the city’s most impressive pieces.

Week-Long Itinerary for Yonkers

If you have a week to spare, Yonkers is the perfect destination for an immersive vacation. Here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

  • Day 1: Visit the Hudson River Museum and explore Glenview Mansion.
  • Day 2: Take a guided city tour of Yonkers and visit Philipse Manor Hall State Historic Site.
  • Day 3: Hike the Old Croton Aqueduct Trail and enjoy a picnic at Untermyer Gardens.
  • Day 4: Visit the Science Barge and enjoy a sunset cruise on the Hudson River.
  • Day 5: Explore the city’s street art scene and take a cooking class at a local restaurant.
  • Day 6: Go shopping at Ridge Hill Shopping Center and take a yoga class in the park.
  • Day 7: Visit the Empire City Casino and enjoy a final dinner at a waterfront restaurant.

Food and Drink Scene of Yonkers

Yonkers offers a diverse culinary scene with plenty of options for food and drink enthusiasts. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pizza from Louie & Johnnie’s CheeseSteaks and Clam Bar: This local pizzeria is known for its old-school pies, with crispy crusts and generous toppings.
  • Pierogi from Piast Bakery: This Polish bakery is a Yonkers institution, serving up delicious pierogi, kielbasa, and other European specialties.
  • Lobster Roll from Yonkers Fish Market: This family-owned seafood market offers fresh catches and delicious seafood sandwiches, including a classic lobster roll.

Local Beverages

  • Yonkers Brewing Co. Beer: This local brewery offers a variety of craft beers, including the popular Yonkers Lager and the Hoptileitious IPA.
  • Hudson Valley Wine: Yonkers is located in the heart of the Hudson Valley wine region, known for its world-class wines. Visit nearby vineyards to sample some of the best wines in the area.
  • Yonkers Roasters Coffee: For coffee lovers, Yonkers Roasters is a must-visit. The local coffee shop offers a variety of single-origin and blended coffees, as well as pastries and other snacks.

Where to Stay in Yonkers: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Yonkers offers a range of accommodation options for travelers, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the best places to stay in Yonkers:

  • Hampton Inn & Suites Yonkers: This modern hotel is located near popular attractions and offers comfortable rooms, free breakfast, and an indoor pool.
  • Royal Regency Hotel: This upscale hotel is perfect for travelers seeking luxury accommodations. The hotel features spacious suites, a fitness center, and an on-site restaurant.
  • Yonkers Gateway Motel: For budget-conscious travelers, Yonkers Gateway Motel is a great option. The motel offers simple rooms and free parking, as well as easy access to public transportation.

What to Buy in Yonkers: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or unique local products to bring home, Yonkers has plenty to offer. Here are some ideas:

  • Piast Bakery goods: Bring home some delicious pierogi, kielbasa, or other European bakery items from Piast Bakery.
  • Yonkers Roasters coffee: Take home some locally roasted coffee beans from Yonkers Roasters.
  • Hudson River Museum gift shop: The museum’s gift shop offers a variety of unique gifts, including jewelry, art prints, and books.

Local Culture and Customs of Yonkers

Yonkers is a diverse city with a rich cultural heritage. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, including:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious or cultural sites.
  • Respect the privacy of local residents and avoid taking photographs without permission.
  • Tip your restaurant servers 15-20% of the total bill.

Best Time to Visit Yonkers

Yonkers can be visited year-round, but the best time to visit depends on your interests. For outdoor activities and events, summer is the best season. For fall foliage and wine tasting, visit in the autumn. Winter is a great time for indoor activities and holiday events, while spring offers beautiful blooming flowers and weekend festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yonkers

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Explore the city’s cultural attractions and scenic outdoor areas.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Litter or vandalize public property.
  • Be loud or disruptive in public areas.
  • Engage in any illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Yonkers

Yonkers is a safe city for travelers, but visitors should take normal precautions to stay safe. Avoid walking alone in deserted areas at night, and keep your valuables out of sight.

Health in Yonkers

Yonkers offers several health facilities for travelers, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. Make sure to bring any necessary medications with you, and use sunscreen and insect repellent to protect against sunburn and bug bites.

Getting to Yonkers: Transportation Options and Logistics

Yonkers is easily accessible by several transportation options, including:

Airports Near Yonkers

  • Westchester County Airport: The closest airport to Yonkers, located approximately 20 miles away.
  • LaGuardia Airport: Located approximately 18 miles away.
  • John F. Kennedy International Airport: Located approximately 29 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Yonkers

  • Yonkers Bus Station: Located in the downtown area, this station serves several bus lines, including Greyhound and Trailways.
  • Broadway and Vark Street Bus Stop: This stop serves several local bus routes.

Other Options

  • Metro-North Railroad: Yonkers is served by the Metro-North Railroad, which provides easy access to New York City and other nearby destinations.
  • Taxi and Ride-Sharing Services: Several taxi and ride-sharing services operate in Yonkers.

How to Get Around in Yonkers

Yonkers offers several public transportation options, including buses and trains, as well as taxi and ride-sharing services. Visitors can also explore the city on foot or by bike.

Where to Go Next from Yonkers: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Yonkers is located in an area with several great nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some ideas:

  • New York City: Yonkers is located approximately 30 minutes away from New York City, making it a perfect day trip or overnight destination.
  • Hudson Valley Wineries: The Hudson Valley is known for its world-class wineries, with several located within a short drive of Yonkers.
  • Bear Mountain State Park: Located approximately 30 minutes away, Bear Mountain State Park offers scenic hiking trails and stunning views of the Hudson River.

Conclusion

Yonkers is a hidden gem that offers plenty of attractions and activities for visitors. Whether you’re exploring the city’s rich history, enjoying scenic outdoor areas, or indulging in the local cuisine, Yonkers has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your next visit to this vibrant city.
