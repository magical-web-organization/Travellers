Introduction to Hastings-on-Hudson

Located in Westchester County, in Downstate New York, in New York State, in Mid-Atlantic, in the United States of America, in North America, Hastings-on-Hudson is a charming village known for its natural beauty and cultural significance. It’s a small but bustling town with a population of around 8,000 residents, offering a diverse range of attractions and activities for visitors to explore. From museums, historical landmarks, and outdoor adventures to delicious food, cozy accommodations, and local products, Hastings-on-Hudson has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Hastings-on-Hudson

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hastings Historical Society: A non-profit organization dedicated to preserving and sharing the local history of Hastings-on-Hudson. The museum features exhibits, artifacts, and a research library with archives that date back to the 17th century.

  • Draper Park: Home to a collection of 19th-century buildings, including a restored barn, cottage, and schoolhouse, which offer a glimpse into the village’s past. Visitors can also enjoy a stroll through the park’s beautiful gardens and picnic areas.

  • Municipal Building Art Gallery: A public gallery space featuring rotating exhibits by local and regional artists, as well as special events and workshops.

Natural Attractions of Hastings-on-Hudson: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hastings Waterfront Walkway: A picturesque pedestrian pathway that runs along the Hudson River, offering stunning views of the Palisades and Manhattan skyline. The walkway also features public art installations and a playground for children.

  • Hillside Woods: A 167-acre nature preserve with hiking trails, bird-watching opportunities, and scenic views of the Hudson River. Visitors can also explore the historic stone walls, foundations, and ruins of old buildings that remain from the area’s agricultural past.

  • Farragut Woods Nature Preserve: A beautiful and secluded forested area with marked hiking trails, scenic overlooks, and picnic areas. The preserve is home to a variety of wildlife, including birds, deer, and foxes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hastings-on-Hudson

  • Old Croton Aqueduct Trail: A 26-mile trail that follows the path of the historic Croton Aqueduct, which supplied water to New York City in the mid-1800s. The trail runs through Hastings-on-Hudson, offering beautiful views of the Hudson River and access to other area parks and trails.

  • Bronx River Pathway: A 23-mile paved trail that follows the Bronx River from the Kensico Dam to the Bronx Zoo. The trail passes through Hastings-on-Hudson, providing opportunities for hiking, cycling, and bird-watching.

  • South County Trailway: A 14.1-mile paved trail that runs through Westchester County from Yonkers to Elmsford. The trail passes through Hastings-on-Hudson, offering scenic views of the Hudson River and connecting to other area trails and parks.

Best Things to Do in Hastings-on-Hudson: Activities and Tours

  • Hastings Farmers Market: A weekly market held on Saturdays from May to November, featuring fresh produce, artisanal products, and live music. The market is a hub for the local community and offers a unique opportunity to experience the village’s culture and flavors.

  • Waterfront Festival: An annual event held in June, featuring live music, food and drink vendors, crafts, and family-friendly activities. The festival celebrates the village’s location on the Hudson River and highlights its natural beauty and maritime history.

  • Hastings Art Walk: A self-guided tour that showcases the work of local artists and galleries throughout the village. The tour takes place in October and provides a unique opportunity to explore the village’s creative scene.

Food and Drink Scene of Hastings-on-Hudson

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hastings Prime: A local restaurant that offers a variety of delicious burgers made with locally-sourced ingredients.

  • Harvest on Hudson: A farm-to-table restaurant that specializes in Italian cuisine, using fresh ingredients and herbs from the on-site garden.

  • Saint George Bistro: A French-inspired restaurant with a cozy atmosphere and a menu that includes classic dishes like escargots and coq au vin.

Local Beverages

  • Captain Lawrence Brewing Company: A local brewery that offers a variety of handcrafted beers, including IPAs, lagers, and stouts.

  • Harvest Spirits: A distillery that produces vodka, brandy, and other spirits made with locally-grown fruit and grain.

  • Wined Up: A wine bar that offers a selection of local and international wines, as well as small plates and cheese boards.

Local Culture and Customs of Hastings-on-Hudson

Hastings-on-Hudson is a welcoming and friendly community that values its natural surroundings, cultural heritage, and creative spirit. Visitors are encouraged to explore the village’s many attractions, talk to the locals, and savor its unique flavors and experiences. However, it’s important to be respectful of the environment and follow local customs, such as recycling, preserving the natural landscape, and adhering to noise ordinances.

Best Time to Visit Hastings-on-Hudson

The best time to visit Hastings-on-Hudson is from late spring to early autumn, when the weather is mild and the village’s natural beauty is at its peak. The summer months are especially popular, thanks to the many festivals, outdoor events, and water activities taking place along the Hudson River. Fall is also a great time to visit, as the leaves turn colors and the village’s farms and wineries offer harvest festivals and other seasonal activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hastings-on-Hudson

Do:

  • Do explore the village’s natural surroundings, from the Hudson River waterfront to the Hillside Woods Nature Preserve.

  • Do try the local food and drinks, including the delicious burgers at Hastings Prime and the craft beers at Captain Lawrence Brewing Company.

  • Do attend the village’s many festivals and cultural events, such as the Hastings Farmers Market, Waterfront Festival, and Hastings Art Walk.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hastings-on-Hudson

Hastings-on-Hudson is a safe and welcoming community with low crime rates and a friendly atmosphere. However, visitors should still exercise caution and follow common sense safety measures, such as locking their car doors, avoiding unlit areas at night, and keeping an eye on their personal belongings.

Health in Hastings-on-Hudson

Hastings-on-Hudson has several medical facilities, including the Hastings-on-Hudson Health Department, which provides a variety of health services to residents and visitors. Visitors should also carry any necessary medication with them, as well as stay hydrated and protect themselves from the sun and other environmental hazards while hiking or engaging in other outdoor activities.

Getting to Hastings-on-Hudson: Transportation Options and Logistics

Hastings-on-Hudson is located just 30 minutes from New York City, making it easy to reach by car, train, or bus. Visitors can also fly into one of the nearby airports, such as LaGuardia or JFK, and take a taxi or shuttle service to the village.

Airports Near Hastings-on-Hudson

  • LaGuardia Airport: Located approximately 18 miles from Hastings-on-Hudson, LaGuardia is a popular choice for domestic travelers.

  • John F. Kennedy International Airport: Located approximately 32 miles from Hastings-on-Hudson, JFK is a hub for both domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Hastings-on-Hudson

  • Hastings-on-Hudson Train Station: Located in the center of the village, the train station offers daily commuter service to New York City and other nearby destinations via the Metro-North Railroad.

Other Options

  • Driving: Visitors can reach Hastings-on-Hudson via the Saw Mill River Parkway or the New York State Thruway.

  • Taxi/Shuttle: Visitors can take a taxi or shuttle service from the airport to Hastings-on-Hudson, such as Westchester Express Airport Shuttle or Hastings-On-Hudson Taxi.

How to Get Around in Hastings-on-Hudson

Visitors can easily get around Hastings-on-Hudson by walking, cycling, or driving. The village is small and compact, with most attractions located within walking distance of each other. Cycling is also a popular option, especially along the Old Croton Aqueduct Trail or the Bronx River Pathway. For visitors who prefer to drive, there are several parking options available, including street parking and public lots.

Where to Go Next from Hastings-on-Hudson: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tarrytown: A charming village located just 3 miles from Hastings-on-Hudson, offering a variety of shops, restaurants, and historic landmarks, including Kykuit, the Rockefeller Estate.

  • Croton-on-Hudson: A scenic village located about 13 miles from Hastings-on-Hudson, with outdoor attractions like Croton Gorge Park, which features hiking trails, picnic areas, and scenic views of the Croton Dam.

  • New York City: Just a quick train ride away, New York City offers endless possibilities for sightseeing, shopping, and entertainment, from the towering Empire State Building to the world-class museums and galleries.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, outdoor adventures, or simply relaxing and enjoying the beautiful scenery, Hastings-on-Hudson has something to offer everyone.
