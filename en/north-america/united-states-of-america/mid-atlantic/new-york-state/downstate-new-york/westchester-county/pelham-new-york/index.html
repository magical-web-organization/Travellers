Introduction to Pelham (New York)

Pelham is a small town in Westchester County, in Downstate New York. It is a sought-after destination for people looking for a quiet retreat from the bustling urban life in New York City. Pelham boasts an abundance of lush parks, awe-inspiring vistas, and a rich cultural heritage that make it an ideal spot for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and culture seekers alike.

List of Places to Visit in Pelham (New York)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Pelham Picture House

Pelham Picture House is a classic Art Deco theater that has been restored to its original grandeur. It serves as a venue for independent films, cultural events, and concerts.

Pelham Art Center

Pelham Art Center is a nonprofit organization that promotes the arts in Westchester County. The art center boasts a gallery, art classes, workshops, and a variety of community programs.

Glover’s Rock

Glover’s Rock is a famous historical landmark in Pelham. It is a huge boulder that served as a lookout point during the Revolutionary War. It has since been preserved and marked with a bronze plaque.

Natural Attractions of Pelham (New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pelham Bay Park

Pelham Bay Park is the jewel of the Bronx park system. The park boasts more than 2,700 acres of parkland, including forests, trails, and beaches. Visitors can enjoy hiking, bird watching, swimming, and fishing.

Twin Islands

Twin Islands is a natural preserve that offers stunning views of the Long Island Sound. Visitors can enjoy hiking, bird watching, and fishing.

Glen Island Park

Glen Island Park is a beautiful peninsula park that features picnic areas, hiking trails, and scenic views of the Long Island Sound. Visitors can also enjoy swimming, fishing, and boating.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pelham (New York)

Hutchinson River Greenway

Hutchinson River Greenway is a scenic trail that spans 4 miles from Pelham Bay Park to Scarsdale. Visitors can enjoy hiking, cycling, or rollerblading as they take in the beauty of the natural surroundings.

Pelham Bay Loop

Pelham Bay Loop is a 4.5-mile hike that takes visitors through the heart of Pelham Bay Park. The trail offers stunning views of the Long Island Sound and the Manhattan skyline.

Best Things to Do in Pelham (New York): Activities and Tours

Kayaking and Paddleboarding

Visitors can rent kayaks and paddleboards from the Pelham Bay Park Beach and explore the beautiful Long Island Sound.

Spooky Walk

The annual Spooky Walk is a Halloween event that takes place at the Pelham Art Center. Visitors can enjoy a guided tour of the art center, while learning about the history of Pelham.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pelham (New York)

Day 1: Spend a day exploring Pelham Bay Park, hiking or cycling Hutchinson River Greenway, and renting kayaks from the Pelham Bay Park Beach.

Day 2: Visit Pelham Picture House and enjoy a concert or independent film.

Day 3: Explore the Pelham Art Center and take an art class or workshop.

Day 4: Take a day trip to nearby City Island and enjoy fresh seafood and unique boutiques.

Day 5: Visit Glen Island Park and enjoy a picnic or a scenic boat ride on the Long Island Sound.

Day 6: Spend the day exploring the historic landmarks, including Glover’s Rock and the Thomas Pell Mansion.

Day 7: Take a guided tour of Pelham’s wine country and go wine tasting.

Food and Drink Scene of Pelham (New York)

Must-Try Dishes

Clam Chowder

Pelham is known for its clam chowder, which is made with locally caught clams.

Thin Crust Pizza

Pelham’s thin crust pizza is a local delicacy. It is made with fresh ingredients and cooked to perfection.

Local Beverages

Mulled Wine

Mulled wine is a popular drink in Pelham during the winter months.

Local Brews

Visitors can enjoy a variety of local beers from Pelham’s breweries and gastropubs.

Where to Stay in Pelham (New York): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pelham Lodge

Pelham Lodge is a beautifully restored Victorian-style guesthouse that offers comfortable accommodations for visitors.

New York Marriott East Side

New York Marriott East Side is a luxury hotel in the heart of Manhattan. It offers easy access to Pelham and other nearby attractions.

What to Buy in Pelham (New York): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Wines

Visitors can bring home a bottle of Pelham’s famous wines as a souvenir.

Artisanal Goods

Pelham is home to many artisans who sell handmade goods, including pottery, jewelry, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Pelham (New York)

Pelham is a welcoming community that values its history and heritage. Visitors are encouraged to engage with the locals and learn about the town’s cultural traditions.

Best Time to Visit Pelham (New York)

The best time to visit Pelham is during the spring and fall seasons, when the weather is mild and the foliage is at its peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pelham (New York)

Do:

  • Explore Pelham Bay Park and its many hiking trails and scenic vistas.
  • Try the local cuisine, including clam chowder and thin crust pizza.
  • Engage with the locals and learn about the town’s rich cultural heritage.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural habitats within Pelham Bay Park.
  • Trespass on private property or disturb historical landmarks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pelham (New York)

Pelham is a safe and welcoming community. Visitors should exercise the same caution they would in any other urban area.

Health in Pelham (New York)

Pelham has several medical clinics and hospitals nearby. Visitors should ensure they have adequate health insurance coverage during their stay.

Getting to Pelham (New York): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pelham (New York)

John F. Kennedy International Airport and LaGuardia Airport are both located within a 30-minute drive of Pelham.

Bus Stations Near Pelham (New York)

Greyhound and other bus companies serve the nearby towns of New Rochelle and Mount Vernon.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Pelham by taxi or rental car.

How to Get Around in Pelham (New York)

Visitors can rent a car or use public transportation to explore the town and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Pelham (New York): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

City Island

City Island is a small fishing village located less than 10 miles from Pelham. Visitors can enjoy fresh seafood, local boutiques, and scenic vistas.

Tarrytown

Tarrytown is a charming village located along the Hudson River. It is home to several historic landmarks, including the Sleepy Hollow Cemetery and the Lyndhurst Mansion.

Conclusion

Pelham is a gem of a town, with something for everyone to enjoy. Whether you’re a sports enthusiast, culture seeker, or foodie, Pelham has it all. We hope this guide has given you a better idea of what to expect during your visit to Pelham.
