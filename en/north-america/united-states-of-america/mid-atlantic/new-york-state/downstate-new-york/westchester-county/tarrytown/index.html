Introduction to Tarrytown

Located in Westchester County, New York, Tarrytown is a charming and historic village set along the Hudson River. Known for its connections to Washington Irving’s timeless classic, Sleepy Hollow, Tarrytown offers a variety of attractions and activities for visitors to enjoy. From outdoor adventures and historical sites to delicious dining options and places to stay, there’s something for everyone in Tarrytown.

List of Places to Visit in Tarrytown

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lyndhurst Mansion: This elegant Gothic Revival Mansion belonged to railroad magnate Jay Gould in the late 1800s. It features art, antiques, and beautiful gardens, as well as guided tours that provide insight into the wealth and opulence of the Gilded Age.
  • Philipsburg Manor: A well-preserved colonial estate, visitors can take a guided tour, participate in hands-on agricultural activities, or attend historical reenactments.
  • Washington Irving’s Sunnyside: Take a tour of the home of Washington Irving, author of “The Legend of Sleepy Hollow.” This charming cottage is set on beautiful grounds with views of the Hudson River.

Natural Attractions of Tarrytown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hudson Riverwalk Park: A scenic pathway along the Hudson River, perfect for taking a stroll or biking. Enjoy views of the water and the Tappan Zee Bridge.
  • Rockefeller State Park Preserve: A 1,400-acre nature preserve with miles of hiking trails, woodlands, and streams.
  • Tarrytown Lakes Park: Offering fishing, boating, and picnics, this park is a favorite among locals.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tarrytown

  • Old Croton Aqueduct Trail: A 26-mile trail that traces the route of a 19th-century aqueduct, offering views of the river and quaint towns along the way.
  • South County Trailway: A 14-mile bike trail that runs from Yonkers to Elmsford, passing through parks, nature preserves, and historic landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Tarrytown: Activities and Tours

  • Sleepy Hollow Cemetery: Take a guided tour of this historic cemetery, final resting place of Washington Irving and other famous authors, politicians, and soldiers.
  • Music at Lyndhurst: Enjoy live music and entertainment on the beautiful grounds of Lyndhurst Mansion, with views of the Hudson River.
  • Horseman’s Hollow: A spooky haunted attraction that takes visitors through the woods of Sleepy Hollow, with special effects and live actors.

Where to Stay in Tarrytown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Castle Hotel & Spa: Luxury accommodations in a historic castle with a spa and fine dining.
  • Tarrytown House Estate: A charming estate with multiple buildings surrounded by beautiful gardens.
  • Sheraton Tarrytown Hotel: Contemporary hotel with comfortable rooms and a convenient location.

What to Buy in Tarrytown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hudson Valley wine: Tarry Wine Merchants offers a wide selection of local wines.
  • Antiques: Explore the shops and auction houses along Main Street.
  • Artwork: Tarrytown is home to many local artists and galleries.

Local Culture and Customs of Tarrytown

Tarrytown has a rich history, including its connection to the Revolutionary War, Washington Irving, and the Gilded Age. Visitors are encouraged to explore the local museums and landmarks to learn more about the village’s past. Additionally, Tarrytown has a strong arts community, with many galleries and live performances throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Tarrytown

The best time to visit Tarrytown is during the fall, when the leaves change colors and the village celebrates Halloween with events like Horseman’s Hollow and the Sleepy Hollow Cemetery Tours. Spring and summer offer plenty of outdoor activities, while the winter months offer holiday cheer and cozy fireside dining.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tarrytown

Do:

  • Explore the historic landmarks and cultural attractions.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities and scenic views.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the village’s rich history and culture.
  • Miss out on the village’s seasonal events and festivals.
  • Forget to support local businesses and artists.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tarrytown

Tarrytown is generally a safe and friendly village. Visitors should take standard safety precautions, like locking their cars and securing valuables. The village has a police department and emergency services available.

Health in Tarrytown

Visitors should bring any necessary prescription medications with them. Tarrytown has several medical facilities in the area, including a hospital and medical centers.

Getting to Tarrytown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tarrytown

  • Westchester County Airport (HPN): Located about 20 minutes away, serves domestic flights and connects to major hubs such as Atlanta, Chicago, and Miami.
  • LaGuardia Airport (LGA): Located about 40 minutes away, serves domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Tarrytown

  • Tarrytown Metro North Station: Serves the Hudson Line, connecting to New York City and other destinations.
  • Bee-Line Bus System: Operates multiple bus routes throughout Westchester County.

Other Options

  • By Car: Tarrytown is easily accessible by car via major highways such as the New York State Thruway (I-87) and the Saw Mill River Parkway.
  • By Train: The Metro-North Hudson Line stops at the Tarrytown station, with trains running regularly to New York City and other destinations.

How to Get Around in Tarrytown

Tarrytown is a small village that is easy to explore on foot or by bike. However, for those who prefer not to walk, there are several taxi services and car rental agencies available.

Where to Go Next from Tarrytown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • The City of Hudson: A charming town about an hour and a half north, with great shopping, dining, and art galleries.
  • Storm King Art Center: A world-renowned sculpture park about an hour’s drive away, with over 100 works of art on a 500-acre property.
  • Bear Mountain State Park: A scenic park with hiking trails, a zoo, and stunning views of the Hudson River, just 20 minutes away.

Conclusion

With its rich history, natural beauty, and vibrant culture, Tarrytown offers visitors a unique and memorable experience. From hiking trails to cultural landmarks, this charming village has something for everyone. Plan a visit to Tarrytown today and discover its hidden gems.
