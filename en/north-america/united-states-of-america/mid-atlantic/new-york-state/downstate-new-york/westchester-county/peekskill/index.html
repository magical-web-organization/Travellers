Introduction to Peekskill

Nestled in the heart of Westchester County, Peekskill is a charming and vibrant city in Downstate New York. Known for its rich history, scenic views, and vibrant arts scene, Peekskill is a popular destination for travelers looking to explore the best of New York State beyond the hustle and bustle of New York City.

List of Places to Visit in Peekskill

If you’re planning a trip to Peekskill, be sure to check out these must-visit attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hudson Valley Center for Contemporary Art: If you’re an art lover, be sure to visit this contemporary art museum featuring works by renowned artists.
  • Peekskill Museum: Explore the history of Peekskill and learn about the people, events, and landmarks that shaped the city’s past.
  • The Lincoln Depot Museum: Discover the important role that Peekskill played in the Civil War and the history of Abraham Lincoln’s journey to his inauguration.

Natural Attractions of Peekskill: Outdoor Adventures & Scenic Views

  • Blue Mountain Reservation: This 1,538-acre park offers miles of hiking and biking trails, stunning views of the Hudson River, and opportunities to fish and picnic.
  • Hendrick Hudson Park and Beach: With 20 acres of land and a sandy beach on the Hudson River, this park is perfect for swimming, fishing, and catching scenic views.
  • Fort Hill Park: Explore this scenic park perched atop a hill overlooking the Hudson River and enjoy the stunning panorama of the valley below.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Peekskill

For outdoor enthusiasts, Peekskill offers miles of scenic trails to explore on foot or bike. Here are some popular routes to check out:

  • Old Croton Aqueduct Trail: This historic trail stretches 26 miles from Croton-on-Hudson to Yonkers and passes through the heart of Peekskill.
  • Blue Mountain Reservation Trail: Hikers and bikers can enjoy scenic views and challenging terrain on the many trails in this popular park.
  • Bear Mountain Loop Trail: Offering stunning views of the Hudson River valley, this 4.5-mile loop trail is perfect for moderate to advanced hikers.

Best Things to Do in Peekskill: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and exploring the city’s cultural and natural attractions, there are plenty of other fun activities to try in Peekskill:

  • Hudson River Cruises: Take a scenic boat tour of the Hudson River and enjoy stunning views of the valley and historic landmarks.
  • Bird Watching: Peekskill is home to a diverse array of bird species, including herons, eagles, and osprey, making it a popular destination for birdwatchers.
  • Craft Beer Tasting: The city is home to several craft breweries and taprooms where visitors can sample local brews and enjoy tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Peekskill

Day 1: Check into your hotel and explore the city’s downtown area, featuring eclectic shops, galleries, and restaurants.

Day 2: Take a hike or bike ride in Blue Mountain Reservation and enjoy a picnic lunch in the park.

Day 3: Explore the city’s cultural attractions, including the Hudson Valley Center for Contemporary Art and the Peekskill Museum.

Day 4: Visit the Lincoln Depot Museum and learn about the city’s role in the Civil War.

Day 5: Take a Hudson River Cruise and enjoy scenic views of the valley and its historic landmarks.

Day 6: Spend the day at Hendrick Hudson Park and Beach, swim and sunbathe on the sandy beach, or picnic in the park.

Day 7: Enjoy some local brews and a craft beer tasting at one of Peekskill’s several breweries or taprooms.

Food and Drink Scene of Peekskill

Peekskill boasts a thriving food and drink scene, offering visitors a wide range of cuisines and dining experiences. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Peekskill Brewery: Baja Fish Tacos: Enjoy crispy fish, pickled slaw, and spicy crema in a soft tortilla at this popular brewery.
  • Iron Vine: Lamb Chops: Savor tender lamb chops with chimichurri sauce at this Mediterranean restaurant.
  • The Peekskill Coffee House: Avocado Toast: Start your day with a delicious and healthy breakfast at this cozy cafe.

Local Beverages

  • Peekskill Brewery: Eastern Standard IPA: Enjoy this hoppy and refreshing IPA at Peekskill Brewery.
  • River Outpost Brewing Company: Cold Spring Kolsch: This light and crisp beer is perfect for hot summer days.
  • Dylan’s Wine Cellar: Local Wine: Sample some of the local wines at Dylan’s Wine Cellar and discover the unique flavors of Peekskill.

Where to Stay in Peekskill: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Peekskill offers a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some popular choices:

  • The Abbey Inn and Spa: This luxury hotel features a full-service spa and restaurant, with scenic views of the Hudson River.
  • The Hudson Valley Hotel and Conference Center by Fairbridge: This budget-friendly hotel offers comfortable rooms and an outdoor pool.
  • The Chapel Restoration Guest House: Located in a historic chapel, this charming guest house offers cozy rooms and a peaceful atmosphere.

What to Buy in Peekskill: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring home, be sure to check out these shops and markets:

  • Peekskill Farmers Market: Featuring fresh produce, baked goods, and artisanal products, this market is held every Saturday from June to November.
  • The Gift Guide: This eclectic gift shop offers a wide range of locally made and fair trade products, including jewelry, candles, and home decor.
  • Painted Pieces Artisan Market: Browse a unique selection of vintage and handmade goods, including furniture, pottery, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Peekskill

Peekskill is a diverse and welcoming community, with a rich history and vibrant arts scene. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions and be open to experiencing the city’s unique culture.

Best Time to Visit Peekskill

The best time to visit Peekskill is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and the foliage is at its most beautiful. Summer is also a popular time to visit, but be prepared for higher temperatures and more crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Peekskill

Do:

  • Do explore the city’s cultural attractions, natural beauty, and vibrant food and drink scene.
  • Do be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Do try the local craft beer and wine.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage local parks and natural areas.
  • Don’t be loud or disruptive in residential neighborhoods.
  • Don’t ignore traffic laws or engage in risky behavior on hiking or biking trails.

Safety and Health

Safety in Peekskill

Peekskill is a safe and welcoming city, but visitors should take basic safety precautions, including locking car doors and securing valuables.

Health in Peekskill

Peekskill has several hospitals and clinics to provide medical support to visitors in case of emergency.

Getting to Peekskill: Transportation Options and Logistics

Peekskill is easily accessible via several transportation options, including:

  • Car: Peekskill is conveniently located near several major highways and is approximately one hour from New York City.
  • Train: Metro-North trains run regularly from Grand Central Terminal in New York City to Peekskill Station.
  • Bus: Several bus companies, including Greyhound and Trailways, operate routes to Peekskill from New York City and other parts of New York and nearby states.

Airports Near Peekskill

The closest major airports to Peekskill are:

  • Westchester County Airport: Approximately 30 minutes away by car.
  • LaGuardia Airport: Approximately 1 hour and 15 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Peekskill

Several bus stations serve the Peekskill area, including:

  • Peekskill Terminal: Serves local bus routes within Peekskill.
  • Trailways Bus Station: Located in nearby Cortlandt Manor, offers bus routes to New York City, Albany, and other destinations.

Other Options

  • Taxi service: Several local taxi companies serve the Peekskill area.
  • Rideshare services: Uber and Lyft operate in Peekskill and the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Peekskill

Peekskill is a walkable city, but visitors may want to use public transportation or rent a car to explore the surrounding area. Buses and taxis serve the city, and bike rentals are available from several local shops.

Where to Go Next from Peekskill: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of the region, consider these nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Cold Spring Village: Located just downriver from Peekskill, this charming village is known for its antique shops, cafes, and historic landmarks.
  • Bear Mountain State Park: This popular state park offers hiking, boating, and swimming, as well as a zoo and scenic views of the Hudson River.
  • Catskill Mountains: Just a few hours from Peekskill, the Catskill Mountains offer stunning views, outdoor adventures, and charming towns to explore.

Conclusion

Peekskill is a charming and vibrant city with plenty to offer visitors, from cultural landmarks and natural beauty to a thriving food and drink scene. Whether you’re looking to hike, bike, or explore the city’s history and arts scene, Peekskill is a destination worth visiting any time of year.
