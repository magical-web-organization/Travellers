Introduction to Islip

Located in Suffolk County, on Long Island, Islip is a charming town known for its beautiful beaches, fascinating history, and vibrant culture. With its picturesque downtown area, wonderful local attractions, and easy access to nearby landmarks, Islip is the perfect destination for a weekend getaway or an extended vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Islip

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in history and culture, don’t miss these museums and historical sites in Islip:

  • Islip Art Museum: Dedicated to showcasing contemporary art, the Islip Art Museum features bold, innovative exhibitions and a variety of arts-related programs throughout the year.
  • Islip Grange: Built in the late 18th century, the Islip Grange is a beautifully restored historic home that offers a glimpse into life in early America.
  • Seatuck Environmental Association: Dedicated to the preservation of Long Island’s natural ecosystems, Seatuck Environmental Association offers a variety of educational programs, workshops, and nature walks for visitors of all ages.

Natural Attractions of Islip: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Islip is home to some of Long Island’s most spectacular natural scenery, including:

  • Heckscher State Park: This beautiful state park features trails through wooded areas and along the scenic shoreline of the Great South Bay.
  • Bayard Cutting Arboretum: With its acres of rolling lawns, nature trails and gardens, the Bayard Cutting Arboretum is a must-see for nature lovers.
  • Roberto Clemente Park: This picturesque park features a swimming pool, baseball field, and playground, as well as a walking trail that winds through the woods.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Islip

If you enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking and biking, Islip has plenty of scenic routes to explore. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in Islip include:

  • Connetquot River State Park: This beautiful park spans over 3,400 acres and features miles of hiking and horseback riding trails that wind through the woods alongside the park’s namesake river.
  • Belmont Lake State Park: A popular destination for both hiking and biking, Belmont Lake State Park features a 9-mile bike path that winds through woodland and along the lake’s edge.
  • Roberto Clemente Park: The walking trail at Roberto Clemente Park is also a great place to go for a scenic bike ride.

Best Things to Do in Islip: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other fun things to do in Islip, including:

  • Long Island Aquarium: One of the most popular attractions in Islip, the Long Island Aquarium is home to a wide variety of marine animals and features several interactive exhibits and daily shows.
  • Holbrook Country Club: If you’re a golf enthusiast, be sure to check out the Holbrook Country Club, one of the premier golf destinations on Long Island.
  • The Islip Lanes: This classic bowling alley offers a fun and nostalgic way to spend an afternoon or evening with friends and family.

Week-Long Itinerary for Islip

Day 1: Visit the Islip Art Museum and take a hike through Heckscher State Park.

Day 2: Explore historic downtown Islip and spend the afternoon at Roberto Clemente Park.

Day 3: Take a day trip to Fire Island and spend some time on the beach.

Day 4: Tour the Bayard Cutting Arboretum and play a round at the Holbrook Country Club.

Day 5: Check out the Long Island Aquarium and do some shopping at the local boutiques in town.

Day 6: Visit Seatuck Environmental Association and take a hike through Connetquot River State Park.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the beautiful scenery by taking a leisurely bike ride through Belmont Lake State Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Islip

Must-Try Dishes

When it comes to food, Islip has plenty of delicious options to offer. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Lobster Roll: A classic Long Island specialty, the lobster roll is a must-try for any seafood lover.
  • Bagels: Long Island is famous for its bagels, and Islip is no exception. Be sure to try one (or several) during your visit.
  • Italian Deli Sandwiches: With a large Italian-American population, Islip has some of the best Italian delis in the area.

Local Beverages

Long Island is known for its great vineyards and craft breweries, and Islip is no exception. Some local beverages to try in Islip include:

  • Long Island Wine: With over 40 wineries on Long Island, there are plenty of options to choose from when it comes to locally produced wine.
  • South Shore Brewing Company: A great local brewery with a variety of unique brews on tap.

Where to Stay in Islip: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Choosing where to stay in Islip depends on your budget, travel style, and preferences. Some good options include:

  • The Veranda House Bed & Breakfast: This charming bed and breakfast is a great choice for travelers who want a more personal, homey experience during their stay.
  • Hilton Garden Inn Islip/MacArthur Airport: Business travelers and families will appreciate the modern amenities and convenient location of this Hilton property.
  • Holiday Inn Express Hauppauge - Long Island: With its cozy rooms and free breakfast, this affordable hotel is a great choice for budget-conscious travelers.

What to Buy in Islip: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When shopping in Islip, be sure to check out some of the local boutiques and specialty shops for unique items and souvenirs, such as:

  • Islip Art Museum Gift Shop: Pick up a piece of local artwork or a book on contemporary art at the museum’s gift shop.
  • The Crushed Olive: This gourmet olive oil and vinegar shop is a great place to find unique culinary gifts and souvenirs.
  • The Vintage Vibe: With a large selection of vintage and antique items, The Vintage Vibe is a must-visit for anyone looking for a unique home decor piece or gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Islip

Islip has a rich history and culture that reflects its unique Long Island location and New England heritage. Some key local customs and traditions to be aware of include:

  • Seaside Culture: Islip has a deep connection to the ocean and beach culture, with many activities, events, and traditions centered around the shoreline.
  • Fishing and Boating: With its proximity to the Great South Bay, Islip has a large fishing and boating community.
  • Italian-American Heritage: With a large Italian-American population, Islip has a strong culinary and cultural heritage that is evident in its cuisine and festivals.

Best Time to Visit Islip

The best time to visit Islip is during the warmer months, from May to October, when outdoor activities and beach trips are at their peak. However, if you’re looking for a quieter, less crowded experience, consider visiting in the off-season, from November to April.

Do’s and Don’ts of Islip

Do:

  • Do try the seafood and Italian cuisine.
  • Do visit the local museums and parks.
  • Do spend time on the beach.

Don’t:

  • Don’t disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t expect a bustling nightlife scene in Islip.

Safety and Health

Safety in Islip

Islip is generally a safe destination for travelers, but as with any town or city, it’s important to take reasonable precautions to stay safe, such as:

  • Staying aware of your surroundings, especially in unfamiliar areas.
  • Keeping your valuables secure.
  • Following traffic laws and using caution when driving or biking on busy roads.

Health in Islip

There are several medical facilities in Islip, including:

  • Southside Hospital: A full-service hospital that offers emergency and urgent care, maternity services, surgical services, and more.
  • Islip Health Center: A primary care center that offers a range of services, including preventative care, diagnostic services, and chronic disease management.

Getting to Islip: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Islip

The main airport near Islip is the Long Island MacArthur Airport (ISP), which is located just minutes from the town. Other nearby airports include:

  • John F. Kennedy International Airport (JFK): Approximately 50 miles from Islip
  • LaGuardia Airport (LGA): Approximately 40 miles from Islip

Bus Stations Near Islip

The closest bus station to Islip is the Ronkonkoma Train Station, which is located just a few miles outside of town. Several bus lines connect to the Ronkonkoma station, including:

  • Greyhound
  • Peter Pan

Other Options

Driving is also a viable option for getting to Islip, as the town is easily accessible via major highways such as the Long Island Expressway and Southern State Parkway.

How to Get Around in Islip

Getting around Islip is easy, with a variety of transportation options available, including:

  • Driving: Islip is easy to navigate by car, and there is ample parking available in the downtown area.
  • Public Transportation: The Long Island Railroad provides train service to Islip from New York City, and there are several bus lines that serve the area as well.
  • Taxis and Ride-Sharing Services: Taxi and ride-sharing services are available throughout the town.

Where to Go Next from Islip: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore other nearby destinations and excursions, some good options include:

  • Montauk: Located at the easternmost tip of Long Island, Montauk is a beautiful seaside town known for its lighthouse, beaches, and charming downtown area.
  • The Hamptons: One of the most iconic resort areas on the East Coast, the Hamptons offer stunning beaches, high-end shopping, and a variety of other attractions.
  • New York City: Just a short train ride away, New York City offers endless cultural, culinary, and recreational possibilities.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking for a quiet getaway in nature or a fun-filled weekend exploring the local culture and attractions, Islip has something for everyone. With its beautiful beaches, historic landmarks, and vibrant downtown area, Islip is the perfect destination for your next trip to Long Island.
