Introduction to Northport (New York)

Located in Suffolk County, Long Island, Northport offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, history, and culture. This charming village is known for its picturesque harbor, quaint shops and cafes, and friendly community. Whether you are looking for outdoor adventures or cultural immersion, Northport has something to offer every kind of traveler. In this comprehensive guide, we will explore the best of Northport, from its cultural attractions and hiking routes to its vibrant food scene and unique souvenirs.

List of Places to Visit in Northport (New York)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. John W. Engeman Theater

Located on Main Street, the John W. Engeman Theater is a popular venue year-round for live performances, including Broadway productions, concerts, and comedy shows. With its elegant decor and state-of-the-art facilities, this theater promises a memorable night out.

2. Northport Historical Society and Museum

The Northport Historical Society and Museum provides a glimpse into the village’s rich history. Exhibits include colonial times to the 21st century, with a focus on the maritime history that has shaped this community since its founding.

Natural Attractions of Northport (New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Crab Meadow Beach

Crab Meadow Beach is the perfect spot to enjoy a peaceful day in the sun. With soft sand and gentle waves, this beach is great for swimming, sunbathing, and picnicking. Don’t forget to catch the stunning sunset view over the Long Island Sound.

2. Makamah Beach Nature Preserve

Makamah Beach Nature Preserve features a variety of habitats including wetlands, woodlands, meadows, and dunes. It is a fantastic place for birdwatching, hiking, or just strolling along the beach.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northport (New York)

1. Northport Village Trail

This scenic hiking trail stretches along the coastline and offers stunning views of the harbor and the town. With several entry points throughout the village, this trail is an excellent choice for beginners or those looking for a leisurely walk.

2. Caumsett State Historic Park Preserve

This 1,400-acre state park is a cyclist’s paradise, with several biking trails to choose from, each with varying difficulty levels. The park offers picturesque views of the Long Island Sound, an equestrian center, and several fishing spots.

Best Things to Do in Northport (New York): Activities and Tours

1. Long Island Maritime Museum

The Long Island Maritime Museum offers visitors a unique understanding of the area’s rich maritime history. The museum features exhibits, educational programs, and demonstrations, including wooden boatbuilding and historic sailboat restoration.

2. Northport Tasting Room and Wine Cellar

The Northport Tasting Room and Wine Cellar is a must-visit spot for wine enthusiasts. With hundreds of wines to choose from, this cozy vineyard offers tastings, charcuterie boards, and live music events.

Week-Long Itinerary for Northport (New York)

Day 1: Explore the picturesque Northport Harbor, indulge in local seafood at Skipper’s Restaurant, and catch a performance at the John W. Engeman Theater.

Day 2: Take a stroll along the Northport Village Trail and discover hidden beaches and scenic views.

Day 3: Visit the Makamah Beach Nature Preserve and go birdwatching or hiking. In the evening, relax over a glass of wine and a cheese platter at the Northport Tasting Room and Wine Cellar.

Day 4: Take a short drive to the Caumsett State Historic Park Preserve, where you can cycle along the scenic trails.

Day 5: Learn more about Northport’s history at the Northport Historical Society and Museum. In the afternoon, visit the Long Island Maritime Museum.

Day 6: Take a ferry ride to Fire Island and enjoy the sandy beaches, nature trails, and wildlife.

Day 7: Shop for unique souvenirs at the Northport Farmers Market and end your visit with a gorgeous sunset at Crab Meadow Beach.

Food and Drink Scene of Northport (New York)

Must-Try Dishes

1. Lobster Roll

Northport is famous for its superb seafood, especially its mouth-watering lobster rolls. Some of the best places to try this delicacy include Skipper’s Restaurant and the Whales Tale.

2. Pizzeria Uno Chicago Bar and Grill

For a more casual dining experience, head to Pizzeria Uno Chicago Bar and Grill. This family-friendly restaurant is known for its deep-dish pizzas and salads.

Local Beverages

1. Northport Brewing Company

Northport Brewing Company is a microbrewery that offers a variety of craft beers, from citrusy IPAs to rich stouts. Their taproom is a perfect place to sample their creations while enjoying live music and local food trucks.

2. Sand City Brewing Co.

Whether you are a beer connoisseur or a casual drinker, Sand City Brewing Co. has something for everyone. This brewery’s signature beers include hoppy IPAs and creamy porters.

Where to Stay in Northport (New York): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

1. Harbor Lights Inn

Overlooking the Northport Harbor, the Harbor Lights Inn is a boutique hotel that promises a delightful stay. Its beautifully decorated rooms, panoramic water views, and charming porches make it an ideal choice for couples and families.

2. The John Milton Inn

The John Milton Inn is another stylish hotel that offers spacious rooms, a beautiful garden, and a complimentary breakfast. Located just minutes away from the vibrant downtown, it is the perfect spot for exploring Northport’s shops and restaurants.

What to Buy in Northport (New York): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Northport Farmers Market

The Northport Farmers Market is open every Saturday morning from 8 AM to 12 PM. Offering a range of locally-sourced produce, flowers, and artisanal goods, this market is a great place to pick up unique souvenirs and gifts.

2. Rocking Horse Ranch

Rocking Horse Ranch is a local shop that specializes in handmade wooden toys and gifts. With a variety of unique designs, this store offers a whimsical touch to any home.

Local Culture and Customs of Northport (New York)

Northport is known for its relaxed, friendly community. As with any small town, locals appreciate visitors who show respect for the town’s history, customs, and natural resources.

Best Time to Visit Northport (New York)

The best time to visit Northport is during the summer months (June - August) when the weather is warm and sunny. Fall (September - November) is also a great season to explore the village’s natural beauty, with the changing leaves casting a gorgeous hue over the harbor.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northport (New York)

Do:

  • Try local seafood, especially the lobster rolls.
  • Shop at the Northport Farmers Market for unique souvenirs and gifts.
  • Take a stroll along the Northport Village Trail and enjoy the sweeping views of the harbor.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash on the beaches.
  • Trespass on private property or restricted areas.
  • Ignore the safety rules while swimming or fishing.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northport (New York)

Northport is generally a safe place for visitors, but it’s always good to take basic safety precautions. Keep an eye on your belongings in public areas, and beware of unexpected weather changes while hiking or exploring outdoors.

Health in Northport (New York)

There are several medical facilities in and around Northport, including Northport VA Medical Center, St. Catherine of Siena Medical Center, and Huntington Hospital. It is always a good idea to carry personal health insurance and handy medication while travelling.

Getting to Northport (New York): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Northport (New York)

The closest airport to Northport is Long Island MacArthur Airport, which is located in Ronkonkoma, about 20 miles away. JFK Airport in Queens and LaGuardia Airport in New York City are also nearby and offer additional transportation options.

Bus Stations Near Northport (New York)

Several bus companies operate in and out of Northport, including Suffolk County Transit and Greyhound Bus Lines. The Northport Train Station located on Northport and Woodbine avenue also offers commuter trains to Manhattan and other Long Island towns.

Other Options

Taxis and car rentals are readily available in and around Northport. Ride-sharing apps like Uber and Lyft are also convenient ways to get around town.

How to Get Around in Northport (New York)

Northport is a small village that can easily be explored on foot. Alternatively, bicycles are available for rent in the village. There are also several car rental agencies in the nearby towns for those who prefer to drive.

Where to Go Next from Northport (New York): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

1. Montauk Point State Park

A two-hour drive from Northport, Montauk Point State Park offers stunning ocean views, rocky cliffs, and lighthouses. There are several hiking and biking trails to choose from, as well as surf spots and campsites.

2. Fire Island

A short ferry ride from Northport, Fire Island is a barrier island that offers miles of unspoiled beaches, charming villages, and wildlife sightings. Activities include hiking, swimming, kayaking, and birdwatching.

Conclusion

Northport is a charming village that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and cultural attractions. Whether you are looking for outdoor adventures or cultural immersion, this vibrant community welcomes visitors with open arms. We hope this guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan your unforgettable trip to Northport.
