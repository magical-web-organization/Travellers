Introduction to Brookhaven (town, New York)

Brookhaven is a town located in Suffolk County in Long Island in Downstate New York. It is a part of the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States of America and is situated in North America. The town is known for its natural beauty and outdoor activities that attract tourists from around the world.

List of Places to Visit in Brookhaven (town, New York)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Long Island Museum – a museum featuring art, history, and carriages
  • Brookhaven National Laboratory – a scientific research center
  • Cedar Beach County Park – a park known for its beaches and fishing pier
  • William Floyd Estate – a national park featuring a historic house and nature trail
  • The Davis Town Meeting House – a historic building from the 1700s
  • Nation Art Museum of Sports – a museum dedicated to the art of sports

Natural Attractions of Brookhaven (town, New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Fire Island National Seashore – a 26-mile long barrier island
  • Wertheim National Wildlife Refuge – a nature preserve with trails for wildlife viewing
  • Southaven County Park – a scenic park with nature trails and camping sites
  • Avalon Park and Preserve – a nature preserve with walking and hiking trails
  • Swan River Preserve – a wetland nature preserve with trails for bird watching

Hiking/Biking Routes of Brookhaven (town, New York)

Brookhaven is a great place for hiking and biking. Some of the popular trails include:

  • Greenbelt Trail – a 32-mile trail that runs through Suffolk County
  • Blydenburgh County Park Trail – a trail that takes you through wetlands
  • Swan River Preserve Trail – a trail that runs through a wetland nature preserve
  • Pine Barrens Trail – a trail that takes you through a unique ecosystem

Best Things to Do in Brookhaven (town, New York): Activities and Tours

  • Fishing – Brookhaven is home to many lakes, ponds, and waterways that are perfect for fishing
  • Kayaking and canoeing – the many bays and waterways in Brookhaven offer amazing kayaking and canoeing opportunities
  • Golfing – there are many golf courses in and around Brookhaven
  • Wine Tours – Long Island is known for its wineries, and Brookhaven offers many wine tours
  • Whale Watching – you can go whale watching in the Atlantic Ocean off the shores of Brookhaven
  • Horseback Riding – several stables in Brookhaven offer horseback riding tours

Week-Long Itinerary for Brookhaven (town, New York)

Day 1: Visit the Long Island Museum, stroll along the Stony Brook Harbor and eat at the local restaurants.

Day 2: Explore the nature trails at William Floyd Estate National Park and have a picnic there. In the evening attend a show at Gallery North.

Day 3:Spend a day at Cedar Beach County Park, enjoy fishing, and have a picnic.

Day 4: Visit Fire Island National Seashore, swim, sunbathe and relax.

Day 5: Spend a day in Avalon Park and Preserve, hike, ride bikes, and end the day with a sunset picnic.

Day 6: Visit one of the wineries on the North Fork of Long Island.

Day 7: Take a whale watching tour and go fishing in the ocean.

Food and Drink Scene of Brookhaven (town, New York)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster roll – it’s a popular dish served at most seaside restaurants
  • Clams on the half-shell – Brookhaven is famous for its fresh seafood
  • Bagel and Lox – it’s a delicious breakfast served at many cafes and delis
  • Long Island Wine – the regional wine is exceptional and deserves a try

Local Beverages

  • Wine – Long Island’s wine is popular, especially their red blends and Chardonnay.
  • Beer – many craft breweries have emerged on Long Island that deserves a taste, particularly the Port Jeff Brewing Company and Moustache Brewing Company.
  • Cocktails – try a new cocktail at Toast in Patchogue or visit the Blue Point Brewery Tasting Room for a summer beer sample.

Where to Stay in Brookhaven (town, New York): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Danford’s Hotel & Marina - a hotel located in nearby Port Jefferson offering waterfront views.
  • Hilton Garden Inn Stony Brook - located close to museums and other attractions.
  • Hyatt Regency Long Island – located on a golf course and offers outdoor activities
  • The Inn & Spa at East Wind – this hotel features a spa and many activities like wine-tasting and golfing
  • Patchogue Village Inn – an 1800s house converted into a hotel in the heart of Patchogue’s bustling nightlife.

What to Buy in Brookhaven (town, New York): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Long Island Wine - take home a bottle or two of wine from one of the wineries located in the region.
  • Sea Glass Jewelry – a wide variety of skilled jewelry makers in the Brookhaven area make stunning pieces made of colorful sea glass.

Local Culture and Customs of Brookhaven (town, New York)

Brookhaven is a culturally diverse region, so cultural customs vary. When visiting, it’s best to take time to learn about the local customs and culture of the region. Brookhaven is a friendly, welcoming place; visitors must behave respectfully and follow the local customs established in the area.

Best Time to Visit Brookhaven (town, New York)

The best time to visit Brookhaven is from June to September, as it’s warm with plenty of sunshine. Spring and fall are also good times to visit if you want to avoid crowds. Winter is cold, but there are also winter activities and festivals to be held at that time of year.

  • Take time to enjoy the natural beauty of the region
  • Visit the museums and historical sites to learn about the area’s rich and varied history
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Ignore rules and regulations of the parks and protected areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Brookhaven (town, New York)

Brookhaven is generally a safe place to visit, with low crime rates, but like most places, visitors should use common sense. It’s best to avoid going out alone at night and keep valuables hidden when in public places.

Health in Brookhaven (town, New York)

Bugs and mosquitoes are a common problem in Brookhaven, so travelers should bring insect repellent. Tap water is safe to drink in Brookhaven, but the overuse of antibiotics has led to medication-resistant diseases, so take appropriate precautions while being in the region.
