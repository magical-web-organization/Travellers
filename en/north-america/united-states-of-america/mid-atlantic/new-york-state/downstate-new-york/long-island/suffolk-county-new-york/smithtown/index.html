Introduction to Smithtown

Nestled in Suffolk County, Long Island, Smithtown is a charming and historic town located in New York (State), Mid-Atlantic region, United States, North America. Known for its scenic beauty, rich history, and vibrant cultural scene, Smithtown has a lot to offer its visitors.

Whether you’re looking for natural beauty, outdoor adventure, or cultural attractions, Smithtown has something for everyone. With its top-rated hotels, unique eateries, friendly locals, and easy access to nearby attractions, Smithtown is the perfect destination for a memorable vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Smithtown

Here’s our curated list of must-see attractions in Smithtown that you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Smithtown Historical Society: Explore the rich history of Smithtown at the Historical Society, which houses a treasure trove of memorabilia and artifacts that trace the town’s evolution from a small hamlet to a bustling suburban community.

  • Caleb Smith State Park Preserve: This sprawling park is home to an 18th-century farmstead, a nature museum, and 543 acres of pristine woods and wetlands. Visitors can explore the Nature Museum, hike along the park’s trails, fish in the ponds, or simply relax amidst the natural beauty.

  • Long Island Museum: Located in nearby Stony Brook, the Long Island Museum offers an insightful glimpse into Long Island’s past, with exhibits on local art, science, and history.

Natural Attractions of Smithtown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Blydenburgh County Park: This park is a nature-lover’s paradise with its sprawling forests, shimmering lakes, and hiking trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding landscapes. Visitors can enjoy boating, fishing, horse-riding, and hiking in the park.

  • The Smithtown Bull: A symbol of the town’s heritage and culture, the Smithtown Bull located in Main Street is a must-see attraction. Legend has it that the town was founded after Richard Smith, the town’s founder, chose the location based on where a bull took rest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Smithtown

Smithtown’s hiking/biking trails offer visitors a great way to explore the region’s scenic beauty while also enjoying some outdoor adventure. Here’s a list of top hiking/biking trails in Smithtown:

  • Caleb State Park Hiking Trail: This 1.25 mile beautiful trail takes visitors through the lush green woods of Caleb State Park and is an ideal option for biking or hiking enthusiasts.

  • Short Beach Nature Trail: This 1.5 mile trail is perfect for nature lovers and features breathtaking views of the lake, marshes, and beaches.

Best Things to Do in Smithtown: Activities and Tours

There’s never a dull moment in Smithtown, thanks to a wide range of activities and tours that cater to every age and interest. Here are our top picks for things to do in Smithtown:

  • Fishing Charters: Smithtown is a great place for fishing enthusiasts, with various charter companies offering guided tours to popular fishing spots in the region.

  • Vineyard Tours: Smithtown’s vineyards are a must-visit destination for wine lovers. Take a guided tour of the region’s vineyards and taste some of the best wines in the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Smithtown

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for Smithtown that covers all the best attractions in the town:

Day 1: Visit the Smithtown Historical Society and learn about the town’s history.

Day 2: Explore the Long Island Museum in nearby Stony Brook.

Day 3: Spend the day hiking and biking in Caleb State Park Preserve.

Day 4: Take a fishing charter to one of the popular fishing spots in the region.

Day 5: Visit Blydenburgh County Park and enjoy boating, horse-riding, and hiking.

Day 6: Join a guided vineyard tour and taste the best wines in the region.

Day 7: Take a relaxing stroll through town and visit local shops and cafes.

Food and Drink Scene of Smithtown

Smithtown’s diverse culinary scene offers visitors a chance to taste some of the best local cuisine and beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Smithtown:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Rolls: Head to Hap’s Clam Shack to taste their famous lobster rolls, which are packed with fresh lobster meat.

  • Bagels: Visit the Bagel Gallery to taste some of the best bagels in Smithtown.

  • Italian Cuisine: Dine at La Famiglia, a well-known family-owned restaurant known for its authentic Italian cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Smithtown’s vineyards offer a great selection of locally-made wines, which are a must-try for wine lovers.

  • Beer: Try local craft beers at the Crooked Ladder Brewery in Riberts St.

Where to Stay in Smithtown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Smithtown offers a range of accommodation options to fit every budget and preference. Here are some top-rated options:

  • Smithtown Inn: A historic inn located near the town center and offers traditional rooms and suites.

  • Hyatt Regency Long Island: This luxury hotel offers spacious rooms, world-class amenities and is set on a stunning golf course.

  • Clegg’s Hotel: Ideal for budget travelers, Clegg’s Hotel offers comfortable guest rooms at affordable prices.

What to Buy in Smithtown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some local products and souvenirs that you can bring back home to remember your trip to Smithtown:

  • Handmade jewelry: Browse through the selection of handmade jewelry at the Smithtown Historical Society’s gift shop.

  • Wines: Bring back home some of the locally made wines available in vineyards and wineries across Smithtown.

  • Pottery: Visit the Potter’s Workshop and purchase locally-made pottery pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of Smithtown

Smithtown’s culture and customs are shaped by its rich history, wildlife, and natural beauty. Some local customs worth noting are:

  • Respect for Wildlife: Smithtown is home to a wide variety of wildlife, which residents and visitors are advised to respect and protect.

  • Friendly locals: Smithtown is known for its welcoming community and friendly locals, who are always eager to help visitors.

Best Time to Visit Smithtown

The best time to visit Smithtown is during the shoulder seasons of spring (April-May) and autumn (September-November) when the weather is mild, and the crowds are fewer. However, peak tourist season in Smithtown is summer (June-August), where visitors can enjoy boating, fishing, and hiking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Smithtown

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Smithtown:

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife.

  • Engage with the locals and ask for recommendations for things to do and places to see.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places.

  • Disrespect wildlife or hunt without permission.

  • Be loud and noisy at night, especially in residential areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Smithtown

Smithtown is generally a safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors are advised to take some general precautions, such as keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Smithtown

Smithtown offers plenty of medical facilities, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. Visitors are advised to carry their prescription medication and consult a doctor in case of any medical emergency.

Getting to Smithtown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Smithtown

The most convenient airports to Smithtown are Long Island MacArthur Airport and John F. Kennedy International Airport, which are located within an hour drive of the town.

Bus Stations Near Smithtown

The Smithtown Bus Terminal offers bus services to and from New York City and other nearby destinations.

Other Options

Smithtown is easily accessible by car or bike, and visitors can rent a car or bike for easy transportation.

How to Get Around in Smithtown

Renting a car is the best option for getting around Smithtown, as public transportation is limited. Visitors can explore the town on foot or by bike in the town center or can drive to nearby destinations.

Where to Go Next from Smithtown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some more time to explore, here are some nearby destinations that are worth considering:

  • Montauk: A charming seaside town located at the tip of Long Island.

  • Fire Island: This barrier island is home to pristine beaches, spectacular views, and some of the best outdoor activities in the region.

Conclusion

Smithtown is a hidden gem offering a unique blend of scenic beauty, outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and local cuisine. It’s the perfect destination for visitors looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and enjoy a relaxing vacation amidst nature in a charming and welcoming town. Whether you’re planning a weekend getaway or a week-long vacation, Smithtown is a destination that won’t disappoint.
