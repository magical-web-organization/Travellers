Introduction to East Hampton

Located in Suffolk County, New York, East Hampton is a charming town that is known for its beautiful beaches, picturesque landscapes, and rich history. The town was founded in 1648 and has since evolved into a popular tourist destination.

This comprehensive tour guide will help you uncover the best things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and more in East Hampton. Whether you are looking for a relaxing beach vacation or an outdoor adventure, East Hampton has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in East Hampton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Home, Sweet Home Museum

Located on James Lane in East Hampton, the Home Sweet Home Museum is a must-visit attraction. This historic house museum was built in 1720 and was home to John Howard Payne, who wrote the famous song “Home, Sweet Home.”

Visitors to the museum can take a guided tour of the home and learn about its history, architecture, and the life of John Howard Payne. The museum also hosts a variety of events and educational programs throughout the year.

Mulford Farm

Built in the 17th century, Mulford Farm is a historic house museum that showcases the rich history and heritage of East Hampton. The house is a testament to the early colonial architecture of the town and is filled with artifacts and exhibits that offer insight into life in the 17th and 18th centuries.

Visitors to Mulford Farm can take a guided tour of the property or participate in one of the many events and educational programs that are offered throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of East Hampton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Montauk Point State Park

Montauk Point State Park is a must-visit attraction for anyone who loves the great outdoors. Located on the easternmost point of Long Island, the park features stunning ocean views, towering cliffs, and miles of beautiful beaches.

Activities at Montauk Point State Park include hiking, fishing, camping, and more. The park also features a historic lighthouse that is open for tours.

Amagansett National Wildlife Refuge

If you are looking for a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of East Hampton, the Amagansett National Wildlife Refuge is the perfect destination. This 36-acre refuge is home to a variety of wildlife, including migratory birds, mammals, and reptiles.

Visitors to the refuge can take a leisurely walk along the trails, take part in birdwatching, and explore the many different habitats that exist within the refuge.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East Hampton

Cedar Point County Park

Located on the eastern end of East Hampton, Cedar Point County Park is a popular destination for hiking and biking. The park features a network of trails that wind through the woods, offering stunning views of Gardiners Bay and the surrounding countryside.

Walking Dunes Trail

The Walking Dunes Trail is a popular hiking trail that winds through the Hither Hills State Park. The trail features towering sand dunes that are constantly shifting, offering a unique and constantly changing landscape.

Best Things to Do in East Hampton: Activities and Tours

East Hampton Village Farmers Market

If you are looking for a taste of the local culture, the East Hampton Village Farmers Market is a must-visit destination. Held every Friday from May to September, the market offers a wide selection of fresh produce, baked goods, artisanal products, and more.

Visitors to the market can also enjoy live music and other entertainment, making it a fun and family-friendly destination.

Food and Drink Scene of East Hampton

Must-Try Dishes

Lobster Roll

No trip to East Hampton is complete without trying the famous lobster roll. This iconic dish features chunks of fresh lobster meat, mixed with mayo, and served on a buttery roll.

Whether you prefer it hot or cold, there are plenty of restaurants and seafood shacks in East Hampton that serve up delicious lobster rolls.

Clam Chowder

Another popular dish in East Hampton is clam chowder. Made with fresh clams, potatoes, and cream, this hearty soup is a staple at many local restaurants and taverns.

Local Beverages

Montauk Brewing Company

The Montauk Brewing Company is a local brewery that is famous for its craft beers. Visitors to the brewery can take a tour of the facility, sample the beers, and even fill a growler to bring home.

Where to Stay in East Hampton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Maidstone Hotel

Located in the heart of East Hampton, The Maidstone Hotel is a charming boutique hotel that offers a unique and luxurious experience. The hotel features 19 individually-designed rooms, each with their own unique decor and amenities.

East Hampton House Resort

For those who prefer a more laid-back atmosphere, the East Hampton House Resort is the perfect destination. This family-friendly resort features a variety of accommodations, including studio apartments, suites, and cottages.

What to Buy in East Hampton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sag Harbor Florist

For a unique and locally-made souvenir, head to Sag Harbor Florist. This charming shop offers a variety of floral arrangements, home decor, and other gifts that are made by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of East Hampton

East Hampton is known for its rich history and cultural heritage. One of the most important traditions in East Hampton is the preservation and restoration of historic buildings and landmarks.

Best Time to Visit East Hampton

The best time to visit East Hampton is during the summer months, from June to September. During this time of year, the weather is warm and sunny, and the town is bustling with tourists and locals alike. However, be prepared for higher prices and more crowds during this time as well.

Do’s and Don’ts of East Hampton

Do:

  • Do take the time to explore the local beaches and natural attractions
  • Do try the lobster rolls and other local seafood specialties
  • Do take part in the local culture and traditions

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to bring your sunscreen and bug spray
  • Don’t litter or leave trash on the beach or in local parks
  • Don’t ignore the historical sites and cultural landmarks

Safety and Health

Safety in East Hampton

Overall, East Hampton is a safe destination for tourists. However, it is always important to be aware of your surroundings and take common sense precautions to ensure your safety.

Health in East Hampton

East Hampton has several medical facilities and hospitals to ensure tourist’s health and safety. It is important to note, however, that healthcare can be expensive in the United States, so it’s always important to have proper travel insurance.

Getting to East Hampton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near East Hampton

The closest airport to East Hampton is the Francis S. Gabreski Airport, located in Westhampton. Another option is the East Hampton Airport, which is a small regional airport that accommodates private planes.

Bus Stations Near East Hampton

The Hampton Jitney is a popular bus service that runs from New York City to East Hampton and other destinations in the Hamptons. There are also several other bus companies that offer service to East Hampton and the surrounding area.

Other Options

For those who prefer to drive, East Hampton is accessible from several major highways. The Long Island Rail Road also provides service to East Hampton and other destinations in the area.

How to Get Around in East Hampton

The best way to get around East Hampton is by car. There are several car rental companies in the area, and many hotels and resorts offer shuttle service to popular attractions.

Biking is also a popular way to get around East Hampton, thanks to the many bike trails and dedicated bike lanes in the area.

Where to Go Next from East Hampton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Shelter Island

Located just a short ferry ride from East Hampton, Shelter Island is a charming and secluded destination that is perfect for a day trip or weekend getaway. The island features beautiful beaches, quaint shops and restaurants, and plenty of opportunities for outdoor recreation.

The North Fork

If you are looking for a more laid-back destination, the North Fork is a must-visit. This picturesque region is known for its wineries, farm stands, and charming small towns like Greenport and Mattituck.

Conclusion

We hope this comprehensive guide has helped you plan your trip to East Hampton. With its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and charming attractions, East Hampton is a destination that is sure to delight visitors of all ages and tastes. Enjoy your trip!
