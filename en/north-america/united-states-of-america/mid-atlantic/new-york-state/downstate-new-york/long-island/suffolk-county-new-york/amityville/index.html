Introduction to Amityville

Welcome to Amityville, located in Suffolk County in Long Island, New York. This charming town offers a unique blend of rich history, beautiful nature, and a vibrant food scene. Discover this hidden gem of Long Island with this guide.

List of Places to Visit in Amityville

Here are some of the must-see places in Amityville:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Amityville Historical Society: Visit the museum to learn about the town’s history, architecture, and culture. Don’t forget to check out the annual summer exhibit and outdoor concerts.

  • Lauder Museum: This historic house-turned-museum offers a glimpse into the life of the Lauder family and showcases the town’s history.

  • Babylon Village Historical Society: Explore the history of Babylon Village, located just a few miles away from Amityville. Don’t miss the annual Fall Festival.

Natural Attractions of Amityville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Amityville Beach: Enjoy a day in the sun at the beautiful beach, perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and picnicking.

  • Walt Whitman Trail: Hike through the scenic woods and discover the beauty of Long Island’s nature.

  • Scudder Park: This waterfront park offers stunning views of the Great South Bay and activities such as fishing, kayaking, and more.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Amityville

Explore the beauty of Amityville’s nature with these outdoor activities:

  • Bethpage Bikeway: This 12.5-mile trail runs from Massapequa Park to Bethpage State Park and offers scenic views of Long Island’s nature.

  • Belmont Lake State Park: This state park offers bike trails and hiking trails for all levels of experience. Don’t forget to take a boat ride on the lake.

Best Things to Do in Amityville: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best activities and tours in Amityville:

  • Amityville Trolley Tour: Discover the history and culture of Amityville on a 90-minute trolley tour.

  • Fishing Charters: Go on a fishing trip with the experienced guides to catch striped bass, fluke, and more.

  • Worried Woolies Alpaca Farm: Visit the alpacas and shop for wool products, socks, mittens, and more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Amityville

Here’s a recommended itinerary for a week-long stay in Amityville:

Food and Drink Scene of Amityville

Discover the tasty food and drink spots in Amityville:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll at Rhum: Enjoy a lobster roll with house-made potato chips on the waterfront.

  • Corned Beef and Pastrami at The Unofficial Deli: Enjoy a classic deli-style sandwich paired with a craft beer.

  • Margarita Pizza at Da Nicola: Indulge in a classic Italian thin-crust pizza paired with wine.

Local Beverages

  • Amity Ales: This microbrewery offers a variety of craft beers and breathtaking views of Great South Bay.

  • Dunkin’ Donuts: Satisfy your sweet tooth with a coffee and donut from the beloved American franchise.

Where to Stay in Amityville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some of the top accommodation options in Amityville:

  • Amityville Beach House: Enjoy a cozy stay near the beach with the house’s private deck and grill.

  • Anchor Inn: Stay in a comfortable room, just a short walking distance from the waterfront restaurants.

  • Heritage Inn: Explore the nearby dining, shopping, and attractions while staying in a spacious room with essential amenities.

What to Buy in Amityville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bring back some of Amityville’s unique products as souvenirs, such as:

  • Wool Products from Worried Woolies Alpaca Farm: Shop for wool socks, gloves, mittens, and more, made from Alpaca wool.

  • Craft Beer from Amity Ales: Grab a six-pack of pleasantly hoppy beers.

Local Culture and Customs of Amityville

Amityville is a small town with a close-knit community, where residents take pride in their history and enjoy the beautiful nature around them. It’s a welcoming and easy-going culture, where visitors can take time off from the city’s hectic pace and unwind by the water, enjoy seafood, and explore the town’s museums and nature preserves.

Best Time to Visit Amityville

The best time to visit Amityville is from June to September, to enjoy the beach and outdoor activities. Spring (April to May) and autumn (September to November) offer milder temperatures, and the fall is an excellent season to stroll around the town and witness the changing colors of the leaves. Winter (December to February) can be cold and snowy, but still, offer opportunities to explore the great indoors such as museums, shopping, and dining.

Do’s and Don’ts of Amityville

Here are some things you should keep in mind during your visit to Amityville:

Do:

  • Enjoy natural beauty with respect and care.

  • Be courteous with your camera.

  • Take part in local culture and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Litter the town and beaches.

  • Be intrusive with private property.

Safety and Health

Keep these safety and health tips in mind during your visit to Amityville:

Safety in Amityville

The town has low crime rates, but it’s always better to be cautious and aware of your surroundings. When walking on the roads, always use the crosswalks and wait for the traffic lights to avoid accidents.

Health in Amityville

The closest hospitals are Good Samaritan Hospital in West Islip and Southside Hospital in Bay Shore.

Getting to Amityville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some convenient ways of getting to Amityville:

Airports Near Amityville

The closest airport is Long Island Islip MacArthur Airport, about 8 miles away from the town.

Bus Stations Near Amityville

The Babylon train station and the Amityville bus station connect the town with the Long Island Rail Road and NYC Subway system.

Other Options

Taxis and Ubers are available throughout Long Island, and the Babylon Taxi service is based in the town.

How to Get Around in Amityville

The best way to explore the town and its surroundings is by biking or walking. Bike rental options are available, and Uber and taxi services are available as well.

Where to Go Next from Amityville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can explore on your trip from Amityville:

  • Fire Island: Take a ferry from Bay Shore to Fire Island and indulge in its pristine beaches and charming beach towns.

  • Montauk: The surfing capital of New York, Montauk offers scenic beauty, great seafood, and water sports.

  • Oyster Bay: This charming town, located about 20 miles away, offers historical attractions, beautiful gardens, and waterfront dining.

Conclusion

Amityville is a beautiful town, offering an excellent blend of natural beauty, history, and delicious food. We hope this guide has helped you plan a memorable trip to this charming Long Island town. Enjoy your stay!
