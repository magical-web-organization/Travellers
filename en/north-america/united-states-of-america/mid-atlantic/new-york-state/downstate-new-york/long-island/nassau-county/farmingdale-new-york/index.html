Introduction to Farmingdale (New York)

Farmingdale is a village located in Nassau County, in Long Island, New York, United States. It is a quaint and charming place filled with vibrant culture and rich heritage. The village is renowned for its aerospace industry, historical sites, outdoor recreations, and authentic food.

Whether you explore its cultural spots, natural attractions, or indulge in its delicious food or go on an adventurous trek, Farmingdale will exceed your expectations.

List of Places to Visit in Farmingdale (New York)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The village of Farmingdale boasts several culturally important attractions that have been preserved to give visitors a glimpse into its past.

  1. American Airpower Museum: This museum focuses on the history of military aviation and features a vast collection of vintage aircraft and related artifacts. The site is open year-round, and you can even ride in a World War II aircraft during special events.

  2. Old Bethpage Village Restoration: A living museum located nearby, provides an interactive educational experience through its historic exhibits, reenactments, and special events. Visitors can wander through restored houses, shops, and farms to get a glimpse of life in the 19th century.

  3. The Farmingdale Historical Society: Located in a restored Victorian-era home, the historical society offers a glimpse into the village’s rich past. The exhibits feature pictures, tools, and other historical artifacts that tell the story of Farmingdale’s evolution.

Natural Attractions of Farmingdale (New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Farmingdale is blessed with natural beauty which can be explored by embarking on outdoor adventures or taking leisurely strolls along scenic routes.

  1. Bethpage State Park: The park is spread over 1,400 acres and is brimming with activities for all ages and interests. It has five golf courses; a polo field; tennis courts; picnic areas; hiking and biking trails; and more. The park is an ideal outdoor destination for families and adventure enthusiasts.

  2. Conklin House Museum: Located on a 1.2-acre plot, the Conklin House Museum has beautifully manicured gardens and grounds for leisurely walks and enjoying pastoral views. It is a historic farmhouse that offers insights into the area’s agricultural heritage.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Farmingdale (New York)

The village of Farmingdale has a lot to offer to hikers and cyclists of varying abilities and interests.

  1. Farmingdale Greenbelt Trail: This 9-mile trail is a perfect destination for hikers and bikers who want to enjoy nature while staying close to the Village. You will see the remarkable beauty of woods, wetlands, and wildflowers, that offer a view of Long Island.

  2. Massapequa Preserve: With miles of trails and a serene lake, the preserve is a haven for hikers and mountain bikers. The diverse trails here range from beginner level to advanced, and you can explore the boathouse and nature center too.

Best Things to Do in Farmingdale (New York): Activities and Tours

There is a lot to do in Farmingdale, from outdoor activities to immersing yourself in the region’s culture. Here are some must-try activities and tours when you visit:

  1. Taste Farmingdale Food Tours: If you want to experience the area through its food scene, then take a food tour, it is an excellent way to try out many different local delights and learn about the area’s dining culture. There will be a stop at the famous Republic Pub and Kitchen for crispy calamari tacos and raw bar selections if you want to quench your thirst, you can end the tour at The Nutty Irishmen where you can grab a cold brunch cocktail or some beer on tap.

  2. Wine Tasting: Farmingdale is located near some of Long Island’s most famous wineries, such as the Bedell Cellars and Kontokosta Winery, tour and experience vineyard in the East Coast’s premier wine region and spend an afternoon tasting award-winning wines.

Week-Long Itinerary for Farmingdale (New York)

Here’s a sample itinerary to make the most out of your weeklong stay in Farmingdale:

Day 1: Take a food tour of Farmingdale, and visit Republic Pub and Kitchen for lunch.

Day 2: Visit the American Airpower Museum, explore the exhibits, and ride in a World War II aircraft.

Day 3: Visit the Conklin House Museum and enjoy a leisurely stroll in the beautifully manicured gardens.

Day 4: Spend a day in Bethpage State Park, enjoying the golf courses, hiking, and mountain biking trails.

Day 5: Visit Old Bethpage Village Restoration and immerse yourself in 19th-century life in the village.

Day 6: Go on a wine tasting tour of Long Island’s premier vineyards

Day 7: Take a hot air balloon ride and get an aerial view of the picturesque village.

Food and Drink Scene of Farmingdale (New York)

Must-Try Dishes

Farmingdale is a melting pot of cultures, and its restaurant scene reflects that diversity. Here are some must-try dishes:

  1. Chicken Parmesan from Vespa Italian Kitchen and Bar: Tender chicken cutlet smothered in marinara sauce and mozzarella, served with a side of spaghetti.

  2. BBQ ribs from Carlyle on the Green: BBQ pork ribs drenched in a sweet and smoky sauce served with a side of cornbread and coleslaw.

Local Beverages

Farmingdale also has an impressive collection of local beverages, with beautiful bars and breweries.

  1. Lithology Brewing Company: This is a microbrewery that offers visitors a wide array of craft beers to sample, keeping it fresh, diverse and exciting.

  2. Dark Horse Tavern: The perfect spot for a cold pint and a game day, Dark Horse Tavern offers a selection of beer and wine options. Its menu features bar bites like sliders, wings, calamari, and nachos.

Where to Stay in Farmingdale (New York): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Farmingdale offers a variety of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences.

  1. Hilton Garden Inn Melville: The hotel is located a few minutes away from the village center and offers visitors breakfast, comfortable rooms, and a wide range of amenities.

  2. Courtyard by Marriott: The Courtyard by Marriott is a hotel located right next to the famous Bethpage State Park. It offers visitors easy access to all the exciting outdoor activities the park has to offer while providing comfortable accommodation.

What to Buy in Farmingdale (New York): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a unique gift to take home? Here are some local products to consider:

  1. Wine from Long Island’s wineries: There are many vineyards in Farmingdale, and a bottle of wine from one of the wineries is an excellent way to savor the memories of your trip to Farmingdale.

  2. Hand-crafted artifacts and antiques: The Farmingdale Antiques Marketplace and the Delaware Plazas antique shops offer a wide variety of antique artifacts that visitors can purchase as a gift to remember their journey.

Local Culture and Customs of Farmingdale (New York)

  1. Farmingdale has a proud history of pioneering the aviation industry, and visitors should respect the importance the village places on aviation.

  2. The village is also respectful of cultural diversity, so visitors should be mindful of that and be respectful of the customs of others.

Best Time to Visit Farmingdale (New York)

The best time to visit Farmingdale is during the spring or the fall. Visitors will enjoy the mild weather, and the fall’s vibrant colors add to the charm of the village.

Getting to Farmingdale (New York): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Farmingdale (New York)

  1. JFK International Airport: Located approximately 30 miles from Farmingdale.

  2. LaGuardia Airport: Located approximately 27 miles from Farmingdale.

Bus Stations Near Farmingdale (New York)

  1. Farmingdale Bus Terminal: Located in the center of the village.

  2. Nassau Inter-County Express Bus: NICE bus system, buses run frequently between Farmingdale and the surrounding area.

Other Options

Visitors can also avail of taxi, cab, or rideshare service to get around town.

How to Get Around in Farmingdale (New York)

The village is best explored on foot or by bike, as many of the popular attractions are located within walking distance. Visitors can also use rental cars or public transportation options.

Where to Go Next from Farmingdale (New York): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Fire Island: A popular island destination located 30 miles to the southeast of Farmingdale.

Conclusion

Farmingdale is a charming village that offers the perfect blend of outdoor activities, entertainment, and cultural experiences. With its year-round moderate weather, visitors can enjoy this paradise in any season. So pack your bags and explore Farmingdale!
