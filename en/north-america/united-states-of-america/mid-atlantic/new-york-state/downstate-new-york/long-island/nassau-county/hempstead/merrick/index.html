Introduction to Merrick

Merrick is a charming hamlet in Nassau County, located in Long Island, in Downstate New York. Just about thirty minutes drive from Manhattan, Merrick boasts of a whole bunch of alluring attractions ranging from scenic parks, fascinating museums, and historic landmarks to delicious food and local markets. The hamlet is renowned for its recreational opportunities, including hiking and biking trails, scenic views of stunning waterways and wildlife intermixed with nature reserves, and lush green golf courses.

This travel guide will help you uncover the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and what to eat in Merrick, New York.

List of Places to Visit in Merrick

When it comes to exploring Merrick, there’s an endless list of places to visit. From museums and historic landmarks to parks and beaches, below is a list of the most recommended places to visit in Merrick.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Merrick Art Gallery - an art gallery featuring a permanent collection of over 100 pieces of contemporary art
  • Merrick Train Station Museum - a historic train station turned museum showcasing the history of the Long Island Rail Road
  • Mount Sinai Cemetery - a southern Nassau County historical landmark featuring a large number of historic grave sites
  • Old Mill, Baldwin - a historic site displaying a restored mill, the structure dates back to the mid-18th century
  • Baldwin First United Methodist Church - an architectural landmark featuring decorative interiors and fascinating sculptures

Natural Attractions of Merrick: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Norman J. Levy Park and Preserve - a popular park in Merrick offering beautiful views of Merrick Bay and miles of hiking & bike trails
  • Cedar Creek Park - a park offering trails for hiking, jogging, and biking, picnicking areas, and a few baseball fields
  • Jones Beach State Park - a sprawling park with a large swimming pool and miles of beaches overlooking the Atlantic Ocean
  • Fire Island National Seashore - a scenic park offering plenty of activities to enjoy like swimming, biking, and nature trails
  • Theodore Roosevelt Sanctuary & Audubon Center - a 375-acre wildlife sanctuary with walking trails and a visitor center featuring temporary exhibitions and educational programs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Merrick

There are plenty of hiking and biking trails in and around Merrick, ready to explore and hike for those who love the outdoors. Here are our top picks:

  • Norman Levy Preserve - a beautiful trail with stunning views of Merrick Bay located in the southern end of Merrick
  • Wantagh Parkway Trail - a serene trail that follows the Wantagh Parkway’s median to the south shore of Jones Beach State Park
  • Cedar Creek Park Trail - a scenic trail located south of Merrick Road, that offers multiple loops covering the park’s perimeter & interior areas
  • Captree State Park Trail - a scenic trail surrounded by the stunning views of coastal wetlands, small islands, and ocean beaches with boardwalks and nature trails

Best Things to Do in Merrick: Activities and Tours

From sightseeing tours to exploring the local market, here are some of the best things to do while in Merrick.

  • Take a walk around Norman J. Levy Park and Preserve
  • Go Fishing in Jones Beach State Park
  • Visit the Merrick Art Gallery
  • Head to Lido Beach Golf Course for a game of Golf
  • Go Shopping at Roosevelt Field Mall
  • Explore the Baldwin Historical Museum

Week-Long Itinerary for Merrick

To make the most of your stay in Merrick, here’s our recommended week-long itinerary for Merrick:

  • Day 1 - Visit Norman J. Levy Park and Preserve, Jones Beach State Park, and check-in at the acclaimed Bellmore Inn.
  • Day 2 - Explore Merrick Art Gallery, the Theodore Roosevelt Sanctuary & Audubon Center, and take a relaxing stroll around the Baldwin Historical Museum.
  • Day 3 - Go shopping at the Roosevelt Field Mall, and end the day with a round of Golf at the Lido Beach Golf Course.
  • Day 4 - Take a walking tour of the Old Mill in Baldwin and the Baldwin First United Methodist Church, followed by lunch at the nearby Piccola Bussola.
  • Day 5 - Head over to the beautiful Fire Island National Seashore, welcoming visitors with its endless ocean views and natural beauty.
  • Day 6 - Explore the Merrick Train Station Museum, or indulge in a well-deserved spa day at The Red Door Spa Station.
  • Day 7 - Take in the stunning views of Merrick Bay & walk the Cedar Creek Park Trail, followed by a tranquil dusk at the serene Captree State Park.

Local Culture and Customs of Merrick

Merrick has a rich and fascinating culture common among many towns in Nassau County. Below are some of the customs and traditions that make Merrick unique:

  • Poetry and literature readings are highly appreciated in the town
  • The Bellmore-Merrick Central School District promotes arts and music talent
  • Merrick has a rich sports culture, from Football, Basketball, Swimming, and Lacrosse

Best Time to Visit Merrick

The best time to visit Merrick is during fall, September to November, when the weather is mild and the foliage is breathtaking. The town also bustles with numerous cultural events like reading series, poetry nights, and trivia nights.

Safety and Health

Safety in Merrick

Merrick is a relatively safe town, and the crime rate is moderate. However, tourists are advised to keep an eye on their belongings and avoid walking in parks and secluded areas during the night.

Health in Merrick

Merrick has several primary care facilities, including the Northwell Health-GoHealth Urgent Care and Park Avenue Gynecology and Obstetrics. The town has several strong animal control codes to ensure that there is minimal chance of diseased animals.

Getting to Merrick: Transportation Options and Logistics

Below are the various options for getting into Merrick:

Airports Near Merrick

  • John F. Kennedy International Airport (JFK) - 20 miles from Merrick
  • LaGuardia Airport (LGA) - 25 miles from Merrick

Bus Stations Near Merrick

  • Nassau Inter-County Express (NICE) - a public bus service that runs daily routes through Nassau County and New York City
  • Queens Village (LIRR) - rail service connecting the hamlets of Merrick and the nearby Empire State

Other Options

  • Rental Cars - several car rental companies providing services in Merrick, including Hertz, Budget, and Enterprise Rent-A-Car

How to Get Around in Merrick

Merrick has a well-connected transportation network that includes taxis, bus services, and rail services. The most cost-effective way to get around Merrick is by using the Nassau Inter-County Express, which operates every day.

Where to Go Next from Merrick: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Merrick has a range of picturesque attractions, but if you have extra time on your hands, you can also consider exploring nearby destinations, including:

  • The vibrant city of New York, where you get to experience the city that never sleeps and explore several iconic sites
  • Wantagh - neighboring hamlet that is home to the Jones Beach State Park, one of Long Island’s favorite beaches
  • Hempstead - a perfect destination for history buffs, offering a variety of landmarks, such as Hofstra University, the first to offer a private law school.

Conclusion

Merrick is a charming hamlet that boasts of unmatchable natural beauty, plenty of recreational activities, historical landmarks and museums, delectable food, and local markets. It’s a perfect destination for a peaceful weekend getaway or an extended one-week vacation. Follow this comprehensive travel guide to make the most out of your stay in Merrick, New York.
