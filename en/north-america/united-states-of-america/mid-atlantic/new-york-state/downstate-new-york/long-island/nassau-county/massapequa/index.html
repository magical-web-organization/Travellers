Introduction to Massapequa

Located in the center of Nassau County, Massapequa is a charming hamlet on Long Island that offers visitors a wonderful mix of outdoor adventure, history, and culture. With its close proximity to New York City, it’s the perfect destination for visitors who want to explore both the city and the suburbs.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at all the great things Massapequa has to offer, including must-visit attractions, hiking and biking routes, food and drink recommendations, accommodations, and more!

List of Places to Visit in Massapequa

Massapequa has no shortage of exciting places to explore, from historical sites to natural wonders. Here are some of our top recommendations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Old Grace Church: This historical site has been standing since 1844 and was used as a hospital during the Civil War. The church is now a museum where visitors can learn about local history and take guided tours.

  • The Massapequa Preserve: A picturesque nature reserve that spans over 400 acres. It’s a perfect spot for hiking, birdwatching, and photography.

  • Massapequa History Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving the history of Massapequa and the surrounding area. Here you can explore exhibits featuring artifacts and photographs from the past.

  • The Hicksville Gregory Museum: This small institution is dedicated to local history and natural science. It offers engaging programming geared toward visitors of all ages.

Natural Attractions of Massapequa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bar Harbor Preserve: A nature preserve where visitors can enjoy hiking trails, birdwatching, and scenic views of the Great South Bay.

  • Tackapausha Museum & Preserve: A popular attraction that features nature trails, interactive exhibits, and educational programs on ecology, geology, and archaeology.

  • Jones Beach State Park: A popular destination for beachgoers with over six miles of white sandy beaches. Visitors can also enjoy the boardwalk, bike trails, and other outdoor activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Massapequa

Massapequa is blessed with many hiking and biking trails, some of which offer stunning views of the surrounding landscape. Here are some of the most popular trails:

  • Massapequa Park Preserve: This beautiful nature preserve is home to three miles of trails, including a wooden boardwalk, which takes visitors through marshlands and oak forests.

  • Bethpage State Park: This park is home to five world-renowned golf courses, but there are also hiking and biking trails available. Don’t miss the 1.5-mile Greenbelt Trail, which offers scenic routes through the park.

Best Things to Do in Massapequa: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or unique tours, Massapequa has something to offer. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • 361 Overlook: For a unique view of the Great South Bay, book a tour with 361 Overlook. Visitors can enjoy a ride on a helicopter or take a sunset cruise on a luxury boat, while enjoying breathtaking views of the sunset and the city skyline.

  • Cinema Arts Center: This non-profit cinema hosts movie screenings, film festivals, and educational programs that celebrate independent and foreign films.

Week-Long Itinerary for Massapequa

To make the most of your trip to Massapequa, here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long visit:

Day 1: Explore the Massapequa Preserve.

Day 2: Visit the Old Grace Church and the Massapequa History Museum.

Day 3: Hike the Bethpage State Park Greenbelt Trail.

Day 4: Take a Victorian Tea at the Hicksville Gregory Museum.

Day 5: Head to Tackapausha Museum & Preserve to explore nature and science exhibits.

Day 6: Enjoy a day at Jones Beach State Park.

Day 7: Take a 361 Overlook tour for a unique view of Massapequa from above.

Food and Drink Scene of Massapequa

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bagels: New York-style bagels are famous around the world, and Massapequa has no shortage of great bagel shops.

  • Pizza: Another New York classic, Massapequa is home to several great pizzerias where you can enjoy a slice or a whole pie.

  • Seafood: With its close proximity to the ocean, it’s no surprise that Massapequa has some amazing seafood options. Try the fried clams at All American Hamburger Drive-In.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer: Long Island is well-known for its craft beer scene, and Massapequa is no exception. Check out Lithology Brewing Co. for some great local brews.

Where to Stay in Massapequa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of options for accommodations in and around Massapequa, whether you’re looking for a luxury hotel or a budget-friendly hostel. Some of our top recommendations include:

  • The Inn at Fox Hollow: This luxury hotel offers spacious suites with private balconies, a fitness center, and an indoor pool.

  • Extended Stay America: For a more budget-friendly option, this hotel offers comfortable rooms with kitchenettes for extended stays.

What to Buy in Massapequa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Honey from The Bee Wagon: This local honey company offers a range of delicious products, including flavored honey, honey sticks, and beeswax candles.

Local Culture and Customs of Massapequa

Massapequa is a close-knit community that values family and tradition. Visitors should be prepared to encounter friendly locals who take pride in their hometown and love to share their experiences with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Massapequa

The best time to visit Massapequa is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. However, visitors can also enjoy the beautiful autumn foliage or visit during the winter holidays, when the town is decorated with lights and festive events take place.

Do’s and Don’ts of Massapequa

Do:

  • Stay hydrated during outdoor activities.

  • Follow the posted rules and regulations when visiting natural or cultural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural or cultural attractions.

  • Ignore safety precautions when partaking in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Massapequa

Massapequa is a relatively safe community, but visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take precautions to secure their belongings.

Health in Massapequa

While Massapequa doesn’t have any health hazards unique to the area, visitors should be aware of any necessary vaccinations or precautions before traveling to the United States.

Getting to Massapequa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Massapequa

  • John F. Kennedy International Airport (JFK): Located about 30 miles west of Massapequa, JFK is one of the largest airports in the United States and serves both domestic and international flights.

  • LaGuardia Airport (LGA): Located about 25 miles west of Massapequa, LGA is another large airport with many domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Massapequa

  • Massapequa is served by the Nassau Inter-County Express (NICE) bus system, which provides transportation throughout Nassau County.

Other Options

  • Visitors can also rent a car or hail a taxi to Massapequa.

How to Get Around in Massapequa

Visitors can explore Massapequa on foot, or they can rent a bike to take advantage of the many scenic trails and parks in the area. Taxis, ubers, and the NICE bus system are other transportation options available, but it is recommended to have a personal car for ultimate convenience.

Where to Go Next from Massapequa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • New York City: Only 45 minutes by car or train, the Big Apple is a must-visit destination.

  • Fire Island: This beautiful island just south of Long Island has stunning beaches and a charming town that’s worth exploring.

Conclusion

With so much to offer, Massapequa is a destination that should be on everyone’s travel list. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, there’s something for everyone in this charming hamlet. Follow our recommendations for an unforgettable trip to Massapequa!
