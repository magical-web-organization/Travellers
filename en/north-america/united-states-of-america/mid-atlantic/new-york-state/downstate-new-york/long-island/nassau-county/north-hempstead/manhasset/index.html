A Comprehensive Tour Guide for Manhasset, New York

Manhasset is a charming village situated in Nassau County, Long Island, NY. With its combination of historic charm, natural beauty, and modern amenities, Manhasset is a great destination for travelers of all ages and interests. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take a closer look at what Manhasset has to offer and provide plenty of insider tips to help you make the most of your visit.

Introduction to Manhasset

Manhasset is a census-designated place in North Hempstead, Nassau County, NY, which is a part of the New York metropolitan area. This village boasts a rich history dating back to the colonial era, which is evident in its architecture and landmarks. Visitors to Manhasset will appreciate its tree-lined streets, charming main street, and vibrant community spirit.

List of Places to Visit in Manhasset

Here are some must-see attractions to add to your Manhasset itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sands-Willets House: This beautifully restored house museum dates back to the Revolutionary era and is a great place to learn about Manhasset’s colonial history.
  • North Hempstead Beach Park: A picturesque park on the water, North Hempstead Beach Park is great for picnics, swimming, and hiking.
  • Manhasset Bay Yacht Club: A historic sailing and social club that offers a lovely atmosphere and amazing views of Manhasset Bay.

Natural Attractions of Manhasset: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Manhasset Bay: This stunning bay offers visitors breathtaking views of Long Island Sound. Visitors can take nature walks and cycling on the shore of the bay or be a part of the summer kayaking excursions there.
  • Apple Trees: If you want to experience a little bit of rural life, check out this charming apple orchard.
  • The Preserve: A tranquil nature preserve that’s great for a peaceful walk and bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Manhasset

  • Port Washington Loop: This scenic route starts in Manhasset and leads you through the high-end areas of Sands Point. You’ll get to experience amazing sea views and spot fur seals from the cliff areas there.
  • Roosevelt Forest: A peaceful, rustic park with plenty of nature trails for hiking and biking enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Manhasset: Activities and Tours

  • Whitman’s Candy Store: This is a quintessential old-fashioned candy store that displays an outstanding collection of vintage treats. You also get to taste-test their famous signature pecan caramel patties.
  • Massage Envy Established: Relax and rejuvenate at this premier spa center.
  • Sonic Drive-In: Be sure to enjoy a classic drive-in dining experience at this popular Manhasset eatery.

Week-Long Itinerary for Manhasset

Day 1: Sands-Willets House, Apple Trees, Port Washington Loop

Day 2: North Hempstead Beach Park, Manhasset Bay Yacht Club, Sonic Drive-In

Day 3: The Preserve, Massage Envy Established, Roosevelt Forest

Day 4: Whitman’s Candy Store, Sands Point Preserve, O’Hagan’s Tavern

Day 5: Museum of American Armor, Hicks Nurseries, St. Mary’s Roman Catholic Church

Day 6: Science Museum of Long Island, Tattenham Corner Stables, Ben’s Kosher Delicatessen

Day 7: North Shore Farms, Pelican Ski and Snowboard, Americana Manhasset

Food and Drink Scene of Manhasset

Manhasset boasts a vibrant food and drink culture that reflects the village’s diverse community and rich history.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Louie’s Clam Bar & Restaurant: A neighborhood institution that has been serving up some of the best clam chowder and lobster rolls on Long Island for over a century.
  • T&O Thai and Japanese Cuisine: Taste the bold flavors of Thailand and Japan at this popular eatery.
  • Eddie’s Pizza: Enjoy classic New York-style pizza that’s been praised by locals and visitors alike.

Local Beverages

  • C Town Wine & Spirits: This premier wine and spirits store offers a massive selection of wine, liquor, sake, and beer.
  • Brews Brothers Grille: This pub features an impressive selection of draft beer and an extensive cocktail menu that’s perfect for happy hour.

Where to Stay in Manhasset: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Manhasset is home to a range of accommodation options, from cozy bed and breakfasts to luxurious resorts.

  • Towers at Water’s Edge: This 20-story co-op overlooks the Long Island Sound and offers residents an unparalleled view of the city skyline.
  • Inn at Great Neck: A chic boutique hotel that combines classic elegance with modern luxury.
  • Amsterdam Hotel: A charming bed and breakfast situated in a historic building that was once a tavern.

What to Buy in Manhasset: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Gatsby Salon: Treat yourself to high-end beauty products at this stylish salon.
  • Little Switzerland: A jewelry store that carries a wide range of fine jewelry and watches.
  • Manhasset Specialty Foods: A gourmet food store that stocks an impressive selection of local and international products.

Local Culture and Customs of Manhasset

One of the things that makes Manhasset so special is its vibrant community spirit. Visitors to Manhasset should be prepared to embrace the town’s friendly and welcoming culture, which is rooted in traditions and local customs.

Best Time to Visit Manhasset

Manhasset is a year-round destination, but the summer months are the most popular due to pleasant weather and a plethora of outdoor activities. Fall is also an excellent time to visit, as the trees begin to change color and the village is transformed into a picturesque autumn wonderland. Winter and spring are great for those looking to avoid crowds and experience Manhasset’s quieter side.

Do’s and Don’ts of Manhasset

While Manhasset is a friendly and welcoming place, there are a few things visitors should keep in mind to have a comfortable and enjoyable stay.

Do:

  • Get outside and explore Manhasset’s natural beauty.
  • Visit the village’s historic landmarks and museums.
  • Take advantage of the food and drink scene.
  • Embrace the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Neglect sun protection, especially during summer months.
  • Leave garbage or litter in public areas.
  • Disrespect Manhasset’s historic landmarks or natural beauty.
  • Be afraid to ask locals for advice and recommendations.

Safety and Health

Manhasset is a relatively safe place to visit, with a low crime rate and a strong community spirit. Visitors should take standard precautions while traveling, such as locking up valuables in their hotel rooms and being aware of their surroundings.

Safety in Manhasset

Manhasset is a pedestrian-friendly village with well-lit streets and safe walkways. Visitors should be aware of the potential for traffic congestion during peak travel times, and exercise caution when crossing busy roads.

Health in Manhasset

Manhasset’s healthcare facilities offer excellent medical care for visitors. In case of an emergency, visitors should dial 911 for medical assistance.

Getting to Manhasset: Transportation Options and Logistics

Manhasset is easily accessible from New York City and the surrounding areas. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Manhasset

  • LaGuardia Airport (LGA): Located just 14 miles west of Manhasset, LGA is the closest airport to the village.
  • John F. Kennedy International Airport (JFK): Situated 20 miles south of Manhasset, JFK is another convenient option for travelers.

Bus Stations Near Manhasset

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: Connects Manhasset with other cities and major destinations in the United States.
  • Megabus: Offers bus rides to popular cities in the northeastern United States.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car is one of the most convenient ways to get around Manhasset and the surrounding areas.
  • Taxi/Uber/Lyft: Taxis and ride-sharing services are widely available in Manhasset, making it easy to get around the village and beyond.

How to Get Around in Manhasset

Visitors to Manhasset can get around by walking, biking or taking a shuttle service. Renting a car is another popular option, as it allows visitors to explore the surrounding areas more easily.

Where to Go Next from Manhasset: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time and want to explore more of Long Island, there are plenty of nearby destinations worth visiting.

  • Old Westbury Gardens: A beautiful estate featuring gardens, museums, and cultural events.
  • Planting Fields Arboretum State Historic Park: A sprawling estate with historic homes, gardens, and walking trails.
  • Jones Beach State Park: A popular beach destination offering swimming, surfing, and fishing.

Conclusion

Manhasset is a charming village that offers visitors an excellent mix of history, culture, nature, and modern amenities. Whether you’re looking to explore the village’s colonial history, sample local cuisine, or simply relax and enjoy the scenery, Manhasset has something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive tour guide has given you plenty of inspiration for your next visit to this unique and charming destination.
