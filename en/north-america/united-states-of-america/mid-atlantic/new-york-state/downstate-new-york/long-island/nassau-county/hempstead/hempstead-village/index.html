Introduction to Hempstead (village)

Hempstead (village) is a charming and historic village located in the town of Hempstead, Nassau County, Long Island, New York. This beautiful village is rich in history and culture, featuring a variety of attractions and activities for tourists of all ages. Whether you’re interested in history, nature, food, shopping, or just soaking up the local culture, Hempstead (village) has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Hempstead (village)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hempstead House – This magnificent mansion is a must-visit for history lovers. Built in 1912, this stunning landmark offers a glimpse into the lives of Long Island’s wealthy elite.
  • Nassau County Museum of Art – The Nassau County Museum of Art features a wide range of exhibitions, from contemporary art to medieval sculpture. Set on a beautiful estate, this museum is perfect for art lovers of all ages.
  • Hofstra University Museum of Art – Hofstra University boasts a wonderful art museum with a permanent collection that includes over 5,000 works of art, ranging from ancient to modern art.

Natural Attractions of Hempstead (village): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jones Beach State Park – Jones Beach is one of the most popular summer destinations on Long Island, with miles of beautiful beaches, a two-mile boardwalk, and a range of activities for visitors. Perfect for swimming, sunbathing, fishing, and picnicking.
  • Gardiner’s Creek Park – This beautiful park offers a wide range of outdoor activities, from hiking and biking to picnicking and fishing. There’s also a playground for kids and plenty of open space for a game of Frisbee.
  • Norman J. Levy Park & Preserve – Adventure seekers will love this beautifully preserved nature reserve, with miles of hiking trails, stunning vistas, and plenty of wildlife to spot.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hempstead (village)

Hempstead (village) offers an abundance of hiking and biking trails for outdoor enthusiasts. The following are some of the most popular routes:

  • Jones Beach Bikeway – This 6.5-mile scenic bike path runs parallel to the Wantagh Parkway and offers stunning views of the Great South Bay.
  • Bethpage Bike Path – This eight-mile path is perfect for biking, jogging, and hiking.
  • Norman J. Levy Park and Preserve Trail – This rugged trail offers fantastic views of the surrounding area, and is perfect for experienced hikers and bikers.

Best Things to Do in Hempstead (village): Activities and Tours

  • Long Island Adventure Park – This exciting outdoor attraction features ropes courses, zip lines, and other challenges for the whole family.
  • The Escape Game Long Island – Put your problem-solving skills to the test with this exciting escape room experience.
  • Hempstead Taxi and Airport Service – Enjoy the best views of Hempstead (village) with this unique and luxurious tour of the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hempstead (village)

Day 1: Visit Hofstra University Museum of Art, followed by a picnic in nearby Kennedy Memorial Park. Day 2: Explore Hempstead House and its beautiful gardens, followed by a seafood dinner at nearby Jordan Lobster Farms. Day 3: Spend the day at Jones Beach State Park, followed by dinner at one of the beachfront restaurants. Day 4: Take a hike in Norman J. Levy Park & Preserve and enjoy the beautiful views. Follow it up with a visit to the American Airpower Museum. Day 5: Take a bike path to explore Bethpage State Park and its beautiful hiking trails. Day 6: Visit Nassau County Museum of Art and its beautiful exhibitions, followed by a dinner at one of the nearby restaurants. Day 7: Head to Long Island Adventure Park for some outdoor adventure and challenge course, and end the day with a drink by the water at Tropix on the Mile.

Getting to Hempstead (village): Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can reach Hempstead (village) by air, train, or car. There are several airports located within a 30-minute drive from the village, including LaGuardia Airport and John F. Kennedy International Airport. Long Island Railroad provides a direct train to Hempstead Station from Penn Station in Manhattan and offers connecting service from JFK and LaGuardia airports.

Once in Hempstead (village), visitors can use public transportation, taxi service, or rental cars to get around and explore the area.
