Introduction to Oyster Bay

Oyster Bay is a quaint little town in Nassau County, on the north shore of Long Island, New York. Steeped in history and natural beauty, Oyster Bay is where you can escape the hustle and bustle of big cities and indulge in the relaxing and charming beauty of a small town. Visitors to Oyster Bay can explore its vibrant cultural and natural attractions, hiking/biking routes, food and drink scene, and local customs. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you on a journey through the best that Oyster Bay has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Oyster Bay

Here is a list of must-visit places in Oyster Bay:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sagamore Hill National Historic Site: The home of former President Theodore Roosevelt, this site offers a glimpse into the life and legacy of the famed statesman and conservationist.
  • Raynham Hall Museum: A Georgian mansion built in the 18th century, this museum is a hub of Long Island history and culture.
  • Christ Episcopal Church: Built in 1725, this church served as a British Army hospital during the Revolutionary War and is a beautiful representation of colonial architecture.
  • Oyster Bay Railroad Museum: A fascinating attraction that showcases the history of rail travel in Long Island.

Natural Attractions of Oyster Bay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Planting Fields Arboretum State Historic Park: A nature lover’s paradise, home to several gardens, walking trails, and even the Coe Hall Mansion that have historical importance.
  • Oyster Bay National Wildlife Refuge: A peaceful gem with beautiful hiking trails where visitors can spot a plethora of bird species, foxes, and other wildlife.
  • Roosevelt Memorial Park and Beach: A postcard-worthy beach that offers stunning views of Long Island Sound.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Oyster Bay

With its diverse landscape, picturesque views, and a network of trails, Oyster Bay is perfect for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. Here are some must-try hiking/biking routes in Oyster Bay:

  • Cold Spring Harbor State Park Trails: A 40-acre park with many hiking options suitable for different skill levels.
  • Muttontown Preserve: A vast piece of land with 550 acres, great for hiking, biking and has other outdoor activity opportunities.
  • Stillwell Woods Preserve: A popular spot for mountain biking that offers a range of trails for all levels.

Best Things to Do in Oyster Bay: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking on Oyster Bay: Enjoy the sound of the waves and the tranquillity of the water by renting a kayak on the bay.
  • Sailing with Let’s Go Sailing: A wonderful experience to gaze upon the stunning sunsets, refreshing sea breeze.
  • Discover Long Island’s Wine Country: Take a visit to two award-winning wineries in Long Island Wine country for a taste of fantastic local wines!
  • Oyster Bay Food Crawl: Sample some of the best and unique delicacies of Oyster Bay on this adventurous culinary trail.

Week-Long Itinerary for Oyster Bay

Here’s a sample itinerary for an exciting week-long trip to Oyster Bay:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Oyster Bay, explore the historical attractions like Sagamore Hill National Historic Site and Raynham Hall Museum
  • Day 2: Visit Oyster Bay’s natural attractions like Planting Fields Arboretum State Historic Park and Oyster Bay National Wildlife Refuge
  • Day 3: Head to the beach at Roosevelt Memorial Park and Beach, and spend the day relaxing and soaking in the natural beauty.
  • Day 4: Explore the hiking & biking trails of Cold Spring Harbor State Park Trails and Muttontown Preserve
  • Day 5: Experience fantastic local wines with Discover Long Island’s Wine Country.
  • Day 6: Enjoy the picturesque sunset views on a sailing tour of Oyster Bay with Let’s Go Sailing.
  • Day 7: Spend the day at an adventurous food crawl in Oyster Bay.

Food and Drink Scene of Oyster Bay

Oyster Bay is full of amazing culinary experiences that would make your taste buds dance, not only seafood, but also farm-fresh produce and baked treats.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Oysters from Oyster Bay: Fresh and tasty oysters with a distinct briny flavor.
  • Clam chowder: A bowl of hot and comforting soup made with creamy chowder and chunks of fresh seafood.
  • Lobster Roll: A classic dish in New England and Long Island, juicy chunks of lobster meat with a buttered bun.
  • Local produce: Oyster Bay’s local farmer’s market offers a range of fresh seasonal produce, such as sweet corn, heirloom tomatoes, and strawberries.

Local Beverages

Long Island is home to more than 50 vineyards that produce a variety of wines, including red, white, rose, and sparkling wines. Various breweries like Oyster Bay Brewing Company and Garvies Point Brewery offer tasty and unique craft beers.

Where to Stay in Oyster Bay: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Oyster Bay has many options for accommodation, ranging from luxurious hotels to budget hostels and quaint guesthouses. Some of the popular lodging options include:

  • Oheka Castle Hotel & Estate: A luxurious estate with a castle-like structure and scenic gardens.
  • Inn at Fox Hollow Hotel: A five-star rated hotel with lavish suits, a nice restaurant, and great amenities.
  • The Harbor Rose: A charming bed and breakfast, offering cozy and comfortable stays.
  • Youth Hostelling International Oyster Bay: A budget-friendly option with shared dorms and private rooms, well-suited for solo travelers and backpackers.

What to Buy in Oyster Bay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Oyster Bay has many unique and handcrafted products that make perfect souvenirs. Here are some things to consider buying:

  • Handmade soaps from the Sweet Tomato Soap Shop - amazing scents made from natural ingredients.
  • Illuminations by Derynoski: Hand-painted candles with intricate designs.
  • Gourmet Sea Salts by Oyster Bay Salt Co. - artisanal salts made with Oyster Bay’s own briny water.
  • Hand-blown glass ornaments from the Glass Greenhouse - elegant and unique designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Oyster Bay

Oyster Bay is a town that is proud of its heritage and customs. The locals here are famous for their hospitality and welcoming spirit towards tourists. It’s essential to observe common courtesies like not littering the streets and being respectful to locals.

Best Time to Visit Oyster Bay

The best time to visit Oyster Bay is during the months of June through August, when the weather is warm and the natural beauty of this location is at its peak. Winter (December through February) is also a great time to visit if you prefer the cold and the snow.

Do’s and Don’ts of Oyster Bay

Do:

  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Explore the hiking/ biking trails and enjoy the stunning natural beauty of Oyster Bay.
  • Sample the local seafood and wine, as well as other delicacies.
  • Learn the history of this charming town to appreciate its cultural heritage.

Don’t:

  • Litter the streets, parks, or tourist sites.
  • Smoke in public areas, like beaches and parks.
  • Overindulge in alcohol or other substances that impair judgment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Oyster Bay

Oyster Bay is generally a safe town to visit, however, tourists are advised to be cautious at night-time, especially in unlit areas. Always keep your valuables secure and never leave them unattended in public areas.

Health in Oyster Bay

Oyster Bay has several pharmacies and clinics available, and emergency medical services are available if needed. It’s always a good idea to carry a first aid kit while traveling.

Getting to Oyster Bay: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Oyster Bay

The nearest airport to Oyster Bay is John F. Kennedy International Airport (approx. 25 miles) and LaGuardia Airport (approx. 27 miles). From either of these airports, visitors can take a taxi, or arrange for a rental car to get to Oyster Bay.

Bus Stations Near Oyster Bay

The Transdev Long Island operates several bus services to Oyster Bay that originate from locations like Queens, Nassau, and Suffolk counties.

Other Options

Visitors can also opt for train services, as the Long Island Rail Road offers frequent trains to Oyster Bay from New York City.

How to Get Around in Oyster Bay

Local bus services like Nassau Inter-County Express (NICE) run in and around Oyster Bay. Visitors can also rent cars, bikes, or mopeds to explore the town and its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Oyster Bay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Oyster Bay is a gateway to several other exciting tourist destinations in Long Island, such as:

  • The Hamptons: A famous vacation spot for the rich and famous, this location boasts scenic views, luxurious homes, and pristine beaches.
  • Montauk: Home to some of Long Island’s most famous beaches, this laid-back town is perfect for surfing, fishing, and other water sports.
  • Fire Island: This barrier island, known for its scenic beaches, boating, and lively nightlife, is just a short ferry ride from the town.

Conclusion

Oyster Bay is one of the best-kept secrets of Long Island, with its beautiful natural landscapes, fascinating cultural attractions, and delicious local delicacies. The town is perfect for travelers who want to escape the city’s hustle and bustle and slow down to enjoy the natural beauty of a charming small-town. We hope this comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip to Oyster Bay and make the best of your vacation.
