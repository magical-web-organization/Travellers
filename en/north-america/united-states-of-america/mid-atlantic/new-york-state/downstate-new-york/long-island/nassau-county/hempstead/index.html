Introduction to Hempstead

Located in Nassau County, Long Island, Hempstead is a small town that offers a mix of history, outdoor adventures, and cultural attractions. Founded in 1644, Hempstead played a significant role in the development of Long Island and the United States of America. Today, the town is home to several interesting museums, natural wonders, and a lively food and drink scene.

List of Places to Visit in Hempstead

Whether you are interested in history, culture, or nature, Hempstead has a lot to offer. Here are some of the top places to visit in Hempstead:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Long Island Children’s Museum: This museum features interactive exhibits focused on science, art, and culture, designed for children aged 12 and under.

  • Hofstra University Museum of Art: This museum has a vast collection of modern and contemporary art, including works by famous artists such as Jackson Pollock and Mark Rothko.

  • Nassau County Museum of Art: Located in a magnificent mansion, this museum has a vast collection of American and European art, as well as sculptures and decorative arts.

  • Cradle of Aviation Museum: This museum features interactive exhibits and displays focused on the history of aviation, including a restored WWII fighter plane and space shuttles.

  • Old Westbury Gardens: This mansion and gardens are a National Historic Landmark and feature elegant rooms and gardens from the Gilded Age.

Natural Attractions of Hempstead: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jones Beach State Park: This park is a popular spot for swimming, sunbathing, and fishing, with miles of white sandy beaches and a boardwalk.

  • Bethpage State Park: This park is known for its four golf courses, including the famous Black Course, which hosted the U.S. Open in 2002 and 2009.

  • Theodore Roosevelt Sanctuary and Audubon Center: This nature reserve is home to many species of birds and wildlife, as well as eight hiking trails.

  • Merrick Road Park: This park has a paved hiking and biking trail that runs for 7.6 miles, as well as basketball and tennis courts and picnic areas.

  • Eisenhower Park: This park has 930 acres of trails, sports fields, picnic areas, and a golf course.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hempstead

Hempstead has some excellent biking and hiking routes that allow visitors to explore the town’s natural scenery. Some of the popular trails are:

  • Merrick Road Park Trail: This 7.6-mile trail runs from Freeport to Merrick, passing through several parks and scenic spots.

  • Jones Beach State Park Bike Path: This 5.5-mile path runs along the beach and connects several areas of the park.

  • Massapequa Preserve Trail: This 8-mile trail runs through the Massapequa Preserve, a 423-acre park with several ponds and streams, and a diverse ecosystem.

  • Hempstead Harbor Trail: This 3.1-mile trail runs along the shoreline of the Hempstead Harbor, with several scenic views and birdwatching spots.

  • Tackapausha Preserve Trail: This 3.5-mile trail runs through the Tackapausha Preserve, a nature preserve with several hiking trails and wildlife areas.

Best Things to Do in Hempstead: Activities and Tours

Hempstead has many activities and tours designed for visitors looking for a unique experience. Here are some of the best:

  • Boat tour of the Long Island Sound: This boat tour takes visitors along the Long Island Sound and offers stunning views of the surrounding area.

  • Wine tasting at the Raphael Vineyard and Winery: This winery offers tastings of their award-winning wines, as well as tours of the vineyards and cellar.

  • The Hempstead House Murder Mystery: This interactive murder mystery dinner theater takes place in the Hempstead House, where guests can participate in solving the crime.

  • Helicopter Tour of Long Island: This tour takes visitors on a scenic helicopter ride over the Long Island region, offering a unique perspective on the area.

  • Escape Room Adventure: This escape room challenge features several themed rooms with puzzles and riddles to solve.

Food and Drink Scene of Hempstead

Hempstead has a vibrant food and drink scene, featuring many restaurants, cafes, brewpubs, and wineries. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lobster Roll: This classic New England dish is a favorite at many seafood restaurants in Hempstead.

  • Pastrami sandwich: This Jewish delicacy is available at many delis and restaurants in Hempstead.

  • Bagels and Lox: This traditional Jewish breakfast dish is a favorite at many bagel shops in Hempstead.

  • Pork Ribs: This classic American BBQ dish is available at many restaurants in Hempstead.

  • Pizza: New York-style pizza is famous around the world and is available at many pizzerias in Hempstead.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Long Island has several craft breweries, many of which are located in Hempstead, such as Barrier Brewing Company and Oyster Bay Brewing Company.

  • Wine: Long Island has a thriving wine scene, with several vineyards and wineries located in Hempstead, such as Raphael Vineyard and Winery and Channing Daughters Winery.

  • Cider: Jericho Cider Mill is a popular spot for fresh cider and apple-based treats.

Local Culture and Customs of Hempstead

Hempstead has a diverse population, with people from several ethnic and cultural backgrounds. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, including:

  • Religion: Many residents of Hempstead practice minority religions, such as Hinduism, Buddhism, and Islam. Visitors should be mindful of cultural and religious practices when interacting with locals.

  • Language: English is the most common language spoken in Hempstead, but many residents also speak Spanish, Chinese, and other languages.

  • Etiquette: Visitors should be respectful of local etiquette, including tipping at restaurants, being punctual for appointments, and being polite and courteous in public.

Best Time to Visit Hempstead

The best time to visit Hempstead is during the summer months, when the weather is warm, and the outdoor activities are at their peak. Spring and fall are also good times to visit, with comfortable temperatures and fewer crowds. Winter can be cold and snowy, but many indoor attractions and events can still be enjoyed.

Safety and Health

Hempstead is a relatively safe town, with a low crime rate. However, visitors should still take precautions when exploring the town.

Safety in Hempstead

  • Be aware of your surroundings when walking or hiking.

  • Avoid walking alone at night in areas with little foot traffic.

  • Keep valuables out of sight, and lock your car and hotel room when leaving.

Health in Hempstead

  • Hempstead has several hospitals and medical facilities that offer urgent care and emergency services.

  • Visitors should always carry their health insurance cards and any necessary medication.

  • Wear sunscreen when spending time outdoors, particularly during the summer months.

Other Options

How to Get Around in Hempstead

Hempstead is relatively small and easy to navigate by car or bike. Visitors can also use local buses, taxis, or ride-sharing services to get around the town. Walking is also a popular option, particularly in downtown areas and parks.
