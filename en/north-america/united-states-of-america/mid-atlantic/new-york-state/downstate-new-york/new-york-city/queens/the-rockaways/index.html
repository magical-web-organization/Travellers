Introduction to The Rockaways

Nestled in the borough of Queens in New York City, The Rockaways boast an array of attractions, from sand-duned beaches, bustling boardwalks, historical landmarks, to outdoor adventures that appeal to every type of traveler.

List of Places to Visit in The Rockaways

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Explore The Rockaways’ rich history by visiting museum exhibits and historical landmarks. The Rockaways are home to the oldest public golf course in the United States, The Rockaway Beach Golf Club, which dates back to 1901. Also worth a visit is the Rockaway Beach Fire Department Museum which gives an extensive insight into the history of the RBF.

Natural Attractions of The Rockaways: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Rockaways feature a range of natural wonders, perfect for those who love the great outdoors. Spend a day on the beach enjoy the sun and surf. For a scenic view of the city, head to Beach 17th Street, where you’ll find a striking mural that brings together the neighborhood’s cultural roots, vibrant history and natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of The Rockaways

The Rockaways have several trails fit for hiking and biking. For a chance to soak up stunning coastal views of the Atalntic Ocean head down to the Rockaway Network Greenway, the popular beachside trail.

Best Things to Do in The Rockaways: Activities and Tours

Activities and tours in The Rockaways offer visitors a chance to explore and experience the area’s rich culture and natural beauty. Fishing charters, sailing, surfing, and paddleboarding are just some of the activities available.

Week-Long Itinerary for The Rockaways

Most people wouldn’t think of spending an entire week in The Rockaways - but trust us, you won’t regret a weeklong stay. Start off your first day at the beach, and soak up the sun and scenery. On day two, explore famous landmarks and visit museums. Day three can be adventurous by renting surfboards or bikes for a little exercise. On day four try the locally produced cuisine; our takes on Irish, Latino, and comfort food will awaken your senses. Take your family to splash or enjoy the rides at Luna Park on the fifth day. The rest of the week in The Rockaways promises to be equally as satisfying and memorable.

Food and Drink Scene of The Rockaways

Must-Try Dishes

The coming together of different cultures in The Rockaways has resulted in a mix of rich and delicious flavors. Some of the must-try dishes include the famous Irish-American burger from Donovan’s, classic pizzas from Uma’s, and seafood delight at The Palms.

Local Beverages

Manhattan has its martinis, Brooklyn its craft breweries, and The Rockaways have delightfully delicious locally made cocktails as the best drink for on sunny or laid back afternoons. Opt for a frozen Margherita at Connollys Bar, or sip a pina colada slurpee at Rippers.

Where to Stay in The Rockaways: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Rockaways offer various accommodation options. For a luxurious stay, we recommend booking at The Rockaway Hotel, with their rooftop pool and premium restaurant. For affordable yet comfortable lodgings, try Ocean Bay Hotel.

What to Buy in The Rockaways: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take home a piece of The Rockaways with you by buying local art, beach niceties, or fashion printed shirts. Visit Hoax, where you’ll find locally made T-shirts and jewelry; The Rockaway Artist Alliance, for unique artwork, an made-to-order stuff at Memories of Home & Hope and Wave t-Shirts.

Local Culture and Customs of The Rockaways

The Rockaways has a unique variety of cultures, each with its distinct set of customs. The locals are friendly, always up for a chat, and the community is known for rallying round when needed.

Best Time to Visit The Rockaways

The best time to visit is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and perfect for beach days and outdoor activities. The winter months can be harsh and cold, so avoid them when planning your trip.

Do’s and Don’ts of The Rockaways

Do:

  • Remember to wear your sunscreen when you plan to spend time at the beach.

  • Visit the famous surfing spots to catch some waves.

  • Try the wide variety of local cuisine and enjoy cocktails with beach views.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beaches or in any public space.

  • Forget to pack a pair of comfortable shoes for hiking or biking.

  • Disrespect locals, their traditions and culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in The Rockaways

The Rockaways is a safe place for visitors, but it’s always advisable to be cautious as with any major city in the U.S. Make sure to keep your belongings close while on the beach, especially if you’re going for a swim. Coastal emergencies are dealt with swiftly with the ample lifeguards on duty.

Health in The Rockaways

The Rockaways have access to various medical facilities, with St. John’s Episcopal Hospital as the primary medical center. However, it is essential to ensure you have medical insurance while traveling to the United States.

Getting to The Rockaways: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near The Rockaways

The Rockaways are accessible by public transportation from both JFK International Airport, located on by AirTrain; and LaGuardia Airport by taking Q53 bus to Woodhaven Boulevard Station.

Bus Stations Near The Rockaways

The Via bus operates on-demand transportation, while The NYC Transit bus operates seven local routes, with stops along most of the main street of The Rockaways.

Other Options

You can hail any of the city’s yellow taxis to get to or from the Rockaways as well.

How to Get Around in The Rockaways

The Rockaways offers a cluster of transportation options including NYC Transit, free seasonal beach shuttles, and bicycles.

Where to Go Next from The Rockaways: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Make use of your time in Rockaway by exploring the nearby neighborhoods like the surf haven in Montauk and the happening beaches of Cape May. Take the time to see other popular New York City neighborhoods like Soho, Williamsburg, and Harlem.

Conclusion

The Rockaways is without a doubt, a popular destination that’s steeped in history, culture, and adventure. With its delightful beaches, peaceful neighborhoods, and fascinating landmarks, make sure to plan an extended stay. From your daily outdoor activities, local beaches, and lovely cuisine – you’ll be glad you made the trip. Book your vacation now and let yourself be swept away by the magic of The Rockaways.
