Introduction to Jackson Heights

Located in Queens, New York City, Jackson Heights is a culturally-rich neighborhood that offers visitors a glimpse into the diverse communities that call this city home. With bustling commercial streets lined with shops, markets, and restaurants, there’s plenty to explore here. This travel guide will give you an overview of the must-see attractions, top things to do, and local cuisine that you should not miss during your visit to Jackson Heights.

List of Places to Visit in Jackson Heights

There are several culturally important attractions and natural landmarks in Jackson Heights that you should add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Louis Armstrong House Museum: This museum is dedicated to the life and legacy of the legendary jazz musician, Louis Armstrong, who lived in this house until his passing. You can take a guided tour of the home to see some of Armstrong’s personal belongings, as well as learn about his musical career and impact on the jazz scene.

  • Queens Museum: The Queens Museum has a range of exhibits showcasing art and history about NYC. Its dedication is to reflect the diversity of Queens and its residents; it’s a perfect introduction for first-timers in NYC.

  • Flushing Meadows-Corona Park: Home to the 1964 World’s Fair, this expansive park boasts plenty of attractions and activities for visitors. You can visit the New York Hall of Science or Queens Zoo, take a stroll around the iconic Unisphere, or enjoy a picnic on the Great Lawn.

Natural Attractions of Jackson Heights: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Travers Park: This park is located in the heart of Jackson Heights and has plenty of green spaces for you to relax in. With fun features such as playgrounds, a swimming pool, and basketball courts, it’s perfect for spending an afternoon with the family.

  • Roosevelt Island: There are breathtaking views of Manhattan’s skyline from this tiny island close to Jackson Heights. Take the scenic tramway that links the island to Manhattan and witness one of the few public spaces in NYC, with lush parks, recreational activities, and cultural sites.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jackson Heights

While there are no specific hiking or biking routes in Jackson Heights, there are a few nearby options such as Flushing Meadows-Corona Park and Astoria Park. Walk or bike the manicured and scenic paths throughout these parks – some of the most extensive parks in Queens.

Best Things to Do in Jackson Heights: Activities and Tours

  • Food Tour: Take a food tour of Jackson Heights and try out delicious cuisine from different cultures, such as Latin America, India, and South Asia. Taste seasonal dishes, learn about the local markets, and pick up tips for dining like a local.

  • Shop at Roosevelt Avenue: Discover new things in Jackson Heights with some unique items at affordable prices in Roosevelt Avenue, one of the main commercial streets in the neighborhood.

  • Visit the Jackson Heights Greenmarket: Ride over to the Sunday farmer’s market in Travers Park and pick up fresh fruits, vegetables, and other local produce. This greenmarket is open all year-round and presents many vendors’ local products.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jackson Heights

Day 1: Visit Louis Armstrong House Museum and then have lunch at a Mexican restaurant on Roosevelt Avenue. Shop around, and experience the diversity of the neighborhood.

Day 2: Enjoy outdoor activities at Flushing Meadows Corona Park, like the Queens Botanical Garden, the Queens Museum, New York Hall of Science, or dine in various restaurants located around the park.

Day 3: Participate in one of our food tours of Jackson Heights, where you will taste and learn about the various ethnic cuisines and their culture.

Day 4: Take the scenic tram to Roosevelt Island – experience one of the few public spaces in NYC, and have amazing views of the skyline and visit cultural sites.

Day 5: Visit the weekly farmer’s market at Travers Park and experience local products and vendors. Hop over to nearby Astoria Park and walk by the scenic paths around the water to enjoy the sunset.

Day 6: Shop at Jackson Heights Greenmarket, and then have the opportunity of exploring some of the lesser-known landmarks, street art, and murals around Jackson Heights.

Day 7: Go for a bike ride around Northern Boulevard and then have lunch at the famous Joe’s Pizzeria, known for its thin crust pizza.

Food and Drink Scene of Jackson Heights

Jackson Heights offers a diverse range of cuisines comprising a mix of South Asian, Mexican, South American and Caribbean. It is a melting pot of cultures and traditions, resulting in various types of street foods that you should try at some iconic places in the neighborhood.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Arepa: A cornmeal tortilla stuffed with cheese, meat, and vegetables. Try it at the Arepa Lady on Roosevelt Ave.

  • Dosa: This crispy crepe is made of rice and lentils and is a staple in Southern India. Visit Jackson Diner and try one with coconut chutney.

  • Enchiladas: Rolled tortillas filled with beef or chicken, and topped with rich tomato sauce and melted cheese. Try them at Taqueria Coatzingo on 76th Street.

Local Beverages

  • Horchatas: A refreshing drink made of rice or almonds, vanilla, and cinnamon. Sip it at Coatzingo bakery on Roosevelt Ave.

  • Agua de Jamaica: This hibiscus-flavored drink is a perfect refresher on a hot day. Try it at the Food Stall on 37th Avenue and 78th Street.

Where to Stay in Jackson Heights: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There aren’t many options for staying in Jackson Heights, but you can find hotels, hostels, and guesthouses nearby. Some of the best accommodations in the area include the Akwaaba Mansion Bed & Breakfast, Holiday Inn LaGuardia Airport, and The Local NYC.

What to Buy in Jackson Heights: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are plenty of local products and souvenirs in Jackson Heights that you can bring home, such as:

  • Mexican Ceramic Plates: Colorful, hand-painted plates that are perfect for serving. Buy them at Dolores Gift Shop (37th Avenue).

  • Tortilla Press: Make your own fresh tortillas with a tortilla press - buy one for yourself or as a gift at La Marqueta.

Local Culture and Customs of Jackson Heights

Overall, the culture of Jackson Heights is very hospitable, and the community takes pride in its diversity. Residents and local businesses welcome guests with open arms and are willing to share their stories and culture. It is advised that visitors respect and appreciate the various cuisines offered, languages spoken, and festivals celebrated.

Best Time to Visit Jackson Heights

The best time to visit Jackson Heights is in the summer months. You can enjoy outdoor activities, such as lounging in the park, biking, and attending street fairs. The weather is generally mild and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jackson Heights

Do:

  • Try the street food and explore the diversity of local shops and markets.
  • Be respectful of the cultural differences and diversity within the neighborhood.
  • Take a food tour and learn about the rich cultural origins of the dishes.

Don’t:

  • Be loud and disruptive in the neighborhood.
  • Leave garbage or litter behind.
  • Ignore the cultural richness of Jackson Heights.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jackson Heights

Jackson Heights is generally safe, but visitors should be wary of pickpockets and scams. It is best to avoid walking around Jackson Heights late at night.

Health in Jackson Heights

There are plenty of pharmacies, urgent care facilities and hospitals in the Queens area, but it’s always best to have travel insurance when going anywhere unknown.

Getting to Jackson Heights: Transportation Options and Logistics

Jackson Heights is relatively easy to get to, with various transportation options and services available:

Airports Near Jackson Heights

  • John F. Kennedy International Airport – 15 miles away
  • LaGuardia Airport – 4 miles away

Bus Stations Near Jackson Heights

  • Port Authority Bus Terminal – 10 miles away
  • Greyhound Bus Station – 9 miles away

Other Options

  • Subway (E, F, M, R trains) directly connected to Manhattan and Brooklyn.
  • Ride-hailing services such as Uber or Lyft.

How to Get Around in Jackson Heights

Jackson Heights is a relatively small neighborhood and easy to navigate on foot. Biking is also a popular option, and Citi Bike stations are located throughout Queens.

Where to Go Next from Jackson Heights: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Astoria: Located a short distance from Jackson Heights, Astoria offers a range of activities and attractions. You can visit its beaches, go shopping, or dine at one of its many restaurants.

  • Long Island City: This area just a few miles away from Jackson Heights offers magnificent views of Manhattan, artsy vibes, delightful markets, and riverside parks.

Conclusion

Jackson Heights is a must-visit neighborhood in New York City and a fun place to explore due to its mix of cultures, cuisines, and activities. Make sure to visit the famous landmarks, try different ethnic foods, and participate in local tours to get the most out of your visit. With a good balance of activities, food, and accommodation options, Jackson Heights is a perfect addition to your NYC itinerary.
