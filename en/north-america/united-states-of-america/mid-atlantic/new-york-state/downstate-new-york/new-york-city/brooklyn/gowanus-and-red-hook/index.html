Introduction to Gowanus and Red Hook

Gowanus and Red Hook are two neighborhoods located on the west side of Brooklyn, in New York City. These two once industrial neighborhoods are now home to creative hubs, historic architecture, outdoor activities, and a thriving food and drink scene. Gowanus and Red Hook are a perfect getaway for travelers who seek a different experience that goes beyond the touristy spots in New York City.

List of Places to Visit in Gowanus and Red Hook

There’s no shortage of things to do or places to visit in Gowanus and Red Hook. From museums and historical sites to outdoor adventures and scenic views, the neighborhoods offer a great variety of attractions to visitors.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Brooklyn Museum: A world-renowned museum that showcases a vast collection of art and artifacts, ranging from ancient Egyptian masterpieces to contemporary art.
  • Museum of Contemporary African Diasporan Arts (MoCADA): A gallery that highlights contemporary African art and culture.
  • Lighthouse Museum: Located in the landmarked 1820s Keeper’s House, this museum tells the story of lighthouse keepers and the maritime history of Brooklyn and New York City.
  • The Transit Museum: Housed in a decommissioned subway station, this museum displays the history of New York City’s subway, bus, and commuter rail systems.

Natural Attractions of Gowanus and Red Hook: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Red Hook Waterfront: A scenic waterfront area with parks, piers, and great views of the Statue of Liberty and the New York Harbor.
  • Columbia Waterfront District: A residential area with several parks, green spaces, and a promenade with fantastic views of Manhattan.
  • Gowanus Canal: Despite its rather infamous reputation as one of the most polluted waterways in the US, the canal’s industrial landscape has attracted artists and photographers alike and it has become famous for its rainbow-colored oil slicks caused by bacteria blooms.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gowanus and Red Hook

Gowanus and Red Hook offer excellent options for hiking and biking enthusiasts who want to explore the green spaces of the neighborhoods.

  • Red Hook Waterfront Greenway: A scenic route that follows the waterfront from Red Hook to Sunset Park.
  • Brooklyn Greenway Initiative: A network of bike paths and green spaces that connect neighborhoods and parks across Brooklyn.
  • Green-Wood Cemetery: A National Historic Landmark full of ornate tombs, sculptures, trees and gardens, that offers a peaceful respite from the thriving city.

Best Things to Do in Gowanus and Red Hook: Activities and Tours

Gowanus and Red Hook offer plenty of opportunities for visitors to get hands-on experience and participate in activities.

  • Brooklyn Crab: A trendy seafood shack with mini-golf and horseshoe pits on the Red Hook waterfront.
  • Royal Palms Shuffleboard Club: An indoor shuffleboard court that offers cocktails, food, and a chance to relax and socialize with friends.
  • Brooklyn Winery: A winery and event space that offers tours, wine tastings, and a chance to learn about winemaking.

Food and Drink Scene of Gowanus and Red Hook

Gowanus and Red Hook have become a foodie’s paradise, with restaurants, bars, and breweries offering a unique dining experience.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Korean Fried Chicken from Insa: This Brooklyn favorite offers juicy and crispy fried chicken served with a variety of sauces.
  • Po’boys from Lobster Joint: A New England take on the Louisiana sandwich featuring shrimp and lobster.
  • Burgers from Brooklyn Burger Joint: Mouthwatering burgers with unique toppings such as kimchi, avocado, and crispy onions.

Local Beverages

  • Sixpoint Brewery: A local brewery that offers a variety of beers, including its popular Sweet Action ale.
  • Red Hook Winery: A winery that produces artisanal wines with grapes sourced from the North Fork of Long Island and the Finger Lakes region.
  • Brooklyn Farmacy & Soda Fountain: A vintage soda fountain that serves old-fashioned sodas and milkshakes.

Where to Stay in Gowanus and Red Hook: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Gowanus Inn & Yard: A trendy boutique hotel with a relaxed atmosphere and outdoor space.
  • Red Hook Hotel: A comfortable hotel with a prime location for exploring the neighborhood.
  • The Lookout Guesthouse: A beautifully-restored guesthouse with a stunning rooftop terrace that offers incredible skyline views.

What to Buy in Gowanus and Red Hook: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Brooklyn Dark Chocolate Espresso Beans: A chocolate-covered coffee bean snack that makes a perfect souvenir for coffee lovers.
  • Gowanus Canal Eau de Parfum: A unisex fragrance inspired by the scent of the infamous Gowanus Canal.
  • Brooklyn Brewery Beer Jelly: A spreadable jelly made with the brewery’s signature Brooklyn Lager that pairs well with cheese and bread.

Local Culture and Customs of Gowanus and Red Hook

  • Art and Culture: Gowanus and Red Hook are home to numerous art galleries and studios, and cultural institutions like MoCADA, and the Brooklyn Museum.
  • Sustainability: The neighborhoods have a strong commitment to sustainability, visible among transportation alternatives, local markets, and green spaces.
  • Reclaimed Architecture: Gowanus and Red Hook have utilized the former industrial architecture to create modern and innovative living spaces, creative venues, and local shops.

Best Time to Visit Gowanus and Red Hook

The best time to visit Gowanus and Red Hook is during the spring and fall when the weather is mild, and the outdoors areas are at their best. The winter and summer months could be harsh, but they offer indoor activities such as museums and warm restaurants.

Gowanus and Red Hook are both walkable neighborhoods and offer an excellent opportunity for cycling enthusiasts. Taxi and ride-sharing services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Gowanus and Red Hook: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Make Gowanus and Red Hook your next destination and explore it like a local.
