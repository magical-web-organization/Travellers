Introduction to Downtown

Located in the heart of Brooklyn, Downtown is a bustling urban area that boasts a unique blend of cultures, cuisines, and architecture. The area is a hub for business and government, with many offices and courthouses located here. Downtown’s central location also makes it an excellent base for exploring the rest of Brooklyn and New York City. The area is rich in history, with many museums and historical landmarks scattered throughout the neighborhood. In this comprehensive guide, we will explore the best things to do in Downtown, Brooklyn, and how to make the most of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Downtown

Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventure, cultural attractions, or delicious food, Downtown has it all. Here are our top picks for the best places to visit in Downtown:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Downtown is rich in culture, with many museums and historical landmarks scattered throughout the neighborhood. Here are some of the must-visit cultural attractions:

  • Brooklyn Museum: A massive art museum with over 1.5 million works.
  • Brooklyn Historical Society Museum: The museum features exhibits and artifacts that tell the story of Brooklyn.
  • New York Transit Museum: Located in an old subway station, this museum is a must-visit for transportation enthusiasts and history buffs alike.
  • Federal Reserve Bank of New York: Located in a stunning Beaux-Arts building, you can take a guided tour and learn about the history of American banking.

Natural Attractions of Downtown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

While Downtown is primarily an urban area, there are still plenty of outdoor adventures to be had. Here are some of our favorite natural attractions:

  • Brooklyn Bridge Park: A stunning waterfront park that offers unbeatable views of the Manhattan skyline.
  • MetroTech Center: A beautiful urban plaza with art installations and seasonal programming.
  • Brooklyn Heights Promenade: One of the best spots in the city to take in views of the Manhattan skyline.
  • Cadman Plaza Park: A public park that offers its visitors a spacious spot to take a break from the hustle and bustle of town.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Downtown

For visitors looking to get out and explore nature, Downtownoffers many excellent hiking and biking trails. Here are some of our favorite routes:

  • The Brooklyn Greenway: A scenic 14-mile trail that winds through waterfront neighborhoods, parks, and historic sites.
  • Brooklyn Bridge Walkway: While not a designated bike path, the walkway offers stunning views of Downtown, Brooklyn, and Manhattan.
  • The Transmitter Park: A small, scenic park with views of the Manhattan skyline and the Pulaski Bridge.

Best Things to Do in Downtown: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re a history buff, an art lover, or a foodie, there are plenty of activities and tours in Downtown that cater to every interest. Here are some of our favorite things to do:

  • Take a walking tour of historic Brooklyn Heights and learn about the neighborhood’s fascinating history.
  • Visit Juniors Restaurant and Bakery for a taste of New York-style cheesecake.
  • Visit the Brooklyn Flea & Smorgasburg to shop for vintage and artisanal goods and savor some of the city’s best food.
  • Take a Sunset Sail or NYC Lights Cruise to experience Manhattan’s beauty from the water.

Food and Drink Scene of Downtown

Downtown’s food scene is rich in diversity, with a range of cuisines for every taste preference. Here’s what you have to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • New York-style pizza from Grimaldi’s Pizzeria.
  • Classic New York-style bagel with lox from Terrace Bagels.
  • The famous cheesecake from Juniors Restaurant and Bakery.
  • Burger from Shake Shack, the known landmark of Flatbush Avenue.

Local Beverages

  • A cup of Jo from Devocion (Colombian Coffee).
  • A pint of Brooklyn Lager from Brooklyn Brewery.
  • Unique Cocktails at the Brooklyn Social.

Where to Stay in Downtown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Downtown has no shortage of accommodation options available, whether you’re looking for a luxury hotel or a budget-friendly hostel. Here are some of our top picks.

  • 1 Hotel Brooklyn Bridge: A stylish and eco-friendly hotel with unparalleled views of the Brooklyn Bridge and Manhattan skyline.
  • Sheraton Brooklyn New York hotel: A convenient, modern hotel in Downtown Brooklyn.
  • The Tillary Hotel Brooklyn: A boutique hotel located in the heart of the city, close to many attractions.

What to Buy in Downtown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Brooklyn is home to many unique, locally-made products that make for perfect souvenirs or gifts. Here are some of our favorite items:

  • Mast Brothers Chocolate: Artisanal chocolate made in Brooklyn.
  • Brooklyn-made hot sauce, from small-batch producers like Queen Majesty and Brooklyn Grange.
  • Merchandise from the Brooklyn Flea Market.

Local Culture and Customs of Downtown

Brooklyn has a rich history and a unique culture, and Downtown is no exception. Here are some customs and cultural points you should know:

  • Brooklynites tend to stick to the right-hand side of sidewalks to allow fast walkers to pass on the left.
  • Do not litter: the trash cans are every few blocks.
  • Tip your server at least 20%.
  • Use a metro card to get yourself around, cab rides are expensive.

Best Time to Visit Downtown

The best time to visit Downtown is during the shoulder seasons (spring and fall) when the weather is mild and tourist crowds are fewer. You can also enjoy many events and activities happening in Downtown throughout these seasons, such as open-air concerts, fairs, and farmers markets.

Do’s and Don’ts of Downtown

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while exploring Downtown:

Do:

  • Explore neighborhoods outside of Downtown, like Williamsburg and Park Slope.
  • Tip well, especially in restaurants.
  • Use public transportation wisely.
  • Carry cash.

Don’t:

  • Toss your trash in the street.
  • Take a swig of your drink before finishing the one before it in pair with stopping by some of the best bars in Brooklyn.

Safety and Health

As with any major city, safety and health are essential priorities. Here are some tips to keep in mind while exploring Downtown:

Safety in Downtown

  • Avoid walking alone at night, especially in secluded areas.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and any suspicious individuals or behaviors.
  • Be cautious when crossing the street, and always use crosswalks.

Health in Downtown

  • Use sunscreen to protect yourself from the sun’s harmful rays.
  • Stay hydrated, especially in the heat of the summer.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and the activities you have planned.

Getting to Downtown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Downtown can be easily accessed by public transportation. Here are some options:

Airports Near Downtown

  • John F. Kennedy International Airport (JFK)
  • LaGuardia Airport (LGA)
  • Newark Airport (EWR)

Bus Stations Near Downtown

  • Port Authority Bus Terminal
  • Greyhound Bus Terminal

Other Options

  • Amtrak to Atlantic Terminal in Brooklyn.
  • Subway - Take lines 2/3 or R from Manhattan and Q/C from Midtown Manhattan.

How to Get Around in Downtown

The best way to get around Downtown is on foot, as many attractions, restaurants, and shops are conveniently located within walking distance. CitiBike bike share system is also an excellent option. Subway trains L,M, K, N, Q and R are conveniently close to many Downtown attractions.

Where to Go Next from Downtown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Downtown is an excellent base for exploring other parts of Brooklyn and New York City. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions worth checking out:

  • Williamsburg: A vibrant neighborhood filled with trendy bars, restaurants, and boutiques.
  • DUMBO: Known for its iconic views of the Brooklyn Bridge and promenade, it’s a great place to shop, eat, and take breathtaking photos.
  • Coney Island: An iconic boardwalk with an amusement park and beach.
  • Prospect Park: A huge 585-acre public park with a zoo, museum, and botanical garden.

Conclusion

Downtown, Brooklyn, is a unique and vibrant place to visit, with plenty of cultural attractions, outdoor adventure, and delicious food to explore. With this comprehensive guide, you’re equipped to make the most of your trip to downtown, so you can take in the best of what this historic neighborhood has to offer.
