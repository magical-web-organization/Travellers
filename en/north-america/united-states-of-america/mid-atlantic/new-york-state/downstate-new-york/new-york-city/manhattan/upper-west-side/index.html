Introduction to Upper West Side

Upper West Side, situated in Manhattan, New York City, is a bustling, metropolitan hub where a global sense of culture meets the quintessential “New Yorker” way of life. This uptown community stretches from Central Park West to the Hudson River and is bordered by West 59th Street and West 110th Street.

The Upper West Side offers visitors a plethora of activities to enjoy, from the vast cultural sights and museums to natural attractions and scenic hikes, there’s always something to suit every kind of traveler. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a detailed look at the best places to visit, eat, stay, and things to do in Upper West Side.

List of Places to Visit in Upper West Side

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Upper West Side is renowned for its cultural and historical significance in the USA. The area has several famous landmarks which are worth exploring.

American Museum of Natural History

This museum is a two-block walk from Central Park. It showcases an extensive collection of scientific and cultural exhibits, including dinosaur fossils, space, and ocean exploration displays, an IMAX theater, and a planetarium.

Lincoln Center for the Performing Arts

It is a leading performing arts center in New York that hosts world-class events, including live shows, concerts, operas, and ballets. The center covers a vast area and features several theatres, including the famous Metropolitan Opera House.

Cathedral of St. John the Divine

This is one of the most magnificent and largest Gothic Revival churches in the world. The Cathedral of St. John the Divine features beautiful stained-glass windows, towering spires, intricate stonework, and unique architecture.

Natural Attractions of Upper West Side: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Central Park

Central Park spans over 843 acres of lush greenery and is the most significant public park in Manhattan. The park provides various recreational activities such as biking, horseback riding, hiking, boat rides, picnicking, and more. It also has several iconic landmarks such as the Bethesda Fountain and the famous Central Park Zoo.

Riverside Park

Riverside Park is a scenic 330-acre waterfront park stretching from 72nd to 158th Street along the Hudson River. This park houses many recreational facilities such as bike trails, picnic areas, playgrounds, and volleyball courts. It also offers stunning views of the George Washington Bridge and the Palisades.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Upper West Side

The Upper West Side provides many opportunities for outdoor enthusiasts to get some exercise and enjoy the serene surroundings.

Riverside Park Greenway

This is a beautiful trail ideal for biking enthusiasts. The trail offers breathtaking views of the Hudson River while winding along the waterfront, designed for transportation and recreation. It is an 11-mile-long path that extends from West 72nd to West 135th Street.

Central Park Loop

Central Park’s famous Central Loop is a must-do for both cyclists and joggers. The loop is approximately 6.1 miles in length, and you can wind your way through an array of wooded trails, lakes, hills, open fields, and more.

Best Things to Do in Upper West Side: Activities and Tours

Big Onion Walking Tours

Big Onion Walking Tours are perfect for individuals interested in exploring the history of the Upper West Side. The two-hour tour covers many famous landmarks such as Central Park, Riverside Drive, The Jewish Museum, and others.

Pinch Food Design Culinary Tours

Take a culinary tour with Pinch Food Design and discover the food scene of Upper West Side. They offer a three-hour tour through the local restaurants and food shops to explore the delicious food offered in the Upper West Side community.

Week-Long Itinerary for Upper West Side

Day 1: Visit the American Museum of Natural History.

Day 2: Take a stroll through the iconic Central Park and enjoy the beautiful scenery.

Day 3: Take a Pinch Food Design Culinary Tour of the Upper West Side.

Day 4: Check out the cultural significance of the Lincoln Center for the Performing Arts.

Day 5: Visit the Cathedral of St. John the Divine.

Day 6: Explore the scenic views of the Riverside Park Greenway.

Day 7: Spend a day shopping around the Upper West Side’s most prestigious stores.

Food and Drink Scene of Upper West Side

Must-Try Dishes

Levain Bakery cookies

Levain Bakery is known for its delicious freshly baked cookies, which are a must-try while you’re in the Upper West Side.

Pastrami on Rye Sandwich

The Upper West Side has many deli shops that offer a wide variety of sandwiches. A classic pastrami on rye is a must-try when you’re in the area.

Local Beverages

Shake Shack’s Local Craft Beer

Shake shack serves up local brews that you can enjoy while snacking on their delicious burgers and fries.

Maison Pickle’s Pickle Martini

Maison Pickle offers a unique beverage that features pickle juice and gin. This pickle martini is a must-try for adventurous drinkers.

Where to Stay in Upper West Side: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Empire Hotel

The Empire Hotel is located just one block away from Lincoln Center and features a rooftop pool where guests can unwind and enjoy the stunning views.

Marrakech Hotel

The Marrakech Hotel has a unique Moroccan décor, and its rooms are beautiful and comfortable. The hotel is located in the Upper West Side’s heart and is perfect for those looking to stay within a budget.

What to Buy in Upper West Side: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Zabar’s Local Delicacies

Zabar’s is a famous Upper West Side store selling high-quality food products and souvenirs, mainly featuring smoked fish, cheeses, deli meats, and more.

Local Culture and Customs of Upper West Side

The Upper West Side community has a strong sense of intellectualism and liberalism. The area is known for maintaining many Jewish traditions, and the cultural environment is very welcoming and accepting of diverse perspectives.

Best Time to Visit Upper West Side

The best time to visit the Upper West Side is from April through June and September through November. The weather is pleasant and comfortable during these months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Upper West Side

Do:

  • Dress appropriately for the event or occasion
  • Tip servers and waiters for their services
  • Use the appropriate crosswalks to avoid accidents

Don’t:

  • Block the sidewalk while walking
  • Litter in public spaces
  • Violate noise ordinances

Safety and Health

Safety in Upper West Side

Upper West Side is a relatively safe community to walk and explore. It is always best to remain alert and aware of your surroundings, especially after dark.

Health in Upper West Side

The Upper West Side has many healthcare facilities that offer high-quality medical services. It is advisable to travel with basic travel insurance in case you require emergency medical services.

How to Get Around in Upper West Side

The Upper West Side offers several transportation options, including buses, subway trains, and taxi cabs. The most affordable and efficient way to get around the city is by using the subway.

Where to Go Next from Upper West Side: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Times Square

Times Square is one of the most iconic neighborhoods in New York and is located just a few minutes away from the Upper West Side. The area has a plethora of attractions, and it’s always bustling with activities and energy.

The High Line

The High Line is a beautiful walking trail built on an old elevated railway track, offering incredible views of the surrounding cityscape. It is a unique and stunning way to explore the New York landscape.

Conclusion

The Upper West Side is a bustling community that has something to offer every traveler. Whether you’re interested in history and culture or outdoor activities and scenic views, this area will not disappoint. We hope our comprehensive guide helps you experience the very best that Upper West Side has to offer.
