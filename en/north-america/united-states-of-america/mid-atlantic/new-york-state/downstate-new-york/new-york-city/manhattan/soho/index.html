SoHo, short for South of Houston, is a trendy neighborhood in Manhattan known for its upscale shopping, art galleries, and restaurants. It is a popular destination for tourists and locals alike, and boasts a vibrant cultural scene. This guide covers everything you need to know to make the most of your trip to SoHo, including a list of places to visit, food and drink scene, where to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in SoHo

SoHo has a plethora of attractions that cater to a range of interests. Here are some of the must-see places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Museum of Modern Art (MoMA) - which houses some of the world’s finest masterpieces, featuring works by artists such as Van Gogh, Monet, and Picasso.
  • The Leslie-Lohman Museum of Art - which showcases queer art and culture.
  • The New York City Fire Museum - featuring the historical development of firefighting in New York City.

Natural Attractions of SoHo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • SoHo Walking Tour - walk through the streets of SoHo and discover hidden gems such as street art, trendy boutiques, and peaceful parks.
  • The High Line - a mile-long elevated public park built on a former railway, offering stunning views of the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of SoHo

  • Hudson River Greenway - an 11-mile biking trail along the Hudson River, featuring breathtaking views, parks, and art installations.
  • Central Park - located in the heart of New York City, Central Park has miles of biking and hiking trails, as well as gardens, lakes, and historical landmarks.

Best Things to Do in SoHo: Activities and Tours

  • The SoHo Food Tour - discover the culinary delights of SoHo’s diverse food scene, including tacos, pizza, bagels, and more.
  • Shopping in SoHo - explore the many unique boutiques, designer stores, and high-end shops that line the streets of SoHo.

Week-Long Itinerary for SoHo

Here is an ideal week-long itinerary for your trip to SoHo:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Museum of Modern Art (MoMA)
Day 2 Walk the streets of SoHo and take in the street art, trendy boutiques, and peaceful parks
Day 3 Explore the High Line Public Park and Chelsea Piers
Day 4 Take a Food Tour around SoHo and try some of the best dishes in the area
Day 5 Go shopping in SoHo and visit some of the designer stores and boutiques
Day 6 Take a bike ride through Central Park and enjoy the scenery
Day 7 Relax in one of SoHo’s rooftop bars and enjoy a cocktail while taking in the views

Food and Drink Scene of SoHo

SoHo boasts a diverse food scene, with plenty of options to suit any palate. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pizza at Prince Street Pizza - a classic New York slice enjoyed by locals and tourists alike.
  • Lobster Rolls at Pearl Oyster Bar - a quintessential East Coast dish, stuffed with fresh lobster meat.
  • Bagels at Russ & Daughters - this famous deli has been serving up fresh bagels and smoked salmon since 1914.

Local Beverages

  • Coffee at Everyman Espresso - a cozy cafe with a great selection of coffee and pastries.
  • Beer at The Ear Inn - a historic bar with a great selection of local brews.
  • Cocktail at Pegu Club - an upscale cocktail bar with creative drinks and a retro atmosphere.

Where to Stay in SoHo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

SoHo has plenty of options for accommodation, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some suggestions:

  • The James New York - a sleek, modern hotel with a rooftop pool and bar.
  • The Bowery Hotel - a luxurious hotel in a historic building.
  • HI New York City Hostel - a budget-friendly option that offers both private and shared rooms.

What to Buy in SoHo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in SoHo, make sure to pick up some unique souvenirs and local products to bring back home. Here are some ideas:

  • Artwork from one of SoHo’s many galleries.
  • Trendy clothing and accessories from boutiques like Opening Ceremony and Acne Studios.
  • Local food products such as chocolates, coffee blends, and spices from specialty stores like Dean & Deluca.

Local Culture and Customs of SoHo

SoHo is a diverse neighborhood that is known for its avant-garde art and fashion scenes. The community is welcoming, friendly, and multicultural. It’s important to respect the people and their customs while visiting and to keep in mind that certain areas may be closed off or restricted at specific times.

Best Time to Visit SoHo

SoHo is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is during the mild seasons of spring and fall, when the weather is comfortable. Summer can be hot and crowded, and winters can be cold and windy.

Do’s and Don’ts of SoHo

Do:

  • Enjoy the food and drinks scene.
  • Explore the diverse and interesting neighborhoods.
  • Respect the privacy of residents and business owners.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage public property.
  • Be disrespectful to locals.
  • Be unnecessarily noisy or disruptive.

Safety and Health

Safety in SoHo

SoHo is generally a safe place to visit and has lower crime rates than other neighborhoods in New York City. As a tourist, it is still important to take basic safety precautions, such as staying aware of your surroundings, especially at night.

Health in SoHo

There is an extensive healthcare system in New York City, and SoHo is home to some of the best hospitals and medical facilities in the world. It’s important to stay hydrated while exploring and to wear sunscreen, especially in the summer months.

Getting to SoHo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near SoHo

  • John F. Kennedy International Airport (JFK) - located 16 miles from SoHo.
  • LaGuardia Airport (LGA) - located 9 miles from SoHo.

Bus Stations Near SoHo

  • Greyhound Bus Lines - located in Midtown Manhattan.
  • Port Authority Bus Terminal - located in Hell’s Kitchen, Manhattan.

Other Options

  • Subway - SoHo is easily accessible by the New York City subway system with several stations in the area.

How to Get Around in SoHo

There are several ways to get around SoHo:

  • Walking - SoHo is a great neighborhood to explore on foot, with plenty of interesting shops, art galleries, and restaurants.
  • Biking - Biking is a popular way to see SoHo, with several bike rental services located throughout the neighborhood.
  • Subway - The New York City subway system has several stations in the SoHo area, making it easy to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from SoHo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

SoHo is located in the heart of Manhattan and is close to several other popular tourist destinations, including:

  • The Brooklyn Bridge - a 1.3-mile walk across the East River, offering stunning views of the city skyline.
  • The Statue of Liberty - a must-see attraction located on Liberty Island in New York Harbor.
  • Times Square - the iconic “Crossroads of the World,” known for its bright lights, billboards, and theaters.

Conclusion

SoHo is a vibrant and diverse neighborhood in Manhattan, full of interesting shops, art galleries, and restaurants. With its mix of history, culture, and modernity, it’s a great destination for both locals and tourists alike. This guide has covered everything you need to know to make the most of your trip to SoHo, including what to see, do, and eat, as well as tips for getting around the city and nearby excursions.
