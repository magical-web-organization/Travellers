Introduction to Upper East Side

Located in Manhattan, New York City, the Upper East Side is a posh residential and commercial district that is home to some of the city’s most significant cultural landmarks, high-end boutiques, luxury hotels, and fine-dining restaurants. The neighborhood stretches from 59th Street to 96th Street and is situated between Central Park and the East River, making it an excellent location for enjoying outdoor activities, scenic views, and cultural attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Upper East Side

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Metropolitan Museum of Art: One of the largest and most comprehensive art museums in the world, the Met houses over two million works of art from around the globe.

  • The Frick Collection: A museum with an impressive collection of European paintings, sculptures, and decorative arts.

  • The Museum of the City of New York: This museum showcases the city’s history, culture, and diverse communities through exhibits, photographs, and artifacts.

  • The Cooper Hewitt Smithsonian Design Museum: A museum dedicated to the history and contemporary design, featuring exhibitions, interactive displays, and workshops.

  • The Neue Galerie: A museum showcasing early 20th-century German and Austrian art and design.

Natural Attractions of Upper East Side: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Central Park: The most famous park in New York City, Central Park offers a variety of outdoor recreational activities, including walking paths, bike paths, playgrounds, and picnic areas.

  • East River Greenway: A beautiful riverfront pathway along the East River that offers stunning views of Manhattan and the Brooklyn Bridge.

  • Carl Schurz Park: A serene and beautiful park that houses the Gracie Mansion, the official residence of the New York City mayor.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Upper East Side

  • Central Park Loop: A scenic 6-mile loop that takes you through Central Park’s most beautiful areas, including the Reservoir, the Great Lawn, and the Plaza Hotel.

  • East River Greenway: A 9.44-mile bike path along the East River that connects the Battery Park City to East Harlem.

Best Things to Do in Upper East Side: Activities and Tours

  • Central Park Carriage Rides: A romantic and traditional way to experience the park’s beauty and history.

  • Food Tasting Tour: A culinary adventure through the Upper East Side’s diverse cuisine and food culture.

  • The Bronx Zoo: One of the largest zoos in the world, this attraction features over 6,000 animals from around the world.

Food and Drink Scene of Upper East Side

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bagels and lox: A classic New York City breakfast dish consisting of a bagel, cream cheese, and cured salmon.

  • Black and white cookie: A soft, cake-like cookie with half vanilla icing and half chocolate icing.

  • Hot dogs: A popular street food in New York, hot dogs are typically served with toppings such as onions, sauerkraut, and mustard.

Local Beverages

  • Cocktail: The Upper East Side is known for its innovative and creative cocktail scene. Some of the neighborhood’s most popular bars include Lexington Bar and Books and The Penrose.

  • Coffee: The local coffee shops are not to miss and its cafes, such as Bluestone Lane and Alice’s Tea Cup, offer an array of coffee blends and teas for every palate.

Where to Stay in Upper East Side: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Surrey: A luxury hotel located near Central Park with a renowned restaurant, bar and spa.

  • The Carlyle: A legendary hotel with world-class amenities and a famous bar.

  • The Franklin Hotel: A good option for budget travelers, offering comfortable accommodation and proximity to attractions like the Museum Mile.

What to Buy in Upper East Side: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Books from The Strand Bookstore: A landmark bookstore with over 18 miles of books.

  • Gourmet Food from Maison Kayser: A patisserie and boulangerie that offers a wide selection of pastries, bread, and sandwiches.

Local Culture and Customs of Upper East Side

The Upper East Side neighborhood is known for its affluent residents, including business executives, celebrities, and elites, and the etiquette is polite. It’s recommended to dress professionally, appropriately and not to be overly aggressive in one’s demeanor.

Best Time to Visit Upper East Side

The best time to visit the Upper East Side is in summer, from June to August, when the neighborhood is alive with tourists and locals alike. The Spring season when temperatures begin to warm up is also a good time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Upper East Side

Do:

  • Dress appropriately in professional attire.
  • Respect the privacy and space of others.
  • Try the local food, such as bagels and lox, hot dogs, and black and white cookies.

Don’t:

  • Be overly aggressive in your behavior.
  • Take unauthorized photographs of buildings, people, or artwork.
  • Litter or damage public property.

Safety and Health

Safety in Upper East Side

The Upper East Side is mostly safe, but like any urban area, it is always a good idea to be aware of one’s surroundings, especially late at night. The neighborhood’s residents and local businesses are vigilant, and the streets are well-lit.

Health in Upper East Side

There are numerous medical facilities that travelers can go in case of emergency. The New York-Presbyterian Hospital and Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center are among the top healthcare providers in the country.

Getting to Upper East Side: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Upper East Side

  • John F. Kennedy International Airport: Approximately 16 miles from the Upper East Side.

  • LaGuardia Airport: Approximately 7 miles from the Upper East Side.

Bus Stations Near Upper East Side

  • Port Authority Bus Terminal: Approximately 7 miles from the Upper East Side.

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Approximately 8 miles from the Upper East Side.

Other Options

  • Subway: The Upper East Side has several subway lines that provide easy access to the rest of the city.

  • Taxi and Ridesharing App: Taxis and ridesharing services like Uber and Lyft are readily available in the neighborhood.

How to Get Around in Upper East Side

Walking and bicycling are convenient and safe transportation options in the Upper East Side. The neighborhood is serviced by the MTA buses, which travel all over Manhattan, and several subway lines that run through the area.

Where to Go Next from Upper East Side: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Brooklyn Bridge Park: A waterfront park that offers spectacular views of the Manhattan skyline.

  • The Lower East Side: A historically significant neighborhood that has a unique New York City charm and diverse cultural offerings.

  • Williamsburg: A trendy neighborhood known for its artistic scene, excellent food, and cool bar scene.

Conclusion

The Upper East Side of New York City is one of the most exclusive and culturally significant neighborhoods in the United States. The area has something for everyone, from its many world-class museums and cultural landmarks to the wide range of recreational activities and outdoor attractions. This travel guide offers a comprehensive look at all that the Upper East Side has to offer, from the best places to stay and eat to what to do and see during your visit.
