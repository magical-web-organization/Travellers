Introduction to Stony Point

Located in Rockland County, Downstate New York, Stony Point is a charming town that offers a mix of rich history, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. Once a strategic location during the American Revolution, Stony Point is now home to several museums and historical sites that offer a glimpse into its past. Outdoor enthusiasts will love the town’s many hiking and biking trails, which showcase the area’s natural beauty. In this guide, we’ll explore the top things to do, see, and eat in Stony Point, including cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, hiking and biking routes, where to stay, and how to get around.

List of Places to Visit in Stony Point

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Stony Point Battlefield State Historic Site: This historic site was the site of a revolutionary war battle in 1779. Visitors can learn about the battle and the role the site played in the war, as well as explore the museum on-site.

  • Haverstraw Brick Museum: Housed in a restored 1909 building, the Haverstraw Brick Museum showcases the town’s brick-making history.

  • Stony Point Lighthouse: Built in 1826, this lighthouse offers panoramic views of the Hudson River.

  • The King’s Daughters Association of New York: Founded in 1896, this association is dedicated to preserving French-Canadian heritage in Stony Point and the surrounding area. Visitors can learn about the association’s history and their role in the community over the years.

Natural Attractions of Stony Point: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Harriman State Park: This sprawling park offers hiking, biking, skiing, and other outdoor activities. Visitors can explore over 200 miles of trails, several lakes, and scenic views.

  • Doodletown: Once a bustling community, Doodletown is now a ghost town. Visitors can explore the remains of the town, including old homes and businesses, and hike the nearby trails.

  • Bear Mountain State Park: This park is home to a zoo, a lake, and several hiking and biking trails. Visitors can also take a scenic drive up to the top of Bear Mountain, which offers spectacular views of the Hudson Valley.

  • Stony Point Battlefield Trail: This trail takes visitors through the site of the revolutionary war battle, offering scenic views of the Hudson River along the way.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Stony Point

Harriman State Park

  • The Red Cross Trail: This 7.5-mile trail offers scenic views of the surrounding landscape.

  • The Bare Rock Trail: This challenging trail takes visitors to the top of Bald Mountain, offering panoramic views of the Hudson Valley.

Bear Mountain State Park

  • The Major Welch Trail: This trail takes visitors to the top of Bear Mountain and offers stunning views of the Hudson Valley.

  • The Appalachian Trail: Visitors can hike a portion of the famous Appalachian Trail, which passes through Bear Mountain State Park.

Best Things to Do in Stony Point: Activities and Tours

  • Hudson Valley Food Tours: Visitors can experience the area’s culinary scene with a food tour of the region.

  • Hudson Valley Wine and Chocolate Tours: This tour combines wine tasting with chocolate sampling, offering visitors a unique taste of the area.

  • Hudson Highlands Nature Museum: This museum offers nature programs, live animal exhibits, and interactive exhibits.

  • Cruise on the Hudson: Visitors can explore the Hudson River on a scenic cruise.

Food and Drink Scene of Stony Point

Must-Try Dishes

  • Haverstraw Sausage: This sausage is made with pork, veal, and potatoes and is a local favorite.

  • Apple Cider Donuts: Made with fresh apples and cider, these donuts are a seasonal favorite.

Local Beverages

  • Hudson Valley Wine: Visitors can sample locally produced wines at several wineries in the area.

  • Craft Beer: Several local breweries offer craft beer made with local ingredients.

What to Buy in Stony Point: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hudson Valley Wine: Visitors can purchase locally produced wine to bring home as a souvenir or gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Stony Point

Stony Point has a rich history and cultural heritage, which is celebrated through several museums and historical sites in the area. The town also has a strong agricultural and food tradition, with several local farms and food producers. Visitors should respect the town’s history and local customs, and be mindful of local practices when visiting.

Best Time to Visit Stony Point

The best time to visit Stony Point is between late spring to early fall, when the weather is mild and the outdoor activities are at their peak. Visitors can enjoy hiking, biking, and outdoor adventures during this time, as well as seasonal events like apple picking and fall foliage tours.

Do’s and Don’ts of Stony Point

Do:

  • Respect the town’s history and cultural heritage.

  • Explore the area’s natural beauty and outdoor activities.

  • Sample the local food and drink.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.

  • Leave trash or litter in natural areas.

  • Cause damage or disturbance to the area’s wildlife and natural resources.

Safety and Health

Safety in Stony Point

Stony Point is generally a safe town, but visitors should exercise caution when hiking or biking in remote areas and should be aware of their surroundings at all times.

Health in Stony Point

Stony Point has several medical facilities and pharmacies, but visitors should bring any necessary medications and take proper precautions to stay healthy during their stay.

Conclusion

Stony Point is a charming town that offers a mix of history, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. With several museums and historical sites, as well as a plethora of hiking and biking trails, there’s something for everyone in Stony Point. Visitors can sample the local food and drink, explore the area’s natural beauty, and experience the town’s rich cultural heritage. With easy access to nearby destinations and excursions, Stony Point is the perfect destination for a weekend getaway or a longer vacation.
