Introduction to Monsey

Monsey is a beautiful town located in Rockland County, New York, United States. With its scenic views, outdoor activities, and rich cultural history, Monsey is an excellent destination for travelers seeking relaxation and adventure alike. Visitors can enjoy a range of activities, such as hiking, biking, historical tours, and more. This comprehensive guide will provide you with all the essential information you need to plan your trip to Monsey.

List of Places to Visit in Monsey

Here is a list of the top attractions to visit in Monsey:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Historical Society of Rockland County: This museum showcases the history of Rockland County, including its role in America’s Revolution and the Civil War. The museum also features several exhibits on local arts and culture.
  • Rockland Center for the Arts: A cutting-edge art center that host regular exhibitions, performances, art education and artists’ residences.

Natural Attractions of Monsey: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Harriman State Park: With its scenic hiking trails, skiing slopes, and tranquil lakes, Harriman State Park is worth the visit.
  • Bear Mountain State Park: Famous for its stunning views of the Hudson River, Bear Mountain State Park features an array of outdoor activities, such as hiking trails, wildlife watching, and outdoor ice-skating rink.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Monsey

Monsey offers various hiking and biking trail options for fitness enthusiasts. Some popular routes include:

  • The Long Path: A hiking trail that goes from Fort Lee, New Jersey, to Altamont in Albany County, New York, running through Monsey and Harriman State Park.
  • Tackamack Park: A family-friendly park featuring cycling and hiking trails, playgrounds, and picnic areas.

Best Things to Do in Monsey: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the Sukkah and Harness Factory: Visit the Monsey site to learn about the unique history and customs of the local community.
  • Walking Tour of Monsey: A self-guided tour that includes a visit to the oldest house of worship in Monsey, the Bais Medrash of Monsey (Now used as the Monsey Academy).
  • Farmers Market: Visit local farm markets selling fresh produce, certified organic vegetables, fruits, dairy, and eggs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Monsey

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Monsey, here’s a suggested itinerary that will allow you to see all the must-visit places mentioned in this guide:

  • Day 1: Visit Historical Society of Rockland County, and explore the arts and culture of the local people.
  • Day 2: Take a hike or bike the Tackamack Park Trail.
  • Day 3: Spend the day at Bear Mountains State Park with a picnic and hike to the top of the mountain for incredible views.
  • Day 4: Enjoy a walking tour of Monsey, incorporating the oldest house of worship and enjoy some of the local cuisine at the farmers market.
  • Day 5: Explore Harriman State Park, with its many trails and opportunities for outdoor activities.
  • Day 6: Take a trip to Rockland Center for the Arts.
  • Day 7: Take part in all the things you enjoyed the most of the week such as hiking, exploring the parks, and discovering new areas of the city that caught your fascination.

Food and Drink Scene of Monsey

In Monsey, food is an essential part of the community and the tradition, with a plethora of family-owned eateries offering everything from traditional Jewish fare to classic sandwiches and quick bites.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kosher Pizza: Try a classic kosher pie at Pizza Time.
  • Matzah ball soup: A Matzah ball soup is an absolute classic must-try dish, and the best place to have it is at the Grape Juice in Monsey NY.
  • Falafel: Head to Jerusalem Cafe for the best falafel in town.

Local Beverages

  • Kosher Wine: To experience traditional kosher wine, visit Royal Wine for tasting options.
  • Fresh juice bar: Nutri-Juice serves up a variety of smoothies and fresh juices.

Where to Stay in Monsey: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Monsey offers a range of accommodations featuring cozy guesthouses, standard hotels, and budget-friendly hostels. Here are a few of the best options:

  • Crowne Plaza Suffern-Mahwah: A modern hotel located just a few miles away from Monsey, the hotel is a midpoint between staying in the city and being closer to nearby Lake Welch or Seven Lakes Drive (a scenic route through Harriman State Park).
  • Monsey Dorms: A budget-friendly option suitable for backpackers and those who prefer to stay in a hostel.
  • Hilton Garden Inn: A high standard hotel located just few miles away from Monsey.

What to Buy in Monsey: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Monsey offers a range of unique souvenirs and local products that make great memories to take home. Here are a few things you should consider purchasing:

  • Bereishit Publishing: Purchase unique Jewish books to keep as family heirlooms.
  • Wegman’s Grocery: Try fresh fruit and vegetables grown by local farmers and small markets in Monsey.

Local Culture and Customs of Monsey

Monsey is home to a thriving Jewish community. Visitors are encouraged to be respectful of the local customs when visiting synagogues and religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Monsey

Monsey is an ideal destination to visit in the fall and winter months, where the temperatures are relatively milder and the annual fall foliage is breathtaking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Monsey

Here are some do’s and dont’s that you should keep in mind when visiting Monsey:

Do:

  • Dress respectably when entering a religious site or synagogue.
  • Follow the trail rules and guidelines when hiking or biking
  • Convey the traditional Jewish greetings such as “Shabbat Shalom” when in the local community.

Don’t:

  • Wear inappropriate or revealing clothing.
  • Take photographs in sensitive areas or without prior permission from individuals in images.
  • Smoke in non-designated areas.

Safety and Health

Monsey is relatively safe to visit, with a below-average crime rate. It’s advised to use common sense safety measures like securing valuables and staying aware of surroundings when hiking or biking in Harriman State Park.

Health in Monsey

Hospitals and medical centers are available in the surrounding area, including a hospital in the town of Suffern.

Getting to Monsey: Transportation Options and Logistics

Monsey is reachable by both car, bus, and plane.

Airports Near Monsey

  • Westchester County Airport (HPN)
  • LaGuardia Airport (LGA)

Bus Stations Near Monsey

  • The Port Authority Bus Terminal, New York City
  • The Shortline Bus company

Other Options

You can also drive to Monsey if travelling by car, it’s a short drive from nearby areas like New York City and Jersey City.

How to Get Around Monsey

Monsey is best explored on foot but renting a car is also an option for those who prefer to explore the surrounding area. Taxi and ride-sharing services are available for transport, should you need them.

Where to Go Next from Monsey: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many other places to explore near Monsey, including the nearby city of Suffern and would be an excellent, quick day trip.

Conclusion

Monsey is a hidden gem of New York and more than obtains a visit for the activities, food, and natural beauty. This guide provides an overview of the best places to visit in the area, from hiking trails to historical sites to Jewish cultural attractions. We hope this guide will help you plan an unforgettable trip to Monsey.
