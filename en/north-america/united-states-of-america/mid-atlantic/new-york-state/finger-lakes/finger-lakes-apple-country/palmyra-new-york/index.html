Introduction to Palmyra (New York)

Palmyra is a small town located in the Finger Lakes region of New York, known for its scenic beauty and apple orchards. It is also home to several historical sites that played a significant role in the early development of the United States. The town is named after the ancient city of Palmyra in Syria and was founded in 1789.

List of Places to Visit in Palmyra (New York)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Palmyra is steeped in history and has several culturally significant attractions that are worth exploring.

1. The Hill Cumorah

The Hill Cumorah is a site of religious significance and is known to be the location where Joseph Smith discovered the golden plates, which led to the establishment of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. The site is marked by a monument and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.

2. The Palmyra Historical Museum

The Palmyra Historical Museum is a must-visit destination for history buffs. The museum has a vast collection of artifacts that tell the story of Palmyra’s rich and varied past, including exhibits on local Native American history, agricultural heritage, and the town’s role in the Underground Railroad.

Natural Attractions of Palmyra (New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Palmyra has a wealth of natural attractions that are perfect for the outdoorsy traveler.

1. The Erie Canal

The Erie Canal is a popular attraction of Palmyra and is one of the most significant engineering feats in American history. Visitors can enjoy scenic boat tours along the canal or explore its impressive lock system on foot or bike.

2. The Ganargua Creek Meadow Preserve

The Ganargua Creek Meadow Preserve is a beautiful natural area comprising over 300 acres of wetlands, meadows, and forests. Visitors can hike or bike the trails, go birdwatching, or simply enjoy the stunning views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Palmyra (New York)

Palmyra has terrific hiking and biking routes that offer breathtaking views of the countryside.

1. The Erie Canalway Trail

The Erie Canalway Trail is a 360-mile route that follows the Erie Canal’s path, including a section that runs through Palmyra. The trail is primarily used for hiking and biking and offers scenic views of the canal and the surrounding countryside.

2. The Auburn Trail

The Auburn Trail is a 10.6-mile long hiking and biking route that passes through Palmyra. The trail is suitable for all levels of ability and offers picturesque views of the region’s natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Palmyra (New York): Activities and Tours

Palmyra offers a wide variety of activities and tours that cater to travelers of all tastes.

1. Wine Tasting Tours

Palmyra is located in the Finger Lakes Region, known for its excellent wineries. Visitors can take wine tasting tours and enjoy a variety of locally-produced wines.

2. Apple Picking in Finger Lakes Apple Country

Palmyra is in Finger Lakes Apple Country, known for its apple orchards. Visitors can pick their apples and enjoy all sorts of apple-themed treats and snacks.
