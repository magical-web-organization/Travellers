Introduction to Sterling (New York)

Nestled in the heart of Finger Lakes Apple Country in New York, Sterling boasts a peaceful and picturesque town. It’s a great place for outdoor activities, whether hiking, biking, or just enjoying the scenic views.

In this Ultimate Travel Guide to Sterling (New York), we’ll take a closer look at the best things to do, places to visit, food to try, and more. So, buckle up and get ready for an adventure!

List of Places to Visit in Sterling (New York)

Here is a list of must-visit places in Sterling (New York):

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sterling Renaissance Festival: One of the most popular attractions in Sterling, this annual festival transports visitors back in time to the 16th century, with immersive shows, games, and crafts.
  • Sodus Bay Lighthouse Museum: Located in nearby Sodus Point, this museum showcases the history of lighthouse keeping and the maritime industry in the area.
  • Sterling Historical Society: The society hosts a museum with exhibits that highlight the town’s past and hosts events throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Sterling (New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Fair Haven Beach State Park: This beautiful park on the shores of Lake Ontario boasts swimming, fishing, boating, camping, and more.
  • Sterling Nature Center: With over 1400 acres of trails, wetlands, and woodlands, nature lovers will enjoy hiking, fishing, and bird watching in this stunning nature center.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sterling (New York)

Sterling is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Sterling (New York):

  • Sterling Nature Center Loop Trail: This moderate 3.8-mile hike takes you through stunning woodlands, wetlands, and open fields. Perfect for wildlife spotting and bird watching.
  • Sterling Renaissance Loop: This 6-mile bike ride takes you through the Sterling Renaissance Festival and the scenic countryside of Sterling.

Best Things to Do in Sterling (New York): Activities and Tours

  • Sterling Renaissance Festival: This annual festival is a must-visit for an immersive experience in the life and times of the 16th century. Visitors can enjoy live shows, games, and crafts.
  • Boating on Lake Ontario: Experience the spectacular views of the great lake and enjoy some water sports.
  • Wine Tasting: Visit the nearby Finger Lakes wine country for a tasting of some of the best wines in the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Sterling (New York)

Sterling (New York) may be a small town, but it offers some excellent food and drink options. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Apple Pie: As part of Finger Lakes Apple Country, Sterling is famous for its apple pies.
  • Grape Pie: Similar to the apple pie, this is a unique dish that Sterling is known for, especially during the fall season.

Local Beverages

  • Finger Lakes Wines: Experience the excellent wines of the Finger Lakes region.
  • Sterling Cidery: This craft cidery offers a range of ciders to enjoy, perfect for anyone who wants to taste a variety of flavors.

Where to Stay in Sterling (New York): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sterling (New York) offers some great accommodation options. Here are some places to stay:

  • Little Sodus Inn: A bed and breakfast on the shores of Lake Ontario, with beautiful views and excellent service.
  • Sterling Renaissance Festival Glamping: For an immersive experience, stay in one of the festival’s unique tents.
  • Sterling Stage Kampitheater: A campground with an amphitheater, hosting events throughout the year.

What to Buy in Sterling (New York): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sterling (New York) is known for its agricultural products, so here are some souvenirs and local products to bring back home:

  • Apple butter and fruit preserves: Locally made fruit preserves are perfect for a sweet memory of Sterling (New York).
  • Handmade crafts: The Sterling Renaissance Festival offers many handmade items from local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Sterling (New York)

Most of the year, Sterling is a peaceful and quiet town. But during the Sterling Renaissance Festival, the town comes alive with its unique customs and traditions from the 16th century.

Best Time to Visit Sterling (New York)

The best time to visit Sterling (New York) is from June to October when the weather is pleasant.

Safety and Health

Sterling (New York) is a safe town. Visitors should exercise basic precautions like avoiding unfamiliar areas and not leaving valuables in plain sight.

Where to Go Next from Sterling (New York): Nearby Destinations and Excursions
