Introduction to Finger Lakes Apple Country

Finger Lakes Apple Country is a scenic region located in the heart of New York State, known for its abundant apple orchards, serene landscapes, and numerous outdoor recreational opportunities. The region encompasses 6 counties – Cayuga, Wayne, Ontario, Monroe, Orleans, and Yates – and is home to over 20 apple varieties grown on more than 6,000 acres of land.

Week-Long Itinerary for Finger Lakes Apple Country

Day 1: Visit Watkins Glen State Park and hike the Gorge Trail, followed by wine tasting at Hazlitt 1852 Vineyards. Day 2: Explore nearby wineries on the Seneca Lake Wine Trail, and stop at Taughannock Falls State Park for a scenic hike. Day 3: Take a hot air balloon ride with Liberty Balloon Company, followed by a visit to the George Eastman Museum in Rochester. Day 4: Spend a day on the Erie Canal with Sam Patch Tour Boat, followed by lunch at the acclaimed Dinosaur Bar-B-Que. Day 5: Visit Letchworth State Park, known as the “Grand Canyon of the East”, followed by dinner at Warfield’s Restaurant. Day 6: Spend the day exploring the Cornell Botanic Gardens and the beautiful campus of Cornell University. Day 7: End your trip with apple picking at Beak & Skiff Apple Orchards, and a visit to Blue Toad Hard Cider.

Local Culture and Customs of Finger Lakes Apple Country

Finger Lakes Apple Country has a unique culture that is heavily influenced by the region’s agricultural heritage. The locals take pride in their apple orchards, and many festivals and events are held throughout the year to celebrate the harvest season.

Best Time to Visit Finger Lakes Apple Country

The best time to visit Finger Lakes Apple Country is during the fall season (September-November), when the apple orchards are in full swing and the leaves are changing colors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Finger Lakes Apple Country

Do:

  • Try the local apple-based dishes and beverages.
  • Visit the region’s cultural landmarks and natural wonders.
  • Support small local businesses.

Don’t:

  • Pick apples without permission from the orchard owners.

  • Disrespect local customs or people.
