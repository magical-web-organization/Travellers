Introduction to Watkins Glen

Watkins Glen is a small village located in the Eastern Finger Lakes region of New York state, known for its natural beauty, outdoor activities and world-class wineries. The village is named after Seneca Indian Chief, Atuahalpa, who was also known as General John Watkins, and the deep, narrow gorge that runs through town, Watkins Glen State Park, draws visitors from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Watkins Glen

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Watkins Glen History Museum: Located in downtown Watkins Glen, this museum is a must-visit for history buffs. Exhibits feature the village’s rich history, including its role in transportation, agriculture and industry.

  • International Motor Racing Research Center: This museum is dedicated to the history of motor racing, from its early days to the present. It has a vast collection of photographs, books, and other memorabilia that will thrill any racing enthusiast.

Natural Attractions of Watkins Glen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Watkins Glen State Park: This is the crown jewel of the village. The park is famous for its 19 waterfalls, towering cliffs, and lush vegetation. Visitors can hike along the Gorge Trail, which follows the stream that flows through the park, and experience the natural beauty of Watkins Glen firsthand.

  • Seneca Lake: This is the largest of the Finger Lakes, and it’s a popular destination for water sports like sailing, fishing, and swimming. Visitors can take a boat tour of the lake or just relax on the beach.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Watkins Glen

  • Catharine Valley Trail: This is a 12-mile long trail that is perfect for hiking and biking. It follows an old railway bed and passes through a scenic valley that is often overlooked by tourists.

  • Finger Lakes Trail: This trail is over 560 miles long and winds its way through the Finger Lakes region. It passes through Watkins Glen State Park, and visitors can take a section of it for a day hike or overnight trip.

Best Things to Do in Watkins Glen: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting: The Finger Lakes is home to over 100 wineries, and visitors to Watkins Glen should take advantage of the opportunity to sample some of the best wines in the world. Many wineries offer tastings and tours.

  • Boat Tour of Watkins Glen State Park: This tour is a unique way to experience the park’s beauty. Visitors will ride a boat through the gorge and get an up-close look at the waterfalls and cliffs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Watkins Glen

Day 1 Arrival, check into hotel, explore downtown
Day 2 Visit Watkins Glen State Park and hike the Gorge Trail
Day 3 Take a scenic drive around Seneca Lake and visit wineries
Day 4 Bike the Catharine Valley Trail in the morning, visit local museums in the afternoon
Day 5 Take a boat tour of Seneca Lake, swim, and relax on the beach
Day 6 Visit nearby towns and attractions, like the Corning Museum of Glass
Day 7 Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Watkins Glen

Must-Try Dishes

  • Grilled Peach Salad: This dish features fresh, juicy peaches that are grilled and served over a bed of baby spinach and arugula. It’s light, refreshing, and perfect for a summer meal.

  • Finger Lakes Riesling: This dry white wine is a regional specialty and pairs perfectly with the local cuisine. It’s fruity and floral, with a crisp finish.

Local Beverages

  • Finger Lakes Beer: The region is also known for its craft beer. Local breweries like Two Goats Brewing and Horseheads Brewing offer a wide selection of brews that are sure to satisfy any beer connoisseur.

Where to Stay in Watkins Glen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Watkins Glen Harbor Hotel: This upscale hotel is located on the shores of Seneca Lake and features luxurious accommodations and breathtaking views.

  • Watkins Glen International Hostel: This is a budget-friendly option for travelers who want to explore the region without breaking the bank. The hostel offers communal living spaces and private rooms.

What to Buy in Watkins Glen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hand-Blown Glassware: The Corning Museum of Glass is located nearby and is a great place to pick up some unique, hand-blown glassware.

  • Local Wine: Finger Lakes wines are highly sought after and make great souvenirs. Most wineries offer tastings and sell bottles directly to visitors.

Local Culture and Customs of Watkins Glen

Watkins Glen has a rich history, and visitors can learn about the village’s past by visiting local museums and historical sites. The Finger Lakes region also has a thriving arts community, and visitors can attend concerts, performances, and art shows throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Watkins Glen

The best time to visit Watkins Glen is in the summer, when the weather is warm and the outdoor activities are in full swing. Spring and fall are also great times to visit, as the foliage is beautiful, and the wineries are less crowded.

Do’s and Don’ts of Watkins Glen

Do:

  • Visit Watkins Glen State Park and hike the Gorge Trail
  • Sample local wines at a Finger Lakes winery
  • Explore downtown Watkins Glen and its local shops and restaurants

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural beauty of the parks and lakes
  • Drive under the influence of alcohol or drugs
  • Ignore posted signs or warnings in the outdoors

Safety and Health

Safety in Watkins Glen

Watkins Glen is a safe destination, but visitors should still take precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. Lock your car and hotel room, be aware of your surroundings, and take appropriate safety measures when hiking or biking in the natural areas.

Health in Watkins Glen

Visitors to Watkins Glen should be aware of the potential hazards associated with outdoor activities, like ticks, poison ivy, and heat exhaustion. Use insect repellent, wear protective clothing, and take breaks when necessary to avoid these risks.

Getting to Watkins Glen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Watkins Glen

The nearest major airports to Watkins Glen are the Syracuse Hancock International Airport and the Elmira/Corning Regional Airport. From there, visitors can rent a car or take a shuttle to their final destination.

Bus Stations Near Watkins Glen

Trailways Bus Lines and Greyhound offer regular service to the nearby towns of Elmira and Corning. From there, visitors can rent a car or take a taxi to Watkins Glen.

Other Options

There are also several private shuttle services and car rental companies that offer transportation to Watkins Glen from the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Watkins Glen

Visitors can easily get around the village on foot or by bike, and most attractions are within walking distance. Taxis and rental cars are also available for those who prefer to drive.

Where to Go Next from Watkins Glen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Corning Museum of Glass: This museum is located just a short drive from Watkins Glen and features over 50,000 glass objects from around the world.

  • Taughannock Falls State Park: Located just north of Watkins Glen, this park features a 215-foot waterfall and stunning views of Cayuga Lake.

Conclusion

Watkins Glen is a hidden gem in the Finger Lakes region, with its stunning natural scenery, outdoor activities, and world-class wineries. With this comprehensive tour guide, visitors can plan the perfect trip to Watkins Glen and experience all that this beautiful village has to offer.
