Introduction to Trumansburg

Located in Eastern Finger Lakes, Trumansburg is a charming village in the Finger Lakes region of New York. It is particularly well-known for the Taughannock Falls State Park, which features the tallest waterfall in the Northeastern United States and great outdoor recreational activities. Trumansburg is also home to an array of cultural and historical attractions, such as museums and art galleries, making it an ideal destination for experiencing the local culture. This comprehensive guide has everything you need to plan your trip to Trumansburg, New York.

List of Places to Visit in Trumansburg

Trumansburg offers a wide range of places to visit for both tourists and locals. The following list highlights some of the most popular attractions in the town.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • GrassRoots Festival of Music and Dance: This popular annual arts and musical festival takes place every third week of July and attracts a diverse crowd from across the globe.

  • Cayuga Nature Center: This museum has interactive exhibits about the wildlife and nature of the Finger Lakes region.

  • John Brown Farm State Historic Site: This historic site honors the abolitionist John Brown and his commitment to ending slavery in America.

Natural Attractions of Trumansburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Taughannock Falls State Park: Explore the fascinating outdoors of Taughannock Falls State Park, known for the tallest waterfall in the Northeastern United States and providing plenty of recreational activities.

  • Buttermilk Falls State Park: Known for its scenic views and magnificent waterfalls, Buttermilk Falls State Park is a paradise for hikers and nature lovers.

  • Ithaca Farmers Market: Check out the Ithaca Farmers Market, which offers a variety of artisanal products, fresh produce, and handcrafted items.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Trumansburg

Trumansburg offers a range of hiking and biking trails, from relatively easy to strenuous hikes. Here are the top hiking and biking routes to explore in Trumansburg:

  • The Cayuga Trail: This 35-mile trail is ideal for long-distance hikers and those looking for a challenging adventure.

  • The Black Diamond Trail: This 10-mile trail is perfect for mountain bikers and hikers of all skill levels and provides breathtaking views of Cayuga Lake.

  • Finger Lakes Trail: This trail is perfect for those seeking a more challenging itinerary with a vast array of trails to choose from.

Best Things to Do in Trumansburg: Activities and Tours

Trumansburg offers many different activities, tours, and experiences for tourists to explore. These are some of the best things to do in Trumansburg:

  • Wine Tours: Trumansburg is home to several local wineries like Americana Vineyards, King Ferry Winery, and Lucas Vineyards, where visitors can discover local wines, history, and even take part in tastings.

  • Kayaking and Canoeing: Head out to Cayuga Lake and try your hand at kayaking or canoeing for a great adventure on the water.

  • Horseback Riding: Enjoy the scenic beauty of Trumansburg’s countryside by horseback riding with experienced guides.

Week-Long Itinerary for Trumansburg

If you plan on staying the week, here’s an itinerary for your trip:

Day Itinerary
Day 1 Visit the Cayuga Nature Center and take a walk along the trails. End the day at the Ithaca Farmers Market.
Day 2 Head out to Buttermilk Falls State Park for a day of hiking and nature exploration.
Day 3 Go for a horseback ride along Trumansburg’s countryside.
Day 4 Take a wine tour around the local wineries, such as Americana Vineyards or King Ferry Winery.
Day 5 Discover the village of Trumansburg with its charming historic sites featuring art galleries and unique cafes.
Day 6 Explore Taughannock Falls State Park and go for a swim in the falls’ basin.
Day 7 Try kayaking or canoeing on Cayuga Lake and end the week-long trip with a sunset view.

Food and Drink Scene of Trumansburg

Trumansburg’s food and drink scene is all about supporting the local community. Visitors can enjoy delicious local cuisine and drinks at some of the town’s best establishments. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mac and Cheese: This classic comfort dish can be found at many local restaurants, such as The Rongovian Embassy, a local favorite.

  • Ithaca Bakery Bread: This bakery is famous for its freshly baked bread, which is used in many restaurants around the town.

  • Finger Lakes Wine: Trumansburg is home to many wineries, including regional favorites like Americana Vineyards or King Ferry Winery.

Local Beverages

  • Ithaca Beer Company Beers: These locally brewed beers are popular throughout Upstate New York and may be found at the Ithaca Beer Co. Taproom.

  • Cayuga Lake Wine: Visitors will find a wide range of local wines in Trumansburg, including many award-winning brands.

  • Finger Lakes Cider House: Stop by Finger Lakes Cider House to enjoy locally made ciders using fresh, local apples.

Where to Stay in Trumansburg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Trumansburg is home to many different types of accommodations, from budget to premium hotels, hostels, and B&Bs. This enables visitors to find the perfect option to match their budget and travel style. Here are a few recommendations for places to stay in Trumansburg:

  • Ulysses Historical Society Guesthouse: This charming guesthouse is located in a historic building near the sites of Trumansburg.

  • Taughannock Farms Inn: This boutique hotel is located near Taughannock Falls State Park with beautiful outdoor grounds.

  • Juniper Hill Bed & Breakfast: This beautiful B&B is located in Trumansburg’s village center, offering guests convenience and charm.

What to Buy in Trumansburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Trumansburg is known for its many local products and crafts. Here are a few must-buy souvenirs to bring home:

  • Pottery by John and Kate Smith: This husband-and-wife duo creates popular pottery pieces with unique designs.

  • Finger Lakes Coffee Roasters: This coffee roaster produces high-quality blends with unique profiles that you cannot find anywhere else.

  • Handmade Jewelry by Rebecca Hess: This local artist produces beautiful and unique jewelry designs using various fabrics and materials.

Local Culture and Customs of Trumansburg

The people of Trumansburg are known for their friendly and welcoming culture. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and etiquette when visiting. The area is also known for music and arts, so don’t miss the opportunity to catch a show or visit an art gallery while in town.

Best Time to Visit Trumansburg

The best time to visit Trumansburg is from May to October when the weather is warm and pleasant. Summer is the peak season and offers a wide range of activities, events, and festivals to experience.

Do’s and Don’ts of Trumansburg

To make the most of your visit, here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Try local cuisine and beverages, including local wines and beers.

  • Visit Taughannock Falls State Park and Buttermilk Falls State Park to experience Trumansburg’s natural beauty.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the town’s local businesses and choose chain restaurants instead.

  • Leave any trash or litter after visiting the areas natural attractions.

  • Limit yourself to one activity when there are plenty more to experience.

Safety and Health

Safety in Trumansburg

Trumansburg is a peaceful and safe town, but like with most places, visitors should take caution. Before heading out on any outdoor adventures, be sure to check the local weather conditions and dress appropriately.

Health in Trumansburg

Trumansburg has a hospital staffed to provide medical care, but before visiting Trumansburg, it’s recommended to have travel insurance to account for unexpected emergencies.

Getting to Trumansburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Trumansburg is located about four hours northwest of New York City and two hours east of Buffalo by car. Visitors can take the bus or hire a car rental service to get to Trumansburg.

Airports Near Trumansburg

The Ithaca Tompkins Regional Airport is the closest airport to Trumansburg, and it offers flights to major cities across the United States.

Bus Stations Near Trumansburg

Ithaca Bus Station is the closest bus station to Trumansburg, located only 10 minutes away from the town nearer to many restaurants, hotels, and attractions.

Other Options

Visitors can also get around Trumansburg and the surrounding areas by rental car, bike, or even by boat.

How to Get Around in Trumansburg

Trumansburg is a relatively small town, and visitors can get around by T-Cat, the local bus service that runs through many towns in the area. Bikes and tour buses are also available to rent for those who prefer to take a more active role in exploring the town.

Where to Go Next from Trumansburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Trumansburg’s location makes it an ideal starting point for exploring the Finger Lakes region. Nearby attractions include:

  • Watkins Glen State Park: Located about an hour’s drive away from Trumansburg, the park offers spectacular waterfalls and rock formations.

  • Cayuga Lake: Visitors can take a lake cruise, try water sports, or relax on one of the beaches.

  • Cornell University: Check out the Ivy League university, which is also located in Ithaca and includes significant sites such as the campus arboretum.

Conclusion

Trumansburg is a wonderful destination for travelers looking for an immersive cultural experience, outdoor adventure or both. From museums and art galleries to nature trails and kayaking, Trumansburg is an ideal vacation spot for both families, couples or solo travelers.
