Introduction to Skaneateles

Skaneateles is a charming village located in Eastern Finger Lakes, in Upstate New York. Known for its crystal-clear lake and picturesque scenery, it has become a popular destination for visitors seeking outdoor activities, cultural experiences, and great food.

List of Places to Visit in Skaneateles

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are interested in history and culture, you won’t want to miss these top attractions:

  • The Skaneateles Historical Society - This museum showcases the area’s rich history through exhibits and artifacts.

  • The Creamery Museum - Located in the beautifully restored Creamery building, this museum shares the story of dairy farming in the region.

  • The John D. Barrow Art Gallery - Housed in a restored 1820s mansion, this gallery features changing exhibitions of contemporary and historical art.

Natural Attractions of Skaneateles: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Skaneateles is a great place to enjoy the outdoors, with its crystal-clear lake and rolling hills. Here are some top attractions for nature lovers:

  • Skaneateles Lake - The clear, sparkling waters of Skaneateles Lake offer opportunities for swimming, fishing, boating, and sailing. You can also take a scenic boat tour on one of its historic vessels.

  • Baltimore Woods Nature Center - This nature preserve offers hiking trails through forests and wetlands, as well as educational programs and events.

  • Bear Swamp State Forest - With over 3,000 acres of forest and wetlands, Bear Swamp State Forest provides plenty of opportunities for hiking, mountain biking, and hunting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Skaneateles

Skaneateles offers several hiking and biking trails that provide fantastic views of the lake and surrounding countryside. Here are some of the most popular:

  • Skaneateles Conservation Area Loop Trail - This 1.2-mile loop trail takes you through forests and wetlands, with scenic views of Skaneateles Lake.

  • Baltimore Woods Nature Center Trail System - With over 6 miles of trails, Baltimore Woods offers hiking and mountain biking routes through forests, swamps, and meadows.

  • Bear Swamp State Forest Trails - This state forest offers several hiking and biking trails, ranging from easy to challenging.

Best Things to Do in Skaneateles: Activities and Tours

Whether you prefer outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or simply relaxing with a great meal and a glass of wine, Skaneateles has something for everyone. Here are some top things to do:

  • Take a Boat Tour on Skaneateles Lake - Skaneateles Lake is one of the cleanest and clearest lakes in the world, and its historic boat tours provide a unique way to experience its beauty.

  • Visit a Winery or Brewery - The Finger Lakes region is known for its wine and beer, and Skaneateles has several wineries and breweries that offer tastings and tours.

  • Take a Cooking Class at Skaneateles Artisans - This culinary school offers classes in a variety of cuisines, using fresh, locally sourced ingredients.

Week-Long Itinerary for Skaneateles

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Skaneateles, here is a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Explore the Skaneateles Historical Society and Creamery Museum, and enjoy dinner at The Krebs, a former historic mansion turned farm-to-table restaurant.

Day 2: Take a scenic boat tour on Skaneateles Lake, followed by a wine tasting at Anyela’s Vineyards.

Day 3: Go hiking or mountain biking at Baltimore Woods Nature Center, followed by dinner at Joelle’s French Bistro.

Day 4: Visit the John D. Barrow Art Gallery, and then take a cooking class at Skaneateles Artisans.

Day 5: Spend the day at Bear Swamp State Forest, with a picnic lunch from Rhubarb Kitchen.

Day 6: Visit a brewery, such as Skaneateles Brewery, and then enjoy dinner at the historic Sherwood Inn.

Day 7: Relax with a spa day at Mirbeau Inn & Spa, followed by a final dinner at Moro’s Table.

Food and Drink Scene of Skaneateles

Skaneateles is a foodie’s paradise, with a diverse range of restaurants, wineries, and breweries. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cider Braised Pork Shank - The Krebs serves a delicious pork shank braised in cider and herbs.

  • Lobster Bisque - Joelle’s French Bistro offers a rich, creamy lobster bisque that is a must-try.

  • Smoked Trout - Rhubarb Kitchen’s smoked trout sandwich is a perfect lunch option.

Local Beverages

  • Finger Lakes Wine - Skaneateles is surrounded by wineries, and you can’t go wrong with any of their award-winning options.

  • Skaneateles Brewery Beer - This microbrewery serves a variety of craft beers made on-site, including their popular Hefeweizen.

  • Coffee or Tea at Creekside Coffeehouse - This cozy coffeehouse serves up delicious coffee and tea drinks, as well as locally sourced baked goods.

Where to Stay in Skaneateles: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Skaneateles has several accommodation options, ranging from luxurious hotels to cozy guesthouses. Here are a few options:

  • Mirbeau Inn & Spa - This luxurious inn and spa offers a variety of luxurious guest rooms and suites, as well as spa treatments and fine dining.

  • The Sherwood Inn - This historic inn offers elegant accommodations in the heart of Skaneateles.

  • 10 Fitch Luxurious Romantic Inn - This award-winning bed and breakfast offers luxurious accommodations, including fireplaces and Jacuzzis.

What to Buy in Skaneateles: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Skaneateles is known for its charming boutiques and local artisan products. Here are some souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  • Skaneateles Pottery - This local pottery offers unique, handmade pieces inspired by the natural beauty of Skaneateles.

  • Finger Lakes Wine - Don’t forget to bring home a bottle of Finger Lakes Wine as a souvenir.

  • Local Maple Syrup - The Finger Lakes region is known for its maple syrup, and you can find locally sourced syrup at the Skaneateles Farmers Market.

Local Culture and Customs of Skaneateles

Skaneateles is a friendly and welcoming village, known for its community events and festivals. Here are some local customs and culture to keep in mind:

  • Support Local Businesses - Skaneateles is a small village, and supporting local businesses is important to maintaining its community spirit.

  • Participate in Community Events - Skaneateles hosts several community events throughout the year, including the Dickens Christmas and the Skaneateles Festival.

Best Time to Visit Skaneateles

The best time to visit Skaneateles is in the summer or fall, when the weather is warm and the foliage is at its peak. The village also hosts several events and festivals during these seasons.

Do’s and Don’ts of Skaneateles

Do:

  • Take a Boat Tour on Skaneateles Lake - You won’t want to miss the opportunity to experience the crystal-clear waters of Skaneateles Lake.

  • Try the Local Wine and Beer - The Finger Lakes region is known for its wine and beer, and Skaneateles has several wineries and breweries that are definitely worth a visit.

  • Hike or Bike through the Scenic Trails - Skaneateles has several scenic hiking and biking trails that provide stunning views of the lake and countryside.

Don’t:

  • Forget to Bring a Sweater or Jacket - Even in the summer, Skaneateles can get chilly in the evenings, so make sure to bring a sweater or jacket.

  • Miss Out on the Local Food Scene - Skaneateles has some amazing restaurants and cafes that serve delicious locally sourced food. Don’t miss out on the chance to try them!

  • Litter or Damage the Natural Environment - Skaneateles is known for its beautiful natural scenery, so take care to avoid littering or damaging the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Skaneateles

Skaneateles is a safe village, but like any tourist destination, it’s important to take basic safety precautions. Here are some tips:

  • Be Aware of Your Surroundings - While Skaneateles is generally safe, it’s always a good idea to be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.

  • Protect Your Belongings - Keep your valuables secure and be cautious of pickpockets in crowded areas.

  • Swim in Designated Areas - Skaneateles Lake has designated swimming areas that are safe and monitored by lifeguards.

Health in Skaneateles

Skaneateles has several medical facilities that provide general and emergency care. Here are some options:

  • Skaneateles Medical Center - This medical center offers general medical care, including urgent care and laboratory services.

  • Auburn Community Hospital - This hospital, located about 20 minutes outside Skaneateles, provides emergency and specialized medical care.

Getting to Skaneateles: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Skaneateles

Skaneateles is located about 35 minutes from Syracuse Hancock International Airport. From there, you can rent a car or take a taxi or shuttle to Skaneateles.

Bus Stations Near Skaneateles

The nearest bus station to Skaneateles is located in Auburn, about 20 minutes away. From there, you can take a taxi or rental car to Skaneateles.

Other Options

If you are driving, Skaneateles is easily accessible from major highways, including Interstate 90. If you are taking the train, the nearest station is located in Syracuse, about 30 minutes from Skaneateles.

How to Get Around in Skaneateles

Skaneateles is a small village and is easily walkable. However, if you prefer to drive, rental cars and taxis are available.

Where to Go Next from Skaneateles: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Skaneateles is surrounded by charming small towns and natural attractions, making it a great base for exploring the Finger Lakes region. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Watkins Glen State Park - About an hour away from Skaneateles, this state park boasts 19 waterfalls and stunning gorge trails.

  • Auburn - About 20 minutes from Skaneateles, this historic town is home to several museums and cultural attractions.

  • Finger Lakes Wine Trail - Skaneateles is situated in the heart of the Finger Lakes wine region, and a self-guided tour of the wine trail is a fun way to explore its vineyards and wineries.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventure, cultural experiences, or simply a relaxing getaway, Skaneateles has something for everyone. From its clear lake to charming downtown and historic culture, Skaneateles is the perfect destination for a Finger Lakes vacation.
