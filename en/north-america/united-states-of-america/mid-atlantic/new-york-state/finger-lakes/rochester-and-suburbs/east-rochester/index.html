Introduction to East Rochester

East Rochester is a charming village located in the Finger Lakes region of New York. With its rich history, natural beauty, and strong local culture, it’s a fantastic destination for travelers looking to explore a less touristy side of the state.

List of Places to Visit in East Rochester

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • East Rochester Historical Society Museum: This small but well-curated museum is housed in a historic 19th-century schoolhouse and showcases the local history of East Rochester and the surrounding area.

  • Susan B. Anthony Museum and House: Located just a short drive from East Rochester, the Susan B. Anthony Museum and House is a must-visit for history buffs and anyone interested in women’s rights. Anthony lived in this home for over 40 years and it has been preserved as a National Historic Landmark.

Natural Attractions of East Rochester: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ellison Park: With over 447 acres of scenic trails, sports fields, and picnic areas, Ellison Park is a popular destination for locals and visitors alike. Don’t miss the picturesque Lucien Morin Park within the park, with its cascading waterfall and serene pond.

  • Powder Mills Park: This park boasts over 380 acres of woodlands, fishing streams, and hiking trails. There’s also a fish hatchery and historical museum on-site.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East Rochester

  • Auburn Trail: This 9-mile trail runs from near Fairport to Pittsford, and passes right through East Rochester. It’s a great option for bikers and hikers alike, with plenty of scenic spots to stop and take in the views along the way.

  • Corbett’s Glen Nature Park: This small but beautiful park is just a short drive from East Rochester and features winding trails that lead through lush forests and past a tranquil waterfall.

Best Things to Do in East Rochester: Activities and Tours

  • Music on the Porches: Every August, East Rochester hosts Music on the Porches, a two-day music festival featuring over 70 local musicians. It’s a great time to experience the town’s vibrant music scene and soak up the community spirit.

  • East Rochester Farmers Market: Open from May through September, the East Rochester Farmers Market is a great place to sample local produce and homemade goods, as well as mingle with the friendly locals.

Food and Drink Scene of East Rochester

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nick’s Seabreeze Inn: This family-owned restaurant has been serving up fresh, delicious seafood since the 1920s. Don’t miss their famous clam chowder or the lobster bisque.

  • Hansen’s Meat Market: This old-school butcher shop is a local favorite, with a wide selection of meats and homemade sausages. Be sure to try their famous bologna, a regional specialty.

Local Beverages

  • Three Heads Brewing: Located just a few miles from East Rochester, Three Heads Brewing is a popular destination for beer lovers. They offer a rotating selection of craft beers on tap, as well as food from local vendors.

  • Embark Craft Ciderworks: This apple orchard and cidery offers a variety of unique hard ciders made with locally grown apples. Stop by for a tasting or a tour of the orchard.

Where to Stay in East Rochester: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Dartmouth House Bed and Breakfast: This charming B&B is housed in a historic Victorian home and offers luxurious rooms and a homemade breakfast.

  • East Avenue Inn and Suites: Located just a short drive from East Rochester, this hotel offers spacious rooms and a convenient location near downtown Rochester.

What to Buy in East Rochester: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Locally-made crafts: East Rochester is home to several artisans and craft shops, so be sure to pick up a unique handmade item as a souvenir.

  • Maple syrup: The Finger Lakes region is known for its maple syrup production, so don’t leave without trying some of the delicious local syrup.

Local Culture and Customs of East Rochester

East Rochester is known for its friendly and welcoming community spirit, so don’t be surprised if you’re struck up in conversation by a local. The town also has a strong sense of community pride, as evidenced by the many local festivals and events that take place throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit East Rochester

The best time to visit East Rochester is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. However, there’s plenty to see and do in the summer months as well, particularly during the Music on the Porches festival in August.

Do’s and Don’ts of East Rochester

Do:

  • Do take the time to explore the small shops and artisan craft stores around town.

  • Do try the local cider and craft beer.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to pack comfortable hiking shoes if you plan to explore the town’s outdoor attractions.

  • Don’t disrespect the town’s proud history and community spirit.

Safety and Health

Safety in East Rochester

East Rochester is a safe and friendly town, with low crime rates and a close-knit community. However, as with any destination, it’s important to keep an eye on your belongings and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in East Rochester

There are several medical and dental clinics located in and around East Rochester, as well as a hospital just a short drive away in nearby Rochester.

Getting to East Rochester: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near East Rochester

  • Greater Rochester International Airport: Located just 10 miles from East Rochester, this airport offers daily domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near East Rochester

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: Serves the Rochester area and stops at the downtown Rochester bus station, just a few miles from East Rochester.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car is a convenient option for exploring the surrounding Finger Lakes region.

  • Bike rental: Biking is a popular and eco-friendly way to explore East Rochester, and several rental shops can be found in town.

How to Get Around in East Rochester

The best way to get around in East Rochester is by car or bike, as public transportation is limited. However, most attractions are within walking distance in the small downtown area.

Where to Go Next from East Rochester: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Finger Lakes Region: Just a short drive from East Rochester, the Finger Lakes region is known for its scenic beauty and excellent wineries.

  • Letchworth State Park: Located about an hour’s drive from East Rochester, Letchworth State Park boasts 14,000 acres of natural beauty and is known as the “Grand Canyon of the East.”

Conclusion

East Rochester is a hidden gem in the Finger Lakes region, offering a unique blend of history, natural beauty, and local culture. Whether you’re looking to hike, bike, or simply soak up the community spirit, there’s plenty to see and do in this charming village. Plan your trip now to discover all that East Rochester has to offer!
