Introduction to Bushnell’s Basin

Bushnell’s Basin is a charming hamlet in the town of Perinton, located just 10 miles southeast of Rochester. It is known for its quaint historic district, quaint local businesses, and scenic location on the Erie Canal and North Country Trail. Bushnell’s Basin attracts visitors who are looking for a peaceful escape from city life while still being close to all the amenities that Rochester has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Bushnell’s Basin

Here are some of the top places to visit in Bushnell’s Basin:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bushnell’s Basin Historic District: This picturesque district is home to several historic buildings dating back to the 1800s, including the Bushnell’s Basin United Methodist Church and the former home of pioneer Doctor Davis. Take a stroll through the district and admire the charming architecture.

  • Fairport Historical Museum: Located just a mile down the Erie Canal, this museum showcases the history of the town of Perinton and the Erie Canal. Exhibitions include artifacts and photographs from the canal’s construction and operation, as well as exhibits on the area’s agriculture, Native American history, and more.

Natural Attractions of Bushnell’s Basin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Erie Canalway Trail: This multi-use trail stretches 360 miles from Buffalo to Albany and passes through Bushnell’s Basin. It is a popular spot for walking, cycling, and rollerblading, offering scenic views of the Erie Canal.

  • North Country Trail: This 4,600-mile trail passes through seven states, including New York, and is accessible from Bushnell’s Basin. It offers wilderness hiking and camping experiences, with stunning views of local flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bushnell’s Basin

There are many hiking and biking routes in Bushnell’s Basin for outdoor enthusiasts to explore. Some favorite trails include:

  • Erie Canalway Trail: This 360-mile trail offers scenic views of the Erie Canal and is perfect for cycling and walking.

  • Perinton Hike and Bike Trail: This trail begins at Egypt Park in Perinton and passes through Bushnell’s Basin on its way to the town of Pittsford. It is a great trail for hiking and biking, and it offers lovely views of Perinton’s rolling hills and historic buildings.

Best Things to Do in Bushnell’s Basin: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours in Bushnell’s Basin that are perfect for visitors of all ages. Some of the top things to do include:

  • Erie Canal Boat Tours: Take a relaxing boat tour along the Erie Canal and learn about the history of the waterway and its impact on the local area.

  • Farmers Market: Visit the local farmers market on Tuesday afternoons and sample some of the delicious local produce.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bushnell’s Basin

Day 1: Explore the Bushnell’s Basin Historic District and attend a farmers market.

Day 2: Take a boat tour along the Erie Canal and visit the Fairport Historical Museum.

Day 3: Hike or bike the Perinton Hike and Bike Trail, and enjoy scenic views of the surrounding hills.

Day 4: Visit nearby Rochester and explore the city’s attractions, such as the Rochester Museum and Science Center and Highland Park.

Day 5: Take a day trip to the Finger Lakes region and visit local wineries and breweries.

Day 6: Explore more of the North Country Trail.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy Bushnell’s Basin’s scenic beauty on a peaceful walk or picnic along the canal.

Food and Drink Scene of Bushnell’s Basin

Bushnell’s Basin has a diverse food and drink scene, with plenty of local favorites to taste. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • White Hots: This regional delicacy is a type of hot dog made from finely ground pork and veal. It is typically served with spicy mustard and onions.

  • Grape Pie: This dessert is a specialty of the Finger Lakes region, and it is made with concord grapes.

Local Beverages

  • Genesee Beer: This Rochester-based brewery is known for its classic American lagers and ales.

  • Finger Lakes Wine: The Finger Lakes region is home to over 100 wineries, producing award-winning Rieslings and other varietals.

Where to Stay in Bushnell’s Basin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bushnell’s Basin has a variety of accommodation options for visitors. Here are some of the top places to stay:

  • Woodcliff Hotel and Spa: This luxury hotel offers stunning views of the Rochester skyline and is surrounded by wooded hillsides and lush greens.

  • Perinton Inn: This charming bed and breakfast is located in the historic district of Bushnell’s Basin and offers cozy rooms with antique furnishings.

What to Buy in Bushnell’s Basin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are plenty of souvenirs and local products to take home from Bushnell’s Basin. Here are a few ideas:

  • Erie Canal Memorabilia: Pick up a souvenir from the Erie Canal Museum, such as a t-shirt or mug.

  • Local Honey: There are many local honey producers in the area, and their honey is said to have a unique flavor due to the area’s flora.

Local Culture and Customs of Bushnell’s Basin

Bushnell’s Basin is a small town with a tight-knit community, and visitors will find that locals are welcoming and friendly. The town is proud of its history, particularly its role in the Erie Canal’s construction, and visitors will find many historic landmarks and monuments dotted throughout the town.

Best Time to Visit Bushnell’s Basin

The best time to visit Bushnell’s Basin is during the spring and fall months, when the weather is mild and the fall leaves are changing color. The summer months can be hot and humid, while the winter months can be cold and snowy.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bushnell’s Basin

Bushnell’s Basin is a generally safe town, but visitors should take common sense precautions such as locking car doors and securing valuables. Visitors should also be aware of their surroundings, particularly when hiking or biking on trails.

Health in Bushnell’s Basin

There are several medical facilities located near Bushnell’s Basin, including Urgent Care centers and pharmacies. Visitors should also be aware of local ticks and mosquitoes and take appropriate precautions, such as wearing long sleeves and pants and using bug repellent.

Getting to Bushnell’s Basin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bushnell’s Basin

The closest airport to Bushnell’s Basin is the Greater Rochester International Airport, located about 18 miles away. Visitors can rent a car or take a taxi or shuttle service from the airport to Bushnell’s Basin.

Bus Stations Near Bushnell’s Basin

The closest bus station to Bushnell’s Basin is the Trailways Bus Station in downtown Rochester, located about 10 miles away. Visitors can take a taxi or rideshare service from the bus station to Bushnell’s Basin.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Bushnell’s Basin by taking Amtrak or Greyhound to Rochester and then taking a taxi or rideshare service to Bushnell’s Basin.

How to Get Around in Bushnell’s Basin

Visitors can easily get around Bushnell’s Basin on foot or by bike. Rental cars are also available for visitors who prefer to drive.

Where to Go Next from Bushnell’s Basin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
