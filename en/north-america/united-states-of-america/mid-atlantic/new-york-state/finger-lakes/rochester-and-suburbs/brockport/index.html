Introduction to Brockport

Nestled in the heart of the Finger Lakes region, Brockport is a small, charming village with a rich history and a variety of things to see and do. Located in western New York State, Brockport is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and anyone looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life.

Whether you’re planning a weekend getaway or an extended vacation, Brockport offers something for everyone. From natural attractions and hiking trails to cultural landmarks and museums, there’s no shortage of things to explore in this picturesque town.

List of Places to Visit in Brockport

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Brockport is steeped in history, and there are plenty of cultural attractions to explore in the area. Some of the top museums and historical landmarks in Brockport include:

  • Seymour Library - A beautiful, historic library that dates back to 1869 and features a stunning collection of books, artwork, and artifacts.
  • Emily L. Knapp Museum and Library - An impressive collection of local history that includes exhibits on Native American life, early settlers, and the Erie Canal.
  • Morgan-Manning House - A beautifully restored historic home that provides a fascinating glimpse into life in Brockport during the 19th century.

Natural Attractions of Brockport: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Brockport is surrounded by natural beauty, and there are plenty of outdoor attractions to enjoy. Some of the must-see natural landmarks in Brockport include:

  • Canal Park - A picturesque, tree-lined park that sits alongside the historic Erie Canal and offers walking and biking trails, picnic areas, and scenic views of the water.
  • Brockport Dam - A stunning man-made waterfall that provides a peaceful and serene backdrop for picnicking or relaxing.
  • Greenwood Park - A family-friendly destination that features hiking and nature trails, a playground, and a large community pool.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Brockport

Brockport is a hiker’s paradise, with a variety of trails and paths for both novice and experienced hikers. Some of the top hiking and biking trails in the area include:

  • Erie Canal Trail - This scenic trail offers stunning views of the Erie Canal and the surrounding countryside, and runs for miles through the heart of the village.
  • Greenwood Park Trail - A beautiful wooded trail that winds through the park and offers plenty of opportunities for birdwatching and wildlife spotting.
  • SUNY Brockport Trail - A challenging hiking trail that takes you through the woods and up onto the ridge overlooking the college campus.

Best Things to Do in Brockport: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or outdoor adventure, there’s no shortage of things to see and do in Brockport. Some of the top activities and tours in the area include:

  • Erie Canal Cruise - A scenic and informative boat tour of the historic Erie Canal.
  • Golfing - Brockport features a number of beautiful and challenging golf courses, including Deerfield Country Club and Lakeside Country Club.
  • Brockport Farmer’s Market - A bustling farmer’s market that features fresh produce, baked goods, crafts, and more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Brockport

If you’re planning an extended stay in Brockport, consider the following itinerary for a memorable week-long vacation:

  • Day 1: Explore downtown Brockport and the historic Morgan-Manning House.
  • Day 2: Take a scenic boat tour of the Erie Canal and enjoy a picnic lunch at Canal Park.
  • Day 3: Hike the SUNY Brockport Trail and enjoy lunch at one of the village’s charming cafes.
  • Day 4: Spend the day at Greenwood Park, hiking, swimming, and enjoying the natural beauty of the area.
  • Day 5: Visit the Emily L. Knapp Museum and Library to learn more about the area’s history, then enjoy a round of golf at Deerfield Country Club.
  • Day 6: Take a day trip to nearby Rochester to explore the city’s museums and cultural attractions.
  • Day 7: Relax, unwind, and enjoy some of the village’s excellent dining options, such as Stoneyard Brewing Company or Jimmy Z’s.

Food and Drink Scene of Brockport

Brockport is home to a variety of delicious dining options, from casual cafes to fine dining restaurants. Some of the top dishes and beverages to try in Brockport include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Garbage Plate - A local specialty that features a variety of proteins, such as hamburger patties and hot dogs, served alongside macaroni salad, French fries, and a variety of toppings.
  • White Hots - A regional variation of the hot dog that features a white casing and a unique blend of spices.
  • Cornell Chicken - A local favorite that features marinated grilled chicken with a tangy, herb-infused sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Genny Cream Ale - A beloved regional beer that has been brewed in nearby Rochester since 1878.
  • Finger Lakes Wine - The Finger Lakes region is famous for its wineries, and Brockport is the perfect place to start exploring the area’s wine scene.
  • Apple Cider - A fall favorite that is available at local orchards and the Brockport Farmer’s Market.

Where to Stay in Brockport: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Brockport offers a variety of accommodation options, from charming bed and breakfasts to budget-friendly hostels. Some of the top places to stay in Brockport include:

  • The Pillars Estate - A beautifully restored mansion that offers luxury accommodations and plenty of Victorian charm.
  • Bella Rose Bed and Breakfast - A cozy and welcoming bed and breakfast that provides a warm and inviting home away from home.
  • Econo Lodge Brockport - A budget-friendly option that offers comfortable and convenient accommodation in the heart of Brockport.

What to Buy in Brockport: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring home a souvenir or local product from your time in Brockport, consider the following options:

  • Erie Canal memorabilia - From t-shirts to fridge magnets, there’s no shortage of souvenirs that pay homage to the historic Erie Canal.
  • Finger Lakes wine - Take a bottle (or two) of Finger Lakes wine home with you as a reminder of your time in the area.
  • Local produce - From fresh apples to artisanal cheeses, there are plenty of delicious local products to take home from the Brockport Farmer’s Market.

Local Culture and Customs of Brockport

Brockport is known for its friendly and welcoming community, and visitors are sure to feel at home in this charming village. Some of the local customs and traditions to keep in mind include:

  • Community engagement - Brockport residents take pride in their village and are often involved in community events and activities.
  • Respect for history - Brockport has a rich history, and visitors are encouraged to explore the village’s many historic landmarks and museums.
  • Love of the outdoors - With so much natural beauty around, it’s no surprise that Brockport residents are passionate about outdoor activities like hiking, fishing, and biking.

Best Time to Visit Brockport

Brockport is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on your interests and preferences.

  • Summer - The summer months (June-August) are perfect for outdoor activities like hiking, biking, and swimming. The warm weather and long days make it easy to explore all that Brockport has to offer.
  • Fall - The fall months (September-November) are beautiful in Brockport, with vibrant fall foliage and plenty of fun fall events like the Brockport Farmer’s Market and the AppleUmpkin Festival.
  • Winter - While it can be cold and snowy in Brockport during the winter months (December-February), this is a great time to experience winter sports like skiing, snowboarding, and ice fishing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Brockport

Do:

  • Explore the Erie Canal - The historic Erie Canal is one of the most iconic attractions in Brockport, and exploring it by boat, bike, or foot is a must-do.
  • Try the local cuisine - Brockport has a unique and delicious culinary scene, and trying the local specialties like the Garbage Plate or Cornell Chicken is a must.
  • Experience the natural beauty - From the stunning Brockport Dam to the peaceful Greenwood Park, Brockport is surrounded by natural beauty that is not to be missed.

Don’t:

  • Overlook the culture and history - Brockport has a rich history that is worth exploring, from the Emily L. Knapp Museum and Library to the historic Morgan-Manning House.
  • Forget proper outdoor etiquette - Respect the natural environment by taking any trash with you and adhering to proper hiking and biking etiquette when on the trails.
  • Underestimate the weather - Like most places in upstate New York, the weather in Brockport can be unpredictable, so be sure to pack for all types of weather and seasons.

Safety and Health

Safety in Brockport

Brockport is a relatively safe and crime-free community, but visitors should still exercise common sense and stay alert while exploring the village. Some safety tips to keep in mind include:

  • Stay aware of your surroundings - Whether walking, biking, or driving, always remain alert and aware of your surroundings.
  • Be cautious at night - While Brockport is generally a safe community, it’s always a good idea to use caution when walking alone at night.
  • Be prepared for inclement weather - Brockport can experience everything from snowstorms to thunderstorms, so travelers should be prepared for all types of weather conditions.

Health in Brockport

Brockport has a variety of medical facilities and healthcare providers in the area, and visitors can rest easy knowing that medical care is never far away. Some of the top healthcare providers in Brockport include:

  • Brockport Urgent Care - A top-notch urgent care center that offers quality healthcare services for all types of medical conditions.
  • Unity Hospital of Rochester - A full-service hospital that provides advanced medical care and treatment for a variety of health issues.
  • Walgreens Pharmacy - A convenient and accessible pharmacy that offers a range of health and wellness products, as well as prescription medications.

Getting to Brockport: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Brockport

The nearest major airport to Brockport is the Greater Rochester International Airport, which is located approximately 25 miles away from the village. Other nearby airports include Buffalo Niagara International Airport and Syracuse Hancock International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Brockport

Several bus companies operate in the area, including Greyhound and Trailways. The village also has its own public transportation system, which provides convenient and affordable transport around Brockport and the surrounding areas.

Other Options

For those driving to Brockport, there are two major highways that pass through the village: the New York State Thruway (I-90) and State Route 531. Brockport is also located on the historic Erie Canal, making it accessible by boat.

How to Get Around in Brockport

Brockport is a relatively small village, and most visitors find that walking or biking is the best way to get around. In addition to walking and biking, there are also a number of public transportation options available, including buses and shuttles.

Where to Go Next from Brockport: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of New York State after your visit to Brockport, there are plenty of exciting destinations and attractions nearby. Some of the top nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Rochester - With its museums, art galleries, and cultural landmarks like the Strong Museum of Play, Rochester is a great destination for anyone interested in history and culture.
  • Niagara Falls - The breathtaking natural wonder of Niagara Falls is just a few hours’ drive from Brockport, making it an ideal day trip.
  • Finger Lakes Region - With its beautiful lakes, incredible vineyards, and charming small towns, the Finger Lakes region is a must-see for anyone visiting Brockport.

Conclusion

Brockport may be a small village, but it offers a wealth of activities, attractions, and natural beauty for visitors to explore. Whether you’re interested in hiking and biking, history and culture, or just relaxing and soaking up the local atmosphere, Brockport is the perfect destination for a fun and memorable vacation. With its charming Bed and Breakfasts, delicious dining options, and friendly community, Brockport is sure to leave you with lasting memories and a desire to return again and again.
