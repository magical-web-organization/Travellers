Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Spencerport is steeped in history, and it has numerous museums and historical landmarks worth visiting. The Spencerport Depot & Canal Museum is a great place to learn about the village’s early history and its importance to the Erie Canal. The Ogden Historical Society Museum showcases the history of Ogden - the town in which Spencerport is located. Other notable historical places include the Brockport-Spencerport Cemetery, Spencerport United Methodist Church, and Colby-Pulver House.

Natural Attractions of Spencerport: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Spencerport has an array of natural attractions that provide breathtaking views and excellent outdoor adventures. Head to Northampton Park for hiking, fishing, and camping. For picturesque views of the Erie Canal, visit the Canal Park. The Hopper Hills-Montezuma Marsh Wildlife Management Area is a birdwatcher’s paradise, as it is home to an array of water fowls, including bald eagles, ospreys, and great blue herons.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Spencerport

Spencerport has incredible hiking trails that cater to hikers of all skill levels. Opt for a leisurely hike on the Spencerport Canal Trail or challenge yourself with the Northampton Park Trail System. For biking enthusiasts, the Erie Canalway Trail is an excellent choice.

Best Things to Do in Spencerport: Activities and Tours

Activities and tours are popular among visitors in Spencerport. Check out the annual Canal Days Festival, which brings together live entertainment, arts and crafts, and delicious food. The Seabreeze Amusement Park is another fun-filled adventure destination.

Week-Long Itinerary for Spencerport

Day 1: Visit the Spencerport Canal Trail and Canal Park Day 2: Visit the Spencerport Depot & Canal Museum and the Colby-Pulver House Day 3: Go hiking at Northampton Park Day 4: Tour the Ogden Historical Society Museum and the Brockport-Spencerport Cemetery Day 5: Visit Hopper Hills-Montezuma Marsh Wildlife Management Area Day 6: Explore the Seabreeze Amusement Park Day 7: Go shopping for souvenirs and local products before heading back home

Food and Drink Scene of Spencerport

The food and drink scene in Spencerport is fantastic, offering an array of options for visitors. For traditional American cuisine, head to the Village Cafe. For a taste of the Mediterranean, check out Amaya Mediterranean Cuisine. For some refreshing drinks, visit the Spencerport Winery or the Old Canal Inn.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken pot pies at the Village Cafe
  • Lamb gyros at Amaya Mediterranean Cuisine
  • Pizza at Perri’s Pizzeria
  • Bagels and pastries at Bruegger’s Bagels

Local Beverages

  • Local wines at Spencerport Winery
  • Craft beers at Blue Barn Cidery

What to Buy in Spencerport: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Spencerport, there are numerous souvenirs worth taking back home. The Spencerport Depot & Canal Shop sells apparel, jewelry, and home decor, all inspired by the village’s rich history. The Blue Barn Cidery also sells local wines and ciders perfect for gifting.

Local Culture and Customs of Spencerport

While in Spencerport, it is essential to respect the local culture and customs. The village values community and family and believes in cherishing traditions. Visitors are expected to be friendly and courteous.

Best Time to Visit Spencerport

The best time to visit Spencerport is between May and September when the weather is pleasant, and there are numerous outdoor activities to enjoy.

Safety and Health

Safety in Spencerport

Spencerport is generally safe. However, visitors should practice caution, especially when exploring natural areas. Always carry a map, water, and a first aid kit when hiking or camping.

Health in Spencerport

Visitors should ensure they have medical insurance in case of any emergency. Local hospitals include Unity Hospital and Rochester General Hospital.
