Introduction to Rochester and Suburbs

Located in the Finger Lakes region of New York, Rochester and its suburbs offer visitors a unique combination of natural beauty, history, culture, and urban attractions. Home to world-class museums, picturesque parks, scenic views, and gourmet food, Rochester is a top tourist destination on the East Coast.

From hiking trails and biking routes to art galleries and live music venues, Rochester and its suburbs offer something for everyone. Whether you’re traveling solo, with friends, or family, this comprehensive tour guide to Rochester and suburbs will help you plan a memorable trip.

List of Places to Visit in Rochester and Suburbs

Here is a list of must-visit places in Rochester and suburbs:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Strong Museum of Play: This interactive museum features exhibits on games, toys, and pop culture from around the world.

  2. George Eastman Museum: Dedicated to photography and film, this museum showcases the life and work of George Eastman, the founder of Kodak.

  3. Susan B. Anthony Museum and House: Former home of women’s rights activist Susan B. Anthony, this museum offers a glimpse into her life and legacy.

  4. The National Museum of Play at The Strong: Another museum worth visiting for all ages.

  5. The Rochester Museum and Science Center: Explore exhibits on science, history, and natural wonders.

Natural Attractions of Rochester and Suburbs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Seneca Park Zoo: Home to over 90 species of animals from around the world.

  2. Letchworth State Park: Known as the “Grand Canyon of the East,” this park features waterfalls, hiking trails, and scenic vistas.

  3. Ontario Beach Park: A great place to relax, swim, play beach volleyball or explore the small nature reserve.

  4. Genesee Riverway Trail: A bike path, walking trail and great site for fishing.

  5. Seabreeze Amusement Park: Thrill-seekers will love the rides at this family-friendly park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rochester and Suburbs

Rochester and its suburbs are home to numerous hiking and biking trails. Here are some of the popular routes:

  1. Erie Canalway Trail: This multi-use trail stretches over 360 miles from Buffalo to Albany.

  2. Genesee Riverway Trail: A 24-mile paved trail that runs along the Genesee River and offers stunning views of the city.

  3. Mendon Ponds Park: With over 2,500 acres of woodlands, ponds, and wetlands, this park offers miles of hiking and biking trails.

  4. Powder Mills Park: This park has over 6 miles of trails for hiking, biking, and horseback riding.

  5. Canal Path: A 15-mile trail that follows the Erie Canal and offers views of historic towns and villages.

Best Things to Do in Rochester and Suburbs: Activities and Tours

  1. Rochester Public Market: A great place to find fresh produce and food vendors.

  2. Rochester Philharmonic Orchestra: Voted one of the top orchestras in the country, the RPO offers a variety of concerts throughout the year.

  3. The Little Theatre: Showing indie and foreign films

  4. Rochester Pedal Tours: Explore the city’s pubs and bars on a bike tour.

  5. Genesee Brewery Tour: Learn about the history of beer-making and enjoy a tasting at this historic brewery.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rochester and Suburbs

Day 1: Arrival and check-in

Day 2: Visit the Strong Museum of Play, Susan B. Anthony Museum and House, and Mount Hope Cemetery

Day 3: Explore the Letchworth State Park, Erie Canalway Trail, and Genesee Riverway Trail.

Day 4: Take a city tour to see The Little Theatre and the George Eastman Museum.

Day 5: Spend the day at the Seneca Park Zoo and Ontario Beach Park.

Day 6: Take a bike tour of the Rochester Public Market and local breweries.

Day 7: Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Rochester and Suburbs

Here’s a list of must-try dishes and drinks in Rochester and suburbs:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Garbage Plate: A local favorite consisting of a combination of home fries, macaroni salad, baked beans, and meat sauce. Composed to your liking, usually.

  2. White hots: A type of sausage popular in upstate New York.

  3. Rochester-style pizza: Known for its thick crust and sweet sauce, this pizza is a must-try for pizza lovers.

  4. Nick Tahou Hots: Famous for its “Garbage Plate” and having an unknown origin dish.

Local Beverages

  1. Genesee Beer: Founded in 1878, Genesee is one of the best-known brewery in Rochester.

  2. Finger Lakes Wine: Visit one of the many wineries in the Finger Lakes region to try some of the best local wines in the country.

  3. Jolt cola: Created in Rochester, NY

Where to Stay in Rochester and Suburbs: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some of the best accommodation options in Rochester and suburbs:

  1. Strathallan Rochester Hotel & Spa: A luxury hotel located in the city with spa facilities, dining services, and other amenities.

  2. The Inn on Broadway: A boutique hotel in the heart of downtown Rochester with a restaurant and bar.

  3. Woodcliff Hotel & Spa: A resort-style hotel overlooking the Finger Lakes region, featuring a golf course and spa services.

What to Buy in Rochester and Suburbs: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

You can’t leave Rochester without trying some of its local products:

  1. Wegmans Products: Headquartered in Rochester, Wegmans is a regional grocery chain that sells locally made products and souvenirs.

  2. Finger Lakes Wine: The region is famous for its wine, with many vineyards offering tastings and tours.

  3. Cooper’s Hawk: An American winery that also has a restaurant and offers wine tasting.

Local Culture and Customs of Rochester and Suburbs

Rochester and its suburbs have a unique history and culture. Visitors can learn more about the city’s heritage at museums and historical sites.

Best Time to Visit Rochester and Suburbs

The best time to visit Rochester and its suburbs is during the spring and fall months when there is less humidity and the weather is cooler. Summer months are also a great time to visit for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rochester and Suburbs

Do:

  1. Visit the Strong Museum of Play for a fun and interactive experience.

  2. Take a hike/bike on one of the many trails around the Genesee River.

  3. Try local food and wine, especially the Garbage Plate and Finger Lakes Wine.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t forget to check the weather forecast before you plan your trip.

  2. Avoid littering on the hiking/biking trails and in public areas.

  3. Don’t ignore road signs and laws while driving on the Parkway.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rochester and Suburbs

Rochester is generally a safe city, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions like avoiding unfamiliar neighborhoods at night, locking your car doors, and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Rochester and Suburbs

Visitors must be vaccinated - specifically, flu, measles, mumps, rubella, and chickenpox (effective vaccines) and Hepatitis B if you anticipate any form of healthcare.

Where to Go Next from Rochester and Suburbs: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Niagara Falls: Located just two hours away, Niagara Falls is a must-see attraction in New York.

  2. Finger Lakes: A breathtaking region known for its wineries, lakes, and scenic beauty.

  3. Buffalo: A city with a rich history, impressive architecture, and a bustling food and arts scene.

Conclusion

If you’re planning a trip to the East Coast, be sure to add Rochester and its suburbs to your itinerary. With its unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty, Rochester is a top tourist destination for all kinds of travelers.
