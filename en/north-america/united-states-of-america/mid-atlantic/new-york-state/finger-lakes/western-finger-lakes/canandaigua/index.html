Introduction to Canandaigua

Located in the Western Finger Lakes region, Canandaigua is a small city in the state of New York, known for its stunning natural scenery, rich history, and exciting recreational activities. With its vibrant cultural scene, picturesque downtown, and beautiful lake, Canandaigua is a popular destination for tourists from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Canandaigua

Here are some of the must-visit places in Canandaigua:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sonnenberg Gardens and Mansion State Historic Park: A 50-acre estate with nine historic gardens, a Queen Anne style mansion, and a charming greenhouse complex.

  • Finger Lakes Wine Country: A world-renowned wine-growing region with over 100 wineries and vineyards.

  • Granger Homestead and Carriage Museum: A fascinating museum that showcases the lifestyle and transportation of a wealthy family in the early 1800s.

  • Ontario County Historical Society/Museum: A repository of local history, with over 55,000 artifacts, exhibits, and archival collections.

Natural Attractions of Canandaigua: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Canandaigua Lake: One of the ten Finger Lakes, Canandaigua Lake is known for its crystal-clear waters, sandy beaches, and stunning sunsets.

  • Bristol Mountain Ski Resort: A ski resort with 35 trails, four terrain parks, and majestic views of the surrounding mountain ranges.

  • Grimes Glen County Park: A hidden gem with three waterfalls and a scenic hiking trail.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Canandaigua

Canandaigua has a variety of hiking and biking trails for every level of experience. Some of the popular routes are:

  • Canandaigua Lakefront Trail: A scenic 4-mile trail that runs along the lake’s shore, offering stunning views of the water.

  • Ontario County Park at Gannett Hill: A beautiful park with 12 miles of hiking, biking, and horseback riding trails with gorgeous views of the surrounding hills.

  • Hemlock-Canadice State Forest: A vast forest reserve with over 6,000 acres of rolling hills, streams, and woodland.

Best Things to Do in Canandaigua: Activities and Tours

  • Canandaigua Wine Trail: A self-guided tour of the wineries and vineyards of the Finger Lakes region.

  • Roseland Water Park: A 56-acre water park with a variety of water attractions, including slides, wave pools, and a lazy river.

  • Canandaigua Lady: A majestic paddlewheel tour boat that sails on Canandaigua Lake through mid-June to mid-October.

Week-Long Itinerary for Canandaigua

Here is a suggested itinerary for spending a week in Canandaigua:

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore Sonnenberg Gardens and Mansion State Historic Park.
Day 2 Take a wine tour of Finger Lakes wine country
Day 3 Hike or bike Ontario County Park at Gannett Hill.
Day 4 Visit Grimes Glen County Park and see its three waterfalls.
Day 5 Relax at Canandaigua Lake, sunbathe on the sandy beaches or even go for a swim.
Day 6 Ski at Bristol Mountain Ski Resort.
Day 7 Take a paddlewheel tour on Canandaigua Lady boat.

Food and Drink Scene of Canandaigua

Canandaigua is a foodie’s paradise, with a wide selection of restaurants and eateries offering local and international flavors. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Grape Pie: A local specialty made with Canandaigua’s famous Concord grapes and a flaky crust.

  • Garbage Plate: A hearty plate of greasy goodness that includes a mix of meats, potatoes, beans, and sauce.

  • Chicken Spiedie: A marinated and grilled chicken sandwich served with a soft roll.

Local Beverages

  • Finger Lakes Wine: Canandaigua’s wineries produce world-class wines, including Riesling, Chardonnay, and Cabernet Franc.

  • Genesee Beer: A local favorite beer that’s been around since 1878.

  • Apple Cider: Canandaigua’s local orchards produce crisp, sweet apple cider that’s a perfect drink for fall.

Where to Stay in Canandaigua: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Canandaigua has a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Some of the best places to stay are:

  • The Inn on the Lake: A lakeside resort that offers stunning views of Canandaigua Lake.

  • Finger Lakes Bed and Breakfast: A charming B&B with cozy rooms and delicious homemade breakfast.

  • Canandaigua Lakefront Cottage: A private cottage with a beachfront and stunning views of the lake.

What to Buy in Canandaigua: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Canandaigua is known for its delightful souvenirs and local products that make excellent gifts for family and friends. Some must-buy souvenirs are:

  • Grape Jelly: Made from the locally harvested Concord grapes, grape jelly is a favorite local delicacy.

  • Pottery: Handmade pottery and ceramics by local artisans make unique gifts.

  • Maple Syrup: Canandaigua’s local maple syrup is a delicious and healthy sweetener.

Local Culture and Customs of Canandaigua

Canandaigua has a rich history and culture that reflects its diverse past. The town has a friendly and welcoming atmosphere, and the locals are proud of their heritage.

Best Time to Visit Canandaigua

The best time to visit Canandaigua is during the fall season from September to November when the weather is pleasant, and the foliage is stunning. The Spring season from March to May is another great time to visit, with fewer crowds and milder weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Canandaigua

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Canandaigua:

Do:

  • Do try the local wines and delicacies.

  • Do visit the natural attractions, such as the waterfalls and state parks.

  • Do explore the local culture and history at the museums and historical sites.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter, and respect the environment.

  • Don’t forget to tip the service staff in restaurants.

  • Don’t forget to check the weather before planning any outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Canandaigua

Canandaigua is a safe town with low crime rates. However, it’s always advisable to take precautions while visiting any new place, such as avoiding walking alone in secluded areas at night.

Health in Canandaigua

Canandaigua has excellent medical facilities and hospitals. However, it’s essential to take precautions and be cautious of tick bites, which are prevalent in the area. Dress in long-sleeved shirts and pants, use tick repellent, and check for ticks after being outside.

Getting to Canandaigua: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Canandaigua

The nearest airport to Canandaigua is Greater Rochester International Airport (ROC), located about 30 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Canandaigua

There are no bus stations directly in Canandaigua, but Greyhound Bus Lines serve nearby cities such as Rochester and Syracuse.

Other Options

The most convenient way to travel to Canandaigua is by car. Interstate 90 runs through the town, making it easily accessible from major cities such as Buffalo and Albany.

How to Get Around in Canandaigua

The best way to get around Canandaigua is by car. Car rental companies such as Enterprise, Hertz, and Budget are available in town. Taxis and Uber/Lyft services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Canandaigua: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Canandaigua’s central location makes it an excellent base for exploring nearby attractions such as:

  • Letchworth State Park: Known as the “Grand Canyon of the East,” this spectacular park is just a short drive from Canandaigua.

  • Watkins Glen State Park: A stunning park with 19 waterfalls, a gorge trail, and stunning views.

  • Rochester, New York: A major city located just a short 30-minute drive from Canandaigua, offers a plethora of attractions, including museums, theaters, and a lively nightlife.

Conclusion

Canandaigua is a charming town that offers something for everyone. From its beautiful natural attractions to its rich culture and history, Canandaigua is the ideal destination for a memorable vacation. Plan your trip to Canandaigua today and experience the magic of the Western Finger Lakes region.
