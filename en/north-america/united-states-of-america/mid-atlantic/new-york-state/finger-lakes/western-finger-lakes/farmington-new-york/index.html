Introduction to Farmington (New York)

Nestled in the heart of Western Finger Lakes, Farmington is a town known for its scenic beauty, fascinating history, and welcoming community. Located in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States, Farmington is a perfect destination for those who wish to explore the natural wonders and cultural hotspots of New York State.

Whether you are interested in outdoor adventures, museums, local cuisine, or peaceful getaways, Farmington has something for everyone. From the stunning views of Finger Lakes to the charm of small-town America, Farmington offers an unforgettable travel experience.

List of Places to Visit in Farmington (New York)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Oliver House Museum: This historic home turned museum houses a collection of artifacts and exhibits that showcase the history of Farmington and its contributions to the abolitionist movement in America.
  • Ontario County Historical Society Museum: Located in nearby Canandaigua, the museum displays artifacts and objects related to the rich history of Ontario County and the Finger Lakes region.
  • Ganondagan State Historic Site: A cultural center that celebrates the history and traditions of the Haudenosaunee people who lived in the area for centuries. The site includes a museum, a reconstructed longhouse, and hiking trails.

Natural Attractions of Farmington (New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Finger Lakes: A stunning series of 11 freshwater lakes that span 120 miles, offering picturesque views, boating, fishing, and swimming opportunities.
  • Mertensia Park: A serene park offering scenic walking trails, a playground, and a picnic area that is perfect for a day out with family.
  • Sonnenberg Gardens & Mansion State Historic Park: A beautiful estate that features several gardens, a restored mansion, and a wine center with a tasting room.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Farmington (New York)

Farmington is a paradise for nature lovers, with several trails and routes perfect for hiking or biking. Some of the most popular areas to explore are:

  • Erie Canalway Trail: A 360-mile trail that runs along the historic Erie Canal and offers scenic views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Mertensia Park Trail: A scenic trail that winds through the park’s woods and streams, and offers stunning views of the Finger Lakes region.
  • Macedon Canal Park: A beautiful park with a paved 6-mile trail along the Erie Canal that is perfect for biking or walking.

Best Things to Do in Farmington (New York): Activities and Tours

  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Experience the breathtaking beauty of the Finger Lakes region from above with a hot air balloon ride.
  • Wine Tasting Tours: Visit the vineyards and wineries of the Finger Lakes region and taste some of the best local wines.
  • Ontario County Fair: A yearly event that celebrates the agricultural heritage of the region, with live music, carnival rides, and more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Farmington (New York)

Day 1: Visit the Oliver House Museum and learn about the history of Farmington.

Day 2: Explore the scenic beauty of Finger Lakes with a scenic drive, a boat ride, or a wine tasting tour.

Day 3: Spend time at Mertensia Park, walk the trails, or hike the nearby hills.

Day 4: Discover the cultural heritage of the Haudenosaunee people at Ganondagan State Historic Site.

Day 5: Visit the vineyards of the Finger Lakes region and learn about wine production.

Day 6: Take a hot air balloon ride over the lakes and discover breathtaking views of the region from above.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy a picnic at Sonnenberg Gardens & Mansion State Historic Park

Food and Drink Scene of Farmington (New York)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Garbage Plate: A Rochester classic that consists of a plate of macaroni salad, home fries, cheeseburgers, and hot dogs all smothered in hot sauce and onions.
  • Beef on Weck: A sandwich made with roast beef and horseradish on a Kummelweck roll.

Local Beverages

  • Finger Lakes Wine: The Finger Lakes region is known for its wine production, with several vineyards producing high-quality wines.
  • Genesee Beer: A Rochester-based brewery that produces a variety of beers, including the iconic Genesee Cream Ale.

Where to Stay in Farmington (New York): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Woodcliff Hotel & Spa: A luxurious hotel with a spa, a golf course, and fine dining restaurant.
  • Farmington Inn: A budget-friendly option with comfortable rooms and easy access to local attractions.
  • Bella Vista Inn: A cozy bed and breakfast with scenic views of the Finger Lakes and a charming ambiance.

What to Buy in Farmington (New York): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maple Syrup: New York is known for its high-quality maple syrup, and Farmington is no exception.
  • Local Crafts and Artworks: Several local artisans sell their creations, including pottery, jewelry, and paintings.

Local Culture and Customs of Farmington (New York)

Farmington is a small town with a rich history and a strong sense of community. Visitors are welcome to explore the local culture and participate in community events. The town is known for its appreciation of the great outdoors and its agricultural heritage, so visitors are encouraged to sample local foods and enjoy the stunning scenery.

Best Time to Visit Farmington (New York)

The best time to visit Farmington is during the summer months (June-August), when the weather is warm and sunny, and outdoor activities are in full swing. However, fall (September-November) is also a great time to visit, as the leaves turn to vibrant shades of orange and red, making for beautiful scenery.

Do’s and Don’ts of Farmington (New York)

Do:

  • Explore the Finger Lakes and the surrounding countryside.
  • Try local foods and beverages.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Ignore traffic laws or safe hiking practices.
  • Disrespect the locals or their property.

Safety and Health

Safety in Farmington (New York)

Farmington is a safe town with a low crime rate. However, visitors are advised to take precautions, such as not leaving valuables in plain sight and avoiding isolated areas.

Health in Farmington (New York)

Visitors to Farmington should stay hydrated during hot weather and be aware of ticks and other pests in wooded areas. It is also a good idea to pack a basic first aid kit for outdoor activities.

Getting to Farmington (New York): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Farmington (New York)

  • Greater Rochester International Airport: Located 25 miles east, and offers daily flight connections to major cities in the USA.

Bus Stations Near Farmington (New York)

  • Trailways Bus Station in Rochester: Located 20 miles east, provides bus connections to several cities in New York and nearby states.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: A popular option for those who wish to explore the region independently.
  • Taxis and Private Transportation: Available from Rochester for an easy and quicker way to get to Farmington.

How to Get Around in Farmington (New York)

The best way to get around Farmington is by car. Biking and hiking are popular activities too.

Where to Go Next from Farmington (New York): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Rochester: A vibrant city with a rich cultural scene, museums, and a lively downtown.
  • Canandaigua: A charming lake town with several parks, trails, and cultural attractions.
  • Watkins Glen State Park: A stunning park with waterfalls, gorges, and hiking trails.

Conclusion

Farmington offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, rich history, and small-town charm. From the breathtaking views of Finger Lakes to the cultural sites that showcase the region’s past, Farmington is a perfect destination for those seeking an authentic travel experience. With its friendly community, local cuisine, and outdoor adventures, Farmington is truly a gem of New York State.
