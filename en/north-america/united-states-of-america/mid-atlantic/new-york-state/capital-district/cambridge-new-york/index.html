Introduction to Cambridge (New York)

Nestled in the heart of Washington County, New York, Cambridge offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, rich history, and small-town charm. Often called “the Gateway to the Adirondacks,” this picturesque town boasts scenic rolling hills, lush forests, and stunning waterfalls.

Cambridge is bursting with unique attractions, cultural events, and outdoor adventures that cater to individuals, families, and groups of all ages. With its vibrant downtown, cozy streets, and friendly locals, Cambridge is the perfect destination for any traveler looking to escape into a quaint and peaceful destination.

List of Places to Visit in Cambridge (New York)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hubbard Hall: This historic venue hosts performances, classes, and workshops in an intimate, cozy setting. This is a must-visit for anyone who loves the arts.

  • Cambridge Valley Farmers Market: Open every Sunday, this farmers market is perfect for those looking to indulge in delicious local produce, homemade baked goods, and artisanal products.

  • Underground Railroad History Project: This project aims to preserve the history of the Underground Railroad in the Capital District area, including the towns of Greenwich, Cambridge, and Salem.

  • Salem Courthouse: Located just 15 minutes from Cambridge, this historic courthouse has been restored and transformed into a cultural center, with art exhibitions, lectures, workshops, and more.

Natural Attractions of Cambridge (New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cambridge Valley Trail: Stretching over 9 miles, this scenic trail is perfect for walking, cycling, or jogging. It offers breathtaking views of the rolling hills, woods, and farms of the valley.

  • Glens Falls Farmers Market: Located just a short drive from Cambridge, this farmers market offers a wide variety of fresh produce, meats, cheeses, and artisanal products.

  • Lake Lauderdale: This serene lake offers plenty of recreational opportunities, such as swimming, kayaking, canoeing, fishing, and picnicking.

  • Battenkill River: Known for its excellent fishing opportunities, the Battenkill River is perfect for anglers of all levels. It’s also a great spot for swimming, tubing, and picnicking by the riverside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cambridge (New York)

  • Cambridge Valley Trail: This scenic trail is perfect for hiking or biking, with several access points throughout the trail. Enjoy the serene beauty of the valley while getting some exercise.

  • Battenkill River Trail: This hiking trail follows the Battenkill River as it meanders through the lush countryside, providing breathtaking views of the river and surrounding hills.

  • Taconic Crest Trail: This challenging hiking trail follows the ridgeline of the Taconic Mountains, offering stunning panoramic views of the valley below.

Best Things to Do in Cambridge (New York): Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking and Canoeing: Rent a kayak or canoe and explore the scenic waterways of the Cambridge area. The Battenkill River, Lake Lauderdale, and other nearby lakes and rivers offer plenty of opportunities for water-based fun.

  • Horseback Riding: Explore the rolling hills and forests of the area on horseback, with several stables offering guided trail rides.

  • Sightseeing Tours: Take a guided tour of the historic downtown area, or visit the nearby attractions of Salem Courthouse, Saratoga Springs, and more.

Food and Drink Scene of Cambridge (New York)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maple Pecan Pie: Made with locally-sourced maple syrup and crunchy pecans, this sweet treat is a must-try for anyone with a sweet tooth.

  • Chicken and Biscuits: This delicious comfort food dish features tender chicken in gravy, served over fluffy homemade biscuits.

  • Pulled Pork Sandwiches: Slow-cooked and seasoned to perfection, Cameron’s pulled pork sandwiches are finger-licking good.

Local Beverages

  • Hard Cider: The Cambridge area boasts several local cideries, offering a range of delicious hard ciders made with locally-sourced apples.

  • Craft Beer: The Capital District area is home to several craft breweries, offering a wide selection of IPAs, stouts, and other brews.

Where to Stay in Cambridge (New York): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Nest: A cozy bed and breakfast located in the heart of downtown Cambridge, offering comfortable and affordable rooms and hearty breakfasts.

  • The Cambridge Hotel: This historic hotel has been beautifully restored and offers luxurious rooms and suites, as well as an on-site restaurant and bar.

  • Camping: There are several campgrounds in the area, offering both RV and tent sites and plenty of outdoor recreation opportunities.

What to Buy in Cambridge (New York): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maple Syrup and Products: New York State is known for its delectable maple syrup, and Cambridge is no exception. Make sure to take home some maple syrup, maple candy, and other maple-based products.

  • Cheese: The Cambridge area is home to several artisanal cheesemakers, offering a range of delicious cheeses made from local milk.

Local Culture and Customs of Cambridge (New York)

Cambridge is a small town with a rich history and a strong sense of community. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local culture, traditions, and customs.

Best Time to Visit Cambridge (New York)

Cambridge is beautiful year-round, but the best time to visit is during the fall, when the leaves of the trees turn into vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow. The summer months are also perfect for outdoor adventures, such as kayaking, canoeing, and hiking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cambridge (New York)

Do:

  • Try the local delicacies, such as maple syrup products and artisanal cheeses.

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Explore the natural beauty of the area.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings.

  • Disrespect the locals or their culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cambridge (New York)

Cambridge is generally a safe destination. Visitors should still be aware of their surroundings, keep valuables out of sight, and be cautious when exploring rural areas.

Health in Cambridge (New York)

There are several medical facilities in the area, including hospitals and clinics. Visitors should also be aware of ticks during the summer months and take necessary precautions.

Getting to Cambridge (New York): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cambridge (New York)

The nearest major airport is the Albany International Airport, located about an hour’s drive from Cambridge.

Bus Stations Near Cambridge (New York)

The Capital District Transportation Authority (CDTA) offers bus services to nearby towns and cities, including Albany, Saratoga Springs, and more.

Other Options

Visitors can rent a car, take a taxi or shuttle, or use ride-sharing services like Uber or Lyft to get around the area.

How to Get Around in Cambridge (New York)

The best way to explore Cambridge and the surrounding areas is by car. There are also several bike rental shops and guided tours available for visitors who prefer to travel on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Cambridge (New York): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Saratoga Springs: This nearby city offers a range of cultural attractions, outdoor recreation opportunities, and dining and shopping options.

  • Greenwich: Just a short drive away, this historic town features charming streets, unique boutiques, and a variety of restaurants.

  • Lake George: Known as the “Queen of American Lakes,” Lake George is a popular destination for swimming, boating, and other water-based fun.

Conclusion

Cambridge (New York) is a hidden gem that offers something for everyone. From its natural beauty and outdoor recreation opportunities to its rich history and unique culture, this small town is sure to delight visitors of all ages.
