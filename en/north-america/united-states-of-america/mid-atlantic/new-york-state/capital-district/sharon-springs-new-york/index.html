Introduction to Sharon Springs (New York)

Sharon Springs, a small village located in the Capital District of New York, is a hidden gem of the state. The charming village is famous for its rich history, picturesque landscape, and welcoming community. Once a bustling spa town, Sharon Springs has now transformed into a desirable travel destination for those seeking a relaxing getaway in a serene setting. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the best things to see, do, and experience in Sharon Springs.

List of Places to Visit in Sharon Springs (New York)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sharon Springs boasts a rich history, and you can experience it by visiting its culturally significant museums and historical sites. Here are a few must-visit places:

  • Beekman 1802 Mercantile - Known for their artisanal crafts, Beekman 1802 Mercantile is a shopping destination for those who love locally-made goods. You can find a variety of products from home decor to skincare that are made in Sharon Springs.
  • Roseboro Hotel - Listed on the National Register of Historic Places, the Roseboro Hotel is a restored Victorian-era hotel built-in 1871. Today, the Roseboro Hotel operates as a bed and breakfast, where visitors can experience a luxurious stay with vintage charm.
  • Spring House Spa - A famous spa in the village, the Spring House Spa offers various treatments, including mineral baths, body treatments, facials, and massages. You can indulge in a relaxing spa experience amidst the serene beauty of Sharon Springs.

Natural Attractions of Sharon Springs (New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sharon Springs provides a picturesque view of nature and an array of adventurous activities for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some top-rated natural attractions to explore:

  • Camping at Limekiln Lake Campground - If you’re looking for an outdoor adventure, consider camping at Limekiln Lake Campground, which is just a short drive from Sharon Springs. The campground offers a serene environment with various activities, such as hiking, boating, and fishing.
  • Hiking at Betty Brook Trailhead - The Betty Brook Trailhead is a nature trail ideal for hikers and nature lovers. You can explore the greenery and wildlife of Sharon Springs while indulging in a tranquil hiking experience.
  • Canoeing and Kayaking - Sharon Springs has various lakes and rivers, making it an excellent destination for canoeing and kayaking. You can rent kayaks and canoes and explore the streams and waterways on your own.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sharon Springs (New York)

Sharon Springs is an ideal destination for hikers and bikers, offering a scenic view of nature and perfect terrain. Here are the best hiking and biking routes to explore in Sharon Springs:

  • Litchfield Loop - A 12-mile-long route that offers scenic views of the Catskill Mountains. The route is ideal for experienced bikers and hikers.
  • Canadarago Lake Bike Route - A 22-mile-long route that offers lake views, farms, and hills, making it an excellent route for both hikers and bikers.
  • Ny Berlin Loop - A 26-mile-long route that offers a scenic view of Hessenputtle Lake, making it an ideal spot for nature lovers.

Best Things to Do in Sharon Springs (New York): Activities and Tours

Sharon springs offer an array of activities and tours to make your trip more enjoyable. Here are a few must-try activities:

  • Sharon Springs Walking Tours - A comprehensive walking tour of the village that explores its history and provides a glimpse into the lives of its residents.
  • Wine Tasting at Cottlestone Winery - Cottlestone Winery offers tastings of their locally produced wines with a mesmerizing view of the countryside.
  • Kayak Rentals at Canadarago Lake - Rent kayaks and explore the lakes and surrounding areas on your own or with a guided tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sharon Springs (New York)

It’s recommended to spend at least a week exploring Sharon Springs and its surrounding areas. Here is a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1 - Arrive at Sharon Springs and explore the local markets, shops, and restaurants.
  • Day 2 - Visit Roseboro Hotel and Mineral Springs.
  • Day 3 - Explore Betty Brook Trailhead or Limekiln Lake Campground.
  • Day 4 - Take the Sharon Springer Walking Tour and visit the local museums.
  • Day 5 - Indulge in a relaxing spa experience at Spring House Spa.
  • Day 6 - Visit Cottlestone Winery and explore the Canadarago Lake with kayak rentals.
  • Day 7 - Depart Sharon Springs.

Food and Drink Scene of Sharon Springs (New York)

Must-Try Dishes

Sharon Springs offers an exquisite culinary experience as it has various farm-to-table restaurants. You must try these dishes while visiting:

  • Beef and Cheddar Sandwich from The Black Cat Cafe - The sandwich is made with locally raised beef and cheddar and served on freshly baked bread.
  • Maple Syrup from Pops Place - Pops Place is a popular eatery known for its pure maple syrup which is made with locally-produced maple syrup on pancakes or waffles.
  • Farm-to-table cuisine at The American Hotel - The American Hotel is a fine dining restaurant that serves locally produced meats, seafood, and vegetables. The restaurant is known for its exquisite menu featuring seasonal dishes.

Local Beverages

Sharon Springs is home to various local breweries, distilleries, and wineries. You must try these local beverages:

  • American Hotel’s Wine List - The American Hotel is known for its extensive wine list featuring various locally produced wines.
  • Dragging Canoe Whiskey from Albany Distilling Company - A quality small-batch, distillery-aged whiskey with bold flavor.
  • Big Alice Brewing’s Unique Brews - Big Alice Brewing is known for its unique beer flavors. The brewery uses locally sourced ingredients to brew various seasonal and limited edition beers.

Where to Stay in Sharon Springs (New York): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sharon Springs offers a wide array of hotels, hostels, and guesthouses to rest and relax. Here are some recommended options:

  • The Roseboro Hotel - A restored Victorian-era hotel that offers a luxurious stay with vintage charm.
  • The Alfred House Bed and Breakfast - A cozy Bed and Breakfast offering a relaxing stay in a serene environment.
  • Sunny Hill Resort and Golf Course - A family-friendly resort situated a few miles from Sharon Springs that provides various activities, including golf, swimming, and skiing.

What to Buy in Sharon Springs (New York): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sharon Springs is famous for its unique, artisanal products that you can pick up as souvenirs or gifts. Here are some suggestions:

  • Beekman 1802 Products - You can purchase a variety of locally-made products, such as skincare, home decor, and cookbooks.
  • Maple Syrup from Pops Place - You can buy locally produced pure maple syrup, which is the perfect gift for a foodie.
  • Handmade Jewelry from Shaker Boxes - Shaker boxes, handmade by local artisans, are beautiful and unique pieces of jewelry that you can take home as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Sharon Springs (New York)

Sharon Springs has a rich cultural history, and you can experience it by participating in its local customs and events. Here are a few notable customs that you should be aware of:

  • Harvest Festival - A famous festival celebrated in Sharon Springs, where locals celebrate the harvest season with local food and drinks.
  • Beekman 1802 Mercantile Seasonal Events - Beekman 1802 Mercantile hosts various seasonal events, such as the Fall Festival and Christmas Market, featuring locally made goods and produce.

Best Time to Visit Sharon Springs (New York)

Sharon Springs is beautiful year-round, with each season offering a unique experience. However, the best time to visit Sharon Springs is from May to September, when the weather is mild. You can also enjoy various local festivals and outdoor activities during these months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sharon Springs (New York)

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Explore the local markets and shops.
  • Try the locally produced food and drink.
  • Participate in local festivals and events.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public spaces.
  • Ignore local etiquette, such as holding doors for others.
  • Take pictures of the locals without asking for permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sharon Springs (New York)

Sharon Springs is a safe and welcoming destination with friendly locals. However, visitors should use common sense when exploring the village and surrounding areas, like any other tourist destination.

Health in Sharon Springs (New York)

Sharon Springs is safe and clean. However, visitors should be adequately vaccinated for COVID-19 and follow all COVID-19 guidelines to ensure their own safety and that of the local community.

Sharon Springs is a small village, and most of the attractions are within walking distance or a short drive away. Most visitors prefer walking around the village to enjoy the serene surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Sharon Springs (New York): Nearby Destinations and Excursions
