Introduction to Keene (New York)

Keene is a charming town located in the eastern part of the Adirondack Mountains in New York. It’s an outdoor enthusiast’s paradise, with plenty of opportunities for hiking, biking, fishing, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Keene (New York)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Adirondack History Center Museum: Explore the history and culture of the Adirondacks through interactive exhibits and artifacts.
  • Fort Ticonderoga: Visit the historic fort and military museum that played a significant role in the American Revolution.
  • John Brown Farm State Historic Site: Learn about the life and legacy of John Brown, a famous abolitionist who lived and worked in the Adirondacks.

Natural Attractions of Keene (New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Marcy: The highest peak in New York, Mount Marcy offers breathtaking views of the Adirondacks and is a popular destination for hikers.
  • High Falls Gorge: Take a self-guided tour of High Falls Gorge and witness the breathtaking beauty of waterfalls, rocky cliffs, and pristine forests.
  • Lake Placid: Home to two Winter Olympic Games, Lake Placid is a beautiful lake surrounded by the mountains and offers boating, swimming, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Keene (New York)

  • The Jackrabbit Trail: A popular ski trail in the winter, Jackrabbit Trail is open for hiking and biking in the summer and offers stunning views of the Adirondacks.
  • Giant Mountain: A challenging hike, Giant Mountain rewards hikers with panoramic views of Keene and the surrounding mountains.
  • Ausable Chasm Trail: A scenic hike along the Ausable River, Ausable Chasm Trail includes a variety of bridges, walkways, and stairs, and offers a unique way to explore the natural beauty of the Adirondacks.

Best Things to Do in Keene (New York): Activities and Tours

  • Whitewater Rafting: Experience the thrill of whitewater rafting on the Hudson River and other nearby streams.
  • Fishing: Keene offers a variety of fishing opportunities, including fly-fishing on the Ausable River, and ice fishing in the winter.
  • Golfing: Practice your swing at the Keene Valley Country Club, a popular 9-hole golf course in the heart of the Adirondacks.

Week-Long Itinerary for Keene (New York)

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Keene:

Day 1: Arrive in Keene and get settled in your accommodation. Day 2: Hike Mount Marcy and enjoy the stunning views from the summit. Day 3: Explore the Adirondack History Center Museum, Fort Ticonderoga, and John Brown Farm State Historic Site. Day 4: Go whitewater rafting on the Hudson River. Day 5: Play a round of golf at the Keene Valley Country Club and enjoy a relaxing afternoon in town. Day 6: Visit High Falls Gorge and take a scenic drive around Lake Placid. Day 7: Spend the day fishing on the Ausable River or exploring more hiking trails.

Food and Drink Scene of Keene (New York)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Adirondack Smokehouse BBQ: Famous for their smoked meats, Adirondack Smokehouse BBQ has a variety of sandwiches, salads, and soups to choose from.
  • The Noon Mark Diner: A classic American diner, The Noon Mark Diner offers burgers, sandwiches, and homemade desserts.
  • ADK Cafe: With a focus on local and organic ingredients, ADK Cafe offers a variety of dishes, including vegetarian and gluten-free options.

Local Beverages

  • Keene Valley Beer Pub: Featuring a selection of local craft beers, Keene Valley Beer Pub is a cozy spot for a drink after a day of hiking or biking.
  • The Ausable Brewing Company: A family-owned brewery, The Ausable Brewing Company offers a variety of craft beers made with locally sourced ingredients.

Where to Stay in Keene (New York): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • RoosterComb Inn: Located in the heart of the Adirondacks, RoosterComb Inn is a charming bed and breakfast that offers comfortable rooms and a delicious breakfast.
  • The Alpine Lodge: A family-friendly hotel with an indoor pool and hot tub, The Alpine Lodge is a great option for those looking for some extra amenities.
  • The Mountaineer Inn: A cozy inn located near some of Keene’s best hiking trails and outdoor activities, The Mountaineer Inn offers affordable rooms and friendly service.

What to Buy in Keene (New York): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Adirondack Wildlife Refuge Gift Shop: Shop for handmade gifts, books, and souvenirs at the Adirondack Wildlife Refuge Gift Shop.
  • The Birch Store: A boutique shop that features handmade jewelry, clothing, and home goods from local artisans.
  • The ADK Cafe Marketplace: Located in the ADK Cafe, the Marketplace offers a variety of locally made products, including honey, maple syrup, and baked goods.

Local Culture and Customs of Keene (New York)

Keene is a small, tight-knit community with a love for the outdoors and a deep appreciation for the natural beauty of the Adirondacks. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local environment and outdoor traditions.

Best Time to Visit Keene (New York)

The best time to visit Keene is during the summer and fall months, when the weather is mild, and the foliage is at its peak. However, winter can be a great time to visit for those who enjoy skiing and other winter sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Keene (New York)

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and environment.
  • Explore the many hiking and biking trails in the area.
  • Try the local cuisine and craft beer.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter on the trails or in town.
  • Disturb wildlife or plant life in the area.
  • Ignore safety guidelines when participating in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Keene (New York)

Keene is generally a safe community, but visitors should take normal precautions to ensure their safety. Always be aware of your surroundings and keep valuables out of sight.

Health in Keene (New York)

Visitors should be aware of the dangers of tick-borne illnesses in the Adirondacks and take precautions when participating in outdoor activities. Be sure to check for ticks and wear protective clothing when hiking or spending time outdoors.

Getting to Keene (New York): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Keene (New York)

  • Adirondack Regional Airport: Located in Saranac Lake, Adirondack Regional Airport is the closest airport to Keene.

Bus Stations Near Keene (New York)

  • Adirondack Trailways: Adirondack Trailways provides bus service to and from Keene, with stops in nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car from any of the nearby airports or in town.
  • Train Service: Amtrak provides train service to nearby communities, with connections to major cities in the northeast.

How to Get Around in Keene (New York)

The best way to get around Keene is by car or bike. However, there are also taxis and shuttle services available for those who prefer not to drive.

Where to Go Next from Keene (New York): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lake Placid: Just a short drive from Keene, Lake Placid is known for its stunning scenery and winter sports.
  • Saranac Lake: A charming town with a variety of shops, restaurants, and outdoor activities.
  • Whiteface Mountain: A popular ski resort with miles of trails for skiers and snowboarders.

Conclusion

Keene is a hidden gem in the Adirondacks, offering visitors a unique blend of outdoor adventure, cultural attractions, and culinary experiences. Whether you’re looking for a weekend getaway or a longer vacation, Keene has something for everyone.
