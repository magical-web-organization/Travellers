Introduction to Old Forge

Old Forge is a charming town located in the heart of Adirondack Mountains in New York (state), Mid-Atlantic region of the United States. Surrounded by scenic views, fresh air, and wildlife, Old Forge is a perfect destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts.

This comprehensive tour guide will help you plan your trip to Old Forge, New York. We’ve included all the information you need to know about the town, including the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay and eat, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Old Forge

Old Forge offers a plethora of attractions that cater to a wide range of interests. We’ve compiled a list of places that you shouldn’t miss.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Adirondack Experience, The Museum on Blue Mountain Lake - This museum features exhibits that showcase the natural history and culture of the Adirondack region.

  • Goodsell Museum - A historic site that houses a collection of Adirondack artifacts and photographs.

  • Fulton Chain History Center - This museum documents the history of the Fulton Chain of Lakes and the surrounding communities.

Natural Attractions of Old Forge: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Enchanted Forest Water Safari - This water park is the largest in New York and features over 50 rides and attractions.

  • McCauley Mountain Scenic Chairlift - This chairlift takes visitors to the top of McCauley Mountain, where they can enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding areas.

  • Old Forge Lake Cruises - Explore the Fulton Chain of Lakes on one of the Old Forge Lake Cruises.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Old Forge

Old Forge is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with a network of hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the Adirondacks. Some of the top hiking and biking routes include:

  • Black Bear Mountain - This moderate hike rewards visitors with a stunning view of the Fulton Chain of Lakes.

  • McCauley Mountain - This trail is perfect for beginners and families. The summit offers great views of the Old Forge area.

  • Bald Mountain - This popular trail offers panoramic views of the Fulton Chain of Lakes and is perfect for hikers of all levels.

Best Things to Do in Old Forge: Activities and Tours

Old Forge offers plenty of activities and tours that cater to a variety of interests. Some of the best things to do include:

  • Whitewater Rafting - Enjoy the thrill of whitewater rafting on the Moose River.

  • Scenic Flights - Take a scenic flight and enjoy a bird’s-eye view of the Adirondack Mountains.

  • Fishing - Spend a day on one of the area’s many lakes and enjoy some of the best fishing in the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Old Forge

If you’re planning to spend a week in Old Forge, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Explore the town and visit the Adirondack Experience Museum.
2 Take a hike to the summit of Black Bear Mountain.
3 Enjoy a scenic flight and get an aerial view of the Adirondack Mountains.
4 Spend the day fishing on one of the many lakes.
5 Take a whitewater rafting trip on the Moose River.
6 Enjoy a lazy day on the beach at the Enchanted Forest Water Safari.
7 Take a relaxing scenic chairlift ride up McCauley Mountain.

Food and Drink Scene of Old Forge

No trip is complete without indulging in the local cuisine and beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Old Forge:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish fry - This dish is a must-try. Usually served with French fries and coleslaw.

  • Adirondack Trout - Fresh trout is a local delicacy that is served in many restaurants in Old Forge.

  • Blueberry pie - The Adirondacks are known for their blueberries, and no trip is complete without trying a slice of homemade blueberry pie.

Local Beverages

  • Adirondack Brewery - This local brewery produces a range of craft beers that are brewed using locally sourced ingredients.

  • Adirondack Winery - This winery offers wine tastings and has a wide variety of locally produced wines.

Where to Stay in Old Forge: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Old Forge has a range of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Some of the most popular options include:

  • Waters Edge Inn - This cozy inn is located on the shores of the Old Forge Lake and offers stunning views of the Adirondack Mountains.

  • Van Auken’s Inne - This historic inn features comfortable rooms and is located in the heart of the town.

  • Old Forge Camping Resort - For those who prefer camping, this resort offers tent and RV sites, as well as cabins.

What to Buy in Old Forge: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring back home, here are some options:

  • Adirondack crafts - Old Forge has many artisan shops that sell Adirondack-themed crafts, such as pottery, woodworking, and textiles.

  • Maple syrup - The Adirondacks are known for their maple syrup, and many local shops sell a variety of maple products, including syrup, candy, and cream.

Local Culture and Customs of Old Forge

Old Forge is a friendly town with a rich culture and history. Here are some customs and traditions you should be aware of:

  • Respecting nature - The Adirondacks are a protected wilderness area, and visitors are expected to respect nature and practice Leave No Trace principles.

  • Seasonal activities - Old Forge has a range of seasonal activities, and visitors should be prepared for different weather conditions depending on the time of year.

Best Time to Visit Old Forge

Old Forge is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on your interests. Here are some suggestions:

  • Summer - The summer months are perfect for outdoor activities, such as hiking, fishing, and boating.

  • Fall - The fall foliage is an attraction in Old Forge, and visitors can enjoy the stunning colors of the Adirondack Mountains.

  • Winter - The winter months offer a range of winter sports, including skiing, snowboarding, and snowmobiling.

Do’s and Don’ts of Old Forge

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Old Forge:

Do:

  • Respect nature and practice Leave No Trace principles.

  • Follow local regulations and guidelines, particularly during hunting season.

Don’t:

  • Feed the wildlife.

  • Leave trash behind.

Safety and Health

Old Forge is a safe destination, but visitors should still take precautions. Here are some tips:

Safety in Old Forge

  • Always wear appropriate clothing and gear, particularly if you plan to engage in outdoor activities.

  • Follow local regulations and guidelines.

Health in Old Forge

  • Visitors should bring a sufficient supply of any necessary medication.

  • During the summer months, it’s important to protect yourself from ticks and mosquitoes.

Getting to Old Forge: Transportation Options and Logistics

Old Forge is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Old Forge

  • Adirondack Regional Airport - Located in Lake Clear, NY, this airport is just 42 miles from Old Forge.

  • Syracuse Hancock International Airport - This airport is approximately 100 miles from Old Forge and offers flights from major US cities.

Bus Stations Near Old Forge

  • Trailways Bus Station - Located in Utica, NY, this bus station is approximately 45 miles from Old Forge.

Other Options

  • Car rental - Visitors can rent a car from one of the major car rental companies at any of the nearby airports or bus stations.

How to Get Around in Old Forge

Getting around Old Forge is easy, and visitors have a range of options, including:

  • Walking or biking - Many of the town’s attractions are within walking or biking distance.

  • Car rental - Visitors can rent a car and explore the surrounding areas.

  • Taxi and shuttle services - There are several taxi and shuttle services that operate in Old Forge and the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Old Forge: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Old Forge is a great base for exploring the Adirondack Mountains and the surrounding areas. Some nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Saranac Lake - A short drive from Old Forge, Saranac Lake is a charming town with a range of outdoor activities and cultural attractions.

  • Lake Placid - Host of the 1932 and 1980 Winter Olympics, Lake Placid offers a range of winter sports and attractions.

  • Chapel Island - Located on Raquette Lake, Chapel Island is a popular destination for kayaking and camping.

Conclusion

Old Forge is a beautiful and charming town located in the heart of the Adirondack Mountains. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, culture vulture, or just looking for a relaxing vacation, Old Forge has something for everyone.
