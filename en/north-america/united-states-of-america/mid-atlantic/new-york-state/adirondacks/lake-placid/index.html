Introduction to Lake Placid

Lake Placid, located in the majestic Adirondack Mountains of New York State, is one of the most picturesque and serene travel destinations in the United States. This village is famed for its abundant summer and winter activities, ranging from hiking, swimming, and bird watching to downhill skiing, snowshoeing, and ice skating. Regardless of when you visit, the natural beauty of the surrounding wilderness is always on display, with breathtaking views of the Adirondacks reflected in the crystal-clear waters of Lake Placid. In this guide, we’ll give you our top recommendations for places to visit in Lake Placid, hiking and biking routes, top things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and much more to help you plan a fantastic trip.

List of Places to Visit in Lake Placid

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Lake Placid Olympic Museum - A great place to learn about the 1932 and 1980 Winter Olympic Games, held in Lake Placid. You’ll see Olympic torches, medals, and even a bobsled and ski jumping simulator.

  2. John Brown Farm State Historic Site - The John Brown Farm is the homestead of famous abolitionist, John Brown, and offers guided tours where you’ll learn about his life and legacy. You can also visit his grave on site.

  3. Adirondack Experience, The Museum on Blue Mountain Lake - This extensive museum is housed in over 121 acres and features exhibits about Adirondack wilderness, history and culture.

Natural Attractions of Lake Placid: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. High Falls Gorge - An awe-inspiring mix of waterfalls and natural rock formations set in a dense forest along the West Branch of the Ausable River.

  2. Mirror Lake - A tranquil lake in the heart of Lake Placid village where you can rent a paddleboat or a kayak and explore at your own pace.

  3. Whiteface Mountain - A 4,870-foot peak, where you can hike, bike, or drive to the summit for breathtaking views of the Adirondack wilderness.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lake Placid

  1. Mt. Jo - An easy hike offers excellent views of the surrounding Adirondacks.

  2. Cascade Mountain - One of the area’s most popular hikes, rewarding you with stunning views from the peak and smaller waterfalls along the way.

  3. Jackrabbit Trail - A 34-mile ski and bike trail through the Adirondack wilderness, linking Saranac Lake to Keene.

Best Things to Do in Lake Placid: Activities and Tours

  1. Lake Placid Olympic venues - Visit historical Winter Olympic sites, such as the bobsled and luge track, and remember the 1980 “Miracle on Ice.”

  2. Scenic Flights - Fightseeing tours offer a bird’s eye view of Lake Placid’s amazing beauty.

  3. Boat Tours - Take a tour of Lake Placid by boat and enjoy the magnificent views.

Food and Drink Scene of Lake Placid

Must-Try Dishes

  1. ADK Buffalo Burger Fry - A local twist on a classic burger, using buffalo meat and crispy fries.

  2. Basil and Wick’s Wings - The perfect comfort food for vacations and wing enthusiasts.

  3. The Cowboy - Tasty BBQ chicken, good beer, and scenic views of Mirror Lake

Local Beverages

  1. Lake Placid Craft Brewing Company - Features a wide variety of brewed beers.

  2. Lake Placid Pub and Brewery - Features several distinctive home-made brews.

  3. ADK Winery & Cidery - Offers a range of wines and ciders made with locally grown fruits.

Where to Stay in Lake Placid: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Crowne Plaza Lake Placid - A luxurious resort set in the heart of the Adirondacks.

  2. High Peaks Resort - A relaxed lakeside hotel in Lake Placid village.

  3. Adirondack Loj - A hostel-style lodge located in the heart of the Adirondack Mountains.

What to Buy in Lake Placid: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Adirondack Maple Syrup - A perfect souvenir for your friends and family.

  2. Handmade Candles - Handcrafted and unique candles, featuring a range of scents inspired by the Adirondacks.

  3. Adirondack Berries - A perfect sweet treat that is sure to be cherished by loved ones.

Local Culture and Customs of Lake Placid

The local culture and customs of Lake Placid are strongly tied to the surrounding natural environment, so be sure to respect and preserve the delicate balance of forests, lakes, and wildlife. Additionally, be friendly and open to meeting new people – visitors to Lake Placid are often greeted with genuine warmth and hospitality.

Best Time to Visit Lake Placid

The best time to visit Lake Placid is during Autumn (mid-September to early November), when the leaves turn color and the weather is mild. However, if you are intending on skiing and snowboarding, you will want to visit in the winter months (December to March).

Do’s and Don’ts of Lake Placid

Do:

  1. Plan ahead, especially if you are traveling during peak seasons.

  2. Wear comfortable shoes and clothing.

  3. Try the local cuisines and beverages.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t forget to respect the natural environment and the wildlife.

  2. Don’t litter.

  3. Don’t use drones or otherwise disrupt the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lake Placid

  1. Check for any trail closures or possible dangers before starting your outdoor adventures.

  2. Wear appropriate footwear, especially when hiking or trekking.

  3. Be aware of the weather forecast, especially during winter months.

Health in Lake Placid

  1. There is no shortage of clean drinking water, but it’s always a good idea to bring your own refillable water bottles.

  2. Bring any necessary medications and medical devices.

  3. Research any known allergies or risks before embarking on any outdoor activities.

Getting to Lake Placid: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lake Placid

  1. Adirondack Regional Airport (SLK) - 20 mi

  2. Plattsburgh International Airport (PBG) - 70 mi

  3. Burlington International Airport (BTV) - 80 mi

Bus Stations Near Lake Placid

  1. Adirondack Trailways - 17 mi

  2. Greyhound Bus Lines - 20 mi

Other Options

  1. Car rentals are very common in Lake Placid, and a rental vehicle provides the freedom to explore the Adirondacks on your schedule.

  2. Taxis and shuttle services provide transportation to and from the airports for travelers without vehicles.

How to Get Around Lake Placid

  1. Renting a car is always an option, and allows you to explore the surrounding region as well.

  2. A good alternative option is renting a bike to explore the village and nearby trails.

Where to Go Next from Lake Placid: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Saranac Lake - Located 10 miles west of Lake Placid, Saranac Lake offers many outdoor activities, cultural events, and attractions such as the Adirondack Carousel.

  2. Lake George - An hour’s drive South of Lake Placid and home to Sagamore Resort and Six Flags Great Escape.

  3. Albany - The capital of New York State and only a 2.5-hour drive South.

Conclusion

We hope that this guide has been helpful in planning your trip to Lake Placid. The village offers outdoor activities throughout every season, natural attractions, and world-class entertainment, so it’s a destination that is perfect for families, couples or solo travelers. We’re confident that you’ll have a visit filled with unforgettable memories of Adirondack Adventure and relaxation – set in one of the most beautiful places in the US.
