Food and Drink Scene of Saranac Lake

Saranac Lake has a diverse and vibrant food and drink scene. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • (1) Adirondack Salad. This salad features locally grown greens, goat cheese, apples, and walnuts.
  • (2) Adirondack Smoked Duck Breast. This savory dish features locally sourced duck breasts that are smoked and served with fruit sauce and mashed potatoes.
  • (3) Maple Syrup-Cured Pancetta. This dish is a twist on the classic bacon and eggs breakfast and is served at many local restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • (1) Saranac Brewery. This brewery has been crafting beer in Saranac Lake since 1888 and is a must-visit spot for beer lovers.
  • (2) Blue Line Brewery. This brewery features Adirondack themed beers, including “46’er IPA,” named after the 46 highest peaks in the Adirondacks.
  • (3) Adirondack Cidery. This cidery offers apple and fruit-based ciders, and a beautiful location to enjoy them at.

Where to Stay in Saranac Lake: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxurious stay or a budget-friendly option, Saranac Lake has accommodations to suit every need. Here are some of the best places to stay in Saranac Lake:

  • (1) Hotel Saranac. This historic hotel is located in the heart of Saranac Lake and offers comfortable rooms with Adirondack-style decor and modern amenities.
  • (2) Alpine Motel. This budget-friendly motel is located just 5 minutes away from downtown and offers clean and comfortable rooms.
  • (3) Kiwassa Lake Bed & Breakfast and Cabins. This is a B&B located on a peaceful lake with cabins as well as private rooms and suites.

What to Buy in Saranac Lake: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and gifts to bring back home, Saranac Lake has a lot to offer. Here are some of the best local products to buy in Saranac Lake:

  • (1) Adirondack Maple Syrup. This pure maple syrup is made from the maple trees of the Adirondacks and makes for a delicious gift.
  • (2) Adirondack Art. Saranac Lake has many art galleries showcasing Adirondack-inspired paintings, sculptures, and home decor items.
  • (3) Handmade Crafts. There are many artisans in Saranac Lake selling handmade jewelry, pottery, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Saranac Lake

Saranac Lake has a rich history and culture that is deeply rooted in its natural surroundings. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and the community is tight-knit. When visiting Saranac Lake, it’s important to respect the local customs and traditions, and treat the area with care so that it can be preserved for future generations.

Best Time to Visit Saranac Lake

The best time to visit Saranac Lake depends on your preferences. Summer is the most popular time to visit, with warm weather and plenty of outdoor activities. Fall offers beautiful foliage and hiking opportunities, while winter is ideal for winter sports, including skiing, snowboarding, skating, and snowshoeing. Spring is quieter, with fewer crowds, and a great time to enjoy wildflowers and wildlife.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saranac Lake

Saranac Lake is a small village with a close-knit community. It’s important to follow some basic etiquette to ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip.

Do:

  • (1) Respect local customs and traditions.
  • (2) Follow the Leave No Trace principles while enjoying outdoor activities.
  • (3) Try local foods and beverages.

Don’t:

  • (1) Litter or damage natural areas.
  • (2) Ignore wildlife and plants while exploring.
  • (3) Be loud or disrespectful towards locals.

Safety and Health

Saranac Lake is a safe area, but it’s important to take some basic precautions while visiting.

Safety in Saranac Lake

  • (1) Always wear proper gear while engaging in outdoor activities.
  • (2) Follow rules and regulations of local parks and trails.
  • (3) Plan accordingly and avoid hiking or biking alone.

Health in Saranac Lake

  • (1) Stay hydrated and wear sunscreen while outdoors.
  • (2) Know your limits and don’t overexert yourself while hiking or biking.
  • (3) Bring insect repellent and check for ticks after hiking or being in wooded areas.

Getting to Saranac Lake: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Saranac Lake is easy, with several transportation options available.

  • (1) Airports Near Saranac Lake. The closest airport is Adirondack Regional Airport in Saranac Lake, but Burlington International Airport in Vermont is another option.
  • (2) Bus Stations Near Saranac Lake. Greyhound and Adirondack Trailways offer bus services to Saranac Lake from nearby cities.
  • (3) Other Options. Driving is also an option, with Saranac Lake located just off of Route 3.

How to Get Around in Saranac Lake

Renting a car or biking are the best options for getting around Saranac Lake. Walking is a viable option to get to places in the small village as well.

Where to Go Next from Saranac Lake: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Saranac Lake is surrounded by many other destination options in the Adirondacks. Here are some popular places to visit near Saranac Lake:

  • (1) Lake Placid. This Olympic Village is located just a short drive from Saranac Lake and is known for its ski slopes, hiking trails, and charming shops and restaurants.
  • (2) Tupper Lake. This small village is known for its natural beauty and is a great place for fishing, skiing, snowmobiling, and other outdoor activities.
  • (3) Ausable Chasm. Located about an hour’s drive away from Saranac Lake, this natural wonder features dramatic cliffs, waterfall, and hiking trails.

Conclusion

Saranac Lake is a hidden gem in the Adirondacks, offering a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or simply a relaxing getaway, Saranac Lake has something for everyone. Use this guide to plan your next trip to Saranac Lake and explore all that this charming village has to offer!
