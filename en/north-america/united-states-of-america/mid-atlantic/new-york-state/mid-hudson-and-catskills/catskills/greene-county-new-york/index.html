Introduction to Greene County (New York)

Nestled in the Catskills region of New York State, Greene County is a beautiful getaway destination that offers visitors a chance to reconnect with nature and explore local history. The county is located in Mid-Hudson and Catskills, in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States of America. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will take you on a journey through Greene County, highlighting its top attractions, best places to stay and eat, and the cultural customs of the area.

List of Places to Visit in Greene County (New York)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Thomas Cole National Historic Site

The Thomas Cole National Historic Site is a museum located at the former home of the famous landscape painter, Thomas Cole. Visitors can tour the home and galleries, which boast a large collection of Cole’s paintings and memorabilia. It’s a great place to learn about the Hudson River School of Art, founded by Cole himself.

Bronck Museum

The Bronck Museum is a collection of historic buildings that depicts life in the 18th century. Visitors can tour the farmstead and farmhouse and learn about the daily life and customs of the Dutch immigrants who settled in the region.

Mountain Top Arboretum

Nestled in the Catskills Mountains, the Mountain Top Arboretum is a beautiful garden showcasing native trees and plants. Visitors can take guided hiking trails to the top of the mountain and enjoy panoramic views of Greene County.

Natural Attractions of Greene County (New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kaaterskill Falls

One of the most popular natural attractions in Greene County is Kaaterskill Falls – the highest cascading waterfall in New York. Visitors can hike the trail to the falls and enjoy stunning views.

North-South Lake Campground

The North-South Lake Campground is a popular spot for camping and hiking in the Catskills Mountains. Visitors can explore the winding trails, go fishing, and enjoy the beautiful views of the lake.

Hunter Mountain

Hunter Mountain is a popular ski destination in the winter but visitors can enjoy hiking, ziplining, and scenic chairlift rides throughout the year. The view from the summit of Hunter Mountain on a clear day is breathtaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Greene County (New York)

Escarpment Trail

The Escarpment Trail is a long-distance hiking trail that spans 25 miles and offers sweeping views of Greene County’s mountain ranges.

Blackhead Mountain Range

The Blackhead Mountain Range is another popular hiking destination in Greene County. The trailhead is only a short drive from the village of Tannersville, and visitors can enjoy hiking on well-marked trails with panoramic views of the Catskill Mountains.

Best Things to Do in Greene County (New York): Activities and Tours

Catskill Mountain Railroad

Take a ride on the Catskill Mountain Railroad and experience the natural beauty of Greene County. Visitors can board at the town of Kingston, NY, and enjoy a scenic train ride through the Catskill Mountains.

Historic Catskill Point

Catskill Point is an excellent place to take a relaxing stroll by the Hudson River and see some of the county’s historic sites. Visitors can tour the lighthouse, which was built in 1870, and learn about the river’s role in the region’s history.

Food and Drink Scene of Greene County (New York)

Must-Try Dishes

Kale salad

Freshly harvested kale salad served with locally sourced dressing is a must-try dish in Greene County. The freshness of the ingredients and the local, artisanal dressing make for a unique taste experience.

Corn on the cob

Corn on the cob, cooked to perfection, served with locally sourced butter, is another dish you cannot miss while in Greene County. The sweetness of the corn is perfectly complemented by the rich flavor of the butter.

Local Beverages

Local beer

Greene County is a popular destination for beer lovers. Visitors can sample local craft beers at many breweries in the area.

Hudson Valley wine

The Hudson Valley is known for its vineyards and wineries. Visitors can take a wine-tasting tour and sample local wines.

Where to Stay in Greene County (New York): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Catskill Lodge

The Catskill Lodge is a rustic retreat offering guests a chance to enjoy the scenic views of the Catskill Mountains. The lodge is located near hiking trails and offers comfortable rooms with modern amenities.

Copper Kettle Cottages

The Copper Kettle Cottages is a charming bed and breakfast that provides guests a cozy retreat in the heart of the Catskill Mountains. The cottages are only a short drive from local attractions like Kaaterskill Falls and North-South Lake.

What to Buy in Greene County (New York): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Handmade pottery

Greene County is known for its pottery, crafted by skilled artisans who use locally sourced materials.

Local honey

Visitors can take home a jar of raw, unprocessed honey or visit one of the local apiaries to learn about beekeeping and the importance of honeybees to the ecosystem.

Local Culture and Customs of Greene County (New York)

The local culture of Greene County is deeply rooted in the area’s history and natural landscape. The region is famous for its artistic heritage and the Hudson River School of Art, named for the Hudson River that runs through the region. The county is also home to many Dutch settlers, and visitors can explore the customs and traditions of this community at the Bronck Museum.

Best Time to Visit Greene County (New York)

The best time to visit Greene County is during the fall when the leaves are changing color, and the temperatures are moderate. Visitors can enjoy hiking, scenic drives, and local fall festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Greene County (New York)

Do:

  • Respect nature and the local environment.
  • Take advantage of the area’s cultural heritage and explore the local attractions and tours.
  • Engage with the local artisans and learn about the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural and historic sites.
  • Ignore safety instructions while participating in outdoor activities.
  • Disrespect local customs and culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Greene County (New York)

Visitors to Greene County should be aware of the potential for slippery hiking trails and dangerous terrain. Visitors should always follow safety guidelines and heed warning signs and recommendations given by park rangers and other officials.

Health in Greene County (New York)

Visitors should be aware of the potential for tick-borne illnesses like Lyme Disease, which is common in wooded areas. Visitors should take precautionary measures and check themselves for ticks after spending time in the woods.

Getting to Greene County (New York): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Greene County (New York)

The closest airport to Greene County is the Albany International Airport, located about an hour’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near Greene County (New York)

Adirondack Trailways and Greyhound Bus Lines serve the city of Catskill, NY, with regular bus services.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car to explore Greene County or take a train ride on the Catskill Mountain Railroad.

How to Get Around in Greene County (New York)

The county is small enough that visitors can get around by car easily. However, the area also has many hiking trails that visitors can use to explore the natural beauty of the area.

Where to Go Next from Greene County (New York): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hudson, NY

The city of Hudson is located along the Hudson River and has a thriving arts scene, antiques shops, and historic architecture.

Albany, NY

The state capital of Albany is only an hour’s drive from Greene County and has many cultural attractions, including the State Capitol building, the New York State Museum, and the Albany Institute of History and Art.

Conclusion

Greene County is a hidden gem in the Catskill Mountains of New York. With its natural beauty, historic sites, and vibrant culture, it offers visitors a chance to reconnect with nature and explore local heritage. Whether you are hiking in the mountains, sampling local cuisine, or visiting historic sites, Greene County is a destination you will never forget.
