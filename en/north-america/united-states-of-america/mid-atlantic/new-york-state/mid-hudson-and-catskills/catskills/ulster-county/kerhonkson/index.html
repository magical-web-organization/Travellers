Introduction to Kerhonkson

Nestled in the heart of Ulster County, in the Catskills region of upstate New York, Kerhonkson is a charming small town that offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor recreation, cultural exploration, and relaxation. Known for its scenic beauty, vibrant arts scene, and rich history, Kerhonkson is a popular destination for visitors from all over the world.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kerhonkson

Whether you want to hike, bike, or just stroll around and take in the natural beauty of the area, Kerhonkson has plenty of trails and parks to explore. Some of the most popular options include:

  • Mohonk Preserve trails
  • Minnewaska State Park Preserve trails
  • Shawangunk Ridge Trail
  • Sam’s Point Preserve trails
  • Catskill Mountains trails

Best Things to Do in Kerhonkson: Activities and Tours

  • Rock climbing or bouldering at Shawangunk Ridge
  • Wine tasting at Whitecliff Vineyard & Winery or Robibero Winery
  • Horseback riding at Pine Ridge Dude Ranch or Arrowhead Farms
  • Hot air balloon ride over the Hudson Valley
  • Art classes or workshops at Wired Gallery or Unison Arts Center
  • Guided ATV tours or paintball games at New York Zipline Adventures
  • Skiing or snowboarding at Belleayre Mountain or Hunter Mountain
  • Fishing or kayaking on the Hudson River or Rondout Creek

Local Culture and Customs of Kerhonkson

Kerhonkson has a strong appreciation for the arts and local history, with several museums and galleries showcasing works by local artists. The town is also home to many locally-owned shops and businesses, and the residents are friendly and welcoming to visitors.

Best Time to Visit Kerhonkson

The best time to visit Kerhonkson is during the fall, when the leaves change color and the weather is mild. The summer months are also popular, but can be crowded with tourists. In the winter, you can enjoy skiing and snowboarding, while the spring is perfect for hiking and wildflower spotting.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kerhonkson

Do:

  • Respect nature and leave no trace when hiking or camping.
  • Sample the local food and drink offerings, such as hard cider, craft beer, and farm-to-table cuisine.
  • Take in the local art and history, by visiting the many museums and galleries in town.
  • Practice eco-friendly habits by recycling, conserving water, and using public transportation when possible.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Ignore local customs or traditions.
  • Engage in disruptive or illegal behavior.
  • Leave valuables unattended in your car or hotel room.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kerhonkson

Kerhonkson is a relatively safe town, but like any destination, visitors should take precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. Always lock your car and hotel room, and never leave valuables unattended. In case of emergencies, dial 911.

Health in Kerhonkson

There are no major health or safety concerns in Kerhonkson, but visitors should be aware of tick-borne illnesses and take precautions when hiking or camping in wooded areas. It is also a good idea to bring sunscreen, insect repellent, and a first aid kit when spending time outdoors.

  • Train: Amtrak’s Empire Service runs to nearby Poughkeepsie, with connecting bus service to Kerhonkson.

How to Get Around in Kerhonkson

Kerhonkson is small enough to explore on foot or by bike, and many of the major attractions are located within a short distance of one another. However, if you need to travel further, renting a car is a good option.
