Introduction to Kingston (New York)

Kingston, a charming city in Ulster County, New York, is a perfect blend of rich history, natural beauty, and modern amenities. Situated in the heart of the Hudson Valley, Kingston is a popular tourist destination that has something for everyone. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Kingston won’t disappoint you.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take you on a journey through the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more in Kingston.

List of Places to Visit in Kingston (New York)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Senate House State Historic Site: This 18th-century house belonged to the first Senate of New York, and it’s now a museum that showcases the region’s history.
  • Hudson River Maritime Museum: This museum celebrates the maritime history of the Hudson River. It has interactive exhibits, a boat-building workshop, and a collection of historic vessels.
  • Kingston Uptown Historic District: Take a stroll around this historic neighborhood and admire the beautiful architecture from the 1700s and 1800s.
  • Old Dutch Church: One of the oldest churches in New York State, this church has been restored to its original appearance and is open for tours.

Natural Attractions of Kingston (New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Catskill Mountains: Kingston is nestled in the Catskill Mountains, providing stunning views and endless opportunities for hiking, fishing, and skiing.
  • Walkway Over the Hudson State Historic Park: This pedestrian bridge is the longest in the world, offering breathtaking views of the Hudson River and the surrounding mountains.
  • Kingston Point Beach: A popular spot for swimming, picnicking, and boating, this beach offers beautiful views of the river.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kingston (New York)

  • Kingston Point Rail Trail: This 2.5-mile trail is an easy hike or bike ride that offers stunning views of the river.
  • Overlook Mountain Trail: This 5.2-mile trail is a challenging hike that rewards you with panoramic views of the Catskill Mountains.
  • Wallkill Valley Rail Trail: This 24-mile trail is perfect for a leisurely bike ride, and it passes through scenic farms, forests, and wetlands.

Best Things to Do in Kingston (New York): Activities and Tours

  • Trolley Museum of New York: Take a ride on a vintage trolley and learn about the history of public transportation in New York.
  • Hudson River Cruises: Take a scenic cruise on the Hudson River and enjoy the stunning views of the shoreline and the Catskill Mountains.
  • Kingston Farmer’s Market: Sample the local produce and crafts at this weekly market held year-round.

Food and Drink Scene of Kingston (New York)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Apple cider donuts: These delicious treats are a Hudson Valley specialty.
  • Hudson Valley foie gras: Visit a local farm and try the foie gras, a staple of the region.
  • New York-style pizza: Kingston has plenty of pizza places that serve authentic New York-style pies.

Local Beverages

  • Hudson Valley wine: The region has over 20 wineries that produce a variety of award-winning wines.
  • Craft beer: Kingston has several breweries that serve locally brewed craft beer.
  • Apple cider: Enjoy a refreshing glass of apple cider, made from the region’s abundant apple orchards.

Where to Stay in Kingston (New York): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Forsyth B&B: This charming bed and breakfast offers cozy rooms and a delicious breakfast.
  • Best Western Plus Kingston Hotel & Conference Center: This modern hotel offers comfortable rooms, an indoor pool, and a fitness center.
  • Rondout Inn: This historic guesthouse is located in the heart of the Kingston Uptown Historic District.

What to Buy in Kingston (New York): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handcrafted pottery: The region has many local potters who create beautiful handcrafted pottery.
  • Maple syrup: The Hudson Valley is known for its delicious maple syrup, perfect as a gift or to enjoy yourself.
  • Antiques: Kingston has several antique shops where you can find unique treasures and souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Kingston (New York)

Kingston prides itself on its long and rich history, which is evident in the architecture of its downtown area. The city is also known for its creative community, with many artists, musicians, and writers calling it home. The residents of Kingston are friendly and welcoming, and they take pride in their local businesses and culture.

Best Time to Visit Kingston (New York)

The best time to visit Kingston is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild, and the scenery is stunning. The spring brings blooming flowers and foliage, while the fall offers colorful foliage and apple-picking season. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kingston (New York)

Do:

  • Take a scenic drive through the Catskill Mountains.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Visit the local farmer’s market.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the historic sites and neighborhoods.
  • Forget to pack comfortable shoes for hiking and walking.
  • Leave litter in the natural areas or historic sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kingston (New York)

Kingston is a safe city, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions when traveling. Keep your valuables with you at all times and be vigilant in crowded areas.

Health in Kingston (New York)

The main hospital in Kingston is the HealthAlliance Hospital, and there are several urgent care centers and pharmacies in the city. It’s also a good idea to bring sunscreen and bug spray when enjoying outdoor activities.

Getting to Kingston (New York): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kingston (New York)

  • Stewart International Airport (SWF) is 31 miles from Kingston.
  • Albany International Airport (ALB) is 70 miles from Kingston.

Bus Stations Near Kingston (New York)

  • Adirondack Trailways Bus Station: This bus station is located in the heart of Kingston and offers service to several destinations in the region.

Other Options

  • Kingston is located near several major highways, including the New York State Thruway and the Taconic State Parkway.
  • There are several taxi and ride-sharing services available in Kingston.

How to Get Around in Kingston (New York)

The best way to get around Kingston is by car, but you can also bike or walk around the downtown area. There are several taxi and ride-sharing services available, and the city has a reliable bus system.

Where to Go Next from Kingston (New York): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Woodstock: This artsy town is just 20 minutes from Kingston and has a vibrant arts and music scene.
  • Mohonk Mountain House: This Victorian-style resort is located in the nearby Shawangunk Mountains and offers stunning views and outdoor activities.
  • Hyde Park: This historic town is just over an hour from Kingston and is home to the Vanderbilt Mansion National Historic Site and the Franklin D. Roosevelt Presidential Library and Museum.

Conclusion

Kingston (New York) is a hidden gem in the Hudson Valley, offering rich history, natural beauty, and modern amenities. Whether you’re a history buff or an outdoor enthusiast, Kingston has something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive tour guide has given you a taste of what this charming city has to offer and inspires you to plan your own visit.
