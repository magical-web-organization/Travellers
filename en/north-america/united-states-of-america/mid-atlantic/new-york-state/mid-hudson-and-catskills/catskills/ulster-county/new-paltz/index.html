Introduction to New Paltz

Nestled in the scenic Mid-Hudson and Catskills region of New York state, New Paltz is a charming village located in Ulster County. It is a perfect combination of historic significance, natural beauty, and cultural diversity. Whether you are looking for a place to unwind, to explore, to adventure, or to learn, New Paltz has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in New Paltz

New Paltz boasts a variety of attractions that appeal to all kinds of travelers. Here is a list of top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Historic Huguenot Street: A National Historic Landmark District lined with beautifully preserved 18th-century stone houses.
  • Samuel Dorsky Museum of Art: Exhibits contemporary art and photography from around the world.
  • Elting Memorial Library: Houses a collection of rare books and local history archives.
  • Wallkill Valley Rail Trail: A 22-mile-long trail that follows the former Wallkill Valley Railroad through various towns of Ulster County.

Natural Attractions of New Paltz: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mohonk Preserve: A 8,000-acre nature preserve that offers endless opportunities for hiking, biking, rock climbing, and more.
  • Minnewaska State Park Preserve: Known for its scenic beauty, hiking trails, and opportunities for swimming, fishing, boating, and picnicking.
  • Shawangunk Mountains: A must-visit destination for rock climbers of all levels.
  • Walkway Over the Hudson: A pedestrian walkway on an old railroad bridge that spans 1.28 miles over the Hudson River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of New Paltz

The Mid-Hudson and Catskills region is a hiker’s paradise with plenty of challenging and scenic trails. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes in New Paltz:

  • Mohonk Preserve: Offers 85 miles of trails with breathtaking views of the Gunks’ rock formations and Hudson Valley.
  • Minnewaska State Park Preserve: Trails range from easy to difficult, while offering panoramic views of the Hudson Valley and the Catskills.
  • Wallkill Valley Rail Trail: A 22-mile long, flat trail that’s easy to bike, walk or run.
  • Hudson Valley Rail Trail: A flat and easy 4-mile trail that passes through wooded areas and wetlands.

Best Things to Do in New Paltz: Activities and Tours

  • Visit Tuthilltown Spirits Distillery: Take a tour and taste locally made whiskey, vodka, and gin, or enjoy a cocktail at their beautiful bar.
  • Go fruit picking: Several farms in the area offer pick-your-own berries, apples, and pumpkins.
  • Take a hot air balloon ride: Discover the beauty of the Hudson Valley from above.
  • Visit local wineries: Several wineries and vineyards in the area offer tastings and tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for New Paltz

  • Day 1: Hike in the Shawangunk Mountains and explore the scenic Mohonk Preserve.
  • Day 2: Visit the historic Huguenot Street and learn about the rich local history.
  • Day 3: Take a hot air balloon ride or go fruit picking.
  • Day 4: Explore the contemporary art exhibitions at the Samuel Dorsky Museum of Art.
  • Day 5: Visit local wineries and enjoy wine tastings and tours.
  • Day 6: Go rock climbing in the Gunks or take a leisurely bike ride on the rail trail.
  • Day 7: Relax at Minnewaska State Park Preserve with a picnic or a swim on Lake Minnewaska.

Food and Drink Scene of New Paltz

New Paltz has a vibrant food and drink scene that caters to all budgets and tastes.

Must-Try Dishes

  • The Nectarine Restaurant: Serves fresh, locally sourced dishes with a Mediterranean twist.
  • Bacchus Restaurant: Offers American cuisine, handcrafted cocktails, and local beers.
  • Huckleberry: A farm-to-table restaurant that highlights seasonal ingredients in a cozy atmosphere.

Local Beverages

  • Tuthilltown Spirits Distillery: Produces whiskey, gin, and vodka from locally grown ingredients.
  • Robibero Winery: Offers wine tastings and beautiful views from their hilltop vineyard.
  • Yard Owl Craft Brewery: Makes small-batch beers with locally sourced ingredients.

Where to Stay in New Paltz: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

New Paltz offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury resorts.

  • Mohonk Mountain House: A historic and luxurious resort overlooking the scenic Lake Mohonk.
  • The 1850 House Inn & Tavern: A beautiful 1850s inn with 12 unique guest rooms and a tavern.
  • The Village Hub: A budget-friendly hostel located in the heart of New Paltz.

What to Buy in New Paltz: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Vinyl and Co.: A record shop offering a wide variety of vinyl records and cassette tapes.
  • Water Street Market: A unique shopping experience in a historic building, with a variety of shops and eateries.
  • Mohonk Preserve Gift Shop: Sells outdoor gear and gifts, with all proceeds supporting the preserve.

Local Culture and Customs of New Paltz

New Paltz has a rich cultural diversity and vibrant local arts scene, with a variety of galleries, music venues, and cultural events.

Best Time to Visit New Paltz

The best time to visit New Paltz is during the fall season, when the colors of the leaves change and the weather is mild. However, the village is beautiful year-round and offers a range of festivals and events throughout the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of New Paltz

Do:

  • Visit the local farmers’ market for fresh produce and local products.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take advantage of the many outdoor activities the area has to offer.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash in public areas.
  • Park in restricted areas.
  • Ignore safety guidelines when hiking or rock climbing.

Safety and Health

Safety in New Paltz

New Paltz is a safe and friendly destination, with low crime rates. However, it’s important to take necessary precautions when hiking or participating in outdoor activities.

Health in New Paltz

New Paltz has several medical facilities, including urgent care centers, hospitals, and pharmacies.

Getting to New Paltz: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near New Paltz

The closest major airport to New Paltz is Stewart International Airport, located just 20 miles away.

Bus Stations Near New Paltz

The Trailways bus company has several stops in New Paltz, connecting it to the surrounding areas and major cities.

Other Options

Many visitors choose to drive to New Paltz, as it’s conveniently located near major highways.

How to Get Around in New Paltz

New Paltz is a walkable village, and many attractions are within walking distance of each other. Biking, driving, and using ride-share services are also convenient options.

Where to Go Next from New Paltz: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

New Paltz is conveniently located near several other popular destinations, including:

  • Woodstock: A historic town known for its arts and music culture.
  • Kingston: A historic waterfront city with a variety of cultural attractions.
  • Rhinebeck: A charming town famous for its natural beauty, historic architecture, and antique shops.

Conclusion

New Paltz is a unique and charming destination that appeals to a wide range of travelers. With its rich history, natural beauty, vibrant culture, delicious cuisine, and outdoor adventures, there is never a dull moment in this picturesque village.
