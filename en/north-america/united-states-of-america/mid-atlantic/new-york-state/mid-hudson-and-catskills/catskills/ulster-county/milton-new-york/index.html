Introduction to Milton (New York)

Nestled in Ulster County, in the heart of the Catskills mountain range, Milton (New York) is a small town that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. With scenic views, historic sites, and exciting activities, there’s something for everyone here. In this travel guide, we’ll take you on a tour of Milton (New York) and help you plan your next trip to this hidden gem in the region.

List of Places to Visit in Milton (New York)

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, nature, or adventure, Milton (New York) has plenty of attractions to explore. Here are some of the must-visit places in the town:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Gomez Mill House

Built in 1714, the Gomez Mill House is the oldest surviving Jewish residence in North America. Today, it serves as a museum and cultural center that showcases the contributions of the Jewish community to American history and culture.

The West Point Foundry Preserve

This historic site was once a center of innovation and production during the American Industrial Revolution. Today, it’s a nature preserve that features ruins, walking trails, and exhibits that celebrate the area’s industrial heritage.

Natural Attractions of Milton (New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Shaupeneak Ridge

This natural area offers hiking trails, scenic views, and opportunities for wildlife watching. With over 570 acres of forests, wetlands, and rock formations, Shaupeneak Ridge is a great place to explore the natural beauty of the region.

Esopus Creek Preserve

This preserve features over 150 acres of forests, wetlands, and meadows, as well as a network of trails that offer a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, bird watching, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Milton (New York)

For outdoor enthusiasts, Milton (New York) offers a range of hiking and biking trails that showcase the beauty of the area. Here are some of the best routes to try:

Shaupeneak Ridge Trail

This 4-mile trail offers scenic views of the Hudson River and the Catskills mountain range, as well as opportunities for wildlife watching. The trail is rated as moderate and takes around 2-3 hours to complete.

Franny Reese State Park Trail

This 2.5-mile trail is perfect for hikers of all levels, offering stunning views of the Hudson River and the surrounding mountains. The trail is also popular for biking and horseback riding.

Best Things to Do in Milton (New York): Activities and Tours

From outdoor adventures to cultural experiences, there’s plenty to do in Milton (New York). Here are some of the best activities and tours to try:

Shawangunk Wine Trail Tour

This tour takes you through some of the area’s best wineries, offering tastings and behind-the-scenes tours of the winemaking process. With stunning views of the Shawangunk Mountains, this tour is a must for wine lovers.

Hudson Valley Rail Trail Bike Tour

This 7.5-mile bike tour takes you along the scenic Hudson Valley Rail Trail, offering views of the Hudson River and the historic sites of Highland and New Paltz.

Week-Long Itinerary for Milton (New York)

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Milton (New York), here’s a suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

  • Day 1: Visit the Gomez Mill House and explore the town’s historic district.

  • Day 2: Hike the Shaupeneak Ridge Trail and enjoy a picnic lunch in the park.

  • Day 3: Take a tour of the area’s wineries on the Shawangunk Wine Trail.

  • Day 4: Visit the West Point Foundry Preserve and learn about the area’s industrial heritage.

  • Day 5: Bike the Hudson Valley Rail Trail and explore the nearby town of New Paltz.

  • Day 6: Visit the Esopus Creek Preserve and enjoy a day of hiking and bird watching.

  • Day 7: Relax and unwind at one of the town’s cozy bed and breakfasts.

Food and Drink Scene of Milton (New York)

Milton (New York) offers a range of dining options that showcase the area’s local flavors and culinary traditions. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Beef on weck: This regional specialty features thinly sliced roast beef on a kummelweck roll, topped with horseradish and au jus sauce.

  • Chicken spiedies: These marinated and grilled chicken skewers are a popular street food in the area.

Local Beverages

  • Apple cider: With numerous orchards in the region, apple cider is a must-try drink in Milton (New York).

  • Craft beer: The area is home to several craft breweries that produce a range of unique brews, from IPA to lagers.

Where to Stay in Milton (New York): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Milton (New York) offers a range of accommodation options that cater to different tastes and budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • The Village Inn of Milton: This historic inn offers rooms with antique furnishings and modern amenities, as well as a restaurant and a bar.

What to Buy in Milton (New York): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • The Gomez Mill House Museum Gift Shop: This small shop offers books, jewelry, and other items that reflect the museum’s Jewish heritage and American history.
