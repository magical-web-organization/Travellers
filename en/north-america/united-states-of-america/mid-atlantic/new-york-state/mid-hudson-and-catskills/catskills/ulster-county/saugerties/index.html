Introduction to Saugerties

Nestled in Ulster County in the Mid-Hudson and Catskills region of New York State, Saugerties is a serene and culturally rich village with a thriving food and drink scene and an abundance of outdoor activities.

Despite its small size, Saugerties offers an eclectic mix of attractions, from museums and historical sites to natural wonders and adventure sports. With so much to offer, Saugerties is an ideal destination for travelers who crave a retreat from the bustle of city life.

List of Places to Visit in Saugerties

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The village of Saugerties boasts a rich history and is home to several museums that celebrate its cultural legacy. These attractions provide a window into the region’s past and allow visitors to learn more about the village’s storied history.

  • Saugerties Lighthouse: Constructed in 1835, the lighthouse is a National Historic Landmark and an iconic feature of Saugerties.
  • Kiersted House: Built in 1727, this beautiful historic home gives visitors a vivid glimpse of life in colonial times.
  • Opus 40: A sprawling outdoor sculpture park located just outside of Saugerties, Opus 40 features breathtaking stonework and hosts several cultural events throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Saugerties: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Saugerties is surrounded by natural beauty and is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. Whether hiking in the Catskill Mountains or kayaking on the Hudson River, there are countless ways to fully immerse yourself in the area’s natural splendor.

  • Catskill Mountains: This breathtaking mountain range offers miles of hiking trails, crystal-clear streams, and stunning views of the landscape.
  • Esopus Creek: A pristine waterway that flows for 65 miles through the village, the Esopus Creek is ideal for fishing, tubing, and kayaking.
  • Falling Waters Preserve: This nature reserve boasts picturesque waterfalls, ruins of historic structures, and several hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saugerties

With so much natural beauty, hiking and biking are popular pastimes in Saugerties. Whether you’re a seasoned hiker or just looking for an easy day hike, there’s a trail for everyone.

  • Overlook Mountain: A challenging but rewarding hike that offers stunning 360-degree views of the surrounding area from the summit.
  • Dutchman’s Landing: A flat, easy, mile-long path that runs alongside the Hudson River, offering spectacular views of the water and the mountains beyond.
  • Esopus Bend Nature Preserve: A beautiful network of paths that meander through the preserve’s mixed hardwood forest and along the banks of the Esopus Creek.

Best Things to Do in Saugerties: Activities and Tours

From outdoor adventures to cultural excursions, there’s always something to do in Saugerties. The village offers several activities and tours that are sure to keep visitors entertained throughout their stay.

  • Farm-to-Table Tours: Explore the region’s vibrant food scene with a farm-to-table tour, featuring visits to local farms, markets, and eateries.
  • Saugerties Farmers Market: Open every Saturday from May through October, the farmers market is a great place to sample fresh produce and artisanal products.
  • Saugerties First Friday: On the first Friday of every month, the village comes alive with live music, artist exhibitions, and more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Saugerties

Day 1: Explore downtown Saugerties and visit the Kiersted House and Saugerties Lighthouse. Day 2: Go on a hike in the Catskill Mountains, followed by a visit to Opus 40. Day 3: Take a farm-to-table tour and visit local farms, cheese makers, and vineyards. Day 4: Kayak or tubing on the Esopus Creek or Hudson River. Day 5: Explore the village’s thriving art scene, with a visit to the Emerge Gallery. Day 6: Visit Falling Waters Preserve and go on a guided nature walk. Day 7: Enjoy a relaxing day in the village, strolling through local shops and cafes.

Food and Drink Scene of Saugerties

Saugerties is home to a vibrant food and drink scene, with an emphasis on farm-to-table cuisine and locally sourced ingredients.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Wood-fired pizza at Village Pizza: Made with fresh, locally sourced ingredients, Village Pizza’s wood-fired pies are a must-try for any foodie.
  • Grass-fed beef at Love Bites Cafe: This cozy cafe serves up delicious grass-fed beef dishes alongside creative cocktails and artisanal wines.
  • Hudson Valley Duck Breast at Miss Lucy’s Kitchen: This classic dish is elevated to new heights at Miss Lucy’s Kitchen, where it’s served with apples, cabbage, and local cider jus.

Local Beverages

  • Hudson Valley Wine Trail: Take a tour of the Hudson Valley Wine Trail, where you’ll sample some of the region’s best wines and spirits.
  • Hetta Glogg: A local mulled wine, served warm and infused with a variety of spices and herbs.
  • Keegan Ales: This local brewery offers a variety of creative beers, including the popular Mother’s Milk Stout.

Where to Stay in Saugerties: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Saugerties offers a variety of accommodations to suit every traveler’s needs and budget.

  • Diamond Mills Hotel & Tavern: This elegant hotel boasts luxury accommodations and sweeping views of the Esopus Creek.
  • The Winston Motel: A charming motel with affordable rates and easy access to the Catskill Mountains.
  • Village Inn of Saugerties: A historic guesthouse located in the heart of the village, with cozy rooms and a warm, welcoming atmosphere.

What to Buy in Saugerties: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

From artisanal cheeses to handmade pottery, Saugerties is home to a variety of unique items that make perfect souvenirs and gifts.

  • Cheese from Cheese Louise: This artisanal cheese shop offers a wide variety of locally made cheeses, including several award-winning selections.
  • Pottery at Pottery Trail Studios: This cooperative studio offers handmade pottery from several local artisans, a perfect complement to any home.
  • Books at Inquiring Minds Bookstore: Stock up on books and magazines at this charming independent bookstore, which hosts several author events throughout the year.

Local Culture and Customs of Saugerties

Saugerties takes pride in its sense of community and offers several events and festivals throughout the year that celebrate local culture.

  • Saugerties Farmers Market: A weekly market that brings together local farmers, artists, and producers.
  • Hudson Valley Garlic Festival: A celebration of all things garlicky, with live music, cooking demonstrations, and, of course, plenty of garlic.
  • Mum Festival: A celebration of the fall harvest, with a parade, apple pie contest, and craft fair.

Best Time to Visit Saugerties

The best time to visit Saugerties is between late May and early October, when the weather is mild and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. Fall is also a beautiful time to visit, with the changing colors of the leaves creating a stunning backdrop for outdoor adventures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saugerties

Do:

  • Take a farm-to-table tour and explore the region’s vibrant food scene.
  • Go on a hike in the Catskill Mountains for breathtaking views of the landscape.
  • Visit the Saugerties Lighthouse, a National Historic Landmark and an iconic feature of the village.

Don’t:

  • Leave without trying some of the local cuisine, such as the grass-fed beef at Love Bites Cafe.
  • Miss the Hudson Valley Wine Trail, where you can sample some of the region’s best wines and spirits.
  • Forget to bring hiking boots and a camera, as there are countless photo opportunities in Saugerties.

Safety and Health

Safety in Saugerties

Saugerties is generally a safe area, but it’s always best to exercise caution and be aware of your surroundings. When hiking or enjoying outdoor activities, make sure to stay on marked trails and carry plenty of water and snacks.

Health in Saugerties

There are several medical facilities in Saugerties, including a hospital and several urgent care clinics. It’s always a good idea to bring any necessary medications, as well as first-aid supplies, when traveling to the area.

Getting to Saugerties: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Saugerties

The closest major airport to Saugerties is Albany International Airport, approximately 50 miles away. Other airports in the area include Stewart International Airport and Westchester County Airport.

Bus Stations Near Saugerties

Trailways offers several bus stops in the area, including one in the heart of Saugerties and several in nearby Kingston.

Other Options

Saugerties is easily accessible by car, with several major highways passing through the region. There are also several car rental companies in the area, making it easy to explore the village and surrounding areas at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Saugerties

Saugerties is a small village, making it easy to explore on foot. For those who prefer to drive, there are several car rental companies in the area, and parking is generally easy to find.

Where to Go Next from Saugerties: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations that make for great day trips from Saugerties, including:

  • Kingston: This historic city is just a short drive from Saugerties and offers a thriving arts scene, as well as several historic sites and museums.
  • Woodstock: This famous hippie town is located just a few miles from Saugerties and is known for its vibrant arts scene.
  • Hudson: Just across the river from Saugerties, Hudson offers charming antique shops, gourmet restaurants, and stunning views of the Hudson River.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventure, cultural enrichment, or simply a relaxing retreat, Saugerties has something to offer everyone. With its rich history, beautiful natural surroundings, and vibrant food and drink scene, Saugerties is a hidden gem that’s sure to leave a lasting impression on any traveler.
