Ulster County is a beautiful destination located in the Catskills region of New York State, known for its stunning natural landscapes, charming villages, and rich history. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, or simply a relaxing getaway, Ulster County has something to offer everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will share everything you need to know to plan an unforgettable trip to Ulster County.

Introduction to Ulster County

Located in the Mid-Hudson and Catskills region of New York State, Ulster County is a popular destination for both locals and tourists. The county is home to a variety of natural attractions, including breathtaking mountains, scenic rivers, and serene forests. Visitors can also explore the county’s rich history, with many museums and historical sites scattered throughout the area. Ulster County is also known for its vibrant cultural scene, with many festivals, concerts, and art galleries to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Ulster County

Ulster County is home to a vast array of attractions, ranging from natural wonders to cultural landmarks. Here are some of the top places to visit in Ulster County:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Senate House State Historic Site: This beautiful property in Kingston has the distinction of being the first capital of New York State.
  • The Hudson Valley Rail Trail: This 4-mile path near Highland lets hikers and bikers explore the area’s scenic beauty.
  • The Old Dutch Church: This historic church in Kingston was built in 1852 and is a popular destination for architecture and history enthusiasts.
  • Woodstock Museum: This museum is dedicated to celebrating the vibrant cultural scene that emerged in Woodstock in the 1960s, with exhibitions focused on music, art, and politics.
  • Samuel Dorsky Museum of Art: Located on the campus of SUNY New Paltz, this museum houses an impressive collection of contemporary and modern art.

Natural Attractions of Ulster County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Catskills Mountains: Ulster County is home to some of the best hiking and skiing in the Catskills.
  • Shawangunk Ridge: This beautiful ridge is known for its stunning rock formations and beautiful views.
  • Minnewaska State Park Preserve: This park is perfect for hiking, with many beautiful trails to explore.
  • Mohonk Mountain House: This beautiful resort is located on the shores of Lake Mohonk and offers guests a wide range of outdoor activities, including hiking, boating, and rock climbing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ulster County

Ulster County is a fantastic destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with miles of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the best routes to take:

  • Ashokan Rail Trail: This 11.5-mile trail takes you through some of the most beautiful landscapes in the area, including farmland, woods, and creeks.
  • Wallkill Valley Rail Trail: This 22-mile trail stretches from Kingston to Gardiner and offers a scenic path for bikers and hikers.
  • Overlook Mountain Trail: This 4.6-mile hike takes you to the top of Overlook Mountain, where you can enjoy stunning views of the valley below.
  • Catskill Scenic Trail: This 26-mile trail is perfect for bikers and hikers, with beautiful views of the Catskill Mountains.

Best Things to Do in Ulster County: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for adventure, relaxation, or cultural enrichment, Ulster County has something to offer everyone. Here are some of the best things to do in Ulster County:

  • Visit Woodstock: This charming town is famous for its vibrant arts scene and is home to many galleries, music venues, and shops.
  • Take a boat tour of the Hudson River: Enjoy beautiful views of the Hudson River while learning about the history and ecology of the area.
  • Visit a local farm: Ulster County is home to many farms, where you can pick fresh produce, enjoy a hayride, or pet farm animals.
  • Go hot air ballooning: Enjoy a bird’s eye view of the beautiful Ulster County landscapes from a hot air balloon.
  • Attend a cultural festival: There are many festivals throughout the year celebrating the music, art, and food of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ulster County

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Ulster County, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive and settle into your accommodations. Spend the afternoon exploring the charming village of Woodstock.

Day 2: Spend the day hiking in the Catskills or Shawangunk Ridge.

Day 3: Take a boat tour of the Hudson River and visit the Hudson Valley Rail Trail.

Day 4: Visit the Senate House State Historic Site and other historical landmarks in Kingston.

Day 5: Tour a local farm and explore the beautiful farmland of Ulster County.

Day 6: Go hot air ballooning and attend a cultural festival.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy your last day in Ulster County. Visit Mohonk Mountain House or take a leisurely bike ride on the Wallkill Valley Rail Trail.

Food and Drink Scene of Ulster County

Ulster County is home to a vibrant food and drink scene, with many local specialties and farm-to-table restaurants to enjoy. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to sample:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Apple cider donuts: Made with fresh, local apples, these donuts are a fall favorite in Ulster County.
  • Blueberry pancakes: Many local restaurants serve pancakes made with fresh blueberries picked at farms in the area.
  • Grass-fed beef: Ulster County is home to many farms that raise grass-fed beef, which is known for its delicious taste and health benefits.

Local Beverages

  • Hard cider: Ulster County is one of the top producers of hard cider in the country, with many local cideries to visit.
  • Craft beer: There are many local breweries in Ulster County, offering a variety of craft beers to sample.
  • Wine: Many local wineries offer tastings and tours, allowing visitors to sample the best wines of the region.

Where to Stay in Ulster County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ulster County offers a wide range of accommodations, from rustic cabins to luxurious resorts. Here are some options to consider:

  • Mohonk Mountain House: This beautiful resort offers guests access to a private lake, hiking trails, and a spa.
  • Kingston Bed and Breakfast: This charming bed and breakfast is located in the heart of Kingston and offers guests personalized service and comfortable rooms.
  • Woodstock Inn on the Millstream: This cozy inn is located in the charming village of Woodstock and features a beautiful garden and creek.
  • Lake Minnewaska Lodge: This picturesque lodge is located in the heart of Minnewaska State Park and offers guests stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

What to Buy in Ulster County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ulster County is home to many artisanal shops and boutiques, offering unique souvenirs and local products to bring home. Here are some suggestions:

  • Handmade pottery: Many local artists create beautiful pottery inspired by the natural beauty of Ulster County.
  • Local honey: Ulster County is known for its delicious honey, which is made from local wildflowers.
  • Maple syrup: Many farms in Ulster County produce maple syrup, which is perfect for pancakes and waffles.

Local Culture and Customs of Ulster County

Ulster County is home to a diverse culture and proud traditions that reflect its rich history. Some things to keep in mind as you explore the county include:

  • Respect the natural environment: Ulster County is known for its beautiful landscapes, so be sure to pack out any trash and avoid damaging plants and wildlife.
  • Celebrate local traditions: Many festivals and cultural events take place throughout the year, offering visitors an opportunity to learn about the history and traditions of the region.
  • Embrace slow living: Many locals in Ulster County embrace a slower pace of life, so take your time and enjoy the beautiful scenery and charming towns.

Best Time to Visit Ulster County

Ulster County is a year-round destination, with each season offering its own unique activities and attractions. Here’s what to expect throughout the year:

  • Spring: This is the perfect time to visit Ulster County if you love flowers and blooming landscapes. Many farmers’ markets open during this time of year.
  • Summer: This is the busiest season in Ulster County, with many outdoor activities and festivals taking place throughout the area.
  • Fall: This is the most popular season in Ulster County, with beautiful fall foliage and many apple orchards and pumpkin patches to visit.
  • Winter: This is a great time to visit Ulster County if you love skiing and winter sports, with many mountains and resorts offering activities for visitors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ulster County

As a visitor to Ulster County, it’s important to be respectful and mindful of the local culture and customs. Here are some general do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Embrace the slow living culture of Ulster County.
  • Respect the natural environment and local wildlife.
  • Try local food and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Disrespect local traditions and customs.
  • Overlook the local farm-to-table movement.

Safety and Health

Ulster County is generally a safe and healthy destination for visitors, but it’s important to be mindful of potential hazards and health concerns.

Safety in Ulster County

  • Be aware of ticks and tick-borne illnesses, which are common in the area.
  • Stay hydrated and be mindful of the heat during the summer months.
  • Practice basic safety precautions when hiking or participating in other outdoor activities.

Health in Ulster County

  • Make sure you have all necessary vaccinations before visiting Ulster County.
  • Be aware of any food allergies and inform restaurant staff of any dietary restrictions.
  • Pack appropriate clothing and gear for the weather and activities you plan to participate in.

Getting to Ulster County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ulster County is relatively easy to reach by car or public transportation. Here are some options to consider:

Airports Near Ulster County

  • Albany International Airport: located approximately 90 miles from Kingston
  • Stewart International Airport: located approximately 20 miles from New Paltz
  • Newark Liberty International Airport: located approximately 120 miles from Kingston

Bus Stations Near Ulster County

  • Adirondack Trailways: offers bus service to Kingston, New Paltz, and other areas in Ulster County.
  • Greyhound: offers bus service to Kingston and other areas in Ulster County.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: connects New York City to Rhinecliff and Poughkeepsie, which are both located near Ulster County.
  • Rental Car: rental cars are available at all major airports and in the nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Ulster County

Ulster County offers several transportation options for visitors, including:

  • Car rental: rental cars are available from all major airports and in the nearby cities.
  • Bike rental: many bike rental shops are located throughout Ulster County, offering visitors a fun and eco-friendly way to explore the area.
  • Public transportation: Adirondack Trailways offers bus service to many areas in Ulster County.

Where to Go Next from Ulster County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
