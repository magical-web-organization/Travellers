Introduction to Woodstock (New York)

Nestled in the heart of Catskills, Woodstock is a charming town known for its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and vibrant arts scene. While the town became famous for the legendary 1969 music festival, it has since transformed into a haven for artists, writers, and musicians.

Visitors can look forward to hiking in picturesque forests, enjoying live music at local venues, exploring museums and galleries, and savoring delicious local cuisine. In this Woodstock (New York) travel guide, we will take you on a tour of the town’s must-see attractions, share hiking and biking routes, recommend the best things to do, suggest nearby excursions, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Woodstock (New York)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Woodstock Artists Association & Museum (WAAM): Founded in 1919, WAAM is a renowned institution dedicated to showcasing the works of local artists. The museum features an impressive collection of over 2,000 pieces of art, including paintings, sculptures, and photographs.

  • Historic Byrdcliffe Art Colony: Established in 1902, this art colony has been home to some of the most influential artists of the 20th century, including Bob Dylan and Philip Guston. Visitors can explore the scenic 250-acre campus and attend art exhibitions and workshops.

Natural Attractions of Woodstock (New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Overlook Mountain: This 2,000-foot mountain offers stunning panoramic views of the Hudson Valley. Visitors can hike up to the summit, explore an abandoned hotel, and visit a historic fire tower.

  • Kaaterskill Falls: Just a short drive from Woodstock, this 260-foot waterfall is a popular destination for hikers and photographers. Visitors can explore the surrounding trails and enjoy breathtaking views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Woodstock (New York)

  • Tremper Mountain Trail: This 5-mile hike takes you through stunning forests and leads to the summit of Tremper Mountain. With an elevation gain of 2,000 feet, it is a challenging but rewarding hike.

  • Rail Trail: This 12.5-mile trail is popular with both hikers and bikers. The trail follows the old Ulster and Delaware Railroad and offers stunning views of the Catskill Mountains and lush forests.

Best Things to Do in Woodstock (New York): Activities and Tours

  • Upstate Films: This historic movie theater showcases independent and foreign films and is a favorite of locals and visitors alike.

  • The Colony: This popular music venue hosts regular live performances by local and national artists.

Week-Long Itinerary for Woodstock (New York)

Day 1: Explore downtown Woodstock, visit the Woodstock Artists Association & Museum (WAAM), and enjoy dinner at Cucina.

Day 2: Go on a hike to Overlook Mountain, explore the abandoned hotel, and enjoy a picnic lunch.

Day 3: Explore the Historic Byrdcliffe Art Colony, attend an art workshop or exhibition, and enjoy dinner at Garden Cafe on the Green.

Day 4: Go on a scenic drive to Kaaterskill Falls, hike to the waterfall, and enjoy a packed lunch.

Day 5: Take a day trip to Hudson, NY and explore the charming town, visit stores and galleries, and enjoy lunch at Backbar.

Day 6: Attend a live performance at The Colony, enjoy dinner at Bread Alone Bakery & Cafe, and take a stroll through the town.

Day 7: Enjoy breakfast at Oriole 9, take a bike ride on the Rail Trail, and relax at your hotel.

Food and Drink Scene of Woodstock (New York)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hudson Valley Foie Gras: This local delicacy is a must-try for food lovers. It is available at many local restaurants, including Silvia.

  • Artisanal Cheese: Woodstock is home to many artisanal cheese shops, including the Cheese Plate and Sunfrost Farm. Visitors can shop for a variety of locally made cheeses.

Local Beverages

  • Overlook Mountain Winery: This winery produces award-winning wines using locally grown grapes.

  • Woodstock Brewing: This local brewery is known for its craft beer, made from locally sourced ingredients.

Where to Stay in Woodstock (New York): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Woodstock Inn on the Millstream: This charming hotel is located in the heart of Woodstock and offers beautiful rooms, a refreshing pool, and lush gardens.

  • The Lodge Woodstock: This hip and trendy hotel offers stylish rooms, a cozy fireplace lounge, and complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Woodstock (New York): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Jewelry: Woodstock is known for its artisanal jewelry shops, including Tinker Toys and Jane’s Jewelry.

  • Handmade Crafts: Visitors can shop for unique handmade crafts at the annual Woodstock-New Paltz Art & Crafts Fair.

Local Culture and Customs of Woodstock (New York)

Woodstock has a rich artistic and bohemian culture, and visitors are encouraged to embrace the town’s unique vibe. Visitors should be prepared for occasional street performances and impromptu live music events.

Best Time to Visit Woodstock (New York)

The best time to visit Woodstock is in the summer, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the town is bustling with activity. Fall is also a great time to visit, as the surrounding forests transform into a stunning palette of colors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Woodstock (New York)

Do:

  • Embrace the town’s artistic and bohemian culture.
  • Enjoy local cuisine, including artisanal cheese and Hudson Valley Foie Gras.
  • Go on hikes and explore the natural beauty of the area.

Don’t:

  • Expect a traditional, mainstream experience.
  • Be disrespectful of the town’s unique culture and heritage.
  • Skip the opportunity to attend a live performance or explore local art galleries.

Safety and Health

Safety in Woodstock (New York)

Woodstock is generally a safe town, but visitors should take standard precautions, such as locking their cars and securing valuables.

Health in Woodstock (New York)

Visitors should be aware of the risk of Lyme disease, which is common in the area. It is recommended to wear protective clothing and use insect repellent when outdoors.

Getting to Woodstock (New York): Transportation Options and Logistics

  • Airports Near Woodstock (New York): The closest airport is Albany International Airport, which is approximately 50 miles away.

  • Bus Stations Near Woodstock (New York): Visitors can take a bus from New York City to Woodstock via Shortline Bus.

How to Get Around in Woodstock (New York)

Woodstock is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance. Taxis and rental cars are also available for visitors who prefer other modes of transportation.

Where to Go Next from Woodstock (New York): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Hudson, NY: This charming town is located just a short drive from Woodstock and is known for its antique shops, galleries, and restaurants.

  • Hunter Mountain: This skiing destination is located approximately 30 miles from Woodstock and offers stunning views and a variety of winter sports activities.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a music lover, an art enthusiast, a history buff, or an outdoor adventurer, Woodstock has something for everyone. We hope this travel guide has inspired you to plan your trip and explore all that this charming town has to offer.
