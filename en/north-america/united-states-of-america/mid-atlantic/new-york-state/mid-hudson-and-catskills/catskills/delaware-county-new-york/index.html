Introduction to Delaware County (New York)

Nestled in the heart of Catskills in New York, Delaware County offers a magnificent blend of natural beauty and cultural attractions. Famous for its serene villages, unspoiled landscapes, and historical architecture, Delaware County is a hidden gem for those who love exploring the countryside.

Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, a history buff, or a foodie, Delaware County has something for everyone. In this travel guide, we will help you discover the best attractions, food, accommodations, and more so that you can plan your best trip to Catskills yet.

List of Places to Visit in Delaware County (New York)

Delaware County has a lot to offer, from historical landmarks to natural attractions, and more. Here’s our curated list of things to see and do while you’re in the county.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Delaware & Ulster Railroad - Take a scenic train ride through the Catskill Mountains and learn about the history of the region.
  • Hanford Mills Museum - Discover the rich history of the industrial heritage of upstate New York.
  • The Belleayre Conservatory - Enjoy live music performances, lectures, and cultural events at the stunning Belleayre Mountain.
  • Delaware County Historical Association - Explore the history of the county through an extensive collection of artifacts and archives.

Natural Attractions of Delaware County (New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Catskill Park - Hike and camp amidst the stunning natural beauty of the Catskill Mountains.
  • Pepacton Reservoir - A popular spot for fishing and kayaking, with stunning views of the surrounding hills.
  • Plattekill Ski Area - A pristine skiing destination with some of the best trails in the state.
  • Bear Spring Mountain Wildlife Management Area - A great spot for hiking, fishing, and picnicking amidst the natural beauty of the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Delaware County (New York)

Delaware County is a paradise for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of its most popular routes:

  • the Long Path Trail
  • the Finger Lakes Trail
  • the Onteora Trail System
  • the Catskill Scenic Trail

Best Things to Do in Delaware County (New York): Activities and Tours

From food tours to art galleries, Delaware County has something for everyone. Here are some of the best things to see and do:

  • Tulip and Rose Cafe - Try their delicious Caribbean-inspired cuisine and handmade beverages.
  • Pakatakan Farmers Market - Visit the market to get fresh ingredients and other locally made products.
  • Roxbury Arts Group - Explore the local art scene through their art exhibitions and cultural events.
  • Burn Ayr Farm - Take a farm tour and learn about sustainable farming practices.

Food and Drink Scene of Delaware County (New York)

Delaware County is a foodie’s paradise, with plenty of local flavors and cuisine to explore. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • BBQ Pork Ribs - A specialty of Delaware County’s food scene.
  • The Famous Chicken Spiedie - A marinated chicken kebab, grilled to perfection and served on a bun or over salad greens.
  • Maple Syrup - A local favorite, used in a variety of dishes and desserts.

Local Beverages

  • Beers from the Catskill Brewery - A diverse selection of locally brewed beers.
  • Roxbury Wine & Spirits - Sample premium wines, spirits, and cider made from local fruits and grains.

Where to Stay in Delaware County (New York): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Delaware County has various accommodation options offering a comfortable and memorable stay. From cozy inns to luxurious resorts, here are our top picks for where to stay:

  • Andes Hotel - A historic hotel that exudes charm and comfort.
  • West Branch Resort - An unmatched luxury resort that is perfect for a romantic getaway.
  • Fire Mountain Inn - A dreamy retreat nestled in the hills of the Catskill Mountains.

What to Buy in Delaware County (New York): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Delaware County has various local products that you can take home as souvenirs, gifts, or keepsakes. Here are some of our favourite items:

  • Handmade Crafts from the Roxbury Arts Group
  • Hand-Dipped Candles from the Cave Mountain Brewing Company

Local Culture and Customs of Delaware County (New York)

The locals of Delaware County are friendly, outgoing, and embrace visitors with open arms. The community takes pride in their rich heritage, cultural diversity, and beautiful landscapes, and they are open to sharing their traditions with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Delaware County (New York)

The best time to visit Delaware County is during the spring and summer months, as the weather is mild and sunny, making it perfect for outdoor activities. The fall season also brings breathtaking foliage and cooler temperatures that are ideal for hiking and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Delaware County (New York)

Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting Delaware County:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Embrace outdoor activities, and scenic views of the landscapes.
  • Support local businesses and products from local artisans.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Disrespect historical landmarks or artifacts.
  • Flout traffic laws, or overlook safety and health protocols.

Safety and Health

Safety in Delaware County (New York)

Being a relatively safe area, Delaware County has low crime rates, making it an overall secure place to travel and explore. However, visitors should remain cautious and adhere to traffic laws to avoid any injuries or accidents.

Health in Delaware County (New York)

Delaware County has a robust healthcare system with various clinics and hospitals in the region that offer quality services to local residents and visitors.

Public transportation is not as heavily developed in Delaware County as other areas. So, we recommend renting a car or using a ride-sharing service for optimal convenience and mobility during your stay.

Where to Go Next from Delaware County (New York): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re reluctant to leave the beautiful Catskills region, here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can consider visiting:

  • Visit the neighbouring county of Schoharie, a scenic region with rich history and cultural attractions.
  • Go on a day trip to the Ashokan Reservoir, a popular recreational area for fishing, hiking, and picnicking.
  • Explore the Cooperstown region, known for its national baseball museum and hall of fame.

Conclusion

Delaware County is a true gem of upstate New York, filled with natural beauty, historical architecture, and vibrant culture. From hiking and skiing to enjoying local cuisine and artisanal products, this county has something for everyone. Plan your trip today, and get ready to experience the best of Catskills.
