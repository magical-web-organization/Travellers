Introduction to Orange County (New York)

Orange County (New York) is a hidden gem in the Catskills region of New York state, situated in the Mid-Hudson and Catskills area of the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States of America. The county is packed with natural wonders, charming villages, and rich history, making it an ideal destination for travelers seeking outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or a romantic weekend getaway.

List of Places to Visit in Orange County (New York)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Orange County Arboretum: A beautiful 35-acre garden with walking trails, a railway garden, and several rare and unusual trees and shrubs.
  • Storm King Art Center: One of the world’s leading open-air sculpture parks, housing more than 100 contemporary sculptures on 500 acres of fields, hills, and woodlands.
  • Washington’s Headquarters State Historic Site: George Washington’s Revolutionary War headquarters, with informative tours and exhibits on the history of the American Revolution.
  • Brotherhood Winery: America’s oldest winery, producing award-winning wines since 1839 in a charming renaissance-style building listed on the National Register of Historic Places.
  • Orange County Choppers: A custom motorcycle manufacturer famous for its reality TV show, American Chopper, showcasing over the top motorcycles as well as TV/movie memorabilia.

Natural Attractions of Orange County (New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hudson Highlands State Park: A 7,000-acre park with hiking trails, camping, fishing, boating, and stunning views of the Hudson River and the surrounding mountains.
  • Bear Mountain State Park: A 5,000-acre park with hiking trails, swimming and boating on Hessian Lake, and the famous Bear Mountain Zoo.
  • Black Rock Forest: A 3,800-acre forest reserve with hiking trails, birdwatching, and research facilities, offering breathtaking views from its summits.
  • Sterling Forest State Park: A 21,000-acre park with hiking and biking trails, fishing, hunting, skiing, and snowshoeing, as well as the famous Renaissance Faire.
  • Shawangunk Ridge: A famous rock-climbing and hiking destination, part of the Appalachian Trail, with stunning views from its peaks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Orange County (New York)

  • The Appalachian Trail: A 2,200-mile trail stretching from Georgia to Maine, passing through Orange County and offering breathtaking views of the mountains and valleys.
  • Walkway Over the Hudson: A 1.28-mile linear park on a former railroad bridge, connecting Poughkeepsie to Highland and offering stunning views of the Hudson River and the surrounding area.
  • Riverfront Park: A scenic trail along the Hudson River, perfect for walking or biking, with plenty of picnic areas and benches to enjoy the views.
  • Heritage Trail: A 15-mile multi-use rail trail connecting Goshen to Middletown, passing through several picturesque villages and offering stunning views of the countryside.

Best Things to Do in Orange County (New York): Activities and Tours

  • Hot air balloon rides: Experience Orange County from a bird’s eye view and enjoy the stunning scenery of the Hudson Valley from a hot air balloon.
  • Horseback riding: Explore the natural beauty of Orange County on horseback, with several stables offering guided tours and riding lessons.
  • Wine tours: Visit Orange County’s wineries, taste their award-winning wines, and learn about the wine-making process and the history of the region.
  • Golfing: Several world-class golf courses are available in Orange County, including West Point Golf Course, Hudson Hills Golf Course, and Monticello Golf Club.
  • River cruises: Take a scenic cruise on the Hudson River or the Wallkill River and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Food and Drink Scene of Orange County (New York)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Beef brisket sandwich: Slow-cooked beef brisket on a seeded bun with pickles and onions, served with a side of coleslaw and barbecue sauce.
  • Apple cider donuts: A local specialty made from apple cider, cinnamon, and sugar, served fresh from the oven.
  • Hudson Valley foie gras: A delicacy made from the liver of ducks or geese, served with fruit preserves and crisp bread.
  • Locally brewed beer: Orange County has several microbreweries, including Rushing Duck Brewery, Equilibrium Brewery, and Sloop Brewing Co., offering a wide variety of craft beers.
  • Bialys: A traditional Jewish bread roll, baked with onions and filled with cream cheese or lox.

Local Beverages

  • Apple cider: A refreshing drink made from pressed apples, often served warm with cinnamon and cloves in the fall.
  • Wine: Orange County’s wineries produce several award-winning wines, including Chardonnay, Cabernet Franc, and Riesling.
  • Hard cider: A fermented alcoholic beverage made from apples, often sweetened and flavored with fruit or spices.
  • Bourbon: Orange County Distillery produces a delicious bourbon, aged in charred oak barrels and made from locally sourced ingredients.
  • Hudson Valley coffee: Hudson Valley is known for its artisanal coffee shops, serving quality coffee and espresso drinks.

Where to Stay in Orange County (New York): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Thayer Hotel at West Point: A historic hotel located on the scenic grounds of West Point Military Academy, offering luxurious rooms, suites, and villas.
  • The Caldwell House Bed and Breakfast: A charming B&B located in the historic village of Salisbury Mills, offering comfortable rooms and homemade breakfast.
  • Hampton Inn Harriman Woodbury: A modern hotel located near the Woodbury Common Premium Outlets, offering spacious rooms, free breakfast, and a fitness center.
  • Mountain Creek Resort: A mountain resort located in nearby Vernon, NJ, offering skiing, snowboarding, and other outdoor activities.
  • Yogi Bear’s Jellystone Park™ at Birchwood Acres: A family-friendly campground located in the scenic countryside of Greenfield Park, offering cabins, lodges, and campsites.

What to Buy in Orange County (New York): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maple syrup: Orange County is known for its maple syrup, made from the sap of sugar maple trees and often sold in decorative glass bottles.
  • Hand-crafted pottery: Several local artisans create unique and beautiful pottery pieces, often sold at craft fairs and farmer’s markets.
  • Antiques: Orange County has several antique shops and flea markets, offering a wide selection of vintage items and collectibles.
  • Local honey: Honeybee products, including honey, beeswax candles, and lip balms, are available at several local apiaries.
  • Artwork: Several local artists create beautiful paintings, sculptures, and photographs, often inspired by the natural beauty of the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Orange County (New York)

Orange County has a rich cultural history, influenced by its proximity to New York City and its location in the Hudson Valley. The region is known for its artistic and literary communities, as well as its agricultural heritage. Orange County is home to several agricultural fairs and festivals, celebrating everything from apples and maple syrup to strawberries and pumpkins. The region is also known for its historical landmarks, including the West Point Military Academy, the home of Franklin D. Roosevelt, and the site of the Battle of Minisink, the only Revolutionary War battle fought in Orange County.

Best Time to Visit Orange County (New York)

The best time to visit Orange County is during the fall, from September to November, when the foliage is at its peak and the weather is cool and crisp. This is also the time for several harvest festivals, including the Warwick Applefest, the Hudson Valley Wine and Food Festival, and the Warwick Valley Quilters Guild Quilt Show. Other popular times to visit are the spring, from April to June, when the flowers are in bloom, and the summer, from July to August, when the weather is warm, and outdoor activities abound.

Do’s and Don’ts of Orange County (New York)

Do:

  • Do visit the region’s wineries and microbreweries and try their award-winning wines and beers.
  • Do explore the local art galleries and museums, showcasing a wide variety of artistic styles and mediums.
  • Do visit the local farmer’s markets and try the fresh, locally sourced produce and artisanal products.

Don’t:

  • Don’t miss out on the natural attractions, including hiking trails, parks, and wildlife preserves.
  • Don’t forget to bring warm clothing during the fall and winter months, as the weather can be unpredictable.
  • Don’t hesitate to ask the locals for recommendations on the best restaurants, bars, and shopping destinations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Orange County (New York)

Orange County is generally a safe destination for tourists, with low crime rates and a welcoming community. However, visitors should take standard safety precautions, such as locking car doors and avoiding unfamiliar neighborhoods at night. Hikers and outdoor enthusiasts should be aware of potential hazards, such as poison ivy, ticks, and slippery rocks and trails.

Health in Orange County (New York)

Orange County has several world-class hospitals and medical facilities, offering top-notch medical care and emergency services. Visitors should have adequate travel insurance, as medical treatment in the United States can be very expensive. Additionally, visitors should be aware of potential health risks, such as Lyme disease from tick bites, and take appropriate precautions, such as wearing long sleeves and pants and using insect repellent.

Getting to Orange County (New York): Transportation Options and Logistics

Orange County is easily accessible by car, bus, and train, with several airports located nearby.

Airports Near Orange County (New York)

  • Stewart International Airport (SWF): Located in Newburgh, NY, offering flights to major cities across the United States, as well as international destinations.
  • Newark Liberty International Airport (EWR): Located in Newark, NJ, offering flights to major cities across the United States, as well as international destinations.
  • John F. Kennedy International Airport (JFK): Located in Queens, NY, offering flights to major cities across the United States, as well as international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Orange County (New York)

  • Shortline Bus: Lines 196, 197, and 198 connect Orange County with New York City and several other major cities in the region.
  • Trailways: Provides service to Orange County from several major cities in the region, including New York City, Albany, and Binghamton.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: Provides train service to the region, with stops in Poughkeepsie and Rhinecliff.
  • Car rental: Several car rental agencies are available in the region, including Avis, Budget, and Hertz.

How to Get Around in Orange County (New York)

Orange County is easily navigable by car, with several major highways and scenic roads connecting the various towns and attractions. Additionally, several taxi and ride-sharing services are available in the region, including Uber and Lyft. Visitors can also explore the region by bike, with several bike rental shops available in the area.

Where to Go Next from Orange County (New York): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Orange County is surrounded by several other fascinating destinations, offering diverse experiences and attractions.

  • New York City: Just an hour’s drive from Orange County, New York City offers endless entertainment possibilities, including Broadway shows, world-class museums, and iconic landmarks.
  • Woodstock: A hippie haven in the Catskills region of New York, known for its vibrant arts scene, local boutiques, and natural beauty.
  • Cooperstown: A small town in upstate New York, home to the National Baseball Hall of Fame and Museum, as well as several art galleries and antique shops.
  • Philadelphia: A historic city with a rich cultural heritage, offering several iconic landmarks, including the Liberty Bell and Independence Hall.
  • Newport: A historic city in Rhode Island, known for its stunning mansions, scenic beaches, and vibrant cultural scene.

Conclusion

Orange County (New York) offers a wealth of attractions and experiences for travelers of all interests and ages. Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or a relaxing escape from everyday life, Orange County has something to offer. From the stunning natural beauty of the Hudson Valley to the charming villages and historic landmarks, this region is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to explore the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States.
