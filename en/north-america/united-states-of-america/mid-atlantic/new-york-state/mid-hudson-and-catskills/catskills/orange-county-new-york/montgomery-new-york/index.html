Introduction to Montgomery (New York)

Montgomery, a town located in Orange County, is known as the gateway to the Catskills in Mid-Hudson and Catskills, New York (state). The town is full of rich history, scenic views, and outdoor adventures. Visitors can spend days exploring the town, its museums, galleries, and hiking trails.

List of Places to Visit in Montgomery (New York)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Hill-Hold Museum

Hill-Hold Museum is a historic stone farmhouse built in the 1760s that has been restored to showcase a typical 19th-century farm. The museum offers guided tours of the farmhouse, carriage house, and restored barn, making it an excellent place to learn about the history of farming in Montgomery.

2. Orange County Farmers Museum

The Orange County Farmers Museum is dedicated to preserving the agricultural history of the region and is an excellent opportunity to learn about the area’s farming practices and traditions. Visitors can explore the museum’s collection of agricultural tools, implements, and machinery from the 19th and 20th centuries.

3. Montgomery’s Historic District

Montgomery’s historic district is home to several well-preserved historical buildings dating back to the 18th and 19th centuries. It’s the perfect place to take a stroll and admire the beautiful architecture of the period.

Natural Attractions of Montgomery (New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Thomas Bull Memorial Park

Thomas Bull Memorial Park is a sprawling public park that offers a range of activities for visitors of all ages. The park features hiking and biking trails, a golf course, tennis courts, picnic areas, and a playground.

2. Great Shawangunk Mountains

The Great Shawangunk Mountains offer breathtaking views of the region and are popular among hikers and climbers. Visitors can explore several trails leading to the mountain’s summit, offering stunning panoramic views of the Hudson Valley.

3. Stewart State Forest

Stewart State Forest comprises over 6,700 acres of wooded areas and offers visitors a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, horseback riding, and hunting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Montgomery (New York)

Montgomery boasts several hiking and biking trails, including the Winding Hills Park, Orange Heritage Trail, and Stewart State Forest. These trails offer a range of difficulty levels and are perfect for those who love outdoor activities and exploring nature.

Best Things to Do in Montgomery (New York): Activities and Tours

1. Apple Picking at DuBois Farms

DuBois Farms is a historic farm that offers apple picking in the fall season, making it a must-visit activity for all tourists. The farm also has a corn maze, pumpkin patch, and petting zoo, making it an excellent place for families with kids.

2. Wine Tasting at Brotherhood Winery

Brotherhood Winery is one of the oldest wineries in the United States and offers tours and wine tastings. It’s an excellent place to visit if you’re interested in learning about the history of winemaking in the region.

3. Shopping at Montgomery Farmers Market

Montgomery Farmers Market is a perfect place to explore local crafts, fresh produce, and sample delicious food. The market is open every Saturday between June and September.

Food and Drink Scene of Montgomery (New York)

Must-Try Dishes

Montgomery boasts many restaurants and eateries serving delicious local cuisine, including apple cider donuts, Hudson Valley-inspired brisket, farm-to-table dishes, and wines from local wineries.

Local Beverages

Montgomery is known for its wineries, breweries, and distilleries. Visitors can try local wines at Brotherhood Winery, or enjoy locally brewed beer at Pine Island Brewing Company. Warwick Valley Winery and Distillery offers a range of wines, ciders, and spirits.

Where to Stay in Montgomery (New York): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors to Montgomery can choose from a range of accommodation options, including Airbnb rentals, hotels, bed and breakfasts, and guesthouses. Some popular options include The Borland House Bed & Breakfast and the Bricktown Inn.

What to Buy in Montgomery (New York): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Montgomery has several unique shops offering souvenirs, local arts and crafts, and local produce. Visitors can explore the shops and take home handmade soap, candles, knick-knacks, and other souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Montgomery (New York)

Montgomery’s local culture is influenced by the town’s rich history and agricultural heritage. Visitors can expect to find friendly locals, lush countryside, and an appreciation of local culture and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Montgomery (New York)

The best time to visit Montgomery is between May and October when temperatures are mild, and visitors can enjoy outdoor activities and festivals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Montgomery (New York)

Montgomery is a safe town. However, visitors are advised to take precautions while hiking or exploring the outdoors, such as staying on designated trails and being alert for wildlife.

Health in Montgomery (New York)

Visitors to Montgomery should take standard health precautions, such as wearing sunscreen and staying hydrated while enjoying outdoor activities.

Getting to Montgomery (New York): Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can reach Montgomery by car, bus, or train. The nearest airports are Stewart International Airport and Albany International Airport.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Montgomery by train. The nearest train station is in Middletown.

How to Get Around in Montgomery (New York)

The best way to get around Montgomery is by car. Visitors can also explore the area by bike or on foot.

Conclusion
