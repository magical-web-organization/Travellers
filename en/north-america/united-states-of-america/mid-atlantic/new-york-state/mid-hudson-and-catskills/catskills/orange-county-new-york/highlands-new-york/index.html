Introduction to Highlands (New York)

Highlands is a charming town situated in Orange County, New York, within the Catskill Mountains. Known for its natural beauty, recreational opportunities, and rich history, Highlands has become a popular destination for travelers seeking a peaceful escape from their busy lives.

Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast looking for an adventure or a history buff interested in exploring museums and cultural attractions, Highlands has something to offer. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll cover the top things to do in Highlands, including must-visit attractions, hiking and biking routes, local food and drink scene, accommodations, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Highlands (New York)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • West Point Military Academy Museum: Located within the grounds of the United States Military Academy at West Point, this museum houses an impressive collection of military memorabilia and artifacts dating back to the American Revolution.
  • Fort Montgomery State Historic Site: Built in 1776 to guard the Hudson River during the American Revolution, Fort Montgomery is a National Historic Landmark and a popular destination for history buffs.
  • The Irish Cultural Center: The center celebrates the rich Irish heritage of the Hudson River Valley, featuring cultural and educational programs, live music and entertainment, and more.
  • DIA:Beacon Museum: A modern art museum located in a former Nabisco factory, showcasing some of the most significant contemporary art pieces of the 1960s to the present day.
  • Bannerman Castle: Located on Pollepel Island, the Bannerman Castle is a national historic landmark and offers guided tours that take visitors through the history of the island and the extraordinary story of Bannerman’s Island arsenal.

Natural Attractions of Highlands (New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Beacon Park: A popular hiking destination that offers panoramic views of the Hudson Valley. The hike to the top of the mountain takes about 2 hours, but the view is well worth the effort.
  • Hudson Highlands State Park: This 6,000-acre park is a top destination for hiking and rock climbing enthusiasts, offering stunning views of the Hudson River and surrounding mountains.
  • Bear Mountain State Park: Located just a short drive from Highlands, guests can enjoy hiking, biking, boating, picnicking, swimming, and more while taking in the stunning natural beauty of the park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Highlands (New York)

  • Breakneck Ridge Trail: This challenging four-mile trail features steep, rocky climbs and breathtaking views of the Hudson River and the surrounding mountains.
  • Fishkill Ridge Loop Trail: A moderate six-mile hike that provides panoramic views and showcases the natural beauty of the Hudson Valley.
  • Hudson Highlands Gateway Park Trail: A network of trails that connect several state parks within the region, including the Hudson Highlands State Park and the Fahnstock State Park.

Best Things to Do in Highlands (New York): Activities and Tours

  • Hudson River Adventures: This tour company offers a variety of scenic tours, including sunset wine cruises, fall foliage trips, and visits to Bannerman’s Castle.
  • Storm King Art Center: A 500-acre outdoor sculpture museum that features over 100 sculptures, including works by Alexander Calder, Roy Lichtenstein, and Maya Lin.
  • Kayaking the Hudson River: Several companies offer guided kayaking tours that showcase the stunning views of the Hudson River from a unique perspective.

Week-Long Itinerary for Highlands (New York)

For visitors planning a week-long trip to Highlands, we recommend the following:

  • Day 1-3: Explore the cultural and historical attractions, including the West Point Military Academy Museum, Fort Montgomery State Historic Site, and the Bannerman Castle.
  • Day 4-6: Enjoy the natural beauty of the Hudson Valley by visiting Mount Beacon Park, Hudson Highlands State Park, and Bear Mountain State Park.
  • Day 7: Take a scenic tour of the Hudson River or kayak through the stunning views of the Hudson River.

Food and Drink Scene of Highlands (New York)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hudson Valley apple pie: A traditional dessert made with locally sourced apples, cinnamon, and a flaky crust
  • Hudson Valley duck: A popular dish that showcases the fresh, local produce and meats of the region.
  • Artisanal cheese plates: The Hudson Valley is known for producing some of the best artisanal cheese in the world, featuring a wide range of flavor profiles and textures.

Local Beverages

  • Hudson Valley wine: The region is home to several award-winning wineries that produce a variety of varietals, including Chardonnay, Cabernet Franc, and Riesling.
  • Local craft beer: Highlands has become a hub for local craft breweries, featuring a wide range of beer styles and flavors.
  • Apple cider: Made with fresh, locally sourced apples, the apple cider of the Hudson Valley is a must-try for visitors.

Where to Stay in Highlands (New York): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Roundhouse at Beacon Falls: A luxury hotel located in an old mill, offering beautiful views of the waterfall and surrounding mountains.
  • Hudson Valley Hostel: A cozy hostel located within walking distance of the hiking trails and scenic views of the Hudson Highlands State Park.
  • Buttermilk Falls Inn and Spa: A charming inn and spa that offers a relaxing escape from the city life.

What to Buy in Highlands (New York): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Artisanal cheese: The Hudson Valley produces some of the best cheese in the world, and visitors can bring home the taste of the region by purchasing different varieties of cheese.
  • Local wines and craft beer: Visitors can bring home a taste of the Hudson Valley by purchasing locally produced wine and craft beer.
  • Handmade crafts and art: The Hudson Valley is home to several artisans and artists who create unique handmade crafts and art pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of Highlands (New York)

The culture of the Hudson Valley is a unique blend of local customs and traditions. Visitors can experience the local culture by attending festivals, farmers markets, and cultural events, such as the Hudson River Shakespeare Festival and the Hudson Valley Garlic Festival.

Best Time to Visit Highlands (New York)

The best time to visit Highlands is during the fall season when the leaves are changing colors. The months of September, October, and November offer beautiful views of the changing autumn leaves and are perfect for hiking and outdoor activities. The winter season is also a great time to visit, with the opportunity to enjoy winter sports and cozy up by the fireplace after a day on the slopes.

Do’s and Don’ts of Highlands (New York)

Do:

  • Do explore the natural beauty of Highlands by taking a scenic hike or kayak trip on the Hudson River.
  • Do try some of the local food and drinks, including the fall apple pie and locally brewed beer and wine.
  • Do attend cultural events, such as the Hudson River Shakespeare Festival and the Hudson Valley Garlic Festival.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t trespass or enter private property without permission.
  • Don’t underestimate the difficulty of some hiking trails. Be sure to bring appropriate gear and be prepared for the conditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Highlands (New York)

Highlands is a relatively safe town, but visitors should take standard safety precautions. Travelers should be aware of their surroundings, avoid walking alone at night, and keep their valuables in a safe place.

Health in Highlands (New York)

Visitors to Highlands should be up-to-date on routine vaccinations, including measles, mumps, and rubella (MMR), diphtheria-tetanus-pertussis, chickenpox, and the annual flu shot.

Getting to Highlands (New York): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Highlands (New York)

The closest airport to Highlands is the Stewart International Airport, located approximately 15 miles away. Other nearby airports include Newark Liberty International Airport and LaGuardia Airport.

Bus Stations Near Highlands (New York)

Visitors to Highlands can take a bus from the Port Authority Bus Terminal in New York or the Newburgh Bus Terminal in Newburgh, NY.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to the Poughkeepsie Station and then take a taxi or hire a car to get to Highlands.

How to Get Around in Highlands (New York)

The best way to get around Highlands is by car, but visitors can also take taxis or explore the town on foot or bicycle.

Where to Go Next from Highlands (New York): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • New York City: Just 60 miles away from Highlands, visitors can explore the Big Apple and its famous attractions, including the Statue of Liberty, the Empire State Building, and Central Park.
  • Hudson Valley Wine Trail: A scenic route that showcases the best wineries in the region and the stunning natural beauty of the Hudson Valley.
  • Cold Spring: A quaint town that is a short drive from Highlands and features boutique shops, antique stores, and outdoor recreational opportunities.

Conclusion

Highlands is an exciting destination that offers a variety of attractions and experiences for visitors. Whether you’re looking to explore the local history, enjoy the natural beauty, or indulge in the local food and drink scene, Highlands has something for everyone.
