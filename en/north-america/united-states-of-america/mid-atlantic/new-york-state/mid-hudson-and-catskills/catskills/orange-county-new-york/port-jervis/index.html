Introduction to Port Jervis

Located in Orange County in the state of New York, Port Jervis is a small, picturesque town situated at the convergence of the Delaware and Neversink Rivers. Nestled in the foothills of the Catskill Mountains, Port Jervis offers visitors a chance to experience the natural beauty and outdoor activities of the area while also exploring its rich history and cultural attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Port Jervis

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Minisink Battle Monument: This monument commemorates the clash between American forces and British loyalists during the Revolutionary War.

  • Tri-States Railway Preservation Society: Located in a restored train station, this museum showcases the history of railroading in the region.

  • Grey Towers National Historic Site: This elegant mansion was the summer home of Gifford Pinchot, the first Chief of the US Forest Service and two-term governor of Pennsylvania.

  • Fort Decker Museum of History: Discover the early history of Port Jervis through this museum’s exhibits on Native Americans, early settlers, and the Revolutionary War.

Natural Attractions of Port Jervis: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Delaware River Waterfront: Enjoy scenic views of the river while hiking, fishing, kayaking, or tubing along its shores.

  • Hawk’s Nest: Drive along this winding road for stunning views of the Delaware River Valley.

  • Elks-Brox Memorial Park: A popular spot for picnics and outdoor activities, this park offers beautiful views of the surrounding mountains.

  • Port Jervis Watershed Trails: Explore the natural beauty of the area with a hike along one of the many trails in the nearby watershed.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Port Jervis

  • Elks-Brox Memorial Park Loop Trail: A beginner-friendly hike with scenic views of the surrounding mountains and river.

  • Point Peter Loop: A moderate hike through the forest with views of the Delaware River and a rocky outcrop.

  • Hawks Nest Trail: A challenging but rewarding hike with stunning views of the Delaware River Valley.

  • The D&H Canal Towpath Trail: A flat, easy bike ride along the historic canal towpath with beautiful views of the Delaware River.

Best Things to Do in Port Jervis: Activities and Tours

  • Hang Gliding and Paragliding: Take to the skies with a tandem flight over the Delaware River Valley.

  • River Trips and Tubing: Explore the river by kayak, canoe, or tube.

  • Tri-State Rock Climbing: Scale the rocks and cliffs of the Shawangunk Mountains with a guided climbing tour.

  • Antique Shopping: Hunt for treasures at the many antique shops and flea markets around Port Jervis.

Local Culture and Customs of Port Jervis

Port Jervis is a small, friendly town where visitors can experience the best of small-town hospitality. Locals are proud of their rich history and natural resources, so be sure to ask for recommendations on the best things to do in the area.

Best Time to Visit Port Jervis

The best time to visit Port Jervis is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and a variety of outdoor activities are available. Fall is also a great time to visit, as the foliage in the area is breathtaking. Winter can be cold and snowy, but visitors can enjoy winter sports like skiing and snowboarding nearby.

Safety and Health

Safety in Port Jervis

Port Jervis is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should still take basic safety precautions like locking car doors and not leaving valuables in plain sight. When hiking or exploring outdoors, be sure to bring appropriate gear and tell someone your plans.

Health in Port Jervis

There are several medical facilities in Port Jervis, including the Bon Secours Community Hospital and several urgent care centers. Visitors should also take precautions against tick-borne illnesses, as the area is known to have a high prevalence of ticks.

Getting to Port Jervis: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Port Jervis

  • Stewart International Airport: Located in Newburgh, NY, this airport is about an hour’s drive from Port Jervis.

  • Lehigh Valley International Airport: Located in Allentown, PA, this airport is about an hour and a half’s drive from Port Jervis.

Bus Stations Near Port Jervis

  • Trailways of New York: This bus company has a stop in Port Jervis, providing connections to New York City and other nearby destinations.

  • Greyhound: Greyhound buses also stop in Port Jervis, providing connections to destinations around the country.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Port Jervis from New York City, which is about a 2-hour drive away. Alternatively, visitors can take the train to the nearby Middletown station and then take a cab or rental car to Port Jervis.

How to Get Around in Port Jervis

The best way to get around Port Jervis is by car, as public transportation in the area is limited. Visitors can also bike or walk around the town and nearby parks.

Where to Go Next from Port Jervis: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bethel Woods Center for the Arts: This outdoor music venue is located about an hour’s drive from Port Jervis and hosts concerts and events throughout the summer.

  • Delaware Water Gap National Recreation Area: This beautiful park offers hiking, camping, and other outdoor activities just a short drive from Port Jervis.

  • Woodbury Commons: Located about 40 minutes from Port Jervis, this outlet mall is a popular shopping destination in the region.
