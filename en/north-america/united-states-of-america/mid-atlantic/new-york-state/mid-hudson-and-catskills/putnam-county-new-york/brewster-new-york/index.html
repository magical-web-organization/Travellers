Introduction to Brewster, NY

Brewster is a charming village located in Putnam County, New York. It is situated in the Mid-Hudson and Catskills region, making it a great destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike. In this guide, we’ll take you through the best things to do, places to visit, and where to stay in Brewster, NY.

List of Places to Visit in Brewster, NY

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Putnam History Museum: A museum that showcases the history of Putnam County, from Native American times to current day.
  • Southeast Museum: A local history museum that features exhibits and cultural events.
  • Dykeman Farmhouse Museum: Built in the 18th century, this restored house tells the story of Dutchess County’s early settlers.
  • Thunder Ridge Ski Area: A popular ski resort that attracts visitors from all over the state.

Natural Attractions of Brewster, NY: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tilly Foster Farm: A beautiful working farm and nature preserve. Visitors can hike the trails, fish in the pond, and enjoy the scenery.
  • Wonder Lake State Park: A state park that’s perfect for bird watching, picnics, and paddle boating.
  • Green Chimneys: A nature-oriented facility that offers hands-on, animal-assisted therapy programs for children and young adults.
  • Patterson Environmental Park: A sprawling park that features hiking trails, fishing spots, and a natural wetland.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Brewster, NY

  • Appalachian Trail: A scenic trail that runs through Brewster, offering picturesque mountain views.
  • Mine Hill Preserve: A hiking and biking trail that takes you through a wooded area with beautiful views.
  • Taconic-Hereford Multiple Use Area: A large park with over 35 miles of trails for hiking, biking, cross-country skiing, and snowmobiling.

Best Things to Do in Brewster, NY: Activities and Tours

  • Brewster Farmers Market: A weekly farmers’ market that offers fresh produce, baked goods, and other artisanal products.
  • Thunder Ridge Ski Area: A wonderful ski resort that is perfect for families and beginner skiers.
  • The Somers Holocaust Memorial Park: A moving memorial that honors those who lost their lives in the Holocaust.
  • The Captur Adventure Park: A family-friendly park that offers a range of outdoor activities, including zip lining, archery, and more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Brewster, NY

Day 1: Arrive in Brewster, NY and explore the local sights, like the Putnam History Museum, Southeast Museum, and Dykeman Farmhouse Museum.

Day 2: Take a hiking tour of the Appalachian Trail, followed by a visit to Thunder Ridge Ski Area.

Day 3: Enjoy the natural beauty of Tilly Foster Farm and the Patterson Environmental Park.

Day 4: Take a boat tour of Wonder Lake State Park and enjoy a picnic lunch.

Day 5: Participate in animal-assisted therapy activities at Green Chimneys.

Day 6: Explore the local food scene by visiting the Brewster Farmers Market and local restaurants.

Day 7: Depart Brewster, NY.

Food and Drink Scene of Brewster, NY

Must-Try Dishes

  • Brewster Pizza: Enjoy a slice of classic New York-style pizza.
  • The Barn: Try one of their famous burgers or sandwiches.
  • Sugar & Spice Cafe: Start your day with one of their homemade muffins or pastries.

Local Beverages

  • Meadowland Wine & Spirits: A locally-owned wine and spirits store that features a wide variety of New York State-made craft beverages.
  • Brewster Beer Garden: A popular spot for craft beer lovers that offers a rotating selection of beers from all over the state.

Where to Stay in Brewster, NY: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hampton Inn Danbury: Located just a short drive from Brewster, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and amenities like free hot breakfast and an indoor pool.
  • SpringHill Suites Danbury: This hotel is right off of I-84 and features spacious suites with separate living areas.
  • The Putting Green B&B: A charming bed and breakfast that is perfect for those who want a more relaxed and quiet stay.

What to Buy in Brewster, NY: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • The Carmel Farmers Market: A great place to buy fresh produce, artisanal cheese, and baked goods.
  • The Apothecary: A locally-owned boutique that offers handmade soaps, lotions, and other bath products.

Local Culture and Customs of Brewster, NY

Brewster is a friendly and welcoming community. While there are no specific customs to be aware of, it is always appreciated when visitors show respect for local culture and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Brewster, NY

The best time to visit Brewster is during the spring and summer months when the weather is mild and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Brewster, NY

Do:

  • Respect local wildlife and natural areas.
  • Keep an eye out for historical markers and landmarks while exploring Brewster.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural areas.
  • Ignore town ordinances or laws.

Safety and Health

Safety in Brewster, NY

Brewster is a safe community. Visitors should exercise usual precautions and keep their belongings secure.

Health in Brewster, NY

There are several hospitals and medical clinics in the area. Visitors should make sure they have any necessary medications with them, and seek medical attention if needed.

Getting to Brewster, NY: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Brewster, NY

  • Stewart International Airport: A regional airport located about 30 miles from Brewster.
  • Westchester County Airport: A larger airport located about 30 miles from Brewster.

Bus Stations Near Brewster, NY

  • Brewster Transportation Center: A bus station that offers MTA service to New York City.
  • Danbury CT Bus Station: A Greyhound bus station located about 10 miles from Brewster.

Other Options

  • Driving: Brewster is located off of Interstate 84 and is easily accessible by car.
  • Train: Metro-North Railroad offers service to Brewster from New York City.

How to Get Around in Brewster, NY

Most attractions in Brewster are within walking distance of each other, but visitors who want to explore the surrounding areas will need to rent a car or take a bus or train.

Where to Go Next from Brewster, NY: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • New York City: Take a day trip to the Big Apple and explore all it has to offer.
  • Poughkeepsie, NY: Explore the nearby town of Poughkeepsie, which is home to Vassar College and the Walkway Over the Hudson State Historic Park.

Conclusion

Brewster, NY is a wonderful destination for those who love the outdoors, local history, and small town charm. With a variety of activities, attractions, and accommodations, there’s something for everyone in this charming village.
