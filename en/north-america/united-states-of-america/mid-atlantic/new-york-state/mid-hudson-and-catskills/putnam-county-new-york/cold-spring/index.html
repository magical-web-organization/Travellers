Introduction to Cold Spring

Cold Spring is a picturesque village nestled right along the Hudson River, in Putnam County, New York. This charming town is a popular getaway destination for both locals and tourists thanks to its rich history, beautiful natural surroundings, and relaxed countryside ambiance.

List of Places to Visit in Cold Spring

Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or just looking for a relaxing place to spend some quality time with your loved ones, Cold Spring has something to offer for everyone. Here are some of the best places to visit in Cold Spring:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • West Point Military Academy – Visit the United States Military Academy at West Point, also known as West Point or The Academy.
  • Putnam History Museum – Learn about the local history of Putnam County and its role in the American Revolution.
  • Boscobel House and Gardens – Beautiful Georgian mansion converted into an art museum, with beautiful gardens included.

Natural Attractions of Cold Spring: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Breakneck Ridge Trail – A challenging hiking trail that provides stunning views of the Hudson River.
  • Hudson Highlands State Park – A massive park that offers an abundance of activities including hiking, fishing, and camping.
  • Cold Spring Farmers Market – A great place to get some fresh local produce and sample some delicious homemade goods from the surrounding areas. Open every Saturday from May to November.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cold Spring

  • Bull Hill – A moderate hiking trail that offers breathtaking views of the Hudson River.
  • Hudson Highlands Nature Museum – A unique hiking trail that includes a guided tour led by a naturalist and a visit to the Butterfly Sanctuary.

Best Things to Do in Cold Spring: Activities and Tours

  • Cold Spring Trolley Tours – Discover the history and beauty of Cold Spring aboard a charming vintage trolley.
  • Storm King Adventure Tours – Get an aerial view of the Hudson Valley aboard a hot air balloon or helicopter.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cold Spring

  • Day 1: Arrival in Cold Spring. Spend the afternoon strolling through Main Street and explore local shops.
  • Day 2: Visit West Point Military Academy and explore the stunning Hudson River views.
  • Day 3: Take a hike in the Hudson Highlands State Park and spend the afternoon sampling local cuisine at the Cold Spring Farmers Market.
  • Day 4: Relax and spend the day at Boscobel House and Gardens.
  • Day 5: Take a trolley tour of Cold Spring and discover its history and beauty.
  • Day 6: Take a hot air balloon or helicopter tour of the Hudson River Valley with Storm King Adventure Tours.
  • Day 7: Departure from Cold Spring.

Food and Drink Scene of Cold Spring

Cold Spring boasts a vibrant food and drink scene, with plenty of great restaurants and bars. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cold Spring Pizza – Enjoy a slice (or two) of delicious pizza from this local favorite.
  • Hudson Hils Café – A cozy spot for breakfast, brunch, or lunch that serves up delicious and fresh ingredients.
  • The Depot – Old-fashioned ice cream parlor that serves up some of the best homemade ice cream you’ll ever try.

Local Beverages

  • Peekskill Brewery – A local brewery that serves up some fantastic craft beer and food. A great place for lunch or dinner.
  • Hudson House Inn – Waterfront restaurant that offers delicious cocktails, wine, and beer along with beautiful views of the Hudson River.

Where to Stay in Cold Spring: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cold Spring offers a range of accommodations for travelers of all budgets. Some of the best options include:

  • The Hudson House Inn – A charming waterfront property with beautiful views of the Hudson River.
  • The Pig Hill Inn – A historic bed and breakfast that offers a cozy and comfortable stay.
  • The Old Drovers Inn – A beautiful country inn with antique decor and modern amenities.

What to Buy in Cold Spring: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products that you can buy in Cold Spring:

  • Cold Spring Apothecary – Natural beauty and wellness products made with locally sourced ingredients.
  • The Country Goose – A charming gift shop with unique home decor items and accessories.
  • Cold Spring General Store – A unique store that sells everything from artisanal soap to handcrafted candles.

Local Culture and Customs of Cold Spring

Cold Spring is a small and tight-knit community that takes pride in its heritage and traditions. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and way of life, including preserving the natural beauty of the area and supporting local businesses.

Best Time to Visit Cold Spring

The best time to visit Cold Spring is during the fall and spring months when the weather is mild, and the foliage is vibrant. However, the town is lovely all year round, and there are plenty of activities and events to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cold Spring

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Visit the local shops and support the small businesses.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Be loud or disruptive in residential areas.
  • Ignore the local history and culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cold Spring

Cold Spring is a relatively safe area. However, visitors should still take necessary precautions when traveling, such as locking their vehicles and valuables and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Cold Spring

Drinking tap water in Cold Spring and the surrounding areas is generally safe and free from harmful contaminants.

Getting to Cold Spring: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cold Spring

  • Stewart International Airport – 25-mile drive from Cold Spring

Bus Stations Near Cold Spring

  • Cold Spring Metro-North Station – Served by trains from New York City and beyond.

Other Options

  • Private car services (such as Uber)
  • Rental car services

How to Get Around in Cold Spring

Cold Spring is a walkable town, and visitors can easily explore the area on foot. However, renting a bike or a car is also an option for those who want to explore the surrounding areas more easily.

Where to Go Next from Cold Spring: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Beacon, NY

Explore the charming town of Beacon, NY, just 20 minutes away from Cold Spring. This picturesque town boasts beautiful river views, a vibrant art scene, and delicious cuisine.

New York City

The Big Apple is just over an hour away from Cold Spring. Take a day trip to explore the city’s famous landmarks and attractions, such as the Statue of Liberty, Central Park, and the Empire State Building.

Conclusion

Cold Spring, NY, is a charming town that offers an abundance of activities, attractions, and natural beauty. From hiking trails to historic landmarks, great food, and drink, there’s something for everyone in Cold Spring. Whether you’re looking to relax, explore, or learn, Cold Spring is a must-visit destination in the Hudson Valley.
