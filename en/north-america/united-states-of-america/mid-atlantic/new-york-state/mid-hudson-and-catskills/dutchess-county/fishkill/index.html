Introduction to Fishkill

Fishkill, a historic town in Dutchess County, New York, is nestled in the heart of the Mid-Hudson and Catskills regions. It was founded in the 17th century and served as a vital trading post. Today, it is a vibrant town that boasts a rich history, natural beauty, and plenty of recreational activities. From hiking to shopping, museums to cultural events, Fishkill offers something for everyone. In this guide, we invite you to explore the best of Fishkill and create lasting memories in this charming town.

List of Places to Visit in Fishkill

Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or simply looking for fun things to do, Fishkill has a lot to offer. From museums to outdoor adventures, here are some of the must-visit places in Fishkill:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Fishkill has a rich history, and you can experience it by visiting these museums and historical landmarks:

  • Old Dutch Church and Burying Ground: Located in the heart of Fishkill, the historic Old Dutch Church dates back to 1716. Its graveyard is the final resting place of many Revolutionary War soldiers, including one of George Washington’s bodyguards.
  • Van Wyck Homestead Museum: This historic house museum offers a glimpse into Fishkill’s past and highlights the lives of the Van Wyck family, who played a significant role in the Revolutionary War.
  • Fishkill Supply Depot: This National Historic Site was once a vital supply depot during the Revolutionary War. Today, you can take a self-guided tour and see what life was like for soldiers during the war.

Natural Attractions of Fishkill: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Fishkill is also home to various natural attractions that offer breathtaking views and outdoor activities:

  • Fishkill Ridge: This stunning mountain range provides challenging hiking trails and scenic vistas of the Hudson River Valley.
  • SplashDown Beach: This waterpark is a perfect destination for families, featuring more than a dozen rides and attractions.
  • Stony Kill Farm Environmental Education Center: This 1,000-acre farm offers opportunities to explore nature, with hiking trails, fishing, and educational programs about farm animals and sustainable agriculture.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fishkill

Hiking is one of the most popular activities in Fishkill, thanks to its beautiful natural landscapes. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Fishkill:

  • Fishkill Ridge: With its various trails and levels of difficulty, Fishkill Ridge is a favorite among hikers and mountain bikers. The trails offer stunning vistas of the Hudson Highlands.
  • Beacon-Newburgh Rail Trail: This 10-mile trail connects the towns of Beacon and Newburgh, offering stunning views of the Hudson River. The trail is suitable for walking, running, biking, and roller-skating.
  • East Fishkill Recreation Trail: This 5.5-mile trail is perfect for walkers, joggers, and bikers, with views of the Wappinger Creek and peaceful surroundings.

Best Things to Do in Fishkill: Activities and Tours

Fishkill offers various activities and tours that cater to different interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Fishkill:

  • Hudson Valley Food Tours: Experience the region’s culinary scene with a guided food tour that takes you to some of the best eateries and bars in the area.
  • Constitution Marsh Audubon Center and Sanctuary: This sanctuary is a perfect destination for bird-watching and offers educational programs on conservation and wildlife.
  • Namaste Organic Spa: Relax and rejuvenate with a massage, facial, or other wellness services at this top-rated spa.

Food and Drink Scene of Fishkill

Fishkill boasts a bustling food and drink scene, with a range of eateries, bars, and breweries that cater to all tastes. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and chips from The Dutchess Biercafe: This popular dish is a favorite among locals and visitors alike, served with crispy fries and creamy slaw.
  • BBQ chicken pizza from Mario’s Pizza: This pizza joint offers a unique twist on a classic pizza, with tender BBQ chicken, red onions, and a tangy BBQ sauce.
  • Grass-fed beef burger from The Hop: This craft beer and burger restaurant sources its beef from local farms and offers customizable burgers with inventive toppings.

Local Beverages

  • Craft beer from Sloop Brewing Co.: This brewery is a must-visit for beer lovers, with a taproom that offers a variety of unique brews.
  • Cider from Angry Orchard: This famous cidery is located just a few miles from Fishkill and offers tours, tastings, and a taproom.
  • Wine from Millbrook Vineyards & Winery: This award-winning winery is located in nearby Millbrook, offering tours and tastings of its high-quality wines.

Where to Stay in Fishkill: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Fishkill has a range of accommodation options that cater to different preferences and budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay in Fishkill:

  • The Roundhouse: This boutique hotel features modern, industrial-style rooms, on-site dining, and scenic views of the Hudson River.
  • Hampton Inn Fishkill: This popular hotel offers comfortable rooms, an indoor pool, and complimentary breakfast.
  • Magnuson Hotel Fishkill: This budget-friendly hotel offers clean rooms and a convenient location near the town’s attractions.

What to Buy in Fishkill: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring back home, Fishkill offers various options:

  • LaGrange Farm Market: This market offers fresh produce, baked goods, and other local products.
  • Country Goose: This gift shop features a range of handcrafted items, including candles, quilts, and pottery.
  • The Stormville Airport Antique Show and Flea Market: This market offers a unique shopping experience, with vintage items, antiques, and collectibles.

Local Culture and Customs of Fishkill

Fishkill, like many small towns in America, has a strong sense of community and a unique culture. Residents are proud of their town’s rich history and natural beauty and are welcoming to visitors. It is customary to greet people with a friendly smile and engage in small talk when meeting someone new.

Best Time to Visit Fishkill

Fishkill is a year-round destination, with each season offering something unique. If you’re interested in outdoor activities, spring and fall are the best times to visit, with mild temperatures and colorful landscapes. Summer is ideal for water activities, while winter offers skiing and other winter sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fishkill

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Fishkill:

Do:

  • Tip servers and bartenders 15-20%.
  • Take advantage of the town’s natural beauty by going hiking, biking, or taking a scenic drive.
  • Try the local cuisine and visit the farmers’ markets for fresh produce and other local products.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places or parks.
  • Drive while under the influence of alcohol or drugs.
  • Ignore parking regulations or traffic laws.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fishkill

Fishkill is a relatively safe town, with a low crime rate. However, it’s important to practice basic safety precautions, such as locking your car and not leaving valuables in plain sight.

Health in Fishkill

Fishkill has several medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics. It’s important to have appropriate travel insurance before arriving in Fishkill.

Getting to Fishkill: Transportation Options and Logistics

Fishkill is easily accessible by car, train, or bus. Here are your options:

  • By Car: Fishkill is located off Interstate 84, which connects with other major highways, including the New York State Thruway (I-87).
  • By Train: The Fishkill station is served by Amtrak and the Metro-North Railroad, connecting to New York City and other towns in the Hudson River Valley.
  • By Bus: Trailways NY and others have scheduled bus service to Fishkill from various major cities in the United States.

Airports Near Fishkill

  • Stewart International Airport, located in Newburgh, NY, is the closest airport to Fishkill, located approximately 15 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Fishkill

  • Trailways NY provides regular bus service to Fishkill from various destinations in the Northeast.

Other Options

  • Taxi service, car rentals, and ride-sharing services are available in Fishkill and can be booked in advance or upon arrival.

How to Get Around in Fishkill

Fishkill is a walkable town with a compact downtown area, but a car is necessary to explore the surrounding areas and attractions. You can also rent a bike or use ride-sharing services.

Where to Go Next from Fishkill: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Fishkill is located near various other charming towns and attractions in the Mid-Hudson and Catskills regions. Here are some recommendations for nearby destinations:

  • Hudson: This town is known for its art galleries, antique shops, and historic architecture.
  • Rhinebeck: This village features a vibrant downtown area with shops and restaurants, and a historic site, the Rhinebeck Aerodrome.
  • Catskills: This mountainous area encompasses various towns and attractions, including hiking trails, ski resorts, and charming historic homes and inns.

Conclusion

Fishkill is a hidden gem that offers visitors a unique blend of history, natural beauty, and recreational activities.
