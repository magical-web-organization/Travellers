Introduction to Beacon

Located in the Mid-Hudson and Catskills of New York, Beacon is a charming small town that boasts a rich cultural and industrial history, beautiful natural scenery, and a thriving arts community. With its close proximity to New York City, Beacon is an ideal destination for a day trip, weekend getaway, or even a longer vacation. In this guide, we’ll take a comprehensive look at everything you need to know to plan your trip to Beacon.

List of Places to Visit in Beacon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Dia:Beacon

Art lovers should definitely check out Dia:Beacon, one of the largest contemporary art museums in the country. Housed in a former Nabisco box-printing facility, the museum boasts a massive collection of art from the 1960s to the present day, including works by renowned artists such as Andy Warhol, Dan Flavin, and Richard Serra.

Bannerman Castle

Located on an island in the Hudson River, Bannerman Castle is a unique and intriguing historical attraction. Built by an eccentric Scottish arms dealer in the early 20th century, the castle features turrets, battlements, and other architectural flourishes, as well as stunning views of the river and surrounding mountains.

Mount Beacon

Hiking enthusiasts should definitely check out Mount Beacon, a popular local spot for outdoor recreation. Rising 1,500 feet above sea level, the mountain offers plenty of challenging trails and breathtaking views of the Hudson Valley.

Natural Attractions of Beacon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hudson Highlands State Park

Located just a short drive from Beacon, Hudson Highlands State Park offers over 6,000 acres of rugged terrain, winding trails, and stunning vistas. Visitors can explore the park via foot, bike, or horseback, and enjoy a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, boating, and camping.

Denning’s Point State Park

Denning’s Point State Park is another popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with over 60 acres of woodland, meadows, and waterfront along the Hudson River. The park offers plenty of opportunities for nature walks, bird watching, and other outdoor activities, and is the perfect spot for a picnic or a peaceful stroll.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Beacon

Beacon is a popular destination for hikers and bikers, with plenty of scenic trails and routes to explore. Some top options include:

  • The Beacon Hill Trail
  • The Fishkill Ridge Trail
  • The Dutchess Rail Trail

Best Things to Do in Beacon: Activities and Tours

Hudson River Kayak Adventures

For a unique and exciting way to explore the Hudson River, check out Hudson River Kayak Adventures. The company offers guided tours and rentals of kayaks, canoes, paddleboards, and other watercraft, allowing visitors to explore the river and its surrounding natural beauty up close.

The Roundhouse

Foodies and wine lovers should definitely check out The Roundhouse, a chic and stylish event space and restaurant complex that offers a top-tier dining experience. With a focus on locally-sourced ingredients and artisanal techniques, The Roundhouse is the perfect spot to enjoy a romantic dinner or a night out with friends.

Week-Long Itinerary for Beacon

Day 1 Start your trip with a visit to Dia:Beacon for an introduction to the town’s arts and culture scene.
Day 2 Take a leisurely nature walk in Denning’s Point State Park, and enjoy a picnic lunch by the water.
Day 3 Spend the day exploring the town’s charming shops and boutiques, and sample some of the delicious local food and drink.
Day 4 Take an unforgettable kayaking tour of the Hudson River, and enjoy breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.
Day 5 Hit the trails and hike to the top of Mount Beacon for panoramic views of the Hudson Valley.
Day 6 Take a tour of Bannerman Castle and learn about the fascinating history of this unique attraction.
Day 7 Wrap up your trip with a leisurely bike ride along the Dutchess Rail Trail, and soak up the natural beauty of the Hudson Valley.

Food and Drink Scene of Beacon

Must-Try Dishes

Beacon is home to a vibrant and eclectic food scene, with plenty of wonderful restaurants and cafes to choose from. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Artisanal pizza at Vito’s Pizzeria
  • Tasty breakfast sandwiches and coffee at Bank Square Coffeehouse
  • Rustic Italian cuisine at Trattoria San Giorgio

Local Beverages

Craft beer lovers will definitely want to check out Hudson Valley Brewery, a popular local brewery that offers a rotating selection of small-batch beers. Meanwhile, wine enthusiasts should definitely stop by Glorie Farm Winery, a family-owned operation that produces a variety of delicious wines using locally-sourced ingredients.

Where to Stay in Beacon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Beacon offers plenty of great options when it comes to accommodations. Some top choices include:

  • The Roundhouse: This chic and stylish hotel complex offers luxurious accommodations and top-tier amenities, as well as a great location in the heart of town.
  • The Inn and Spa at Beacon: For a more relaxing and laid-back option, check out the Inn and Spa at Beacon, a charming bed and breakfast that offers comfortable rooms and spa services.
  • Beacon Hermitage: If you’re looking for a unique and secluded experience, consider staying at the Beacon Hermitage, a beautiful and tranquil guesthouse that offers breathtaking views of the Hudson Valley.

What to Buy in Beacon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Beacon is home to plenty of charming and unique shops and boutiques, offering everything from handmade crafts to vintage clothing to locally-produced food and drink. Some top choices include:

  • Hudson Beach Glass: This local glass studio produces a variety of beautiful and unique handmade glassware and gifts.
  • Beacon Bath and Bubble: For locally-made bath and body products, check out Beacon Bath and Bubble, featuring handmade soaps, bath bombs, and more.
  • The Hop: Craft beer and homebrew enthusiasts should definitely stop by The Hop, a local shop that offers a variety of brewing supplies and ingredients.

Local Culture and Customs of Beacon

Beacon is a friendly and welcoming town, known for its vibrant arts community and strong civic spirit. Visitors should also be aware of the town’s strong environmental and conservation ethos, with a focus on sustainability and green living.

Best Time to Visit Beacon

The best times to visit Beacon are during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and comfortable and the town is bustling with events and activities. Summer can be quite hot and crowded, while winter can be very cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Beacon

Do:

  • Explore the town’s vibrant arts and culture scene, with plenty of museums, galleries, and performance spaces to choose from.
  • Take advantage of the town’s beautiful natural scenery, with plenty of opportunities for hiking, biking, kayaking, and more.
  • Sample the delicious local food and drink, with plenty of great restaurants, cafes, and bars to choose from.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the town’s natural resources, like its parks and hiking trails.
  • Don’t be too loud or disruptive, especially in residential areas.
  • Don’t forget to be respectful of the town’s history and heritage, including its important landmarks and historical sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Beacon

Beacon is generally a safe and secure town, but visitors should still exercise caution and take basic safety precautions. Some tips include:

  • Keep your valuables secure and out of sight, especially in crowded or public areas.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and trust your instincts.
  • Follow common sense safety guidelines when hiking, biking, or participating in other outdoor activities, like staying on marked trails and carrying adequate water and supplies.

Health in Beacon

Beacon has several medical facilities in the area that can help with a variety of medical needs and issues. However, it is always recommended that you bring any necessary medications with you, like allergy medicine or prescription drugs.

Getting to Beacon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Beacon is easily accessible by car, train, and bus. Some top options include:

Airports Near Beacon

Beacon is located within driving distance of several major airports, including:

  • John F. Kennedy International Airport (JFK)
  • LaGuardia Airport (LGA)
  • Newark Liberty International Airport (EWR)

Bus Stations Near Beacon

  • Short Line Bus: Offers service from New York City, Upstate New York, and beyond.
  • Coach USA: Offers service from New York City and other nearby destinations.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: Offers regular train service from Grand Central Terminal in New York City, making it easy to reach Beacon by rail.
  • Metro-North Railroad: Offers regular train service from New York City, as well as other nearby destinations.

How to Get Around in Beacon

Beacon is a walkable town, making it easy to explore on foot. Additionally, there are several options for getting around town, including:

  • Taxis and ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft
  • Rental cars and bikes

Where to Go Next from Beacon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Beacon is located in the heart of the beautiful Hudson Valley, making it an ideal base for exploring the many attractions and destinations in the area. Some top options include:

  • The town of Cold Spring, a charming and historic town located just a short drive from Beacon.
  • The nearby Minnewaska State Park Preserve, a beautiful and expansive park with plenty of hiking trails and natural beauty to explore.
  • The city of Poughkeepsie, a larger town that offers a variety of cultural attractions, shopping, and dining opportunities.

Conclusion

With its unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty, Beacon is a fantastic destination for anyone looking to experience the best of upstate New York. Whether you’re interested in hiking, kayaking, or simply relaxing and enjoying the local food and drink scene, Beacon has plenty to offer. So start planning your trip today, and get ready to discover all the wonders of this charming and vibrant town!
