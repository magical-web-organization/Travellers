Introduction to Red Hook

Welcome to Red Hook, a charming town located in Dutchess County in the Mid-Hudson and Catskills region of New York State. Red Hook boasts a rich history, stunning natural scenery, and a vibrant community. Whether you’re looking to explore the town’s cultural and historical attractions, embark on outdoor adventures, or simply relax and unwind, Red Hook has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Red Hook

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Staatsburgh State Historic Site: One of the most popular attractions in Red Hook, Staatsburgh State Historic Site is a grand Gilded Age mansion that offers a glimpse into how wealthy families lived at the turn of the 20th century.
  • Old Rhinebeck Aerodrome Museum: Aviation enthusiasts won’t want to miss the Old Rhinebeck Aerodrome Museum, which showcases vintage airplanes and offers thrilling air shows during the summer months.
  • Bard College: Located just a few miles from Red Hook, Bard College is a prestigious liberal arts institution that boasts beautiful architecture, a world-class art museum, and a thriving arts community.

Natural Attractions of Red Hook: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Poets’ Walk Park: Located along the Hudson River, Poets’ Walk Park is a tranquil nature preserve with stunning views of the Catskill Mountains. The park offers hiking trails, picnic areas, and plenty of spots to relax and enjoy the scenery.
  • Tivoli Bays Wildlife Management Area: Birdwatchers and nature lovers should head to the Tivoli Bays Wildlife Management Area, a picturesque wetland area that is home to a wide variety of bird species.
  • Red Hook Golf Club: Golfers will appreciate the stunning views and challenging course at the Red Hook Golf Club, which is open to the public and offers affordable rates.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Red Hook

Red Hook has plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore, from easy nature walks to challenging mountain climbs. Some popular routes include:

  • Winnakee Nature Preserve: This easy, 4-mile loop trail takes you through scenic forests and meadows.
  • Ferncliff Forest: With over 20 miles of trails, Ferncliff Forest offers something for hikers and bikers of all skill levels.
  • Stissing Mountain: If you’re looking for a challenging hike, Stissing Mountain is a must-visit. The steep trail rewards hikers with stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Red Hook: Activities and Tours

  • Red Hook Winery: Wine enthusiasts won’t want to miss a visit to the Red Hook Winery, which offers tours and tastings of their award-winning wines.
  • Old Rhinebeck Aerodrome Air Shows: In addition to the museum, the Old Rhinebeck Aerodrome also hosts thrilling air shows featuring vintage planes and pilots in period costumes.
  • Bike Tours: Red Hook is home to several bike tour companies that offer guided tours of the town’s scenic routes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Red Hook

Day 1: Visit Staatsburgh State Historic Site and explore the grounds. Day 2: Embark on a hiking or biking adventure at Poets’ Walk Park. Day 3: Take a tour of the charming town of Red Hook, visiting the local shops and restaurants. Day 4: Explore the art and culture of Bard College. Day 5: Spend the day at the Red Hook Golf Club. Day 6: Visit the Old Rhinebeck Aerodrome Museum and take in an air show. Day 7: Take a guided bike tour of Red Hook’s scenic routes.

Food and Drink Scene of Red Hook

Must-Try Dishes

Red Hook is home to several outstanding restaurants that offer delicious dishes made from locally sourced ingredients. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Hudson Valley Duck Breast: A local favorite, the Hudson Valley Duck Breast is a savory dish that pairs perfectly with a glass of red wine.
  • Savory Apple Tart: This delicious pastry is made with fresh apples and savory goat cheese.
  • Pork Belly Sliders: These decadent sliders are made with juicy pork belly and topped with a tangy BBQ sauce.

Local Beverages

Red Hook is also home to several outstanding breweries and wineries that offer tasty beverages made from locally grown ingredients. Some must-try drinks include:

  • Hudson Valley Wine: The wineries in the Hudson Valley produce some of the finest wines in the country.
  • Hudson Valley Beer: Red Hook is home to several craft breweries that offer a wide range of tasty beers.

Where to Stay in Red Hook: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Red Hook offers a variety of lodging options, from cozy bed and breakfasts to upscale hotels. Some popular choices include:

  • The Red Hook Country Inn: This charming bed and breakfast offers cozy rooms and a delicious breakfast.
  • The Rhinecliff: Located just a few miles from Red Hook, The Rhinecliff is a luxurious hotel with stunning river views.
  • The Barn on the Pond: This stylish guesthouse offers a unique stay in a converted barn, complete with a swimming pool and hot tub.

What to Buy in Red Hook: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Red Hook is home to several unique shops that offer locally made products and souvenirs. Some of the best places to shop include:

  • Paper Trail: This charming stationary shop offers a wide range of unique cards, journals, and invitations.
  • Hudson Valley Vintage: For vintage lovers, Hudson Valley Vintage is a must-visit. The shop offers a wide range of vintage clothing and accessories.
  • Tivoli Mercantile: This unique shop offers a wide range of locally made products, including candles, pottery, and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Red Hook

Red Hook is a tight-knit community that values sustainability, local farming, and the arts. Visitors should be respectful of the local customs and try to support the town’s local businesses and artists.

Best Time to Visit Red Hook

The best time to visit Red Hook is during the fall, when the leaves are changing colors and the weather is mild. Spring and summer are also beautiful times to visit, with plenty of outdoor activities and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Red Hook

Do:

  • Do support the town’s local businesses and artists.
  • Do take time to explore the stunning natural scenery of Red Hook.
  • Do sample the town’s delicious local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the town’s natural attractions.
  • Don’t be disrespectful to the town’s residents or local customs.
  • Don’t forget to tip your waiter or bartender at local restaurants and bars.

Safety and Health

Safety in Red Hook

Red Hook is a safe town with a low crime rate. Visitors should take normal precautions to protect themselves and their belongings.

Health in Red Hook

Red Hook has several medical facilities, including urgent care centers and hospitals. Visitors should also remember to bring sunscreen and insect repellent, as ticks and mosquitoes are common in the area.

Getting to Red Hook: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Red Hook

  • Albany International Airport: Located approximately 55 miles from Red Hook, Albany International Airport offers daily flights to several major cities.
  • Stewart International Airport: Located approximately 30 miles from Red Hook, Stewart International Airport offers daily flights to several major cities.

Bus Stations Near Red Hook

  • Trailways: The Red Hook Trailways station is located in the heart of the town and offers bus service to several major cities.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Several car rental companies, including Avis and Hertz, offer service in Red Hook.
  • Taxi and Shuttle Service: Several taxi and shuttle companies offer service in the Red Hook area.

How to Get Around in Red Hook

Red Hook is a small town that is easily walkable. Visitors can also rent a bike or car to explore the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Red Hook: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kingston: Located just a few miles from Red Hook, Kingston offers a charming downtown area and plenty of cultural attractions.
  • Hudson: Just a 30-minute drive from Red Hook, Hudson boasts stunning architecture, trendy shops and restaurants, and fun events throughout the year.
  • Rhinebeck: Another charming town just a few miles from Red Hook, Rhinebeck is home to several outstanding restaurants, shops, and galleries.

Conclusion

Red Hook is a beautiful town full of history, natural beauty, and friendly locals. Whether you’re here to explore the town’s cultural attractions, embark on outdoor adventures, or simply relax and unwind, Red Hook has something for everyone.
