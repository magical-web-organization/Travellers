Introduction to Mid-Hudson and Catskills

Nestled between New York City and Albany, the Mid-Hudson and Catskills region is a beautiful getaway destination that offers a mix of natural scenery, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures.

The region comprises ten counties, including Ulster, Greene, Dutchess, and Columbia, and is well-known for its picturesque landscapes, including the Catskill Mountains and the Hudson River.

This guide will offer an in-depth look at the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and what to eat in Mid-Hudson and Catskills.

List of Places to Visit in Mid-Hudson and Catskills

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Franklin D. Roosevelt Presidential Library and Museum

The Franklin D. Roosevelt Presidential Library and Museum is a must-visit for history buffs. Located in Hyde Park, the museum is home to personal papers, memorabilia, and artifacts from the 32nd President’s life and offers a glimpse into his presidency.

Olana State Historic Site

Olana State Historic Site features a Persian-style mansion that overlooks the stunning Hudson River. The property was inhabited by the famous Hudson River School painter Frederic Edwin Church, and now offers tours, exhibits, and beautiful grounds to explore.

Natural Attractions of Mid-Hudson and Catskills: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Catskill Mountains

The Catskill Mountains offer unparalleled natural beauty and an abundance of outdoor recreational activities. Visitors can hike one of the many trails, go skiing at one of the resorts or take a scenic drive to the popular Kaaterskill Falls.

Walkway Over the Hudson

The Walkway Over the Hudson, located in Poughkeepsie, is the longest pedestrian bridge in the world. Walking, jogging, cycling, and rollerblading are among the many outdoor activities available on the bridge.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mid-Hudson and Catskills

The Mid-Hudson and Catskills region offers an endless array of hiking and biking trails. Some of the most popular include the Devil’s Path, the Hudson Valley Rail Trail, and Overlook Mountain Trail.

Best Things to Do in Mid-Hudson and Catskills: Activities and Tours

Storm King Art Center

The Storm King Art Center, located in Mountainville, boasts of over 100 large-scale sculptures by artists from around the world. Visitors can take a tour and exploring the beautiful grounds, book a petting zoo tour, or enjoy a picnic.

Hudson River Cruises

Hudson River Cruises offer sightseeing tours along the Hudson River with spectacular views of the surrounding landscapes. Visitors can choose either lunch, dinner, or sunset cruise.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mid-Hudson and Catskills

Day 1: Visit the Franklin D. Roosevelt Presidential Library and Museum, followed by a tour of the Vanderbilt Mansion National Historic Site.

Day 2: Explore the Catskill Mountains with a hike up to Indian Head and Sunset Rock.

Day 3: Take a scenic drive to the Kaaterskill Falls and enjoy the stunning views of the waterfalls.

Day 4: Visit the Walkway Over the Hudson and take a stroll while enjoying the sunset over the Hudson River.

Day 5: Take a trip to the Storm King Art Center and explore the breathtaking sculptures that dot the sprawling gardens.

Day 6: Explore local breweries and wineries.

Day 7: Have a picnic at the Olana State Historic Site and enjoy the picturesque surroundings.

Food and Drink Scene of Mid-Hudson and Catskills

Must-Try Dishes

The Mid-Hudson and Catskills region offers a variety of culinary delights. Must-try dishes include:

  • Wood-fired pizza from the Art of New York Style Pizza in Woodstock
  • Trout from Hickory BBQ and Smokehouse in Kingston
  • Gourmet sandwiches from Bread Alone Bakery in Boiceville
  • Glazed Donuts from the Kingston Farmer’s Market

Local Beverages

The Mid-Hudson and Catskills region is renowned for its wine and beer. Local beverages worth a try include:

  • Hudson Valley Beer Trail
  • Brotherhood Winery in Washingtonville
  • Applewood Winery in Warwick
  • Whitecliff Vineyard in Gardener

Where to Stay in Mid-Hudson and Catskills: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Woodstock Way Hotel

Woodstock Way Hotel combines modern amenities and vintage charm. The hotel features luxurious suites, outdoor lounge area, and serves a sumptuous breakfast.

The Roxbury Motel

The Roxbury Motel located in Roxbury, is a trendy hotel featuring unique themed rooms, a heated pool, and a vibrant 1960s-themed cocktail lounge.

Mohonk Mountain House

Mohonk Mountain House, located in New Paltz, is a historic castle-like resort featuring an award-winning spa, wellness programs, and activities such as horseback riding and rock climbing.

What to Buy in Mid-Hudson and Catskills: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Hudson Valley Seed Co.

Hudson Valley Seed Co. offers an array of garden activities and seeds perfect for planting a beautiful garden.

The Good Witch

The Good Witch is a botanical shop in Kingston featuring an array of organic skincare products, houseplants, and various other gift items.

Local Culture and Customs of Mid-Hudson and Catskills

The Mid-Hudson and Catskills Region is an area rich in history and culture. Visitors can see the influence of the Hudson River School on the land and also appreciate the popularity of the Woodstock Music Festival that took place in 1969 in nearby Bethel.

Best Time to Visit Mid-Hudson and Catskills

The best time to visit Mid-Hudson and Catskills is during the fall season when the leaves are changing colors, making it the perfect time for scenic drives and hikes. The summers provide an opportunity to kayak in the Hudson River and visit local streams, waterfalls, and swimming holes.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mid-Hudson and Catskills

Do:

  • Respect the surrounding nature and keep on the marked trails.
  • Visit local restaurants and breweries.
  • Take home a souvenir from Hudson Valley Seed Co.

Don’t:

  • Litter anywhere in the woods or mountains.
  • Disturb the wildlife or touch the plants and wildflowers.
  • Trespass on private property without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mid-Hudson and Catskills

Crime rates in Mid-Hudson and Catskills are relatively low, but visitors are advised to take precautions and practice basic safety measures like locking car doors and keeping valuables out of sight. The mountain trails can be unpredictable, so it’s recommended to tell someone where you’re going and what your estimated return time is.

Health in Mid-Hudson and Catskills

Visitors are advised to check ticks and mosquito bites while traveling in the Mid-Hudson and Catskills region. There are no major health risks, but it’s recommended to carry insect repellent and sunscreen.
