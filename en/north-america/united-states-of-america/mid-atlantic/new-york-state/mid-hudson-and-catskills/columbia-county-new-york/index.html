Introduction to Columbia County (New York)

Nestled in the heart of Mid-Hudson and Catskills, Columbia County (New York) is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and culture aficionados alike. With its scenic views, quaint towns, and welcoming communities, Columbia County offers a unique blend of rural charm and urban sophistication.

List of Places to Visit in Columbia County (New York)

Whether you’re looking to explore the cultural heritage of the region or immerse yourself in the natural beauty of the area, Columbia County has something to offer everyone. Here’s a list of places to visit in Columbia County:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Olana State Historic Site
  • Martin Van Buren National Historic Site
  • Clermont State Historic Site
  • Columbia County Historical Society Museum
  • FASNY Museum of Firefighting

Natural Attractions of Columbia County (New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bash Bish Falls State Park
  • Lake Taghkanic State Park
  • Harlem Valley Rail Trail
  • Taconic State Park
  • Hudson River Skywalk

Hiking/Biking Routes of Columbia County (New York)

Columbia County offers some of the best hiking and biking trails in the region. From easy walking paths to challenging mountain treks, there’s something for everyone. Here are some of our favorite routes:

  • The Harlem Valley Rail Trail
  • The Taconic State Park Trail
  • The Hudson River Skywalk Trail
  • The Dutchess Rail Trail
  • The Columbia Land Conservancy Trails

Best Things to Do in Columbia County (New York): Activities and Tours

From wine tastings to hot air ballooning, Columbia County has plenty of activities and tours to keep you busy. Here are some of the best things to do in Columbia County:

  • Take a hot air balloon ride with the Eastern Balloon Adventures
  • Visit the local breweries and wineries.
  • Explore the local farms.
  • Shop for local crafts and antiques.
  • Attend local festivals and events.

Week-Long Itinerary for Columbia County (New York)

If you’re planning a week-long visit to Columbia County, here’s a sample itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit Olana State Historic Site, the home of Frederic Edwin Church, an American landscape painter who created his own magnificent home.
  • Day 2: Explore Lake Taghkanic State Park and hike to Bash Bish Falls.
  • Day 3: Visit the FASNY Museum of Firefighting and learn about the history of firefighting equipment.
  • Day 4: Take a hot air balloon ride with the Eastern Balloon Adventures and enjoy the sunset views.
  • Day 5: Visit some of the local farms and wineries, and sample the local produce.
  • Day 6: Take part in Hudson River Skywalk tour on the Hudson River.
  • Day 7: Visit Clermont State Historic Site and learn about the history of the Livingston family.

Food and Drink Scene of Columbia County (New York)

Columbia County is renowned for its farm-to-table cuisine, wineries, distilleries, and breweries. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hudson Valley duck, the local specialty.
  • Artisanal cheeses, fresh bread
  • Wood-fired pizza from a local restaurant
  • Freshly-baked pastries, pies, and cakes
  • Locally-sourced meats

Local Beverages

  • Hudson Valley wines
  • Local craft beers
  • Locally-made spirits and gins
  • Ciders

What to Buy in Columbia County (New York): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Columbia County is home to an array of unique boutiques, antique shops, and local artisan markets. Here are some of the best places to buy souvenirs and local products:

  • Olana Museum Store
  • Hudson River Exchange
  • Hudson Opera House Farmer’s Market

Local Culture and Customs of Columbia County (New York)

Columbia County is a melting pot of different cultures and ethnicities. The locals are known for their friendliness, openness and willingness to share their heritage. Respect for local customs, traditions, and beliefs is crucial when you are visiting Columbia County.

Best Time to Visit Columbia County (New York)

The best time to visit Columbia County depends on what you want to do. Every season, however, has something unique to offer. Spring and summer are ideal for outdoor pursuits, and autumn is perfect for foliage viewing and harvest events. Winter brings with it the prospect of skiing, snowshoeing and many indoor pursuits such as concerts and plays.

Do’s and Don’ts of Columbia County (New York)

Here are some things to keep in mind during your visit to Columbia County:

Do:

  • Rely on your personal transport techniques/public transport
  • Wear light layers of clothing in the spring, summer, and autumn
  • Purchase locally-sourced goods and produce
  • Seek permission before taking photographs of private property

Don’t:

  • Leave the garbage out in the open.
  • Approach farm animals or wildlife without permission.
  • Exceed the speed limit or park illegally in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Columbia County (New York)

Columbia County is generally safe for tourists, but like any other place, you should always take precautions. Be aware of your surroundings, keep valuable items close to you, and lock your doors etc. while you drive around.

Health in Columbia County (New York)

Medical facilities are available in some parts of Columbia County. Make sure you bring any necessary prescription medication along with you and wear appropriate protective equipment if you are visiting designated areas.

Getting to Columbia County (New York): Transportation Options and Logistics

Columbia County is well-connected by air, bus, and train services. Here are some options for getting to Columbia County:

Airports Near Columbia County (New York)

The nearest airports to Columbia County are:

  • Albany International Airport (ALB)
  • Stewart International Airport (SWF)
  • Westchester County Airport (HPN)

Bus Stations Near Columbia County (New York)

Here are some bus services to Columbia County:

  • Greyhound Bus Lines
  • Adirondack Trailways

Other Options

Columbia County is easily accessible by car, with good road connections to major highways.

How to Get Around in Columbia County (New York)

Public transport is not well-connected throughout some rural regions in Columbia County. A car rental or taxi services can be used to get around the county, however, some hotels and resorts provide shuttle services also.

Where to Go Next from Columbia County (New York): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions from Columbia County:

  • Albany, the capital of New York State
  • Rhinebeck, Dutchess County
  • Woodstock, Ulster County

Conclusion

Columbia County (New York) is a must-visit destination for anyone who loves nature, history, food, and wine. From scenic trails and breathtaking views to unique cultural experiences and gourmet cuisine, Columbia County has something to offer to everyone.
