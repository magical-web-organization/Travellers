Introduction to Kinderhook

Kinderhook is a charming town located in Columbia County, New York, in the beautiful Mid-Hudson and Catskills regions. Known for its rich cultural and historical background, Kinderhook boasts a diverse range of attractions that are sure to appeal to every kind of traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Kinderhook

If you’re planning on visiting Kinderhook, here are some must-see destinations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Martin Van Buren National Historic Site: A former home of the eighth President of the United States, the Martin Van Buren National Historic Site is a museum that offers visitors a glimpse into the life and times of one of America’s most significant statesmen.
  • The Kinderhook Cemetery: The final resting place of President Martin Van Buren, this historic cemetery is a walking tour destination that features fascinating insights into the life and death of the kinderhook elite.

Natural Attractions of Kinderhook: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Kinderhook Creek: A pristine waterway that runs through Kinderhook, the Kinderhook Creek offers visitors a range of exciting outdoor activities, like fishing, kayaking, and swimming.
  • The Ooms Conservation Area: A sprawling 182-acre nature reserve located in the heart of Kinderhook, the Ooms Conservation Area includes miles of hiking trails, scenic vistas of the Kinderhook Creek, and abundant wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kinderhook

Exploring the natural beauty of Kinderhook is a must-do activity for visitors. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • The Kinderhook Creek Trail: This 6-mile trail runs along the Kinderhook Creek and through picturesque farmland, making it a popular destination for hikers and bikers of all level.
  • The Old Post Road Trail: A 6-mile bike path that follows the historic Old Post Road, this trail offers visitors a glimpse into Kinderhook’s rich past while enjoying the great outdoors.

Best Things to Do in Kinderhook: Activities and Tours

From cultural outings to outdoor adventures, Kinderhook has no shortage of things to see and do. Here are some of the best activities and tours in the area:

  • Kinderhook Walking Tours: These walking tours take visitors on a journey through the town’s rich history, with stops that include the Martin Van Buren National Historic Site, the Kinderhook Cemetery, and other significant landmarks.
  • Kinderhook Wine Trail: If you’re a wine enthusiast, you won’t want to miss the Kinderhook Wine Trail, which showcases some of the best wineries, vineyards, and tasting rooms in the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kinderhook

Day 1: Visit the Martin Van Buren National Historic Site and take a walking tour of the town.

Day 2: Spend the day exploring the Ooms Conservation Area and hiking some of the trails.

Day 3: Take a bike ride along the Old Post Road Trail and enjoy a picnic lunch at one of the scenic stops on the way.

Day 4: Spend the day driving the Kinderhook Wine Trail, stopping at wineries and vineyards along the way.

Day 5: Take a day trip to the nearby town of Hudson for shopping, dining, and cultural outings.

Day 6: Visit some of Kinderhook’s local shops and buy souvenirs to bring home.

Day 7: Round out your trip with a day of relaxation by the Kinderhook Creek.

Food and Drink Scene of Kinderhook

Kinderhook offers a diverse culinary scene that reflects the town’s rural roots. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dutch-style pancakes: Made with buckwheat flour and often served with local maple syrup, Dutch-style pancakes are a staple of Kinderhook’s breakfast scene.
  • Hudson Valley Foie Gras: A local delicacy that’s known worldwide, Hudson Valley Foie Gras is a must-try for anyone who considers themselves a foodie.

Local Beverages

  • Hard Cider: Kinderhook is home to several cideries that produce some of the best hard cider in the country.
  • Craft Beer: The town also has several craft breweries that offer a range of beer styles, from traditional farmhouse ales to hoppy IPAs.

Where to Stay in Kinderhook: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kinderhook offers a variety of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to upscale hotels. Here are a few recommendations:

  • The Vanderbilt House Hotel: Located in the historic village center, the Vanderbilt House Hotel offers guests a luxurious stay in a beautifully restored 18th-century mansion.
  • The Harrier Hill Parkside Cottage: Ideal for families or groups, this spacious guesthouse is located on a 100-acre farm and includes a fully equipped kitchen, outdoor patio, and plenty of room to relax.

What to Buy in Kinderhook: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kinderhook’s local shops offer a range of unique items that make for excellent souvenirs. Some of our favorites include:

  • Handmade pottery: Kinderhook is home to several local craftsmen who produce beautiful handmade pottery that’s perfect for adding to your collection.
  • Maple syrup: A staple of the local food scene, Kinderhook’s maple syrup is some of the best in the country.

Local Culture and Customs of Kinderhook

Kinderhook is known for its rich cultural heritage, as well as its traditions and customs that are still observed today. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and etiquette, such as dressing appropriately for public places and events.

Best Time to Visit Kinderhook

The best time to visit Kinderhook is from May to October when the weather is mild, and the town’s attractions are all open to visitors. Peak season runs from July to August, so plan accordingly for larger crowds and higher prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kinderhook

Do:

  • Take a walking tour of the town to learn about Kinderhook’s rich history.
  • Visit local wineries and breweries to experience the town’s thriving beverage scene.
  • Try some of Kinderhook’s traditional dishes, like Dutch-style pancakes and Hudson Valley Foie Gras.

Don’t:

  • Ignore local customs and traditions.
  • Leave without trying some of the town’s famous maple syrup.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kinderhook

Kinderhook is a relatively safe town, and crimes of any kind are rare. Visitors should take standard precautions like keeping their valuables in a safe place and staying aware of their surroundings.

Health in Kinderhook

Kinderhook has several medical facilities that can provide necessary healthcare services. However, visitors should pack any required medication and travel insurance to ensure they’re prepared for any unforeseen circumstances.

Getting to Kinderhook: Transportation Options and Logistics

  • Stewart International Airport: 90 minutes away
  • Bradley International Airport: 2 hours away

Bus Stations Near Kinderhook

Other Options

How to Get Around in Kinderhook

Driving or cycling are the most popular ways to get around Kinderhook. Visitors who prefer not to drive can use ride-sharing apps like Uber to get around the town, or use local taxi services that offer transportation to nearby destinations.

Where to Go Next from Kinderhook: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kinderhook’s central location makes it an ideal base for exploring other nearby destinations. Some of our top recommendations include:

  • Hudson, NY: Just 10 minutes away from Kinderhook, Hudson is a thriving town known for its funky shops, art galleries, and vibrant cultural scene.
  • Woodstock, NY: About an hour away from Kinderhook, Woodstock is a picturesque town that’s famous for its music festivals and stunning natural scenery.

Conclusion

Kinderhook is a hidden gem that offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. From hiking through the Ooms Conservation Area to tasting some of the town’s famous maple syrup, there’s no shortage of things to see and do in Kinderhook. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your visit and experience all that this charming town has to offer.
