Introduction to Medina (New York)

Located in Orleans County, in the Niagara Frontier region of New York, Medina is a small village with a rich history. The village was incorporated in 1832 and was named after the Arabian city of Medina. Medina is famous for its sandstone buildings and is often referred to as the “Sandstone Mecca”. If you’re planning a trip to this charming village, here’s a comprehensive tour guide to help you make the most of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Medina (New York)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Medina Railroad Museum

Located in the restored 1905 New York Central Freight Depot, the Medina Railroad Museum is one of the largest freight depot museums in the country. The museum showcases a collection of trains, telegraphs, and train-related artifacts.

Medina Sandstone Hall of Fame

The Medina Sandstone Hall of Fame is a tribute to the village’s famous sandstone buildings. The hall features plaques of the most prominent sandstone buildings in Medina, including the Medina Armory, S.M. Stafford House, Medina City Hall, and more.

Cobblestone Society Museum

The Cobblestone Society Museum is a carefully restored complex of buildings that features a cobblestone church, a cobblestone schoolhouse, a brick mansion, and a carriage barn. The museum showcases exhibits on the history of cobblestone construction and the town’s heritage.

Natural Attractions of Medina (New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Glen Iris Falls

Located in Letchworth State Park, Glen Iris Falls is a breathtaking waterfall that drops 70 feet into a gorge. The falls are easily accessible with beautiful hiking trails leading up to them.

Oak Orchard Wildlife Management Area

The Oak Orchard Wildlife Management Area covers 2,269 acres and is a great place to spot migrating birds. The area is home to several species of waterfowl, including ducks and geese, as well as raptors, like bald eagles.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Medina (New York)

Erie Canalway Trail

The Erie Canalway Trail is a 360-mile trail that follows the iconic Erie Canal. The trail passes through Medina, offering beautiful views of the canal and the surrounding countryside.

Medina Railroad Heritage Trail

The Medina Railroad Heritage Trail is a 1.4-mile trail that runs along the old New York Central Railroad line. The trail offers great views of the rail line that once connected the village to the rest of the country.

Best Things to Do in Medina (New York): Activities and Tours

Lockport Cave and Underground Boat Ride

The Lockport Cave and Underground Boat Ride is a unique tour that takes visitors through the underground passageways of the Erie Canal. The tour includes a boat ride through narrow tunnels and a walk through a cave that was used by the Underground Railroad.

Wine Country Tours

Medina is located near the Niagara Wine Trail, which is home to over 20 wineries. Wine Country Tours offers guided tours through the trail, allowing visitors to sample some of the best wines in the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Medina (New York)

Must-Try Dishes

Zambistro

Zambistro is a farm-to-table restaurant that serves seasonal dishes made with local ingredients. The restaurant is known for its Boeuf Bourguignon, which is slow-cooked beef in red wine sauce served with mashed potatoes.

Rudy’s

Rudy’s is a local diner that serves classic American fare. The diner is known for its homemade pies and hand-dipped milkshakes.

Local Beverages

Leonard Oakes Estate Winery

Located in nearby Appleton, Leonard Oakes Estate Winery produces award-winning wines made with locally grown grapes. The winery offers tours and tastings.

810 Meadworks

810 Meadworks is a small-batch meadery that produces honey wine. The meadery offers tours and tastings, and visitors can enjoy a glass of mead on the outdoor patio.

Where to Stay in Medina (New York): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hart House Hotel

Located in the heart of Medina’s Historic District, the Hart House Hotel is a beautifully restored 1860s mansion that offers luxurious accommodations. The hotel features five guest rooms and a delicious breakfast every morning.

Canal View Bed and Breakfast

Canal View Bed and Breakfast is a charming B&B located near the Erie Canalway Trail. The B&B offers comfortable rooms, a beautiful garden, and a homemade breakfast every morning.

What to Buy in Medina (New York): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Medina Railroad Museum Gift Shop

The Medina Railroad Museum Gift Shop offers a variety of souvenirs related to railroads, including t-shirts, coffee mugs, and train whistles.

Studley’s Flower Gardens

Studley’s Flower Gardens is a local flower shop that sells beautiful locally grown flowers and plants. Visitors can also purchase unique gifts, like pottery, candles, and books.

Local Culture and Customs of Medina (New York)

Medina is a small village with a strong sense of community. Visitors should expect warm hospitality and a friendly atmosphere. The village is also proud of its sandstone heritage, and visitors should take the time to appreciate the unique architecture of the buildings.

Best Time to Visit Medina (New York)

The best time to visit Medina is during the summer months, from June to August. The weather is pleasant, and many outdoor activities, like hiking and boating, are available. Fall is also a great time to visit, as the foliage is spectacular.

Do’s and Don’ts of Medina (New York)

Do:

  • Explore the village’s sandstone heritage.
  • Visit local shops and restaurants.
  • Enjoy the natural beauty of the area.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the village’s historic buildings.
  • Litter in the parks and natural areas.
  • Ignore the friendly villagers.

Safety and Health

Safety in Medina (New York)

Medina is a safe village with a low crime rate. Visitors should take the normal precautions, like locking their cars and keeping their valuables safe.

Health in Medina (New York)

Medina has several medical facilities, including a hospital and several clinics. Visitors with medical issues should have no trouble finding the care they need.

Getting to Medina (New York): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Medina (New York)

The nearest airport to Medina is the Buffalo Niagara International Airport, which is located about 35 miles west of Medina. The airport offers flights from major airlines, including Delta, United, and American.

Bus Stations Near Medina (New York)

The nearest bus station to Medina is the Greyhound Bus Station in Batavia, which is located about 25 miles east of Medina. The station offers bus connections to major cities, including New York City and Toronto.

Other Options

Medina is easily accessible by car, and visitors can rent a car at the Buffalo Niagara International Airport or in nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Medina (New York)

Medina is a small village, and many of the attractions, shops, and restaurants are within walking distance. Visitors can also rent a bike or take a taxi to get around town.

Where to Go Next from Medina (New York): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Rochester

Rochester is a vibrant city located about 35 miles east of Medina. The city offers a variety of attractions, including the Strong National Museum of Play, the George Eastman Museum, and the Rochester Museum and Science Center.

Niagara Falls

Niagara Falls is a natural wonder located about 65 miles northwest of Medina. The falls are accessible from both the American and Canadian sides and offer stunning views of the waterfalls.

Conclusion

Medina is a charming village with a rich history and beautiful natural attractions. Whether you’re interested in the sandstone architecture or outdoor adventures, there’s something for everyone in Medina. With this comprehensive tour guide, you’re sure to have an unforgettable visit.
