Introduction to Lewiston (New York)

Located in Niagara County, in the Niagara Frontier region of New York state, Lewiston is a quaint village with a rich history. One of the oldest settlements in Western New York, Lewiston is home to several interesting historical landmarks and natural attractions.

Visitors to Lewiston can immerse themselves in rich culture and history, breathtaking outdoor scenery, and local cuisine that celebrates the village’s roots. This comprehensive travel guide provides everything you need to know to have an unforgettable experience in Lewiston.

List of Places to Visit in Lewiston (New York)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Lewiston Museum: Set in a historic brick house built-in 1810, the museum contains exhibits that detail Lewiston’s rich history.
  • The First Presbyterian Church of Lewiston: A historic church built-in 1824 and listed on the National Register of Historic Places.
  • The Freedom Crossing Monument: A tribute to the Underground Railroad located at the entrance of the Niagara Gorge.

Natural Attractions of Lewiston (New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Niagara River Gorge: A stunning natural wonder with hiking trails that offer breathtaking views of the Niagara River.
  • Artpark: A scenic park with an outdoor amphitheater and hiking trails. Enjoy a picnic and a view of the Niagara River.
  • Whirlpool State Park: A beautiful natural park that offers stunning views of the Niagara River from the Whirlpool observation deck. Take a hike on the trails, enjoy a picnic or go fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lewiston (New York)

  • Robert Moses Parkway Trail: A scenic biking trail that hugs the Niagara Gorge and offers breathtaking views of the river.
  • Devil’s Hole State Park Trail: This hiking trail takes you through the stunning Niagara Gorge and ends at Devil’s Hole State Park.
  • Great Gorge Railway Trail: A popular hiking and biking trail that offers stunning views of the Niagara River and Gorge.

Best Things to Do in Lewiston (New York): Activities and Tours

  • Niagara Wine Trail: Explore the nearby wineries for some delicious wine tasting.
  • Historic Lewiston Jazz Festival: Held annually in August, this festival offers world-class jazz performances and local food stands.
  • Jet Boat Niagara: Take an exciting jet boat ride through the Niagara River Gorge.

Food and Drink Scene of Lewiston (New York)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Beef on Weck: A local favorite, this sandwich is made with roast beef and a kummelweck roll.
  • Buffalo Chicken Wings: Made famous in Buffalo, NY, these hot wings are a must-try.
  • Sponge Candy: A local sweet treat made with sugar, syrup and chocolate.

Local Beverages

  • Local breweries: Try some delicious craft beer at the many breweries located in the area.
  • Niagara Wine Trail: Explore the nearby wineries for some delicious wine tasting.

Where to Stay in Lewiston (New York): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Barton Hill Hotel & Spa: A charming boutique hotel located in the heart of Lewiston.
  • Red Coach Inn: A historic inn located in nearby Niagara Falls, with easy access to Lewiston and other attractions.
  • The Hospitality House: A cozy guesthouse located in Lewiston that offers a homey atmosphere.

What to Buy in Lewiston (New York): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Lewiston Keepsake Ornaments: A perfect souvenir to remember your trip.
  • Silk Scarves from the Silk Trading Company: These locally-made silk scarves are a great keepsake.
  • Locally-made Jellies and Sauces: Take home some delicious locally-made jellies and sauces to enjoy at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Lewiston (New York)

Lewiston celebrates its rich history and cultural heritage. The village is home to several art galleries, historic landmarks, and museums that pay homage to the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Lewiston (New York)

The best time to visit Lewiston is from May to September when the weather is warm and ideal for outdoor activities. The Historic Lewiston Jazz Festival and other outdoor events are held during the summer months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lewiston (New York)

Do:

  • Do explore the Niagara River Gorge and take a hiking or biking trail.
  • Do try the local cuisine, including the famous Beef on Weck sandwich.

Don’t:

  • Don’t miss the Freedom Crossing Monument and other historical landmarks.
  • Don’t miss the Niagara Wine Trail and local breweries.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lewiston (New York)

Lewiston is a safe village with low crime rates. However, visitors should always take precautions when visiting the natural attractions.

Health in Lewiston (New York)

There are several medical facilities available in the nearby area, including hospitals and clinics.

Getting to Lewiston (New York): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lewiston (New York)

  • Buffalo Niagara International Airport (BUF): Located about 30 minutes from Lewiston.

Bus Stations Near Lewiston (New York)

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in nearby Buffalo, NY.
  • Niagara Scenic Bus: Offers bus tours and transportation in the Lewiston area.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car is the best way to explore the area on your own.

How to Get Around in Lewiston (New York)

Taxis and rideshare services are available in the area. However, walking or biking are the best ways to get around and explore the village.

Where to Go Next from Lewiston (New York): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Niagara Falls: Only a few miles from Lewiston, Niagara Falls is a must-see destination.
  • Buffalo, NY: Located about 30 minutes from Lewiston, Buffalo offers a variety of cultural and historic landmarks to explore.

Conclusion

Lewiston is a charming village with a rich history and a variety of outdoor activities to enjoy. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can plan a memorable trip to Lewiston and explore all the natural wonders, local cuisine, and cultural landmarks the village has to offer.
