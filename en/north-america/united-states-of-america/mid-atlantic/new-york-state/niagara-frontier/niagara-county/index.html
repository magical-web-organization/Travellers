Niagara County is a beautiful destination located in upstate New York. Best known for the magnificent Niagara Falls, the county is home to a range of natural scenery, outdoor adventures, cultural and historic sites, and delicious food. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll go over all the information you need to know to plan a trip to Niagara County.

Introduction to Niagara County

Niagara County is located in Niagara Frontier, which is in New York state, in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States of America, in North America. The county is best known for the magnificent Niagara Falls, which draws millions of visitors every year.

But there is much more to Niagara County than just the falls. The county is home to a range of natural scenery, outdoor adventures, cultural and historic sites, and delicious food. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing vacation in nature or a fun-filled adventure with your family, Niagara County is the perfect destination.

List of Places to Visit in Niagara County

There are many places to visit in Niagara County, ranging from cultural and historic sites to natural wonders. Here is a list of must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Old Fort Niagara State Historic Site: This historic site features a fort that played a key role in the American Revolution. Visitors can tour the fort and learn about its history.

  • Castellani Art Museum of Niagara University: This art museum features a range of exhibits ranging from contemporary art to ancient artifacts.

  • Niagara Falls Underground Railroad Heritage Center: This museum educates visitors on the history of the Underground Railroad and its impact on Niagara Falls.

Natural Attractions of Niagara County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Niagara Falls: This stunning natural wonder offers breathtaking views of the falls and surrounding scenery.

  • Whirlpool State Park: This park features hiking trails, a scenic gorge, and access to the Niagara River.

  • Niagara Wine Trail: This scenic drive takes visitors through the Niagara wine region, where they can sample local wines and enjoy the beautiful scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Niagara County

Niagara County is home to a range of hiking and biking trails, offering visitors the chance to explore the beautiful natural scenery. Some must-visit trails include:

  • Niagara Gorge Trailhead Center: This trailhead offers access to a variety of hiking and biking trails, including the Niagara Gorge Rim Trail.

  • Niagara River Greenway: This scenic trail follows the Niagara River, offering beautiful views of the water and surrounding scenery.

  • Devil’s Hole State Park: This park features hiking trails through a scenic gorge, with beautiful views of the Niagara River.

Best Things to Do in Niagara County: Activities and Tours

There are many fun activities and tours to enjoy in Niagara County. Here are some must-do activities:

  • Maid of the Mist Boat Tour: This boat tour takes visitors on a thrilling ride into the mist of Niagara Falls.

  • Cave of the Winds: This tour takes visitors on a walkway behind the Bridal Veil Falls, offering an up-close look at the falls.

  • Helicopter Tour: This tour offers a bird’s-eye view of Niagara Falls and the surrounding scenery.

Week-Long Itinerary for Niagara County

Here is a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Niagara County:

Day 1: Niagara Falls

Spend the day exploring Niagara Falls, with a Maid of the Mist boat tour and Cave of the Winds tour.

Day 2: Historic Sites

Visit Old Fort Niagara State Historic Site and the Niagara Falls Underground Railroad Heritage Center.

Day 3: Niagara Wine Trail

Explore the Niagara wine region, with stops at several wineries for tastings and tours.

Day 4: Whirlpool State Park

Spend the day hiking in Whirlpool State Park and enjoying the scenic gorge.

Day 5: Outdoor Adventures

Take a helicopter tour of Niagara Falls, followed by a bike ride along the Niagara River Greenway.

Day 6: Local Cuisine

Explore the local food scene, with stops at several restaurants and markets to sample regional dishes.

Day 7: Relaxation Day

Spend the day relaxing at a local spa or enjoying one of the many golf courses in the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Niagara County

Niagara County is home to a range of delicious cuisine, with a focus on local and regional ingredients. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Beef on weck: A local sandwich made with roast beef piled high on a kummelweck roll.

  • Pierogi: A traditional Polish dish, often filled with cheese or potatoes.

  • Chicken wings: Buffalo-style chicken wings are a regional favorite, with a variety of sauces to choose from.

Local Beverages

  • Niagara Wine Trail: This scenic drive takes visitors through the Niagara wine region, where they can sample local wines and enjoy the beautiful scenery.

  • Local craft breweries: Niagara County is home to several craft breweries, offering a range of unique beers to try.

  • Local cideries: Hard cider is a popular drink in the area, with several local cideries offering tastings and tours.

Where to Stay in Niagara County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many accommodation options in Niagara County, ranging from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some top options:

  • Seneca Niagara Resort and Casino: This luxurious hotel features a range of amenities, including a spa and multiple restaurants.

  • Red Coach Inn: This historic inn offers charming rooms with great views of the falls.

  • Hostelling International Niagara Falls: This budget-friendly hostel offers dorm-style rooms and private rooms.

What to Buy in Niagara County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Niagara County is known for its local products, including wine, hard cider, and handmade goods. Some top souvenirs to bring home include:

  • Local wine: Niagara County is home to many wineries, offering a range of unique wines to bring home.

  • Hard cider: Local hard cider is a must-try in the area, with several cideries offering tastings and tours.

  • Handmade gifts: Local artisans offer a range of handmade goods, including jewelry, pottery, and more.

Local Culture and Customs of Niagara County

Niagara County is home to a diverse population, with a range of cultural traditions and customs. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, including tipping at restaurants and respecting local wildlife.

Best Time to Visit Niagara County

The best time to visit Niagara County depends on what you’re looking for. The summer months are the busiest, with warm weather and peak tourist season. The fall is a popular time to visit, thanks to the beautiful fall foliage. Winter is the least busy season, but offers beautiful snow-covered scenery and winter sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Niagara County

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities and natural scenery.

  • Try the local cuisine, including the beef on weck and chicken wings.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

  • Disrespect local wildlife.

  • Ignore safety precautions when near waterfalls or other natural attractions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Niagara County

Niagara County is a safe destination for travelers. Visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take precautions when hiking or participating in outdoor activities. Follow all safety guidelines when near waterfalls or other natural attractions.

Health in Niagara County

There are no specific health risks associated with visiting Niagara County. Visitors should use sunscreen and insect repellent when spending time outdoors, and stay hydrated during the summer months.

Getting to Niagara County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Niagara County is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Niagara County

  • Buffalo Niagara International Airport: Located just outside of Buffalo, this airport offers flights from major airlines.

  • Niagara Falls International Airport: Located in Niagara Falls, this airport offers flights from budget airlines.

Bus Stations Near Niagara County

  • Niagara Frontier Transportation Authority: This bus service operates throughout the region, with stops in Niagara County.

  • Greyhound: This national bus service offers stops in Buffalo and Niagara Falls.

Other Options

  • Car rental: A car rental is a great option for exploring the area at your own pace.

  • Taxi or rideshare: Taxis and rideshare services are available throughout the area.

How to Get Around in Niagara County

The easiest way to get around Niagara County is by car. There are several car rental options available, as well as taxis and rideshare services. Biking and hiking are also popular options for exploring the area.

Where to Go Next from Niagara County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursions to consider after visiting Niagara County:

  • Buffalo: Located just a short drive away, Buffalo is a vibrant city with a range of cultural attractions.

  • Finger Lakes: Located just a few hours east, the Finger Lakes region offers beautiful scenery and delicious wine.

  • Toronto: Located just across the border in Canada, Toronto is a vibrant city with a range of cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Niagara County is a beautiful destination with something to offer for everyone. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, or delicious cuisine, there is something for everyone to enjoy in Niagara County. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your trip, and discover all the wonders that this region has to offer.
