Introduction to Lockport

Located in Niagara County in New York, Lockport is a charming city that boasts a rich history and stunning natural scenery. Lockport is home to the storied Erie Canal, which played a vital role in the transportation of goods during the 19th century. Today, visitors can explore the canal and its many locks, along with a variety of other attractions that showcase the area’s cultural and natural heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Lockport

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lockport Locks & Erie Canal Cruises – Take a cruise down the Erie Canal and see the historic locks up close.
  • Lockport Cave and Underground Boat Ride – Explore the historic tunnels and underground aquifer that once fueled the Erie Canal.
  • Niagara County Historical Society – Learn about the history and culture of the Niagara County region, with exhibits featuring Native American artifacts, local art, and more.
  • Kenan Center – The Kenan Center is a stunning cultural center that houses art galleries, a performance theater, and more.

Natural Attractions of Lockport: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lockport Nature Trail – Take a hike or leisurely stroll along the Lockport Nature Trail, which winds through forests and wetlands, along the Erie Canal.
  • Lockport Town Park – This picturesque park is perfect for picnics, recreational activities, and enjoying the great outdoors.
  • Gulf Natural Area – Featuring stunning geological formations, Gulf Natural Area offers hiking trails, fishing opportunities, and breathtaking views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lockport

  • The Erie Canalway Trail is perfect for biking enthusiasts, running from Buffalo to Albany and winding through many of Upstate New York’s most picturesque towns. Take in the stunning scenery of the Erie Canal and its locks along the way.
  • For hiking enthusiasts, the Lockport Nature Trail is an excellent option, offering lush scenery and unique geological formations.

Best Things to Do in Lockport: Activities and Tours

  • Lockport Cave and Underground Boat Ride
  • Lockport Locks & Erie Canal Cruises
  • Visit the Niagara County Historical Society
  • Explore the Kenan Center
  • Hike or bike the Erie Canalway Trail
  • Visit the Gulf Natural Area

Week-Long Itinerary for Lockport

Day 1 - Take a Lockport Locks & Erie Canal Cruise
Day 2 - Visit Lockport Cave and Underground Boat Ride, then walk the Lockport Nature Trail
Day 3 - Explore the Niagara County Historical Society

Food and Drink Scene of Lockport

Must-Try Dishes

  • Beef on Weck – A regional favorite, Beef on Weck is a hearty sandwich made with roast beef, au jus, and a Kaiser roll topped with caraway seeds and coarse salt.
  • Spiedies – a grilled kebab made with marinated meat (usually chicken, lamb or pork), vegetables and spices on a skewer.

Local Beverages

  • Wine – Niagara County is home to a number of excellent wineries, producing both red and white varieties.
  • Beer – The Lockport Brewing Company specializes in small-batch, handcrafted beers, and the Woodcock Brothers Brewery offers a variety of unique brews.

Where to Stay in Lockport: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Lockport Inn and Suites – This comfortable, affordable hotel is located conveniently near many of Lockport’s top attractions.
  • Lake Effect Inn Bed and Breakfast – If you’re looking for a charming bed and breakfast experience, the Lake Effect Inn is a great choice.
  • Victorian Bed and Breakfast – This beautifully restored Victorian house offers comfortable rooms and a warm, welcoming atmosphere.

What to Buy in Lockport: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Niagara County Wine Trail Products – Pick up a bottle of local wine or other handcrafted products from one of the area’s many wineries.
  • Lockport Brewery Souvenirs – Show off your love for Lockport Brewing with a t-shirt or other souvenir.
  • Artisanal Goods at the Kenan Center – The Kenan Center is home to a wide variety of artisanal goods, including handmade jewelry, pottery, and more.

Local Culture and Customs of Lockport

Lockport’s rich cultural heritage is tied to its history as a major transportation hub, with the Erie Canal playing a prominent role in the city’s development. Today, visitors can explore Lockport’s many cultural and historical attractions and get a sense of how the city’s industries have evolved over time.

Best Time to Visit Lockport

The best time to visit Lockport is in the summer, when temperatures are warm and outdoor activities are abundant. However, spring and fall are also great times to visit, as the weather is mild and visitors can enjoy seasonal activities such as apple picking and wine tasting.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lockport

Do:

  • Take a Lockport Locks & Erie Canal Cruise.
  • Visit the Niagara County Historical Society.
  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Explore the area’s natural beauty.

Don’t:

  • Miss out on the Lockport Cave and Underground Boat Ride.
  • Forget to bring comfortable hiking shoes.
  • Skip a visit to the Kenan Center.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lockport

Lockport is a generally safe city, but visitors should take standard safety precautions when traveling, such as being aware of their surroundings and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Lockport

Lockport has a number of excellent medical facilities, including the Eastern Niagara Hospital and a variety of clinics and urgent care centers.

Getting to Lockport: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lockport

  • Buffalo Niagara International Airport is the closest airport to Lockport, located about 30 minutes away by car.
  • Niagara Falls International Airport is also nearby, located less than 20 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Lockport

  • The Niagara Frontier Transportation Authority (NFTA) provides bus service from Buffalo to Lockport and other destinations in the region.

Other Options

  • Car rentals are available at both Buffalo Niagara International Airport and Niagara Falls International Airport.

How to Get Around in Lockport

Lockport is a small, walkable city, and many of its top attractions are located within a few miles of each other. Visitors can also rent a bike or car to explore the area more extensively.

Where to Go Next from Lockport: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Niagara Falls – Located just a short drive away, Niagara Falls is one of the world’s most famous natural wonders.
  • Buffalo – The city of Buffalo is known for its stunning architecture, great food, and lively arts scene.
  • Finger Lakes – The Finger Lakes region is home to some of New York State’s most stunning natural scenery and offers a variety of activities such as hiking, wine tasting, and water recreation.

Conclusion

Lockport is a hidden gem in Upstate New York, offering a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in exploring the area’s historic locks and canals, hiking through lush forests, or simply enjoying some delicious local cuisine, Lockport has something for everyone. Plan your visit today and discover all that this charming city has to offer!
