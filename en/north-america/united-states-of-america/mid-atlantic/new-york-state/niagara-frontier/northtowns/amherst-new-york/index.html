Introduction to Amherst (New York)

Located in Niagara Frontier, Amherst is a charming suburban town in the western part of New York State. If you’re looking for a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of the city, then Amherst can be the perfect destination for you. With a wide range of natural and cultural attractions, Amherst has something to offer for everyone, from history buffs and outdoor enthusiasts to foodies and shopaholics.

List of Places to Visit in Amherst (New York)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Buffalo Niagara Heritage Village: Explore the rich history and culture of Western New York through interactive exhibits, demonstrations, and events.
  • North Forest Park Cemetery: Take a solemn walk through this historic cemetery that has been in use since the 1860s, and pay your respects to notable figures of the area.
  • Amherst Museum: Learn about the town’s fascinating past and see its impressive collection of artifacts, including Native American objects, historic documents, and antique household items.

Natural Attractions of Amherst (New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Isle View Park: Enjoy picturesque views of Lake Erie and catch a glimpse of the Canadian shoreline at this scenic park that also offers hiking trails, playgrounds, and picnic areas.
  • Buckhorn Island State Park: Discover the beauty of the Niagara River’s marshland ecosystem and spot wildlife such as bald eagles, beavers, and herons while kayaking, fishing, or hiking.
  • Reinstein Woods Nature Preserve and Environmental Education Center: Go on a family-friendly adventure through this lush forested area that includes swamps, ponds, and wetlands teeming with plants and animals.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Amherst (New York)

  • Amherst Canalway Trail: Pedal or walk along this 1.5-mile-long multi-use trail that follows the course of the Erie Canal.
  • Ellicott Creek Bike Path: This tranquil trail spans 7.5 miles and meanders through forests, parks, and residential areas, offering an opportunity to unwind and reconnect with nature.
  • Audubon Trail: Explore the diverse ecosystems of the Amherst State Park and get a chance to see various bird species while walking or jogging on this scenic trail.

Best Things to Do in Amherst (New York): Activities and Tours

  • Niagara Wine Trail: Take a guided tour or go on a self-guided adventure along the trail that features over 20 wineries and vineyards offering tastings and special events.
  • Lasertron: Have a fun-filled day playing laser tag, arcade games, and indoor mini-golf at this family entertainment center that also serves food and drinks.
  • Niagara Falls: Take a short 20-minute drive to the world-famous Niagara Falls and witness the natural wonder that attracts millions of visitors each year.

Week-Long Itinerary for Amherst (New York)

Day Activities
1 Visit the Amherst Museum, explore the historic North Forest Park Cemetery, and have a picnic at Island View Park.
2 Head to the Reinstein Woods Nature Preserve and Environmental Education Center, hike the Audubon Trail, and go fishing at Buckhorn Island State Park.
3 Take a tour of the Niagara Wine Trail and taste local wine and food offerings.
4 Have a family-friendly day at Lasertron, and enjoy shopping and dining at the nearby Boulevard Mall.
5 Discover the natural beauty of Letchworth State Park, known as the “Grand Canyon of the East.”
6 Drive to Niagara Falls and marvel at the majestic sight that is one of the world’s natural wonders.
7 Explore the nearby city of Buffalo, known for its architecture, art, and food scene. Visit the Albright-Knox Art Gallery, tour the Frank Lloyd Wright-designed Darwin D. Martin House Complex, and try some of the city’s famous wings.

Food and Drink Scene of Amherst (New York)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Buffalo Wings: Indulge in this legendary dish that originated in Buffalo and is now a staple across the world. Anchor Bar, Duff’s, and Gabriel’s Gate are among the best-known places to try them.
  • Beef on Weck: Sink your teeth into this local specialty made with roast beef and served on a kummelweck roll with horseradish sauce. Charlie the Butcher’s, Schwabl’s, and Bar Bill Tavern are some of the top places to check out.
  • Sponge Candy: Satisfy your sweet tooth with this crunchy confection that’s quite popular in Western New York. Try samples from Watson’s Chocolates, Parkside Candy, and Alethea’s Chocolates.

Local Beverages

  • craft beer: visit Woodcock Brothers Brewing Company, Thin Man Brewery, and Resurgence Brewing Company to taste the local microbreweries’ craft beers.
  • wine: go on a tour of the Niagara Wine Trail and sample wines made from locally grown grapes.

Where to Stay in Amherst (New York): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Embassy Suites by Hilton: This all-suites hotel offers a comfortable stay with an indoor pool, fitness center, and complimentary cooked-to-order breakfast.
  • Motel 6 Buffalo - Amherst: If you’re on a budget, then this budget-friendly option can be an excellent choice with features like free Wi-Fi, outdoor pool, and pet-friendly policies.
  • Lord Amherst Hotel: For a bit of local history and charm, stay at this boutique hotel that has been a part of Amherst’s architecture since the 1960s.

What to Buy in Amherst (New York): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Niagara Falls Memorabilia: From postcards and magnets to T-shirts and mugs, you’ll find a wide range of Niagara Falls-themed souvenirs at gift shops across Amherst.
  • Locally Made Goods: Check out shops like Made in America Store, BFLO Gallery & Gift Shop, and Vidler’s 5 & 10 to buy unique products made by local artisans and craftspeople.

Local Culture and Customs of Amherst (New York)

Amherst is a diverse community with a strong sense of community values, including respect for nature, the past, and each other. The town celebrates its cultural heritage through festivals, events, and organizations like the Polish Heritage Society and the Irish Cultural and Folk Art Association.

Best Time to Visit Amherst (New York)

The best time to visit Amherst is from late spring to early fall when the weather is mild and ideal for outdoor activities. Summer months also bring various events and festivals, such as the Taste of Buffalo, to the area; however, be prepared for crowds and higher accommodation prices during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Amherst (New York)

Do:

  • Try local specialties such as Buffalo wings, beef on weck, and sponge candy.
  • Explore the many outdoor activities, historical sites, and cultural events available.
  • Respect nature and avoid littering when visiting parks and natural areas.
  • Use public transport or carpool to reduce carbon emissions and promote sustainable travel.

Don’t:

  • Speed while driving or running traffic signals, as traffic laws are strictly enforced in the area.
  • Wait until the last minute to book your accommodation or other travel plans, as prices and availability may be limited during peak season.
  • Engage in any activities that harm wildlife or damage natural areas, as this is illegal and can result in hefty fines and penalties.

Safety and Health

Safety in Amherst (New York)

Amherst is a generally safe place to visit, but it’s always wise to take precautions. Some important tips include staying aware of your surroundings, securing your valuables, and following traffic laws while driving or walking. Also, while exploring nature, make sure to stay on marked trails and avoid touching or provoking wildlife.

Health in Amherst (New York)

As with any travel destination, it’s essential to keep yourself healthy and safe when visiting Amherst. Make sure to pack any necessary medications, sunscreen, and insect repellent. Also, stay hydrated and exercise regularly, as the area has plenty of opportunities for outdoor activities and adventures.

Getting to Amherst (New York): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Amherst (New York)

  • Buffalo Niagara International Airport: Located approximately ten miles from Amherst, this airport offers domestic and international flights to over thirty destinations.

Bus Stations Near Amherst (New York)

  • Downtown Buffalo Greyhound Bus Terminal: This bus station serves national and regional carriers with routes to several major destinations.
  • Niagara Frontier Transportation Authority (NFTA) Metro Bus: NFTA offers bus service throughout the Northtowns region, including Amherst.

Other Options

  • Amtrak Train: Departs from Buffalo-Exchange Street Station and travels to destinations all over the Northeast, including Albany, Boston, and New York City.
  • Car Rental: Major car rental companies like Enterprise, Budget, and Hertz operate in the area and can be convenient for exploring the region on your own.

How to Get Around in Amherst (New York)

The best way to get around Amherst and Niagara Frontier is by car. However, there are also bus services provided both by the Niagara Frontier Transportation Authority (NFTA). For a greener alternative, consider renting bikes through services like Reddy Bikeshare to explore the area’s trails and parks.

Where to Go Next from Amherst (New York): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Buffalo: Check out the vibrant food, art, and music scenes and visit landmarks like the Erie Basin Marina and the Buffalo and Erie County Botanical Gardens.
  • Niagara-on-the-Lake: Explore the historic town across the border in Ontario, known for its wineries, theater festivals, and charming architecture.
  • Rochester: Experience the cultural and historical offerings of this vibrant city, such as the historic Susan B. Anthony Museum & House and the Strong National Museum of Play.

Conclusion

Whether you’re seeking an outdoor adventure, a cultural experience, or simply a relaxing getaway, Amherst has a lot to offer. Plan your visit using this comprehensive tour guide, and explore all the charms of Niagara Frontier.
