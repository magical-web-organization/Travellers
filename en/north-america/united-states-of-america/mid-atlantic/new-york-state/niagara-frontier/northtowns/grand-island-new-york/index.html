Introduction to Grand Island (New York)

Grand Island is located in the Niagara River between Buffalo and Niagara Falls, in the Northtowns region of Western New York. Known as the “Grandest Island in the World,” this picturesque island offers a natural retreat with plenty of outdoor adventures and scenic views.

Grand Island is a popular destination for its natural beauty, rich history, and world-class attractions. With its prime location, the island is also a convenient base for exploring the surrounding areas.

List of Places to Visit in Grand Island (New York)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Buffalo and Erie County Naval and Military Park: This museum exhibits historic artifacts, memorabilia, and interactive exhibits dedicated to the United States military. Visitors can tour several decommissioned ships, including the USS Little Rock and USS The Sullivans.
  • Theodore Roosevelt Inaugural National Historic Site: This site commemorates the presidential inauguration of Theodore Roosevelt in the Ansley Wilcox House in 1901. Visitors can learn about the 26th president’s life, work, and legacy.
  • Martin’s Fantasy Island: With over 25 rides and attractions, Martin’s Fantasy Island is Western New York’s premier amusement park. The park features thrilling rides, water slides, and activities for all ages.

Natural Attractions of Grand Island (New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Beaver Island State Park: This beautiful state park offers visitors a range of activities, from swimming, boating, and fishing to hiking, cycling, and picnicking. The park features a sandy beach, playgrounds, picnic areas, and a golf course.
  • Buckhorn Island State Park: This nature reserve is a popular spot for birdwatching and wildlife observation. Visitors can hike or cycle through the trails, fish in the creek, or enjoy a scenic picnic by the waterfront.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Grand Island (New York)

  • Niagara Falls State Park Trail: This scenic trail winds through Niagara Falls State Park and offers stunning views of the Niagara River Gorge and Bridal Veil Falls. The trail is suitable for hiking and cycling.
  • West River Parkway Trail: This picturesque trail runs along the Niagara River and offers excellent views of Fort Niagara and the Canadian skyline. Visitors can enjoy hiking, cycling, or picnicking along the trail.

Best Things to Do in Grand Island (New York): Activities and Tours

  • Niagara Jet Adventures: Visitors can enjoy an exhilarating boat tour of the Niagara River rapids with Niagara Jet Adventures. The tour features Class 5 whitewater rapids and spectacular views of the Niagara Gorge.
  • Niagara Wine Trail: This scenic route winds through the Niagara Lake Plain and offers visitors a chance to sample some of the region’s best wines. The trail features several wineries and tasting rooms, each with its own unique charm and character.

Week-Long Itinerary for Grand Island (New York)

  • Day 1: Visit Beaver Island State Park and enjoy swimming, boating, and fishing.
  • Day 2: Explore the Buffalo and Erie County Naval and Military Park and the Theodore Roosevelt Inaugural National Historic Site.
  • Day 3: Take a boat tour of the Niagara River rapids with Niagara Jet Adventures.
  • Day 4: Hike or cycle along the West River Parkway Trail and enjoy the scenic views.
  • Day 5: Visit Martin’s Fantasy Island and enjoy the amusement park rides.
  • Day 6: Explore Buckhorn Island State Park and enjoy birdwatching and wildlife observation.
  • Day 7: Take the Niagara Wine Trail and sample some of the region’s best wines.

Food and Drink Scene of Grand Island (New York)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Beef on Weck: This regional specialty is made with thinly sliced roast beef and served on a kimmelweck roll with horseradish.
  • Chicken wings: Buffalo-style chicken wings are a favorite throughout the West New York region.

Local Beverages

  • Niagara Wines: The region is famous for its wines, and visitors can sample a range of delicious local wines at several wineries along the Niagara Wine Trail.
  • Craft Beer: Western New York has a thriving craft beer scene, and visitors can sample a range of local brews at several breweries in the area.

Where to Stay in Grand Island (New York): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Byblos Niagara Resort and Spa: This luxury resort offers guests a range of amenities, including an outdoor pool, spa services, and a fitness center.
  • Sunrise Motel: This budget motel offers guests comfortable rooms and easy access to the natural attractions of Grand Island.
  • Grand Island KOA Holiday: This family-friendly campground offers guests a range of amenities, including a playground, pool, and activities for all ages.

What to Buy in Grand Island (New York): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Wine: Visitors can bring home a bottle (or more) of their favorite local wine from the Niagara Wine Trail.
  • Buffalo-themed Souvenirs: Visitors can find a range of Buffalo-themed souvenirs, including T-shirts, mugs, and magnets, at local gift shops and souvenir stores.

Local Culture and Customs of Grand Island (New York)

Grand Island is a friendly and welcoming community, and visitors are encouraged to explore the natural beauty of the region and get to know the local culture and customs. Some local customs and traditions include:

  • Buffalo-style food: Visitors are encouraged to try the local specialties, including Beef on Weck and Buffalo-style chicken wings.
  • Niagara wines: Visitors can sample some of the best wines in the region and learn about the local winemaking traditions.
  • Outdoor adventures: With its natural beauty and array of outdoor activities, Grand Island is an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts and adventure seekers.

Best Time to Visit Grand Island (New York)

The best time to visit Grand Island is during the summer months (June to August), when the weather is warm and sunny and the natural attractions are in full bloom. Visitors can enjoy swimming, boating, and outdoor activities during this time.

Getting to Grand Island (New York): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Grand Island (New York)

  • Buffalo Niagara International Airport (BUF): This airport is located approximately 30 minutes from Grand Island and offers daily flights to major destinations in North America.

Bus Stations Near Grand Island (New York)

  • Niagara Frontier Transportation Authority: This bus company operates several routes throughout the Northtowns region and offers affordable transportation options.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: Visitors can rent a car at Buffalo Niagara International Airport and drive to Grand Island.
  • Taxis and Ride-Sharing Services: Several taxi and ride-sharing services operate in the area and offer convenient transportation options.

How to Get Around in Grand Island (New York)

Visitors can get around Grand Island by car, bike, or foot. The island is relatively small, and most attractions are located within easy driving or cycling distance.

Where to Go Next from Grand Island (New York): Nearby Destinations and Excursions
