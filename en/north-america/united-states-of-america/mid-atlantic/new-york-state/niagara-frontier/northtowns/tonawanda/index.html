Tonawanda is a city situated in the Niagara Frontier region of New York, in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States of America. It is surrounded by picturesque natural landscapes, historic sites, and cultural attractions that make it an ideal destination for travelers. In this comprehensive guide to Tonawanda, we’ll take a closer look at the must-visit places around the city, hiking and biking routes, food and drink scene, where to stay, what to buy, and when to visit, among other topics.

Introduction to Tonawanda

Tonawanda is named after an Iroquois word Tahnawá•teh, which means “confluent stream.” The city is located along the Niagara River, which separates it from Canada’s province of Ontario. The city has a rich history, dating back to pre-colonial times when Native American tribes used the area for fishing and hunting. During the 19th century, Tonawanda became an industrial hub, thanks to its proximity to the Erie Canal and Buffalo, which was a significant port city at the time. Today, Tonawanda is a thriving city with many attractions, including historic sites, cultural institutions, and natural landscapes.

List of Places to Visit in Tonawanda

Tonawanda is home to numerous attractions, including museums, art galleries, parks, trails, and historic sites. Here are some of the must-visit places that you should include on your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Herschell Carrousel Factory Museum: This museum showcases the history of the carrousel industry in Tonawanda. Visitors can view antique carrousels, learn about the mechanics of carrousel-making, and take a ride on a fully operational carrousel.
  • The Niawanda Park: This waterfront park features playgrounds, picnic areas, kayak rentals, fishing piers, and a boat launch.
  • The Richardson Olmsted Campus: This historic complex was once a psychiatric hospital built-in 1870, designed by Henry Hobson Richardson and the landscape architect Frederick Law Olmsted. Now it has been restored and has gained new life as a cultural center with restaurants, lodging, and event space.

Natural Attractions of Tonawanda: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Tonawanda Rails to Trails: These trails are located on the site of an old railway line. Visitors can explore the Tonawanda Creek and Canalway trail system, which features a network of bike and hike trails through varied landscapes.
  • Niagara Falls: Although not located directly in Tonawanda, Niagara Falls is only a short drive away and is one of the most famous natural wonders of the world. Visitors can explore the falls by foot, boat, or helicopter tours.
  • The Isle View Park: This park offers a beautiful view of the Niagara River, with several playgrounds, sports fields, and picnic spots. It also has a boat launch for small boats and Jet Skis.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tonawanda

There are numerous trails and routes for hiking and biking around Tonawanda, ranging from easy nature walks to challenging climbs. Here are some of the most popular:

  • The Niagara River Greenway: This trail is a part of a network of trails that run alongside the Niagara River, starting from Buffalo and ending in Niagara Falls. It is a beautiful route that offers a variety of scenery, including the river views, forests, and parks.
  • The Erie Canalway Trail: This trail runs 365 miles long and passes through Tonawanda. It follows the route of the historic Erie Canal, which was essential in the development of trade and commerce in the state.
  • The Ellicott Creek Trail: This trail travels through the Ellicott Creek Park in Tonawanda and offers several miles of scenic paths through woods, fields, and along the creek.

Best Things to Do in Tonawanda: Activities and Tours

Tonawanda has many activities and tours that visitors can enjoy. Here are some of the best:

  • Kayaking on the Niagara River: Thrill-seekers can rent kayaks and paddle down the Niagara River for a unique adventure.
  • Maid of the Mist Boat Tour: This boat tour takes visitors to the base of the Niagara Falls, providing stunning views of the falls from the water.
  • Buffalo Bike Tours: This company offers guided tours of Buffalo and Tonawanda by bike, providing visitors with an exciting and unique way to experience the city.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tonawanda

Looking for activities to fill out a week-long itinerary? Here is a sample itinerary for you:

  • Day 1: Visit the Herschell Carrousel Factory Museum, followed by lunch at one of the local restaurants.
  • Day 2: Take a boat tour of Niagara Falls, and then explore the Niagara River Greenway trail system.
  • Day 3: Hike the Ellicott Creek Trail in the morning, then visit art galleries in the afternoon.
  • Day 4: Spend the day at the Niawanda Park, with fishing and boating activities, followed by dinner at a local restaurant.
  • Day 5: Visit the Buffalo Zoo, followed by a bike ride along the Erie Canalway Trail.
  • Day 6: Take a tour of the Richardson Olmsted Campus and then enjoy dinner at a local brewery.
  • Day 7: Wrap up your trip by visiting the local farmers market in the morning and then paddle on the Niagara River in the afternoon.

Food and Drink Scene of Tonawanda

The food and drink scene in Tonawanda is as diverse as the city itself. From farm-to-table restaurants to breweries and wineries, there is something for everyone. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Buffalo Wings: These spicy chicken wings are a local favorite, and Tonawanda is the birthplace of the Buffalo-style chicken wing.
  • Beef on Weck: This is a famous sandwich that originated from the Buffalo/Tonawanda area. It is a roast beef sandwich with au jus and horseradish served on a caraway seed bun.

Local Beverages

  • Labatt Beer: This Canadian beer brewed in Buffalo is an essential beverage of Tonawanda.
  • Wine: Niagara is a famous wine region, with several wineries nearby Tonawanda. The Niagara Falls wine region also offers breathtaking views of the Niagara River

Where to Stay in Tonawanda: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tonawanda offers several accommodation options for visitors, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some of the best places to stay in the city:

  • Curtiss Hotel: This 5-star hotel is a luxury boutique hotel in Buffalo, offering top-class service, accommodation, and amenities.
  • Holiday Inn Express: This is a budget-friendly option that provides excellent value for money with comfortable rooms and good amenities.
  • The Delavan Hotel & Spa: This hotel offers spa services, a fitness center, bike hire, and access to beautiful nearby hiking and biking trails.

What to Buy in Tonawanda: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tonawanda is a great place to buy souvenirs and other locally made products. Here are some of the best places to shop and what to buy:

  • Niagara County Produce: This is a farmers market that features a variety of locally grown fruits and vegetables.
  • Fat Bob’s Smokehouse: This is a popular BBQ spot in Tonawanda that also sells sauces and rubs to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Tonawanda

Tonawanda has a rich heritage, which is manifested in its historic sites, cultural institutions, and arts. If you want to experience the local culture and customs, visit the following places:

  • Albright-Knox Art Gallery: This is a contemporary art museum that houses over 8,000 works of art from the 20th and 21st century.
  • Buffalo & Erie County Naval and Military Park: This is an outdoor naval museum that features several historic ships and military aircraft.
  • Chautauqua Institution: This institution is a summer retreat that offers lectures, cultural events, and performing arts festivals.

Best Time to Visit Tonawanda

The best time to visit Tonawanda is from May to October when the weather is comfortable for outdoor activities. Summer is the peak season and hence crowded. Spring and fall are less crowded, and prices are more affordable. Winter is the offseason, but visitors can enjoy snow sports and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tonawanda

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisines and beverages.
  • Do explore the outdoors by hiking, biking, or kayaking.
  • Do take a boat tour of Niagara Falls.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural or historic sites.
  • Don’t ignore local customs or etiquette.
  • Don’t leave food unattended when dining outdoors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tonawanda

Tonawanda is a relatively safe city but like anywhere, visitors should still take precautions. Here are some safety tips:

  • Don’t leave valuables in your car.
  • Avoid walking alone at night
  • Keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas.

Health in Tonawanda

Tonawanda has modern healthcare facilities, and visitors can access medical care if needed. Visitors should have health insurance to cover any medical expenses.

Getting to Tonawanda: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tonawanda is accessible by air, car, and bus. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Tonawanda

  • Buffalo Niagara International Airport: This airport is the closest to Tonawanda and is about 12 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Tonawanda

  • Greyhound Bus Station: The Greyhound bus station is located in Buffalo, which is about 9 miles away.

Other Options

  • Visitors can also take a train to the Buffalo-Depew Amtrak station.
  • Rental cars offer the most flexibility, especially for traveling to nearby sites.

How to Get Around in Tonawanda

Tonawanda has several transportation options for visitors, including:

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car to explore the region.
  • Bike Rental: Visitors can rent a bike to explore the city and nearby trails.
  • Public Transportation: NFTA Metro local bus service is available, but taxi rides can be expensive.

Where to Go Next from Tonawanda: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tonawanda is close to several other destinations that visitors can add to their itineraries. Here are some of the best:

  • Niagara Falls: This is one of the most famous natural wonders in the world. It is located just a short drive away and is worth a visit.
  • Buffalo: This city is only a few miles away from Tonawanda and offers several attractions such as Art Deco architecture, cultural institutions, and Frank Lloyd Wright designs.
  • Letchworth State Park: This state park offers scenic views, hiking trails, and several waterfalls.

Conclusion

Tonawanda is a charming destination that offers something for every type of traveler. From natural landscapes to historic sites to cultural attractions, Tonawanda has it all. This comprehensive guide will help visitors plan their trip and experience the best of what Tonawanda has to offer.
