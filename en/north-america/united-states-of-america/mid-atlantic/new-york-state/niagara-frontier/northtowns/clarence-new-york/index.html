Introduction to Clarence (New York)

Nestled within Niagara Frontier, Clarence (New York) is a charming town that is often overshadowed by its neighboring city of Buffalo. However, this town has plenty to offer visitors looking for a quieter, more relaxed getaway.

Clarence boasts a rich cultural heritage, with a range of attractions and landmarks that offer a glimpse into the town’s past. Alongside this, there is a wealth of natural beauty to discover, with parks and scenic views to explore.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore everything that Clarence has to offer. From must-visit places to cultural customs, this guide will help you make the most of your trip to this charming town.

List of Places to Visit in Clarence (New York)

When it comes to things to do in Clarence, there is no shortage of options. From cultural landmarks to natural attractions, this town is full of surprises. Here are some of the must-visit places in Clarence:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Clarence Historical Society Museum: This museum offers a fascinating insight into the town’s history, showcasing artifacts and exhibits that highlight Clarence’s cultural heritage.

  • Eastern Hills Mall: As one of the largest malls in the region, Eastern Hills Mall is a must-visit for anyone looking to indulge in some retail therapy.

  • Asa Warren House: This historic home is a protected landmark and offers guided tours to visitors looking to learn more about the town’s unique past.

Natural Attractions of Clarence (New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Clarence Town Park: With a range of recreational facilities and stunning views, Clarence Town Park is a great place to spend a lazy afternoon.

  • Hunter Creek Park: This picturesque park is home to a range of wildlife, making it the perfect place for nature lovers to explore.

  • Tillman Road Wildlife Management Area: This protected area contains over 500 acres of land and offers a range of trails and scenic viewpoints for visitors to enjoy.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Clarence (New York)

For those looking for an outdoor adventure, Clarence has plenty of options for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Clarence Pathways: With over 10 miles of paved trails, Clarence Pathways is a popular destination for both hikers and bikers.

  • Tillman Road Wildlife Management Area: As mentioned earlier, this protected area offers a range of trails, perfect for hikers and mountain bikers looking to explore the great outdoors.

  • Great Baehre Conservation Park: This serene park is home to a range of trails that wind through the wooded landscape, offering stunning views along the way.

Best Things to Do in Clarence (New York): Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and exploring the town’s cultural landmarks, there are plenty of other things to do in Clarence. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • Beechwood Stables: This family-friendly attraction offers horseback riding lessons and trail rides for visitors.

  • Spa at Falling Waters: For those looking to relax and unwind, this spa offers a range of treatments to soothe the mind and body.

  • Flying Bison Brewing Company: This brewery offers tours and tastings to visitors looking to sample some of the town’s best craft beer.

Week-Long Itinerary for Clarence (New York)

If you’re planning to spend a week in Clarence, here’s an itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

  • Day 1: Kick off your trip with a visit to the Clarence Historical Society Museum, followed by a leisurely afternoon in Clarence Town Park.

  • Day 2: Explore Hunter Creek Park, then head to the Spa at Falling Waters for a relaxing afternoon.

  • Day 3: Visit the Asa Warren House in the morning, then spend the afternoon at Great Baehre Conservation Park.

  • Day 4: Spend the day at Eastern Hills Mall, indulging in some shopping and taking in the sites.

  • Day 5: Get in touch with nature at Tillman Road Wildlife Management Area, then head to Flying Bison Brewing Company for some beer tasting.

  • Day 6: Take a horseback riding lesson at Beechwood Stables, then head to Clarence Pathways for an afternoon of hiking or biking.

  • Day 7: Spend a lazy day exploring the town’s cafes and restaurants, sampling some of the local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Clarence (New York)

As with any destination, the food and drink scene in Clarence plays a big role in its appeal. Here’s what you need to know:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Buffalo Wings: This regional dish is a must-try when visiting Clarence. Head to Duff’s Famous Wings for some of the best wings in the area.

  • Beef on Weck: Another regional specialty, beef on weck features thinly sliced roast beef on a kummelweck roll. Try it at Schwabl’s Restaurant, a favorite with locals.

  • Apple Cider Donuts: Clarence is known for its apple orchards, and apple cider donuts are a must-try when in town. Head to Blackman Homestead Farm for some of the best around.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: With a range of breweries and taprooms in the area, Clarence is a great destination for craft beer enthusiasts. Flying Bison Brewing Company and West Shore Brewing Company are two popular options.

  • Wine: While Clarence is not known for wine, nearby Niagara Wine Trail offers a range of vineyards and wineries to explore.

Where to Stay in Clarence (New York): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

As a smaller town, Clarence does not have a wide range of accommodation options. However, there are some great places to stay that offer a comfortable, homey atmosphere. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Asa Ransom House: This historic inn offers elegant rooms and a range of amenities, including a restaurant and afternoon tea service.

  • Clarence Inn: This budget-friendly option offers basic rooms and a convenient location.

  • Vintage Gardens Bed and Breakfast: This cozy bed and breakfast offers a range of themed rooms and a hearty breakfast each morning.

What to Buy in Clarence (New York): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to souvenirs and local products, Clarence has a few options to choose from:

  • Apple Cider: While you’re enjoying the apple cider donuts, be sure to pick up some fresh apple cider to bring home with you.

  • Maple Syrup: Another local specialty, maple syrup is a great option if you’re looking for a sweet souvenir.

  • Handmade Crafts: Check out Clarence’s local farmers’ markets for handmade crafts and artisanal products.

Local Culture and Customs of Clarence (New York)

Clarence is a town with a strong sense of community and pride in its heritage. When visiting, be sure to respect the town’s customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Clarence (New York)

The best time to visit Clarence is during the fall, when the leaves are changing and the apple orchards are in full swing. However, the town is a great destination year-round, with plenty to offer in every season.

When traveling to Clarence, it’s important to keep both safety and health in mind.

While there are no major health concerns in Clarence, it’s important to ensure that you have up-to-date vaccinations and take necessary precautions to safeguard your health during your trip.

Getting to Clarence (New York): Transportation Options and Logistics

Here’s everything you need to know about getting to Clarence:

  • Airports Near Clarence (New York): Buffalo Niagara International Airport is the closest major airport to Clarence.

  • Bus Stations Near Clarence (New York): Greyhound and Trailways both offer bus service to nearby Buffalo.

  • Other Options: Renting a car or taking a taxi may be the best option for getting around Clarence.

How to Get Around in Clarence (New York)

As a smaller town, getting around Clarence is relatively easy. Renting a car is the most convenient option, but biking and hiking are also great ways to explore the town.

Where to Go Next from Clarence (New York): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

When you’re ready to explore beyond Clarence, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Buffalo: Just a short drive away, Buffalo offers a range of attractions, including the Albright-Knox Art Gallery and the Buffalo Zoo.

  • Niagara Falls: One of the most famous natural wonders in the world, Niagara Falls is just a short drive from Clarence.
