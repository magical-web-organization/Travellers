Introduction to East Aurora

East Aurora is a charming village located in the Southtowns of Western New York. It’s nestled in the rolling hills of the Niagara Frontier region and is a perfect blend of rural and urban living. With a rich history, breathtaking natural beauty, and cultural attractions, East Aurora is an ideal destination for those looking for a unique and authentic travel experience.

List of Places to Visit in East Aurora

Here are some of the best places to visit in East Aurora:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Roycroft Campus: A National Historic Landmark, the Roycroft Campus is home to a group of artisans and craftspeople who produce and sell unique art and crafts.
  • Elbert Hubbard Roycroft Museum: A museum dedicated to the life and works of Elbert Hubbard, founder of the Roycroft campus.
  • East Aurora Historical Society and Museum: Displays artifacts and memorabilia related to the history of East Aurora and its surrounding areas.

Natural Attractions of East Aurora: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Knox Farm State Park: A 633-acre park that offers hiking, biking, fishing, and horseback riding trails, and more.
  • Hunter’s Creek Park: A 215-acre park that offers hiking, picnic, and cross-country skiing trails, and more.
  • Emery Park: A 280-acre park that offers hiking, picnic, and playground facilities, and more.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East Aurora

  • Roycroft Trail: A four-mile trail that runs through the village and passes through several historic landmarks and natural attractions.
  • Hunters Creek Trail: A five-mile trail that winds through Hunters Creek Park and features scenic views of the surrounding hills and valleys.

Best Things to Do in East Aurora: Activities and Tours

  • Take a hot air balloon ride over the village and enjoy breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Visit the Vidler’s 5 & 10, the largest variety store in the world, and shop for unique gifts, toys, and household items.
  • Attend the annual East Aurora Music Fest and enjoy live music from local and national artists.

Week-Long Itinerary for East Aurora

Food and Drink Scene of East Aurora

East Aurora has a thriving food and drink scene that offers a variety of options for foodies and casual diners alike.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Beef on Weck: A local specialty, beef on weck is a roast beef sandwich served with horseradish on a kummelweck roll.
  • Fish Fry: A popular dish during Lent, fish fry is a battered and fried fish served with fries and coleslaw.
  • Spiedies: A marinated and grilled meat skewer typically made of pork, chicken or lamb.

Local Beverages

  • Big Ditch Brewing Company: A craft brewery that offers a wide selection of beers and tours of the brewing process.
  • 42 North Brewing Company: A craft brewery that offers farm-to-table beers and tours of the brewing process.
  • Mazza Chautauqua Cellars / Five & 20 Spirits & Brewing: A winery and distillery that offers wine tasting, beer sampling, and tours.

Where to Stay in East Aurora: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Roycroft Inn: A historic hotel that dates back to 1905 and features upscale amenities and dining options.
  • Hampton Inn East Aurora: A modern hotel that offers comfortable rooms and a complimentary breakfast.
  • Aurora Inn: A cozy bed and breakfast that features spacious guest rooms and homemade breakfasts.

What to Buy in East Aurora: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Roycroft Art and Crafts: handmade jewelry, pottery, metalwork, woodworking, and textiles.
  • East Aurora Village Market: pottery, glassware, hand-woven baskets, and woolen items.
  • Vidler’s 5 & 10 Store: unique gifts and souvenirs, retro toys, and household items.

Local Culture and Customs of East Aurora

East Aurora is a welcoming and friendly village that takes pride in its rich cultural heritage. Visitors are expected to respect the local customs, including:

  • Respect for historic landmarks and cultural attractions.
  • Enjoyment of local cuisine and customs.
  • Consideration for local businesses and their services.

Best Time to Visit East Aurora

The best time to visit East Aurora is during the summer months, from June to August when the weather is warm and sunny, and many outdoor activities are available.

Do’s and Don’ts of East Aurora

Do:

  • Do visit the Roycroft Campus, the Elbert Hubbard Roycroft Museum and Vidler’s 5 & 10.
  • Do take a hot air balloon ride and attend the East Aurora Music Fest.
  • Do respect the local customs and cultural heritage of East Aurora residents.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or vandalize historic sites or natural attractions.
  • Don’t harass or disturb local wildlife in natural parks.
  • Don’t break any local laws or regulations, including Public Safety Codes and Ordinances.

Safety and Health

Safety in East Aurora

East Aurora is a relatively safe village, but visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety, including:

  • Use common sense when walking or biking in the village or natural parks.
  • Protect your belongings, including valuables and travel documents.
  • Stay informed of weather conditions and prepare for severe weather when necessary.

Health in East Aurora

East Aurora has a well-established healthcare system, and visitors will find a range of medical facilities and services in the local area.

Getting to East Aurora: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can reach East Aurora by plane, train, bus, or car.

Airports Near East Aurora

  • Buffalo Niagara International Airport (BUF): located 20 miles northwest of East Aurora, offers domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near East Aurora

  • Eastern Hills Mall Bus Terminal: located 5 miles northeast of East Aurora, offers local and regional bus services.

Other Options

  • Amtrak: A train station located in Buffalo, 15 miles northwest of East Aurora.
  • Car Rentals: A car rental is available at the Buffalo Airport or through local companies.

How to Get Around in East Aurora

Visitors can get around East Aurora by car, bike, or on foot. Car rentals are available through local companies, and bike rentals are available in the village.

Where to Go Next from East Aurora: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Buffalo: A bustling city located 16 miles northwest of East Aurora that offers a range of cultural attractions, museums, and restaurants.
  • Niagara Falls: A world-famous landmark located 30 miles northeast of East Aurora.
  • Chautauqua-Allegheny Region: A picturesque region located 90 miles southwest of East Aurora that offers scenic beauty, cultural landmarks, and outdoor adventures.

Conclusion

East Aurora is a hidden gem in New York State’s Mid-Atlantic region that offers visitors a unique and authentic travel experience. From historic landmarks and cultural attractions to outdoor adventures and a thriving food and drink scene, East Aurora has something to offer for everyone. So pack your bags, and head on to East Aurora for a vacation of a lifetime.
