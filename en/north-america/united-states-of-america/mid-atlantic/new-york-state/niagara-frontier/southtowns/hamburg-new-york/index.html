Introduction to Hamburg (New York)

Hamburg (New York) is a town located in the Southtowns region of the Niagara Frontier, New York State, USA. Hamburg is situated just outside of Buffalo, NY with a population of approximately 57,000 people. The town is known for its rich history, natural beauty, and charming small-town atmosphere. Visitors can explore a variety of attractions, including historical properties, scenic parks, and hiking and biking trails. This comprehensive tour guide will introduce you to the best places to visit, things to do, and where to eat and stay in Hamburg.

List of Places to Visit in Hamburg (New York)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Hamburg Historical Society Museum

The Hamburg Historical Society Museum is an excellent place to start your visit to Hamburg. The museum displays historic artifacts and photographs, and visitors can learn about the town’s rich history through interactive exhibits.

The Hamburg Palace Theater

The Hamburg Palace Theater is a beloved cultural landmark in the heart of Hamburg. Originally opened in the 1920s as a vaudeville theater, the palace now showcases movies and live performances.

Penn Dixie Fossil Park and Nature Reserve

Penn Dixie Fossil Park and Nature Reserve is a popular destination for paleontologists and nature enthusiasts. The park features over 10 acres of fossils, including trilobites and cephalopods. Visitors can also enjoy guided tours and nature walks.

Natural Attractions of Hamburg (New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lackawanna State Park

Lackawanna State Park is a picturesque park just a short drive from Hamburg. The park includes a lake, hiking trails, picnic areas, and camping facilities. Visitors can enjoy fishing, boating, and swimming, or simply enjoy the beautiful scenery.

Hamburg Beach

Hamburg Beach is located on the shores of Lake Erie and offers over a half-mile of beachfront. Visitors can enjoy swimming, sunbathing, and picnicking. The beach also features playgrounds, volleyball courts, and concessions.

Niagara Falls State Park

Niagara Falls State Park is just a short drive from Hamburg and is one of the most famous natural attractions in the world. Visitors can take boat tours, hike around the falls, or simply enjoy the breathtaking views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hamburg (New York)

Penn Dixie Fossil Park and Nature Reserve Trails

The Penn Dixie Fossil Park and Nature Reserve Trails offer visitors a chance to explore the park’s geological wonders. The trails are open year-round, and visitors can enjoy guided tours or hike at their leisure.

Eighteen Mile Creek Trail

The Eighteen Mile Creek Trail is a popular destination for hikers and bikers. The trail winds through wooded areas and alongside the creek, offering great views of the surrounding countryside.

Chestnut Ridge Park Trail

Chestnut Ridge Park Trail is located in nearby Orchard Park and is a popular destination for hikers and bikers. The trail offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and countryside and is open year-round.

Best Things to Do in Hamburg (New York): Activities and Tours

Canalside Buffalo

Canalside Buffalo is a popular destination for visitors to the region. The area features a variety of activities and events, including concerts, festivals, and ice-skating in the winter.

Hamburg Farmers Market

The Hamburg Farmers Market is held every Saturday during the summer months and is a great place to buy local produce, meats, and other specialty foods. Visitors can also enjoy live music and other entertainment.

Hamburg Gaming and Buffalo Raceway

Hamburg Gaming and Buffalo Raceway is a great destination for visitors who enjoy gambling and horse racing. The casino features over 900 slot machines and several table games, while the racetrack hosts live racing from January to July.

Food and Drink Scene of Hamburg (New York)

Must-Try Dishes

Buffalo Wings

Buffalo Wings are a beloved dish throughout the region, and Hamburg is no exception. The dish consists of crispy chicken wings coated in a spicy, tangy sauce.

Beef on Weck

Beef on Weck is a local specialty consisting of thinly sliced roast beef served on a kummelweck roll, topped with horseradish sauce and served au jus.

Sponge Candy

Sponge Candy is a delectable candy made from sugar, corn syrup, and baking soda. The candy is light and airy, with a crispy texture.

Local Beverages

Loganberry

Loganberry is a fruity, non-alcoholic beverage that is popular throughout Western New York. The drink is made from loganberries, which are a cross between raspberries and blackberries.

Labatt Blue

Labatt Blue is a popular Canadian beer that is widely available throughout Hamburg and the surrounding area.

Where to Stay in Hamburg (New York): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Red Roof Inn Buffalo Hamburg

The Red Roof Inn Buffalo Hamburg is a budget-friendly hotel located near the Thruway and other local attractions. The hotel offers free Wi-Fi, parking, and continental breakfast.

The Village Inn

The Village Inn is a charming bed and breakfast located in the heart of Hamburg. The inn features cozy rooms and a complimentary breakfast served in a lovely dining room.

Local Culture and Customs of Hamburg (New York)

Hamburg is known for its friendly residents and welcoming atmosphere. Visitors to the region should be prepared for cold winters and hot summers, and should always be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Hamburg (New York)

The best time to visit Hamburg is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and there are plenty of outdoor festivals and activities to enjoy. However, visitors can find things to do in Hamburg year-round.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hamburg (New York)

Hamburg is a relatively safe town, but visitors should always take precautions while traveling. Always be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Hamburg (New York)

Hamburg has several hospitals and medical clinics in the area, and visitors can easily find medical services if needed. Visitors should also be aware of ticks and take precautions against Lyme disease if hiking or spending time outdoors.

Getting to Hamburg (New York): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hamburg (New York)

The nearest airport to Hamburg is Buffalo Niagara International Airport (BUF). The airport has several rental car options, as well as public transportation options to get to Hamburg.

Bus Stations Near Hamburg (New York)

The nearest bus stations to Hamburg are in Buffalo, NY. Visitors can take a bus from New York City to Buffalo and then connect to Hamburg.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Hamburg via the New York State Thruway or take a train to Buffalo, NY, and then connect to local transportation to Hamburg.

How to Get Around in Hamburg (New York)

Hamburg is a relatively small town, and visitors can easily get around on foot or by bicycle. Rental cars are also available for visitors who want to explore the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Hamburg (New York): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Niagara Falls, NY

Niagara Falls, NY is just a short drive from Hamburg and is one of the most famous natural attractions in the world. Visitors can take boat tours, hike around the falls, or simply enjoy the breathtaking views.

Buffalo, NY

Buffalo, NY is just a short drive from Hamburg and offers a variety of cultural attractions, including museums, galleries, and theaters.

Conclusion

Hamburg (New York) is a small town with a lot to offer visitors. From natural attractions to historical landmarks, there is something for everyone in this charming town. Visitors should be sure to try local foods and beverages and take the time to explore the town’s rich history and welcoming culture.
