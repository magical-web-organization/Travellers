Introduction to Lackawanna

Located in Southtowns, in Niagara Frontier, in New York (state), in Mid-Atlantic, in the United States of America, Lackawanna is a quiet and beautiful suburb of Buffalo. Named after the Lackawanna Steel Company, this city has undergone significant transformation in the last couple of decades. From cultural attractions to natural wonders, Lackawanna has a lot to offer to visitors.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we have compiled a list of must-visit places and activities to make your trip to Lackawanna a memorable one.

List of Places to Visit in Lackawanna

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Our Lady of Victory National Shrine and Basilica: The shrine is a popular pilgrimage site and a National Historic Landmark. It boasts awe-inspiring architecture and impressive ceiling mosaics.

  2. Steel Plant Museum of Western New York: Experience the city’s industrial heritage at this museum through interactive exhibits and artifacts.

  3. Lackawanna Train Station: A former train station, now a historical landmark with an art gallery, gift shop, and visitor center.

Natural Attractions of Lackawanna: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Tifft Nature Preserve: This 264-acre nature preserve boasts extensive trails and opportunities for birdwatching.

  2. Franklin Gulf Park: A perfect spot for a picnic, hiking, and biking with scenic views of the gorge and creek.

  3. Eternal Flame Falls: An incredibly unique natural phenomenon where a tiny flame burns behind a waterfall.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lackawanna

Lackawanna has several hiking and biking trails to explore the natural beauty of the region. These include:

  1. The Greenbelt Trail: A 37-mile trail that encircles the city of Buffalo and runs through Lackawanna.

  2. Shale Creek Preserve: The preserve has three miles of trails spread across 50 acres, perfect for a light and scenic hike.

  3. Seufert Falls Trail: A half-mile trail leading to stunning Seufert Falls with views of natural wildlife and flora.

Best Things to Do in Lackawanna: Activities and Tours

  1. Explore downtown Lackawanna: Take a stroll through the quaint streets filled with historic landmarks, local shops, and restaurants.

  2. Kayak on Lake Erie: Experience the stunning views of Lake Erie by renting a kayak and taking a tour.

  3. Take a ghost tour: Buffalo and Lackawanna are known for their haunted history, so why not take a ghost tour to learn about the spooky stories and see haunted buildings?

Week-Long Itinerary for Lackawanna

Day 1: Visit Our Lady of Victory National Shrine and Basilica, explore the historical Lackawanna Train Station area and stroll down Ridge Road.

Day 2: Head over to Franklin Gulf Park for a picnic and hike around Tifft Nature Preserve.

Day 3: Take a biking tour through the Greenbelt Trail.

Day 4: Walk the Seufert Falls Trail and explore the Steel Plant Museum of Western New York.

Day 5: Visit Eternal Flame Falls and hike the nearby trails.

Day 6: Take a ghost tour around Buffalo.

Day 7: Spend the day kayaking on Lake Erie and visiting downtown shops and restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Lackawanna

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Buffalo Chicken Wings: This is the quintessential dish of the region, and many restaurants serve their unique take on the classic.

  2. Beef on Weck: A beef sandwich served on a ‘Kummelweck’ roll with caraway seeds and coarse salt; this sandwich is also typical of the area.

  3. Sponge Candy: A chocolate-coated, crunchy candy that is uniquely found in this area.

Local Beverages

  1. Craft Beer: Buffalo and neighboring towns are known for their craft beer scene, and Lackawanna is no exception. Many local breweries offer different styles of beer.

  2. Wine: The nearby Niagara region is famous for producing great wine, which is available at many bars and restaurants.

Where to Stay in Lackawanna: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lackawanna has several options for accommodation, ranging from budget-friendly to luxurious. Some of the popular choices include:

  1. Best Western Plus - The hotel features excellent facilities including free breakfast, a fitness center and an indoor pool.

  2. Comfort Inn & Suites: Affordable and centrally-located, the hotel offers comfortable rooms and free amenities such as breakfast, Wi-Fi, and parking.

  3. Lackawanna Guest House: A beautiful 1901-built guest house with a friendly and accommodating staff, high-end amenities, and immaculate rooms.

What to Buy in Lackawanna: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Sponge Candy: This local delicacy is the perfect souvenir to bring home for loved ones.

  2. Buffalo-themed merchandise: From sports apparel to buffalo-shaped chocolate, there are numerous souvenir options with Buffalo icons.

Local Culture and Customs of Lackawanna

Lackawanna is known for its friendly and welcoming locals who frequently engage with visitors, so don’t be shy to join in on conversations. The town also has a significant Catholic community, with many churches and religious events throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Lackawanna

The best time to visit Lackawanna is in the summer months between June and August, when the weather is pleasant, and outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lackawanna

Do:

  1. Experience the local food and drink scene.

  2. Respect the religious and cultural events of the area.

  3. Take advantage of hiking and biking trails.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or damage the natural beauty of the region.

  2. Be disrespectful to locals or their customs.

  3. Ignore safety precautions while participating in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lackawanna

Lackawanna is generally a safe destination for travelers. Visitors should use common sense and precautionary measures while exploring the area and avoid traveling alone at night.

Health in Lackawanna

Medical facilities are easily accessible in Buffalo, just a few miles away from Lackawanna. Visitors should have travel insurance and be sure to take all necessary prescribed medications.

Getting to Lackawanna: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lackawanna is easily accessible via several modes of transportation.

Airports Near Lackawanna

The nearest airport is Buffalo Niagara International Airport, which is approx. 10 miles from the city center. The airport has good connectivity to various nearby cities and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Lackawanna

The Greyhound bus station is located in the city of Buffalo, a few miles from Lackawanna. From there, visitors can take a local bus to Lackawanna.

Other Options

Visitors can rent a car from the airport or take a taxi.

How to Get Around in Lackawanna

The best way to get around Lackawanna is by car or bike. Visitors can also take local buses to get around the city and to nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Lackawanna: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Niagara Falls: One of the most popular attractions in North America and just a short drive from Lackawanna.

  2. Buffalo: Known for its architecture, art galleries, and food scene, Buffalo is a lively city.

Conclusion

Lackawanna is a perfect destination for travelers looking to explore the beautiful natural scenery and rich cultural history of the region. This comprehensive guide provides ample options for activities, attractions, and local cuisine, making it the ultimate travel companion for those visiting the city.
