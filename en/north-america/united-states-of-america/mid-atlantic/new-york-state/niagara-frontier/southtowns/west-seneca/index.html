Introduction to West Seneca

Located in the Southtowns region of Niagara Frontier, West Seneca is a charming town that has something for everyone. With its rich history, natural beauty, and diverse range of attractions, it is no wonder that it has become a popular tourist destination in Western New York. In this travel guide, we provide a comprehensive tour of West Seneca, from places to visit to things to do to where to stay.

List of Places to Visit in West Seneca

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

West Seneca Historical Society Museum

If you are interested in West Seneca’s history, visiting the West Seneca Historical Society Museum is a must-do. Located in the center of town, the museum showcases the town’s development from a small farming community to a bustling industrial town.

Charles E. Burchfield Nature & Art Center

For art lovers, the Charles E. Burchfield Nature & Art Center is a great place to visit. The center features exhibitions of art, artifacts, and natural specimens that explore the relationship between art and nature.

Ebenezer Railcar

If you are a railroad enthusiast, you will not want to miss the opportunity to see the Ebenezer Railcar. This unique attraction dates back to the early 1900s and is a beautifully restored train car that offers visitors a glimpse into the past.

Natural Attractions of West Seneca: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Cazenovia Park

Cazenovia Park offers visitors a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, and fishing. The park is also home to a large pond that is popular for ice skating in the winter.

Erie County Botanical Gardens

Located just outside of West Seneca, the Erie County Botanical Gardens are a must-see for anyone who appreciates nature’s beauty. The gardens feature a wide range of plants and flowers, as well as guided tours and educational programs.

Tifft Nature Preserve

For a more immersive nature experience, head to Tifft Nature Preserve. The preserve features over 5 miles of nature trails, as well as a floating boardwalk that offers stunning views of the surrounding wetlands.

Hiking/Biking Routes of West Seneca

With its many parks and nature preserves, West Seneca offers ample opportunities for hiking and biking. Some popular routes include the trails at Cazenovia Park and the nature trails at Tifft Nature Preserve.

Best Things to Do in West Seneca: Activities and Tours

Adventure Calls Outfitters

For the adventurous traveler, Adventure Calls Outfitters offers a range of activities, including river rafting, kayaking, and guided hikes.

Jamison Road Conservation Area

If you are interested in conservation efforts, visiting the Jamison Road Conservation Area is a must. This beautiful area features a range of activities, including birdwatching and hiking, and provides educational opportunities for visitors to learn about local conservation efforts.

Week-Long Itinerary for West Seneca

Day 1: Visit the West Seneca Historical Society Museum and take a stroll through Cazenovia Park. Day 2: Explore the Charles E. Burchfield Nature & Art Center and have a picnic at the Jamison Road Conservation Area. Day 3: Take a guided hike with Adventure Calls Outfitters and relax at Cazenovia Park. Day 4: Visit the Ebenezer Railcar and go fishing in the pond at Cazenovia Park. Day 5: Take a guided tour of the Erie County Botanical Gardens and relax at Tifft Nature Preserve. Day 6: Rent a bike and explore the natural trails at Tifft Nature Preserve. Day 7: Visit any attractions you missed and enjoy a local meal at one of West Seneca’s many restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of West Seneca

Must-Try Dishes

No trip to West Seneca would be complete without trying some of the local cuisine. Some must-try dishes include chicken wings (a Buffalo specialty), beef on weck (a roast beef sandwich on a kummelweck roll), and sponge candy (a local sweet treat).

Local Beverages

Beer lovers will be delighted to know that West Seneca is home to several breweries, including Rusty Nickel Brewing Co. and 42 North Brewing Company. Wine lovers can also visit nearby wineries, such as Willow Creek Winery and Victorianbourg Wine Estate.

Where to Stay in West Seneca: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

West Seneca offers a range of accommodations to suit any budget and travel style. Some popular options include the Hampton Inn Buffalo-South/I-90, the Country Inn & Suites by Radisson, and the Fairfield Inn & Suites by Marriott Buffalo South/I-90.

What to Buy in West Seneca: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking for souvenirs to bring home, consider picking up some locally-made products. Some popular options include sponge candy, smoked meat products, and souvenirs from the West Seneca Historical Society Museum.

Local Culture and Customs of West Seneca

West Seneca is a friendly and welcoming town with a strong sense of community. Visitors are encouraged to respect the town’s history and traditions, including its strong ties to the railroad industry.

Best Time to Visit West Seneca

The best time to visit West Seneca is in the summer, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the town’s natural beauty and outdoor activities make it a great destination year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of West Seneca

Do:

  • Respect the town’s history and traditions.
  • Take advantage of the town’s natural beauty and outdoor activities.
  • Try the local cuisine, including chicken wings and beef on weck.

Don’t:

  • Ignore local conservation efforts.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Miss out on the town’s many natural attractions.

Safety and Health

Safety in West Seneca

West Seneca is a safe town, with low crime rates. However, visitors are encouraged to take common sense precautions, such as locking their car doors and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in West Seneca

West Seneca has several hospitals and medical facilities, including Mercy Hospital of Buffalo and Buffalo General Medical Center.

Getting to West Seneca: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near West Seneca

The closest airport to West Seneca is Buffalo Niagara International Airport, which is located about 15 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near West Seneca

The closest bus station to West Seneca is the Buffalo Bus Terminal, which is located about 20 minutes away by car.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to West Seneca by train, with the nearest Amtrak station located in Depew, NY. Additionally, visitors can rent a car or use a ride-sharing service like Uber or Lyft.

How to Get Around in West Seneca

West Seneca is a walkable town, with several parks and attractions within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also rent a bike or use a ride-sharing service to get around.

Where to Go Next from West Seneca: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some nearby destinations and excursions from West Seneca include:

  • Niagara Falls, NY
  • Buffalo, NY
  • Letchworth State Park, NY
  • Fort Erie, ON

Conclusion

West Seneca is a beautiful and welcoming town that offers a range of attractions and activities for visitors of all ages. From its natural beauty to its rich history and culture, it is a destination that should be on every traveler’s list.
