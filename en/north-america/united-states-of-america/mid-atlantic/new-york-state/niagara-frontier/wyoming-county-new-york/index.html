Introduction to Wyoming County (New York)

Wyoming County, located in the Niagara Frontier region of New York, is a small but beautiful county that offers visitors a taste of rural, small-town America. Here, you’ll find charming villages, incredible natural scenery, and plenty of opportunities for outdoor exploration. Whether you’re here for a weekend or a longer stay, there’s something for everyone in Wyoming County.

List of Places to Visit in Wyoming County (New York)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Arcade and Attica Railroad: Visitors can take a ride on a vintage steam train through the scenic countryside.
  • Holland Land Office Museum: This museum tells the story of the Holland Land Company, which purchased much of western New York from Native Americans in the late 18th century.
  • Letchworth State Park: Known as the “Grand Canyon of the East,” Letchworth State Park is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Wyoming County. The park boasts 66 miles of hiking trails, picturesque waterfalls, and stunning views of the Genesee River.
  • Arcade Historical Society and Museum: This museum showcases the history of the Arcade area, including exhibit on Native Americans, early settlers, and military veterans.
  • Wyoming County Historical Society and Museum: A must-visit for those interested in local history, this museum has exhibits on the historical industries that shaped the county, such as salt mining and the lumber industry.

Natural Attractions of Wyoming County (New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Silver Lake: This picturesque lake is a popular spot for boating, fishing, and swimming.
  • Hunters Creek: This scenic creek has six miles of trails for hiking, snowshoeing, and cross-country skiing.
  • Warsaw Village Park: This park features a large lake, playgrounds, and picnic areas.
  • DeWitt Recreation Area: This expansive park has over 27 miles of multi-use trails, making it a great destination for hiking, biking, and horseback riding.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wyoming County (New York)

  • Letchworth State Park Hiking Trails: With over 66 miles of hiking trails, Letchworth State Park is a hiker’s paradise. Not to miss is the Gorge Trail, a 7-mile trek that affords visitors stunning views of the park’s waterfalls and the Genesee River.
  • Silver Lake Outlet Trail: This 11-mile trail follows the Silver Lake Outlet from Silver Lake to Warsaw, with plenty of great stops for fishing and picnicking along the way.
  • Genesee Valley Greenway: Stretching 90 miles through Western New York, the Genesee Valley Greenway is perfect for those looking to spend an extended period of time cycling through the stunning countryside.

Best Things to Do in Wyoming County (New York): Activities and Tours

  • Take a hot air balloon ride: A hot air balloon ride provides a once-in-a-lifetime experience, with breathtaking views of Wyoming County’s picturesque landscape.
  • Visit Hidden Valley Animal Adventure: A working farm and tour, takes visitors on a guided safari ride through grassy hills to see exotic animals and birds, up close.
  • Attend the Wyoming County Fair: Held in August every year, Wyoming County Fair is one of the oldest agricultural fairs in the state and features carnival rides, a petting zoo, and live music.

Food and Drink Scene of Wyoming County (New York)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Beef on Weck: This regional classic features thinly sliced roast beef piled high on a pretzel roll and served with au jus for dipping.
  • Salt Potatoes: Boiled with coarse salt, potatoes were a staple among miners in the area and remain a popular dish in the local cuisine.
  • Grape Pie: A sweet and savory dessert pie made from locally grown concord grapes and a spiced crumbly top.

Local Beverages

  • Cider: Hard apple cider made from local orchards is a popular drink in the fall.
  • Wine: Wyoming County is a part of the Niagara wine region and visit Ledge Rock Hill for some of the best wines.
  • Beer: Silver Lake Brewing Project, Microbrewery is a great place for craft beer lovers, has more than 10 different types of beer.

Where to Stay in Wyoming County (New York): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Attican Motel: A small, affordable motel with clean rooms and friendly staff.
  • The Hillside Inn of Ellicottville: While not in Wyoming County proper, The Hillside Inn is a charming bed and breakfast located in nearby Cattaraugus County.
  • Byrncliff Golf Resort and Banquets: A full-service resort that features a spa, golf course, and tennis courts.

What to Buy in Wyoming County (New York): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maple Syrup: New York is known for its maple syrup, and Wyoming County is no exception.
  • Local Art: There are many local artists in the region producing everything from paintings to pottery.
  • Cheese: Savor artisanal cheeses from Little Red Caboose’s Cheese Shop in Arcade that is freshly made to see them make cheese yourself!

Local Culture and Customs of Wyoming County (New York)

Wyoming County is a rural area where family values and the sense of community are important. While it is a safe destination, visitors should be prepared for some local customs and traditions, such as saying “hello” to people you pass on the street or acknowledging drivers who let you merge into traffic.

Best Time to Visit Wyoming County (New York)

The best time to visit Wyoming County is during the fall months of September through November when the leaves in the forest display an array of colors and the weather is mild. Summer is also a popular time to visit, particularly for outdoor activities like hiking, boating, and biking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wyoming County (New York)

Do:

  • Visit Letchworth State Park
  • Explore the area’s rich history
  • Try the local food

Don’t:

  • Litter or disrespect the natural surroundings
  • Ignore local customs and traditions
  • Underestimate the power and strength of the waterfalls like those in Letchworth state park.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wyoming County (New York)

Wyoming County has a low crime rate and is considered a safe place to visit. Visitors should be aware of their surroundings, and keep valuables secured.

Health in Wyoming County (New York)

Visitors should also take appropriate precautions in regards to health and safety, such as wearing proper footwear while hiking, checking for ticks during outdoor activities, and staying hydrated during times of hot weather.

Getting to Wyoming County (New York): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Wyoming County (New York)

  • Buffalo-Niagara International Airport: Located about an hour away from Wyoming County, this airport offers domestic and international flights.
  • Greater Rochester International Airport: Located about 70 miles from Wyoming County, this airport offers domestic flights as well.

Bus Stations Near Wyoming County (New York)

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: Altira, NY stop is located close to Wyoming County and provide frequent bus service to other destinations in New York and beyond.
  • Trailways Bus Lines: Perry, NY stop is also close to Wyoming County, providing bus services to NYC, Toronto, and more.

Other Options

  • Rental Car: Visitors who prefer to have their own transportation and flexibility may consider renting a car from one of the many car rental companies near airports or bus stations.

How to Get Around in Wyoming County (New York)

While it is generally best to have a car in Wyoming County, there are also buses and taxis available. Visitors can rent a bike and tour the region on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Wyoming County (New York): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip beyond Wyoming County, here are some nearby destinations worth considering:

  • Buffalo, New York: A vibrant city with a rich history and plenty of cultural attractions to explore, including art galleries and museums, shopping, and restaurants.
  • Finger Lakes: A beautiful region known for its wineries, hiking trails, and stunning views of the lakes.
  • Niagara Falls: One of the most popular tourist destinations in the region, Niagara Falls is just a short drive from Wyoming County and offers visitors stunning views of the falls and a host of attractions and activities.

Conclusion

With its beautiful natural scenery, rich historical landmarks, and welcoming, small-town atmosphere, Wyoming County offers visitors a unique and memorable vacation experience. Whether you’re looking to explore Letchworth State Park, sample the local cuisine, or learn about the area’s history, there’s something for everyone in this charming region of western New York.
