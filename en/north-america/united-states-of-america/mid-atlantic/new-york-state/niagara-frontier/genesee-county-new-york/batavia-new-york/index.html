Introduction to Batavia (New York)

Batavia, a charming city in the Genesee County in New York state, is known for its rich history, scenic beauty, and numerous attractions. Located in the Niagara Frontier region, Batavia is a great destination for those looking to explore the outdoors, learn about local history and culture, or simply unwind in a peaceful and relaxing setting.

List of Places to Visit in Batavia (New York)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Holland Land Office Museum: Explore the history of Western New York at this museum, which is dedicated to the region’s pioneers and early settlers. You can see historic artifacts and exhibits, as well as the original land office where the early settlers purchased land from the Holland Land Company.
  • Genesee County Park & Forest Interpretive Center: This interpretive center is located within the Genesee County Park & Forest, and provides insights into the park’s ecology and natural history. You can learn about the plants and animals that call the park home, as well as the various recreational opportunities available.
  • Stafford Historical Society: This museum is dedicated to preserving the history of the Stafford area and includes exhibits on agriculture, industry, and other aspects of local life.

Natural Attractions: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Genesee County Park & Forest: This beautiful park is home to over 4300 acres of rolling hills, woodlands, and meadows. Visitors can enjoy hiking, fishing, picnicking, and other outdoor activities.
  • Horseshoe Lake Wildlife Management Area: This 1294-acre preserve is located just outside of Batavia and features hiking trails, fishing opportunities, and great bird watching. Look out for the resident ospreys and bald eagles!
  • Lake Erie: About an hour’s drive from Batavia, Lake Erie offers a wide variety of recreational opportunities, including swimming, boating, fishing, and more.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Batavia (New York)

  • Lehigh Valley Trail: This 15-mile trail offers a scenic hike or bike ride through the rolling countryside of Western New York. The trail is accessible from several points in Batavia and offers plenty of opportunities to stop and explore along the way.
  • Ellicott Creek Trailway: This 8.6-mile trail runs from Amherst to Tonawanda, passing through several parks and offering great views of the creek. It’s a favorite spot for hiking, jogging, and biking, and is just a short drive from Batavia.

Best Things to Do in Batavia (New York): Activities and Tours

  • Batavia Downs Gaming: Located in Batavia, this casino offers a variety of slot machines and table games, as well as live horse racing.
  • Farmer’s market: This locally-run farmer’s market in downtown Batavia offers fresh produce, baked goods, and other locally-made products.
  • Tasting trails: Batavia is home to several local wineries and breweries, which offer tastings and tours throughout the year.

Week-Long Itinerary for Batavia (New York)

Day 1: Get acquainted with Batavia

Start your trip by exploring downtown Batavia, where you can browse local shops and eateries. Be sure to stop by the Holland Land Office Museum to learn about the history of the region.

Day 2: Enjoy the outdoors

Head to the Genesee County Park & Forest, where you can explore the park’s hiking trails and enjoy a picnic lunch. In the afternoon, visit the Horseshoe Lake Wildlife Management Area for some bird watching and fishing.

Day 3: Discover local history

Take a tour of the Stafford Historical Society Museum and learn about the agricultural and industrial history of Western New York. In the afternoon, visit the Batavia Cemetery, where many of the city’s early settlers and prominent figures are buried.

Day 4: Take a road trip

Spend the day exploring nearby destinations, such as Lake Erie or the Finger Lakes. Both offer plenty of outdoor activities and scenic beauty.

Day 5: Get adventurous

Visit Niagara Falls, just a short drive from Batavia, and take a thrilling boat ride to the base of the falls. In the afternoon, head to Darien Lake Amusement & Water Park for some more excitement.

Day 6: Relax and indulge

Enjoy a day of pampering at one of the local spas or wellness centers. In the evening, head to a local winery or brewery for some tasting.

Day 7: Departure day

Before heading home, stop by the Batavia Farmer’s Market to pick up some fresh produce and local goodies.

Food and Drink Scene of Batavia (New York)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Beef on Weck: This local specialty takes thinly-sliced roast beef, served on a salted kummelweck roll, include mustard and horseradish.
  • Corned Beef Hash: This is a popular breakfast dish, made in various with vegetables, hash browns and other ingredients.
  • Chicken French: this breaded chicken is cooked with lemon and butter sauce.
  • Apple cider doughnuts: These sweet treats are made with fresh-picked apples and are a fall favorite.

Local Beverages

  • Batavia Bourbon: This award-winning bourbon is handcrafted in small batches and aged in charred oak barrels.
  • Glenbrew Beer: Batavia’s own brewery, offering a variety of craft beers.

Where to Stay in Batavia (New York): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Holiday Inn Express: Located in downtown Batavia, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and a complimentary breakfast.
  • Red Roof Inn: This budget-friendly option is conveniently located off the highway and offers clean, basic accommodations.
  • The Little Penthouse: This cozy and charming guesthouse is located in the heart of Batavia and offers a more unique lodging experience.

What to Buy in Batavia (New York): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maple Syrup: This natural sweetener is produced in Western New York and makes a great gift or souvenir.
  • Handcrafted Soap: A local artisan makes them using organic ingredients, and sold at local markets and shops.
  • Artisanal Cheese: Many local farms offer artisanal cheese, including cheddar, gouda, and more.

Local Culture and Customs of Batavia (New York)

  • The people in Batavia are friendly, welcoming and proud of their history and culture.
  • The area is home to many festivals throughout the year, including the Batavia Rodeo in July and the Batavia Wine & Spirits Festival in October.

Best Time to Visit Batavia (New York)

The best time to visit Batavia is in the summer and fall, when the weather is mild and the fall foliage is at its peak. However, winter is also a great time to visit for snow sports and cozy indoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Batavia (New York)

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Explore the outdoors and enjoy the natural beauty of the area
  • Try local cuisine and products

Don’t:

  • Ignore local traffic laws or speed limits
  • Disrespect the wildlife or vegetation in the parks
  • Litter in public areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Batavia (New York)

Batavia is generally a safe and low-crime area. However, as with any destination, it’s always important to take basic safety precautions, such as locking your car and securing valuables.

Health in Batavia (New York)

Batavia is home to several medical facilities, including United Memorial Medical Center and the Batavia VA Medical Center.

Getting to Batavia (New York): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Batavia (New York)

The nearest airport to Batavia is the Greater Rochester International Airport (ROC), which is about 30 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Batavia (New York)

Greyhound operates a bus station in Batavia.

Other Options

The easiest way to get around Batavia is by car, although there are local taxis and rideshare options available.

How to Get Around in Batavia (New York)

Having your car is an asset when exploring Batavia, but some of the nearby sights are best enjoyed on foot or by bike.

Where to Go Next from Batavia (New York): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nearby destinations of interest include the Finger Lakes, which offer beautiful scenery, wine tasting, and water sports. Niagara Falls is an also an easy day trip from Batavia.

Conclusion

Batavia is a fantastic destination for those looking for a mix of outdoor adventure and cultural exploration. From hiking and fishing to exploring local museums and shops, there’s something for everyone in this charming Western New York city.
