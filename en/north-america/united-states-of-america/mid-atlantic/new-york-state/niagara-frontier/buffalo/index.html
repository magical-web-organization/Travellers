Introduction to Buffalo

Buffalo is a city located in Niagara Frontier, in New York (state), in Mid-Atlantic, in United States of America, in North America. It is known for its rich history, stunning architecture, and beautiful natural attractions. Buffalo is a great place to visit for anyone who is interested in exploring the outdoors, immersing themselves in local culture, and discovering the best in food and drink.

List of Places to Visit in Buffalo

Buffalo has something for everyone, from museums and historical places to natural attractions, outdoor adventures, and more. Here a list of the top places to visit in Buffalo:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Albright-Knox Art Gallery: This world-renowned art museum has a vast collection of modern and contemporary art. Make sure to check out the outdoor sculpture garden as well.
  • Buffalo and Erie County Naval & Military Park: A museum dedicated to military history, this attraction features historic ships, military equipment, and interactive exhibits.
  • Buffalo Museum of Science: One of the top science museums in the country, the Buffalo Museum of Science has fascinating exhibits on astronomy, natural history, and more.
  • Frank Lloyd Wright’s Darwin D. Martin House Complex: This architectural masterpiece is one of the highlights of Buffalo. Take a guided tour to learn more about the design and history of the house.

Natural Attractions of Buffalo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Niagara Falls: One of the most famous natural attractions in the world, Niagara Falls is a must-see if you’re in Buffalo. Take a boat tour to get up close and personal with the falls.
  • Buffalo and Erie County Botanical Gardens: This tranquil space is a great place to relax and enjoy nature. Make sure to check out the conservatory, which has rare and exotic plants from around the world.
  • Tifft Nature Preserve: If you’re looking for outdoor adventures, consider visiting Tifft Nature Preserve, a 264-acre nature reserve. It is home to more than 300 species of birds and has numerous trails for hiking and biking.
  • Eternal Flame Falls: This waterfall has a unique feature – a small flame that burns behind the waterfall. It is a popular hiking destination and a great place to take photos.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Buffalo

Buffalo is a great place to explore on foot or by bike. Here are some of the best routes for hiking and biking in Buffalo:

  • Outer Harbor Trail: This 4.5-mile trail takes you along the beautiful Lake Erie shoreline and offers stunning views of the water.
  • Niagara River Greenway: This 35-mile trail extends from Buffalo to Niagara Falls and offers a scenic route for hiking and biking.
  • Delaware Park: This 350-acre park has numerous trails for hiking and biking. Make sure to stop at the Rose Garden on your way through.
  • Ellicott Creek Trailway: This 11-mile trail runs from Tonawanda to Amherst and offers a beautiful route for biking and hiking.

Best Things to Do in Buffalo: Activities and Tours

In addition to the top places to visit, there are also many other things to do in Buffalo. Here are some of the best activities and tours:

  • Canalside: Located in the heart of downtown Buffalo, Canalside is a popular destination for outdoor activities. Take part in water sports, rent a bike, or enjoy live music and events.
  • Food Tours: Buffalo is known for its food scene, and there are many food tours available that will take you to some of the best local restaurants.
  • Brewery Tours: Buffalo is home to many craft breweries, and brewery tours are a great way to learn about the brewing process and sample some local beers.
  • Boat Tours: Take a boat tour of Lake Erie and the Buffalo River to see the city from a unique perspective. Sunset cruises are especially popular.

Food and Drink Scene of Buffalo

Buffalo is known for its unique cuisine, such as Buffalo wings, beef on weck sandwich, and sponge candy. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Buffalo Wings: These spicy chicken wings are a Buffalo classic. Anchor Bar and Duff’s Famous Wings are two of the most famous places to try them.
  • Beef on Weck Sandwich: A sandwich made with thinly sliced roast beef, horseradish, and au jus served on a kummelweck roll.
  • Fish Fry: A Friday night tradition in Buffalo, fish fry is battered and deep-fried fish served with French fries and coleslaw.
  • Sponge Candy: This sweet treat is made with sugar, corn syrup, and baking soda to create a light, airy candy that melts in your mouth.

Local Beverages

  • Loganberry: A sweet and tangy soft drink that originated in Buffalo.
  • Tom & Jerry: A warm holiday drink made with brandy, rum, and spices.
  • Local Beer: Buffalo has many craft breweries, including Flying Bison Brewery, Community Beer Works, and Resurgence Brewing Co.

Where to Stay in Buffalo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Buffalo has many accommodation options for visitors, ranging from high-end hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in Buffalo:

  • The Mansion on Delaware Avenue: This luxury hotel is housed in a historic mansion and offers spacious suites, a spa, and a restaurant.
  • Hostel Buffalo-Niagara: This hostel is a great option for budget-conscious travelers. It offers dorm-style and private rooms and has a communal kitchen and lounge area.
  • InnBuffalo off Elmwood: This bed and breakfast is housed in a historic mansion and offers elegant rooms and a complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Buffalo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique gifts and souvenirs, there are many local products you can buy in Buffalo. Here are some ideas:

  • Buffalo Bills or Buffalo Sabres Merchandise: If you’re a sports fan, consider buying merchandise from one of Buffalo’s professional sports teams.
  • Sponge Candy: As mentioned earlier, sponge candy is a Buffalo classic and a great gift.
  • Craft Beer: Buffalo has many craft breweries, and bringing home a few bottles of local beer is a great way to share your experience with friends and family.
  • Artwork from Local Artists: Buffalo has a thriving arts scene, and there are many galleries and shops where you can buy artwork from local artists.

Local Culture and Customs of Buffalo

Buffalo is a friendly and welcoming city, and visitors are often struck by the community spirit and strong sense of local pride. Some local customs to keep in mind include:

  • Saying “hello” to strangers: It’s common to greet people you don’t know in Buffalo, so don’t be surprised if someone says “hello” to you on the street.
  • Hockey and football are serious business: Buffalo is a sports-crazy city, and people take their hockey and football seriously. If you’re in town during a game, consider joining in the fun.
  • Politeness and respect: Buffalo is a polite and courteous city, and visitors are expected to show respect to locals and their customs.

Best Time to Visit Buffalo

The best time to visit Buffalo is in the summer or fall. The weather is mild, and there are many outdoor events and activities. Winter can be cold and snowy, so it’s best to avoid this time of year if you’re not a fan of the cold.

Do’s and Don’ts of Buffalo

Here are some things to keep in mind during your visit to Buffalo:

Do:

  • Try the food: Buffalo is known for its unique cuisine, so make sure to try some local dishes.
  • Visit Niagara Falls: It’s only a short drive from Buffalo and is one of the most beautiful natural attractions in the world.
  • Take a brewery tour: Buffalo has many great craft breweries, and taking a tour is a great way to experience them.

Don’t:

  • Take public transportation outside of the city: Public transportation can be unreliable outside of the city, so it’s best to rent a car if you want to explore the surrounding areas.
  • Forget to bundle up: Buffalo can get cold in the winter, so make sure to bring warm clothes if you’re visiting during this time.
  • Underestimate the traffic: Buffalo can have heavy traffic during rush hour, so plan accordingly if you’re driving.

Safety and Health

Buffalo is a safe city, but it’s always important to take precautions when traveling. Here’s what you need to know about safety and health in Buffalo:

Safety in Buffalo

  • Stay aware of your surroundings: As with any city, it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings.
  • Use common sense when out at night: Avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.
  • Follow traffic rules: Buffalo can have heavy traffic, so make sure to follow traffic rules, especially when driving.

Health in Buffalo

  • Drink bottled water: While the tap water in Buffalo is safe to drink, some visitors prefer to drink bottled water.
  • Wear sunscreen: If you’re spending time outdoors, make sure to wear sunscreen to protect your skin.

Getting to Buffalo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Buffalo has several transportation options for visitors, including air travel, bus, and train. Here’s what you need to know:

Airports Near Buffalo

Buffalo Niagara International Airport (BUF) is the main airport serving Buffalo. It is located about 10 miles east of the city and is serviced by several major airlines.

Bus Stations Near Buffalo

The Greyhound Bus Station is located just east of downtown Buffalo, and serves several major routes throughout the country.

Other Options

Amtrak has a station located in Depew, just east of Buffalo. It services several routes throughout the northeast and midwest.

How to Get Around in Buffalo

Buffalo has several transportation options for visitors, including public transportation and ride-sharing services. Here’s what you need to know:

  • NFTA Metro Bus and Rail: Buffalo has a comprehensive public transportation system that includes buses and light rail trains. Fares are affordable, and schedules are convenient.
  • Ride-Sharing Services: Companies like Uber and Lyft are available in Buffalo and are a convenient option for getting around the city.

Where to Go Next from Buffalo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Buffalo is a great starting point for exploring the surrounding areas of New York State and beyond. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Niagara Falls: Just a short drive from Buffalo, Niagara Falls is one of the most famous natural attractions in the world.
  • Rochester: This vibrant city is located just an hour east of Buffalo and has a thriving arts and cultural scene.
  • Toronto, Canada: Buffalo is only about a two-hour drive from Toronto, making it a great day trip or weekend excursion.

Conclusion

Buffalo has a unique blend of natural beauty, history, and culture that makes it a great destination for travelers. Whether you’re interested in exploring the outdoors, immersing yourself in local culture, or discovering the best in food and drink, there’s something for everyone in Buffalo.
