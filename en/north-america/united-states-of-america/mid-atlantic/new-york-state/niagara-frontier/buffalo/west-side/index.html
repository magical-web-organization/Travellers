Introduction to West Side

Located in Buffalo, in the heart of Western New York, West Side is a neighborhood that is rich in history and culture. It is home to a diverse community that boasts some of the most beautiful natural attractions in the region. Whether you are looking for a hiking trail, good food, or a unique cultural experience, West Side has something to offer.

List of Places to Visit in West Side

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Frank Lloyd Wright’s Martin House

Frank Lloyd Wright’s Martin House is a fantastic example of the Prairie School style of architecture. It was built for wealthy Buffalo businessman Darwin D. Martin and his family between 1903 and 1905. The house has been beautifully restored and houses a museum and visitors’ center.

Albright-Knox Art Gallery

The Albright-Knox Art Gallery is home to a world-renowned collection of modern and contemporary art, including works by Vincent van Gogh, Pablo Picasso, and Andy Warhol. The gallery also hosts a variety of temporary exhibits throughout the year.

Buffalo Museum of Science

The Buffalo Museum of Science is a family-friendly destination that is dedicated to exploring the natural world. Visitors can explore interactive exhibits, live animals, and a range of hands-on activities.

Natural Attractions of West Side: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Niagara River

The Niagara River is one of the most beautiful natural attractions in Western New York. It is a popular spot for boating, fishing, and birdwatching, with plenty of scenic views.

Delaware Park

Delaware Park is a picturesque urban park that is a popular destination for joggers, picnickers, and anyone who loves the outdoors. It also boasts a golf course, playgrounds, and an outdoor amphitheatre.

West Side Bazaar

The West Side Bazaar is a cultural melting pot that brings together a variety of international vendors under one roof. Visitors can find everything from handmade jewelry to traditional African textiles.

Hiking/Biking Routes of West Side

Riverwalk

The Riverwalk is a scenic trail that winds along the Niagara River, providing stunning views of the water and downtown Buffalo. It’s a great spot for a leisurely stroll or a bike ride.

Buffalo Outer Harbor

The Buffalo Outer Harbor is a vast nature preserve, teeming with wildlife and natural beauty. It is home to numerous trails and hiking paths, and visitors can also rent kayaks and paddleboards to explore the waterways.

LaSalle Park

Lasalle Park is a beautiful lakefront park that is the perfect spot for a family picnic. It is also home to softball fields, tennis courts, and basketball courts.

Best Things to Do in West Side: Activities and Tours

Buffalo City Hall Tour

Buffalo’s City Hall is an impressive Art Deco building that is open for tours. Visitors can take a guided tour of the building and learn about its rich history and unique architectural features.

Buffalo Pedal Tours

Buffalo Pedal Tours is a fun and unique way to explore the city. Visitors can hop on a pedal-powered trolley and tour some of the city’s most popular bars and restaurants.

Buffalo RiverWorks

Buffalo RiverWorks is a popular entertainment complex that features an indoor roller rink, a live music venue, and an outdoor beer garden.

Food and Drink Scene of West Side

Must-Try Dishes

Beef on Weck

Beef on Weck is a local favorite that is a must-try for anyone visiting the Buffalo area. This sandwich features thinly sliced roast beef piled high on a kimmelweck roll and served with a side of au jus for dipping.

Buffalo Wings

No trip to Buffalo is complete without trying the iconic Buffalo wings. These crispy, spicy wings are smothered in a spicy sauce and served with a side of blue cheese dressing and celery sticks.

Sponge Candy

Sponge candy is a sweet confection that is unique to the Buffalo area. It is made with sugar, corn syrup, and baking soda, resulting in a light, airy texture that is covered in chocolate.

Local Beverages

Genesee Beer

Genesee beer is a local favorite that has been brewed in Rochester, New York since 1878. It has a smooth, slightly sweet flavor that is perfect for sipping on a hot summer day.

Local Wine

Western New York is also home to several wineries, and visitors can take a wine tour of the area to sample some of the local vintages.

Where to Stay in West Side: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors to West Side have a variety of accommodation options available, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some popular choices include:

Hotel Henry

The Hotel Henry is an upscale hotel that is housed in a historic building that was once part of the Richardson Olmsted Complex. It features luxurious rooms and amenities, including an on-site restaurant and bar.

Hostel Buffalo Niagara

Hostel Buffalo Niagara is a budget-friendly option that is perfect for solo travelers or anyone on a tight budget. It features shared dormitory-style rooms, as well as private rooms with en suite bathrooms.

Inn Buffalo

The Inn Buffalo is a beautiful bed and breakfast that is housed in a restored 19th-century mansion. It features luxurious rooms and amenities, including a gourmet breakfast each morning.

What to Buy in West Side: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Buffalo-themed Gifts

Visitors to West Side can find a variety of Buffalo-themed souvenirs, including t-shirts, keychains, and magnets.

Local Artwork

The West Side is home to a vibrant arts community, and visitors can find a variety of local artwork, including paintings, prints, and sculptures.

Local Culture and Customs of West Side

West Side is a culturally diverse area, so visitors should be respectful of the different customs and traditions in the area. It is also important to respect the natural environment and leave no trace when exploring the area.

Best Time to Visit West Side

The best time to visit West Side is in the summer, when the weather is warm and sunny. Spring and fall are also good seasons to visit, as the temperatures are mild and the crowds are smaller. Winter can be cold and snowy, but it’s a great time to visit if you’re looking for winter sports activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in West Side

West Side is generally a safe area, but visitors should use common sense and take precautions to avoid becoming a victim of crime. It is important to stay in well-lit areas, lock your car doors and windows, and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in West Side

Visitors to West Side should take precautions to protect themselves from sunburn and insect bites, particularly during the summer months. It is also important to stay hydrated and wear comfortable clothing and shoes for walking and hiking activities.

Getting to West Side: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near West Side

The main airport serving the Buffalo area is the Buffalo Niagara International Airport. Alternatively, visitors can also fly into the Niagara Falls International Airport, which is about 20 miles from West Side.

Bus Stations Near West Side

The main bus station in Buffalo is the Metropolitan Transportation Center, which is located in downtown Buffalo.

Other Options

Visitors to West Side can also rent a car or take a taxi or ride-sharing service to get around the area.

How to Get Around in West Side

The best way to get around West Side is on foot or by bike. Visitors can also use a car or taxi service to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from West Side: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Niagara Falls

Niagara Falls is one of the most iconic natural wonders in North America. Visitors can take a boat tour of the falls, explore the nearby park, or hike along the Niagara Gorge Trail.

Letchworth State Park

Letchworth State Park is known as the “Grand Canyon of the East” and is home to breathtaking scenery and natural beauty. Visitors can hike the park’s numerous trails, fish in the Genesee River, or take a hot air balloon ride over the park.

Conclusion

West Side is a hidden gem in Buffalo, rich in history and culture. From its gorgeous natural attractions to its diverse museums and historical landmarks, there is no shortage of things to do and see in this vibrant neighborhood. Whether you’re coming for a weekend or an extended stay, there’s something for everyone in West Side.
