Introduction to Downtown

Buffalo is a vibrant city with a rich history, and Downtown is its beating heart. Located in Niagara Frontier in New York, Downtown Buffalo is easy to access and full of exciting things to do and see. From historic landmarks to trendy shops and eateries, there’s something for everyone in Downtown Buffalo.

List of Places to Visit in Downtown

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Buffalo has a rich cultural heritage, and Downtown is home to a variety of museums and historical sites that celebrate its history and diversity. Some of the must-visit attractions include:

  • Buffalo & Erie County Naval and Military Park: A tribute to the brave men and women who served in the United States military. The park is home to three decommissioned naval vessels – the USS Little Rock, the USS The Sullivans, and the USS Croaker.
  • Albright-Knox Art Gallery: One of the nation’s oldest public art institutions, featuring a collection of modern and contemporary art from around the world.
  • Buffalo History Museum: A museum dedicated to preserving the history of Buffalo, and featuring exhibits on the city’s Native American heritage, the Erie Canal, and more.

Natural Attractions of Downtown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Buffalo may be a city, but it also has plenty of opportunities to enjoy the great outdoors. Downtown offers a variety of scenic views, parks, and outdoor activities. Some of the top natural attractions in Downtown Buffalo include:

  • Canalside Buffalo: A vibrant waterfront destination with plenty of outdoor activities such as kayaking, paddle boarding, ice skating in winter, and more.
  • Niagara Falls: Just a short drive from Downtown Buffalo is Niagara Falls - one of the most famous natural wonders of the world.
  • Forest Lawn Cemetery: This hidden oasis offers walking and jogging opportunities amidst beautiful landscapes and greenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Downtown

Buffalo offers great biking and hiking opportunities for those who like to be active. If you’re looking for some fresh air and exercise, check out these Downtown routes:

  • The Riverwalk Trail: A 1.3-mile-long trail that offers stunning views of Lake Erie and the Buffalo River, and connects the Canalside and LaSalle Parks.
  • Shoreline Trail: A 10-mile paved trail that runs along the waterfront and offers outstanding views of the lake, islands, and beautiful sunsets.
  • Erie Canalway Trail: A 360-mile-long trail that runs from Buffalo all the way to Albany, and offers gorgeous views of the Erie Canal.

Best Things to Do in Downtown: Activities and Tours

Downtown Buffalo has something for everyone, from history buffs to foodies, and adventure seekers to art enthusiasts. Some of the top things to do in Downtown Buffalo include:

  • Self-Guided Architecture Tours: Buffalo has a rich architectural history, and taking a self-guided tour allows visitors to see firsthand some of the city’s unique buildings and structures, such as the Richardson Olmsted Campus, Guaranty Building, and more.
  • Food Tours: Downtown Buffalo is home to some of the best food and drink spots in the city, from traditional wings to craft cocktails. Take a food tour to sample some of the best the city has to offer.
  • Buffalo River History Tours: Climb aboard a water taxi and learn about the history and heritage of the Buffalo River and the city’s waterfront.

Food and Drink Scene of Downtown

Downtown Buffalo is a food lover’s paradise, with a variety of culinary delights to suit every taste bud. Some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Downtown Buffalo include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Buffalo Wings: The city’s signature dish consisting of chicken wings, hot sauce, and blue cheese dressing.
  • Beef on Weck: A traditional sandwich made with thinly sliced roast beef on a kummelweck roll.
  • Sponge Candy: A sweet and crunchy treat made from caramelized sugar and chocolate.

Local Beverages

  • Loganberry: A sweet and tangy fruit drink that originated in Buffalo and is a local favorite.
  • Craft Beer: Buffalo has a thriving craft beer scene, with many local breweries offering unique flavors and styles.
  • Tom & Jerry Cocktail: This holiday favorite drink is spiked eggnog with rum and cognac accompanied with nutmeg.

Where to Stay in Downtown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Downtown Buffalo offers a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget. Some of the top hotels, hostels, and guesthouses to consider when visiting Downtown Buffalo include:

  • Curtiss Hotel: A luxurious boutique hotel located in the heart of downtown. It features contemporary design and premium amenities such as a rooftop bar, revitalizing spa treatments and indoor pool.
  • Hostel Buffalo-Niagara: A hostel that offers budget-friendly accommodation in Downtown Buffalo while still providing the amenities like free Wi-Fi and a shared kitchen.
  • Hotel Henry: A beautifully restored historical property with a modern twist. With its presidential suites, opulent interiors, and prime location, it’s a perfect option for a luxurious and comfortable stay.

What to Buy in Downtown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

During your stay in Downtown Buffalo, it’s only natural to want to bring back some souvenirs and local products as a memento of your trip. Some of the best souvenirs and local products to purchase in Downtown Buffalo include:

  • Buffalo Bills and Buffalo Sabres merchandise: You can find official merchandise for Buffalo’s famous NFL and NHL team in souvenir shops.
  • Lucy Lawn Ornaments: Tribute to Lucille Ball, who hailed from Jamestown, NY and always remembered as an icon of American humor
  • Frank Lloyd Wright-Designed Tableware: Frank Lloyd Wright designed the Darwin D. Martin House in Buffalo, and you can pick up inspired tableware pieces from the gift shop.

Local Culture and Customs of Downtown

Buffalo is the birthplace of many American cultural icons and the city filled with celebrations that honor its unique heritage. The distinct culture and tradition can be felt through art, architecture, food, festivals, and events. Get the chance to experience it by joining any of the events that may coincide with your visit.

Best Time to Visit Downtown

Downtown Buffalo is a great place to visit all year round, with something to do and see no matter when you go. From warm summers to snowy winters, the city embraces its diversity throughout the year. However, the best time to visit Downtown Buffalo is from June through September when the weather is warmer, and outdoor activities and festivals are at their peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Downtown

Do:

  • Take a food tour and discover some of the best culinary treats the city offers
  • Explore Buffalo’s architectural rich buildings and structures
  • Visit Niagara Falls, one of the most magnificent natural wonders of the world

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful to the staff or servers when eating out
  • Littering, as Buffalo is a city that values cleanliness and sustainability.

Safety and Health

Safety in Downtown

Buffalo, in general, is a relatively safe city. As a precaution, be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night. If you are traveling in a group, stay together to lower any risk of danger.

Health in Downtown

Downtown Buffalo has top-notch medical facilities and hospitals, including Erie County Medical Center and Roswell Park Comprehensive Cancer Center. Pharmacies and clinics are scattered throughout the city, and local residents are kind and generous and can offer help when needed.

Getting to Downtown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Downtown

Buffalo Niagara International Airport is about a 15-minute drive from Downtown Buffalo. It’s the largest airport in the area and is served by many airlines with direct flights to major US and Canadian cities.

Bus Stations Near Downtown

The Buffalo Transportation Center has a bus terminal that is located near the Buffalo Niagara International Airport. It is the major hub for Greyhound and Megabus, making it a convenient option for those coming from other cities in the US.

Other Options

If you want to take public transportation into Downtown Buffalo, the NFTA Metro Bus and Rail system is an excellent option. It serves the city and suburbs 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

How to Get Around in Downtown

Downtown Buffalo is easy to explore on foot, but it’s also a very bike-friendly city, with plenty of bike lanes and trails. You can also explore the city via the NFTA Metro Bus and Rail system, cabs, and Uber.

Where to Go Next from Downtown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Downtown Buffalo is the perfect base for exploring the Niagara Frontier area of New York State and beyond. Some of the must-visit nearby destinations include:

  • Niagara Falls: Just a short drive from Downtown Buffalo is Niagara Falls, one of the most impressive natural wonders of the world.
  • Letchworth State Park: Known as the “Grand Canyon of the East,” Letchworth State Park is just under an hour’s drive from Downtown Buffalo and offers breathtaking scenery and hiking trails.
  • Toronto: Just a few hours north of Buffalo is Toronto, Canada’s largest city. A perfect destination for a day trip, it’s home to a vibrant food and cultural scene that you wouldn’t want to miss.

Conclusion

Downtown Buffalo is a unique and exciting destination to explore, with so much to see, do and experience. From the outdoors to food to culture, Downtown has something that everyone can enjoy. So, pack your bags, grab your walking shoes, and head to Downtown Buffalo – the city that never disappoints.

Designed in 1892 by eminent local architects Green & Wicks, the ornate Beaux-Arts style of the Market Arcade symbolizes the era's optimism and taste for grandeur. Basically a turn-of-the-century shopping mall, the Arcade's inviting storefronts overlooked a skylit corridor that led from Main Street to the Chippewa-Washington Public Market. After World War II, as its namesake market closed and shoppers began to eschew downtown for the suburbs, the Market Arcade fell into decline; by the end of the 1970s it was shuttered. However, it has since been restored and reopened, and it's today the home of the '''Buffalo Adore''' local gift shop, local artist Michael Mulley's '''Queen City Gallery''', '''Squeaky Wheel Film & Media Art Center''', and more. The food court in the lobby of the '''Ellicott Square Building''' is popular with downtown office workers on their lunch break. West Chippewa Street forms the core of downtown Buffalo's most popular entertainment district. The '''Hotel Lafayette''' is one of a growing number of new or newly remodeled hotels that are mushrooming in downtown Buffalo. '''St. Paul's Episcopal Cathedral''', a Nationally Registered Historic Place and a National Historic Landmark, has stood since 1821 at the corner of Pearl and Church Streets in the heart of downtown.
