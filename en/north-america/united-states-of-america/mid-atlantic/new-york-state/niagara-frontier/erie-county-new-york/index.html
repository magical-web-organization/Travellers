Introduction to Erie County (New York)

Erie County is located in the Niagara Frontier region in New York, bordering Lake Erie, and includes the city of Buffalo. The county is home to several cultural and natural attractions and offers a perfect blend of urban and rural experiences. In this guide, we’ll explore the must-visit places, hiking/biking routes, activities and tours, food and drinks scene, local culture and customs, safety and health tips, and logistics of Erie County.

List of Places to Visit in Erie County (New York)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Erie County boasts of several museums and historical sites that tell the story of the county’s rich past and cultural heritage. Some of the must-visit attractions include:

  • Buffalo & Erie County Naval & Military Park
  • Buffalo History Museum
  • Buffalo Museum of Science
  • Theodore Roosevelt Inaugural National Historic Site
  • Albright-Knox Art Gallery

Natural Attractions of Erie County (New York): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Erie County is home to many natural attractions, including parks, beaches, and scenic views. Some of the must-visit natural attractions include:

  • Niagara Falls
  • Chestnut Ridge Park
  • Delaware Park
  • Tifft Nature Preserve
  • Outer Harbor

Hiking/Biking Routes of Erie County (New York)

Erie County has a wide variety of hiking and biking trails. Some of the best routes are:

  • The Erie Canalway Trail
  • Scajaquada Trail
  • Niagara River Greenway Trail
  • The Rim Trail in Chestnut Ridge Park
  • The Shoreline Trail at Tifft Nature Preserve

Best Things to Do in Erie County (New York): Activities and Tours

Erie County offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors. Some of the best things to do include:

  • Visit the Buffalo Zoo
  • Take a food tour of the city
  • Explore the Allentown Historic District
  • Take a sunset cruise on Lake Erie
  • Attend a Buffalo Bills game

Week-Long Itinerary for Erie County (New York)

Here is a suggested week-long itinerary to help you explore the best of Erie County:

  • Day 1: Visit Niagara Falls
  • Day 2: Explore the Buffalo & Erie County Naval & Military Park
  • Day 3: Take a food tour and explore the city
  • Day 4: Visit the Theodore Roosevelt Inaugural National Historic Site
  • Day 5: Go hiking at Chestnut Ridge Park
  • Day 6: Visit Tifft Nature Preserve
  • Day 7: Enjoy a sunset cruise on Lake Erie

Food and Drink Scene of Erie County (New York)

Must-Try Dishes

Erie County is famous for its unique culinary style and must-try dishes, including:

  • Buffalo wings
  • Beef on weck
  • Sponge candy
  • Chicken finger sub
  • Loganberry drink

Local Beverages

Erie County also boasts of many locally brewed beverages, including beers, wines, ciders, and spirits. Some of the popular brands include:

  • Big Ditch Brewing Company
  • Resurgence Brewing Company
  • Chateau Buffalo Winery
  • BlackBird Cider Works
  • Lockhouse Distillery & Bar

Where to Stay in Erie County (New York): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Erie County offers a range of accommodation options, from hotels to hostels and guesthouses. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • The Mansion on Delaware Avenue
  • Hotel Henry
  • Roycroft Inn
  • Hostel Buffalo-Niagara
  • InnBuffalo off Elmwood

What to Buy in Erie County (New York): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Erie County is home to many stores and markets that sell souvenirs and locally made products. Here are some must-buy items:

  • Buffalo-themed apparel and accessories
  • Sponge candy
  • Loganberry drink mix
  • Art by local artists
  • Handmade soap and candles

Local Culture and Customs of Erie County (New York)

Erie County has a rich cultural heritage, influenced by the Native American, French, and British settlers. The county is known for its unique cuisine, music, art, and architecture. The people of Erie County are friendly and welcoming to visitors.

Best Time to Visit Erie County (New York)

The best time to visit Erie County is during the summer months from June to August when the weather is warm, and there are many outdoor events and festivals. Fall is also a great time to visit when the trees turn into beautiful shades of red and yellow.

Do’s and Don’ts of Erie County (New York)

Do:

  • Try the famous Buffalo wings
  • Visit Niagara Falls
  • Explore the local markets and shops
  • Take a food tour of the city
  • Attend a Buffalo Bills game

Don’t:

  • Walk in unfamiliar neighborhoods alone at night
  • Leave your valuables in the car while parked
  • Smoke in non-smoking areas
  • Litter in public places
  • Speed while driving on the roads

Safety and Health

Safety in Erie County (New York)

Erie County is generally a safe place, but it’s always advisable to take precautions such as:

  • Avoid walking in unfamiliar neighborhoods in the night
  • Take caution while parking your car and do not leave valuables inside
  • Secure your doors and windows if you’re staying in an Airbnb or vacation rental

Health in Erie County (New York)

Erie County has many hospitals and medical facilities that offer quality healthcare services. Visitors are advised to have medical insurance before traveling.

Getting to Erie County (New York): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Erie County (New York)

The Buffalo Niagara International Airport is the main airport serving Erie County.

Bus Stations Near Erie County (New York)

The Greyhound Bus Station and Megabus Bus Station are located in Buffalo.

Other Options

Erie County is well-connected by road and can be reached by car or taxi. Amtrak trains serve Buffalo, which also has a train station.

How to Get Around in Erie County (New York)

Erie County has an excellent public transportation system, including buses, trains, and taxis. Visitors can also rent a car or bike to explore the county.

Where to Go Next from Erie County (New York): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many nearby attractions and destinations that visitors can explore from Erie County, including:

  • Cleveland, Ohio
  • Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania
  • Toronto, Canada
  • Finger Lakes Region of New York

Conclusion

Erie County is a fantastic destination for people looking to explore a wide variety of attractions and activities. This travel guide provides a comprehensive overview of must-visit places, activities, food and drinks, and local culture and customs. Whether you’re a foodie, history buff, or nature lover, Erie County has something to offer everyone.
