Introduction to Magnolia

Located in Mid-Atlantic, United States of America, Magnolia is a small and charming town known for its scenic views, outdoor adventures, and cultural attractions. Whether you’re a history buff, nature enthusiast, or food lover, Magnolia has something to offer to everyone. In this travel guide, we will take you on a journey through the best places to visit, things to do, food and drink scene, where to stay, local culture and customs, safety and health, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Magnolia

Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or both, Magnolia has plenty of attractions to keep you entertained throughout your stay. Here are the top places to visit in Magnolia:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Magnolia has a rich history dating back to the 18th century, which is reflected in its many museums and historical places. These include:

  • The Barratt’s Chapel: Built-in 1780, this chapel is the oldest surviving Methodist church in America and is a National Historic Landmark.

  • The Old State House: This historic building served as Delaware’s statehouse from 1791 to 1933 and is now a museum.

  • John Dickinson Plantation: The former home of John Dickinson, a Founding Father of the United States and a signer of the U.S. Constitution. Now a museum, visitors can tour the house and learn about life in the 18th century.

Natural Attractions of Magnolia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Magnolia’s natural beauty is breathtaking, and there are many outdoor activities to enjoy. Here are some of the natural attractions of Magnolia:

  • Killens Pond State Park: This park is a popular destination for hiking, fishing, and boating. There’s a waterpark, campsite, and cabins for visitors.

  • The Bayshore Byway: This scenic drive offers spectacular views of the Delaware Bay and plenty of opportunities for birdwatching and wildlife viewing.

  • Prime Hook National Wildlife Refuge: Home to a diverse range of birds and wildlife, this refuge is perfect for hiking and exploring.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Magnolia

Magnolia’s natural beauty is best experienced through hiking and biking. Here are the best routes to explore:

  • The Junction Breakwater Trail: This scenic trail runs from Lewes to Rehoboth Beach, offering spectacular views of the Delaware Bay and coastal marshes.

  • The Northern Delaware Greenway Trail: This 12-mile trail connects Wilmington with the Bellevue State Park and offers scenic views and wildlife sightings.

  • The Michael N. Castle Trail: This trail runs along the Delaware River and offers stunning views of the skyline of Wilmington.

Best Things to Do in Magnolia: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking and biking, there are many other fun activities and tours to take in Magnolia:

  • Delaware Beer, Wine and Spirits Trail: This tour takes visitors on a journey through Delaware’s craft beer, wine, and spirits industry, with tastings and tours of local breweries, wineries, and distilleries.

  • Diamond State BBQ Championship: Held annually in August, this event features the best BBQ pit masters from all over the country.

  • Rehoboth Beach: Located just a short drive from Magnolia, Rehoboth Beach is the perfect place to relax, sunbathe, and swim.

Week-Long Itinerary for Magnolia

If you’re planning a week-long stay in Magnolia, here’s a suggested itinerary with activities for each day:

Day Activity
1 Visit The Barratt’s Chapel and The Old State House.
2 Hike the Killens Pond State Park and enjoy an afternoon picnic.
3 Take the Delaware Beer, Wine, and Spirits Trail.
4 Relax at Rehoboth Beach and have dinner at the Cultured Pearl.
5 Cycle the Michael N. Castle Trail and visit the John Dickinson Plantation.
6 Explore the Prime Hook National Wildlife Refuge and have lunch at The Pickled Pig Pub.
7 Wrap Up your week with the Diamond State BBQ Championship.

Food and Drink Scene of Magnolia

Magnolia’s food and drink scene is diverse and delicious, featuring many locally sourced ingredients, craft beer, and award-winning wine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crab cakes: Made with freshly caught blue crab and served with a side of Old Bay seasoning.

  • Peach cobbler: A classic southern dessert made with fresh peaches, cinnamon, and a buttery crust.

  • Chicken and dumplings: A hearty and comforting dish made with chicken, vegetables, and fluffy dumplings.

Local Beverages

  • Dogfish Head Brewery: Located in nearby Milton, this brewery is famous for its creative and unique craft beers.

  • Nassau Valley Vineyards: This winery is Delaware’s first commercial vineyard and offers tastings of its award-winning wines.

Where to Stay in Magnolia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many options for accommodations in Magnolia, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some recommendations:

  • The Brick Hotel on the Circle: This historic hotel located in Georgetown offers elegant rooms and an excellent restaurant.

  • The Bellmoor Inn & Spa: This luxury hotel located in Rehoboth Beach offers an outdoor pool, spa services, and rooms with ocean views.

  • The Atlantic Sands Hotel & Conference Center: Located directly on the Rehoboth Beach boardwalk, this hotel offers oceanfront rooms and easy access to the beach.

What to Buy in Magnolia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Magnolia is home to many specialty shops and boutiques, offering unique and locally made products. Here are some souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  • Orchard’s End: This farm and market offers fresh produce, local honey, and homemade jams and jellies.

  • Zwaanandael Museum Shop: Located in Lewes, this shop offers souvenirs and gifts inspired by Delaware’s Dutch heritage.

  • Coastal Goods: This boutique in Rehoboth Beach offers stylish clothing, accessories, and home decor with a coastal flair.

Local Culture and Customs of Magnolia

Magnolia’s culture is heavily influenced by its rich history, natural beauty, and friendly residents. Here are some cultural customs to keep in mind:

  • Respect for nature: Delawareans are proud of their state’s natural beauty and are committed to preserving it.

  • Friendly and welcoming: Magnolia residents are known for their hospitality and friendliness.

  • Love for history: Delaware has a rich history, and Magnolia residents take pride in their state’s historical heritage.

Best Time to Visit Magnolia

The best time to visit Magnolia is during the spring or fall when the weather is mild, and the foliage is at its peak. Summer can be hot and humid, and winter can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Magnolia

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Magnolia:

Do:

  • Respect nature and wildlife: Delawareans are proud of their state’s natural beauty and are committed to preserving it.

  • Be friendly and welcoming: Magnolia residents are known for their hospitality and friendliness.

  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities: There’s plenty of outdoor fun to be had in Magnolia, so make sure to explore the natural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment: Delawareans are committed to preserving the state’s natural beauty and wildlife, so make sure to dispose of trash properly and avoid damaging the environment.

  • Be disrespectful towards the residents: Magnolia residents are friendly and welcoming, so make sure to treat them with the same respect.

  • Ignore the historical and cultural attractions: Delaware has a rich history and heritage, so make sure to take the time to learn about it and appreciate it.

Safety and Health

Magnolia is a safe and healthy place to visit, but it’s always important to take precautions when traveling. Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Magnolia

  • Be aware of your surroundings: Like any town or city, it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and avoid dangerous areas.

  • Don’t leave valuables in your car: Car thefts are common, so make sure to keep valuables out of sight or bring them with you.

Health in Magnolia

  • Drink plenty of water: Delaware’s summers can be hot and humid, so make sure to stay hydrated.

  • Wear sunscreen and insect repellent: Protect yourself from the sun and mosquitoes by wearing sunscreen and insect repellent.

Getting to Magnolia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Magnolia is located just off Route 1, making it easily accessible by car. If you’re flying in, the nearest airport is Philadelphia International Airport, which is about two hours away by car. If you’re taking public transportation, Greyhound offers bus service to Dover, Delaware, which is just a few miles south of Magnolia.

Airports Near Magnolia

  • Philadelphia International Airport: Located about two hours away by car.

  • Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport: Located about two-and-a-half hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Magnolia

  • Dover Bus Terminal: Located in Dover, Delaware, just a few miles south of Magnolia.

Other Options

  • Car rental: A car rental is a convenient way to get around Magnolia and explore the surrounding areas.

  • Taxi service: There are several taxi companies in Magnolia that can provide transportation.

How to Get Around in Magnolia

The best way to get around Magnolia is by car or bike. There are several bike rental shops in the town, making it easy to explore the natural attractions and nearby villages. If you’re staying in Rehoboth Beach, you can take the Jolly Trolley to Magnolia for a day trip.

Where to Go Next from Magnolia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Magnolia is a great base for exploring the surrounding area. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Lewes: This charming seaside town is just a short drive from Magnolia and is known for its historic architecture and fishing villages.

  • Cape May: Located just across the Delaware Bay in New Jersey, Cape May is known for its Victorian architecture, sandy beaches, and horse-drawn carriages.

  • Philadelphia: Located just two hours away by car, Philadelphia is famous for its history, art, and food scene.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or just good food, Magnolia is a charming town with plenty of attractions to keep you entertained throughout your stay. With this comprehensive guide, you’re sure to have an unforgettable trip to Magnolia, Delaware!
