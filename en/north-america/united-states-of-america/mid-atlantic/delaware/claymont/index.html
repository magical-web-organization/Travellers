Introduction to Claymont

Located in Delaware, Claymont is a beautiful and historic community that offers visitors many exciting sights and experiences. The town dates to the early 19th century and is known for its charming neighborhoods, diverse shopping and dining scene, ample opportunities for outdoor recreation, and remarkable cultural attractions. Whether you’re looking for a peaceful getaway or an adventure-filled vacation, Claymont promises to deliver an unforgettable experience.

List of Places to Visit in Claymont

Here is a list of the top places to visit when exploring Claymont.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Claymont Stone School. This historic one-room schoolhouse was built in 1906 and served African American students for decades. Today, it provides valuable educational programs and exhibits on Black history.

  2. The Robinson House. Built in 1723, this is one of the oldest homes in Delaware and served as the headquarters for General Washington during the Revolutionary War. Visitors can take tours and learn about the history of the property.

  3. Delaware History Museum. Located in nearby Wilmington, this museum offers a comprehensive look at Delaware’s fascinating past, including its role in the founding of the nation and the American Revolution.

Natural Attractions of Claymont: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Bellevue State Park. This 328-acre park offers stunning vistas, hiking trails, picnic areas, and a historic mansion. It’s the perfect spot for a day trip or a weekend getaway.

  2. Wilmington State Parks. Hop on your bike and explore the parks’ miles of scenic biking and hiking trails, or take a stroll on the picturesque riverfront.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Claymont

Some of the best hiking and biking trails in Claymont and the surrounding area include:

  1. The Northern Delaware Greenway Trail. This beautiful 10-mile trail winds through scenic waterfront areas, a charming village, and old industrial sites.

  2. The Brandywine Creek Greenway Trail. This peaceful and idyllic trail runs along the Brandywine River and affords spectacular views of waterfalls, wildlife, and flora.

Best Things to Do in Claymont: Activities and Tours

Some of the most popular things to do in Claymont include:

  1. Visit the Claymont Community Center. This vibrant community hub offers fitness classes, art shows, and other fun events.

  2. Take a Food Tour. Claymont has a burgeoning culinary scene, and a walking food tour is a great way to explore the best local restaurants, cafes, and food spots.

Week-Long Itinerary for Claymont

Here is a sample itinerary for a week-long Claymont vacation.

Day 1: Explore the Robinson House and Delaware History Museum.

Day 2: Spend the day at Bellevue State Park and participate in various activities and events.

Day 3: Take a bike ride through the Northern Delaware Greenway Trail and enjoy a picnic lunch by the river.

Day 4: Visit the Claymont Community Center and take part in fitness classes or enjoy a walk through the area.

Day 5: Enjoy a food tour and take advantage of the delicious culinary scene in Claymont.

Day 6: Take a relaxing stroll through the Brandywine Creek Greenway Trail.

Day 7: Wrap up your week in Claymont with a visit to the Claymont Stone School and an evening of entertainment at one of the local bars or pubs.

Food and Drink Scene of Claymont

Claymont’s food and drink scene is diverse and exciting, with plenty of options for every taste and budget. Some must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Crab cakes. This classic mid-Atlantic dish is a popular choice on menus throughout the town.

  2. Philly cheesesteak. Claymont is only a short drive from Philadelphia and offers plenty of delicious renditions of this iconic sandwich.

Local Beverages

  1. Dogfish Head Brewery. This well-known craft brewery has a location in nearby Milton, Delaware, and offers tours and tastings.

  2. Iron Hill Brewery and Restaurant. This popular chain of breweries has a location in Wilmington with great beers and food, perfect for an evening out.

Where to Stay in Claymont: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Claymont offers numerous accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences, such as:

  1. Crowne Plaza Wilmington North. A luxury hotel located a few miles from Claymont that has extensive amenities, including an indoor pool and fitness center.

  2. Fairfield Inn & Suites Wilmington New Castle. A budget-friendly hotel in nearby New Castle, Delaware, which offers comfortable rooms and great value.

What to Buy in Claymont: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home from Claymont include:

  1. Delaware State Parks Annual Pass. A great investment for outdoor enthusiasts who plan to explore the nearby parks.

  2. Local craft beer. Pick up a six-pack or two of craft beer from a local microbrewery like Dogfish Head Brewery.

Local Culture and Customs of Claymont

Claymont locals are generally friendly and welcoming to visitors. It’s essential to treat the town and its residents with respect and appreciation.

Best Time to Visit Claymont
