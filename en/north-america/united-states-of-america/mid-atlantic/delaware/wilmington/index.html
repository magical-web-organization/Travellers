Wilmington, located in the heart of Delaware in the Mid-Atlantic region, is a charming city that offers something for everyone. With a rich history, diverse culture, and scenic beauty, it has become a popular destination for travelers looking for an authentic American experience. In this comprehensive guide, we’ve compiled all the information you need to plan the perfect trip to Wilmington. So pack your bags and get ready to explore this gem in Delaware!

Introduction to Wilmington

Wilmington is the largest city in Delaware, with a population of over 70,000 residents. It is a vibrant and dynamic city that is known for its rich history, diverse culture, and scenic beauty. The city is divided into several historic districts, each with its own unique charm and personality.

List of Places to Visit in Wilmington

Whether you love history, culture, outdoor adventure, or just exploring new places, Wilmington has something for you. Here are some must-visit places in Wilmington:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Winterthur Museum, Garden & Library: This museum showcases an extensive collection of American decorative arts, including furniture, textiles, and ceramics, as well as beautiful gardens and walking trails.
  • Delaware Art Museum: The museum displays over 12,000 works of art and has a vast collection of British Pre-Raphaelite paintings.
  • Nemours Estate: This stunning mansion was built for Alfred I. du Pont and is now open to the public for tours. The estate also features a beautiful French-style garden.
  • Delaware History Museum: This museum offers a fascinating look into Delaware’s rich history, with exhibits focusing on the state’s early inhabitants, colonial period, and modern times.

Natural Attractions of Wilmington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Brandywine Creek State Park: This park offers scenic hiking trails, picnic spots, and a nature center. It’s the perfect place to enjoy nature and get some exercise.
  • Hagley Museum and Library: This museum is located on the site of the DuPont Company’s first black powder factory and offers tours of the historic grounds and beautiful gardens.
  • Rockwood Park: This park is home to the Rockwood Mansion, a stunning example of Gothic Revival architecture. The park features hiking trails, gardens, and picnic areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wilmington

Wilmington is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes in the area:

  • Northern Delaware Greenway Trail: This scenic trail stretches for over 10 miles through Wilmington and offers beautiful views of Brandywine Creek and the surrounding countryside.
  • Alapocas Run State Park: This park features hiking trails, rock climbing, and picnic areas. It’s a great place to explore the outdoors and get some exercise.

Best Things to Do in Wilmington: Activities and Tours

Wilmington is home to a variety of fun activities and tours. Here are some of our favorites:

  • Riverboat Queen: Enjoy a dinner cruise or sightseeing tour aboard this beautiful riverboat. You’ll get to see Wilmington from a whole new perspective.
  • Delaware Park: This is the oldest thoroughbred racetrack in the state and offers live racing during the season. You can also enjoy slot machines and table games at the casino.
  • Wilmington and Western Railroad: Take a scenic train ride through the beautiful Brandywine Valley on this historic steam-powered train.

Week-Long Itinerary for Wilmington

If you’re planning to spend a week in Wilmington, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

Food and Drink Scene of Wilmington

Wilmington has a vibrant food and drink scene, with plenty of options for all tastes and budgets. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crab Cakes: Delaware is known for its delicious crab cakes, and you’ll find some of the best in Wilmington. Try them at places like Harry’s Seafood Grill and Big Fish Grill.
  • Scrapple: This is a local favorite made with pork scraps, cornmeal, and spices. It’s usually served with breakfast and can be found at places like Lucky’s Coffee Shop and Diner.
  • Grotto Pizza: This Delaware classic is a must-try. Grotto Pizza has been serving up delicious, thin-crust pizza since 1960.

Local Beverages

  • Dogfish Head Brewery: This craft brewery is a Wilmington favorite and offers a variety of unique beers, including their famous 60 Minute IPA.
  • Peach Wine: Delaware is known for its peaches, and you’ll find plenty of peach wine to sample at local wineries like Nassau Valley Vineyards and Harvest Ridge Winery.
  • UDairy Creamery: This ice cream shop is run by the University of Delaware and offers delicious, locally-made ice cream in unique flavors like Blue Hen Tracks and Apple Pie a la Mode.

Where to Stay in Wilmington: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Wilmington offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and tastes. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Hotel du Pont: This historic hotel is a Wilmington landmark and offers luxurious accommodations and amenities, including an elegant restaurant and a world-class spa.
  • The Westin Wilmington: This modern, upscale hotel is located in the heart of downtown Wilmington and offers spacious rooms with plenty of amenities.
  • The Inn at Montchanin Village: This charming inn is located in a historic village just outside of Wilmington and offers cozy rooms and a tranquil setting.

What to Buy in Wilmington: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect souvenir to bring home from Wilmington? Here are some local products to consider:

  • DuPont Stoneware: This pottery is made in Wilmington and features beautiful designs and patterns.
  • Delaware Beach Stones: These colorful stones are found on the beaches of Delaware and make great souvenirs or decorations.
  • Delaware Peaches: Delaware is known for its delicious peaches, so be sure to pick some up at a local farmers market or fruit stand.

Local Culture and Customs of Wilmington

Delawareans are known for their friendly and welcoming attitudes, so don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation with locals. It’s also important to remember that Wilmington has a rich history and cultural heritage, so be respectful when visiting museums and historical sites.

Best Time to Visit Wilmington

The best time to visit Wilmington is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wilmington

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Wilmington:

Do:

  • Explore the city’s rich history and cultural heritage
  • Visit local museums and historical sites
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Get outdoors and enjoy the natural beauty of the area

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful to locals or the city’s history
  • Litter or damage natural areas
  • Drive recklessly or park illegally

Safety and Health

Like any city, Wilmington has its share of safety concerns. Here are some tips to stay safe and healthy during your trip:

Safety in Wilmington

  • Avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas
  • Keep your valuables safe and secure
  • Be aware of your surroundings and trust your instincts

Health in Wilmington

  • Stay hydrated during hot and humid weather
  • Protect yourself against ticks and mosquitoes if you plan to spend time outdoors
  • Seek medical attention if you experience any health issues while in Wilmington

Getting to Wilmington: Transportation Options and Logistics

Wilmington is easily accessible by car, train, or plane. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Wilmington

  • Philadelphia International Airport is located approximately 30 miles from Wilmington and offers a variety of domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Wilmington

  • Greyhound and Megabus both offer service to Wilmington from several major cities.

Other Options

  • Amtrak trains stop in Wilmington, making it an easy and convenient option for travelers coming from the Northeast Corridor.

How to Get Around in Wilmington

Wilmington is a compact city that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. There are also several public transportation options, including buses and trains.

Where to Go Next from Wilmington: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Wilmington is a great starting point for exploring other parts of Delaware and the Mid-Atlantic region. Here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Philadelphia: This historic city is located just 30 miles from Wilmington and offers world-class museums, attractions, and dining options.
  • Cape May: This charming seaside town is located just across the Delaware Bay and offers a picturesque setting, beautiful beaches, and Victorian architecture.
  • Washington, DC: The nation’s capital is located just a few hours’ drive from Wilmington and offers a wealth of museums, monuments, and cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Wilmington is an underrated destination that offers something for everyone. With a rich history, diverse culture, and beautiful scenery, it’s the perfect place to explore the best of Delaware and the Mid-Atlantic region. Follow this comprehensive guide to plan your next trip to Wilmington and make the most of your time in this charming city.
