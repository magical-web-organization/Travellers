Introduction to Bridgeville

Bridgeville is a small town located in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States, in Sussex County, Delaware. The town has a population of just under 2,000 people, making it a great place for a peaceful, relaxing getaway. Bridgeville is surrounded by beautiful natural scenery and offers a range of outdoor recreational activities, cultural attractions, and a charming small-town vibe that visitors are sure to enjoy.

List of Places to Visit in Bridgeville

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bridgeville Historical Society Museum: The museum offers visitors the chance to explore the history of Bridgeville and Sussex County through various exhibits, photographs, and artifacts.

  • Governor Ross Mansion and Plantation: An impressive mansion built in 1859 that offers guided tours, showcasing its rich history.

Natural Attractions of Bridgeville: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Trap Pond State Park: One of the largest freshwater ponds in Delaware, offers visitors opportunities to hike, kayak, fish, bird watch, and even camp in over 140 campsites.

  • Bridgeville Wildlife Area: This beautiful area is perfect for nature lovers and offers hiking trails, fishing, bird watching and hunting from September to February.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bridgeville

Visitors who want to explore the great outdoors in Bridgeville can try any of its hiking or biking trails, such as:

  • the Tower Trail, located at the Trap Pond State Park, which is a popular trail with spectacular views of the pond.

  • The Spur Trail, which connects to the Tower Trail, and is about 0.5 miles long, which is perfect for people who want an easy hike.

Best Things to Do in Bridgeville: Activities and Tours

  • Visit a farmers market on Main Street for fresh produce and locally made products, held every Saturday from May to October.

  • Try a wine tasting at the Nassau Valley Vineyards, located just a short drive from Bridgeville. There, visitors can sample locally produced wines, take a tour of the vineyard, and enjoy breathtaking views.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bridgeville

Day 1: Visit the Governor Ross Mansion and Plantation, followed by a hike at Trap Pond State Park.

Day 2: Visit Bridgeville Historical Society Museum and take a stroll down Main Street.

Day 3: Explore the scenic views at Bridgeville Wildlife Area, followed by a picnic at a nearby park.

Day 4: Take a wine tasting tour at Nassau Valley Vineyards, followed by a visit to the local farmers market.

Day 5: Visit nearby towns such as Seaford and Laurel for more sightseeing opportunities.

Day 6: Enjoy outdoor activities such as kayaking, fishing, or biking at Delaware Seashore State Park.

Day 7: Relax and unwind at one of Bridgeville’s parks, followed by a delicious meal at a local restaurant.

Food and Drink Scene of Bridgeville

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crab Cakes: A local delicacy made with fresh Delaware crab.

  • Muskrat: A unique dish that is often served at local eateries in the area.

Local Beverages

  • Dogfish Head Brewery: A popular craft beer brewery in Milton, Delaware, which visitors can tour and sample various beers.

Where to Stay in Bridgeville: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Bridgeville Motel: This affordable motel offers clean and comfortable rooms for guests.

  • Heritage Inn and Suites: Located in nearby Seaford, this hotel is just a short drive from Bridgeville and offers a range of modern amenities.

What to Buy in Bridgeville: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Handicrafts: Visitors can purchase unique and handmade items at the Bridgeville Historical Society Museum.

Local Culture and Customs of Bridgeville

Bridgeville has a rich history and culture that visitors are sure to appreciate. The town is known for its close-knit community and friendly atmosphere. Visitors should always be respectful of the town’s history and culture, and be mindful of local customs.

Best Time to Visit Bridgeville

The best time to visit Bridgeville is during the summer months from May to September when the weather is warm and sunny, making it perfect for outdoor activities and exploring the scenic natural surroundings.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bridgeville

Do:

  • Respect the town’s unique history and culture.

  • Explore the scenic beauty of the Bridgeville Wildlife Area and Trap Pond State Park.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm any of the natural surroundings.

  • Be disruptive or disturb the peaceful atmosphere of the town.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bridgeville

Bridgeville is generally a safe town to visit, however, visitors should take the usual precautions such as keeping their belongings safe, staying in well-lit areas and avoiding dark and isolated places, especially at night.

Health in Bridgeville

Visitors should take note that the nearest hospital is the Nanticoke Memorial Hospital in Seaford about 10 miles from Bridgeville.

Getting to Bridgeville: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bridgeville

The nearest international airport is Philadelphia International Airport, which is around a two-hour drive from Bridgeville. The closest regional airport is Salisbury-Ocean City Wicomico Regional Airport, located about 25 miles from Bridgeville.

Bus Stations Near Bridgeville

There are no major bus stations in Bridgeville, however, visitors can catch a ride from the nearby town of Seaford.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi or ride-sharing apps such as Uber or Lyft.

How to Get Around in Bridgeville

Visitors can get around in Bridgeville by car or bicycle, or on foot for exploring the downtown area. Car rental services are available from various agencies for those who prefer to drive themselves around town.

Where to Go Next from Bridgeville: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can take a day trip to nearby towns and attractions such as:

  • Rehoboth Beach: A popular spot for vacationers, located about 30 miles from Bridgeville.

  • Lewes: A charming coastal town famous for its historic architecture.

Conclusion

Bridgeville offers visitors a mix of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and a relaxing atmosphere. Whether you want to explore the town’s rich history and culture, or simply enjoy the natural scenery, Bridgeville is the perfect destination for a peaceful and enjoyable getaway.
