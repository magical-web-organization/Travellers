Introduction to Georgetown

Georgetown, situated in Mid-Atlantic, Delaware, is a charming town known for its rich history and vibrant culture. With its countless attractions, cultural landmarks, and outdoor recreational activities, Georgetown is an ideal destination for travelers seeking to explore and immerse themselves in the local culture. In this comprehensive guide, we will discover the most remarkable things to do in Georgetown, including a list of must-see places, hiking trails, food, and drink options, local culture and customs, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Georgetown

Georgetown is home to a variety of attractions that showcase its rich cultural heritage and scenic beauty. Here are some of the top places to visit in Georgetown:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Brick Hotel: Located in the heart of Georgetown, the Brick Hotel served as a stopping point for travelers in the early nineteenth century. The hotel is now a museum that gives visitors a glimpse of the past.
  2. Delaware Agricultural Museum and Village: The museum showcases the state’s agricultural heritage with restored farm buildings and exhibits.
  3. The Georgetown Historical Society: This society preserves the town’s heritage and is a veritable treasure trove of Victorian, Arts and Crafts, and Colonial objects and artefacts.

Natural Attractions of Georgetown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Trap Pond State Park: The park is known for its serene beauty highlighted by a network of waterways and walking trails surrounded by cypress trees.
  2. Redden State Forest: The forest covers an area of around 12,000 acres and features walking trails, birdwatching, and hunting opportunities.
  3. Nanticoke Wildlife Area: The area is home to over 25 species of animals, including deer, turkey, and waterfowl; perfect for wildlife spotting and birdwatching.
  4. Gordons Pond: Located on the ocean, this pond offers a peaceful atmosphere perfect for a swim or a picnic.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Georgetown

  1. The Junction and Breaking Trail: A scenic hike at Trap Pond State Park that features a boardwalk and offers stunning views of cypress trees and wildlife sightings.
  2. Adkins Wildlife Refuge and Wetland Loop: This trail is an easy walk on a nature path that is great for wildlife spotting and birdwatching.
  3. Laurel Ramble Trail: A peaceful walking trail that runs along a section of the tidal river.

Best Things to Do in Georgetown: Activities and Tours

  1. Winery Tours: Head to Nassau Valley Vineyard and the Georgetown Wine and Beer Company for a wine and beer tasting session with your friends and family.
  2. River Cruise: Enjoy the Nanticoke River’s gorgeous views and wildlife sightings with a relaxing river cruise.
  3. Boating at Trap Pond State Park: Get closer to nature by boating in the serene waters of Trap Pond State Park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Georgetown

Are you planning to spend a week in Georgetown? Here is a sample itinerary that could make your experience unforgettable.

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Brick Hotel, the Georgetown Circle, and take a stroll through the town center.
Day 2 Learn about Georgetown’s agricultural heritage by visiting the Delaware Agriculture Museum and Village and strawberry picking at Fifer Orchards.
Day 3 Explore the great outdoors by hiking, fishing, or boating at Trap Pond State Park.
Day 4 Experience the history of Georgetown by visiting the Georgetown Historical Society and enjoying a horse-drawn carriage ride.
Day 5 Go wine tasting at Nassau Valley Vineyard or the Georgetown Wine and Beer Company.
Day 6 Visit nearby historic landmarks in Lewes and Rehoboth Beach.
Day 7 Relax on the beach, Gordons Pond or take a scenic river cruise of the Nanticoke River.

Food and Drink Scene of Georgetown

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Seafood: Delaware’s location on the east coast means that residents enjoy some of the freshest seafood in the country.
  2. Grotto Pizza: A Delaware food icon, Grotto Pizza, is a perfect blend of crispy crust, cheesy goodness, and flavorful toppings.

Local Beverages

  1. Dogfish Head Beer: Brewed locally and globally recognized, Dogfish Head Beer is a must-try in Georgetown.
  2. Nassau Valley Vineyard Wine: Delaware’s first and onlywinery, Nassau Valley, produces world-class vintages that are top-rated nationally.

Where to Stay in Georgetown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Brick Hotel: A colonial-style hotel built in 1836, the Brick Hotel offers a unique experience for history lovers.
  2. Microtel Inn & Suites by Wyndham Georgetown: Providing comfortable rooms and great amenities at an affordable price point.
  3. Fairfield Inn & Suites by Marriott Georgetown: An excellent base for travelling and exploring the area.

What to Buy in Georgetown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Handmade Artisan Jewelry: Georgetown is home to many talented jewelry makers, offering unique and high-quality pieces.
  2. Local Art: Artists are free to express themselves in Georgetown, and you can purchase their art from the local stores and galleries.
  3. Farm Products: Take home some of the state’s best farm products, including maple syrup, honey, and jellies.

Local Culture and Customs of Georgetown

  1. Hospitality: Georgetown residents are known for being incredibly hospitable and welcoming to visitors.
  2. Rich Heritage: The town is proud of its colonial-era and agriculture-driven past, which is evident in daily life and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Georgetown

Summer is the best time to visit Georgetown, with warm weather that is perfect for outdoor activities and festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Georgetown

Do:

  • Embrace the town’s unique culture and heritage.
  • Explore the surrounding areas and take advantage of the natural beauty of the area.
  • Try the local delicacies and enjoy the varied menu options available in Georgetown.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter or deface any of the cultural landmarks.
  • Do not be late for any of the planned activities or tours.
  • Avoid inappropriate behavior and be respectful to everyone you encounter.

Safety and Health

Safety in Georgetown

Georgetown is a safe town to visit. Standard precautions like locking car doors and securing valuables are advisable.

Health in Georgetown

In case of any health-related emergency or travel-related illness, the Beebe Healthcare Network is the nearest hospital that can provide the required medical assistance.

Getting to Georgetown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Georgetown

  1. Salisbury-Ocean City Wicomico Regional Airport (SBY), Maryland
  2. Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport (BWI), Maryland
  3. Philadelphia International Airport (PHL), Pennsylvania

Bus Stations Near Georgetown

  1. DART Lewes Transit Center
  2. Salisbury Greyhound Bus Station
  3. Dover Transit Center

Other Options

The best option to get around Georgetown is to rent a car. However, Uber and taxis are also available.

How to Get Around in Georgetown

The best way to get around Georgetown is by renting a car or going on foot. Alternatively, Uber and taxi services are available.

Where to Go Next from Georgetown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Lewes: Located about 25 miles north, Lewes offers its historic downtown, miles of coastline, and tax-free shopping.
  2. Rehoboth Beach: Take a short drive south, Rehoboth Beach, offers a lively boardwalk, amusement park, and plenty of restaurants.
  3. Cape Henlopen: Located south of Lewes, Cape Henlopen is a quiet natural reserve where visitors can observe wildlife and travel via bike trails.

Conclusion

Georgetown is a town that offers the perfect blend of historic charm and modern amenities. From its cultural landmarks and natural scenic beauty to its culinary scene and local culture, Georgetown provides visitors with an unforgettable experience. We hope that this comprehensive guide offers enough ideas and inspiration for your visit to Georgetown, Delaware.
