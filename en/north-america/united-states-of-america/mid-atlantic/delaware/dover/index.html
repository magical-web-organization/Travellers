Introduction to Dover

Welcome to Dover, the capital city of Delaware! Located in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States, Dover offers a unique blend of historical charm and outdoor adventure. As the second-largest city in Delaware, it’s a travel destination that’s worth exploring. Whether you’re a history buff or an outdoor enthusiast, Dover has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Dover

Whether you’re a first-time visitor or a seasoned traveler, these are the top places you won’t want to miss in Dover.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Johnson Victrola Museum: Located in downtown Dover, this museum houses a collection of over 600 phonographs and a variety of records and memorabilia.
  • First State Heritage Park: Explore the history of Dover by taking a walking tour of this park, which includes over a dozen historical sites, from Colonial buildings to a railroad station.
  • Air Mobility Command Museum: Aviation enthusiasts will love exploring this museum, which features over 30 aircraft and thousands of aviation-related artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Dover: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bombay Hook National Wildlife Refuge: This refuge covers over 16,000 acres and provides a habitat for migratory birds and other wildlife. Visitors can take a scenic drive, bike ride or hike through the refuge.
  • Killens Pond State Park: Enjoy fishing, boating, paddleboarding, hiking, or camping at this beautiful park that features a 66-acre pond.
  • Delaware Agricultural Museum and Village: Discover a slice of rural life in Delaware by visiting this museum and village. The museum houses a collection of farming equipment, while the village has restored historic buildings to showcase what life was like in Delaware in the 1800s.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dover

Dover offers a variety of hiking and biking routes that showcase its natural beauty and historic charm. Here are a few of the best routes:

  • Garrison’s Lake Greenway: Offering hiking, biking, and horseback riding trails, this greenway connects the towns of Smyrna and Dover.
  • St. Jones Greenway: Follow the St. Jones River for a scenic trip that begins in downtown Dover and ends at its namesake, the St. Jones River.
  • Dover Park Trail: Enjoy a leisurely stroll through this picturesque park, taking in its beautiful foliage and stunning lake views.

Best Things to Do in Dover: Activities and Tours

From historic sites to unique experiences, there are plenty of things to do in Dover. Here are a few of the top activities and tours:

  • Parasailing at Rehoboth Beach: Soar above Delaware’s coastline with a parasailing adventure that offers a breathtaking view of the Atlantic Ocean.
  • Firefly Music Festival: This multi-day music event takes place each summer and features a lineup of world-renowned musicians.
  • Dover International Speedway: For racing fans, there’s no better place to be than at the Dover International Speedway, which hosts two NASCAR Cup Series races each year.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dover

If you’re planning to spend a week in Dover, here’s an itinerary that will help you make the most of your trip:

Day 1 Johnson Victrola Museum, First State Heritage Park, and Dover Downs Casino
Day 2 Bombay Hook National Wildlife Refuge, Killens Pond State Park, and Biggs Museum
Day 3 Air Mobility Command Museum, Dover International Speedway, and DelDOT Museum
Day 4 St. Jones River Greenway, Dover Park Trail, and Delaware Agricultural Museum
Day 5 Discover Rehoboth Beach, shopping at Tanger Outlets, and walking the boardwalk
Day 6 Firefly Music Festival or exploring Historic Odessa

Food and Drink Scene of Dover

Sample the local flavors of Dover with these must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Scrapple: A traditional Pennsylvania Dutch dish made with pork scraps and cornmeal.
  • Warm Butter Cake: A sweet dessert made with a warm, buttery cake topped with vanilla ice cream and a sugary glaze.
  • Crab Cakes: Fresh and locally caught, Delaware’s crab cakes are a must-try.

Local Beverages

  • Dogfish Head Brewery: Located in nearby Milton, this craft brewery offers a variety of beers including the popular 90-minute IPA.
  • Fordham & Dominion Brewing Company: Sample a variety of beer flavors from this brewery, which includes their flagship beer, award-winning Copperhead Ale.

Where to Stay in Dover: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dover offers a variety of accommodations to suit every budget, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are a few of the top options:

  • Dover Downs Hotel & Casino: This upscale hotel features a casino, spa, and multiple dining options.
  • Sleep Inn & Suites Dover: A budget-friendly option that offers free breakfast, WiFi, and a location within walking distance to the Dover International Speedway.
  • Airbnb: For a more personal experience, consider booking an Airbnb that suits your needs and budget.

What to Buy in Dover: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Want to bring a piece of Dover home with you? Here are a few of the best souvenirs and local products to consider:

  • Delaware License Plate: A stylish souvenir that represents the state’s appreciation for independence, highlighting the motto “The First State”
  • Sea Glass Jewelry: Made with recycled sea glass found along Delaware’s beaches, this jewelry is a unique and sustainable souvenir.
  • Peppered Atlantic Smoked Salmon: This specialty salmon is locally sourced from the Delaware Bay and smoked to perfection.

Local Culture and Customs of Dover

Dover has a rich cultural history that’s deeply rooted in the state’s agricultural heritage. Visitors will also notice a strong military presence, given its proximity to the Dover Air Force Base. Moreover, Delaware is renowned for its state parks, beaches and vibrant restaurant scene.

Best Time to Visit Dover

The best time to visit Dover depends on your interests and preferences. The fall season offers breathtaking foliage and cooler temperatures, making it perfect for outdoor activities. The summer season offers a variety of events, including Firefly Music Festival. Spring is a great time to explore the state parks, while winter offers excellent deals on accommodation and attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dover

When visiting Dover, keep in mind these Do’s and Don’ts:

Do:

  • Visit the state parks and enjoy the natural beauty of Delaware’s beaches.
  • Try the local cuisine and explore the craft beer scene.
  • Learn about the state’s history by exploring the museums and historical sites.

Don’t:

  • Drink and drive: Delaware is known for having strict drinking rules.
  • Litter: It’s against the law.
  • Leave pets unattended or unrestrained.

Safety and Health

While Dover is generally safe, it’s important to take precautions to ensure your safety and health during your visit.

Safety in Dover

  • Stay aware of your surroundings, especially when walking alone at night.
  • Keep an eye on your valuables and secure them when not in use.
  • Follow all traffic rules and regulations.

Health in Dover

  • Wear insect repellent and protective clothing when hiking or exploring wooded areas.
  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated during outdoor activities.
  • Check for ticks after outdoor activities.

Getting to Dover: Transportation Options and Logistics

Dover is located only 90 miles from both Washington, D.C., and Philadelphia and is easily accessible by car, bus, or train.

Airports Near Dover

  • Philadelphia International Airport (PHL): Located 82 miles from Dover, Wilmongton International Airport offers non-stop flights to hundreds of domestic and international destinations.
  • Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport (BWI): Located around 75 miles from Dover, the BWI airport provides a 3 hours’ drive to Dover, offer international and national flights.

Bus Stations Near Dover

  • Dover Transit Center: Greyhound Bus service that connects to major cities across the U.S. It’s located in downtown Dover.
  • Amtrak Dover: Amtrak train service that runs to Washington, D.C., and New York City.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: If you prefer to drive yourself, car rental services are available.
  • Taxis and Rideshare: Taxis and rideshare services such as Uber and Lyft are available within Dover.

How to Get Around in Dover

Dover is a walkable city, and many attractions are within walking distance of each other. For those visiting the nearby parks or beaches, renting a car is recommended.

Where to Go Next from Dover: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’ve had your fill of Dover, here are a few nearby destinations and excursions that are worth exploring:

  • Rehoboth Beach: Only within a one-hour drive, this charming town offers something for everyone, from exciting nightlife to unique shopping.
  • Cape May – Lewes Ferry: Enjoy a scenic ferry ride that takes you to Cape May, NJ. This trip is often rated as one of the best short ferry rides in the country.
  • Philadelphia: One and half hours’ drive from Dover, explore the rich history of the United States by visiting Philadelphia, home of the Liberty Bell and Independence Hall.

Conclusion

If you’re looking for a destination that offers a blend of indoor and outdoor activities, Dover, Delaware is the perfect choice. With its rich history, cultural heritage and national parks and beaches, it’s an excellent travel destination for every kind of traveler. So, come along and enjoy a fascinating journey in the capital of the ‘First State’!
