Introduction to Lewes

Welcome to Lewes, a charming and historic town located in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States. With its scenic landscapes and rich cultural heritage, Lewes offers a unique travel experience for visitors of all ages and interests. Whether you are looking to explore the town’s rich history, enjoy some outdoor adventures, or simply relax and take in the beauty of the surrounding natural scenery, Lewes has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Lewes

Here is a list of some of the best places to visit during your stay in Lewes:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Zwaanendael Museum
  • Delaware Bay Maritime Museum
  • Cannonball House Maritime Museum
  • Lightship Overfalls

These museums and historical places offer a fascinating glimpse into the town’s rich history and cultural heritage.

Natural Attractions of Lewes: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cape Henlopen State Park
  • fishing and boating at the Lewes Harbor Marina
  • Lewes Public Beach

Take a walk along the trails or go birdwatching at Cape Henlopen State Park, enjoy a day of fishing and boating at the Lewes Harbor Marina, or simply relax on the sand at Lewes Public Beach.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lewes

Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes to explore during your stay:

  • Junction and Breakwater Trail
  • Gordon’s Pond Trail

These trails provide stunning scenery and are great for hiking, biking, and walking.

Best Things to Do in Lewes: Activities and Tours

Some of the best things to do during your stay in Lewes include:

  • Dolphin watching tours
  • Sunset sails
  • Brewery Tours

Experience the thrill of dolphin watching, take a scenic sunset sail or enjoy a brewery tour and tasting of locally crafted beer.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lewes

Here is a sample itinerary to help you plan your stay in Lewes:

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit Zwaanendael Museum and the Cannonball House Maritime Museum
Day 2 Explore Cape Henlopen State Park, go on a nature walk or hike
Day 3 Discover the Delaware Bay Maritime Museum and spend an afternoon fishing or boating at Lewes Harbor Marina
Day 4 Go on a dolphin watching tour and enjoy a sunset sail
Day 5 Experience a brewery tour and beer tasting
Day 6 Relax on Lewes Public Beach and enjoy the scenic views
Day 7 Shop for souvenirs and explore the local culture and customs of Lewes

Food and Drink Scene of Lewes

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crab cakes
  • Fresh seafood
  • Cream of crab soup

Lewes is home to a vibrant food scene with locally sourced seafood as the main feature. Don’t forget to try the famous crab cakes, fresh seafood and the creamy crab soup.

Local Beverages

  • Dogfish Head Craft Brewery
  • Nassau Valley Vineyards

Experience local drinks, like specialty beer from the famous Dogfish Head Craft Brewery or take a winery tour at Nassau Valley Vineyards.

Where to Stay in Lewes: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Some popular options for where to stay during your visit to Lewes include:

  • Hotel Blue
  • The Inn at Canal Square
  • Savannah Inn Bed and Breakfast

Explore accommodation options in Lewes, including hotels, hostels and guesthouses, for the perfect stay.

What to Buy in Lewes: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some great souvenirs and gifts from Lewes include:

  • Artwork by local artists
  • Ornament depicting a lighthouse or historic site
  • Local honey or jams

Shopping in Lewes is a must, with so much to offer from locally made artwork to scrumptious locally made jellies and jams, you are sure to find a great souvenir here.

Local Culture and Customs of Lewes

Lewes has a rich culture and history, with a fusion of Dutch and English heritage. The town is famous for hosting a number of festivals and events throughout the year, including the Lewes Historical Society Craft Fair and the Fall Sidewalk Sale.

Best Time to Visit Lewes

The best time to visit Lewes is during the spring and fall months, when the weather is mild and comfortable for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lewes

Do:

  • Try locally made seafood
  • Explore the museums and historical places
  • Enjoy the scenery and natural beauty of the town

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beaches or parks
  • Disturb the natural habitats

Safety and Health

Safety in Lewes

Lewes is a safe town, with low crime rates, making it a great destination for solo, group or family travel. It is always important to take precautions, even in low crime areas, such as locking car doors and not leaving valuables in plain sight.

Health in Lewes

Lewes offers excellent medical facilities with quality emergency services, should you need it. If you have specific health needs or concerns, be sure to consult a medical professional before your trip.

Getting to Lewes: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lewes

The nearest airports to Lewes are:

  • Salisbury-Ocean City Wicomico Regional Airport (SBY)
  • Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport (BWI)

Bus Stations Near Lewes

There are bus services available from the following stations:

  • Greyhound: Wilmington

Other Options

You can also reach Lewes by car or train with plenty of scenic routes and options.

How to Get Around in Lewes

Lewes has public transportation available, including buses, bike rentals and taxis. Walking is also a great way to see the town on foot and enjoy leisurely sightseeing.

Where to Go Next from Lewes: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lewes is surrounded by many great places to explore, including:

  • Rehoboth Beach
  • Cape May, New Jersey
  • Ocean City, Maryland

Rehoboth Beach, Cape May, New Jersey and Ocean City, Maryland are all great options and are only a short distance away.

Conclusion

Lewes, with its rich culture, history and scenic beauty, makes for a perfect travel destination. Plan your stay here and make memories that will last a lifetime.
