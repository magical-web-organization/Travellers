Introduction to Laurel

Located in Delaware, Laurel is a charming town known for its natural beauty, cultural attractions, and friendly locals. It is situated in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States and is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Whether you’re interested in hiking, biking, or exploring museums and historical sites, Laurel has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Laurel

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Laurel Historical Society: This museum showcases the history of Laurel through a collection of artifacts and exhibits. It is also home to the Laurel Heritage Trail, a self-guided walking tour of the town’s historic downtown area.
  • Trap Pond State Park: This park features the northernmost natural stand of bald cypress trees in the United States and is home to several historic structures, including a mill and an old logging camp.
  • Ross Mansion and Plantation: This historic site was once a 19th-century plantation and now serves as a museum where visitors can learn about the area’s agricultural past.

Natural Attractions of Laurel: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Phillips Landing: A beautiful spot for birdwatching and water-based recreation like fishing and kayaking that borders the Broad Creek and Nanticoke River.
  • Nanticoke Wildlife Area: This wildlife area is the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with over 10,000 acres of forest, fields, and wetlands to explore. The area offers hunting, fishing, and hiking opportunities.
  • Trap Pond State Park: This park offers more than just a historical site; it also has over 3,000 acres of woodlands and wetlands for visitors to explore. You can go kayaking, fishing, or take a hike on the many trails offered.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Laurel

Laurel is a great place to get some exercise, and there are many hiking and biking trails to choose from, including the following:

  • Laurel Heritage Trail: This is an easy and flat self-guided tour suitable for walking and biking, and visitors can explore the historic downtown area of Laurel at their own pace.
  • Nanticoke Wildlife Area Trails: The trails take visitors through the breathtaking forest and wetlands of Nanticoke Wildlife Area.
  • Trails at Trap Pond State Park: This state park offers several trails ranging from easy to moderate, winding through some of the most beautiful areas of Delaware.

Best Things to Do in Laurel: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Laurel:

  • Visit the town’s charming downtown: Stroll through downtown to admire the historic architecture, browse unique shops, and grab a bite to eat at one of the local restaurants.
  • Go on a fishing tour: Fishing tours are offered in the Broad Creek and Nanticoke River that spill into Laurel.
  • Visit the farmers’ market: There’s no better way to experience Laurel’s vibrant local food scene than by visiting the farmers’ market.

Food and Drink Scene of Laurel

Must-Try Dishes

Laurel is known for its delicious regional cuisine. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Crab cakes: Made with Atlantic blue crabs caught in the nearby Chesapeake Bay, crab cakes are a popular local dish that you won’t want to miss.
  • Oysters: Delaware Bay oysters are a staple ingredient in many Laurel dishes, and they’re best enjoyed fresh from the sea.
  • Chicken and dumplings: Tender pieces of chicken stewed with vegetables and served with fluffy dumplings are a hearty and delicious Laurel comfort food.

Local Beverages

The Southwestern region of Delaware is considered one of the country’s oldest winemaking regions, so wine lovers will definitely want to check out the local vineyards. The region is home to several award-winning wineries where visitors can sample local wines.

Where to Stay in Laurel: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some of the best places to stay in Laurel:

  • Laurel Inn Motel: This budget-friendly motel offers clean and comfortable rooms and is within easy walking distance to many of Laurel’s top attractions.
  • Woodruff Bed and Breakfast: This charming bed and breakfast features beautiful, antique furnishings and a picturesque garden.
  • Hampton Inn by Hilton Seaford: If you’re looking for a more modern and luxurious experience, this hotel chain is known for its comfortable rooms and excellent amenities.

What to Buy in Laurel: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Crab-themed souvenirs: Bring a piece of coastal Delaware home with you by picking up some crab-themed souvenirs like magnets, t-shirts, and key chains.
  • Newspapers: The local newspaper, Laurel Star, is a great way to stay up-to-date on community news and events.
  • Wine: As previously mentioned, the region is home to several wineries that produce high-quality wine.

Local Culture and Customs of Laurel

Laurel has a rich cultural history, with influences from Native American, African American, and European traditions. Visitors should be respectful of these cultures and customs by following local customs, dressing appropriately, and asking permission before taking photos of people or events.

Best Time to Visit Laurel

The best time to visit Laurel is in the late spring or early fall when temperatures are mild and comfortable for outdoor activities. Summers in Laurel can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and snowy, so plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Laurel

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, especially the crab dishes and oysters.
  • Do visit the area’s museums and historical sites.
  • Do respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage natural areas.
  • Don’t take photos of people without permission.
  • Don’t disrespect local cultures or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Laurel

Laurel is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should use common sense and take normal safety precautions, such as locking valuables in a safe place and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Laurel

There are no major health concerns in Laurel, and medical facilities are readily available in case of emergency.

Getting to Laurel: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Laurel

The nearest airport to Laurel is the Salisbury-Ocean City Wicomico Regional Airport, which is around 30 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Laurel

The closest bus stations are located in Seaford and Salisbury, both of which are approximately 10-15 minutes from Laurel.

Other Options

Another option is to drive to Laurel, as it is conveniently located near several major highways.

How to Get Around in Laurel

The best way to get around Laurel is by car. For those who don’t have a car, taxi and ride-share services are readily available, but you will be more flexibility with a car rental.

Where to Go Next from Laurel: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Rehoboth Beach: This popular beach resort town is just over an hour’s drive away and has a lively boardwalk and excellent seafood restaurants.
  • Chesapeake Bay: The Chesapeake Bay is a short drive away and offers excellent opportunities for boating, fishing, and wildlife watching.
  • Assateague Island National Seashore: This pristine national park is just over an hour’s drive from Laurel and is home to wild ponies and beautiful beaches.

Conclusion

Laurel, Delaware, is a charming town with plenty to offer visitors interested in history, nature, and outdoor recreation. With numerous museums, hiking and biking routes, delicious cuisine, and charming accommodation options, it’s no surprise that Laurel is becoming an increasingly popular destination for travelers looking for a unique East Coast experience.
