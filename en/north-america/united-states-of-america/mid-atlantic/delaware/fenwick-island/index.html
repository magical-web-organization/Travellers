Introduction to Fenwick Island

Fenwick Island, located in Delaware, is a beautiful coastal town that sits on the northern border of Ocean City and southern border of Bethany Beach. With a small town charm feel, Fenwick Island offers its visitors scenic views of the Atlantic Ocean, sandy beaches, diverse wildlife, and many water sports activities. The town of Fenwick Island, during its off-season fall months, experiences tranquility and allows for closer interaction with nature.

List of Places to Visit in Fenwick Island

Whether you’re planning a day trip, a weekend getaway or a week-long vacation, Fenwick Island has something for every visitor.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Fenwick Island Lighthouse is one attraction that should be on your list, as it is the second oldest lighthouse in Delaware. Visitors are allowed to climb to the top of this structure, where the topmost area affords remarkable views of Fenwick Island and the nearby Ocean City. Once done, head over to DiscoverSea Shipwreck Museum, where visitors will discover the rich maritime history of the Delaware area, and the process of a diver’s recovery of artifacts from ships sunk in the waters off the coasts of the area.

Natural Attractions of Fenwick Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers would definitely appreciate the beauty of the Fenwick Island State Park. The stunning beach at the park is perfect for swimming, surfing, sunbathing, and fishing. Other activities to enjoy in environmental Fenwick Island include paddleboarding on the bay, birdwatching, kayaking, and hiking at the nearby Assawoman Bay State Wildlife Area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fenwick Island

Hiking and biking routes are a fascinating way to see the natural beauty surrounding Fenwick Island. Head over to the Discover Sea Shipwreck Museum, where a hiker can find an incredible trail running along the wetlands behind the museum that leads to a magnificent view of Little Assawoman Bay. The Fenwick Island State Park also offers an excellent biking route that traverses from Bethany beach to Fenwick Island and quite close to the lighthouse. Most hotels in the area offer bicycle rentals, making it easier for visitors to enjoy cycling.

Best Things to Do in Fenwick Island: Activities and Tours

Fenwick Island has visitors covered with different activities ranging from amusement parks, water sports, or just chilling at the beach, to name a few. What’s more, there are fantastic nature tours to experience in Fenwick Island. Take a tour of the salt marshes and learn about the area’s diverse and fascinating wildlife population. You could also sail through the Ocean City shoreline taking in the beautiful coastal views. Those interested in watersports, like jet skiing and surfing, can head to the beaches, where the wind and waves are perfect for those activities.

Food and Drink Scene of Fenwick Island

Being a coastal city, Fenwick Island’s main dish is seafood, with some restaurants offering fresh-caught crab.

Must-Try Dishes

You can never go wrong with seafood in Fenwick Island, and the following dishes are a must-try:

  • Crab cakes
  • Clam chowder
  • Fried clam strips
  • Lobster rolls

Local Beverages

Some of the local drinks include:

  • Dogfish Head beer
  • Nassau Valley Vineyards Wine

Where to Stay in Fenwick Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The hotels and beach resorts are inexpensive and provide quality accommodation for visitors. Visitors can enjoy the scenic views provided by the balconies and kitchens. Some great places to stay include the Fenwick Inn, Seaside Inn & Suites, and Roger’s Motel.

What to Buy in Fenwick Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Fenwick Island is famous for its lighthouses and shipwrecks, and visitors can get replicas of shipwreck digs or lighthouse models as souvenirs to take back home. Other souvenirs include custom-made jewelry of the local shells or fish or even handmade pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of Fenwick Island

The people of Fenwick Island are known to be hospitable and friendly, and visitors are advised to be polite and considerate.

Best Time to Visit Fenwick Island

The perfect time to visit Fenwick Island is during the off-season from September to April, as the beaches are less crowded, and hotel prices are more affordable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fenwick Island

Do:

  • Respect nature and wildlife.
  • Visit the Fenwick Island Lighthouse.
  • Enjoy the seafood.

Don’t:

  • Litter.
  • Disturb the wildlife.
  • Leave without exploring the beaches.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fenwick Island

Fenwick Island is a very safe destination, however, visitors should always be cautious around water bodies, especially if undertaking activities such as swimming, kayaking, or surfing. Visitors should also watch out for scam artists and street crime.

Health in Fenwick Island

Medical facilities in Fenwick Island are of high quality. However, it is advisable to take precautions against COVID-19, carrying masks and observing social distancing guidelines.

Getting to Fenwick Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Fenwick Island

There are two airports nearby, Salisbury-Ocean City Wicomico Regional Airport, Maryland, and Philadelphia International Airport, Pennsylvania.

Bus Stations Near Fenwick Island

The Greyhound Bus services the city, and with its proximity to Ocean City, visitors can also take the OC bus to get to Fenwick Island.

Other Options

Visitors can lease a car from any international airport, and it should take them up to 2 hours to get to Fenwick Island. Visitors can also hire a cab or Uber to take them to and from the airport.

How to Get Around in Fenwick Island

Most visitors can walk around the town as the streets are pedestrian-friendly. There are, however, rental cars, taxis, buses as well as bike rentals available for visitors who prefer not to walk.

Where to Go Next from Fenwick Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ocean City, Maryland

Ocean City is famous for its beach, boardwalk, and amusement parks,

Lewes, Delaware

Lewes is known for its history and unique architecture, with many old buildings declared as National Historic Landmarks.

Conclusion

Fenwick Island offers visitors a unique, relaxed, and enjoyable atmosphere, making it a perfect destination for visitors looking to explore the natural beauty of Delaware and its rich cultural history. Following this guide, visitors can maximize their stay in this beautiful coastal town.
