Introduction to Newark

Newark is a charming city located in Delaware, in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States of America. This city boasts a rich cultural heritage, fascinating history, and breathtaking natural beauty. Newark offers a plethora of exciting activities and attractions for visitors of all ages. Whether you are a nature lover, history buff, foodie, or adventurer, Newark has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Newark

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • University of Delaware - This university is the pride of Newark and offers a glimpse into the city’s educational advancements.
  • Iron Hill Museum and Science Center - Discover the rich history of the region in this science center and museum showcasing exhibits on the history and environment of the area.
  • Newark Historical Society - Learn about the history and heritage of Newark through various exhibits and interactive displays.

Natural Attractions of Newark: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • White Clay Creek State Park - Enjoy the great outdoors and explore over 3,600 acres of nature. Hike, bike, camp, or picnic amidst stunning vistas and natural beauty.
  • Middle Run Valley Natural Area - Discover the natural beauty of Delaware by exploring over 850 acres of parks and natural preserves. Hiking, biking, bird watching, horseback riding, and fishing are popular activities here.
  • Newark Reservoir - Take a scenic stroll around the reservoir or go fishing in this tranquil and serene environment.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Newark

Newark boasts some of the best hiking and biking trails that lead through the picturesque landscapes and scenic vistas. Some of the popular hike/bike routes in Newark are:

  • Pomeroy Rail Trail - A scenic track with fewer inclines and perfect for an easy-going bike ride or hike.
  • Cooch’s Bridge Trail - A historical path, which played a significant role in the Revolutionary War. This trail is best for moderate-level bikers and hikers.
  • Iron Hill Trail System - A moderately difficult mountain bike trail, featuring a mix of hills and flats, along with stunning views.

Best Things to Do in Newark: Activities and Tours

  • Newark Farmers’ Market - Go shopping for fresh produce and sample local delicacies at this vibrant market held in downtown Newark every Sunday.
  • Newark Symphony Orchestra - Experience the magical ambiance of a live symphonic performance with Newark’s oldest and most accomplished orchestra.
  • Bridgewater Channel Kayak Tour - Paddle down the scenic channel and enjoy a guided tour of this natural marvel amidst the peace and tranquility of the surroundings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Newark

Day 1 - Begin your day by visiting the University of Delaware and taking a stroll through the stunning, historic campus. End the day with a delicious meal at one of the city’s top restaurants.

Day 2 - Spend the day exploring the Iron Hill Museum and Science Center and then the White Clay Creek State Park. Hike, bike, or enjoy a picnic amidst stunning vistas and natural beauty.

Day 3 - Explore the history and heritage of Newark by visiting the Newark Historical Society and take a guided tour. Top off your day by kayaking on the Bridgewater Channel.

Day 4 - Take a scenic drive around the local vineyards at the Delaware Wine and Ale Trail, and take in the beautiful rolling hills and lush greenery of the area.

Day 5 - Head to Main Street and explore the many local shops, eateries, and nightlife options available in the area.

Day 6 - Take the family to the Delaware Children’s Museum and enjoy interactive exhibits and activities for children of all ages.

Day 7 - Spend the day at the nearby beaches, such as Rehoboth Beach, which is about a two-hour drive from Newark. Enjoy the sun, sand, surf, and local seafood cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Newark

Newark has a vibrant food and drink scene that reflects the city’s diversity and multiculturalism. Some must-try dishes and local beverages are:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Blue Crab Cakes - A Delaware specialty, these crab cakes are made with fresh crab meat, seasoned with old bay and breaded to perfection.
  • Peach Pie - Delaware is known for its juicy and flavorful peaches, which make a delightful summer pie.

Local Beverages

  • Dogfish Head Craft Brewery - Famous for their adventurous IPAs and off-centered ales.
  • Wine from Nassau Valley Vineyards - A local winery that offers a taste of some of the best wines made in the region.

Where to Stay in Newark: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Newark has a range of accommodation options catering to all budgets and preferences. Some of the top places to stay in Newark are:

  • Embassy Suites by Hilton Newark - A luxury hotel with excellent amenities, spacious rooms, and a prime location.
  • Courtyard Newark-University of Delaware - A modern hotel with comfortable rooms, excellent customer service, and proximity to the university.
  • Homewood Suites by Hilton Newark-Wilmington South - A budget-friendly hotel with excellent facilities and services.

What to Buy in Newark: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • University of Delaware Apparel and Merchandise - Grab a souvenir or two from the university’s extensive range of apparel and merchandise.
  • Local Cheese at Fifer Orchards - Pick up some artisanal cheese from this famous orchard, along with various other tasty treats.

Local Culture and Customs of Newark

Newark has a friendly and open culture, welcoming visitors with open arms. The locals are engaging, and the city is known for its deeply-rooted heritage and traditions. Visitors should be respectful of the city’s customs and treat the locals with kindness and courtesy.

Best Time to Visit Newark

The best time to visit Newark is between the months of May and October when the weather is pleasant and perfect for outdoor activities. The city is teeming with life in the summer months, with plenty of fun activities, festivals, and events taking place.

Do’s and Don’ts of Newark

Do:

  • Do be respectful of the city’s history and heritage.
  • Do enjoy the nature and beautiful landscapes of the region.
  • Do try the local seafood and craft beer.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in the parks and nature reserves.
  • Don’t be disrespectful to the locals.
  • Don’t forget to book accommodation in advance during peak seasons.

Safety and Health

Safety in Newark

Newark is a safe city with a low crime rate. However, visitors should take the usual precautions, such as staying in well-lit areas at night and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Newark

Delaware has a humid subtropical climate, with warm summers and cold winters. Visitors should hydrate well and wear sunscreen when outdoor activities, along with keeping warm in winter months.

Getting to Newark: Transportation Options and Logistics

Newark is well-connected to other parts of the country via air, bus, and car. Some popular transportation options are:

Airports Near Newark

  • Philadelphia International Airport (PHL) - 28 miles
  • Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport (BWI) - 80 miles
  • New Castle Airport (ILG) - 7 miles

Bus Stations Near Newark

  • Greyhound Bus Terminal
  • Amtrak Wilmington Station

Other Options

  • Driving: Newark is located off of Interstate 95.
  • Uber/Lyft
  • Bicycle rentals (through University of Delaware Bike Share program)

How to Get Around in Newark

Visitors can get around the city via:

  • Taxi/Uber/Lyft services
  • Rental cars
  • Bike rentals (UD Bike Share program)
  • Public transportation (DART bus service)

Where to Go Next from Newark: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some popular nearby destinations and excursions from Newark are:

  • Wilmington, Delaware - An eclectic mix of art, culture, and history offering exciting activities, museums and gardens.
  • Philadelphia, Pennsylvania - A vibrant city with a rich heritage and a treasure trove of attractions and landmarks, located just 45 miles from Newark.
  • Washington, D.C. - The capital of the United States, presenting a stunning array of iconic landmarks and attractions just a 2.5-hour drive from Newark.

Conclusion

Newark is an ideal destination that offers visitors a chance to discover Delaware’s fascinating culture, rich history, and natural wonders. Whether you want to bike along scenic trails, visit historic sites, sample delicious local cuisine, or simply relax, Newark has everything you need for an unforgettable experience.
