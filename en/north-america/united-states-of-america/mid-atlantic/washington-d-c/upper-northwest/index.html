Introduction to Upper Northwest

Upper Northwest is a vibrant neighbourhood in Washington, D.C. that is known for its rich cultural heritage and natural beauty. It is located in the Mid-Atlantic region of the United States of America and is bordered by the Potomac River to the west, Rock Creek Park to the east, and the National Zoo to the south. Its vast array of museums, historical landmarks, and outdoor recreational areas make it a prime destination for tourists from all around the world. In this guide, we’ll help you navigate the best of what Upper Northwest has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Upper Northwest

If you’re wondering what to do in Upper Northwest, here are some must-visit attractions that should be on your itinerary.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Smithsonian’s National Museum of American History: This museum showcases the history of the United States through interactive exhibits and historical artifacts.

  • National Museum of African American History and Culture: The only national museum exploring African American life, this museum presents history through an African American lens, from slavery to Black Lives Matter, from Harriet Tubman to Oprah Winfrey

  • The John F. Kennedy Center for the Performing Arts: This world-famous venue hosts theater, dance, ballet, opera, and other performing arts activities.

Natural Attractions of Upper Northwest: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rock Creek Park: This urban oasis spans over 2,000 acres and provides a natural habitat for diverse wildlife. With over 32 miles of hiking trails and numerous picnic areas, it is a favorite spot for locals and tourists alike.

  • The National Zoo: The zoo is home to over 2,000 animals and more than 300 species. It boasts of having a Giant Panda Habitat and a variety of exhibits on animal behavior.

  • The Arboretum: The National Arboretum is spread over 446 acres and is a research garden and building in Northeast Washington, D.C. offering visitors everything from bonsai trees to a small red rock garden diorama.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Upper Northwest

If you’re looking for some outdoor adventures in Upper Northwest, there are plenty of opportunities to get moving. The trails in Rock Creek Park are great for hikers and bikers of all levels. The Glover Archbold Park Trail is another popular hiking route that runs for three miles through the lush forests of Upper Northwest. You can also rent bikes from Capital Bikeshare to explore the area on two wheels.

Best Things to Do in Upper Northwest: Activities and Tours

  • Evening Monument Tour: This tour takes you around the city after sunset with a chance to see illuminated landmarks including the Washington Monument, Lincoln Memorial and Jefferson Memorial.

  • History of Chocolate Tour: Discover the rich history of chocolate as you visit some of the most famous chocolatiers in Upper Northwest.

  • Cycling in Tour of the National Mall and Monuments: Take in the sights of the National Mall and Monuments from your bike and get up close to DC’s famous skyline and monuments.

Week-Long Itinerary for Upper Northwest

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Upper Northwest:

Day 1: Visit the National Zoo in the morning, have a picnic lunch in Rock Creek Park, and take a guided evening monument tour.

Day 2: Visit the National Museum of African American History and Culture in the morning, have lunch at one of the many eateries in Upper Northwest, and explore the Dupont Circle area in the evening.

Day 3: Take a bike tour of the National Mall and Monuments in the morning, have lunch in Georgetown, and explore the galleries and boutiques of the Georgetown neighborhood in the afternoon.

Day 4: Hike Glover Archbold Park Trail in the morning, grab lunch at one of the many restaurants in Glover Park, and visit the Embassy Row in the evening.

Day 5: Visit the Museum of American History in the morning, have lunch at one of the many local eateries, and visit the Kennedy Center for the Performing Arts in the evening.

Day 6: Visit the United States Botanic Garden in the morning, have lunch at one of the many dining options in the Southwest Waterfront neighborhood, and explore the H Street Corridor in the evening.

Day 7: Take a food tour of the Upper Northwest neighborhood in the morning, spend the afternoon at the National Mall, and have dinner at one of the many restaurants in Chinatown.

Food and Drink Scene of Upper Northwest

No trip to Upper Northwest is complete without trying its famous cuisine. Here’s what you shouldn’t miss:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maryland Crab Cakes: These cakes are a local favorite made with lump crab meat, Old Bay seasoning, and a mix of breadcrumbs and mayonnaise.

  • Ethiopian cuisine: Enjoy a variety of Ethiopian dishes including injera, a spongy flatbread, and meat or vegetable stews flavored with spices.

Local Beverages

  • The Paloma Cocktail: Try this refreshing cocktail made with tequila, fresh grapefruit juice and soda water.

  • DC Brau Beer: The neighborhood is home to one of the city’s most famous breweries. Try their traditional India Pale Ale, The Corruption, or Citizen Belgian.

Where to Stay in Upper Northwest: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Line Hotel: Striking the perfect balance between industrial-inspired design and modern luxury, this hotel boasts of vibrant decor and a range of amenities.

  • The American Guest House: This quaint B&B situated in the historic Embassy Row is the perfect place for a cozy and homely stay.

  • HI Washington DC hostel: With its chic interiors and modern amenities, this hostel is perfect for budget travelers.

What to Buy in Upper Northwest: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • A Washington D.C. Map: A great souvenir to remember your trip a concise collection of the District’s monuments, museums, attractions and neighborhoods.

  • Artisanal Chocolate and Coffee: There are plenty of chocolatiers in Upper Northwest including the famous Co Co. Sala. You’ll also find local and unique coffee blends like Compass Coffee.

Local Culture and Customs of Upper Northwest

When visiting Upper Northwest, it’s essential to respect local customs and culture. The area is diverse and inclusive, and the people are warm and welcoming. Tipping is customary, with 20% usually expected in most restaurants.

Best Time to Visit Upper Northwest

The best time to visit Upper Northwest is during the Spring and Fall. The weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller. Summers can be hot and muggy, while winters are cold with occasional snowfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Upper Northwest

Do:

  • Visit the National Zoo

  • Rent a bike and explore Rock Creek Park

  • Try local cuisine

Don’t:

  • Wear inappropriate clothing while visiting places of worship

  • Be late for appointments

  • Litter or vandalize public property

Safety and Health

Safety in Upper Northwest

Upper Northwest is generally safe, but it’s still essential to be aware of pickpocketing and bag-snatching especially in crowded tourist areas. It’s also necessary to adhere to traffic rules and stay away from areas that seem unsafe.

Health in Upper Northwest

It’s advisable to get vaccinated before your trip and also carry appropriate medications in case of an emergency. The neighborhood has several pharmacies and health care facilities in case of any health emergencies.

Getting to Upper Northwest: Transportation Options and Logistics

Upper Northwest is easily accessible from any part of Washington, D.C. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Upper Northwest

The nearest major airport is the Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport, which is about 15 miles away. Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport and Washington Dulles International Airport are also within driving distance.

Bus Stations Near Upper Northwest

The Metrobus has many routes that pass through Upper Northwest, making it an affordable option.

Other Options

Ride-sharing like Uber and Lyft are popular options too. You can also rent a car and drive yourself.

How to Get Around in Upper Northwest

The neighborhood is easily accessible by foot, bike, or public transport. Buses and the Metro Subway can get you to all major attractions in the area quickly and affordably. Capital Bikeshare also has many rental stations across Upper Northwest if you’re looking for a more eco-friendly way to get around.

Where to Go Next from Upper Northwest: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Georgetown: A charming historic neighborhood located 15 minutes away from Upper Northwest, known for its tree-lined streets, prestigious university, and lively nightlife.

  • Arlington National Cemetery: A moving and historic site dedicated to honoring American service members. It’s located approximately 20 minutes away from Upper Northwest.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a solo traveler or with family or friends, Upper Northwest has something for everyone. With its rich cultural heritage, natural scenery, and world-class museums, this neighborhood is the perfect place to experience the best of what Washington D.C. has to offer.
