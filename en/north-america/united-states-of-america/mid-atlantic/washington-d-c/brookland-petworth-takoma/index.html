Introduction to Brookland-Petworth-Takoma

Brookland-Petworth-Takoma is a diverse and vibrant neighborhood located in Washington D.C. The area is known for its rich history, beautiful architecture, and local charm. It offers visitors a unique blend of cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and culinary delights. Whether you’re a history buff, foodie, or nature lover, Brookland-Petworth-Takoma has something for you.

List of Places to Visit in Brookland-Petworth-Takoma

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Basilica of the National Shrine of the Immaculate Conception: This is the largest Catholic church in the United States and a National Shrine. The church features beautiful Byzantine-Romanesque architecture, massive marble columns, and the world’s largest collection of contemporary ecclesiastical art.

  • President Lincoln’s Cottage: President Lincoln used this cottage as a soldier’s home during the Civil War. Visitors can take guided tours of the site and learn about the history of the Civil War and Lincoln’s presidency.

  • National Museum of Women in the Arts: This museum features a diverse collection of art that represents women’s contributions to various fields. It is the only museum in the world dedicated solely to women’s art.

Natural Attractions of Brookland-Petworth-Takoma: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rock Creek Park: This park spans across 1,800 acres and features hiking trails, biking paths, picnic areas, and a nature center. Visitors can explore the park’s woods, streams, and wildlife, and enjoy sweeping views of the city.

  • Franciscan Monastery: This monastery boasts beautiful gardens that mimic those of the Holy Land. Visitors can also enjoy guided tours of the church, its catacombs, and the outdoor stations of the cross.

  • Upshur Street Murals: This street is home to a vibrant collection of murals created by local artists. Visitors can take a self-guided tour or book a guided tour to learn more about the murals and the stories behind them.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Brookland-Petworth-Takoma

Brookland-Petworth-Takoma offers several hiking and biking routes that showcase the area’s natural beauty and cultural attractions. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Metropolitan Branch Trail: This 8-mile trail connects Union Station in D.C. to Silver Spring, Maryland. It features beautiful views of the city and several historic landmarks.

  • Rock Creek Park Trails: These trails span across the park and offer hiking, biking, and horseback riding opportunities. Visitors can also explore the park’s nature center, planetarium, and golf course.

Best Things to Do in Brookland-Petworth-Takoma: Activities and Tours

  • Brookland Arts Walk: This self-guided tour features several art studios, galleries, and public murals throughout the neighborhood. Visitors can meet local artists and learn about their work.

  • Guided Food Tours: Visitors can book food tours that take them through the neighborhood’s diverse culinary scene. They can sample dishes from local restaurants, cafes, and food trucks, and learn about the cultures behind the foods.

Week-Long Itinerary for Brookland-Petworth-Takoma

Day 1: Visit the Basilica of the National Shrine of the Immaculate Conception and explore the surrounding neighborhood, including Monroe Street Market and Brookland Farmers Market.

Day 2: Take a guided tour of President Lincoln’s Cottage, followed by a hike in Rock Creek Park.

Day 3: Visit the National Museum of Women in the Arts and enjoy dinner at one of the many local restaurants on Upshur Street.

Day 4: Explore the Franciscan Monastery’s gardens, followed by a guided tour of the murals on Upshur Street.

Day 5: Take a bike tour of the area’s historic landmarks, including the Takoma Park Historic District and Walter Reed Army Medical Center.

Day 6: Take a guided food tour of the neighborhood, followed by a visit to one of the local breweries.

Day 7: Enjoy a day trip to Annapolis, Maryland, and explore the city’s historic waterfront, museums, and restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Brookland-Petworth-Takoma

Must-Try Dishes

  • Half Smoke: This is a local specialty, consisting of a spicy sausage served on a roll with mustard, onions, and chili.

  • Mumbo Sauce: This is a sweet and tangy sauce commonly served with chicken wings and other fried foods.

  • Ethiopian Cuisine: Brookland-Petworth-Takoma is home to several Ethiopian restaurants, offering traditional dishes like injera, a flatbread served with meat and vegetable stews.

Local Beverages

  • DC Brau Brewing Company: This local brewery offers a variety of craft beers, including the popular “The Public” American Pale Ale.

  • Cotton & Reed Distillery: This distillery specializes in rum, and visitors can take guided tours and sample the different varieties.

Where to Stay in Brookland-Petworth-Takoma: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Brookland Inn: This locally-owned bed and breakfast offers charming rooms and a warm, welcoming atmosphere.

  • Holiday Inn Express: This hotel is located just minutes from the National Mall and offers modern amenities and convenient access to public transportation.

  • Hostelling International: This hostel offers affordable accommodations for travelers, including private rooms and dormitories.

What to Buy in Brookland-Petworth-Takoma: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Crafts: Several local shops and markets offer handmade crafts like pottery, jewelry, and textiles.

  • Vintage Clothing: The area is home to several vintage clothing stores, offering unique and trendy finds.

Local Culture and Customs of Brookland-Petworth-Takoma

  • Community Events: The neighborhood hosts several community events throughout the year, including the annual Brookland Arts Walk and the Takoma Park Jazz Festival.

  • Local Music Scene: The neighborhood has a vibrant local music scene, with several venues and performance spaces hosting live music events throughout the week.

Best Time to Visit Brookland-Petworth-Takoma

The best time to visit Brookland-Petworth-Takoma is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and comfortable. The neighborhood can be busy during the summer months, with tourists visiting the national monuments and museums. Winter can be cold, but the holiday season brings festive markets and events to the area.

Do’s and Don’ts of Brookland-Petworth-Takoma

  • Respect the Locals: The neighborhood is home to a diverse group of residents, so it’s important to be respectful and mindful of their customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Brookland-Petworth-Takoma

The neighborhood is generally safe, but visitors should still be vigilant and aware of their surroundings. The usual precautions apply, such as avoiding dark alleys at night and not leaving valuables in plain sight. It’s also a good idea to keep a close eye on your belongings in crowded areas.

Health in Brookland-Petworth-Takoma

The neighborhood has several medical facilities and clinics, including the Washington Hospital Center and Kaiser Permanente. Visitors should also be aware of the local health risks, such as ticks in Rock Creek Park during the summer months.

Getting to Brookland-Petworth-Takoma: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Brookland-Petworth-Takoma

The closest airport to Brookland-Petworth-Takoma is Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport (DCA), located about 8 miles away. Other options include Dulles International Airport (IAD), located about 30 miles away, and Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport (BWI), located about 35 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Brookland-Petworth-Takoma

The neighborhood is serviced by several bus lines, including Metrobus and the DC Circulator. The nearest bus station is the Brookland-CUA Metro Station, located on the Red Line.

Other Options

Visitors can also take the Amtrak train to Union Station, located about 4 miles away from the neighborhood. Several car rental companies also operate in the area, including Avis and Enterprise.

How to Get Around in Brookland-Petworth-Takoma

The neighborhood is fairly walkable, and visitors can easily explore the area on foot. The area is also serviced by several public transportation options, including the Metrobus and the DC Circulator. Bike rentals are also available throughout the area, and visitors can take advantage of the many bike lanes and trails in the neighborhood.

Where to Go Next from Brookland-Petworth-Takoma: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Georgetown: This historic neighborhood is located just a few miles away and features charming shops, restaurants, and historic landmarks.

  • National Harbor: This waterfront development is located about 10 miles away and features hotels, shops, and restaurants, as well as a Ferris wheel and other attractions.

Conclusion

Brookland-Petworth-Takoma is a unique and charming neighborhood located in Washington D.C. With its rich history, diverse culinary scene, and outdoor adventures, it’s the perfect destination for travelers looking for a one-of-a-kind experience. Whether you’re exploring the local museums and monuments, hiking through Rock Creek Park, or sampling the local cuisine, Brookland-Petworth-Takoma has something for everyone.
