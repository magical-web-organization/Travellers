Georgetown is one of the most historic neighborhoods in Washington, D.C., located on the Potomac River waterfront. This charming neighborhood is known for its well-preserved architecture, boutique shopping, and high-end restaurants. Whether you’re a history buff, a foodie, or an outdoor enthusiast, there’s plenty to explore in Georgetown.

List of Places to Visit in Georgetown

Here are some of the top places to visit in Georgetown:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Dumbarton Oaks: A historic estate and garden located in the heart of Georgetown, which dates back to the early 19th century.
  • Old Stone House: A 200-year-old house built in 1765 that’s now a museum.
  • The Georgetown University: One of the oldest Catholic and Jesuit higher education institutions in the United States. It’s also home to the Georgetown University Museum, which houses an impressive collection of rare books, manuscripts, and historical artifacts.
  • National Museum of American Jewish Military History: A museum that honors American Jewish service members and focuses on the contributions of Jews to the American military.

Natural Attractions of Georgetown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The C&O Canal: A National Historical Park that covers 184.5 miles between Washington, D.C. and Cumberland, Maryland. The Georgetown portion of the canal is a great place to take a bike ride or a walk along the water.
  • Rock Creek Park: A stunning park spanning 1,800 acres with hiking trails, horseback riding, and picnic areas.
  • The Potomac River: Georgetown is situated right on the Potomac River, so don’t miss the opportunity to take a boat ride or a kayak tour.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Georgetown

If you’re looking for an outdoor adventure, Georgetown offers plenty of hiking and biking routes. Here are some of the best:

  • The Capital Crescent Trail: A popular bike path that runs from Georgetown to Bethesda, Maryland.
  • The Chesapeake & Ohio Canal National Historical Park: Offers over 180 miles of hiking, biking, and horseback riding along the canal from Georgetown to Cumberland, Maryland.
  • Theodore Roosevelt Island: A great place for hiking and walking trails that is only accessible by a pedestrian bridge from Georgetown.

Best Things to Do in Georgetown: Activities and Tours

From exploring the historic sites to charming shops, there’s plenty to do in Georgetown. Here are some top things to do:

  • Visit the rows of charming shops and unique boutiques along M Street and Wisconsin Avenue.
  • Take a food tour of Georgetown: With world-class restaurants, an exciting food scene, and a noted bakery, this neighborhood is a must-visit for any foodie.
  • Visit the Martin Luther King Jr. Memorial to pay tribute to the civil rights leader.
  • Get the best view of the city from the recently renovated Washington Monument.
  • Embark on a riverboat cruise to enjoy the panoramic views of the Georgetown waterfront.

Week-Long Itinerary for Georgetown

If you’re planning to spend a week in Georgetown, here’s a recommended itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Georgetown

Georgetown offers a variety of options when it comes to its food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • The Original Georgetown Cupcake: This bakery is the birthplace of the popular cupcake craze. Try their classic flavors like Vanilla Birthday and Red Velvet.
  • Baked & Wired: Known for their American-style baked goods and strong coffee.
  • Good Stuff Eatery Georgetown: A popular burger and shake joint that offers mouth-watering creations such as the Prez Obama Burger and the Co. Hill Crab Cake Sandwich.

Local Beverages

  • Boozy, thirst-quenching orange crush: A local staple beverage made with freshly squeezed orange juice and vodka.
  • Washington Apple: A cocktail made with whiskey, apple schnapps, and cranberry juice.

Where to Stay in Georgetown: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Georgetown has a variety of accommodation options available, from luxurious hotels to local guesthouses. Some of the best places to stay in Georgetown are:

  • Four Seasons Washington, D.C.: This luxury hotel features an indoor pool, spa, and fitness center.
  • The Avery Georgetown: Located on Wisconsin Avenue, this hotel offers stylish and comfortable rooms with a rooftop bar.
  • The River Inn: A cozy guesthouse located on Pennsylvania Avenue, just steps away from the Georgetown waterfront.

What to Buy in Georgetown: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for some unique souvenirs and local products to bring home, Georgetown offers a variety of options. Here are some of the best places to shop:

  • The Kennedy Center Gift Shop: Stock up on unique Kennedy Center souvenirs here, including apparel, books, and other memorabilia.
  • Chaia: A popular vegetarian restaurant that also offers handcrafted corn tortillas to buy.
  • A Mano: A souvenir shop that offers handmade pottery from local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Georgetown

Georgetown is one of the oldest neighborhoods in Washington, D.C., so it’s important to be respectful of its historic sites, culture, and traditions. Many people in Georgetown are proud of their heritage, so be sure to show respect for their customs, traditions, and architecture.

Best Time to Visit Georgetown

The best time to visit Georgetown is during the spring (April to May) and fall (September to October). During these months, the weather is mild, and it’s not too crowded. The summertime can be hot and humid, while the winter months can be cold and busy with tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of Georgetown

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Georgetown:

Do:

  • Be respectful of historic landmarks: Georgetown is home to some of the oldest buildings in Washington, D.C., so it’s important to be mindful of its history.
  • Try the local food: From cupcakes to crab cakes, Georgetown has a thriving culinary scene.
  • Take a guided tour: A guided tour is a great way to learn more about Georgetown’s history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Be sure to dispose of your trash in the appropriate bins.
  • Overlook the parks and natural attractions: Georgetown is home to several beautiful parks and outdoor spaces.
  • Be afraid to try new things: Whether it’s trying a new food or exploring a different neighborhood, stepping out of your comfort zone can lead to unforgettable experiences.

Safety and Health

Safety in Georgetown

Georgetown is generally considered a safe neighborhood. However, as with all cities, it’s important to take common-sense precautions, such as being aware of your surroundings, avoiding dark or unsafe areas at night, and not leaving valuables in your car.

Health in Georgetown

There are several hospitals and medical facilities in the area, including Georgetown University Hospital and Sibley Memorial Hospital.

Getting to Georgetown: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Georgetown

The closest airport to Georgetown is Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport (DCA), located approximately 15 minutes away by car. Other airports include Dulles International Airport (IAD) and Baltimore/Washington International Thurgood Marshall Airport (BWI).

Bus Stations Near Georgetown

The Greyhound bus station is located in downtown Washington, D.C., which is approximately 10 minutes away from Georgetown by car. The Union Station is also a common transportation hub that offers bus service and train service to other cities.

Other Options

Georgetown is a compact neighborhood, so it’s easy to explore on foot. However, some other transportation options include:

  • Biking: Georgetown has several bike rental stations available for visitors.
  • Metro: The closest metro station is Foggy Bottom, which is located a short walk from Georgetown.
  • Taxis and Rideshare: Taxis and Uber/Lyft are readily available in Georgetown.

How to Get Around in Georgetown

The neighborhood of Georgetown is relatively compact, so it’s easy to walk around and explore on foot. Biking is also a popular mode of transportation, with several rental stations available throughout the area. Taxis and rideshares are readily available, or alternatively, you can use the metro to travel to other parts of Washington, D.C.

Where to Go Next from Georgetown: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider after exploring Georgetown:

  • The National Mall: A must-visit destination with many iconic monuments, museums, and memorials.
  • Alexandria: A historic city located just across the Potomac River in Virginia.
  • Arlington Cemetery: The final resting place of over 400,000 military service members and veterans.

Conclusion

As you can see, Georgetown has plenty to offer for visitors of all interests. From historic landmarks to the thriving foodie scene, Georgetown is a must-visit destination in Washington, D.C. Be sure to explore the neighborhood’s quirky shops, natural attractions, and scenic views to make the most of your trip.
