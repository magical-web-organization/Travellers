Introduction to Kansas City

Kansas City, Missouri, is a vibrant and dynamic city with a rich history, a diverse culture, and a thriving arts scene. Known as the “City of Fountains,” Kansas City boasts over 200 fountains throughout the city, including the iconic J.C. Nichols Memorial Fountain at Country Club Plaza.

Whether you’re a first-time visitor or a seasoned traveler, there is always something new and exciting to discover in Kansas City. From world-class museums and galleries to delicious local cuisine and outdoor adventures, this city has it all.

List of Places to Visit in Kansas City

Here are some of the top places to visit in Kansas City:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National World War I Museum and Memorial: This museum is dedicated to the history and impact of World War I, and it features an extensive collection of artifacts, interactive exhibits, and educational programs.

  • Negro Leagues Baseball Museum: This museum celebrates the history of Negro League baseball and honors the players, coaches, and executives who helped to break down racial barriers in professional sports.

  • Kemper Museum of Contemporary Art: This museum showcases a diverse range of contemporary art from around the world, and it features rotating exhibits as well as a permanent collection.

Natural Attractions of Kansas City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Loose Park: This 75-acre park features a beautiful lake, walking trails, and a rose garden, and it’s a great place to relax and enjoy the outdoors.

  • Kauffman Memorial Garden: This botanical garden features a variety of plants and flowers, including a beautiful rose garden, and it’s a great place to take a stroll and enjoy the scenery.

  • Missouri Riverfront Trail: This 15-mile trail runs along the Missouri River and offers stunning views of the river and the city skyline.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kansas City

Kansas City is home to a variety of hiking and biking trails, including:

  • Katy Trail: This 240-mile trail runs from Clinton to Machens, Missouri, and it’s a popular destination for hikers, bikers, and horseback riders.

  • Swope Park Mountain Bike Trail: This trail system features over 10 miles of singletrack trails that are perfect for mountain bikers of all skill levels.

  • Blue River Parkway Trail: This 17-mile trail runs through the heart of Kansas City and offers a mix of paved and unpaved trails that are great for hikers and bikers.

Best Things to Do in Kansas City: Activities and Tours

Some of the best things to do in Kansas City include:

  • Catch a Royals or Chiefs game: Kansas City is home to two professional sports teams, the Kansas City Royals (MLB) and the Kansas City Chiefs (NFL).

  • Visit Country Club Plaza: This shopping and dining district features a mix of upscale boutiques, restaurants, and cafes, and it’s one of the most popular destinations in the city.

  • Take a KC Food Tour: Kansas City is known for its barbecue, but it also has a thriving food scene that includes everything from gourmet burgers to Latin American cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Kansas City

Kansas City is known for its delicious barbecue, but it also has a variety of other local dishes and beverages to try.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kansas City-style barbecue: This style of barbecue is known for its sweet and tangy tomato-based sauce, and it’s usually served with beef brisket, pork ribs, or burnt ends.

  • Fried chicken: Kansas City has several great fried chicken spots, including Stroud’s and J. Alexander’s.

  • Barbecue pizza: This unique pizza style features barbecue sauce instead of tomato sauce and is often topped with smoked meats and cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Boulevard Brewing Company: This local brewery produces a variety of craft beers, including the popular Tank 7 Farmhouse Ale and the Unfiltered Wheat Beer.

  • Roasterie Coffee: This local coffee roaster is known for its high-quality beans and unique roasting techniques.

  • KC Tea: This non-alcoholic beverage is a blend of ginger ale and lemon juice and is a refreshing drink on a hot summer day.

Where to Stay in Kansas City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kansas City has a variety of accommodation options for every budget, including:

  • The Fontaine: This boutique hotel is located in the heart of Country Club Plaza and features luxurious amenities and stunning views.

  • Hostelling International Kansas City: This hostel is located in the downtown area and offers affordable dorm-style rooms and private rooms with shared bathrooms.

  • Southmoreland on the Plaza: This bed and breakfast is located in the heart of Country Club Plaza and features cozy rooms and a delicious breakfast.

What to Buy in Kansas City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home from Kansas City include:

  • Barbecue sauce: Kansas City-style barbecue sauce is a popular souvenir, and you can find it at local barbecue restaurants and specialty food stores.

  • Local art: Kansas City has a thriving arts scene, and you can find a variety of local art and crafts at galleries and studio shops throughout the city.

  • Boulevard beer: Boulevard Brewing Company is one of the most popular breweries in Kansas City, and their craft beers make a great souvenir or gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Kansas City

Kansas City has a friendly and welcoming culture, and visitors are often struck by the city’s Midwestern hospitality. It’s common to strike up a conversation with strangers, and locals are known for their friendly and helpful demeanor.

Best Time to Visit Kansas City

The best time to visit Kansas City is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the city is less crowded. Summer can be hot and humid, and winter can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kansas City

Do:

  • Try the barbecue: Kansas City is known for its barbecue, and it’s a must-try when visiting the city.

  • Visit the fountains: Kansas City has over 200 fountains throughout the city, and they’re a beautiful sight to see.

  • Take a brewery tour: Kansas City is home to several great breweries, and taking a tour is a great way to sample local beers.

Don’t:

  • Forget your sunscreen: The sun can be strong in Kansas City, especially in the summer, so make sure to protect your skin.

  • Speed: The speed limit in Kansas City is strictly enforced, so make sure to obey the speed limit signs.

  • Leave your valuables in the car: Like any city, Kansas City has some areas that are less safe than others, so it’s best to leave your valuables at home or in a secure location.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kansas City

Kansas City is generally a safe city, but like any city, it’s important to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings. It’s best to avoid walking alone at night in certain areas, and make sure to keep your valuables in a secure location.

Health in Kansas City

Kansas City has several hospitals and medical clinics, and visitors can expect to receive quality medical care if needed. It’s also a good idea to pack any necessary medications and to bring a copy of your medical records if you have any pre-existing conditions.

Getting to Kansas City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kansas City

  • Kansas City International Airport (MCI): Located 15 miles northwest of downtown Kansas City, this airport serves several major airlines and offers flights to destinations throughout the United States and beyond.

Bus Stations Near Kansas City

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in the downtown area, this station offers daily bus service to destinations throughout the United States.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Kansas City has several car rental companies located at the airport and throughout the city.

  • Uber/Lyft: Both Uber and Lyft operate in Kansas City, and they’re a convenient way to get around the city.

How to Get Around in Kansas City

Kansas City has several transportation options, including:

  • Bus: The Kansas City Area Transportation Authority (KCATA) operates several bus routes throughout the city.

  • Light rail: The Kansas City Streetcar operates a free light rail system that runs through the downtown area.

  • Uber/Lyft: Both Uber and Lyft operate in Kansas City, and they’re a convenient way to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from Kansas City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore the surrounding area, here are some great nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Lawrence, Kansas: This college town is located about 45 minutes west of Kansas City and features a vibrant arts scene and several great restaurants and bars.

  • Lake of the Ozarks: Located about 2 hours southeast of Kansas City, this popular summer destination features a lake, several golf courses, and a variety of outdoor activities.

  • Ozark National Forest: Located about 4 hours south of Kansas City, this scenic national forest features hiking trails, waterfalls, and stunning vistas.

Conclusion

Kansas City is a vibrant and dynamic city with something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in art, history, outdoor adventures, or delicious local cuisine, this city has it all. So pack your bags and get ready for an unforgettable trip to Kansas City!
