Introduction to Ottawa

Located in Eastern Kansas, in Great Plains of the United States of America, Ottawa is a charming city that offers plenty of opportunities for cultural exploration and outdoor adventure. This guide will help you discover the top sights and experiences in Ottawa, from museums and historical landmarks to hiking and biking trails. Explore the local food and drink scene, learn about the local culture and customs, and discover practical information on transportation and logistics for your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Ottawa

Here’s a quick overview of the top places to visit in Ottawa:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Old Depot Museum
  • Franklin County Courthouse
  • Franklin County Historical Society and Museum
  • Ottawa University Heritage Center Museum
  • John Brown Museum State Historic Site

Natural Attractions of Ottawa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wallace Park
  • Prairie Spirit Trail State Park
  • Lake Pomona
  • Ottawa City Park
  • Ottawa Memorial Auditorium

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ottawa

Ottawa offers several scenic hiking and biking trails that are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the top routes:

  • Prairie Spirit Trail State Park - a 51-mile trail that runs from Ottawa to Iola, passing through scenic prairies, lakes, and fields.
  • Ottawa Riverwalk Trail - a 2.6-mile paved path that follows the scenic Marais des Cygnes River, offering beautiful views of the river and surrounding nature.
  • Lake Pomona Trail - a 3-mile hiking and biking trail around Lake Pomona, offering scenic views of the lake and surrounding woods.

Best Things to Do in Ottawa: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or outdoor adventure, Ottawa has plenty to offer. Here are some of the top things to do in Ottawa:

  • Visit the Old Depot Museum and Franklin County Courthouse to learn about the history of the region.
  • Explore the Prairie Spirit Trail State Park, offering a scenic cycling and hiking trail.
  • Enjoy a picnic or relax by the lake at Ottawa City Park.
  • Go fishing, boating or swimming at Lake Pomona.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ottawa

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Ottawa:

Day 1: Start your trip by visiting the Old Depot Museum and Franklin County Courthouse to learn about the history of the region.

Day 2: Spend the day exploring the Prairie Spirit Trail State Park, offering a scenic cycling and hiking trail.

Day 3: Discover the picturesque Lake Pomona area and enjoy a picnic or fishing activity there.

Day 4: Take a day trip to Kansas City, which is located an hour’s drive from Ottawa, and indulge in the city’s art and culture scene.

Day 5: Stroll along the Ottawa Riverwalk Trail and experience its scenic beauty.

Day 6: Visit Ottawa City Park, and chill with the lake view while relishing in outdoor activities.

Day 7: Explore the John Brown Museum State Historic Site and Ottawa University Heritage Center Museum and end your trip by sampling the local food at Ottawa’s eateries.

Food and Drink Scene of Ottawa

Ottawa’s food scene offers plenty of options for travelers. From classic American comfort food to authentic Mexican cuisine, here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Ottawa:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Prairie Fire Pizza - a local favorite topped with spicy sausage, jalapenos, and pepperoni.
  • Fried Chicken - served at one of the city’s homestyle restaurants.
  • Beef Brisket - a traditional smoked dish typically served with coleslaw.
  • Tacos - tasty and fresh tacos available in several varieties, including chicken, pork, and beef.

Local Beverages

  • Kansas Beer - a refreshing and crisp beer made with local ingredients.
  • Margarita - a classic tequila-based cocktail made with fresh lime juice.
  • Sweet Tea - a classic American drink perfect for sipping on hot summer afternoons.

Where to Stay in Ottawa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From cozy bed and breakfasts to chain hotels, Ottawa offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget. Here are some of the top places to stay in Ottawa:

  • Days Inn by Wyndham Ottawa - a budget-friendly option with comfortable rooms and basic amenities.
  • Vintage Park - offering affordable accommodation in the heart of Ottawa and with easy access to nearby attractions.
  • Comfort Inn - a mid-range hotel with clean and spacious rooms and all basic amenities.
  • The Loft on Cedar - a cozy bed and breakfast with charming decor and personalized service.

What to Buy in Ottawa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home from your trip to Ottawa, consider the following options:

  • Cayenne Pepper - a popular spice made from local cayenne peppers.
  • Sunflowers - the state flower of Kansas, and harvested frequently in the region.
  • Arts and crafts - Ottawa has a vibrant arts and crafts scene with plenty of local offerings.
  • Apparel and Accessories - From street-side boutique shops to large shopping centers, Ottawa has a vast collection of cutting-edge fashion.

Local Culture and Customs of Ottawa

Ottawa is known for its friendliness and laid-back way of life. When visiting, keep in mind that:

  • Tipping is common practice, with a customary 15-20% gratuity for waitstaff being appropriate.
  • Midwestern hospitality is high; locals will more than likely greet you with a friendly wave while driving.
  • Kansas is a somewhat conservative state, and visitors are advised to dress modestly.

Best Time to Visit Ottawa

The best time to visit Ottawa is in the summer months from June to August. This is when the weather is warm and the days are long, making it the perfect time for outdoor adventures and sightseeing. Winters can be quite chilly, with snow, making it ideal for winter sports enthusiasts.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ottawa

Do:

  • Try local specialties, like Fried Chicken, Beef Brisket, and Sunflowers.
  • Explore Ottawa, by hiking or biking through the countless trails and parks.
  • Visit Ottawa’s museums and historical landmarks to learn about the region’s history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Forget to tip your waiter or service staff.
  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions.
  • Litter. Ottawa prides itself on maintaining a clean and healthy environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ottawa

Ottawa is a relatively safe city, with low levels of crime. Visitors are advised to take the same precautions as they would in any urban area, such as staying aware of their surroundings, keeping valuables out of sight, and not venturing off into desolate areas.

Health in Ottawa

Ottawa has several hospitals and medical facilities that offer quality health care. It is important for visitors to have appropriate health insurance before traveling and to take necessary precautions, such as sunscreen, protection from mosquitoes and ticks, and staying hydrated in the hot Kansas summers.

Getting to Ottawa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ottawa

The nearest major airport to Ottawa is Kansas City International Airport, which is located approximately 50 miles north of the city.

Bus Stations Near Ottawa

Greyhound bus lines services the Ottawa region.

Other Options

Several car rental agencies operate within Ottawa City.

How to Get Around in Ottawa

Ottawa is a walkable city, with plenty of trails and parks to explore on foot. Public transportation in Ottawa is limited, but visitors can rent a car or bike to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from Ottawa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore the region, consider visiting these nearby destinations and excursions from Ottawa:

  • Kansas City - the largest city in the region, offering a vibrant art and culture scene.
  • Flint Hills - a scenic area known for its rolling hills and expansive prairies.
  • Clinton Lake - a popular destination for outdoor recreation, including hiking, fishing, and boating.

Conclusion

Ottawa may not be the most well-known city in Kansas, but it certainly has a charm and beauty that is worth discovering. From its historical landmarks and museums to its outdoor adventures and scenic beauty, Ottawa offers something for everyone. Whether you’re exploring the city’s food and drink scene, learning about the local culture and customs, or embarking on outdoor adventures, Ottawa is a destination that won’t soon be forgotten.
