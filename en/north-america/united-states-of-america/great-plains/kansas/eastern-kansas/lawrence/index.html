Introduction to Lawrence

Lawrence is a quaint and charming city located in Eastern Kansas and is the sixth-largest city in the state. A bustling college town, it is home to the University of Kansas and is steeped in history, culture, and scenic beauty. Lawrence offers something for everyone, from outdoor enthusiasts and history buffs to foodies and culture vultures. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take you on a journey through the city, exploring its attractions, food and drink scene, culture, safety, and health.

List of Places to Visit in Lawrence

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The University of Kansas Natural History Museum - This museum offers a glimpse into the diversity of life on our planet with exhibits that include fossils, animals, and plants. Visitors can also learn about the history of the university.

  • The Spencer Museum of Art - This museum features over 45,000 works of art from around the world, including paintings, sculptures, prints, and photographs.

  • The Watkins Museum of History - This museum showcases the history of Douglas County, Kansas, with exhibits that include the city’s early settlers, politics, and the role Lawrence played in the Civil War.

Natural Attractions of Lawrence: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Clinton Lake State Park - This park is located on the north shore of Clinton Lake and offers activities like fishing, boating, hiking, and camping.

  • Prairie Park Nature Center - A nature preserve located in the heart of Lawrence that provides visitors with the opportunity to observe wildlife and learn about conservation.

  • Sunflower Fields - From late summer to early fall, visitors can see miles of vibrant sunflowers in full bloom. A stunning visual spectacle that shouldn’t be missed.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lawrence

  • Baldwin City Trails - This network of multi-use trails offers a scenic route through the Kansas prairie, including the popular Prairie Spirit Trail.

  • Clinton Lake Trails - The 25-mile trail system around Clinton Lake is a perfect place to explore the beauty of nature while hiking, biking, or horseback riding.

Best Things to Do in Lawrence: Activities and Tours

  • Massachusetts Street - The heart of downtown Lawrence, Massachusetts Street features history, shopping, and dining.

  • Downtown Lawrence Ghost Tour - Embark on a spooky adventure through Downtown Lawrence and learn about the city’s past.

  • Free State Brewing Company Tour - Take a tour of a local brewery and enjoy delicious craft beer.

Food and Drink Scene of Lawrence

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kansas City-style Barbecue - Pulled pork, ribs, and brisket coated in sweet and tangy barbecue sauce is a must-have.

  • Burgers and Fries - The famous Abe’s burgers are a local favorite, along with truffle fries at Dempsey’s Burger Pub.

  • Pulled Pork Mac and Cheese - A cheesy delight topped with smoky pulled pork and breadcrumbs that’s sure to satisfy your cravings.

Local Beverages

  • Free State Brewing Company - Enjoy a pint of beer at one of the oldest craft breweries in Kansas.

  • Java Break - Indulge in a cup of locally roasted coffee at this popular coffee shop in Downtown Lawrence.

  • Lawrence Beer Company - This local brewery features a rotating selection of seasonal beers and small-batch brews.

Where to Stay in Lawrence: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Eldridge Hotel - This historic hotel offers luxury accommodations in the heart of downtown Lawrence.

  • The Oread - A modern hotel located near the University of Kansas campus that offers stunning views of Lawrence and the surrounding areas.

  • Hostelling International Lawrence - A budget-friendly hostel that offers a comfortable and affordable stay for travelers.

What to Buy in Lawrence: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sunflower Seeds - A popular snack and souvenir that’s a quintessential Kansas product.

  • University of Kansas Apparel - Show your Jayhawk pride with official merchandise from the University of Kansas.

  • Ad Astra Food and Drink - A local company that produces artisanal food and drink products made with Kansas-grown ingredients.

Local Culture and Customs of Lawrence

Lawrence is a diverse and welcoming community that values creativity, art, and education. It is home to a vibrant music scene, a thriving arts community, and the University of Kansas. The locals are friendly and hospitable, and visitors are encouraged to immerse themselves in the local culture and meet new people.

Best Time to Visit Lawrence

The best time to visit Lawrence is during the fall and spring seasons when the weather is mild, and the scenery is stunning. However, Lawrence has something to offer visitors all year round, from the colorful sunflower fields in the late summer to the winter wonderland that is Clinton Lake.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lawrence

Do:

  • Explore the natural beauty of Lawrence by hiking, biking, or camping in one of the area’s beautiful state parks.

  • Visit the local markets and shops to get a taste of the local culture and cuisine.

  • Take time to learn about the city’s history and cultural significance by visiting the museums and historical places.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment - the beauty of Lawrence is something to be cherished and preserved.

  • Ignore the local customs and culture - be respectful of other people’s beliefs and way of living.

  • Drink and drive - Lawrence has strict laws against drinking and driving, and offenders will be held accountable.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lawrence

Lawrence is a safe and peaceful community. However, like any other place, visitors should take necessary precautions to ensure their safety. It is advisable to avoid walking alone at night, avoid dark, secluded areas, and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Lawrence

Lawrence has several medical facilities and hospitals, including Lawrence Memorial Hospital and The University of Kansas Health System. Visitors should take necessary precautions, such as wearing sunscreen, carrying insect repellent, and staying hydrated, especially during hot summer months.

Getting to Lawrence: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lawrence

  • Kansas City International Airport - Located approximately 62 miles from Lawrence and offers flight services from around the world.

  • Topeka Regional Airport - Located approximately 30 miles from Lawrence and offers limited flight services.

Bus Stations Near Lawrence

  • Greyhound Station - Located in downtown Lawrence and offers bus services from several destinations across the country.

Other Options

  • Amtrak Train Station - Located in downtown Lawrence and offers daily train services to several destinations across the country.

How to Get Around in Lawrence

Lawrence is a walkable city and is easy to navigate on foot. Visitors also have the option of renting a bike, hiring a taxi, or using the public transportation system, which includes buses and shuttles.

Where to Go Next from Lawrence: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kansas City - Located 30 miles from Lawrence, Kansas City is a bustling metropolis that offers several attractions, including museums, galleries, and the famous Kansas City Barbecue.

  • Topeka - Located 25 miles from Lawrence, Topeka is the state capital of Kansas and offers several impressive attractions, including the Kansas State Capitol and the Topeka Zoological Park.

Conclusion

Lawrence is a charming and vibrant city that should not be missed. From its cultural attractions and outdoor adventures to its food and drink scene and friendly locals, Lawrence has something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive tour guide has provided you with the information you need to plan your visit and make the most of your stay in this beautiful city.
