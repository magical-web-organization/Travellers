Introduction to Holton

Holton is a small city located in eastern Kansas, United States, known for its rich history and beautiful natural attractions. It is located in the heart of the Great Plains and offers visitors a unique blend of culture and outdoor adventure. Whether you are visiting Holton for the first time or have been a frequent visitor, this travel guide will provide you with all the necessary information to make your trip an unforgettable experience.

List of Places to Visit in Holton

Holton has a number of must-visit destinations for travelers. Here is a list of the most popular:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jackson County Historical Society Museum and Research Library: Learn about the history of Holton and the surrounding areas through this museum’s collection of artifacts, photographs, and exhibits.
  • Cottage House Art Gallery: This art gallery showcases the work of local artists, including paintings, pottery, and sculptures.
  • Kathleen Shaner Art Studio & Gallery: Located in the heart of downtown Holton, this studio features an array of art pieces ranging from traditional to contemporary.

Natural Attractions of Holton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Banner Creek Reservoir and Park: This park offers visitors the opportunity to hike, fish, kayak, and camp in a picturesque setting.
  • Falls Creek Park: This park boasts a beautiful waterfall and hiking trails that lead to scenic views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Prairie Band Potawatomi Reservation: Experience the rich history and culture of the Potawatomi tribe with a visit to this reservation, which features a museum, a bison range, and a casino.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Holton

Holton has plenty of hiking and biking trail options to explore the area’s beautiful scenery. Some of the best routes include:

  • Jackson County Trail: This trail spans over 5 miles and connects the towns of Holton, Circleville, and Soldier, offering scenic views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Prairie Band Potawatomi Reservation Trails: The reservation features over 10 miles of hiking trails that traverse through forested areas and end at beautiful scenic views.

Best Things to Do in Holton: Activities and Tours

  • Safari Zoological Park: This private zoo is home to over 300 animals, including tigers, lions, and bears, providing an adventurous experience.
  • Holton Country Club: This popular golf course offers visitors a challenging game within a picturesque setting.
  • Cedar Oaks Vineyard & Winery: Take a tour of this winery’s beautiful vineyards and enjoy tastings of their delicious wines.

Week-Long Itinerary for Holton

Day 1 - Explore the Jackson County Historical Society Museum and Research Library and the Cottage House Art Gallery.

Day 2 - Hike to the falls at Falls Creek Park and spend the afternoon fishing on Banner Creek Reservoir.

Day 3 - Visit the Safari Zoological Park and finish the day with a round of golf at the Holton Country Club.

Day 4 - Explore the trails at the Prairie Band Potawatomi Reservation and take in the beauty of nature.

Day 5 - Visit Kathleen Shaner Art Studio & Gallery and enjoy a tasting at Cedar Oaks Vineyard & Winery.

Day 6 - Take a day trip to visit Topeka, the capital of Kansas, and enjoy its attractions.

Day 7 - Relax and explore downtown Holton’s local shops and restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Holton

Holton offers a variety of dining options and local flavors to sample, including:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kansas City barbecue: Sample some authentic barbecue at Big Ed’s BBQ, famous for their ribs and homemade sauces.
  • Kansas-made beer and spirits: Visit 23rd Street Brewery to taste their craft beer or visit Great Lakes Distillery for locally distilled whiskey and gin.
  • German cuisine: Enjoy some sauerkraut and schnitzel at the Holton Meat Locker, which offers a wide selection of German-inspired dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Coffee: Sip on some fresh coffee at the Empire Coffee Company, a cozy local coffee shop.
  • Wine: Visit the Cedar Oaks Vineyard & Winery to try their locally produced wines.

Where to Stay in Holton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Holton has a variety of accommodation options to suit different preferences and budgets:

  • Cedar Ridge Retreat and Conference Center: Enjoy a quiet retreat in this beautiful lodge, overlooking the nearby creek.
  • Super 8 by Wyndham Holton: A budget-friendly hotel that offers basic amenities, conveniently located close to downtown Holton.
  • Circle S Ranch & Country Inn: For a unique experience, stay at this beautiful ranch located just outside of Holton.

What to Buy in Holton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Holton has a few local shops that offer unique and authentic souvenirs:

  • Holton Flower & Gift Shop: Pick up some beautiful flowers, plants, or gifts for loved ones.
  • Holton Meat Locker: Take home some locally smoked and cured meats or other delicacies.

Local Culture and Customs of Holton

Holton’s local culture is a blend of traditional and modern influences. Some things to keep in mind during your visit include:

  • Respect for the outdoors: Be sure to clean up after yourself and stay on designated trails when hiking.
  • Strong small-town community: Expect a friendly and welcoming atmosphere from the locals.

Best Time to Visit Holton

The best time to visit Holton is during the spring and fall seasons, when the weather is mild and comfortable for outdoor activities. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter may bring icy conditions and snow.

Do’s and Don’ts of Holton

Do:

  • Explore the local shops and food scene.
  • Respect the natural surroundings and wildlife.
  • Visit the Potawatomi Reservation to experience local history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the parks or on the trails.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.
  • Trespass on private land.

Safety and Health

Safety in Holton

Holton is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should take basic precautions like locking car doors and securing valuables.

Health in Holton

Travelers should bring any necessary medications and be aware of the location of nearby medical facilities.

Getting to Holton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Holton is relatively easy to get to, with a few transportation options:

Airports Near Holton

  • Kansas City International Airport (MCI): Located approximately 1.5 hours away from Holton.

Bus Stations Near Holton

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: Bus service connecting Holton to nearby cities.

Other Options

  • Car rentals: Rental cars are available at most major airports.

How to Get Around in Holton

The best way to get around Holton is by car. Rental cars are available at the Kansas City International Airport.

Where to Go Next from Holton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Holton is located in a great location, with easy access to nearby attractions:

  • Topeka, Kansas: State capital, located approximately 30 miles south of Holton.
  • Lawrence, Kansas: Home to the University of Kansas, located approximately 50 miles east of Holton.
  • Atchison, Kansas: A historic town on the Missouri River, located approximately 25 miles northeast of Holton.

Conclusion

Holton is a charming destination that appeals to travelers seeking natural beauty, history, and culture. With its various attractions, activities, and local flavors, Holton offers something for everyone, making it a worthwhile destination for your next trip.
