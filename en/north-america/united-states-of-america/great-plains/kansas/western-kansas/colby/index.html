Introduction to Colby

Located in the western part of Kansas, Colby is a charming city known for its rich history, natural beauty, and friendly locals. It’s the perfect place to visit if you’re looking for a relaxed vacation away from the hustle and bustle of the big city.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the best things to do, places to visit, and local culture of Colby. We’ll also give you tips on transportation options, where to stay, and what to eat.

Let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Colby

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Prairie Museum of Art and History: This museum is dedicated to preserving the history of the prairie region and features exhibits on pioneer life, farming, and Native American culture.
  • Thomas County Courthouse: Built-in 1905, this beautiful courthouse is listed on the National Register of Historic Places and features stunning Beaux-Arts architecture.
  • Colby Free Press Building: A historic building in downtown Colby with an impressive hand-painted mural on the side.

Natural Attractions of Colby: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Colby Aquatic Park: A fun place to spend a hot summer day, this park has a wave pool, water slides, a lazy river, and more.
  • Colby City Park: A beautiful park with a fishing pond, walking trails, playground equipment, and picnic areas.
  • Monument Rocks National Natural Landmark: A stunning natural formation of towering Chalk Pyramids, Monument Rocks offers sweeping views of the surrounding area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Colby

  • Prairie Museum Nature Trail: A short, easy nature trail that winds through the prairie landscape surrounding the museum.
  • Colby Bike Path: A 3-mile paved path that takes you through some beautiful areas of the city.
  • Castle Rock Trail: A challenging 3-mile trail that rewards hikers with incredible views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Colby: Activities and Tours

  • Colby Community College Cultural Arts Center: This venue hosts a variety of cultural events, including plays, concerts, and lectures.
  • Colby Bowl and Fun Center: A great place to have fun with family and friends, featuring bowling, arcade games, and laser tag.
  • Colby Community Fitness Center: A state-of-the-art facility with a variety of fitness equipment and classes.

Food and Drink Scene of Colby

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kansas BBQ: For a taste of authentic Kansas cuisine, head to Neal’s Bar-B-Q and try their mouthwatering BBQ beef brisket.
  • Chicken Fried Steak: A classic comfort food, enjoy a delicious Chicken Fried Steak at the Oasis Lounge and Grill.
  • Chislic: A local favorite, Chislic consists of cubed meat, usually lamb or beef, that is deep-fried and seasoned with garlic salt.

Local Beverages

  • Sunflower Wheat Beer: Try a refreshing Sunflower Wheat Beer at Colby’s only brewery, Grainfield’s Brewery.
  • Kansas Wine: Visit Oasis Lounge and Grill, and enjoy a glass of locally produced Kansas wine.

Where to Stay in Colby: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Sleep Inn & Suites: A comfortable and modern hotel with convenient amenities such as a swimming pool, a fitness center, and free breakfast.
  • Budget Lodge: A budget-friendly motel that offers clean, comfortable rooms, and free Wi-Fi.
  • Colby Motel: Another budget option with comfortable rooms and basic amenities.

What to Buy in Colby: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Native American Crafts: Stop by the Prairie Museum of Art and History’s gift shop to find locally made Native American crafts.
  • Sunflowers: Colby is known for its beautiful sunflower fields, and you can find plenty of sunflower-themed souvenirs, including postcards, magnets, and t-shirts.
  • Kansas Wine: Take home a bottle of locally produced Kansas wine as a tasty souvenir of your trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Colby

The people of Colby are friendly and welcoming to tourists. It’s important to respect the local culture and customs while visiting the city. Be sure to greet people politely and use “please” and “thank you” when interacting with locals.

Best Time to Visit Colby

The best time to visit Colby is in the fall when the weather is mild, and the leaves are changing colors. It’s also a great time to attend local events like the Thomas County Free Fair and the Colby Community College Cultural Arts Center’s Fall Festival.

Do’s and Don’ts of Colby

Do:

  • Do try Kansas BBQ and other local cuisine.
  • Do greet people politely and use “please” and “thank you”.
  • Do learn about the local history and culture by visiting museums and historical sites.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or disrespect local nature.
  • Don’t assume everyone in Kansas is conservative.
  • Don’t be afraid to ask locals for recommendations on what to do or where to eat.

Safety and Health

Safety in Colby

Colby is a safe city, with a low crime rate. However, it’s always important to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions to keep yourself and your belongings safe.

Health in Colby

Colby has a hospital and several medical clinics to ensure you have access to healthcare if needed.

Getting to Colby: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Colby

The closest airport to Colby is Denver International Airport, located about 200 miles away. You can also fly into Hays Regional Airport, which is about 70 miles from Colby.

Bus Stations Near Colby

Greyhound has a bus station in Hays and another in Goodland, both of which are about 70 miles from Colby.

Other Options

If you’re driving, Colby is easily accessible from Interstate 70 and Highway 25. Amtrak does not have a station in Colby, but you can take the train to nearby cities like Hays or Goodland.

How to Get Around in Colby

The best way to get around Colby is by car. There is limited public transportation, but you can rent a car or take a taxi if needed.

Where to Go Next from Colby: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Scott City: A small town located just 30 minutes south of Colby, Scott City is home to some beautiful parks and hiking trails.
  • Lake Scott State Park: Located about an hour west of Colby, this state park offers camping, hiking, swimming, and other outdoor activities.
  • Topeka: The state capital of Kansas is about a 4-hour drive from Colby and offers a variety of museums, historical sites, and cultural events.

Conclusion

Colby is a hidden gem in Kansas, with beautiful natural attractions, rich history, and friendly locals. From hiking and biking to the delicious local cuisine and nearby excursions, there’s something for everyone in Colby. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip, and you’ll have an unforgettable time exploring this charming city.
