Introduction to Stockton

Located in the western region of Kansas, Stockton is a small town in Rooks County with a population of 1,200 residents. Stockton has a rich history and culture which is shown through the numerous museums and historical landmarks dotted around the town. It’s also known for its picturesque natural scenery and outdoor adventures, making it a great destination for nature lovers.

List of Places to Visit in Stockton

If you’re planning a trip to Stockton, there are plenty of places you should visit. Here’s a list of top places to explore in Stockton:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Rooks County Historical Museum: Explore the history of Rooks County dating back to the 1800s through exhibits that feature Native American artifacts, agricultural tools, and vintage cars.
  • Fort Hays-Fort Dodge Trail: Follow the historic trail that once linked the military forts of Fort Hays and Fort Dodge during the Indian Wars.
  • One-Room Schoolhouse Museum: Get a glimpse of what life was like in a one-room schoolhouse in rural Kansas during the 1800s.

Natural Attractions of Stockton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Webster Reservoir: Go fishing, boating, or kayaking in the waters of Webster Reservoir, a 3,700-acre man-made lake surrounded by picturesque natural scenery.
  • Cedar Bluff State Park: Hike the scenic trails that overlook Cedar Bluff Reservoir, or swim and fish in the reservoir’s pristine waters.
  • Mount Sunflower: Drive to the highest point in Kansas at 4,039 feet above sea level and enjoy the stunning panoramic views of the surrounding Great Plains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Stockton

Stockton has plenty of trails suited for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in Stockton:

  • Prairie Trail: This 7-mile limestone trail takes you through the beautiful Flint Hills area of Kansas, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding prairie.
  • Cedar Bluff Overlook Trail: This 4-mile moderate trail winds through Cedar Bluff State Park, offering scenic overlooks of the Cedar Bluff Reservoir.
  • Webster Reservoir Nature Trail: This 1.5-mile nature trail takes you through the woods and prairies of the Webster Reservoir, offering stunning views of the surrounding natural landscape.

Best Things to Do in Stockton: Activities and Tours

There’s plenty to do in Stockton. Here are some of the best things to do in this charming Kansas town:

  • Attend a Rooks County Fair: Held annually in July, this is the perfect opportunity to experience the rural Kansas way of life, including livestock shows, carnival rides, and country music.
  • Attend a Stockton Sporting Event: Cheer on the Stockton Tigers in a high school football game or attend one of the many rodeos hosted by the town.
  • Take a Scenic Drive: Experience the rolling Flint Hills by taking a scenic drive on one of the many winding roads throughout the county.

Week-Long Itinerary for Stockton

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Stockton, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1 Explore the Rooks County Historical Museum and the One-Room Schoolhouse Museum

Food and Drink Scene of Stockton

Stockton has a vibrant food and drink scene, offering a mix of traditional Kansas cuisine and contemporary flavors.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kansas-Style BBQ: Try a plate of slow-cooked BBQ ribs or brisket, served with homemade BBQ sauce.
  • Bierocks: A traditional Kansas dish, these baked buns are filled with beef, cabbage, and onions.
  • Fried Chicken: Experience crispy and mouth-watering fried chicken that’s been made according to Stockton’s secret family recipe.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer: Visit the “Craft Brew Capital of Kansas” at Hays City Brewery and enjoy local brews such as the 1867 Wheat, the Wizard, or the Roy E. Bierocks Ale.
  • Sweet Wine: Try locally grown wine varieties like the sweet-tasting Concord grape.

Where to Stay in Stockton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Stockton has plenty of lodging options to suit any budget. Here are some of the top places to stay in Stockton:

  • Webster State Park: Set up camp at one of the campsites at Webster State Park and enjoy the beautiful natural surroundings.
  • Stockton Inn: A charming and affordable roadside motel located in the heart of Stockton.
  • Cedar Bluff Lodge: Enjoy amazing views of Cedar Bluff Reservoir from the balcony of your hotel room at Cedar Bluff Lodge.

What to Buy in Stockton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to take back home, here are some suggestions:

  • Prairie Creek Market: Shop for local Kansas products, including homemade jams, honey, and candles.
  • Freedom Gems: Browse unique jewelry made from locally sourced Kansas stones such as jasper, agate, and more.
  • Kansas-Style BBQ Sauce: Take home a bottle of Stockton’s famous BBQ sauce as a souvenir of your trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Stockton

Stockton’s culture is steeped in the traditions of rural Kansas. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to experience the town’s laid-back way of life.

Best Time to Visit Stockton

The best time to visit Stockton is in the late spring and early fall, when the weather is mild, and the fall foliage is at its peak. Summers can be hot and humid, and winters can be cold and snowy. However, the town is beautiful all year round, so plan a trip based on your personal preferences.

Do’s and Don’ts of Stockton

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Stockton:

Do:

  • Do take a scenic drive along Highway 18 and enjoy the stunning views of the Great Plains.
  • Do try a traditional Kansas dish such as bierocks or fried chicken.
  • Do pack comfortable shoes for hiking or biking along Stockton’s scenic trails.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in any of the natural areas around Stockton.
  • Don’t forget to check the weather forecast before your trip, especially if you’re planning on spending time outdoors.
  • Don’t forget to support local businesses when shopping for souvenirs or dining out.

Safety and Health

Safety in Stockton

Stockton is a safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors should still exercise caution and take common-sense safety measures, like locking doors and securing valuables.

Health in Stockton

Stockton has a small hospital and medical facilities that can handle most medical emergencies. However, it’s always a good idea to pack a basic first-aid kit when traveling, just in case.

Getting to Stockton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Stockton

The nearest airports to Stockton are:

  • Hays Regional Airport (kjhays.com)
  • Salina Regional Airport (salinaairport.com)
  • Denver International Airport (flydenver.com)

Bus Stations Near Stockton

  • Stockton Bus Stop: Serves the local bus routes to and from neighboring towns.

Other Options

  • Driving: Stockton is easily accessible by car. The main highways through town are US-183 and Kansas Highway 18.
  • Bike: For those feeling adventurous, biking is a great option to explore the surrounding wilderness.

How to Get Around in Stockton

Stockton is a small town, so it’s easy to get around on foot or by bike. Visitors can also rent a car if they need to travel longer distances.

Where to Go Next from Stockton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore the area around Stockton, here are a few nearby destinations worth a visit:

  • Lucas, Kansas: Visit the world-famous “Garden of Eden” attraction.
  • Hays, Kansas: Explore the Fort Hays State Historic Site and the Sternberg Museum of Natural History.
  • Oakley, Kansas: Go on a fossil hunting adventure at the Fick Fossil and History Museum.

Conclusion

Stockton is a charming and sleepy little town that’s worth a visit for anyone looking to explore the beauty and culture of rural Kansas. With plenty of outdoor adventures, local cuisine, and historical landmarks, there’s something here for everyone. So, pack your bags and start planning your next trip to Stockton!
