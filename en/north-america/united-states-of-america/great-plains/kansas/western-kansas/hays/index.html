Introduction to Hays

Hays, located in Western Kansas, is a charming, lesser-known city that boasts a rich history, cultural and natural attractions, and a vibrant food and drink scene. Known as the “Gateway to the West,” Hays is a hub for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and foodies alike. If you’re planning a trip to the Midwest, make sure to add Hays to your itinerary.

List of Places to Visit in Hays

Whether you’re interested in museums and historical places or outdoor adventures and scenic views, Hays has something for everyone. Here’s a list of top attractions to visit during your trip:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Hays State Historic Site: This historic site played a significant role as a supply and medical station during the American Indian Wars. Visitors can explore seven original limestone structures and learn about frontier life.

  • Sternberg Museum of Natural History: This museum houses an impressive collection of fossils and exhibits showcasing the natural history of Kansas.

  • Ellis County Historical Society Museum: Explore the local history of Ellis County through a vast collection of artifacts and exhibits on display.

Natural Attractions of Hays: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hays Aquatic Park: Cool off during the hot summer months with water slides, a lazy river, and a wave pool.

  • Smokey Hill River: Kayak or paddleboard down the Smokey Hill River and take in the scenic views of the surrounding Great Plains.

  • Chestnut Street District: Take a stroll through this picturesque neighborhood and admire the historic architecture and charming gardens.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hays

For those looking to explore the outdoors on foot or bike, Hays has an abundance of hiking and biking trails to choose from. Here are a few of the most popular:

  • Frontier Park Trail: This 1.5-mile trail leads to the overlook at the top of the park, providing spectacular views of the Smoky Hill River.

  • Hays Recreation Trail: This 4.5-mile trail passes through parks, neighborhoods, and open fields, providing a lovely scenic route.

  • Castle Rock Trail: For a more challenging hike, head to Castle Rock and take the 3-mile trail to the top of the butte for panoramic views of the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Hays: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities and tours to enjoy in Hays. Here are a few highlights:

  • Flyboy Brewery & Eats: Sample local beers and delicious food at this popular brewery in downtown Hays.

  • Hays Arts Council Gallery: View a rotating collection of artwork from local and regional artists at this vibrant arts council.

  • Hays Bison Viewing Area: Watch bison in their natural habitat at this free wildlife viewing area just outside of town.

Food and Drink Scene of Hays

From local brews to classic American comfort food, Hays has a wide range of dining options to satisfy every taste bud. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken Fried Steak: This classic Midwestern dish can be found in many restaurants throughout Hays, and is usually served with mashed potatoes and gravy.

  • Bierocks: A local specialty, bierocks are a pastry filled with ground beef, onions, and cabbage.

  • BBQ: For delicious BBQ options, make your way to Q4U and sample their brisket, pulled pork, and ribs.

Local Beverages

  • Flyboy Brewery & Eats: Sample local beers and delicious food at this popular brewery in downtown Hays.

  • Gella’s Diner & Lb. Brewing Co.: This is a popular spot known for its great beers brewed on site, along with a wide selection of classic American dishes.

Where to Stay in Hays: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From budget-friendly accommodations to more upscale hotels, Hays has plenty of options to choose from. Here are some top choices:

  • Holiday Inn: This standard, chain hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms, a swimming pool, and an onsite restaurant.

  • Best Western Butterfield Inn: This mid-range hotel provides free breakfast, an indoor pool, and a fitness center.

  • Chamberlain House: This charming bed and breakfast provides a more unique experience with cozy rooms, a communal living area, and a gourmet breakfast in the morning.

What to Buy in Hays: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For those looking to bring home a piece of Hays, there are plenty of unique souvenirs and local products to choose from. Here are a few ideas:

  • Bierock baking kits: Take home a piece of Hays cuisine with a bierock baking kit, available at local grocery stores.

  • Sternberg Museum T-shirts: Show off your love for natural history with a Sternberg Museum T-shirt.

  • Hays Arts Council Artwork: Purchase a piece of local art from the Hays Arts Council Gallery to bring home a unique memory of your trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Hays

In Hays, as in much of the Midwest, visitors can expect friendly, welcoming locals who take pride in their city’s history and culture. When visiting, be sure to show respect for local customs and values, such as punctuality and open communication.

Best Time to Visit Hays

The best time to visit Hays is during the spring and fall months, when the weather is mild and the crowds are sparse. In the summer, expect hot temperatures and busy tourist season, while the winter months can bring harsh weather conditions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hays

Do:

  • Do take time to explore the local museums and historical sites to learn about the rich history of Hays and the surrounding area.

  • Do try the local beer and cuisine, including the bierocks and chicken fried steak.

Don’t:

  • Don’t leave without taking a stroll through the picturesque Chestnut Street District.

  • Don’t forget to pack sunscreen and plenty of water for your outdoor adventures.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hays

Overall, Hays is a safe city for visitors. However, visitors should exercise caution when exploring more remote areas and should always be aware of their surroundings.

Health in Hays

Visitors should take precautions when spending time outdoors and should dress for the weather conditions. Be sure to wear sunscreen and bug spray during the summer months, and bring warm clothing during the winter.

Getting to Hays: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hays

The closest airport to Hays is Hays Regional Airport (HYS), located just a few miles outside of town. The airport is served by United Airlines, offering daily flights to and from Denver.

Bus Stations Near Hays

Greyhound Bus Lines operates daily bus service that stops in Hays.

Other Options

Hays is also easily accessible by car, with major highways running through the city. Visitors driving into Hays can easily park and explore on foot or by bike.

How to Get Around in Hays

For those not wanting to walk or bike, Hays has a taxi service, along with car rental options, such as Enterprise Rent-A-Car.

Where to Go Next from Hays: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hays is centrally located in Kansas, making it a prime spot for exploring nearby destinations. Here are a few excursions to consider:

  • Monument Rocks: Located just a two-hour drive from Hays, Monument Rocks is a National Natural Landmark that boasts impressive rock formations and stunning views.

  • Lake Wilson: Make the short drive to Lake Wilson for boating, swimming, and camping opportunities.

  • Black Hills: Take a longer road trip to the Black Hills of South Dakota for a chance to see the famous Mount Rushmore monument.

Conclusion

Hays, Kansas may be off the beaten path, but it is well worth a visit for its rich history, cultural and natural attractions, and charming local culture. Whether you’re looking to explore the outdoors, learn about local history, or sample delicious cuisine, Hays is sure to make for an unforgettable trip in the heart of the Great Plains.
