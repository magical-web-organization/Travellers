Introduction to Dodge City

Dodge City, located in Western Kansas, is a historic city situated in the heart of the Great Plains. Known for its vibrant history and cowboy culture, Dodge City is a must-see destination for history buffs, outdoor enthusiasts, and anyone looking to experience a unique slice of Americana.

List of Places to Visit in Dodge City

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Dodge City has a rich history, and there are many attractions in the city that allow visitors to experience firsthand what life was like in the Wild West. Here are some of the must-visit museums and historical places in Dodge City:

  • Boot Hill Museum: This museum showcases the history of Dodge City and the Wild West through exhibits, interactive displays, and live-action performances.
  • Santa Fe Trail Tracks: These historic tracks are a National Historic Landmark and showcase the history of the Santa Fe Trail through markers and exhibits.
  • Kansas Heritage Center: This museum offers visitors the chance to explore the history of Kansas through exhibits and interactive displays.
  • Fort Dodge: This historic fort was originally established in the 1860s to protect wagon trains and is now a museum showcasing the history of the fort and the surrounding area.

Natural Attractions of Dodge City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Dodge City is also home to a variety of natural attractions, including scenic views, hiking and biking trails, and outdoor recreational opportunities. Some of the must-visit natural attractions in Dodge City include:

  • Cheyenne Bottoms Wildlife Area: This wetlands preserve is a prime destination for birdwatchers and offers visitors the chance to spot a variety of bird species.
  • Santa Fe Trail Tracks: In addition to the historical significance, these tracks also offer scenic views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Trail of Fame: This paved walking trail honors the cowboys, lawmen, and others who made Dodge City famous and offers a unique way to explore the city.
  • Dodge City Trail System: This network of hiking and biking trails provides visitors with opportunities to explore the natural beauty of the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dodge City

Dodge City is an excellent destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with many hiking and biking trails in and around the city. Here are some of the best trails in Dodge City:

  • Trail of Fame: This 3.8-mile paved walking trail offers visitors the chance to explore the city’s history while getting some exercise.
  • Dodge City Trail System: With over 15 miles of trails, this system offers a variety of options for hikers and bikers of all skill levels.
  • Santa Fe Trail Bypass: This 1.5-mile paved trail runs along the north side of the Arkansas River and offers scenic views of the city and the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Dodge City: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to keep visitors busy during their stay in Dodge City. Here are some of the best things to do in Dodge City:

  • Watch a Gunfight Show: The streets of Dodge City come alive with gunfight shows, where actors recreate the gunfights that made the city famous.
  • Horseback Riding: Explore the natural beauty of the area on horseback with a guided tour.
  • Visit the Dodge City Days Rodeo: This annual event takes place every August and showcases the best rodeo talent from around the country.
  • Take a Walking Tour: Learn about the history of Dodge City on a guided walking tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dodge City

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long stay in Dodge City:

Day 1: Boot Hill Museum, Longhorn Cattle Drive Day 2: Santa Fe Trail Tracks, Fort Dodge Day 3: Cheyenne Bottoms Wildlife Area, Kansas Heritage Center Day 4: Trail of Fame, Dodge City Trail System Day 5: Riverfront Park, Wright Park Zoo Day 6: Tour of Historic Homes, Gunfighters Wax Museum Day 7: Dodge City Days Rodeo

Food and Drink Scene of Dodge City

Must-Try Dishes

Dodge City is known for its western-inspired cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • BBQ Beef Brisket: You can’t visit the Wild West without indulging in some classic BBQ beef brisket.
  • Chicken Fried Steak: A classic southern dish, chicken fried steak is a staple in many restaurants in Dodge City.
  • Bison Burger: For a healthier yet delicious option, try a bison burger.
  • Home-style Mashed Potatoes: You can’t go wrong with a classic side dish like mashed potatoes.

Local Beverages

Dodge City has a vibrant bar scene, and there are many local beverages to try, including:

  • Dodge City Brewing Signature IPA: This local brewery is known for its signature IPA, which is made with locally-sourced ingredients.
  • Kansas Wine Trail: Located just outside of Dodge City, this wine trail features a variety of wineries where visitors can sample the local wines.
  • Boot Hill Distillery: This locally-owned distillery produces a variety of liquors, including vodka, gin, and bourbon.

Where to Stay in Dodge City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dodge City offers a variety of lodging options to fit any budget. Here are some of the best places to stay in Dodge City:

  • Dodge House Hotel & Convention Center: A historic hotel located in the heart of downtown Dodge City.
  • Staybridge Suites: Perfect for families or those who want more space, Staybridge Suites offers suites with full kitchens.
  • Kansas Teachers’ Hall of Fame: A unique experience, the Kansas Teachers’ Hall of Fame also offers guest room accommodations.
  • Boot Hill Casino & Resort: This resort offers luxury accommodations, a casino, and a variety of dining and entertainment options.

What to Buy in Dodge City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home, here are some of the best places to shop in Dodge City:

  • The Boot Hill Museum Gift Shop: This museum gift shop offers a variety of souvenirs and Western-themed gifts.
  • Dodge City/Ford County Development Corporation: This organization offers a variety of locally-made products, including jams and jellies, honey, and locally-sourced meats.
  • Flea Market on Boot Hill: This outdoor flea market offers a variety of antiques, collectibles, and other unique items.

Local Culture and Customs of Dodge City

Dodge City has a rich Western culture that is evident in the city’s architecture, cuisine, and entertainment. Visitors to Dodge City should be respectful of the city’s history and culture.

Best Time to Visit Dodge City

The best time to visit Dodge City is in the spring or fall when the weather is mild and there are fewer crowds. However, if you want to experience the city’s famous rodeo, plan your trip in August.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dodge City

Do:

  • Explore the city’s history by visiting museums and historical sites.
  • Try the local cuisine, especially the BBQ beef brisket.
  • Take a walking tour of the city to learn about its history.
  • Attend a gunfight show.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful of the city’s history and culture.
  • Litter or damage any historical sites.
  • Ignore safety precautions when exploring the natural attractions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dodge City

Dodge City is a relatively safe city, but visitors should still take precautions to stay safe. Stick to well-lit areas at night and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Dodge City

Dodge City has excellent healthcare facilities, should the need arise. However, visitors should take precautions against the Midwest’s extreme weather, especially during the heat of summer.

Getting to Dodge City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dodge City

Dodge City Regional Airport is the closest airport to the city, located just three miles away. Other airports within driving distance include the Garden City Regional Airport and Wichita Dwight D. Eisenhower National Airport.

Bus Stations Near Dodge City

The Dodge City Cowboy Bus Stop is located just a few blocks from downtown Dodge City and offers daily bus service to and from nearby cities.

Other Options

For those driving to Dodge City, the city is easily accessible via major highways such as I-70 and US-400.

How to Get Around in Dodge City

Visitors to Dodge City have a variety of options for getting around the city, including taxis, ride-sharing services, and rental cars. The city is also easily walkable, with many attractions located within walking distance of downtown.

Where to Go Next from Dodge City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions from Dodge City:

  • Cimarron National Grassland: Located just south of Dodge City, this federally-managed grassland offers camping, hiking, and exploring opportunities.
  • Wichita, KS: Located just a few hours east of Dodge City, Wichita offers a variety of cultural and recreational opportunities.
  • Colorado Cities: Many Colorado cities, such as Denver and Colorado Springs, are within driving distance of Dodge City and offer a variety of attractions and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Dodge City is a unique and memorable destination that offers a mix of history, culture, and outdoor adventure. With plenty of attractions, activities, and natural beauty, it’s no wonder that Dodge City has become a popular destination for travelers from around the world. Plan your trip today and experience all that Dodge City has to offer.
