Introduction to Baxter Springs

Baxter Springs is a small city in Southeastern Kansas, known for its rich history and outdoor attractions. The city is a part of the famous Route 66, which adds to its historical significance. Surrounded by natural beauty, the city attracts adventurers and historians alike. Here is a comprehensive guide for a perfect trip to Baxter Springs.

List of Places to Visit in Baxter Springs

Baxter Springs has something to offer for everyone - whether you are interested in history, culture, or the outdoors. Here’s a list of places you can’t miss during your stay.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Historic Walking Tour: Step back in time with a self-guided tour of the historical sites in the city. The tour will take you through the Baxter Springs Heritage Center and Museum, the Baxter Springs Cemetery, and other significant places.

  • Fort Blair: Visit the site of the Civil War’s Battle of Baxter Springs. The fort’s museum will provide more insight into the event.

  • Death Alley Motor Cycle Museum: Built-in 1909 and now serving as a museum, it tells the story of motorcycling in America while focusing on the contributions by Harley-Davidson, Indian, Excelsior, and other historic brands.

Natural Attractions of Baxter Springs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Spring River: Go canoeing or tubing down the Spring River, which flows through the city.

  • Downstream Casino Resorts: Play at the casino, or go for a round of golf at the championship course.

  • Ozark Plateau: Take a hike on one of the Ozark Plateau’s many trails and enjoy the beautiful views of the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Baxter Springs

Baxter Springs has an extensive network of biking and hiking trails to explore. Here are a few highlights:

  • Mount Tom Trail: A 1.4-mile hike that offers great views of the city.

  • Cow Creek Trail: An 8-mile trail that runs through the Ozark Plateau and offers a challenging hike.

  • Route 66: Bike Trail: A 13-mile trail along the historic Route 66, perfect for a leisurely bike ride.

Best Things to Do in Baxter Springs: Activities and Tours

  • Route 66 Tour: Take a guided tour of Route 66 and learn about the history and significance of the trail.

  • Spring River Adventures: Experience the Spring River by canoeing, kayaking, or tubing.

  • Baxter Springs Badger Sports Park: Play a game of disc golf, sand volleyball, or horseshoes at the Badger Sports Park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Baxter Springs

Day 1: Arrive in Baxter Springs and go on a self-guided walking tour of the historical places.

Day 2: Rent a canoe and go down the Spring River.

Day 3: Take a guided tour of the Route 66 and visit the Death Alley Motor Cycle Museum.

Day 4: Go hiking on the Mount Tom Trail and enjoy the views.

Day 5: Spend a day exploring the Ozark Plateau and take a challenging hike on the Cow Creek Trail.

Day 6: Play a round of golf or try your luck at the Downstream Casino Resort.

Day 7: End your trip with some souvenir shopping or one last exploration of the city.

Food and Drink Scene of Baxter Springs

Baxter Springs offers an excellent mix of restaurants that serve up local delicacies and traditional American cuisine.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken Fried Steak: A classic Southern dish served with gravy and potatoes.

  • Fried Catfish: A local favorite, caught fresh from the Spring River.

  • Burgers: Whether you opt for a classic cheeseburger or a gourmet option, the city has a variety of burger joints.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Try the locally brewed beer at the White Buffalo Bar and Grill.

  • Coffee: Sip on traditional American coffee or try local roasts at The Junction Coffee Company.

Where to Stay in Baxter Springs: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Although Baxter Springs is a small city, it offers a range of accommodation options for tourists.

  • Baxter Inn: A budget-friendly option with clean rooms and a central location.

  • Downstream Casino Resort: A luxurious option with a range of amenities, including a golf course and a casino.

  • Econo Lodge: A great mid-range option with complimentary breakfast and a swimming pool.

What to Buy in Baxter Springs: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Route 66 Memorabilia: Bring back a piece of history with you. The city has a range of shops selling Route 66 memorabilia.

  • Spring River Collectives: Buy locally made crafts and gifts at the Spring River Collectives.

Local Culture and Customs of Baxter Springs

  • Baxter Springs is a welcoming and friendly community that takes pride in its history and natural beauty.

Best Time to Visit Baxter Springs

The best time to visit Baxter Springs is during the spring and fall seasons when the weather is mild and comfortable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Baxter Springs

  • Respect the city’s historical significance
  • Explore the great outdoors
  • Engage with the local community

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the natural beauty
  • Engage in illegal activity

Safety and Health

Safety in Baxter Springs

Baxter Springs is generally a safe city, but it’s always a good precaution to take care of your belongings.

Health in Baxter Springs

It’s always wise to take standard health precautions when traveling. Be sure to bring any necessary medication with you.

Getting to Baxter Springs: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Baxter Springs

The nearest airport is Joplin Regional Airport, located approximately 20 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Baxter Springs

Greyhound Bus Station is the nearest station, located in Joplin, approximately 20 miles away.

Other Options

Renting a car is an excellent option for exploring the local area.

How to Get Around in Baxter Springs

Baxter Springs is a walkable city, and many of its attractions are located within walking distance of each other. Additionally, you can rent a bike or car to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Baxter Springs: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Joplin: Visit nearby Joplin and explore its historical sites, art galleries, and restaurants.

  • Tulsa: Take a scenic drive to Tulsa and experience the city’s vibrant culture and nightlife.

Conclusion

Baxter Springs is a charming city that offers a great mix of history, culture, and outdoor adventures. Follow our comprehensive guide for an unforgettable trip to Southeastern Kansas.
