Introduction to Salina

Salina, a city located in Central Kansas, offers a unique combination of the prairie spirit and small-town charm. It stretches along the Smoky Hill River and features a broad range of attractions that cater to different preferences. Salina is not a huge city, but its size works to its benefit: it’s easy to navigate, and it offers a relaxed atmosphere, and an unbeatable quality of life.

In this travel guide, we will showcase the best places to visit, the hiking and biking routes, the best things to do, where to stay, where to eat, and the local culture and customs of Salina.

List of Places to Visit in Salina

Salina offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in history and culture, or adventure and outdoor activities, you’ll find plenty of places to visit in Salina. Below are some of the must-see attractions of the city.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Salina has a rich history, and you can learn more about it by visiting the following museums and historical places:

  • Smoky Hill Museum: showcases exhibits on the Smoky Hill River Valley history dating back to early Native American inhabitants. It is free to the public and an excellent place to learn about the history of the region.
  • Rolling Hills Zoo: features a variety of wildlife and habitats from around the world. It is considered one of the best zoos in the country.
  • Eisenhower Presidential Library, Museum and Boyhood Home: located 45 minutes from Salina in the small town of Abilene, features the life and presidency of Dwight D. Eisenhower.
  • Stiefel Theatre for the Performing Arts: a historic theatre that has been renovated and transformed into a premier venue for performing arts in the area.

Natural Attractions of Salina: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Salina is blessed with a variety of natural attractions, including hiking and biking trails, scenic drives, and beautiful landscapes. Here are some of the best places to explore in nature:

  • Coronado Heights: provides a panoramic view of the surrounding countryside; climb the historic castle-like tower located at the top of the scenic hill, and you’ll see why this park is a favorite among locals and visitors.
  • Indian Rock Park: gives you a taste of Salina’s natural landscape while walking through a series of trails; it includes a variety of interesting rock formations and scenic views.
  • Salina Parks and Recreation: offers more than 100 acres of parkland for picnicking, camping, fishing, and many other outdoor activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Salina

Exploring Salina doesn’t have to be on the back of a car; instead, it is a better experience when done on foot or on a bike. Here are some of the best cycling and hiking routes in Salina:

  • Campbell Trail: one of the most popular hiking trails in Salina. It meanders through the city along the Smoky Hill River, starting at Centennial Park to the south and ending at the North Front Street bridge toward Salina’s north side.
  • Skyline Park Trail: is a five-mile-long loop that features a scenic view of the city and a moderate level of difficulty. It is an ideal trail for hikers and mountain bikers.
  • Iron Horse Trail: is located 4 miles northwest of Salina and follows a former Union Pacific Railroad track, It is regarded as one of the best mountain biking trails in the area.

Best Things to Do in Salina: Activities and Tours

Salina offers an array of activities and tours that should not be missed. Here are some of the best things to do in Salina:

  • Salina Art Center: presents a variety of contemporary art exhibits, as well as a café and a gift shop.
  • Salina Symphony: If you’re a fan of classical music, you’ll enjoy the Salina Symphony Orchestra, performing classical and modern music throughout the year.
  • Salina Community Theatre: offers something for all audiences, from Broadway musicals, holiday shows to unconventional local performances.
  • Salina Ghost Tour: provides an exciting and fun-filled experience that takes visitors to some of the city’s most haunted locations.

Week-Long Itinerary for Salina

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Salina, here is a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the Smoky Hill Museum and learn about the city’s history, followed by a lunch at Coop’s Pizzeria, and an evening performance at the Stiefel Theatre for the Performing Arts.
  • Day 2: Hike or bike along Campbell Trail, then explore the exotic wildlife of the Rolling Hills Zoo.
  • Day 3: Have breakfast at the Blue Skye Brewery & Eats, then visit the Indian Rock Park and salivate taste buds at the Family-Support-and-Childrens-Services Farmer’s Market.
  • Day 4-5: Take a 2-day trip to Abilene, where you can visit the Eisenhower Museum and Library, as well as experience thrilling historic events in Old Abilene.
  • Day 6: Explore the natural beauty of the Coronado Heights, then head back to Salina for a delightful dinner at Martinelli’s Little Italy.
  • Day 7: Spend the day shopping for souvenirs in Downtown Salina and grab a burger at The Cozy Inn before catching a flight back home.

Food and Drink Scene of Salina

The food scene in Salina is diverse and tasty, featuring both local and global cuisines. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kansas BBQ: Salina provides some of the best BBQ experience featuring locally sourced meat, spices and sauces. The best places to taste it are the Hickory Hut and Big Nose Kate’s Saloon.
  • Runza: This popular sandwich is made with ground beef, sautéed onions, and shredded cabbage, It is considered a staple food of the Salina Area.
  • Bierocks: A savory pastry stuffed with seasoned beef, onions, and cabbage. It’s a Salina staple meal that is identified as the town’s official dish.

Local Beverages

  • Blue Skye Brewery: offers a delightful variety of local seasonal beers brewed on site. It is a must-visit for beer lovers.
  • Prairie Fire Winery: produces a variety of red, white, and dessert wines, and offers guided vineyard tours and tastings all year round.

Where to Stay in Salina: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whatever your style or budget, Salina has a wide variety of accommodations to choose from. Here are some of the best options for hotels, hostels, and guesthouses:

  • Country Inn & Suites by Radisson: a hotel located near the airport that offers complimentary breakfast and a fitness center.
  • Courtyard by Marriott Salina: features a Bistro, indoor pool, and a gym.
  • Super 8 by Wyndham Salina: proudly offers clean rooms, low price, and a warm welcoming service.
  • Baymont Inn & Suites Salina: provides plenty of amenities, including a hot tub, complimentary breakfast, and free Wi-Fi.

What to Buy in Salina: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Salina is known for its handmade goods, souvenirs, and local products. Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  • Smoky Hill Vineyards and Winery: If you’re a wine lover, make sure to visit the vineyard and purchase their products to bring home.
  • ArtisTree: located downtown, offers unique locally made art, home decor, and handmade jewelry. It’s the perfect place to buy souvenirs.
  • Whole Family Bulk Food: offers a variety of locally grown foods, spices, and baked goods. Ideal for those who appreciate home-cooked food.

Local Culture and Customs of Salina

Salina is a friendly and welcoming community. The best way to experience and embrace local culture and customs is by attending various events, interacting with locals and enjoying the local cuisine. The museum and festivals held throughout the year offer a chance to interact with residents and connect with its rich history, customs, and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Salina

The best time to visit Salina is during the fall (September-November) and Spring (March-May) when the weather is mild and the tourist crowds are smaller. During that time, you can experience the full charm, colors and events of the city with fewer visitors, and at favorable rates and deals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Salina

When visiting Salina, the following do’s and don’ts will guide you.

Do:

  • Do be polite and courteous to the locals. Salina prides itself on being a friendly community.
  • Do visit the local attractions and learn about the area’s rich history and culture.
  • Do give your taste buds a vacation and try the local cuisine, From Runza to Bierocks, there’s something for every palette.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter. Respect the natural beauty of the area and make use of the garbage bins along the trails or parks.
  • Don’t be impatient on the road. Salina may be a small town, and traffic moves slower than in urban centers.
  • Don’t forget to tip servers and hotel staff to show your appreciation for good meals, excellent service, and a comfortable stay.

Safety and Health

Salina is relatively a safe town however; it is always important to be informed about safety precautions in any new destination. Here is some guidance for safety and health in Salina:

Safety in Salina

  • Practicing caution when walking or biking along the roads and trails, especially at night.
  • Locking car doors and windows, and keeping valuables out of sight
  • Following all park rules and regulations, and using cameras to record wrong behavior

Health in Salina

  • Staying hydrated during hot weather
  • Exercising caution while exploring outdoor activities to avoid possible injury

Getting to Salina: Transportation Options and Logistics

Salina’s location in Central Kansas makes getting to it easy and accessible, whether you’re traveling by air, bus, or car. Here are some of the transportation options to Salina:

Airports Near Salina

Salina Regional Airport (SLN) is located about 5 miles southwest of downtown Salina, and it offers domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Salina

The Salina Bus Station is located in downtown Salina and is served by Greyhound Bus Lines.

Other Options

If you are driving, Salina is conveniently located off I-70, I-135 and US highway 81. You can also take Amtrak to nearby communities and rent a car to reach your final destination.

How to Get Around in Salina

Getting around in Salina and its surroundings is easy, whether you choose to walk, bike, or drive. Taxis and ride-sharing options are also available for transportation. Regular and airport shuttles are available to and from the city.

Where to Go Next from Salina: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip after visiting Salina, consider the following nearby destinations:

Abilene

Is home to the Seelye Mansion, Historic Dwight D. Eisenhower’s home, Greyhound Museum, Russell Stover Candy Factory, and more.

Kansas City

Located approximately 150 miles from Salina, Kansas City offers many attractions, including the National World War I Museum and Memorial, and the legendary Kansas City BBQ.

Wichita

Located about 100 miles south of Salina, Wichita offers a variety of fascinating museums, including the Museum of World Treasures, and a diverse array of restaurants, bars, and activities.

Conclusion

Salina is a beautiful and welcoming city that offers much to locals and visitors alike. From a variety of outdoor activities, local food, and drink, rich history, authentic culture, and much more. By using this comprehensive travel guide, we hope you can create a fantastic itinerary to explore and enjoy one of the best small towns in the American Midwest.
