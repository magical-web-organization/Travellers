Wichita, the largest city in Kansas, is a vibrant mix of culture, history, and outdoor adventures. It is situated in the heart of the United States and is a hub of the Great Plains region. Whether you are a culture buff, outdoor enthusiast or a food lover, Wichita offers something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through all the must-visit places, things to do, where to eat and stay, and more, to help you plan that perfect trip to Wichita.

List of Places to Visit in Wichita

Wichita is home to an array of attractions and places to visit, from historical landmarks and museums to scenic parks and natural reserves.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Wichita has a remarkable history and culture, and it is evident in its museums and historical places. Some of the top cultural attractions in Wichita include:

  • Exploration Place – One of the top science museums in the Midwest where you can explore the wonders of science and technology with interactive exhibits and displays.
  • Old Cowtown Museum – An outdoor living history museum that showcases the life and times of early Wichita pioneers.
  • Mid-America All-Indian Center – A museum that highlights the rich culture, heritage, and history of Native American tribes in Kansas.
  • Wichita-Sedgwick County Historical Museum – A museum that portrays the history of Wichita from its early days to the present.

Natural Attractions of Wichita: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Wichita is blessed with natural beauty and outdoor adventures, and you can experience it through its parks, gardens, and scenic trails. Some of Wichita’s top natural attractions include:

  • Sedgwick County Zoo – One of the best zoos in the country, with over 3,000 animals, from primates, big cats to reptiles and birds.
  • Botanica Wichita Gardens – A beautiful botanic garden that features themed gardens, waterfalls, and a butterfly house.
  • Great Plains Nature Center – A nature center that provides educational and recreational opportunities for visitors to learn about the natural resources of Kansas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wichita

Wichita has some of the best hiking and biking routes in the state, including the following:

  • Arkansas River Trail – A 10-mile trail that runs along the Arkansas River, and it’s perfect for hiking, running, or biking.
  • Prairie Sunset Trail – A 14-mile trail that goes through Sumner County and offers scenic views of the Flint Hills.
  • Redbud Trail – A 5.4-mile paved trail that winds through Chisholm Creek Park and is known for its beautiful sunset views.

Best Things to Do in Wichita: Activities and Tours

Apart from the museums and natural attractions, Wichita offers a host of activities and tours that you can enjoy with your family and friends. Here are some of the best things to do in Wichita:

  • Wichita Art Museum – One of the leading art museums in the country with over 8,000 works of art, including works by famous artists such as Charles M. Russell and Edward Hopper.
  • Keeper of the Plains – A 44-foot tall steel sculpture located at the confluence of the Arkansas and Little Arkansas Rivers, which lights up in the evening for an impressive display.
  • Dwight D. Eisenhower Presidential Library, Museum and Boyhood Home – Discover the life and times of President Eisenhower at this museum and library in Abilene, Kansas, which is just a short drive from Wichita.

Wichita’s Food and Drink Scene

Wichita is home to a diverse food and drink scene, and you will find everything from farm-to-table restaurants to burger joints and breweries. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kansas City Strip Steak – A classic steak dish that is served in most steakhouses in Wichita.
  • Chili – A Midwest staple, Wichita’s chili is a meaty stew that is perfect for cold winter days.
  • Bierock – A German pastry that is filled with ground beef, onions, and cabbage.

Local Beverages

  • Wheat Beer – A light and refreshing beer that is made with wheat malt and brewed in several local breweries such as Central Standard Brewing, Aero Plains Brewing, and Hopping Gnome Brewing.
  • Sunflower Espresso – A locally roasted coffee that is known for its rich flavor and smooth texture.

Where to Stay in Wichita: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Wichita has accommodation options that cater to all types of travelers and budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay in Wichita:

  • The Ambassador Hotel Wichita, Autograph Collection – A luxurious downtown hotel that boasts upscale amenities and stylish rooms.
  • Hostel on 3rd – A budget-friendly hostel that offers comfortable dorms and private rooms.
  • The Inn at Glenstrae – A charming bed and breakfast that is located in the west side of Wichita and offers a serene setting.

What to Buy in Wichita: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Wichita is known for its local products and souvenirs, which make for great gifts or mementos of your trip. Here are some of the most popular local products:

  • Blue Stem Kansas Wheat Vodka – A locally distilled vodka that is made with Kansas wheat.
  • Firebirds Wood Fired Grill Hot Sauce – A locally-made hot sauce that is available at Firebirds Wood Fired Grill.
  • Wichita Flag Merchandise – Wichita flag merchandise, including t-shirts, hats, and coffee mugs, are popular souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Wichita

Wichita has a friendly and welcoming culture, with a strong sense of community. The locals are known for their easy-going attitude and laid-back lifestyle. Some local customs include saying “hello” to strangers and holding the door open for others.

Best Time to Visit Wichita

The best time to visit Wichita is from April to October when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities are possible. The city experiences four distinct seasons, with hot and humid summers and cold winters.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wichita

Do:

  • Do take time to explore the city’s museums and historical landmarks.
  • Do visit the local breweries and distilleries for a taste of some of Wichita’s finest local beverages.
  • Do take a hike or bike ride along the Arkansas River Trail for scenic views.

Don’t:

  • Don’t underestimate the weather, bring appropriate clothing and gear for the season.
  • Don’t forget to tip your servers at restaurants and bars.
  • Don’t forget to take time to relax and enjoy the laid-back lifestyle of the city.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wichita

Wichita is a safe city, but it is always good to take some precautions, such as:

  • Avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.
  • Keep your valuables safe and secure in your hotel room.
  • Always lock your car when leaving it unattended.

Health in Wichita

Wichita has a good healthcare system, with several hospitals and clinics located throughout the city. It is always advisable to have travel insurance to cover any unexpected medical expenses.

Getting to Wichita: Transportation Options and Logistics

Wichita is well-connected by air, road, and rail.

Airports Near Wichita

  • Wichita Dwight D. Eisenhower National Airport – Located just 7 miles west of downtown, the airport serves several domestic and international airlines.

Bus Stations Near Wichita

  • Greyhound Bus Lines – Located in downtown Wichita, Greyhound offers several daily services to and from Wichita.

Other Options

  • Amtrak – If you prefer to travel by train, the nearest Amtrak station is located in Newton, about 30 miles north of Wichita.

How to Get Around in Wichita

Wichita has a good public transportation system, including buses and taxis. However, if you prefer to explore the city at your own pace, renting a car is the best option. Several car rental companies are located at the airport and throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Wichita: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Wichita is surrounded by several exciting destinations, which make for great day trips or weekend getaways. Some of the nearby destinations include:

  • Kansas City – A vibrant city that is known for its jazz, barbecue, and art scene, located about 3 hours northeast of Wichita.
  • Tallgrass Prairie National Preserve – A historic site that showcases the tallgrass prairie ecosystem of Kansas, located about 100 miles northeast of Wichita.
  • Oklahoma City – A growing city with a rich cowboy culture, located about 3 hours south of Wichita.

Conclusion

Wichita is a hidden gem that is full of surprises and hidden treasures. From its museums and historical landmarks to its natural beauty and outdoor adventures, there is something for everyone in Wichita. Plan your trip today and discover the heart of the Great Plains.
