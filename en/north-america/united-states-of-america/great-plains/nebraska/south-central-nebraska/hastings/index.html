Introduction to Hastings

Hastings, a small yet charming city in the South Central region of Nebraska, USA, is a must-visit destination for any traveler looking for an authentic experience in the Great Plains. Known for its rich cultural heritage, friendly locals, and breathtaking natural scenery, Hastings offers an array of activities and attractions that cater to all interests and budgets.

List of Places to Visit in Hastings

Whether you’re interested in history, cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, or simply relaxing in a cozy cafe or restaurant, Hastings has something to offer for everyone. Here are the top places to visit in Hastings:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hastings Museum - Explore the history and culture of Nebraska through interactive exhibits, a planetarium, and a wildlife diorama.
  • Adams County Historical Society Museum - Discover the rich heritage of Hastings and the surrounding area with fascinating exhibits and artifacts.
  • Hastings Art Center - Admire the works of local and regional artists in a beautifully restored historic building.

Natural Attractions of Hastings: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Hastings - Enjoy fishing, swimming, kayaking, and other water sports in this picturesque lake.
  • Heartwell Park and Nature Center - Take a hike or bike ride through the nature trails, or spot wildlife in the bird sanctuary.
  • Central Community College Prairie Loft Center for Outdoor and Agricultural Learning - Explore the beautiful prairie landscape and learn about sustainable agriculture practices.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hastings

  • Hastings Water Trails - Experience the Platte River like never before by kayaking or canoeing along the scenic water trails.
  • Hastings Trails - Hike, bike, or run in one of the many trails around Hastings, including the C.L. Werner Park Trail and the Brickyard Park Trail.

Best Things to Do in Hastings: Activities and Tours

  • Hastings Symphony Orchestra - Enjoy a night of classical music performed by a world-class orchestra.
  • Hastings Farmers Market - Shop for fresh produce, local crafts, and artisanal products every Saturday morning in Downtown Hastings.
  • Hastings Horse-Drawn Tours - Take a tour of Downtown Hastings on a horse-drawn carriage, and learn about the history and culture of the city.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hastings

Day 1-2: Visit the museums and historical sites, including the Hastings Museum and Adams County Historical Society Museum. Day 3-4: Explore the outdoors by hiking, biking, or kayaking at Lake Hastings, Heartwell Park and Nature Center, and Central Community College Prairie Loft Center for Outdoor and Agricultural Learning. Day 5-6: Participate in local tours and activities, such as the Hastings Symphony Orchestra and Hastings Farmers Market. Day 7: Relax and enjoy the local food and drinks scene.

Food and Drink Scene of Hastings

Hastings is a food lover’s paradise, with an array of restaurants and cafes that offer everything from farm-to-table cuisine and craft beer to classic American comfort food.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Runza Sandwich - A classic Nebraska dish consisting of a meat-filled bread pocket.
  • Smothered Burrito - A delicious Mexican-style burrito smothered in red or green chili sauce.
  • Cornhusker Burger - A burger topped with corned beef, Swiss cheese, and sauerkraut.

Local Beverages

  • Kinkaider Brewing Company - A local brewery that offers craft beer and spirits made with locally sourced ingredients.
  • Hastings Coffee Company - A cozy coffee shop that roasts its own coffee beans and serves delicious pastries.

Where to Stay in Hastings: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hastings offers a range of accommodation options that cater to different preferences and budgets. From luxurious hotels to charming guesthouses, there is something for everyone. Here are some of the most popular places to stay in Hastings:

  • Holiday Inn Express Hotel & Suites - A modern hotel with comfortable rooms, free breakfast, and a fitness center.
  • Rainbow Motel - A budget-friendly motel with clean rooms and friendly service.
  • Lochland Country Club & Resort - A luxurious resort with a golf course, restaurant, and indoor pool.

What to Buy in Hastings: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Hastings is home to many talented artists and craftspeople, and there are plenty of unique souvenirs and gifts to bring back home. Here are some of the best things to buy in Hastings:

  • Local artwork and crafts - Visit the Hastings Art Center and other local galleries to shop for unique handcrafted items.
  • Nebraska-grown products - Pick up some locally made jams, jellies, and honey at the Hastings Farmers Market or other local shops.
  • Cornhusker memorabilia - Show your love for Nebraska’s football team with some merchandise from a local store.

Local Culture and Customs of Hastings

Hastings is a friendly and welcoming city, with a strong agricultural heritage and a deep appreciation for cultural diversity. The locals are proud of their heritage and love to share it with visitors, so don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation and learn more about the history and culture of the area.

Best Time to Visit Hastings

The best time to visit Hastings depends on your interests and preferences. The summer months, from June to August, offer warm weather and plenty of outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and water sports. The fall is a beautiful time to visit, with colorful foliage and fun seasonal events such as the Harvest Festival. The winter months, from December to February, offer a cozy atmosphere and winter activities such as ice skating and holiday shopping.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hastings

Do:

  • Do try local cuisine and drinks, such as the Runza Sandwich and Kinkaider beer.
  • Do explore the outdoors and take advantage of the beautiful natural scenery.
  • Do respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the environment.
  • Don’t be afraid to ask locals for recommendations and tips.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hastings

Hastings is a safe city, with a low crime rate and friendly locals. However, it is important to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions when exploring unfamiliar areas.

Health in Hastings

Hastings has a range of medical facilities and hospitals that provide quality healthcare services. It is important to have adequate travel insurance and to take necessary precautions such as wearing sunscreen and staying hydrated.

Getting to Hastings: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hastings

The closest major airport to Hastings is Central Nebraska Regional Airport, located about 25 miles away. Other airports within a reasonable driving distance include Grand Island Central Regional Airport and Lincoln Airport.

Bus Stations Near Hastings

Greyhound Bus Lines offers services to Hastings from major cities in Nebraska and neighboring states.

Other Options

Renting a car is a popular option, as it allows you to explore the surrounding areas and access the outdoor attractions easily.

How to Get Around in Hastings

Hastings is a small city that can be easily explored on foot or by bike. There are also taxis and rideshare services available for transportation within the city limits.

Where to Go Next from Hastings: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kearney - A nearby city that offers historical landmarks, museums, and local events.
  • Omaha - A cultural hub of Nebraska that features a range of attractions including the Henry Doorly Zoo and the Durham Museum.
  • The Sandhills - A stunning natural region of Nebraska with unique geological formations, hiking trails, and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Hastings is a hidden gem in the Great Plains, offering an abundance of cultural, natural, and culinary treasures to explore. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or simply looking for a place to unwind, Hastings has something to offer for everyone. So pack your bags, grab your camera, and get ready to experience the best of Nebraska in this charming city.
