Introduction to Sutherland

Sutherland is a charming rural town located in South Central Nebraska, in the Great Plains region of the United States. It is nestled in the Platte River valley, surrounded by scenic sand hills, and is known for its outstanding natural beauty, historic sites, and hospitable locals. The town is small, with a population of around 1,000 people, but it offers a wealth of things to see and do, making it a perfect destination for nature lovers, history buffs, and family vacationers.

List of Places to Visit in Sutherland

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are interested in history and culture, Sutherland has several remarkable museums and historical sites to visit:

  1. Oregon Trail Park - This park tells the story of the Oregon Trail, the main route that brought pioneers, traders, and gold seekers to the West Coast in the mid-19th century. It features a pioneer cemetery, a reconstructed trading post, and a replica wagon train.

  2. Lincoln County Historical Museum - This museum showcases the history of Lincoln County, from the Native American era to the present day. It has exhibits on homesteading, railroading, agriculture, and military life, as well as a collection of vintage cars, tractors, and bicycles.

  3. The Original Pony Express Station - This is the site of the first Pony Express station in Nebraska, which operated from 1860 to 1861. It is located on the Oregon Trail near the North Platte River, and features a restored cabin, a museum, and a souvenir shop.

Natural Attractions of Sutherland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sutherland has breathtaking natural scenery, with rolling hills, verdant valleys, and crystal-clear lakes. Here are some of the best places to experience its beauty:

  1. Lake Maloney State Recreation Area - This is a popular destination for swimming, boating, fishing, and camping. The lake is stocked with walleye, catfish, and bass, and has a sandy beach, a marina, and several picnic shelters.

  2. Buffalo Bill Ranch State Historical Park - This park was the home of William F. Cody, also known as Buffalo Bill, the famous frontier scout, buffalo hunter, and showman. It features his restored home, a barn, a log cabin, and a museum.

  3. Wildcat Hills State Recreation Area - This is a rugged region of sandstone cliffs, rocky canyons, and high bluffs, located about 25 miles southwest of Sutherland. It has hiking and biking trails, a scenic drive, and opportunities to observe wildlife, such as bighorn sheep, deer, and eagles.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sutherland

Sutherland is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with over 20 miles of hiking and biking trails that wind through the sand hills, river valleys, and prairies. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  1. South Canyon Loop Trail - This is a 3.5-mile loop that starts at the Oregon Trail Park and follows the South Creek Canyon. It offers stunning views of the canyon walls, the Platte River, and the surrounding hills.

  2. Lake Maloney Trail - This is a 7.5-mile trail that circles Lake Maloney and goes through the woods and grasslands. It is flat and easy, suitable for both novice and experienced hikers and bikers.

  3. Sutherland Trail - This is a 10-mile trail that connects Sutherland with Hershey, a neighboring town. It goes through the Platte River valley and the Wildcat Hills, and has gentle slopes and rolling terrain.

Best Things to Do in Sutherland: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, Sutherland offers many other fun activities and tours to enjoy, such as:

  1. Kayaking and Canoeing - The North Platte River is a great place for kayaking and canoeing, with calm waters and scenic views. You can rent equipment from local outfitters or bring your own.

  2. Horseback Riding - You can explore the sand hills and prairies on horseback, with guided tours that cater to all levels of riding skill. There are several stables and ranches in the area that offer horse rentals and lessons.

  3. Stargazing - Sutherland is blessed with clear skies and low light pollution, making it an ideal spot for stargazing. You can join a stargazing tour or simply find a quiet place to observe the constellations and shooting stars.

Food and Drink Scene of Sutherland

Sutherland has a few local restaurants and bars that serve hearty and delicious meals, as well as local wines and craft beers. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Steak and potatoes - Nebraska is known for its high-quality beef, and Sutherland has several steakhouses that offer prime cuts of sirloin, ribeye, and T-bone, grilled to perfection.

  2. Runza - This is a traditional sandwich made of rolled dough filled with beef, cabbage, onions, and spices, a staple of the Nebraska cuisine.

  3. Corn on the cob - Nebraska is also a major producer of sweet corn, and Sutherland has several farm stands and markets where you can buy fresh and juicy ears of corn.

Local Beverages

  1. Kinkaider Brewing Company - This is a local microbrewery that produces award-winning beers, such as the Devil’s Gap Jalapeno Ale, the Runza Red Amber Ale, and the Snow Beast Winter Ale.

  2. Miletta Vista Winery - This is a family-owned winery that offers a wide selection of red, white, and fruit wines, made from locally grown grapes and other fruits.

Where to Stay in Sutherland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sutherland has a few options for overnight accommodations, ranging from budget-friendly motels to cozy bed and breakfasts. Here are some popular choices:

  1. Sutherland Motel - This is a clean and comfortable motel with affordable rates, free Wi-Fi, and easy access to the Oregon Trail Park and the North Platte River.

  2. Sleepy Hollow Bed and Breakfast - This is a charming and cozy B&B that offers five spacious rooms, each with a private bathroom, a flat-screen TV, and a gourmet breakfast.

What to Buy in Sutherland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sutherland has several shops and stores that sell unique souvenirs and local products, such as:

  1. Sutherland Depot Store - This is a gift shop located in the Oregon Trail Park that offers a wide range of souvenirs, books, and toys related to the pioneer era and the Oregon Trail.

  2. Nebraska Made - This is a store that features handmade crafts, art, and food products from local artisans and producers. You can find anything from pottery and jewelry to salsa and honey.

Local Culture and Customs of Sutherland

Sutherland is a friendly and welcoming town that values its traditions and heritage. Some of the local customs and events that you may encounter during your stay are:

  1. Nebraskaland Days - This is a popular annual festival that celebrates the Western way of life, with rodeo shows, parades, concerts, and a carnival. It takes place in North Platte, about 20 minutes from Sutherland, in June.

  2. Sandhill Crane Migration - Every spring, millions of sandhill cranes stop in the Platte River valley during their migration from Mexico to Canada. This is a spectacular natural phenomenon that attracts birdwatchers and nature lovers from all over the world.

Best Time to Visit Sutherland

The best time to visit Sutherland is from May to October, when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the outdoor activities and events are in full swing. The peak season is from June to August, when the temperatures are the highest, and the crowds are the largest. If you want to avoid the crowds, consider visiting in May or September, when the weather is still good, but the tourist influx is lower.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sutherland

Do:

  1. Greet locals politely and with a smile.
  2. Dress for the weather and wear comfortable shoes for outdoor activities.
  3. Try local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  1. Trespass on private property or disturb wildlife.
  2. Leave trash or litter in natural areas.
  3. Ignore traffic rules and signs while driving.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sutherland

Sutherland is a safe and quiet town, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, you should take some precautions to ensure your safety:

  1. Lock your car and your accommodation when you leave.
  2. Carry a first-aid kit and insect repellent for outdoor activities.
  3. Avoid walking alone at night in unlit areas.

Health in Sutherland

Sutherland is located in a rural area, and medical facilities may be limited. You should take some measures to maintain your health:

  1. Bring any necessary medications and prescriptions.
  2. Sanitize your hands regularly and drink plenty of water.
  3. Use sunscreen and wear a hat to protect yourself from the sun.

Getting to Sutherland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Sutherland is located about 180 miles west of Omaha, and about 280 miles east of Denver. The easiest way to get there is by car, either by driving on Interstate 80, or on the scenic Highway 30, which follows the historic Lincoln Highway. However, you can also travel by air or by bus.

Airports Near Sutherland

The nearest airport to Sutherland is North Platte Regional Airport, which is located about 15 miles east of Sutherland. It has daily flights to Denver on United Airlines.

Bus Stations Near Sutherland

The nearest bus station to Sutherland is in North Platte, about 20 minutes away. It is served by Greyhound and Jefferson Lines, which offer connections to major cities in the region.

Other Options

You can also rent a car or use a taxi or shuttle service from the airports or bus stations to Sutherland. There are several rental car companies and taxi services in North Platte that provide transportation to Sutherland.

How to Get Around in Sutherland

The best way to get around in Sutherland is by car, as the town is small, and the attractions are spread out. You can rent a car from the North Platte airport or from the nearby towns. However, if you prefer biking or walking, there are several trails and paths that connect the attractions and the downtown.

Where to Go Next from Sutherland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sutherland is located in a scenic and historic region of Nebraska, and there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to explore, such as:

  1. Chimney Rock National Historic Site - This is a landmark that rises 300 feet above the North Platte River, and served as a guidepost for the pioneers on the Oregon Trail. It is located about 55 miles southwest of Sutherland, near Bayard.

  2. Scotts Bluff National Monument - This is a natural fortress of rock that towers over the North Platte River valley, and was an important landmark for the Native Americans and the pioneers. It is located about 70 miles west of Sutherland, near Gering.

  3. Fort McPherson National Cemetery - This is a national cemetery that is the final resting place of over 10,000 veterans, dating back to the Civil War. It is located about 25 miles east of Sutherland, near Maxwell.

Conclusion

Sutherland, Nebraska is a hidden gem of the Great Plains, with rich history, spectacular scenery, and friendly people. Whether you are a history buff, a nature lover, or a foodie, you will find something to enjoy in Sutherland. We hope this guide has inspired you to plan your next trip to Sutherland and explore its treasures.
