Introduction to Hershey

Located in the heart of South Central Nebraska, Hershey is a charming town that boasts a rich history, scenic landscapes, and a welcoming community. Surrounded by the Great Plains, Hershey is a perfect destination for nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers. Whether you’re looking for outdoor activities, cultural attractions, or simply a relaxing getaway, Hershey has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Hershey

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Lincoln County Historical Museum: This museum showcases the history of Lincoln County, Nebraska, and its residents. From Native American history to westward expansion, the exhibits here provide a fascinating look at the region’s past.
  • The Great Platte River Road Archway Monument: A unique monument that spans over Interstate 80, giving visitors a glimpse into the history of pioneer migration on the Great Plains.
  • The North Platte Area Children’s Museum: Perfect for families traveling with children, this interactive museum offers a hands-on learning experience that is both fun and educational.

Natural Attractions of Hershey: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Maloney State Recreation Area: Located just a few miles from Hershey, this recreation area offers activities such as fishing, boating, and camping.
  • Buffalo Bill Ranch State Historical Park: The former home of William F. Cody (aka Buffalo Bill) is now a state park that features a museum, picnic area, and nature trails.
  • Ash Hollow State Historical Park: This park features rugged terrain, a historic pioneer cemetery, and stunning views of the surrounding Platte River Valley.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hershey

  • The North Platte River Trail: A popular trail that runs along the North Platte River, offering stunning views and opportunities for bird watching.
  • Lake Maloney State Recreation Area Trail: A 7-mile trail that encircles Lake Maloney, providing scenic views and wildlife watching opportunities.
  • Buffalo Bill Ranch State Historical Park Trail: This easy, 1-mile loop trail takes visitors through the historic ranch land of Buffalo Bill.

Best Things to Do in Hershey: Activities and Tours

  • The Hershey Fall Festival: Held every September, this festival offers live music, food vendors, and crafts.
  • Buffalo Bill Birthday Celebration: A week-long celebration in February that includes live entertainment, parades, and historical reenactments.
  • Prairie Arts Center: A community arts center that offers workshops, classes, and exhibitions.

Food and Drink Scene of Hershey

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nebraska Burger: A juicy beef patty topped with corned beef, sauerkraut, and Swiss cheese.
  • Corn on the Cob: This Nebraska staple is perfectly cooked during the state’s growing season, typically from July to September.
  • Runza Sandwich: A hot sandwich filled with beef, onions, and cabbage, similar to a pasty.

Local Beverages

  • Kinkaider Brewing Co. Beer: A local brewery that offers a variety of craft beers made with Nebraska-grown hops.
  • Sodapop Central: A family-owned soda shop that offers a variety of handcrafted sodas made with real cane sugar.

Where to Stay in Hershey: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hershey Motel: A budget-friendly option located in the heart of Hershey.
  • Victorian Inn Bed and Breakfast: A charming inn that offers a delicious breakfast and cozy rooms.
  • Holiday Inn Express: A convenient option located just a few miles outside of Hershey, with a pool and fitness center.

What to Buy in Hershey: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hershey Chocolate: Made in the nearby town of North Platte, Hershey’s milk chocolate is a local favorite.
  • Cornhusker Gear: Show your love for Nebraska with a t-shirt or hat featuring the state’s iconic Cornhusker logo.
  • Kinkaider Brewing Co. Beer: Bring home a few cans of this locally brewed beer to enjoy back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Hershey

Nebraskans are known for their hospitality, so it’s no surprise that the residents of Hershey are welcoming to visitors. Barbecues, rodeos, and festivals are a common way of life in Hershey, and visitors are always encouraged to partake in the local traditions.

Best Time to Visit Hershey

The best time to visit Hershey is during the summer months of June through August when the weather is mild and outdoor activities are in full swing. Those looking to avoid crowds should consider visiting in the shoulder season of spring (April to May) or fall (September to October).

Do’s and Don’ts of Hershey

Do:

  • Do engage with locals and ask for recommendations.
  • Do take the time to explore the nature and history of the region.
  • Do participate in local traditions and events.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave the region worse off than when you arrived.
  • Don’t forget to bring sunscreen and insect repellent.
  • Don’t disrespect the local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hershey

Hershey is a relatively safe destination, but visitors should be aware of the risks associated with outdoor activities such as hiking and camping. Visitors should also take precautions when driving in rural areas, where wildlife may be present on the roads.

Health in Hershey

In general, Hershey has a relatively low risk of communicable diseases. However, visitors should be aware of the risks associated with outdoor activities such as ticks and mosquitoes. Visitors should also make sure they are up to date on routine vaccinations before traveling to Hershey.

Getting to Hershey: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hershey

  • North Platte Regional Airport: Located just 10 miles north of Hershey, this airport offers daily flights to and from Denver International Airport and connections to other major hubs.

Bus Stations Near Hershey

  • Jefferson Lines: Offers bus service to and from North Platte, located just 10 miles north of Hershey.

Other Options

  • Driving: Hershey is located just off Interstate 80, making it easily accessible by car.
  • Rental Car: Visitors can rent a car at the North Platte Regional Airport or at one of the nearby car rental agencies.

How to Get Around in Hershey

Visitors to Hershey will need a car or bicycle to get around. Bike rentals are available at Lake Maloney State Recreation Area.

Where to Go Next from Hershey: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Scotts Bluff National Monument: Located just 2 hours west of Hershey, this national monument features stunning rock formations and hiking trails.
  • Fort Robinson State Park: Located 90 minutes northwest of Hershey, this historic park offers camping, hiking, and horseback riding.
  • Chimney Rock National Historic Site: Located just over 2 hours east of Hershey, this historic site is known for its striking rock formations and rich history.

Conclusion

Hershey is a gem of a destination in Nebraska, offering a rich mix of culture, history, and outdoor adventures. With so much to see and do, Hershey is the perfect place to escape the hustle and bustle of everyday life. Plan your visit today and experience the best of the Great Plains!
