Introduction to North Platte

North Platte is a charming city located in South Central Nebraska, known for its rich history, stunning wildlife, and breathtaking natural scenery. The city is a popular destination for nature lovers, adventure-seekers, history buffs, and anyone looking for a relaxing getaway in the heart of the great plains.

List of Places to Visit in North Platte

Here are some of the must-visit places in North Platte:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Buffalo Bill Ranch State Historical Park: This state park is home to the sprawling ranch house of Col. William F. Cody, better known as Buffalo Bill. Visitors can take a guided tour of the house and explore the stunning grounds of the ranch.
  • Lincoln County Historical Museum: This museum preserves the history of Lincoln County through fascinating exhibits, including antique automobiles, Native American artifacts, and military items.
  • Golden Spike Tower and Visitor Center: This tower offers a stunning panoramic view of the world’s largest rail yard, Union Pacific’s Bailey Yard. The Visitor Center features interactive displays and exhibits that explain the importance of the railroad to North Platte’s history.

Natural Attractions of North Platte: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Maloney State Recreation Area: This 1,500-acre lake is a favorite spot for water activities such as fishing, boating, and swimming. The park also offers hiking and biking trails, picnic areas, and campsites.
  • North Platte River: The North Platte River flows through the city and is a popular destination for kayaking, canoeing, and fishing.
  • Cody Park: This park is home to many attractions, including a free wildlife exhibit, playgrounds, and picnicking areas. Visitors can also ride the miniature train or the vintage carousel in the park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North Platte

North Platte offers spectacular hiking and biking routes for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some popular routes:

  • River’s Edge Trail: This trail follows the North Platte River and offers beautiful views of the surrounding nature. The trail is suitable for both hiking and biking.
  • Cody Park Trail: This 1.5-mile trail is perfect for a leisurely walk or bike ride. The trail winds through the park and offers stunning views of the North Platte River.
  • Scout’s Rest Ranch Trail: This 1.6-mile trail takes visitors through the scenic Scout’s Rest Ranch, a former residence of Buffalo Bill Cody.

Best Things to Do in North Platte: Activities and Tours

North Platte offers many exciting activities and tours for visitors of all ages. Here are some of the best things to do in the city:

  • Visit the Union Pacific’s Bailey Yard: Visitors can tour the world’s largest rail yard with Union Pacific’s Bailey Yard Tower Tour.
  • Take a scenic drive: Visitors can enjoy stunning views of the area by driving along the Sandhills Journey Scenic Byway, which takes travelers through beautiful landscapes, charming towns, and cultural sites.
  • Attend the Nebraskaland Days: This annual festival celebrates Nebraska’s Western heritage with rodeos, concerts, parades, and other events.

Food and Drink Scene of North Platte

North Platte offers a diverse range of dining options, ranging from classic American dishes to international cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nebraska-style corned beef: A deliciously seasoned version of corned beef, commonly served with mashed potatoes, carrots, and cabbage.
  • Bierock: A savory pastry filled with seasoned beef, cabbage, and onions, traditionally served with mustard.

Local Beverages

  • Kinkaider Brewing Company: This local brewery offers a wide range of brews, including their famous Snow Beast Winter Ale.
  • Feather River Vineyards: This family-owned vineyard produces some of Nebraska’s best wines, including Cabernet Franc and Traminette.

Where to Stay in North Platte: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

North Platte offers a variety of accommodations for visitors, including chain hotels, budget-friendly motels, and boutique guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in the city:

  • Oak Tree Inn: A comfortable chain hotel with an on-site restaurant and easy access to the I-80 highway.
  • Hampton Inn & Suites: A budget-friendly hotel with clean, spacious rooms and a free hot breakfast.
  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites: A modern hotel with a fitness center, an indoor pool, and complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in North Platte: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to North Platte can take home unique gifts and souvenirs from their trip. Here are some popular items to consider:

  • Buffalo Bill memorabilia: Souvenirs related to Buffalo Bill, such as t-shirts, hats, and mugs, can be found at North Platte’s various gift shops.
  • Handmade soap: North Platte offers a vast selection of handmade soaps made with locally-sourced ingredients like goat milk and honey.
  • Sandhills wines: Visitors can take home a bottle of wine from Feather River Vineyards, a local winery that produces unique wines from grapes grown in Nebraska’s high plains.

Local Culture and Customs of North Platte

North Platte has a rich cultural heritage, shaped by the region’s frontier history and Native American roots. Visitors to the city can learn more about local traditions and customs through cultural events like Nebraskaland Days and by visiting cultural sites like the North Platte Area Children’s Museum.

Best Time to Visit North Platte

The best time to visit North Platte is between May and September when the weather is warm and sunny. Visitors can enjoy outdoor activities like swimming, hiking, and biking during these months.

Do’s and Don’ts of North Platte

Do:

  • Respect local wildlife: North Platte is home to many species of wildlife, from bison to prairie dogs. Visitors are advised to keep a safe distance from animals and not feed them.
  • Take part in local traditions: Attend cultural events like Nebraskaland Days to learn more about North Platte’s rich cultural heritage.
  • Explore the outdoors: North Platte offers some of the most beautiful natural scenery in Nebraska. Visitors are encouraged to explore the area’s many parks and hiking trails.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Visitors to North Platte are advised to keep the environment clean and dispose of trash properly.
  • Trespass on private property: Much of North Platte’s scenic land is privately owned, so visitors are advised not to cross fenced areas or go beyond property lines.
  • Disrespect local traditions: Visitors should be respectful of North Platte’s historical and cultural heritage and avoid making insensitive or offensive comments.

Safety and Health

Safety in North Platte

North Platte is a relatively safe city, although visitors should take normal precautions against theft or other common crimes. Visitors are also advised to take caution when hiking or engaging in outdoor activities, as the terrain can be uneven and dangerous.

Health in North Platte

North Platte offers several healthcare facilities, including Great Plains Health Medical Center and North Platte Urgent Care. Visitors are advised to take out travel insurance before arriving in the city.

Getting to North Platte: Transportation Options and Logistics

North Platte is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here are some transportation options available for visitors:

Airports Near North Platte

  • North Platte Regional Airport: This airport offers daily flights to Denver, Colorado, and is serviced by Great Lakes Airlines.

Bus Stations Near North Platte

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: North Platte is serviced by Greyhound buses, with a stop at the bus station on EastFront Street.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Visitors can rent a car from major car rental companies like Hertz, Avis, and Enterprise.
  • Taxi service: Local taxi services like Yellow Cab and City Cab can provide transportation for visitors.

How to Get Around in North Platte

North Platte is a pedestrian-friendly city, with many attractions located close to one another. Visitors can also take advantage of taxis or car rentals for longer trips.

Where to Go Next from North Platte: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

North Platte is located in an ideal position for exploring other popular destinations in the region. Here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Grand Island: Just a three-hour drive from North Platte, Grand Island offers a range of attractions, including the Nebraska State Fairgrounds and the Stuhr Museum of the Prairie Pioneer.
  • North Loup: A small town located just a half-hour drive from North Platte, North Loup is famous for its annual Popcorn Days celebration.
  • Scottsbluff: A four-hour drive from North Platte, Scottsbluff offers stunning natural scenery, including the famous Scotts Bluff National Monument.

Conclusion

North Platte is a hidden gem in the heart of the great plains, offering visitors a unique blend of history, natural beauty, and cultural traditions. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, exciting tours, delicious food, or a relaxing getaway, North Platte has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your trip and explore all that this charming city has to offer.
