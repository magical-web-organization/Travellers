Introduction to Maxwell

Maxwell is a picturesque city located in South Central Nebraska, in the United States of America. It is a gateway to a range of outdoor adventures, natural attractions, and culturally significant landmarks. The city boasts a rich history, diverse culture, and welcoming locals. Whether you are a fan of hiking, biking, museums, or delicious food, Maxwell has something for everyone. In this travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, must-try dishes, where to stay, and more to help you plan a memorable trip to Maxwell.

List of Places to Visit in Maxwell

Maxwell offers visitors an array of options for things to see and do. From culturally important museums to outdoor adventures, here are the top places to visit in Maxwell:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Dawson County Historical Museum: Located just a few minutes from Maxwell, the Dawson County Historical Museum showcases the history of Nebraska with its exhibits, artifacts, and collections.
  • Oregon Trail: Follow the Oregon Trail through the Nebraska Sandhills and across the Platte River, tracing the footsteps of pioneers who made their way westward on this historic route.
  • Buffalo Bill Ranch State Historical Park: Explore the home of Buffalo Bill, one of America’s most famous cowboys, who lived in North Platte from 1886 to 1913. The park offers tours of the home, exhibits, and living history demonstrations.

Natural Attractions of Maxwell: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cottonwood Canyon State Wildlife Management Area: Offering over 2400 acres of prime outdoor recreation opportunities, visitors can go fishing, hiking, cycling, and hunting.
  • Lake Maloney State Recreation Area: This park is an outdoor enthusiasts’ paradise, offering visitors the chance to hike, bike, fish, and boating, as well as play golf and enjoy other activities.
  • Wild Horse Golf Club: This acclaimed golf course is a popular destination for golfers from around the world, offering stunning views and a challenging course.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Maxwell

Maxwell has an abundance of hiking and biking trails that wind through scenic landscapes, providing opportunities for outdoor adventure and recreation. Here are some of the top hiking/biking routes in Maxwell:

  • The Great Divide Basin Trail: This 8.4-mile trail is an out-and-back hike through the native Sandhill prairie of the Nebraska Sandhills. It gives hikers the chance to explore the beauty of the Great Plains.
  • Lake Maloney Trail: A 4-mile paved trail that encircles Lake Maloney, this is a great path for a leisurely bike ride or walk.
  • Cottonmill Loop Trail: This moderate 6.3-mile trail loops through the Cottonwood Canyon State Wildlife Management Area, offering beautiful views of the meadow and trees.

Best Things to Do in Maxwell: Activities and Tours

  • Load up on hamburgers and homemade ice cream at Pop Corner Cafe, a locally renowned diner on Highway 30.
  • Get a deep dive into local history at the Fort Kearny State Historical Park and take a walk through the trees and prairies.
  • Visit the Feather River Vineyards and Winery, just a short drive outside of town, and sample delicious wines made from local Nebraska grapes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Maxwell

Day 1: Arrive in Maxwell, check into hotel, and enjoy dinner at Pop Corner Cafe

Day 2: Visit the Buffalo Bill Ranch State Historical Park and learn about one of America’s most famous cowboys. Take a walk through the grounds and enjoy the scenery.

Day 3: Embark on the Cottonmill Loop Trail. This moderate trail winds through the Cottonwood Canyon State Wildlife Management Area.

Day 4: Explore the Dawson County Historical Museum. Learn about the history of Nebraska and the local area.

Day 5: Take a scenic drive through the Great Plains and stop at the Oregon Trail. Follow the footsteps of the pioneers who made their way westward on this historic route.

Day 6: Visit Lake Maloney State Recreation Area and enjoy hiking, biking, fishing, and other outdoor activities.

Day 7: Depart from Maxwell.

Food and Drink Scene of Maxwell

Maxwell has a rich culinary scene, inspired by its agricultural heritage and Midwestern roots. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Maxwell:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Steak: Nebraska is known for its beef, and steak is a must-try dish when visiting Maxwell. There are many local steakhouses in the area.
  • Runzas: Also known as bierocks, this is a traditional dish in Nebraska. It is a baked sandwich roll filled with beef and cabbage.
  • Homemade ice cream: Pop Corner Cafe is known for its homemade ice cream, made fresh daily with local ingredients.

Local Beverages

  • Nebraska beer: Many local breweries in the area offer a range of craft beers, made with locally sourced ingredients.
  • Nebraska wines: Nebraska has a growing wine industry, with many vineyards producing unique and delicious wines.

Where to Stay in Maxwell: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Maxwell has a variety of lodging options for visitors, ranging from hotels to guesthouses. Here are some of the top choices:

  • Comfort Inn: This hotel is conveniently located in Maxwell and offers comfortable rooms and modern amenities.
  • Cedar Hills Vineyard and Gardens: Visitors can stay in one of the guesthouses on the vineyard, surrounded by stunning views of the vineyard and countryside.
  • Mariposa Bed and Breakfast: Located just outside Maxwell, this charming B&B offers cozy rooms and a warm welcome.

What to Buy in Maxwell: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Maxwell is home to many unique and locally produced products, making it a great place to shop for souvenirs. Here are some of the top items to bring home:

  • Beef: Nebraska is known for its beef, and visitors can take home locally-raised steaks and other cuts of beef.
  • Wine: The vineyards in the area produce delicious wines, making it a great souvenir.
  • Local crafts: Visitors can pick up handmade crafts and artwork at local shops and galleries.

Local Culture and Customs of Maxwell

Maxwell is a friendly and welcoming city, where locals take pride in their agricultural heritage and Midwestern traditions. Visitors can expect a warm welcome and friendly conversation when interacting with locals. It is worth noting that Nebraska is a conservative state, and visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Maxwell

Maxwell is a destination that can be enjoyed year-round. The best time to visit will depend on your interests and the activities you want to participate in. The summer months are ideal for outdoor adventures, while the winter months are perfect for cozying up indoors and enjoying the beautiful winter scenery.

Safety and Health

Safety in Maxwell

Maxwell is a relatively safe destination for travelers, with low crime rates. Visitors should take normal precautions, such as locking their cars and securing valuables.

Health in Maxwell

Getting to Maxwell: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Maxwell

The nearest airport to Maxwell is North Platte Regional Airport, which is approximately 30 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Maxwell

There are no bus stations in Maxwell, but Greyhound services North Platte, which is a short drive away.

Other Options

Maxwell is easily accessible by car, with Interstate 80 and US Highway 30 running through the area.

How to Get Around in Maxwell

The best way to get around Maxwell is by car. Visitors can also bike or walk to explore the downtown area.
