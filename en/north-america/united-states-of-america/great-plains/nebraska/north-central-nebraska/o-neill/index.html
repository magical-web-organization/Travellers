Introduction to O’Neill

Nestled in the heart of North Central Nebraska, O’Neill is a charming city that offers endless opportunities for outdoor adventures, delicious food and drink, and a peek into the region’s rich history and culture. Known as Nebraska’s Irish Capital, the city is known for its vibrant St. Patrick’s Day celebrations.

In this travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at O’Neill’s must-visit attractions, fantastic hiking and biking routes, where to stay, what to eat, and more.

List of Places to Visit in O’Neill

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

O’Neill has a rich history that can be explored through its museums and historical sites. Here are some of the most notable:

  • Irish Capitol Historic District: Take a stroll through the Irish Capitol Historic District, which boasts an impressive collection of Victorian-era homes, churches, and commercial buildings.

  • Holt County Courthouse: Built in 1906, the Holt County Courthouse is a stunning example of Beaux-Arts architecture.

  • Holt County Historical Society Museum: Learn about the region’s history through the exhibits at the Holt County Historical Society Museum.

Natural Attractions of O’Neill: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

O’Neill is surrounded by breathtaking natural beauty and offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures. Here are some of the best places to explore:

  • Cowboy Trail: Go for a scenic hike or bike ride along the famous Cowboy Trail, which winds through North Central Nebraska and offers breathtaking views of the countryside.

  • Rockyford Lake State Recreation Area: Fishing enthusiasts will love Rockyford Lake, which is stocked with bass, trout, and other species. The surrounding state recreation area also has plenty of picnic areas and hiking trails to explore.

  • Holt County State Wildlife Management Area: Birdwatchers and nature lovers alike will appreciate the Holt County State Wildlife Management Area, which is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of O’Neill

There’s no shortage of stunning hiking and biking routes in O’Neill, so make sure to pack your hiking boots or bike helmet! Here are some trails you shouldn’t miss:

  • Cowboy Trail: This 321-mile-long trail winds through some of the most picturesque areas of Nebraska and is perfect for hikers and bikers of all skill levels.

  • Holt County Trail: This 34-mile-long trail is a favorite among locals and offers plenty of scenic views and opportunities to spot local wildlife.

Best Things to Do in O’Neill: Activities and Tours

In addition to exploring the region’s natural beauty and rich history, there are plenty of other activities and tours to enjoy in O’Neill:

  • St. Patrick’s Day Celebrations: If you’re visiting in March, make sure to check out the city’s vibrant St. Patrick’s Day celebrations, which include parades, live music, and plenty of green beer.

  • Drive-In Movie Theater: Take a trip back in time by catching a movie at the O’Neill Drive-In Theater, one of the last remaining drive-ins in Nebraska.

  • Farmers Market: Check out the local farmers market, which showcases the region’s best produce, meats, and artisanal products.

Week-Long Itinerary for O’Neill

Here’s a week-long itinerary for exploring all that O’Neill has to offer:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the Irish Capitol Historic District and enjoy a meal at a local pub.
Day 2 Visit the Holt County Courthouse and Holt County Historical Society Museum.
Day 3 Hike or bike along the Cowboy Trail.
Day 4 Go fishing at Rockyford Lake State Recreation Area.
Day 5 Enjoy a day of birdwatching at the Holt County State Wildlife Management Area.
Day 6 Take a drive out to the nearby Niobrara River for a scenic picnic lunch.
Day 7 Attend a St. Patrick’s Day celebration (if visiting in March) or visit the O’Neill Drive-In Theater.

Food and Drink Scene of O’Neill

Must-Try Dishes

O’Neill’s food scene is a mix of hearty Midwestern fare and traditional Irish offerings. Here are some dishes to try:

  • Corned Beef and Cabbage: A favorite of the city’s St. Patrick’s Day celebrations, this dish is a must-try for lovers of Irish cuisine.

  • Steak: Nebraska is known for its delicious beef, so make sure to try a steak while you’re in town.

  • Runza: This popular sandwich is a Nebraska classic and features a delicious blend of ground beef, cabbage, and onion.

Local Beverages

When it comes to the drink scene, O’Neill offers plenty of options for beer and whiskey enthusiasts.

  • Kinkaider Brewing Co.: This local brewery offers a range of beers, including IPAs, stouts, and lagers.

  • Jameson’s Pub: This cozy pub is a favorite among locals and visitors alike and offers an extensive selection of whiskey and other spirits.

Where to Stay in O’Neill: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

O’Neill has plenty of options for accommodations, whether you’re looking for a cozy guesthouse or a full-service hotel.

  • O’Neill Hotel: Located in the heart of the city, the O’Neill Hotel offers comfortable rooms, an onsite restaurant, and plenty of amenities.

  • The Lofte: This charming guesthouse is perfect for those looking for a cozy, home-away-from-home feel.

What to Buy in O’Neill: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for the perfect souvenir or gift to remember your trip, consider picking up some of these local products:

  • Cornhusker State Apparel: Get decked out in some Nebraska pride with a t-shirt or hat from Cornhusker State Apparel.

  • Nebraska-made food products: Pick up some locally made jams, jellies, and other food items at the farmers market or local shops.

Local Culture and Customs of O’Neill

O’Neill is proud of its Irish heritage, which is evident in the city’s annual St. Patrick’s Day celebrations and numerous pubs and Irish-themed restaurants.

Best Time to Visit O’Neill

If you’re looking to attend the city’s famous St. Patrick’s Day celebrations, March is the best time to visit. Otherwise, spring and fall are both great times to enjoy the region’s beautiful scenery and moderate temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of O’Neill

Do:

  • Do attend a St. Patrick’s Day celebration if you’re visiting in March.

  • Do try the local cuisine, including corned beef and cabbage and Nebraska steak.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to pack comfortable shoes and clothes for exploring the region’s hiking and biking trails.

  • Don’t miss out on the local beer and whiskey scene.

Safety and Health

Safety in O’Neill

O’Neill is a safe and welcoming city, but visitors should still use common sense and take necessary precautions to ensure their safety.

Health in O’Neill

O’Neill has several clinics and pharmacies in case of any medical emergencies.

Getting to O’Neill: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near O’Neill

The closest major airport to O’Neill is Sioux Falls Regional Airport, which is about three hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near O’Neill

Greyhound operates a bus station in Norfolk, Nebraska, which is about an hour’s drive from O’Neill.

Other Options

If you prefer to drive, O’Neill is located at the intersection of Highways 281 and 20, making it easily accessible by car.

How to Get Around in O’Neill

While having your own car is the easiest way to get around, O’Neill also has several taxi and limo services.

Where to Go Next from O’Neill: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of North Central Nebraska, here are some nearby destinations to check out:

  • Niobrara State Park: Located about an hour’s drive from O’Neill, Niobrara State Park boasts stunning views of the Niobrara River and offers plenty of hiking and camping opportunities.

  • Sandhills Journey Scenic Byway: This 272-mile-long scenic byway showcases the beauty of Nebraska’s Sandhills region and offers plenty of opportunities to explore small, quaint towns along the way.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a lover of outdoor adventures, history and culture, or hearty Midwestern cuisine, O’Neill has something for everyone. With this comprehensive guide, you’re sure to have a wonderful trip to this charming Nebraska city.
