Introduction to Wildcat Hills

Welcome to Wildcat Hills, a scenic and tranquil destination in the Nebraska Panhandle. It’s an ideal place for nature lovers, adventure enthusiasts, and those who love to explore the cultural background and history of a place. Wildcat Hills boasts a serene landscape blanketed with prairie grasses, rugged bluffs, and winding canyons that offer a breathtaking scenic view. This travel guide aims to highlight the best places to visit, top things to do, where to stay, and how to get around in Wildcat Hills, Nebraska, along with important travel information to help you plan your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Wildcat Hills

When planning a trip to Wildcat Hills, it’s essential to get a good understanding of the popular attractions, how to get there, as well as some lesser-known places to visit. Here are some top places you should visit in Wildcat Hills.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chimney Rock National Historic Site - A beautiful natural landmark that served as a vital landmark to early pioneers of the Oregon-California Trail.
  • Sallows Military Museum - The museum exhibits an extensive collection of military artifacts and memorabilia, ranging from the Revolutionary War up to the present day.
  • Legacy of the Plains Museum - The museum displays the history of the Platte River Valley and its people through interactive exhibits and special events.

Natural Attractions of Wildcat Hills: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wildcat Hills State Recreation Area - This state recreation area is famous for its numerous hiking and biking trails, which offer stunning scenic views.
  • Scotts Bluff National Monument - This breathtaking monument boasts some of the most dramatic landscapes and hiking trails in the region, making it a must-visit destination in Wildcat Hills.
  • Riverside discovery Center - A sanctuary for rescued animals located in Scottsbluff, this place will be loved by animal lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wildcat Hills

Wildcat Hills is a heaven for outdoor enthusiasts. The rolling hills, rugged bluffs, and winding canyons make it a perfect place for hiking and biking. Here are some noteworthy hiking and biking trails in Wildcat Hills:

  • North and South MacTrail - A scenic four-mile trail that offers spectacular views of the Wildcat Hills.
  • Wildcat Trail - A 4.3-mile trail that winds through the rugged terrain, and features abundant wildlife, including bighorn sheep and mule deer.
  • Prairie View Trail - A beautiful seven-mile trail that rewards visitors with stunning panoramic views of the Wildcat Hills.

Best Things to Do in Wildcat Hills: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, Wildcat Hills offers several other exciting activities and tours. Here are some of the best things you can do in Wildcat Hills:

  • Go birdwatching at Oliver Reservoir - The reservoir is home to a variety of bird species, including sandhill cranes, pelicans, and great blue herons.
  • Take a helicopter ride - For an unforgettable aerial experience, take a helicopter ride to see the Wildcat Hills from above.
  • Join a guided tour - Several guided tours are available to explore the Wildcat Hills, from wildlife watching to fossil hunts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Wildcat Hills

If you’re planning to spend a week in Wildcat Hills, here is an itinerary that will let you experience all of the wonderful things:

Day 1 - 2: Explore Chimney Rock National Historic Site, and visit Scotts Bluff National Monument, and Riverside discovery Center.

Day 3 - 4: Go hiking on the Wildcat Trail, and enjoy the natural beauty of Wildcat Hills State Recreation Area.

Day 5: Take a helicopter ride for an aerial view of Wildcat Hills, followed by bird watching at Oliver Reservoir in the afternoon.

Day 6 - 7: Join a guided tour and explore Legacy of the Plains Museum, and learn more about the Platte River Valley.

Food and Drink Scene in Wildcat Hills

Wildcat Hills has a vibrant food and drink scene, with several options available for food lovers. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nebraska-style steak - A local specialty made with prime cuts of beef, seasoned with a blend of spices.
  • Buffalo and Elk burgers - The meats are sourced from the nearby ranches and offer a unique experience.
  • Kool-Aid - A famous Nebraska drink, invented in Hastings Nebraska, and brought to market through advertising in Omaha, Nebraska.

Local Beverages

  • Nebraska Craft Beer - Wildcat Hills has several breweries, so try some locally crafted beer such as Zipline Brewing, Nebraska Brewing Company or White Elm Brewing Co.

Where to Stay in Wildcat Hills: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Wildcat Hills offers plenty of accommodation options for all budgets and requirements. Here are some of the top places to stay in Wildcat Hills:

  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites Scottsbluff - A modern hotel, located in Scottsbluff, with beautiful views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Circle S Lodge - A cowboy-themed hotel, located near Chimney Rock National Historic Site, offers a western feel in your stay and some chance to hear cowboy poetry.
  • Robidoux RV Park - For campers, Robidoux RV park Provides Campsites and Cabins.

What to Buy in Wildcat Hills: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

From handcrafted items to local produce, there are plenty of souvenir options to purchase from Wildcat Hills. Some of the must-buy local products include:

Local Culture and Customs of Wildcat Hills

Wildcat Hills has a rich history, and its people are friendly. The community here is passionate about preserving the area’s history, and visitors are requested to respect the local customs and culture. The people are welcoming, and visitors are advised to say hello to people they meet walking and engage in a friendly conversation.

Best Time to Visit Wildcat Hills

The best time to visit Wildcat Hills is during the spring and fall seasons when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities. Summers can be hot, and winters can be cold and snowy, so plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wildcat Hills

Safety and Health

Safety in Wildcat Hills

Wildcat Hills is generally safe and offers a peaceful environment to visitors. Important to note that the region does have wildlife, so always pay attention to the warnings and safety guidelines given at the time of the adventure.

Health in Wildcat Hills

Consider getting vaccinated and check with the doctor before heading out. Check with State recreation headquarters or park rangers regarding tick warnings or any other relevant information.

Getting to Wildcat Hills: Transportation Options and Logistics

Wildcat Hills is located in the Western Panhandle of Nebraska, pretty close to Cheyenne, Wyoming, and Denver, Colorado. To get there, you can take a flight to Western Nebraska Regional Airport and rent a car. There are also several bus stations nearby such as the one in Scottsbluff, NE. You can take an Uber, rented car, or taxi from the airport or bus station to reach Wildcat Hills. The area is more car-friendly, and renting a car is an excellent idea since the hike sites are spread far from each other.

Airports Near Wildcat Hills

  • Western Nebraska Regional Airport (BFF) - Located near Scottsbluff, NE, airport serves flights from Denver and places nearby.

Bus Stations Near Wildcat Hills

  • The Point at Scottsbluff bus station is located in the city center and serves travelers from Denver and other nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Wildcat Hills

The roads are well-maintained, and options like rental cars, taxis, or Uber are always available.

Where to Go Next from Wildcat Hills: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ashfall Fossil Beds State Historic Park - Located 70 miles from Wildcat Hills, this park offers visitors a chance to explore prehistoric fossils.
  • Custer State Park - Located 200 miles from Wildcat Hills, South Dakota, this state park is well known for its scenic beauty and several outdoor activities.
