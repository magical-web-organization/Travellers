Your Ultimate Travel Guide to Kimball, Nebraska

Located in the Nebraska Panhandle and part of the Great Plains region in the United States of America, Kimball is a hidden gem that offers a unique outdoor experience and a peek into western culture. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip to Kimball, Nebraska, with must-visit attractions, a week-long itinerary, transportation options, and more.

Introduction to Kimball

Kimball is a small town in western Nebraska that offers visitors a glimpse of western culture. The town is surrounded by picturesque views of sandhills, canyons, and prairie grasslands. Kimball has a rich history that dates back to the early 1800s and has a population of about 2,500 residents.

List of Places to Visit in Kimball

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Great Western Cattle Trail Marker: The marker is a symbol commemorating the historical route that drove cattle from Texas and Mexico to northern markets.
  • Kimball County Historical Society Museum: This museum showcases the area’s history, including displays of pioneer life and railroad history.
  • Little Outlaw RV Park and Museum: This unique museum is home to a collection of antique tractors, automobiles, and other farm-related memorabilia.

Natural Attractions of Kimball: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Oliver Reservoir: The reservoir offers a relaxing atmosphere for boating, fishing, and picnicking.
  • Panorama Point: This scenic spot, located 11 miles north of Kimball, offers a panoramic view of western Nebraska and the neighboring state of Colorado.
  • Wildcat Hills State Recreation Area: Visitors can enjoy hiking and horseback riding trails, picnic areas, and a nature center with educational exhibits.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kimball

  • Wildcat Hills State Recreation Area: The park features several trails of varying difficulty levels that offer scenic views of the landscape.
  • Kimball Bike Path: The three-mile loop is perfect for biking or walking and offers a chance to view local flora and fauna.

Best Things to Do in Kimball: Activities and Tours

  • Oregon Trail Wagon Train: This unique experience takes visitors on a wagon train journey along the Oregon Trail and offers a chance to experience pioneer life.
  • Olde Main Street Inn: The bed and breakfast is located in a restored 1899 building and offers a Western hospitality experience complemented by antique furnishings and decor.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kimball

Day 1: Kimball County Historical Society Museum, Great Western Cattle Trail Marker, and Little Outlaw RV Park and Museum.

Day 2: Explore the Panorama Point scenic route in the morning and picnic at Oliver Reservoir in the afternoon.

Day 3-4: Wildcat Hills State Recreation Area - Hike, explore, and take in the scenic views.

Day 5: Oregon Trail Wagon Train and a visit to the nearby Chimney Rock National Historic Site.

Day 6-7: Take a relaxing day at Olde Main Street Inn Bed & Breakfast, explore local shops, and dine at Whiskey Creek.

Food and Drink Scene of Kimball

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken Fried Steak: Served at most local diners, the dish features lightly breaded and fried steak smothered in gravy.
  • Nebraska Beef: The state is known for producing high-quality beef, which is featured on most local restaurant menus.
  • Western Omelette: This hearty breakfast is made with eggs, diced ham, green peppers, onions, and cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Kinkaider Brewing Company: Located in nearby Scottsbluff, the brewery offers a rotating selection of craft brews.
  • Whiskey Creek: The restaurant also serves a selection of craft beers from Nebraska and surrounding states.

Where to Stay in Kimball: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites: The hotel features an indoor pool, complimentary breakfast, and a fitness center.
  • Little Outlaw RV Park and Museum: The park offers RV spots, tent camping, and rustic cabins.
  • Olde Main Street Inn Bed & Breakfast: The bed and breakfast features Western hospitality, complimentary breakfast, and private bathrooms.

What to Buy in Kimball: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Kimball County Historical Society: The museum’s gift shop features local history books and unique souvenirs.
  • Sandhills Pottery: The gallery features handmade pottery that reflects the unique culture and landscape of the Great Plains.

Local Culture and Customs of Kimball

Kimball takes pride in its agricultural heritage and western culture. Visitors can expect to see many cowboy hats, boots, and western wear. The town also has a strong sense of community and hospitality, and visitors are often welcomed by friendly locals.

Best Time to Visit Kimball

The best time to visit Kimball is during the fall (September-November) or spring (April-June) when the weather is pleasant, and the landscape bursts with vibrant colors. Summer (June-August) can be hot, but it’s a great time for outdoor activities and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kimball

Do:

  • Embrace the western culture and wear cowboy hats and boots.
  • Explore the outdoors and take scenic drives.
  • Visit local small businesses and artisans.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and culture.
  • Leave a mess while hiking or picnicking.
  • Miss out on the local beef and western cuisine.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kimball

Kimball is a safe and friendly town with low crime rates. Visitors should take standard safety precautions such as locking car doors and securing valuables.

Health in Kimball

Kimball has a local hospital, clinics, and pharmacies. Visitors should have adequate health insurance and take standard precautions, such as wearing sunscreen and drinking plenty of water, during outdoor activities.

Getting to Kimball: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kimball

The closest major airport is Denver International Airport in Colorado, which is three hours away. The Western Nebraska Regional Airport, located in nearby Scottsbluff, offers regional flights.

Bus Stations Near Kimball

The closest Greyhound bus station is in nearby Sidney, 30 miles away.

Other Options

Kimball is located along Interstate 80, making it easily accessible by car. Visitors can rent cars at nearby airports or in larger cities.

How to Get Around in Kimball

The town is small and easy to navigate by car or bike. Visitors can also walk to many attractions within the town.

Where to Go Next from Kimball: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Chimney Rock National Historic Site: A short drive from Kimball, this national landmark offers visitors a chance to see one of the most famous landmarks on the Oregon Trail.
  • Scotts Bluff National Monument: Located in nearby Scottsbluff, this monument features a towering rock formation and is also an American Indian sacred site.
  • Fort Laramie National Historic Site: Located just over the Wyoming state line, this historic site offers a glimpse into the early pioneer days of the frontier.

Conclusion

Kimball, Nebraska, offers a unique western experience in the heart of the Great Plains. From outdoor adventures to historical landmarks, there’s something for everyone in this charming town. Use this guide to plan your visit, and discover the beauty and hospitality of Kimball.
