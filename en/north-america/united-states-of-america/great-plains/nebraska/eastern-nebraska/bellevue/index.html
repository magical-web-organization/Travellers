Introduction to Bellevue

Bellevue, a city in Eastern Nebraska, is a hidden gem that offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. This vibrant city is located in the heart of the Great Plains and is a great starting point for travelers who want to explore the Midwest. Bellevue has a rich cultural heritage, and its museums and historical places are a must-visit for anyone interested in American history. Apart from that, there are plenty of outdoor adventures, scenic views, and delicious local cuisine to indulge in.

List of Places to Visit in Bellevue

Whether you want to explore Bellevue’s rich cultural heritage, dine at local eateries, or soak in the natural beauty of the area, there is something for everyone in this charming city. Here are some of the top places to visit in Bellevue:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bellevue has a rich history, and its museums and historical places are a testament to its heritage. Explore the following cultural attractions to get a taste of the past:

1. Fontenelle Bank Building Museum

Built-in 1888, this building now serves as a museum and is listed on the National Register of Historic Places.

2. Sarpy County Museum

Learn about the county’s rich history and the people who made it what it is today.

3. Sarpy County Courthouse Museum

The Courthouse Museum offers a glimpse into local government functions in the early 20th century.

Natural Attractions of Bellevue: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

When it comes to natural attractions, Bellevue is not far behind. The city boasts stunning parks, trails, and wildlife for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the top outdoor attractions to explore:

1. Fontenelle Forest Nature Center

Experience the natural beauty of the area while hiking the trails in this 1,400-acre nature center.

2. Haworth Park

Relax amid nature, walk the trail to the Missouri River, or rent a paddleboat to explore the lake.

3. Platte River State Park

Explore nature on foot, by bike, or by water in this 422-acre park that’s perfect for a day trip or a weekend stay.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bellevue

Bellevue offers visitors plenty of opportunities to get outside and explore. Whether you’re a seasoned hiker or a beginner, there are trails that cater to everyone. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Bellevue:

  • Keystone Trail
  • Papio Trail
  • Fontenelle Forest Trail
  • Two Rivers State Recreation Area Trail

Best Things to Do in Bellevue: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for things to do in Bellevue, apart from exploring its museums, parks, and trails, check out the following activities and tours:

  • Bellevue Berry and Pumpkin Ranch

Week-Long Itinerary for Bellevue

If you have a week to spare, you can explore Bellevue in-depth by visiting the following attractions on each day of the week:

Day 1: Fontenelle Bank Building Museum, Sarpy County Courthouse Museum, and Bellevue Farmers Market

Day 2: Fontenelle Forest Nature Center, Haworth Park, and La Vista Community Center

Day 3: John B. Maiers Jr. Memorial Park, Sarpy County Museum, and Bellevue Berry and Pumpkin Ranch

Day 4: Platte River State Park, Papio Trail, and Two Rivers State Recreation Area Trail

Day 5: Sarpy County Fair, Fontenelle Hills Golf Course, and American Heroes Park

Day 6: Greenville Berry Farm, Take Aim Indoor Shooting Sports and Training Facility, and Offutt Air Force Base

Day 7: Joslyn Castle, Durham Museum, and Heartland of America Park

Local Culture and Customs of Bellevue

Bellevue, like many small towns in the United States, is steeped in local culture and customs. Visitors are encouraged to embrace and respect these customs. Some important customs to note include:

  • Be respectful of local laws and ordinances. The use of recreational marijuana, for instance, is illegal in Nebraska.
  • Be courteous to local residents, and most importantly, respect private property.
  • Tipping is customary in Bellevue; it is an expected practice in restaurants, hairdressers, and any other service that involves personal interaction.

Best Time to Visit Bellevue

The best time to visit Bellevue is in the summer months from June to August, when the weather is pleasant, and the city is alive with events and festivals. However, if you’re looking for a quieter time, consider visiting during the fall or spring months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bellevue

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Bellevue:

Do:

  • Spend time exploring the great outdoors.
  • Respect local customs, traditions, and property.
  • Sample local foods and beverages, like Nebraska craft beers.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public areas.
  • Expect to find recreational marijuana. It is illegal in Nebraska.
  • Disrespect local laws and ordinances.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bellevue:

Bellevue is a safe city for visitors. However, it’s always a good idea to take the necessary precautions when traveling, such as avoiding walking alone at night and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Bellevue:

Bellevue has a range of medical facilities and hospitals should any medical issues arise. Additionally, it’s always a good idea to carry health insurance that covers unexpected medical expenses.
