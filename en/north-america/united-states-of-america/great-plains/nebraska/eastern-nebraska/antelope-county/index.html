Introduction to Antelope County

Antelope County is a beautiful county located in Eastern Nebraska, in the Great Plains region of the United States of America. The county is known for its rich history, natural beauty, and unique culture. The county seat is located in the city of Neligh. Antelope County is a perfect destination for families and adventure enthusiasts who want to explore the great outdoors, learn about history and culture, and indulge in local cuisine. In this guide, we will list the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more in Antelope County.

List of Places to Visit in Antelope County

Visitors to Antelope County have a variety of cultural and natural attractions to choose from. Here are the top places to visit in Antelope County:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Antelope County Museum

The Antelope County Museum is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in the history of Antelope County. The museum is located in Neligh and features exhibits on the county’s early settlers, agricultural heritage, Native American tribes, and more.

2. Neligh Mill State Historic Site

The Neligh Mill State Historic Site is a restored grist mill located in Neligh. Visitors can tour the mill and learn about its history, as well as view the scenic views of the Elkhorn River.

3. Oakdale Carnegie Library

Oakdale Carnegie Library is a historic library located in the town of Oakdale. The library was built in the early 1900s and still serves as a public library today.

Natural Attractions of Antelope County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Grove Lake Wildlife Management Area

The Grove Lake Wildlife Management Area is a popular destination for hunting, fishing, and wildlife viewing. The area is home to a variety of animals, including deer, turkeys, and waterfowl.

2. Ashfall Fossil Beds State Historical Park

Ashfall Fossil Beds State Historical Park is a unique attraction that features preserved fossils from prehistoric animals that were trapped in volcanic ash millions of years ago. Visitors can view the fossils and learn about the history of the area.

3. Rock Creek Station State Historical Park

Rock Creek Station State Historical Park is a beautiful park that offers hiking trails, camping, and scenic views. The park is also home to a museum that showcases the history of the Pony Express and the Oregon Trail.
