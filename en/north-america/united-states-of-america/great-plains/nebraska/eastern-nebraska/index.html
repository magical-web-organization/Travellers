Introduction to Eastern Nebraska

Eastern Nebraska, nestled in the Great Plains region, is a hidden gem in the heart of the United States. From its vibrant cities to its breathtaking natural scenery, Eastern Nebraska offers an array of attractions and activities for travelers to explore. Whether you’re an avid outdoor enthusiast, history buff, or foodie, Eastern Nebraska has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Eastern Nebraska

Here are the top attractions and things to do in Eastern Nebraska:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Joslyn Art Museum
  • Durham Museum
  • SAC Aerospace Museum
  • The Strategic Air Command & Aerospace Museum
  • The Durham Museum
  • Boys Town

  • Pioneer Park Nature Center
  • Arbor Day Farm

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eastern Nebraska

Eastern Nebraska has a plethora of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are a few of the most popular routes:

  • MoPac East Recreational Trail
  • Wabash Trace Nature Trail
  • Homestead Trail
  • Keystone Trail
  • Big Elk Creek Trail

Best Things to Do in Eastern Nebraska: Activities and Tours

  • Hot air balloon Ride over Omaha and the surrounding countryside
  • Visit Nebraska’s largest brewery: the Lucky Bucket Brewing Company
  • Riverboat cruises on the Missouri River
  • Visit the Old Market for unique shopping, dining and cultural experiences
  • Attend the annual Nebraska Renaissance Faire
  • Go ice skating at the Ralston Arena in Ralston

Week-Long Itinerary for Eastern Nebraska

Day 1: Arrive in Omaha and explore the Old Market, Henry Doorly Zoo and Aquarium.

Day 2: Head to Mahoney State Park and go horseback riding, ziplining, and hiking.

Day 3: Visit the Nebraska City and explore the Arbor Day Farm, Kimmel Orchard and Vineyard.

Day 4: Explore the SAC Aerospace Museum, Boys Town, Joslyn Art Museum in Omaha.

Day 5: Go canoeing or kayaking on the Platte River or schedule a hot air balloon ride.

Day 6: Visit Lincoln, the state’s capital, and tour the Nebraska State Capitol and its surroundings.

Day 7: Visit the Fontenelle Forest Nature Center and Pioneer Park Nature Center for outdoor hiking and trails.

Food and Drink Scene of Eastern Nebraska

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nebraska’s popular snack, Kool-Aid was invented here. Try Kool-Aid pickles or Kool-Aid marinated steaks.
  • Nebraska runs on steak. So, do not miss a chance to try the local beef.
  • Reuben sandwich is a local specialty and can be found at most local diners.
  • Enjoy a slice of the famous Valentino’s pizza which was featured by the Food Network

Local Beverages

  • Nebraska is famous for its beer. Visit craft breweries such as Zipline Brewing Co., Empyrean Brewing Co., and Blue Blood Brewing Co.
  • Wine lovers should visit Soaring Wings Vineyard in Nebraska City

What to Buy in Eastern Nebraska: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cornhusker memorabilia: Keychains, t-shirts, and hats at Husker Hounds or Nebraska Redzone.
  • Local beef: Omaha Steaks
  • Craft beer: Zipline Brewing Co. or Empyrean Brewing Co.

Local Culture and Customs of Eastern Nebraska

Nebraskans are warm and friendly, people who take their Cornhusker football seriously. It is customary to wave when meeting someone on the street even if you are a stranger.

Best Time to Visit Eastern Nebraska

Eastern Nebraska has a humid continental climate with four distinct seasons. The best time to visit Eastern Nebraska is between April and October when temperatures are mild, and the trees are lush green. Fall colors in October also make for a scenic view.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eastern Nebraska

Do:

  • Do visit Omaha’s Durham Museum or SAC Aerospace Museum.
  • Do hike the trails at the Fontenelle Forest Nature Center.
  • Do sample the local craft beer.
  • Do order a Reuben Sandwich - a local specialty.

Don’t:

  • Don’t arrive late at a Kool-Aid Festival (they sell out quickly).
  • Don’t forget to bring a light jacket/sweater, even during summer.

Safety and Health

Safety in Eastern Nebraska

Eastern Nebraska is a safe place to travel, with low crime rates. However, it is always advisable to take the necessary safety precautions and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Eastern Nebraska

Medical facilities and health services are readily available throughout Eastern Nebraska. Tourists are advised to carry health insurance and to pack any necessary prescription medication.

Other Options

  • Arrange a shuttle or taxi service from the airport or bus station.

How to Get Around in Eastern Nebraska

The best way to get around Eastern Nebraska is by car. Taxis, Uber, and Lyft are also available in the metro Omaha area with limited service in other parts of Eastern Nebraska.

Where to Go Next from Eastern Nebraska: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Head west to Denver, Colorado, for a road trip through the Rocky Mountains.
  • Visit Kansas City, Missouri, to experience the world-famous barbecue.
  • Drive north to Sioux Falls, South Dakota, for hiking and scenic views.
  • Discover the natural beauty of Western Nebraska’s National Forests and Parks.

Conclusion

Eastern Nebraska is a beautiful and unique destination, offering something for every type of traveler. Its friendly communities, natural beauty, and vibrant cities make it an excellent addition to any travel itinerary. Happy travels!
