Introduction to Wagoner

Wagoner is a charming city located in Green Country, in the state of Oklahoma. The city is known for its rich history, natural beauty, and unique attractions.

Wagoner is home to a variety of outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and delicious food. Whether you’re an adventure seeker, history buff, or foodie, there is something for everyone in this beautiful city.

In this ultimate travel guide to Wagoner, we’ll explore the top attractions, best things to do, where to stay, and more. So, pack your bags and get ready for an unforgettable journey to Wagoner, Oklahoma!

List of Places to Visit in Wagoner

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Wagoner Historical Museum - Learn about the history of Wagoner and its surrounding areas through exhibits, photos, and artifacts.

  • The Territorial Playhouse - Catch a live performance at this beautifully restored, 1920’s historic theatre.

  • The Three Rivers Museum - Discover the rich history of the Three Rivers region, including pottery, Native American artifacts, and more.

Natural Attractions of Wagoner: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sequoyah Bay State Park - This stunning park offers hiking, fishing, boating and camping opportunities, all with beautiful views of nearby Lake Hudson.

  • Cherokee Landing State Park - This park is a popular spot for fishing and camping, with easy access to the Illinois River.

  • The Ozark Trail - Hike through stunning forests, climb rugged terrain, and enjoy the beauty of the Ozarks at this popular trail.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wagoner

  • The Katy Trail - This trail is perfect for hiking, biking, and horseback riding, with beautiful views of nearby Lake Waurika.

  • The Turkey Mountain Urban Wilderness Area - This trail offers stunning views of Tulsa and the Arkansas River, with plenty of challenging terrain for experienced hikers.

Best Things to Do in Wagoner: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Wagoner Farmers Market - Check out the local produce and handmade crafts at this popular outdoor market.

  • Take a Tour of Sequoyah’s Cabin - Explore the home of Sequoyah, the inventor of the Cherokee Alphabet.

  • Go Whitewater Rafting - Take on the rapids of the eight-mile stretch of the Illinois River, through the scenic countryside of Wagoner.

Food and Drink Scene of Wagoner

Must-Try Dishes

  • Oklahoma-style BBQ - Try the local favorite: smoked brisket.

  • Fried Okra - This traditional Southern dish is a must-try.

  • Biscuits and Gravy - This classic breakfast dish is a delicious way to start your day.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer - Wagoner boasts a variety of local breweries, offering unique and delicious craft beer.

  • Pecan Mead - This sweet mead is made with local honey and pecans, making it a uniquely Oklahoma drink.

Where to Stay in Wagoner: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Prairie Song Bed and Breakfast - This charming bed and breakfast offers a quiet retreat, with beautiful gardens and cozy rooms.

  • The Wagoner Inn and Suites - This affordable hotel offers comfortable rooms and a great location, just minutes from downtown Wagoner.

  • The Secret Garden Cottages - These adorable, romantic cottages offer a peaceful escape, with beautiful gardens and luxurious amenities.

What to Buy in Wagoner: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Native American Crafts - Wagoner is located near many Native American reservations, offering a variety of beautifully crafted jewelry and pottery.

  • Pecans - Oklahoma is known for its large pecan orchards, so be sure to grab a bag of locally grown pecans to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Wagoner

Wagoner is home to a variety of cultures, including Native American, Southern, and Western influences. Visitors should be respectful of these different cultures and customs.

Best Time to Visit Wagoner

The best time to visit Wagoner is in the spring, from March to May, or in the fall, from September to November. During these times, the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wagoner

Do:

  • Do take advantage of the beautiful outdoor activities Wagoner has to offer.

  • Do try the local cuisine.

  • Do be respectful of the different cultures and customs in the area.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural beauty of Wagoner.

  • Don’t disrespect the local cultures or customs.

  • Don’t forget to tip your servers and tour guides.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wagoner

Wagoner is a relatively safe city, but visitors should still be aware of their surroundings and take safety precautions, especially when hiking or participating in other outdoor activities.

Health in Wagoner

Wagoner has a variety of healthcare facilities and clinics, but visitors should be aware of tick-borne illnesses in the area, especially during the summer months.

Getting to Wagoner: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Wagoner

  • Tulsa International Airport - About an hour east of Wagoner

Bus Stations Near Wagoner

  • Greyhound Bus Station - Located in Tulsa, about an hour’s drive from Wagoner.

Other Options

Wagoner is easily accessible by car, with several major highways running through the area.

How to Get Around in Wagoner

The best way to get around Wagoner is by car, as public transportation options are limited.

Where to Go Next from Wagoner: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tulsa, Oklahoma - Just an hour’s drive from Wagoner, Tulsa offers a variety of cultural attractions, shopping, and dining.

  • Oklahoma City, Oklahoma - Just a two-hour drive from Wagoner, Oklahoma City offers a variety of museums, parks, and other cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Wagoner, Oklahoma is a charming city with a rich history and beautiful natural surroundings. From outdoor activities and cultural attractions to delicious food and drink, there is something for everyone in this unique and inviting destination.
