Introduction to Bristow

Nestled in Green Country, in the Great Plains of Oklahoma, Bristow is a charming town that boasts a rich historical and cultural heritage, as well as stunning natural landscapes. Founded in 1898, Bristow has a population of just over 4,000 inhabitants and is known for its friendly locals and laid-back atmosphere. Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, you’ll find an array of exciting things to see and do in Bristow.

List of Places to Visit in Bristow

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bristow Historical Museum

The Bristow Historical Museum is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in learning about the town’s history. Housed in a former American Legion hall, this museum features a collection of artifacts and exhibits that showcase the town’s past, including the discovery of oil in the early 20th century and the impact of railroads on the town’s development.

Rock Creek Bridge

Built in 1924, Rock Creek Bridge is listed on the National Register of Historic Places and is one of the most iconic landmarks in Bristow. This 37-foot-long bridge spans across a scenic creek and serves as a picturesque backdrop for photography enthusiasts.

Natural Attractions of Bristow: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Redbud Valley Nature Preserve

Redbud Valley Nature Preserve is a beautiful natural area that covers over 300 acres of land and provides visitors with opportunities for hiking, biking, bird watching, and fishing. The park is home to a variety of wildlife, including white-tailed deer, wild turkeys, and bobcats, and also features several scenic overlooks and picnic areas.

Lake Sahoma

Located just outside of Bristow, Lake Sahoma is a popular spot for fishing, boating, and camping. The lake covers over 200 acres and is home to a variety of fish species, including bass and catfish, making it an ideal destination for anglers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bristow

Whether you prefer hiking or biking, Bristow has plenty of scenic trails to explore. Here are a few of the top routes:

  • Redbud Valley Nature Preserve Trail: This 3-mile trail winds through the park’s wooded areas and features several scenic overlooks.
  • Rock Creek Bridge Trail: This easy, 1.5-mile trail follows the creek and offers scenic views of the bridge and the surrounding landscape.
  • Sahoma Lake Trail: This 5-mile trail circles around Lake Sahoma and provides visitors with stunning views of the water and the surrounding forests.

Best Things to Do in Bristow: Activities and Tours

From cultural tours to outdoor adventures, there’s something for everyone in Bristow. Here are a few of the top things to do:

  • Take a guided tour of the Bristow Historical Museum to learn about the town’s rich history.
  • Go fishing at Lake Sahoma or Redbud Valley Nature Preserve.
  • Explore the scenic trails at Redbud Valley Nature Preserve or take a leisurely bike ride through town.
  • Visit Rock Creek Bridge for a photo opportunity and learn about its history.
  • Attend one of the annual festivals hosted by the town, such as the Oklahoma Freewheel, Bristow Peach Festival, or the Bristow Christmas Parade.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bristow

Day 1: Visit the Bristow Historical Museum and take a guided tour of the exhibits.

Day 2: Explore the scenic trails at Redbud Valley Nature Preserve or take a leisurely bike ride through town.

Day 3: Go fishing at Lake Sahoma and enjoy a picnic at one of the park’s scenic overlooks.

Day 4: Attend a local festival or event, such as the Oklahoma Freewheel or the Bristow Peach Festival.

Day 5: Visit Rock Creek Bridge and take in the scenic views of the surrounding countryside.

Day 6: Explore the quaint downtown area of Bristow and go shopping for souvenirs.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy a local meal at one of Bristow’s restaurants, such as Pete’s Place or the Rock Café.

Food and Drink Scene of Bristow

Must-Try Dishes

Bristow is known for its hearty and delectable meals that are perfect for any hungry traveler. Here are a few dishes that you shouldn’t miss:

  • Chicken Fried Steak: This classic dish is a staple in Bristow and can be found at many local restaurants.
  • Fried Catfish: Fresh catfish is a popular choice in Bristow and can be found on many local menus.
  • Fried Okra: A Southern classic that’s a must-try for any foodie visiting Bristow.

Local Beverages

Bristow may be a small town, but it has a vibrant beer scene. Visitors can enjoy locally brewed craft beers, such as the popular Redbud Lager or Route 66 Wheat, which are available at many local bars and restaurants.

Where to Stay in Bristow: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bristow has several accommodation options for visitors, including hotels, motels, and guesthouses. Here are a few top recommendations:

  • Shamrock Motel: Known for its charming rooms and friendly staff, the Shamrock Motel is a popular choice among travelers on a budget.
  • Holiday Inn Express: For those who prefer more modern amenities, the Holiday Inn Express offers comfortable rooms, an indoor pool, and a fitness center.
  • Rock Creek Guesthouse: This cozy guesthouse is located just steps away from the historic Rock Creek Bridge and features comfortable rooms and a scenic outdoor patio area.

What to Buy in Bristow: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bristow has a variety of local products and souvenirs that are perfect for bringing home as a memento of your trip. Here are a few top choices:

  • Bristow T-Shirts: Show off your love for Bristow with a custom t-shirt from one of the town’s local boutiques.

Local Culture and Customs of Bristow

Bristow is known for its friendly and welcoming locals who are proud of their town’s history and cultural heritage. Visitors should be respectful of the town’s customs and traditions, which include:

  • Celebrating the town’s annual festivals, such as the Oklahoma Freewheel, Bristow Peach Festival, and the Bristow Christmas Parade.

  • Engaging with locals and learning about their personal experiences and stories.

Best Time to Visit Bristow

The best time to visit Bristow is in the spring and fall when the weather is mild, and the town’s outdoor attractions are at their best. Visitors can enjoy the scenic views and outdoor activities without the crowds of the summer months.

Safety and Health

Bristow is a safe and welcoming town for visitors. Here are a few tips to ensure a safe and healthy trip:

Safety in Bristow

  • Avoid walking alone at night and stick to well-lit areas.
  • Lock doors and windows when leaving your accommodations or rental vehicles.
  • Be mindful of traffic and obey all traffic laws, especially when crossing the street.
  • Use caution when swimming or boating in natural areas and follow all posted safety rules and regulations.

Health in Bristow

  • Drink plenty of water and stay hydrated, especially during the hot summer months.
  • Wear sunscreen and protective clothing when spending time outdoors.
  • Check for ticks after spending time in natural areas and seek medical attention if you experience any symptoms of tick-borne illnesses.

Getting to Bristow: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bristow is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here are a few transportation options for getting to Bristow:

Airports Near Bristow

  • Tulsa International Airport: Located 40 miles west of Bristow, this airport provides flights to most major cities in the United States.

Bus Stations Near Bristow

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in Tulsa, this bus station provides transportation to several cities throughout the United States.

Other Options

  • Driving: Bristow is conveniently located off of US-75 and is easily accessible from most major highways.
  • Rental Cars: Rental cars are available at Tulsa International Airport and in surrounding cities.

How to Get Around in Bristow

As a small town, Bristow is easy to navigate on foot, by bike, or by car. Visitors should be mindful of traffic and obey all traffic laws when driving or crossing the street. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available in the area.

Where to Go Next from Bristow: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bristow is conveniently located near several other popular destinations in Oklahoma, including:

  • Tulsa: Located just 35 miles west of Bristow, Tulsa is known for its vibrant arts scene and world-class museums.
  • Oklahoma City: Located approximately 90 miles southwest of Bristow, Oklahoma City is the capital of the state and is home to several popular attractions, including the Oklahoma City National Memorial and Museum and the Oklahoma City Zoo.
