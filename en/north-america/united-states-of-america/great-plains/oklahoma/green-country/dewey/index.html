Introduction to Dewey

Nestled in the heart of Green Country, Oklahoma, Dewey is a charming city that boasts of natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. From its hiking trails to its museums, Dewey has so much to offer to history buffs, nature lovers, and adventure seekers alike.

List of Places to Visit in Dewey

Dewey has a plethora of attractions that visitors can explore. Here are some of the must-visit places in Dewey:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Dewey Hotel Museum: The Dewey Hotel Museum, built-in 1899, was a landmark on the famous Santa Fe Trail. Now, it serves as a museum dedicated to preserving the history and culture of Dewey.
  • Tom Mix Museum: This museum features artifacts and memorabilia related to Tom Mix, a Hollywood star and legendary cowboy, who was born in Dewey.
  • Dewey Cemetery: The Dewey Cemetery is an interesting site to visit for history buffs. Many of Dewey’s pioneer settlers, including local Civil War soldiers and Tom Mix, are buried here.

Natural Attractions of Dewey: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Pisgah: Mount Pisgah, located near Dewey, offers breathtaking views of the surrounding valleys and hills. The hiking trail to the top is a must-do for adventure seekers and nature lovers.
  • Osage Hills State Park: The Osage Hills State Park is a beautiful wilderness area, located just a short drive from Dewey. Visitors can enjoy a host of outdoor activities, such as hiking, fishing, and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dewey

Dewey is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking routes to explore. Some of the popular hiking and biking trails in Dewey include:

  • Prairie Song Trail
  • Tallgrass Prairie Preserve
  • Osage Prairie Trail

Best Things to Do in Dewey: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, visitors to Dewey can indulge in various other activities and tours. Here are some of the best things to do in Dewey:

  • Osage Hills Wine Trail: Dewey is home to several award-winning wineries, and visitors can embark on a wine trail to explore the area’s wine culture.
  • Dewey Fishing Derby: Every year, Dewey hosts a fishing derby that attracts people of all ages and skill levels. It’s a fun event that celebrates the area’s natural resources.
  • Dewey Roundup Club Rodeo: Visitors can experience the thrill of the rodeo by attending one of the Dewey Roundup Club’s rodeo events.

Food and Drink Scene of Dewey

Dewey has a vibrant food and drink scene that offers visitors a taste of local flavors and specialties.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken Fried Steak: A classic Oklahoma dish made with battered and fried steak, topped with gravy, and served with mashed potatoes.
  • Fried Okra: A delicious southern side dish made with breaded and fried okra.
  • Homemade Pies: Visitors must try one of the many homemade pies that Dewey has to offer, including pecan, apple, and berry pies.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: The Osage Hills Wine Trail features several award-winning wineries that produce excellent vintages.
  • Craft Beer: Oklahoma-based craft breweries such as Iron Monk Brewing Company and The Prairie Artisan Ales offer unique beer tasting experiences.

Local Culture and Customs of Dewey

Dewey has a strong cultural heritage, and visitors should be respectful of the local customs and traditions. The people of Dewey are friendly and welcoming, and visitors should take the time to learn about the area’s history and culture.

Best Time to Visit Dewey

Dewey’s temperate climate and moderate precipitation make it an excellent destination all year round. However, the best time to visit Dewey is in the spring or fall when the weather is mild, and the foliage is colorful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dewey

When visiting Dewey, there are certain do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Take the time to explore the city’s rich history and culture
  • Try the local food and beverages

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Disrespect other visitors or locals
  • Ignore the area’s safety guidelines

Safety and Health

Dewey is a safe destination for visitors, but travelers should always take precautions to protect their health and safety.

Safety in Dewey

  • Watch out for rattlesnakes and other wildlife when hiking or exploring natural areas
  • Be cautious when driving on rural roads, especially at night
  • Be mindful of traffic and pedestrian safety when exploring the city

Health in Dewey

  • Visitors should stay hydrated when engaging in outdoor activities, especially during hot weather
  • It’s essential to pack sunscreen and insect repellent to guard against sunburn and bug bites
  • Visitors should be aware of ticks and protect themselves against tick-borne diseases.

How to Get Around in Dewey

Dewey is a small city, and most of the attractions are easily accessible on foot or by car. However, visitors can also rent bicycles to explore the city’s bike trails.
