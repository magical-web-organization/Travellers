Introduction to Miami

Miami, a small town located in Green Country, Oklahoma, on the northeast corner of the state, has some incredible hidden gems waiting to be explored! It is a great destination for history buffs, nature lovers, and outdoor enthusiasts, with its unique combination of natural beauty and cultural attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Miami

Here are some noteworthy places to visit in Miami:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Dobson Museum - A museum dedicated to showcasing Miami’s local history and culture.
  2. Coleman Theatre - A historic theatre, which is now a concert hall and event venue.
  3. Route 66 Vintage Iron Motorcycle Museum - A motorcycle museum with a collection of vintage motorcycles, including a few rare pieces.
  4. Miami Tribal Museum- Home to exhibits and displays on the history and culture of the Miami Tribe

Natural Attractions of Miami: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Twin Bridges Park - A scenic park located just outside Miami, with fishing spots, trails, picnic areas, and playgrounds.
  2. Spavinaw Hills - A range of hills and mountains with hiking trails offering spectacular views of the surrounding countryside.
  3. Neosho Riverwalk- a peaceful trail that follows the winding Neosho River with views of the downtown area

Hiking/Biking Routes of Miami

  1. Findley Nature Trail - A 1.5-mile loop trail that takes you through the woods and past a small waterfall.
  2. Miami Trails System- A 2.5 mile paved trail system beginning and ending at Riverview Park perfect for biking, hiking and skating.

Best Things to Do in Miami: Activities and Tours

  1. Step-On Guide Tours of Miami- A great way to get a detailed understanding of Miami’s history and culture.
  2. Pitcher Recreation Area- A popular spot amongst locals for boating, fishing, and swimming. Also great for camping and picnicking.
  3. Buffalo Run Casino- Enjoy gaming, dining, and entertainment at one of Miami’s top attractions.

Week-Long Itinerary for Miami

Day 1: Dobson Museum and Historical Walking Tour of Downtown Miami Day 2: Spavinaw Hills Hiking Trail and Route 66 Vintage Iron Motorcycle Museum Day 3: Pitcher Recreation Area and Picnic Lunch (or BBQ dinner) Day 4: Daytrip to Tulsa to Visit Philbrook Museum of Art and Tulsa Zoo Day 5: Findley Nature Trail and Miami Trails System Day 6: Miami Tribal Museum and Coleman Theatre Day 7: Buffalo Run Casino and Dinner at Local Restaurant

Food and Drink Scene of Miami

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Chicken Fried Steak - A classic Oklahoma recipe that is a must-try when visiting Miami. Hard Rock Cafe is a great spot for this dish.
  2. Biscuits and Gravy - Delicious breakfast options for those who are looking for something filling and comforting. Auntie Mame’s Cafe or Daylight Donuts are known for their biscuits and gravy.
  3. Fried Pies - Traditional pastry crust pies with a sweet or savory filling, including options such as peach and pecan. Visit The Original Fried Pie Shop or Waylan’s Ku-Ku Burger for this delicious treat.

Local Beverages

  1. Root Beer - Stop by Ragsdale’s Soda Fountain, a piece of Americana history, and try their handcrafted root beer.
  2. Wine Tasting - Taste some high-quality wines made from locally grown grapes at Cabin Creek Vineyard and Winery.
  3. Craft Beer - Visit Iron Monk Brewery and enjoy some local beer brewed from the best ingredients.

Where to Stay in Miami: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Holiday Inn Express - Standard amenities and complimentary breakfast options for a cozy stay.
  2. Econo Lodge - Affordable price range, pet-friendly option with Wi-Fi
  3. Simmons Motel - A historic motel with homely charm and a nostalgic feel. Rooms are named and themed after famous people who have visited Miami in the past.

What to Buy in Miami: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Miami Tribal Art- Creative art pieces, wood carvings and much more to celebrate the local tribal art of Miami.
  2. Route 66 memorabilia- Take home some souvenirs like T-shirts, key chains or coffee mugs that commemorate your trip to Oklahoma and America’s famous highway.

Local Culture and Customs of Miami

The Miami Tribe is one of the primary indigenous groups in Oklahoma and has a rich cultural heritage. Visitors can participate in local events, such as the annual Pow-Wow, to experience the tribal culture firsthand.

Best Time to Visit Miami

The best time to visit Miami is during the month of October when the weather is cooler and fall foliage is in full effect. Spring is another great time to visit, as the temperatures are mild then too.

Do’s and Don’ts of Miami

Do:

  1. Respect the Tribal Heritage - While visiting, take the time to learn and appreciate the Miami Tribe culture and heritage.
  2. Bring Appropriate Clothing - The city runs along the qualifying line between a humid subtropical climate and a humid continental climate; pack accordingly.

Don’t:

  1. Pass Up On the Route 66 sites - Miami is one of the most prominent cities along the historic Route 66, so be sure to explore these sites.
  2. Disrespect the natural surroundings- Be mindful of the environment and dispose of trash appropriately.

Safety and Health

Safety in Miami

Miami is a safe place to visit, like any other destination, travelers should remain vigilant and take necessary precautions to ensure their safety.

Health in Miami

Miami has adequate healthcare facilities, including Miami Regional Hospital, and visitors are advised to have medical insurance to cover any medical expenses.

Getting to Miami: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Miami

  1. Tulsa International Airport - 86 miles, about a 1.5-hour drive to Miami

Bus Stations Near Miami

  1. Greyhound Bus Station - Located in Miami, servicing to and from other cities

Other Options

  1. Car Rental - Many major car rental companies are available in Oklahoma and have convenient pick-up locations.

How to Get Around in Miami

  1. Car Rental - The most convenient way to explore Miami and its surrounding areas is by renting a car.
  2. Public Transport - Miami METS provides public bus services for those traveling without a vehicle.

Where to Go Next from Miami: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Tulsa - A culturally-rich city in Oklahoma with attractions such as Philbrook Museum of Art, Tulsa Zoo, and The Gathering Place.
  2. Grand Lake- A great location to stop and enjoy the views, Grand Lake offers everything from fishing, swimming, and water sports.

Conclusion

Miami, Oklahoma, might not be the most popular destination in America, but it definitely has a lot to offer. From its fascinating museums and historical places to its natural outdoor attractions, it has something for everyone. So pack your bags, hit the road, and prepare to explore this hidden gem and make your trip unforgettable.
