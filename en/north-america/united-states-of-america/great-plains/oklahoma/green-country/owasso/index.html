Introduction to Owasso

Nestled in the heart of the Green Country in Oklahoma, Owasso is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. Whether you are a first-time visitor or a regular, you will be pleasantly surprised by the town’s blend of rich history, natural beauty, and modern amenities. From its fascinating museums and historical landmarks to its picturesque outdoor wonders and hiking trails, this charming town has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Owasso

When planning a trip to Owasso, you should consider visiting some of the following sites.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Owasso has a rich history and culture, which can be explored at its museums and historical places.

  • The Owasso Historical Society Museum - This museum showcases artifacts and documents relating to Owasso’s history. It’s a great place to learn more about the town’s development and growth.
  • Keystone Ancient Forest - This 1,360-acre forest offers visitors a glimpse into Oklahoma’s pre-settlement past.
  • The Redbud Valley Nature Preserve - This nature preserve features the Tulsa Audubon Society’s sanctuary, and is home to various native birds and animals.

Natural Attractions of Owasso: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Owasso has a wide range of natural attractions to explore, from its scenic routes to its hiking trails.

  • Lake Oologah - This artificial lake is a popular spot for boating, water sports, and fishing.
  • Tall Chief Cove - This cove at Lake Skiatook offers stunning views of the Osage Hills.
  • Owasso Trail System - This trail system includes the Centennial Trail, Cherokee Trail, and Seminole Trail. These trails are perfect for hiking, biking, and running.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Owasso

Owasso offers an extensive trail system for hiking, biking, and walking. Those seeking a leisurely stroll can choose from a range of paved walking trails, including the Owasso Trail System. For more experienced hikers, the trails at Tall Chief Cove and Redbud Valley offer more challenging terrain.

Best Things to Do in Owasso: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for things to do in Owasso, there are plenty of activities and tours worth considering:

  • Gathering on Main - This annual event celebrates Owasso’s history and culture with live music, food, and local vendors.
  • Harvest Festival - This annual fall festival features a car show, live music, and a carnival.
  • Owasso Christmas Parade - This festive parade is a great way to celebrate the holiday season.

Week-Long Itinerary for Owasso

If you are planning to spend a week in Owasso, you can follow this itinerary to make the most of your stay:

Day Activities
1 Visit the Owasso Historical Society Museum and explore the town’s history
2 Spend the day at Lake Oologah, enjoying water sports and fishing
3 Hike the Owasso Trail System and enjoy the town’s natural beauty
4 Take a tour of the Redbud Valley Nature Preserve and the Tall Chief Cove
5 Explore the unique shops and eateries along Main Street
6 Attend a local festival or event, such as the Gathering on Main
7 Relax and unwind with a spa treatment at one of Owasso’s local spas

Food and Drink Scene of Owasso

Owasso has a diverse food scene, featuring various local cuisines and world-class dining.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fry Bread Tacos - This Native American dish is served at various food trucks and restaurants around Owasso.
  • Chicken Fried Steak - This Southern favorite is a must-try at local diners.
  • BBQ Brisket - Oklahoma is famous for its BBQ, and Owasso has some of the best in the state.

Local Beverages

  • Black Gold Coffee Co. - This locally-owned coffee shop serves up some of the best coffee in town.
  • Cross Timbers Wine Tasting Room - This tasting room features wines from the Cross Timbers Winery.
  • R Bar & Grill - This local bar serves up unique cocktails and beer.

Where to Stay in Owasso: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Owasso has several options for accommodations, from its chain hotels to its bed and breakfasts.

  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites - This hotel chain offers comfortable accommodations and free breakfast.
  • Fossil Creek Hotel & Suites - This locally-owned hotel offers affordable room rates and access to local attractions.
  • The Legacy Inn & Suites - This hotel features modern amenities and is within walking distance of various restaurants and shops.

What to Buy in Owasso: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home as a memory of your visit to Owasso, consider the following:

  • Oklahoma State Souvenirs - You can find various Oklahoma state souvenirs at local gift shops around Owasso.
  • Native American Crafts - Native American tribes have a rich history in Oklahoma, and their crafts are available at various stores in Owasso.
  • BBQ Sauce - You can pick up some unique BBQ sauces at local shops in Owasso.

Local Culture and Customs of Owasso

Owasso is a town with a rich and unique culture. Respectful behavior is appreciated, and visitors should take it upon themselves to learn and understand the local customs and traditions of the town.

Best Time to Visit Owasso

The best time to visit Owasso is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and pleasant. However, visitors can enjoy various activities and festivals throughout the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Owasso

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and the local culture.
  • Explore the local trails and outdoor spaces.
  • Savor the flavors of local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Embrace negative stereotypes or insult local customs.
  • Disrespect the local traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Owasso

Owasso is generally a safe town with low crime rates. Visitors are advised to take the usual precautions to ensure their safety.

Health in Owasso

Visitors should carry a sufficient amount of water and sunscreen when exploring Owasso’s outdoor attractions. It is also advisable to check the weather forecast and dress appropriately for the conditions.

Getting to Owasso: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Owasso

The Tulsa International Airport is the closest airport to Owasso, located about 17 minutes (14.6 miles) away from the town.

Bus Stations Near Owasso

The Partnership Tower station in Tulsa is the closest Greyhound bus station to Owasso.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Owasso, as the town is located near several major highways. Ubers and taxis are also available for transportation around the town.

How to Get Around in Owasso

The best way to get around Owasso is by car. Visitors can rent a car from the Tulsa International Airport or from one of the local rental companies in town.

Where to Go Next from Owasso: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Owasso is located near several other notable destinations, including:

  • The Tulsa Zoo - A popular attraction located just 20 minutes away from Owasso.
  • The Philbrook Museum of Art - This art museum is located 25 minutes away in Tulsa.
  • The Oklahoma Aquarium - Located 40 minutes away in Jenks, this aquarium features various marine ecosystems.

Conclusion

Owasso is a charming town that offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. With its various museums, hiking trails, and local cuisine, Owasso is an excellent destination for travelers seeking an off-the-beaten-path experience in Oklahoma’s Green Country.
