Skiatook is a hidden gem in the Great Plains region of the United States. The small town in Oklahoma’s Green Country is surrounded by natural beauty, with the Osage Hills to the west and the Skiatook Lake to the east. This Skiatook travel guide will help you plan your trip to this charming destination.

Introduction to Skiatook

Skiatook is a small town located in Osage County, north of Tulsa. It is rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. The town is home to many parks, lakes, and trails, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Skiatook is known for its friendly locals, excellent food, and laid-back lifestyle.

List of Places to Visit in Skiatook

Skiatook has plenty of attractions, ranging from historical sites to outdoor recreational areas. Here is a list of the top destinations to visit in Skiatook.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Skiatook Museum: Discover the rich history of Skiatook through exhibits and artifacts, from Native American culture to the town’s early settlers.
  • Tall Chief Theater: A renovated 1920s theater that regularly hosts concerts, movie nights, and community events.
  • John Zink Ranch Museum: A museum showcasing the history of oil and gas in Oklahoma, as well as the life of John Zink, a pioneer in the industry.

Natural Attractions of Skiatook: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Osage Hills State Park: A 1,100-acre park that features hiking trails, fishing spots, and camping areas. The park also offers stunning views of Skiatook Lake.
  • Skiatook Lake: A 10,000-acre lake that offers various recreational activities, from boating and fishing to picnicking and swimming.
  • Cross Timbers Hiking Trail: A 14.2-mile trail that offers scenic views of Osage Hills, Skiatook Lake, and the surrounding forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Skiatook

Skiatook is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with many scenic trails to choose from. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Osage Hills Trail: A 16-mile hiking trail that offers stunning views of the Osage Hills and wildlife.
  • Cross Timbers Trail: A challenging 14.2-mile trail that winds through the Cross Timbers region of Oklahoma.
  • Skiatook Lake Trail: A 20-mile trail that circles the Skiatook Lake and offers beautiful views of the water and surrounding nature.

Best Things to Do in Skiatook: Activities and Tours

Skiatook offers many exciting activities for visitors, from water sports to cultural tours. Here are some of the best things to do in Skiatook:

  • Fishing and Boating on Skiatook Lake: Skiatook Lake is a popular destination for anglers and boaters, offering many opportunities to catch fish and explore the lake’s beaches and coves.
  • Tallgrass Prairie Preserve Tour: Take a tour of the beautiful Tallgrass Prairie Preserve and learn about the native wildlife and flora.
  • Wine Tasting at Woods and Waters Winery: Sample delicious wines and enjoy scenic views of Skiatook Lake at this local winery.

Food and Drink Scene of Skiatook

Skiatook has a diverse culinary scene that features many local specialties and international cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken Fried Steak: A classic Southern dish that Skiatook does particularly well.
  • BBQ: Oklahoma is known for its delicious BBQ, and Skiatook is no exception.
  • Fried Catfish: Fresh-caught and cooked to perfection, this dish is a Skiatook staple.

Local Beverages

  • Oklahoma Beer: Try some locally brewed beer at the Cabin Boys Brewery in Tulsa or the Prairie Artisan Ales in Tulsa and McAlester.

Where to Stay in Skiatook: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Skiatook has a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxury resorts. Here are some of the best places to stay in Skiatook:

  • Osage Casino Hotel: A full-service resort and casino that offers comfortable rooms, dining, and entertainment.
  • Skiatook Inn: A budget-friendly motel that offers clean and comfortable rooms.
  • Walnut Creek Lodge: A charming bed and breakfast that offers a more personal touch for those who prefer a cozy stay.

What to Buy in Skiatook: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Skiatook has many local products and souvenirs to bring home, including:

  • Native American Crafts: Skiatook has a rich Native American culture, and you can find many handmade crafts and pottery at local shops.
  • Oklahoma-made Products: From locally made hot sauce to handmade jewelry, you can find many Oklahoma-made products at local markets and gift shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Skiatook

Skiatook has a proud history and culture that celebrates its Native American and pioneer heritage. Respect for elders and nature is valued in this community, and you might see these values reflected in the art, music, and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Skiatook

The best time to visit Skiatook is from March to May and September to November when the weather is mild and pleasant. Skiatook can get hot and humid in the summer, and the winter can be chilly, so plan accordingly.

Safety and Health

Safety in Skiatook

Skiatook is generally a safe place for visitors, but it is always important to take safety precautions when traveling. Here are some safety tips for your Skiatook trip:

  • Lock your car and belongings when you leave them unattended.
  • Be aware of your surroundings, particularly at night.
  • Stick to the marked hiking trails and follow safety guidelines when participating in outdoor activities.

Health in Skiatook

Skiatook has excellent healthcare facilities, including St. John Owasso Hospital and Hillcrest Hospital Claremore. It’s always a good idea to have travel insurance in case of an emergency.

Getting to Skiatook: Transportation Options and Logistics

Skiatook is easily accessible by car or plane. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Skiatook

  • Tulsa International Airport: The nearest airport to Skiatook, around 18 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Skiatook

  • Tulsa Greyhound Bus Station: The nearest bus station to Skiatook, around 18 miles away.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: Renting a car is a convenient way to get around Skiatook and explore the surrounding area.
  • Taxis and Ridesharing: Uber and Lyft are available in the Tulsa area and can provide transportation to and from Skiatook.

How to Get Around in Skiatook

Skiatook is a small town, and most attractions are within a short distance from each other. The best way to explore Skiatook is by car or bike. There are many rental car options available, or you can bring your own car. Biking is also a popular and eco-friendly way to get around and explore the town’s beautiful scenery.

Where to Go Next from Skiatook: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time, there are many nearby destinations and excursions to consider, including:

  • Tulsa: The second-largest city in Oklahoma, Tulsa is a cultural and economic hub of the state, with many exciting attractions, restaurants, and events.
  • Pawhuska: A charming small town in Osage County, Pawhuska is home to the famous Pioneer Woman Mercantile and many historic sites.
  • Woolaroc Museum and Wildlife Preserve: A unique wildlife preserve that features exotic animals and a museum showcasing Western art and history.

Conclusion

Skiatook is a hidden gem in Oklahoma that offers a unique mix of nature, history, and culture. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures or cultural experiences, Skiatook has plenty to offer. Plan your next trip to this charming destination today!
