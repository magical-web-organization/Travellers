Introduction to Claremore

Nestled in Green Country, Oklahoma, Claremore is a charming city with a rich cultural heritage and natural beauty. Named after a Cherokee chief, Claremore is known for its western heritage, museums, and outdoor adventures. This travel guide will unveil the best things to see, do, and eat in Claremore to enrich your experience and make your trip unforgettable.

List of Places to Visit in Claremore

Claremore offers an array of attractions for history buffs, nature lovers, and adventure seekers alike. Here are some must-visit places that highlight the best of the city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Will Rogers Memorial Museum: Learn about the remarkable life of Will Rogers, the famous American cowboy, humorist, and actor who grew up in Claremore. The museum features his artifacts, photos, and films.

  2. J.M. Davis Arms & Historical Museum: Admire the world’s largest private collection of firearms and other historical items. The museum also displays Western Americana and Native American artifacts.

  3. Rogers State University: Visit the picturesque campus, which dates back to 1909 and has an important place in Oklahoma’s history. The campus is home to several historic buildings and offers scenic views of the surrounding hills.

Natural Attractions of Claremore: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Oologah Lake: Enjoy fishing, boating, and swimming at this serene lake, spanning over 29,500 acres. The lake also offers hiking and cycling trails, picnic areas, and campsites.

  2. Claremore Lake Park: This 320-acre park is an ideal spot for family outings and nature walks. You can enjoy fishing, kayaking, and paddleboarding at the lake, or explore the park’s hiking trails and wildlife.

  3. The Blue Whale of Catoosa: This quirky landmark is a must-see on Route 66. The 80-foot-long blue whale statue, built in the 1970s, is set beside a pond and invites visitors to swim and picnic.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Claremore

Claremore is blessed with several scenic hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the city and its surroundings. Here are some popular routes to explore:

  1. Claremore Mountain Bike Trail: This 6.5-mile trail caters to all skill levels and features challenging climbs and exciting descents. The trail offers scenic views of Claremore Lake and the Osage Hills.

  2. Claremore Lake Multi-Use Trail: This 5.6-mile trail is ideal for walking, jogging, cycling, and rollerblading. The trail is paved and offers several scenic spots for picnicking and bird watching.

  3. Keystone Ancient Forest Trail: Located about 30 minutes from Claremore, this 1.5-mile trail meanders through an ancient cypress forest and offers a unique nature experience.

Best Things to Do in Claremore: Activities and Tours

Apart from sightseeing and outdoor adventures, Claremore offers several exciting things to do and tours to take, including:

  1. Belvidere Mansion Tea Room: Savor a delicious High Tea experience at this historic mansion, built-in 1907. Apart from the tea room, the mansion also offers guided tours and houses a gift shop.

  2. Cherokee Nation History Museum: Located in nearby Tahlequah, this museum offers an in-depth look into the history and culture of the Cherokee Nation, one of the largest Native American tribes.

  3. Route 66 Interpretive Center: Discover the history and allure of the iconic Route 66, the most famous highway in America. The center features interactive exhibits, vintage cars, and historical artifacts.

Food and Drink Scene of Claremore

Claremore’s food scene is a blend of classic American and international cuisines, with many restaurants offering farm-to-table options and craft beers. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Chicken Fried Steak: Breaded and fried steak topped with creamy gravy, a classic Oklahoma specialty.

  2. Fried Catfish: Enjoy this Southern delicacy at many local restaurants, including Hammett House.

  3. Biscuits and Gravy: A popular breakfast option, served with sausage gravy and homemade biscuits.

Local Beverages

  1. Oklahoma Wines: Visit The Crushed Grape in downtown Claremore for a tasting of local wines.

  2. Craft Beers: Try some unique flavors at Roughtail Brewing Company and American Solera in nearby Tulsa.

Where to Stay in Claremore: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Claremore offers a range of accommodation options, from sleek hotels to cozy guesthouses. Here are some recommended places to stay:

  1. Hampton Inn and Suites Claremore: A modern hotel with an indoor pool, fitness center, and hot breakfast.

  2. Stonebrook Inn: A quaint boutique hotel with an outdoor pool and garden, located near downtown Claremore.

  3. The Belvidere Mansion Bed & Breakfast: A historic B&B, offering beautifully decorated rooms, complimentary breakfast, and tours of the mansion.

What to Buy in Claremore: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to take a piece of Claremore home with you, here are some souvenirs and local products you can buy:

  1. Will Rogers Memorabilia: Visit the museum gift shop to buy books, posters, and other items related to Will Rogers.

  2. Native American Crafts: Explore the craft markets and shops in the area to find authentic Native American crafts, such as jewelry, pottery, and textiles.

  3. Route 66 Memorabilia: Buy some vintage Route 66 souvenirs, such as t-shirts, magnets, and postcards, to remember your trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Claremore

Claremore has a rich Native American and Western heritage, and locals take pride in their history and traditions. Here are a few cultural customs to keep in mind while visiting Claremore:

  1. Respect Native American Culture: Claremore has a significant population of Native Americans, and visitors are expected to respect their customs and traditions.

  2. Cowboy Culture: Claremore has a vibrant cowboy culture, and locals often wear Western attire. Visitors are encouraged to participate in rodeos and other cowboy events.

Best Time to Visit Claremore

The best time to visit Claremore is from March to May and September to November when the weather is pleasant, and the crowds are less. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Claremore

To make the most of your visit to Claremore, here are some do’s and don’ts to follow:

Do:

  • Do visit the historic museums and landmarks to learn about Claremore’s culture and heritage.
  • Do try the local dishes and drinks to savor the flavors of Oklahoma.
  • Do explore the outdoor adventures and scenic trails of Claremore.

Don’t:

  • Don’t disrespect Native American culture or customs.
  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t forget to tip the service staff at hotels and restaurants.

Safety and Health

Safety in Claremore

Claremore is a safe city, with a low crime rate, but visitors should take normal precautions, such as being aware of their surroundings and locking their cars and hotel rooms.

Health in Claremore

Claremore has several medical facilities, including Claremore Indian Hospital and Hillcrest Hospital, that offer quality healthcare services to residents and tourists. Visitors are advised to carry travel insurance and to check with their healthcare provider before traveling.

Getting to Claremore: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Claremore

The nearest airport is Tulsa International Airport, which is about 20 miles from Claremore. The airport has direct flights to several US cities and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Claremore

Greyhound Bus Station and Jefferson Lines Bus Station are the two major bus stations near Claremore, both located in Tulsa.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi from the airport or bus station to reach Claremore. Claremore also has a train station, where Amtrak’s Heartland Flyer stops on its route between Oklahoma City and Fort Worth.

How to Get Around in Claremore

Claremore is a small city, and visitors can easily explore the downtown area and nearby attractions on foot. Renting a car or bike is recommended for visiting far-off places, such as Oologah Lake or Tulsa.

Where to Go Next from Claremore: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Claremore is strategically located near several exciting destinations in Oklahoma and neighboring states. Here are some popular places to explore from Claremore:

  1. Tulsa: Visit Oklahoma’s second-largest city, known for its world-class museums, art galleries, and live music scene.

  2. Oklahoma City: Explore the capital city of Oklahoma, which offers many attractions, such as the National Cowboy and Western Heritage Museum and Oklahoma City National Memorial & Museum.

  3. Fayetteville: Head to this vibrant college town in Arkansas, known for its stunning natural beauty and outdoor adventure activities.

Conclusion

Claremore is a hidden gem in Oklahoma, offering a perfect blend of history, nature, and adventure.
