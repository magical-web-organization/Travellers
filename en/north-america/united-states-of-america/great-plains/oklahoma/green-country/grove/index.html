Grove, located in Green Country, Oklahoma, is known for its stunning natural beauty and rich cultural history. Home to numerous museums and historical sites, this charming city has something to offer for every kind of traveler.

Introduction to Grove

Grove is a small city with a population of around 7,000 people. It was founded in 1895 and has since grown into a vibrant community that’s full of life and energy. Located in the heart of the Green Country region, Grove is surrounded by rolling hills, picturesque lakes, and lush forests, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Grove

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Har-Ber Village Museum: This open-air museum is a tribute to the early pioneer days in Oklahoma and features over 100 buildings and artifacts from the 1800s.

  • Lendonwood Gardens: A unique and beautiful botanical garden that showcases a wide variety of plants and flowers.

  • Cherokee Queen Riverboat: Take a trip down Grand Lake on this authentic riverboat and enjoy breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Grove Historical Museum: This museum showcases the rich history of the city, from its early days as an agricultural community to its present-day status as a center of commerce and culture.

Natural Attractions of Grove: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Grand Lake O’ the Cherokees: With over 1,300 miles of shoreline and crystal-clear waters, this lake is a paradise for boating enthusiasts, fishermen, and water sports enthusiasts.

  • Honey Creek State Park: This beautiful park offers some of the best hiking, biking, and camping opportunities in the region, and boasts stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Har-Ber Village Nature Trail: A short and easy hike that takes you through some of the most beautiful and peaceful areas in Grove, with plenty of opportunities to spot wildlife along the way.

  • Elk River Trail: One of the most popular hiking trails in the area, this 15-mile trail offers breathtaking views of the Elk River and the surrounding countryside.

Food and Drink Scene of Grove

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Okra: A classic Southern staple that’s a must-try when visiting Grove.

  • Chicken Fried Steak: Another Southern favorite, this hearty dish is served in many of the local restaurants.

  • Biscuits and Gravy: A filling and delicious breakfast option that’s perfect for starting your day off right.

  • Cherokee Fry Bread: This traditional Native American dish is a local delicacy that’s often served at powwows and other cultural events.

Local Beverages

  • Grand Lake Beer: A locally brewed beer that’s made with water from Grand Lake O’ the Cherokees.

  • Sweet Tea: A Southern classic that’s often served at local restaurants and cafes.

  • Tulsa Grapefruit Soda: A refreshing soda that’s made with real grapefruit juice.

What to Buy in Grove: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Native American Crafts: Grove is home to a large and vibrant Native American community, and you’ll find plenty of beautiful and unique crafts for sale in local shops and markets.

  • Grand Lake Honey: Made with pure and natural honey from the bees that live along Grand Lake O’ the Cherokees.

  • Local Artwork: From paintings and sculptures to pottery and jewelry, there’s no shortage of beautiful and unique artwork to be found in Grove.

Local Culture and Customs of Grove

Grove has a rich and diverse cultural heritage that’s reflected in its art, music, and cuisine. The Native American community has played a particularly important role in shaping the city’s cultural identity, and you’ll find numerous opportunities to learn about their history and traditions while visiting Grove.

Best Time to Visit Grove

The best time to visit Grove is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and the foliage is at its most vibrant. The winter months can be quite cold and snowy, while the summer months can be hot and humid, so plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Grove

Do:

  • Take time to explore the natural beauty of the area, including Grand Lake O’ the Cherokees and Honey Creek State Park.

  • Learn about the city’s rich cultural history by visiting its museums and historical sites.

  • Try some of the delicious local cuisine, including fried okra, chicken fried steak, and Cherokee fry bread.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the environment while visiting Grove’s natural attractions.

  • Don’t disrespect the local Native American community or its traditions.

  • Don’t miss out on the opportunity to experience the city’s unique cultural heritage.

Safety and Health

Safety in Grove

Grove is generally a safe and welcoming city, but visitors should take the same precautions they would in any unfamiliar area. Be aware of your surroundings, keep valuables secure, and stay in well-lit, populated areas after dark.

Health in Grove

Grove has a number of medical facilities and pharmacies, but visitors should bring any necessary medications with them for the duration of their stay. It’s also a good idea to carry a basic first aid kit, especially if you plan to spend time hiking or exploring the outdoors.
