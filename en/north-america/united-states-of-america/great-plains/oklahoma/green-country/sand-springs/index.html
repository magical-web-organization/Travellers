Introduction to Sand Springs

Located in Green Country, Oklahoma, Sand Springs is a small town with a big heart. This bustling town is surrounded by scenic beauty with rolling hills, verdant forests, and winding rivers, making it a perfect destination for nature lovers. The town has a rich history, steeped in oil and industrial culture that has attracted visitors from all over the world. With so much to explore, let’s delve into our comprehensive tour guide for Sand Springs.

List of Places to Visit in Sand Springs

Sand Springs has a multitude of places to visit that cater to a diverse range of interests.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Explore the town’s rich industrial past and cultural heritage by visiting its museums and historical places.

  • Sand Springs Cultural and Historical Museum: This museum showcases the town’s history and the role it played in oil production in Oklahoma. It’s housed in Tulsa’s first public school building and contains exhibits on the town’s founding, artifacts from local industries and information on Sand Springs’ most legendary citizens.

  • Keystone Ancient Forest: Take a scenic drive to Keystone Lake and witness 1000-year-old trees in their natural surroundings. Follow a hike to fully experience the ancient woods.

  • Osage Pronghorn Ranch: The ranch is a National Register historic site and offers tours about the Osage tribe, who were once the richest people per capita in the world.

Natural Attractions of Sand Springs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sand Springs is surrounded by beautiful vistas, making it a perfect destination for nature enthusiasts. Here are some must-visit natural attractions.

  • Keystone State Park: Divided by the Arkansas River, this state park offers various recreational activities like biking, fishing, hiking, and boating. It’s a great destination for a day-trip or a weekend getaway.

  • Pawnee Bill Ranch and Museum: This museum displays the legacy of the Wild West with over 50,000 artifacts dedicated to Buffalo Bill and Pawnee Bill’s Wild West Show.

  • Tulsa Hills Overlook: Take a drive up winding roads to this hilltop for a stunning view of the Arkansas River Valley. It’s a perfect spot to watch the sunset.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sand Springs

Sand Springs has long trails that cater to walkers, bikers, and hikers alike. Here are some of the most popular.

  • Lake Keystone Trail: This 15-mile trail takes you through a dense forest and along the lake. It’s perfect for hiking, biking or a leisurely stroll.

  • River City Park Trail: This trail takes you along the riverbanks of the Arkansas River. It’s a beautiful route for bikers, runners, and walkers. You can enjoy the scenic views and picnic spots along the way.

  • Osage Hills Trail System: The Osage Hills Trail System has over 25 miles of trails that are perfect for hikers and mountain bikers. They offer a scenic view of the Osage Hills and ridge lines.

Best Things to Do in Sand Springs: Activities and Tours

Sand Springs offers a range of exciting activities and tours for all ages.

  • Visit the Keystone Lake: Take a boat or kayak tour of the Keystone Lake, an artificial lake that’s over 22,000 acres in size.

  • Tulsa Air and Space Museum: Explore the world of aviation and space travel by visiting the Tulsa Air and Space Museum. The museum offers tours and educational exhibits for all ages.

  • Enjoy a Live Show at the Osage Million Dollar Elm Casino: Take in a show at the Osage Million Dollar Elm Casino, where world-class entertainers from all genres perform.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sand Springs

Day 1: Explore the town’s history, culture, and heritage by visiting Sand Springs Cultural and Historical Museum, Keystone Ancient Forest, and Osage Pronghorn Ranch.

Day 2: Take a scenic drive to Keystone Lake and explore its facilities. Enjoy a leisurely day of boating, fishing, swimming, or hiking.

Day 3: Hike Keystone State Park Trails and witness the natural beauty of the Arkansas River. Enjoy downtime picnicking and relaxing in grove and park areas.

Day 4: Take in a show at the Osage Million Dollar Elm Casino, visit Tulsa Air and Space Museum, and experience the thrill of space exploration.

Day 5: Explore Osage Hills Trail System on a mountain bike or on a hiking trail.

Day 6: Enjoy a leisurely day at River City Park Trail and walk through the lush riverbanks of the Arkansas River.

Day 7: Journey up the winding roads to Tulsa Hills Overlook for scenic views and breathtaking sunsets.

Food and Drink Scene of Sand Springs

Sand Springs offers an eclectic mix of food and drink options that cater to all palates.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Catfish: Lawrence’s Chicken and Fish serves up the best-fried catfish in town. It’s a classic Southern dish that’s a must-try.

  • Country Fried Steak: Don’s Alley makes a delectable fried steak dish. It’s a classic Southern treat that melts in your mouth.

  • BBQ Brisket: Oklahoma Joe’s BBQ serves up the best brisket in town. It’s tender, smoky, and delicious.

Local Beverages

  • Coffee and Pastries: The Café in Sand Springs serves up premium coffee and freshly baked patisseries.

  • Local Beer: Iron Monk Brewing Company, located in Stillwater, serves up local craft beer that is a must-try for beer lovers.

Where to Stay in Sand Springs: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sand Springs offers many options when it comes to accommodations.

  • Hampton Inn Tulsa Sand Springs: This hotel offers comfortable bedding, hot breakfast, and standard amenities in comfortable rooms.

  • The Springs Event Center: This destination offers a variety of rooms, from a small gathering to large events spaces.

  • Quality Inn: This hotel offers comfortable rooms, hot breakfast, and standard amenities.

What to Buy in Sand Springs: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sand Springs has a range of local products that are unique and must-buy for those visiting the town.

  • Oil-Pump Jack Models: Buy a miniature model of an oil pump jack to remember your visit to Sand Springs.

  • Souvenir T-Shirts: You’ll find a range of unique souvenir t-shirts at the Sand Springs Cultural and Historical Museum.

  • Wooden Coasters: Buy handmade coasters made from native Oklahoma trees as a unique souvenir to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Sand Springs

Sand Springs residents are friendly and welcoming. It’s a small town that offers a close-knit community, steeped in culture and heritage. The town celebrates its history and culture with numerous events and fairs throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Sand Springs

The best time to visit Sand Springs is during the Spring season from late March to early June when the weather is pleasant. Fall is also a popular time for tourists to visit when the foliage is vibrant and colorful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sand Springs

Do:

  • Be respectful and courteous towards locals.

  • Take advantage of the many outdoor activities and hiking trails.

  • Do attend local events and fairs to experience Sand Springs culture.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter.

  • Don’t disrespect the local culture.

  • Don’t ignore the natural beauty and history of Sand Springs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sand Springs

Sand Springs is a safe and friendly town. As is true everywhere, it’s always important to be cautious and aware of your surroundings.

Health in Sand Springs

Sand Springs has a range of healthcare facilities for the convenience of visitors. Check with your hotel or clinic for more information.

Getting to Sand Springs: Transportation Options and Logistics

Sand Springs is accessible by multiple modes of transportation.

Airports Near Sand Springs

The nearest airport is Tulsa International Airport, which is about 15 miles away from Sand Springs.

Bus Stations Near Sand Springs

The Greyhound bus station is located in downtown Tulsa, approximately 10.3 miles away from Sand Springs.

Other Options

You can drive to Sand Springs from nearby towns via well-maintained highways and roads.

How to Get Around in Sand Springs

Sand Springs is a small town, so it’s easy to get around by walking, biking, or by renting a car.

Where to Go Next from Sand Springs: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sand Springs is surrounded by several other cities that are worth exploring.

  • Tulsa: Visit the vibrant city of Tulsa, which is just a 15-minute drive away. The city offers a range of activities and events for all ages.

  • Oklahoma City: Drive two hours to the state capital, Oklahoma City, and explore its rich history and culture.

  • Bartlesville: Just an hour away from Sand Springs, Bartlesville houses the famous Woolaroc Museum and Wildlife Preserve.

Conclusion

Sand Springs offers a unique experience for nature lovers and history buffs alike. With its rich industrial past, beautiful vistas, and friendly people, Sand Springs is a destination that’s sure to leave a lasting impression on your heart and mind.
