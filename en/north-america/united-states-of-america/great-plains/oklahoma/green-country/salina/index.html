Introduction to Salina

Salina is a quaint town located in Green Country, Oklahoma. Known for its natural beauty and rich history, Salina is a popular destination for travelers seeking an authentic small-town experience. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, a history buff, or a foodie, Salina has something to offer.

In this guide, we’ll introduce you to the best places to visit, hiking and biking routes, things to do, local food and drink scene, where to stay, and the culture and customs of Salina. We’ll also provide you with important safety and health information, transportation options, and nearby destinations.

List of Places to Visit in Salina

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Salina is a town with a rich history, and there are several museums and historical places to visit, including:

  1. Salina Heritage Museum – This museum offers an insight into the town’s past, with exhibits featuring the history of the town and its people.
  2. The Cherokee Strip Regional Heritage Center – Located in nearby Enid, this museum offers a glimpse into the lives of those who lived in the prairies and Great Plains region.
  3. Marland Mansion – Located in nearby Ponca City, this historic mansion is a testament to the wealth and opulence of the oil boom era.

Natural Attractions of Salina: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Salina’s natural beauty is a major draw for visitors, and there are several outdoor adventures and scenic views to explore, including:

  1. Salt Plains National Wildlife Refuge – This refuge is home to the largest salt flat in North America, and visitors can explore the salt plains by hiking or biking along designated trails.
  2. Great Salt Plains State Park – This state park is a great place for camping, fishing, hiking, and bird watching.
  3. Kaw Lake – This 17,000-acre lake is perfect for boating, fishing, and swimming.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Salina

When it comes to hiking and biking routes, Salina offers several options, including:

  1. Salt Plains Nature Trail – This 1.2-mile trail offers scenic views of the salt flats.
  2. Osage Hills Trail – Located in nearby Pawhuska, this 7-mile trail offers a challenging hike with stunning views of the Osage Hills.
  3. Kaw Lake Trail – This 38-mile trail offers a combination of paved and unpaved sections, making it perfect for both hiking and biking.

Best Things to Do in Salina: Activities and Tours

Aside from its natural beauty and rich history, Salina offers visitors a wide range of activities and tours to enjoy, including:

  1. Blackwell Wagon Trail – This 6-mile trail takes visitors on a journey through the prairies and rolling hills of Oklahoma.
  2. Cherokee Trading Post and Boot Outlet – This shop offers a variety of Native American crafts and souvenirs.
  3. Blue Coyote Winery – This winery offers wine tasting and tours, as well as live music events on weekends.

Week-Long Itinerary for Salina

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Salina, here’s a sample itinerary for you to follow:

Day 1: Salt Plains National Wildlife Refuge Day 2: Salina Heritage Museum and Cherokee Strip Regional Heritage Center Day 3: Osage Hills Trail and Kaw Lake Day 4: Marland Mansion and Blackwell Wagon Trail Day 5: Great Salt Plains State Park and Blue Coyote Winery Day 6: Kaw Lake Trail Day 7: Free day to explore the town and its local culture

Food and Drink Scene of Salina

Must-Try Dishes

Salina’s food scene is a mix of American and Southwestern cuisine, with a few local specialties worth trying, including:

  1. Chicken-fried steak – A Southern staple that can be found in many local diners.
  2. Barbecue – Salina boasts some great barbecue joints, with The Butcher BBQ Stand being a standout.
  3. Fry bread – A Native American specialty that can be found at various restaurants and food trucks throughout Salina.

Local Beverages

Oklahoma may not be known for its wine, but Salina has a few wineries worth visiting, including:

  1. Blue Coyote Winery – This winery offers a variety of wines made from locally-sourced grapes.
  2. Oak Hills Winery – Located in nearby Chelsea, this winery offers a wide selection of wines, including sweet wines made from fruits like peaches and strawberries.

Where to Stay in Salina: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

When it comes to accommodation options, Salina has a few hotels, guesthouses, and Airbnbs to choose from, including:

  1. Lakeview Lodge – This hotel is located near Kaw Lake and offers impressive lake views.
  2. Countryside Inn – This budget-friendly hotel is located in downtown Salina.
  3. Cozy Guesthouse – This guesthouse is perfect for those looking for a more homely vibe.

What to Buy in Salina: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home, Salina has a few shops worth checking out, including:

  1. Cherokee Trading Post and Boot Outlet – This shop offers a variety of Native American crafts and souvenirs.
  2. Black Gold Buffalo – This store sells handmade leather goods and Western-style clothing.
  3. Rustic Cuff – This shop offers a variety of bracelets and accessories, many of which are made using natural stones and leather.

Local Culture and Customs of Salina

Salina is a town with a proud Western heritage, and locals take pride in their Native American roots. Visitors should be respectful of this cultural heritage and should also be prepared for the town’s laid-back and welcoming atmosphere.

Best Time to Visit Salina

The best time to visit Salina is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summer can be hot and humid, while winters can be very cold and often bring snow and ice.

Do’s and Don’ts of Salina

Do:

  1. Explore the town’s rich history and natural beauty.
  2. Try the local cuisine, including chicken-fried steak and barbecue.
  3. Be respectful of the town’s Native American culture and customs.

Don’t:

  1. Leave trash in nature reserves or parks.
  2. Bring drugs or other illegal substances into the town.
  3. Disrespect locals or their cultural heritage.

Safety and Health

Safety in Salina

Salina is generally a safe town, but visitors should always take precautions to ensure their personal safety, including:

  1. Being aware of your surroundings, especially at night.
  2. Keeping your valuables secure and out of sight.
  3. Locking your car and hotel room doors at night.

Health in Salina

In terms of health, visitors should be aware of the town’s extremely hot summers and cold winters. It’s important to stay hydrated and dress appropriately for the weather, depending on the time of year.

Getting to Salina: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Salina

The nearest airport to Salina is Tulsa International Airport, which is about 1.5 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Salina

The nearest bus station to Salina is located in Enid, which is about an hour away by car.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car and drive to Salina, as the town is easily accessible from major highways.

How to Get Around in Salina

The best way to get around Salina is by car, as the town is spread out and public transportation is limited. If you don’t have your own car, you can rent one at Tulsa International Airport or in nearby Enid.

Where to Go Next from Salina: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Oklahoma or the surrounding areas, here are a few nearby destinations and excursions worth considering:

  1. Tulsa – Oklahoma’s second-largest city is about 2 hours away by car and offers a variety of cultural attractions and shopping.
  2. Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge – This wildlife refuge is about 2 hours away by car and offers stunning views and hiking trails.
  3. Ponca City – This historic oil boom town is about an hour away by car and offers several museums and historic sites to explore.

Conclusion

Salina, Oklahoma, may be a small town, but it offers a wealth of natural beauty, local culture, and history that can’t be found anywhere else. Whether you’re a seasoned traveler or a first-time visitor, Salina promises an authentic, welcoming experience that’s sure to leave you with lasting memories.
