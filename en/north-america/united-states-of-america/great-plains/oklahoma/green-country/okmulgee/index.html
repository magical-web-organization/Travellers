If you’re planning to explore the heart of Green Country in Oklahoma, you cannot miss visiting Okmulgee. Located in the Great Plains region of the United States, Okmulgee is a small yet fascinating city known for its rich cultural heritage, scenic outdoor spaces, and friendly locals. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through some of the most exciting things to do, see, and experience in Okmulgee and its surrounding areas.

Introduction to Okmulgee

Okmulgee is a charming city located in the east-central part of Oklahoma, around 20 miles south of Tulsa. Historically, Okmulgee has been an important town for the Muscogee Creek Nation, and you’ll still find a significant Native American presence in the city today.

In addition to its cultural significance, Okmulgee is also known for its natural beauty, with plenty of parks and outdoor recreation opportunities available in and around the city. Whether you’re into hiking, biking, fishing, or just taking in the sights, there’s something for everyone in Okmulgee. So, let’s start exploring!

List of Places to Visit in Okmulgee

Here are some of the best places to visit in Okmulgee:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Creek Nation Council House Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving the history of the Muscogee Creek Nation, one of the Five Civilized Tribes of the southeastern United States. Here, you can learn about the tribe’s culture, traditions, and history.
  • The Okmulgee County Historical Society Museum: Located in the restored Oklahoma Territory Orphan’s Home, this museum showcases the history of Okmulgee County, from prehistoric times to the present day.
  • The Henryetta Territorial Museum: Located in the neighboring town of Henryetta, this museum allows you to step back in time and explore life in a late 19th-century Oklahoma frontier town.

Natural Attractions of Okmulgee: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Okmulgee Lake: This scenic lake covers over 1,000 acres and is home to a variety of fish, including bass, catfish, and crappie. It’s a popular spot for fishing, boating, and camping.
  • Okmulgee State Park: Covering over 1,400 acres, this state park is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts. You can hike or bike on the park’s trails or go fishing or boating on Okmulgee Lake.
  • Dripping Springs Lake: Located about 10 miles southeast of Okmulgee, Dripping Springs Lake is another popular spot for fishing, boating, and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Okmulgee

If you’re into hiking or biking, Okmulgee has plenty of trails to explore. Here are a few of the most popular:

  • Okmulgee State Park Trail System: With over 13 miles of trails, this system offers a variety of hiking and biking opportunities for all skill levels.
  • Deep Fork National Wildlife Refuge: Located just south of Okmulgee, this wildlife refuge has several hiking trails that wind through its forests and wetlands.
  • Okmulgee Creek Trail: This 3.5-mile trail runs through downtown Okmulgee and is a great way to see some of the city’s historic sites and architecture.

Best Things to Do in Okmulgee: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do and see in Okmulgee:

  • Take a Historic Walking Tour: Okmulgee has a rich history, and taking a walking tour of the town is a great way to learn about it. You can do it yourself using a guidebook available at the Chamber of Commerce, or you can join a guided tour led by local experts.
  • Attend the Annual Muscogee Creek Nation Festival: This festival, held every summer, is a celebration of the Muscogee Creek Nation’s culture and traditions. It features live music, dance performances, traditional foods, and arts and crafts.
  • Visit the Okmulgee Farmer’s Market: Held every Saturday morning, this farmer’s market is a great place to shop for fresh produce, crafts, and other local goods. You might even get to meet some of the farmers and artisans who make them!

Week-Long Itinerary for Okmulgee

If you have a week to spend in Okmulgee and the surrounding areas, here’s a sample itinerary that will help you make the most of your time:

Food and Drink Scene of Okmulgee

No trip to Okmulgee is complete without trying some of the local cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cornbread: This Southern staple is often served with chili, stew, or other hearty dishes.
  • Fried Okra: This vegetable is breaded and deep-fried until crispy and is a popular side dish in Oklahoma.
  • BBQ: Oklahoma is known for its BBQ restaurants, and Okmulgee has several great ones to choose from, serving everything from brisket to ribs.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea: This classic Southern beverage is a must-try when in Okmulgee.
  • Choc Beer: Brewed in nearby Krebs, this beer is made with chocolate malt and has a distinct flavor that’s popular with locals.

Where to Stay in Okmulgee: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Okmulgee has several great options for accommodation, ranging from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxury hotels. Here are a few you might want to consider:

  • Holiday Inn Express: This modern hotel is located near the Creek Nation Council House Museum and features spacious rooms, a fitness center, and complimentary breakfast.
  • Okmulgee Inn: This budget-friendly motel is located near the airport and features clean, comfortable rooms and free Wi-Fi.
  • The Stone Lion Inn: Located in nearby Guthrie, this bed-and-breakfast is a charming Victorian mansion with luxurious rooms and a delightful, cozy atmosphere.

What to Buy in Okmulgee: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Okmulgee, there are several unique souvenirs and local products you might want to bring home with you. Here are a few ideas:

  • Muscogee Creek Nation Crafts: Visit the Creek Council Gift Shop or the Muscogee Creek Nation Cultural Center and Museum to buy handmade crafts and gifts made by Muscogee Creek artisans.
  • Okmulgee T-Shirts and Hats: Show your love for Okmulgee with a T-shirt or hat featuring the city’s name and logo.
  • Sweet Treats: Stop by local bakeries and candy shops to taste some of the best sweets Oklahoma has to offer.

Local Culture and Customs of Okmulgee

The Muscogee Creek Nation has a rich cultural heritage that is still celebrated in Okmulgee today. If you’re visiting during a festival or special event, be sure to participate and learn more about the tribe’s customs and traditions. Additionally, Okmulgee is known for its friendly locals, so don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation and learn more about life in the city.

Best Time to Visit Okmulgee

The best time to visit Okmulgee depends on what you want to do while you’re here. If you’re interested in outdoor activities like hiking, fishing, and camping, spring and fall are great times to visit, when the weather is mild and comfortable. However, if you’re interested in attending festivals and events, summer might be a better time to visit, as many of these take place during this season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Okmulgee

Here are some things you should do and avoid during your visit to Okmulgee:

Do:

  • Participate in local festivals and events to learn more about the city’s culture and customs.
  • Explore the city’s many parks and outdoor spaces, including Okmulgee State Park and Deep Fork National Wildlife Refuge.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages, including BBQ, fried okra, and sweet tea.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural spaces in and around Okmulgee. Respect the environment and keep it clean for others to enjoy.
  • Don’t make assumptions or offensive comments about Native American culture or traditions. Learn about and respect the customs of the Muscogee Creek Nation and local residents.

Safety and Health

Okmulgee is a safe and friendly city, but it’s always important to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings. Here are some tips to stay safe and healthy during your visit:

Safety in Okmulgee

  • Keep your valuables, especially your phone and wallet, secure when out and about. Use a money belt or keep them in your front pocket.
  • Avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.
  • If you’re driving, be aware of other drivers on the road and follow traffic rules.

Health in Okmulgee

  • Make sure to stay hydrated during hot summer months.
  • Wear sunscreen and insect repellent when hiking or spending time outdoors to avoid sunburn and bug bites.
  • For any medical emergencies, dial 911 or head to the nearest hospital.

Getting to Okmulgee: Transportation Options and Logistics

Okmulgee is easily accessible by car and has several convenient transportation options:

Airports Near Okmulgee

  • Tulsa International Airport: Located about 40 miles north of Okmulgee, Tulsa International Airport is the closest major airport to the city. It has several flights to major U.S. cities.
  • Oklahoma City Will Rogers World Airport: Located about 100 miles southwest of Okmulgee, Will Rogers World Airport is a larger airport with more flight options.

Bus Stations Near Okmulgee

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: Located in Tulsa, Greyhound Bus Lines offers bus service to and from major U.S. cities.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: Rental cars are available at both Tulsa International Airport and Oklahoma City Will Rogers World Airport.
  • Taxis: Taxis are available in the city for local transportation.

How to Get Around in Okmulgee

The best way to get around Okmulgee is by car, as the city is quite spread out and public transportation options are limited. Rental cars are available at airports and in the city, or you can bring your own vehicle if driving from within the United States.

Where to Go Next from Okmulgee: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Okmulgee is a great base camp for exploring the surrounding area. Here are a few nearby destinations and excursions you might want to consider:

  • Tulsa: Just 20 miles north of Okmulgee, Tulsa is a vibrant city with plenty of attractions, restaurants, and culture.
  • Cherokee Nation: Located about 100 miles east of Okmulgee, the Cherokee Nation is another great destination for learning about Native American culture and history.
  • Robbers Cave State Park: Located about 70 miles southeast of Okmulgee, this state park is a great spot for hiking, camping, and exploring hidden caves.

Conclusion

Okmulgee is a hidden gem in the heart of Green Country, offering visitors a chance to explore Oklahoma’s rich cultural heritage and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor recreation, or just taking in the sights and sounds of a small town, there’s something for everyone in Okmulgee. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has given you the inspiration and information you need to plan your visit and make the most of your time in this charming city. Happy travels!
