Introduction to Locust Grove

Located in Green Country, Oklahoma, Locust Grove is a hidden gem that promises a magnificent blend of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and exciting activities.

Nestled in the Great Plains region of the United States, Locust Grove is a small town that offers a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. From hiking and biking trails to museums and historical sites to local food and culture, this town has everything to offer to tourists of all ages and interests.

List of Places to Visit in Locust Grove

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

From museums to historical sites, Locust Grove has plenty to offer to history buffs and culture lovers.

1. Saline Courthouse Museum - Discover the rich heritage of Locust Grove and Green Country at the Saline Courthouse Museum. This museum offers a glimpse into the life of early settlers and Native Americans who called this land their home.

2. John Ross Museum - Learn about the Cherokee heritage and history of the Trail of Tears at John Ross Museum. This museum offers a comprehensive look into the life and legacy of Chief John Ross, the principal chief of Cherokee Nation.

3. Fort Gibson Historic Site - Visit the Fort Gibson Historic Site and explore the ruins of the old fort that played a crucial role in Western expansion and protecting the Trail of Tears.

Natural Attractions of Locust Grove: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers will find plenty of opportunities to explore the great outdoors in and around Locust Grove.

1. Lake Hudson - Go fishing, boating, or just enjoy the stunning scenery at Lake Hudson. This lake is one of the largest bodies of water in Green Country and offers plenty of activities for visitors.

2. Sequoyah Bay State Park - Located just a few miles from Locust Grove, Sequoyah Bay State Park offers breathtaking views of the lake and a variety of outdoor activities like hiking, camping, and fishing.

3. Locust Grove Nature Trail - Take a hike on the Locust Grove Nature Trail and discover the beauty of the Ozark Plateau. This trail offers stunning views of the Grand River and is home to a variety of native wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Locust Grove

If you love hiking or biking, Locust Grove offers several scenic trails to explore.

1. GTT/Muskogee Hiking Trail - Explore the beautiful scenery of Eastern Oklahoma on the GTT/Muskogee Hiking Trail. This trail offers over 50 miles of hiking and biking trails and is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts.

2. Lake Hudson Trail - Enjoy stunning views of Lake Hudson while hiking or biking along the Lake Hudson Trail. This trail offers a mix of paved and unpaved paths, making it suitable for bikers of all levels.

Best Things to Do in Locust Grove: Activities and Tours

From outdoor adventures to cultural experiences, Locust Grove has plenty of activities and tours to offer to visitors.

1. Fishing Tours - Take a guided fishing tour and catch some of the largest fish in Green Country. Whether you’re an experienced angler or a beginner, these tours offer a unique opportunity to explore the local waterways.

2. Cherokee Heritage Center - Immerse yourself in the rich Cherokee culture and history at the Cherokee Heritage Center. From traditional dances to historic sites, this center offers a comprehensive look into the Cherokee way of life.

3. Helicopter Tours - See Locust Grove and Green Country from a whole new perspective with a helicopter tour. These tours offer stunning aerial views of the lake, forests, and towns.

Food and Drink Scene of Locust Grove

Locust Grove offers plenty of local dining and drinking options that cater to all tastes and budgets.

Must-Try Dishes

1. Fried catfish - Enjoy freshly caught catfish, fried to perfection and served with a side of hushpuppies and coleslaw.

2. Cherokee fry bread - This delicious bread is a Cherokee staple, made from a mix of flour, water, and yeast and served with honey or jam.

3. Locust Grove BBQ - Indulge in some mouthwatering BBQ ribs, brisket, and pulled pork at one of the local BBQ restaurants.

Local Beverages

1. Oklahoma wine - Sip on some of the region’s finest wines made from locally grown grapes.

2. Craft beer - Enjoy a cold pint of the locally brewed craft beer that features unique flavors and styles.

Where to Stay in Locust Grove: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a cozy B&B or a luxury resort, Locust Grove offers a variety of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences.

1. Microtel Inn & Suites by Wyndham - This hotel is located near the highway and offers comfortable rooms with modern amenities.

2. Lake Hudson Inn - Enjoy stunning lake views and modern amenities at the Lake Hudson Inn.

3. Cozy Cottages - These charming cottages offer a relaxing stay in the heart of Locust Grove.

What to Buy in Locust Grove: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take home a piece of Locust Grove with you by purchasing locally made souvenirs and products. Some of the popular items include Cherokee art and crafts, locally farmed produce, and artisanal products like honey and cheese.

Local Culture and Customs of Locust Grove

Respect the local culture and customs when visiting Locust Grove. The town has a strong Native American heritage, and visitors are encouraged to learn and appreciate this culture.

Best Time to Visit Locust Grove

The best time to visit Locust Grove is between April and October when the weather is pleasant and outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Locust Grove

Do:

  2. Take advantage of the many outdoor activities and tours.
  3. Try the local food and drink scene.

Safety and Health

Locust Grove is a safe town, but visitors should take the usual precautions like locking cars and avoiding unfamiliar areas at night.

Health in Locust Grove

Visitors should carry their health insurance and take precautions against insect bites, sunburn, and dehydration during outdoor activities.
