Introduction to Antlers

Nestled in Choctaw Country, Oklahoma, Antlers offers a unique blend of history, culture, and outdoor adventures. With its scenic views, charming small-town ambiance, and friendly locals, Antlers is an ideal destination for travelers seeking a break from the hustle and bustle of city life.

If you’re planning a trip to Antlers, this comprehensive guide will help you make the most of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Antlers

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pushmataha County Historical Society Museum - displays a range of artifacts from antiques to Native American and Civil War relics. Also, the Antlers Art Association owns and exhibits artwork here.
  • Kiamichi Owa-Chito Festival of the Forest - hosts a yearly festival with live music, food vendors, games, and a carnival atmosphere.

Natural Attractions of Antlers: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • McGee Creek Natural Scenic Recreation Area - offers over 2,600 acres of forested hills, valleys, and lakes for hiking, fishing and hunting.
  • Honobia Creek Wildlife Management Area - provide great locations for hunting opportunities for deer,^1 elk, and wild hogs.
  • Sardis Lake - the picturesque lake covering over 3,687 acres provides a recreational area for boating, fishing, and swimming.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Antlers

  • Butterfly Trail - a scenic 2.5-mile trail located in the McGee Creek Natural Scenic Recreation Area. The trail runs close to the McGee Creek and has spotting of the resident butterfly species.
  • Deer Trail - a 2.8 miles trail encompassing the McGee Creek Natural Scenic Recreation Area. It provides an overview of the local fauna and flora species abundant in the region.

Best Things to Do in Antlers: Activities and Tours

  • Honey Creek Adventures - explore the great outdoors with guided kayak tours on the Honey Creek National Scenic River.
  • Horseback Riding - explore the Choctaw Nation territories while mounted on horseback for a unique outdoor experience.
  • Antlers Cinema - offers movie screenings, snacks, and beverages in their old-world style cinema.

Week-Long Itinerary for Antlers

Food and Drink Scene of Antlers

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken Fried Steak - breaded steak served with creamy mashed potatoes and gravy.
  • Fried Okra - it is a staple of Southern cuisine, sliced and fried okras served hot and crispy.
  • Sausage Gravy and Biscuits - rich gravy made from sausage drippings paired with flaky buttery biscuits.

Local Beverages

  • Sweet Tea - super sweet black tea served with plenty of ice.
  • Cherry Limeade - a sweet and tangy concoction of lime juice and sweet cherry syrup.
  • Chickasaw Plum Wine - a fruity wine made from the Chickasaw plums native to the area.

Where to Stay in Antlers: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Antlers Budget Inn - comfortable and affordable stay with excellent facilities.
  • Hunt’s Castle - a beautiful bed and breakfast built-in 1922, with well-appointed rooms and a lovely garden.
  • Whispering Cedars Inn - a rustic retreat with ample facilities, located close to outdoor adventure areas.

What to Buy in Antlers: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Choctaw Beaded Jewelry - exquisite beadwork made by members of the Choctaw tribe.
  • Blue River Trading Co. - home décor shop that specializes in Southwestern and Native American-inspired items.
  • Choctaw Nation Gift Shop - offers a wide range of souvenirs that represent the Choctaw culture, including t-shirts, mugs, and other collectibles.

Local Culture and Customs of Antlers

Antlers’ culture is influenced mainly by the Choctaw tribe, with the majority of the town’s residents being proud of their Native American heritage. You can observe cultural events hosted throughout the year, such as the Deer Festival, the Choctaw Powwow, and the Kiamichi Owa-chito Festival of the Forest.

Best Time to Visit Antlers

The best time to visit Antlers is in late spring through early fall. This time of year provides pleasant temperatures ideal for outdoor activities and exploring the region’s natural attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Antlers

Do:

  • Respect the Native American culture and their beliefs.
  • Observe hunting regulations.
  • Wear appropriate clothing while hiking or horseback riding.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of traditional dances or ceremonies without permission.
  • Litter in natural areas or cultural sites.
  • Hike or hunt without a pre-collected checklist or sturdy equipment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Antlers

Antlers is a friendly and safe town with a tight-knit community. However, one needs to pay attention to safety measures, especially while outdoors. One should carry ample supplies, sturdy footwear, and lots of water to keep the trip comfortable.

Health in Antlers

Antlers provides essential healthcare facilities with good medical professionals and pharmacies. It is advisable to carry essential medication and first-aid supplies when traveling.

Getting to Antlers: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Antlers

  • Ada Airport - 1 hr 30 min drive from Antlers, OK, and local to Ada town.
  • Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport - located 150 miles from Antlers and is well-connected with the rest of America.

Bus Stations Near Antlers

  • Greyhound Bus Lines - operates a bus service to destinations like Ada and Idabel from Antlers.
  • Durant Station - can be accessed 45 minutes to the North of Antlers.

Other Options

One can also drive in using route 271 and 2 via North or the route 271 and 3 via South to reach Antlers, Oklahoma. The town is accessible by private transportation, as well.

How to Get Around in Antlers

The best way to get about is by driving. Car rentals are available at the local car rental companies, and it is advisable to call ahead to make reservations. Taxis and ride shares are not widely available in Antlers.

Where to Go Next from Antlers: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Hugo Lake State Park - located just 30 miles from Antlers and offers hiking, fishing, and camping opportunities
  • Beavers Bend State Park - 85 miles southeast of Antlers and is a popular spot for water activities like fishing and kayaking.
  • Gainesville, Texas - 70 miles from Antlers, and visit the culturally rich city filled with museums and arts centers.

Conclusion

Antlers, Oklahoma, is an ideal destination for travelers who want to explore Choctaw culture, enjoy nature, and appreciate small-town charm. Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventure or cultural immersion, Antlers offers an unparalleled travel experience that you’re sure to cherish for a lifetime.
