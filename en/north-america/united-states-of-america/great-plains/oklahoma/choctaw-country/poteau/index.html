A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Poteau, Oklahoma

Located in Choctaw Country, in the Great Plains of the United States of America, Poteau is a charming town full of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and exciting outdoor adventures. Whether you are a history buff, a nature lover, or an adventure seeker, Poteau has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the best things to see and do in Poteau, along with useful information to help plan your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Poteau

Poteau boasts several attractions that offer a glimpse into the town’s history and culture, as well as its stunning natural beauty.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. LeFlore County Museum at Hotel Lowrey: This museum showcases the history and culture of LeFlore County, with exhibits ranging from Native American artifacts to local art and crafts.
  2. Talimena National Scenic Byway: A 54-mile stretch of road that links Poteau with Mena, Arkansas. This stunning drive takes you through the Ouachita Mountains and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  3. Spiro Mounds Archaeological Center: Located a short drive from Poteau, this center houses one of the most important prehistoric Native American sites in the United States. The site features large ceremonial mounds and artifacts that date back over 1,000 years.

Natural Attractions of Poteau: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Wister Lake State Park: Situated just outside of Poteau, this park offers a range of outdoor activities including fishing, boating, hiking, and camping. The park features a large lake and several miles of hiking trails, perfect for exploring the natural beauty of the area.
  2. Cavanal Hill: Known as the world’s tallest hill, this peak offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape. Hikers can take a challenging hike to the top, or drive up the winding road for a more leisurely experience.
  3. Robbers Cave State Park: Located a short drive from Poteau, this park offers a variety of outdoor activities including hiking, fishing, and rappelling. The park is also home to several caves that were once used by outlaws such as Jesse James as a hideout.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Poteau

Poteau is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with several popular routes that showcase the area’s natural beauty.

  1. Charley’s Creek Trail: A challenging 8-mile hike that winds through the scenic Ouachita Mountains. This trail offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape and several waterfalls along the way.
  2. Ouachita National Recreation Trail: A popular hiking trail that passes through Poteau and offers a variety of scenic views and challenging terrain. This trail spans over 220 miles, making it a great option for multi-day hikes.
  3. Choctaw Nation Trail System: This network of trails offers a variety of hiking and biking options, ranging from easy walks to challenging climbs. The trails wind through the Ouachita Mountains, providing stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Poteau: Activities and Tours

  1. Escape Games: Bring your family or friends and enjoy an hour of fun and excitement at Escape Games in Poteau. With multiple rooms to choose from, you can choose a theme that most appeals to you. Escape the room by solving clues, puzzles and teamwork.
  2. Poteau Balloon Fest: Enjoy the skies with Poteau’s annual hot air balloon festival, where you can see the beautiful hot air balloons set against a picturesque background.
  3. Lakeside Theatre Company: Watch live theatre productions and concerts at the Lakeside Theatre.

Week-Long Itinerary for Poteau

Day 1: Visit Wister Lake State Park

Day 2: Explore LeFlore County Museum at Hotel Lowrey

Day 3: Hike or bike the Outlaw Trail

Day 4: Visit Spiro Mounds Archaeological Center

Day 5: Scenic drive along Talimena National Scenic Byway

Day 6: Enjoy outdoor activities at Robbers Cave State Park

Day 7: Shop for souvenirs at local shops and restaurants

Food and Drink Scene of Poteau

Poteau offers a mix of local and international cuisine, with several standout restaurants that are sure to please any palate.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Chicken Fried Steak: This classic Southern dish is a must-try in Poteau, with several restaurants offering their own take on the local favorite.
  2. Fried Catfish: A staple in the Poteau dining scene, fried catfish is served in many places in the area. Local restaurants are known for having the best catfish in town.
  3. Pecan Pie: A Southern classic, pecan pie is a must-try dessert in Poteau. Look for local restaurants that feature this dish on their menu.

Local Beverages

  1. Craft Beer: Poteau has a thriving craft beer scene, with local breweries offering a variety of flavorful brews.
  2. Sweet Tea: This sweet and refreshing drink is a Southern favorite, and is available at many local restaurants and cafes.

Where to Stay in Poteau: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Poteau offers a range of accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels.

Budget Options

  1. Days Inn & Suites by Wyndham Poteau: This budget-friendly hotel offers a convenient location and comfortable rooms at an affordable price.
  2. Green Country Inn: This motel is a great option for budget travelers, with clean and comfortable rooms at a modest price.

Mid-Range Options

  1. Best Western Traders Inn: This mid-range hotel offers comfortable rooms and a range of amenities, including a fitness center and outdoor pool.
  2. Holiday Inn Express & Suites Poteau: This hotel offers spacious rooms and a continental breakfast, making it a great choice for families or groups.

Luxury Options

  1. Choctaw Casino & Resort: This high-end resort features a full-service spa, multiple restaurants, and a range of entertainment options including a casino and live music venue.
  2. The Grey Feather Lodge: This luxury bed and breakfast offers a charming and intimate atmosphere, with beautifully appointed rooms and a range of amenities.

What to Buy in Poteau: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Poteau is known for its local art and crafts, as well as its unique souvenir shops that offer everything from handmade pottery to locally sourced honey.

  1. Poteau Farmers Market: A great place to pick up fresh produce, homemade baked goods, and other locally made items.
  2. Artisan Gallery: This local gallery showcases the work of local artists, including paintings, sculptures, and handmade crafts.
  3. Country Cottage: This gift shop offers a range of unique souvenirs and gifts made in Oklahoma, including homemade jams, pottery, and handmade jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Poteau

Poteau is a melting pot of cultures, with a strong Native American heritage and influences from the surrounding states. Visitors can experience the local culture by attending cultural events, visiting historic sites, and exploring local art and craft galleries.

Best Time to Visit Poteau

The best time to visit Poteau is during the spring and fall months when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summer months can be hot and humid, while winter months can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Poteau

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs.
  2. Take advantage of the outdoor activities and scenic views.
  3. Visit local restaurants and cafes to try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  1. Disrespect nature by littering or damaging the environment.
  2. Ignore street signs and traffic laws.
  3. Be loud or overly boisterous in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Poteau

Poteau is generally a safe town to visit, but visitors should take standard precautions such as locking their cars and keeping valuables secure. Be cautious when hiking in remote areas and be aware of weather conditions that could potentially be hazardous.

Health in Poteau

Poteau has several medical clinics and hospitals, with highly trained medical professionals who can provide proper medical care in case of emergencies. It is always recommended that visitors have appropriate travel insurance before visiting a new place.

Getting to Poteau: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Poteau

The nearest major airport to Poteau is Fort Smith Regional Airport, which is located approximately 30 miles east of Poteau. Another option is the Tulsa International Airport, located approximately 100 miles to the northwest.

Bus Stations Near Poteau

Greyhound Bus Lines has a station in Fort Smith, which is the nearest major bus station to Poteau.

Other Options

Renting a car is an excellent option for visitors who want to explore the town and surrounding area at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Poteau

Visitors can get around Poteau easily by car, bike, or on foot. The town is bike and pedestrian-friendly, with several bike lanes and well-maintained sidewalks.

Where to Go Next from Poteau: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Little River National Wildlife Refuge: A short drive from Poteau, this refuge offers opportunities to see a diverse range of wildlife in their natural habitat.
  2. Oklahoma City: The state capital is located approximately 150 miles to the west of Poteau and is known for its booming arts and culture scene.
  3. Hot Springs, Arkansas: A charming resort town located approximately 70 miles east of Poteau, known for its thermal hot springs and beautiful historic architecture.

Conclusion

Poteau may be a small town, but it offers a wealth of attractions and activities for visitors of all ages. From outdoor adventures to cultural experiences and delicious local cuisine, Poteau has something to offer every traveler. Plan your next trip to Poteau and experience all that this charming town has to offer.
