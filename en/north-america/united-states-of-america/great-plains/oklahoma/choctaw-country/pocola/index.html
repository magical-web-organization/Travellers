Introduction to Pocola

Pocola is a picturesque town nestled in Choctaw Country in Oklahoma, which offers a blend of natural beauty, traditional culture, and small-town charm. This close-knit community boasts of a rich history and offers plenty of attractions to explore.

Visitors to Pocola can expect warm hospitality from the locals, for whom tourism is a significant source of livelihood. From cultural museums to outdoor adventures, this town caters to all travelers’ needs.

List of Places to Visit in Pocola

In Pocola, you can find cultural museums, historical sites, and natural attractions.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Gilcrease Museum

Gilcrease Museum is a must-visit spot to connect with the art and culture of America’s West. This museum features an unparalleled collection of artworks, artefacts, and historical documents that showcase the region’s history and its native people.

Choctaw Nation Museum

Located in nearby Talihina, the Choctaw Nation Museum celebrates the history, culture, and traditions of the Choctaw Nation. The museum houses exhibits that explain the struggles and triumphs of the Choctaw people

Fort Smith National Historic Site

Fort Smith National Historic Site offers a glimpse into frontier history’s tumultuous times, including the Western Expansion era, the Civil War, and the Indian Removal Act of 1830. You can take a tour of the restored courthouse and jail, walk through the nearby trails, and watch reenactments.

Natural Attractions of Pocola: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Cedar Lake

Cedar Lake is an ideal spot for a serene weekend getaway near Pocola. The calm waters offer fishing, hiking, boating, and other outdoor activities. You can immerse yourself in the beauty of the forested shores dotted with rustic cabins.

Ouachita National Forest

Ouachita National Forest has miles of hiking trails that lead to scenic overlooks, crystal-clear streams, and peaceful waterfalls. You can also take a scenic drive or enjoy camping, fishing, and hunting around the forest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pocola

Pocola Bike Trail

Pocola Bike Trail is a paved trail that runs through lush green forests and countryside vistas. The trail is ideal for leisurely cycling or walking amidst nature.

The Heavener Runestone Park

The Heavener Runestone Park offers trails that are perfect for hikers who wish to experience breathtaking scenery. You can explore the ancient runestone and learn about its intriguing tales.

Best Things to Do in Pocola: Activities and Tours

Choctaw Casino

Choctaw Casino has everything from gaming tables to first-class entertainment and a variety of dining options. The casino offers an excellent opportunity to indulge in some fun and frolic during your trip to Pocola.

LeFlore County Balanced Rock Tour

Take the LeFlore County Balanced Rock Tour to witness nature’s wonders. The tour takes you through the rugged mountain terrain and offers breathtaking views of the balanced rock formations.

Food and Drink Scene of Pocola

Must-Try Dishes

Choctaw Frys

Choctaw Frys are potato chips, deep-fried, and topped with cheese, bacon, sour cream, and green onions. They are a local specialty enjoyed throughout the region.

Indian Fry Bread

Indian Fry Bread is made from flour, baking powder, water, and salt, rolled into small balls, patted flat, and then deep-fried until crispy. It is a staple of traditional Native American cuisine.

Local Beverages

Choctaw Tea

Choctaw Tea is a herbal tea made with a blend of sassafras, cherry bark, mint, and other herbs. It is traditionally drunk for good health and has a unique flavour.

Choc Beer

Choc Beer is a locally brewed beer made with a family recipe passed down from generations. It has a slightly sweet flavour with a hint of caramel and is a great drink to enjoy during your stay in Pocola.

Where to Stay in Pocola: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Choctaw Casino & Resort

Choctaw Casino & Resort is one of the best places to stay in Pocola, offering luxurious accommodations, fine dining options, and world-class amenities such as the casino.

Inn at Pocola

Inn at Pocola is a charming Bed and Breakfast that offers a cozy, comfortable stay and a delightful home-made breakfast.

What to Buy in Pocola: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Native American Artifacts

Pocola’s Native American heritage is evident in the art and crafts available in local shops. You can find traditional pottery, woven baskets, and intricate beadwork that make for unique keepsakes.

Choctaw Beaded Jewelry

Choctaw Beaded Jewelry is another excellent souvenir option. The intricate designs, patterns, and colours make each piece unique and beautiful.

Local Culture and Customs of Pocola

The Choctaw Nation’s traditions, beliefs, and language are still essential aspects of the community’s local culture and customs. Visitors can attend cultural events and learn about the history and traditions of the people.

Best Time to Visit Pocola

The best time to visit Pocola is from March to May when the weather is pleasant and the natural beauty of Oklahoma is at its peak. The fall season from September to November is another great time to visit Pocola when the weather is crisp, and the foliage is stunning.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pocola

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Dress appropriately in public places
  • Ensure that you book your hotel rooms in advance.

Don’t:

  • Show disrespect to Native American heritage or culture.
  • Litter
  • Cut down trees or damage natural landmarks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pocola

Overall, Pocola is a safe community with low crime rates. However, it is always advisable to take usual safety precautions, such as being aware of your surroundings and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Pocola

Pocola has a health clinic that offers medical services to visitors in need. It is also essential to stock up on sunscreen, insect repellent, and any required medication.

Getting to Pocola: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pocola

Pocola is located near Fort Smith Regional Airport (FSM), which is served by American Airlines and United Airlines.

Bus Stations Near Pocola

The nearest bus station is located in Fort Smith, which is served by Greyhound Buses.

Other Options

You can reach Pocola by car or motorcycle, using the major highways and interstates, or by cycling, using the scenic routes.

How to Get Around in Pocola

Pocola is a small town that is best navigated by car. You can rent a car from major car rental services or opt for a ride-sharing service. Biking and walking are also great options to explore the town at a leisurely pace.

Where to Go Next from Pocola: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Talihina

Talihina is a beautiful town located east of the Ouachita Mountains. You can visit the Talimena National Scenic Byway, Talihina State Park, and the Choctaw Nation Museum.

Spiro Mounds Archaeological Center

Spiro Mounds Archaeological Center is located about 15 miles southwest of Pocola. You can explore the UNESCO-designated mounds, which are considered one of America’s most significant pre-Columbian sites.

Conclusion

Pocola is a perfect getaway for those seeking natural beauty, traditional culture, and small-town charm.
