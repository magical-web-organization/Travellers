Introduction to Hugo

Nestled in the Great Plains of Oklahoma, Hugo is a city of rich history, natural beauty, and friendly locals. Named after the famous French author Victor Hugo, this town is known for its Choctaw culture, outdoor activities, and delicious food. Whether you’re a history buff, an adventure seeker, or a foodie, Hugo has something to offer. In this comprehensive guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, and local culture tips in Hugo, making sure you have an unforgettable trip.

List of Places to Visit in Hugo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hugo has a rich history that is well-preserved through its numerous museums and historical sites. Here are some must-visit attractions:

  • Frisco Depot Museum: This museum showcases the history of the railroad, which played a significant role in the development of Hugo. It features restored train cars, exhibits, and a gift shop.
  • Showman’s Rest and Bull Rider’s Reprieve: This cemetery is the final resting place of circus performers and bull riders. The unique grave markers and memorials tell the stories of these entertainers and their life on the road.
  • Endangered Ark Foundation: This nonprofit is dedicated to the preservation of Asian elephants. Visitors can take a tour of the sanctuary and learn about elephant behavior, habitat, and conservation.

Natural Attractions of Hugo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hugo is blessed with natural beauty and outdoor activities that attract visitors all year round. Here are the best natural attractions:

  • Hugo Lake: This man-made lake offers fishing, boating, and swimming opportunities. The scenic views, picnic areas, and hiking trails make it a popular spot for families and outdoor enthusiasts.
  • Kiamichi Mountains: The Kiamichi Mountains are part of the Ouachita National Forest and offer hiking, camping, and hunting opportunities. The lush greenery, waterfalls, and wildlife make it a must-visit destination.
  • Hugo Municipal Park: This park has a man-made waterfall and pond, a walking trail, and a playground. Visitors can also see a replica of a historic steam locomotive.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hugo

There are several hiking and biking trails in and around Hugo that cater to different skill levels. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Choctaw Nation Trail: This 3-mile trail is located in Hugo Lake State Park and offers scenic views of the lake and mountains. It is suitable for beginners.
  • Lake Rayburn Trail: This 6-mile trail is located in Beaver’s Bend State Park and offers challenging terrain, river views, and wildlife sightings.
  • Hugo Bike Trail: This 4-mile trail is located in Hugo Municipal Park and offers a smooth surface for both biking and walking. It is suitable for families with kids.

Best Things to Do in Hugo: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking, biking, and exploring the attractions, Hugo offers several activities and tours for visitors. Here are the best options:

  • Choctaw Casino and Resort: This casino offers gaming, live entertainment, and fine dining options.
  • Tania’s Clay Studio: This studio offers pottery classes where visitors can learn the techniques of throwing, glazing, and firing clay.
  • Hugo Walking Tours: This tour company offers guided walking tours of the downtown area, introducing visitors to the local history and architecture.

To make the most of your visit to Hugo, here’s a week-long itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Hugo

Hugo has a thriving food and drink scene that offers a range of cuisines and flavors. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Catfish: This dish is a staple in Oklahoma and can be found in most restaurants. The crispy batter and flaky fish pairs well with tartar sauce.
  • Choctaw Tacos: These are fry bread topped with chili, cheese, lettuce, tomatoes, and onions. They are a traditional dish in the Choctaw culture.
  • Pecan Pie: This dessert is made with local pecans and is a perfect ending to any meal.

Local Beverages

  • Choctaw Beer: This beer is brewed by the local brewery, Choctaw Brew, and has a smooth taste with a hint of honey.
  • Oklahoma Wine: Oklahoma has a robust wine industry, and visitors should try the local wines from the wineries in the region.
  • Sweet Tea: This southern-style tea is a popular drink in Hugo and pairs well with any meal.

What to Buy in Hugo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Hugo has a range of local products and souvenirs that visitors can take home as mementos. Here are some options:

  • Choctaw Crafts: The Choctaw Tribe has a rich history of arts and crafts, and visitors can buy woven baskets, pottery, and beadwork from local shops.
  • Oklahoma Wine: Visitors can buy local wines from the wineries and vineyards in the region.
  • Hugo Train Whistle: Visitors can buy a train whistle from the Frisco Depot Museum gift shop, which is a souvenir of their trip to Hugo.

Local Culture and Customs of Hugo

Hugo has a vibrant culture that is influenced by its Choctaw heritage, outdoor lifestyle, and southern hospitality. Visitors should keep in mind the following customs:

  • Respect the Choctaw culture: The Choctaw people have a unique culture and traditions, and visitors should respect them.
  • Be friendly: The locals in Hugo are welcoming and warm, and visitors should reciprocate with politeness and kindness.
  • Dress appropriately: Hugo has a relaxed dress code, but visitors should avoid revealing clothing and dress modestly.

Best Time to Visit Hugo

The best time to visit Hugo is in the spring and fall when the weather is mild, and the foliage is vibrant. Summer can be hot and humid, and winter can be cold and snowy, making outdoor activities challenging. Visitors should also check the events calendar for festivals and events in the area.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hugo

Here are some do’s and don’ts that visitors should keep in mind while in Hugo:

Do:

  • Explore the outdoors: Hugo is blessed with natural beauty, and visitors should take advantage of the hiking and biking trails.
  • Try the local food: Hugo has a rich culinary scene, and visitors should try the local dishes and beverages.
  • Learn about the history: Hugo has a fascinating history, and visitors should take the time to learn about the local culture and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Visitors should respect the environment and avoid littering in the parks and trails.
  • Disrespect the Choctaw culture: The Choctaw Tribe has a unique culture, and visitors should avoid cultural appropriation or disrespect.
  • Overlook the small shops: Hugo has several local shops and businesses, and visitors should support them by shopping locally.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hugo

Hugo is a safe town, and visitors should not worry about major safety concerns. However, visitors should take the usual precautions, such as locking doors and windows, keeping valuables out of sight, and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Hugo

Visitors should ensure that they have the necessary vaccinations and health insurance before traveling to Hugo. The nearest hospital is Choctaw Nation Health Services in Hugo.
