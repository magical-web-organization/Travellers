Introduction to McAlester

Welcome to McAlester, a charming city in Oklahoma that offers visitors the opportunity to explore its natural beauty and learn about its rich history. Located in Choctaw Country and surrounded by the verdant Great Plains, McAlester is a perfect destination for those who love hiking, biking, and outdoor adventures. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through the top attractions, the best activities to do, and the popular dishes to try in McAlester.

List of Places to Visit in McAlester

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Krebs Heritage Museum: This museum displays artifacts that represent the history of Krebs, a town located near McAlester. The museum has a vast collection of photographs and papers that showcase the town’s development and the lives of its early settlers.
  • Pioneer Coal Miner Memorial: This memorial is set up to honor the many miners who lost their lives in mining accidents that took place in the McAlester region. Visitors can learn about the mining industry, its challenges, and its importance to the development of the region.
  • Choctaw Nation Capitol Museum: Learn about the history and culture of the Choctaw Nation through interactive exhibits, historical artifacts, and educational programs that give insight into the rich past of this Native American tribe.

Natural Attractions of McAlester: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Robbers Cave State Park: This park is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts. It has a large lake where visitors can fish or swim. There are also trails for hiking and biking. The highlight of the park is the Robbers Cave – a series of caves that were used as a hideout by outlaws in the 1800s.
  • Chadick Park: Located in the heart of the city, Chadick Park is a serene park that has playgrounds, picnic areas, and sports fields. The park is perfect for a leisurely stroll or a picnic with family and friends.
  • McAlester Lake: This picturesque lake is an ideal spot for boating, fishing, and swimming. It has several public access points and facilities that are maintained by the Army Corps of Engineers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of McAlester

  • McAlester Lake Trail: This trail is an easy hike that takes you around the serene McAlester Lake. It is a loop trail, and you can start from any access point. Along the way, you will see scenic views of the lake and the surrounding landscape.
  • Great Plains Trail of Oklahoma: This extensive trail system covers many Oklahoma destinations, including McAlester, and offers hiking, biking, and equestrian options. The trail’s entry point in McAlester is located near scenic Robbers Cave State Park.

Best Things to Do in McAlester: Activities and Tours

  • Tour the Oklahoma State Penitentiary: This prison has been in operation since 1908 and has a fascinating history. Visitors can take a tour of the prison and see the cells, exercise yards, and the execution chamber.
  • Explore Downtown McAlester Antique Shops: McAlester’s Downtown area is home to numerous antique shops that offer a vast collection of vintage items and memorabilia, such as books, furniture, and clothing.
  • Visit Eiffler’s Place: This restaurant is a landmark eatery in McAlester that has been serving patrons since 1933. Their specialty is Eiffler’s famous chili with tamales.

Food and Drink Scene of McAlester

Must-Try Dishes

  • Eiffler’s Famous Chili: A hearty bowl of chili with tamales served over cornbread, a dish that has been a staple at Eiffler’s Place since the 1930s.
  • Frenchie’s Famous Fried Chicken: This family-owned establishment has been serving their delicious fried chicken since 1956.
  • Slice of pie at Only One Restaurant: This restaurant is known for its wide selection of homemade pies, which are ideal for dessert after a hearty meal.

Local Beverages

  • Choctaw Brewing Co: A locally owned brewery that produces a variety of craft beers that are loved by locals and visitors alike.
  • McAlester Soda Fountain: A charming soda fountain that offers delicious milkshakes, floats, and fountain sodas, making it the perfect spot to cool off on a hot summer day.

Where to Stay in McAlester: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • La Quinta Inn & Suites by Wyndham: A budget-friendly hotel that offers comfortable accommodation and amenities like complimentary breakfast and outdoor pool.
  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites McAlester: A luxurious hotel with amenities, including a fitness center, business center, and free breakfast.
  • Stone Turtle Lodges: Offbeat stay features private lodges, a tranquil riverside location and trails for hiking.

What to Buy in McAlester: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • McAlester Salt Flats: A local seasoning made entirely of salt and produced by the McAlester Salt Company.
  • Choctaw crafts: Souvenirs reflecting Choctaw Nation crafts such as beadwork, pottery, basketry, and flutes/musical instruments.

Local Culture and Customs of McAlester

The people of McAlester are known for their hospitality and friendliness. Many of the town’s events like the annual Choctaw Festival are deeply rooted in Native American traditions. An event that boasts of traditional dances, arts, and crafts as well as food. The historical significance of the town can be witnessed throughout the museums and parks.

Best Time to Visit McAlester

The best time of year to plan a visit to McAlester is in the spring or fall when the weather is mild, and the scenery is at its best. The annual Choctaw Festival in September and the Mcalester’s Italian Festival in May are two of the town’s popular events.

Do’s and Don’ts of McAlester

Do:

  • Respect the traditional values and customs of the Native American tribes living in the area.
  • Spend some time with the locals as they are known to be hospitable and friendly.
  • Try the traditional dishes of McAlester.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public spaces, and always clean up after yourself.
  • Enter restricted areas without permission.
  • Take pictures of Native American tribes without obtaining their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in McAlester

McAlester is a relatively safe town, but it is always advisable to be cautious when travelling. Always keep your belongings close to you and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in McAlester

It is essential to carry insect repellent if you are spending time outdoors. It is also recommended to wear sunscreen and lightweight, protective clothing.

Getting to McAlester: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near McAlester

The closest international airport to McAlester is Tulsa International Airport, which is about two hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near McAlester

Greyhound Bus Lines offers daily services to and from McAlester from several destinations in Oklahoma.

Other Options

Driving to McAlester is the best option as it offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape. Rental cars are available with good deals available for booking early.

How to Get Around in McAlester

A car is the easiest way to get around in McAlester as it allows you to explore the town at your leisure. Buses are available in the city as well as a taxi service.

Where to Go Next from McAlester: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Talimena Scenic Drive: A scenic route located near McAlester that offers stunning views of the Ouachita Mountains and the surrounding landscape.
  • Oklahoma City: The state’s capital is a vibrant city located about two and a half hours away from McAlester. The city is full of museums, art galleries, and delicious restaurants.
  • Tulsa: A culturally rich city located two hours away from McAlester that boasts of incredible museums, art galleries, and architectural marvels.

Conclusion

McAlester doesn’t disappoint visitors seeking for natural beauty, history, outdoor adventures and authentic cultural experiences. From the scenic Robbers Cave State Park to downtown antique shops to the must-try Eiffler’s chili tamale and Choctaw crafts, this town is full of character and surprises. This travel guide has given you a comprehensive overview of the town’s top attractions, accommodations, and food scene, and we hope it helps you to plan your next exciting adventure to McAlester.
