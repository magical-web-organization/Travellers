Your Ultimate Travel Guide to Wilburton, Oklahoma

Wilburton, a charming town in Choctaw Country, Oklahoma, offers a perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. With a population of just over 2,000, it’s a quiet, peaceful town that boasts of beautiful natural surroundings and an array of cultural attractions that can appeal to visitors of all ages.

Introduction to Wilburton

Located in the Great Plains region of the United States, Wilburton is a small town that is known for its rich cultural heritage and natural beauty. The town is named after its founder, Ellison Wilbur, who established it as a trading post in the late 1800s. Wilburton has a charming small-town vibe, with tree-lined streets, historic buildings, and friendly locals who are always willing to welcome visitors.

List of Places to Visit in Wilburton

Wilburton is home to a variety of attractions that can appeal to visitors of all interests. Here is a list of the top things to do in Wilburton:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Robbers Cave State Park: This state park is home to a series of caves, rock formations, and walking trails that are perfect for nature enthusiasts. It was also a hideout for outlaws and is rumored to have connections to Jesse James and Belle Starr.

  • Eastern Oklahoma State College: Founded in 1908, this college is one of the oldest institutions of higher education in Oklahoma. The college’s museum houses an impressive collection of art, artifacts, and fossils, which can provide insight into the region’s history and culture.

  • Choctaw Cultural Center: This interactive museum explores the history, culture, and traditions of the Choctaw Nation, a Native American tribe that has lived in the area for centuries. Visitors can learn about Choctaw arts, crafts, music, and dance.

Natural Attractions of Wilburton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Talimena National Scenic Byway: This 54-mile drive cuts through Ouachita National Forest and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. There are plenty of spots to stop and take in the scenery, and the autumn foliage is especially stunning.

  • Winding Stair Mountain National Recreation Area: This recreation area encompasses more than 27,000 acres of forests, streams, and waterfalls. Visitors can enjoy hiking, fishing, camping, and picnicking in the area.

  • Hochatown State Park: This park is located just a short drive from Wilburton and is known for its clear waters and abundant wildlife. Visitors can canoe, kayak, or swim in the park’s lake, or explore the several hiking trails in the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wilburton

Wilburton offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor enthusiasts to get out and explore the area’s natural beauty. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Wilburton:

  • Robbers Cave Trail: This 3-mile loop trail takes hikers through Robbers Cave State Park and provides plenty of opportunities to explore the area’s unique rock formations and cave systems.

  • Talimena National Scenic Byway: This 54-mile drive also has several hiking trails that offer impressive views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

  • Wilburton-Clayton Multi-Use Trail: This 4-mile trail is perfect for both hikers and bikers and takes visitors through some of the most scenic areas of Wilburton.

Best Things to Do in Wilburton: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Wilburton offers plenty of other activities and tours for visitors to enjoy:

  • Wilburton Main Street: This charming downtown area is home to a variety of unique shops, galleries, and restaurants that are perfect for an afternoon stroll.

  • Horseback Riding: There are several stables in the area that offer guided horseback tours of the surrounding forests and mountains.

  • Boat Tours: Visitors can take a guided boat tour of Lake Carlton, which offers a unique perspective on the area’s natural beauty.

Week-Long Itinerary for Wilburton

For visitors who want to explore Wilburton and the surrounding area in-depth, here is a week-long itinerary that incorporates some of the town’s top attractions:

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore downtown Wilburton and visit the Choctaw Cultural Center
Day 2 Hike the Robbers Cave Trail and explore the park’s caves and rock formations
Day 3 Take a scenic drive along the Talimena National Scenic Byway and enjoy the fall foliage
Day 4 Spend the day at Lake Carlton, swimming, boating, and fishing

Food and Drink Scene of Wilburton

No trip to Wilburton is complete without sampling some of the area’s local cuisine and beverages. Here are some of the top dishes and drinks to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Catfish: This classic Southern dish is a staple at many of the area’s restaurants and is usually served with hushpuppies and coleslaw.

  • Biscuits and Gravy: This hearty breakfast dish is a favorite among locals and is sure to keep you full until lunchtime.

  • Indian Tacos: A twist on the traditional taco, this dish features fry bread topped with ground beef, lettuce, cheese, and tomatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Choctaw Beer: Brewed by the Choctaw Nation’s own brewery, this beer is the perfect way to celebrate the area’s rich Native American heritage.

  • Oklahoma Wine: Several wineries in the area offer tastings and tours, giving visitors the chance to sample some of Oklahoma’s best wines.

Where to Stay in Wilburton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors to Wilburton have several lodging options to choose from, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious guesthouses. Here are some of the top places to stay in Wilburton:

  • Best Western Inn: This hotel offers spacious rooms, a fitness center, and free breakfast.

  • Robbers Cave State Park Cabins: These rustic cabins offer a unique accommodation option and are located within the state park.

  • The Guest House: This historic guesthouse is located in downtown Wilburton and offers comfortable rooms and a continental breakfast.

What to Buy in Wilburton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For visitors who want to bring a piece of Wilburton home with them, there are several souvenirs and local products that make great gifts. Here are some of the top items to look for:

  • Handmade Pottery: Local artists create beautiful pottery inspired by the area’s natural beauty.

  • Choctaw Crafts: At the Choctaw Cultural Center, visitors can purchase one-of-a-kind crafts made by members of the tribe.

  • Oklahoma Wine: Many of the area’s wineries offer bottles of their locally-produced wine for purchase.

Local Culture and Customs of Wilburton

Wilburton has a rich cultural heritage, with a strong influence from the Choctaw Nation. Visitors should be respectful of the area’s local customs and traditions, including the importance of family and the community.

Best Time to Visit Wilburton

The best time to visit Wilburton depends on your interests. For those interested in natural beauty and outdoor adventure, autumn is a great time to visit, as the leaves change color and temperatures cool down. However, for those interested in cultural attractions and events, spring and summer are great times to visit, as several festivals take place during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wilburton

When visiting Wilburton, there are a few things visitors should keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect the area’s natural beauty by staying on designated hiking trails and packing out all trash.

  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions, including attending the annual Choctaw Nation Labor Day Festival.

  • Take advantage of the area’s outdoor activities and natural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Ignore local traffic laws, particularly when traveling on the Talimena National Scenic Byway.

  • Visit during hunting season without proper permits and permission.

  • Disrespect the area’s Native American heritage.

Safety and Health

Wilburton is generally a safe and welcoming town, but visitors should still take precautions when traveling. Here are some important things to keep in mind:

Safety in Wilburton

  • Be aware of your surroundings, particularly when hiking or exploring natural areas alone.

  • Lock car doors and keep valuables out of sight.

  • Follow local traffic laws and avoid driving after dark in rural areas.

Health in Wilburton

  • Oklahoma is located in “Tornado Alley,” and visitors should be aware of the possibility of severe weather during their visit.

  • Visitors should bring sunscreen and insect repellent, as the area has a high mosquito population during the summer.

  • While there are medical facilities in Wilburton, visitors may want to bring any necessary medications or medical supplies with them.

Getting to Wilburton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Wilburton is located in the southeastern part of Oklahoma, and visitors can reach the town by car, bus, or plane.

Airports Near Wilburton

  • Fort Smith Regional Airport: Located 83 miles east of Wilburton, Fort Smith Regional Airport offers direct flights to several major cities.

  • Tulsa International Airport: Located 117 miles northwest of Wilburton, Tulsa International Airport offers direct flights to several major cities.

Bus Stations Near Wilburton

  • Greyhound Bus Station - Mcalester: Located 23 miles south of Wilburton, this station offers daily bus service to several major cities.

  • Jefferson Lines - Fort Smith: Located 80 miles east of Wilburton, this station offers daily bus service to several major cities.

Other Options

  • Rental Car: Visitors can rent a car at several major airports and drive to Wilburton.

  • Taxi/Shuttle: There are several taxi and shuttle services in the area that can provide transportation to Wilburton.

How to Get Around in Wilburton

Wilburton itself is a small town and easy to navigate on foot. However, for those looking to explore the surrounding area, renting a car or bike is the best option.

Where to Go Next from Wilburton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For visitors who want to explore more of Oklahoma and the surrounding area, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Ouachita National Forest: This nearby national forest offers plenty of hiking and camping opportunities, as well as scenic views of the surrounding mountains.

  • Tulsa: This vibrant city located about two hours northwest of Wilburton is known for its art, music, and cultural attractions.

  • Hot Springs, Arkansas: This historic town is known for its natural hot springs and offers plenty of activities, from hiking to spa treatments.

Conclusion

Wilburton may be a small town, but it offers plenty to see and do, from exploring the area’s natural beauty to learning about its rich cultural heritage. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast or a history buff, Wilburton has something for everyone.
