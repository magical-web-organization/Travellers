Introduction to Oklahoma

Located in the Great Plains region of the United States, Oklahoma boasts a diverse range of attractions. From fascinating museums and historical landmarks to breathtaking natural wonders, there’s no shortage of things to see and do in this state. With a rich cultural heritage and friendly locals, Oklahoma is a must-visit destination for any traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Oklahoma

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Oklahoma is home to several important museums and historical sites that offer a glimpse into the state’s past. One such attraction is the Oklahoma City National Memorial & Museum, which honors the victims of the 1995 Oklahoma City bombing. Other must-visit museums include the National Cowboy & Western Heritage Museum, the Philbrook Museum of Art, and the Museum of Osteology.

Natural Attractions of Oklahoma: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Oklahoma’s diverse landscapes offer plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures. Some of the top natural attractions in the state include the Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge, the Tallgrass Prairie Preserve, and the Ozark National Forest. Visitors can also take a scenic drive on the Talimena Scenic Byway or hike the trails at Red Rock Canyon State Park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Oklahoma

Oklahoma has a rich hiking and biking scene, with trails for all levels of experience. The Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge offers several trails, including the Elk Mountain Trail and the Charon’s Garden Trail. The Osage Hills Trail System is another popular spot for hiking and biking.

Best Things to Do in Oklahoma: Activities and Tours

In addition to museums and outdoor adventures, visitors to Oklahoma can enjoy a variety of activities and tours. For thrill-seekers, there’s the Oklahoma City Skydiving Center or the Frontier City amusement park. Foodies will enjoy the Oklahoma City Food Tours or the Tulsa Food Tour. Other popular activities include hot air balloon rides, wine tastings, and ghost tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Oklahoma

Day 1: Explore Oklahoma City’s museums, including the National Cowboy & Western Heritage Museum and the Oklahoma City National Memorial & Museum.

Day 2: Hike the trails at the Tallgrass Prairie Preserve, followed by a visit to the Woolaroc Museum & Wildlife Preserve.

Day 3: Take a scenic drive on the Talimena Scenic Byway, stopping at the Queen Wilhelmina State Park for lunch.

Day 4: Visit the Philbrook Museum of Art in Tulsa, followed by a tour of the Tulsa Brewery District.

Day 5: Experience the natural wonders of the Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge, including the Charon’s Garden Trail and the Elk Mountain Trail.

Day 6: Take a hot air balloon ride over Oklahoma City, followed by a visit to the Myriad Botanical Gardens.

Day 7: Tour the historic Route 66, stopping in small towns like Chandler and Arcadia along the way.

Food and Drink Scene of Oklahoma

Must-Try Dishes

No visit to Oklahoma is complete without trying some of the state’s famous dishes. Must-tries include chicken fried steak, fried okra, and pecan pie. Other popular dishes include barbecue, chili, and cornbread.

Local Beverages

Oklahoma is home to several local breweries and wineries, including the Stonecloud Brewing Company in Oklahoma City and the Nuyaka Creek Winery in Bristow. Visitors can also try the state’s famous Dr Pepper and Big Red sodas.

Where to Stay in Oklahoma: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors to Oklahoma can choose from a variety of accommodations, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some top options include the Skirvin Hilton Oklahoma City, the Mayo Hotel in Tulsa, and the Shangri-La Resort on Grand Lake.

What to Buy in Oklahoma: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

From handmade pottery to Native American jewelry, there are plenty of unique souvenirs to bring home from Oklahoma. Other popular items include barbecue sauces, hot sauces, and locally-made jams and jellies.

Local Culture and Customs of Oklahoma

Oklahoma has a rich cultural heritage, with influences from Native Americans, cowboys, and settlers. Visitors should be respectful of Native American customs and traditions when visiting areas like the Cherokee Nation.

Best Time to Visit Oklahoma

The best time to visit Oklahoma is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the outdoor attractions are at their best. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and snowy in some parts of the state.

Do’s and Don’ts of Oklahoma

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, including chicken fried steak and fried okra.
  • Visit the state’s museums and natural attractions.
  • Be respectful of Native American customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the weather. Be prepared for hot summers and cold winters.
  • Forget to bring comfortable shoes for hiking and outdoor activities.
  • Disrespect the state’s flag or symbols.

Safety and Health

Safety in Oklahoma

Overall, Oklahoma is a safe state for travelers. Visitors should take standard safety precautions, such as locking car doors and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Oklahoma

Oklahoma has several hospitals and medical facilities throughout the state. In the event of a medical emergency, visitors can call 911.

Getting to Oklahoma: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Oklahoma

Oklahoma has several airports throughout the state, including Will Rogers World Airport in Oklahoma City and Tulsa International Airport in Tulsa.

Bus Stations Near Oklahoma

Greyhound Bus Lines serves several locations in Oklahoma, including Oklahoma City and Tulsa.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a train to Oklahoma. Amtrak serves several cities in Oklahoma, including Oklahoma City and Norman.

How to Get Around in Oklahoma

Renting a car is the most convenient way to get around Oklahoma. Public transportation options are limited, but visitors can take buses or taxis in larger cities.

Where to Go Next from Oklahoma: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Oklahoma is located in the heart of the United States, making it a great starting point for exploring nearby destinations. Some popular options include Texas to the south and Kansas to the north. Visitors can also explore nearby attractions like the Grand Canyon, which is a short flight away in neighboring Arizona.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor adventures, or local cuisine, Oklahoma has something for everyone. Plan your visit today to discover the best of this beautiful state.
