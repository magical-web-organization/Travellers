Durant is a vibrant city located in Chickasaw Country, Oklahoma. It boasts of an abundance of natural attractions and cultural landmarks that make it a great destination for both outdoor enthusiasts and history buffs. Whether you’re looking for hiking trails with breathtaking views or want to explore museums steeped in rich history, Durant has something for you. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan your trip and make the most of your time in this charming city.

Introduction to Durant

Durant has a rich and diverse history that dates back to the early 1800s. It was once home to the Choctaw Nation and played a significant role in the Trail of Tears migration. Today, it’s a thriving city known for its lively college town atmosphere and cultural landmarks.

List of Places to Visit in Durant

Durant has a myriad of attractions and activities to choose from. Here are some of the top things to do when you visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Choctaw Cultural Center: This state-of-the-art museum showcases the history, culture, and traditions of the Choctaw Nation. Visitors can explore exhibits and interactive displays that delve into the tribe’s rich heritage.
  • Three Valley Museum: This small but informative museum highlights the early history of Durant and the surrounding area. The exhibits include artifacts and memorabilia that chronicle the city’s development over time.
  • Fort Washita Historic Site: This former military outpost dates back to the 1800s and is now a historical landmark. Visitors can take a guided tour of the site and explore the restored buildings and barracks.

Natural Attractions of Durant: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Texoma: This sprawling reservoir spans across the Texas-Oklahoma border and offers a range of outdoor activities, including boating, fishing, and kayaking.
  • Durant Multi-Sport Complex: This expansive complex features hiking and biking trails, sports fields, and a disc golf course.
  • Catfish Bay Marina: This marina offers boat rentals and fishing opportunities on Lake Texoma.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Durant

Durant has a variety of scenic trails that are great for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are a few must-visit routes:

  • Choctaw Nation Trail: This 54-mile trail runs through the heart of Durant and offers scenic views of the Red River Valley.
  • Cross Timbers Hiking Trail: This trail is located in the nearby Lake Texoma State Park and features picturesque views of the lake and surrounding woodlands.
  • Durant Multi-Sport Complex Trails: This expansive complex features over three miles of hiking and biking trails that wind through the scenic countryside.

Best Things to Do in Durant: Activities and Tours

Durant is home to a variety of fun and engaging activities to suit any interest. Here are some of the best things to do when you visit:

  • Visit the Downtown Durant Historic District. This picturesque district is home to numerous shops and restaurants and is a great place to explore the city’s historical landmarks.
  • Take a guided fishing tour of Lake Texoma. This reservoir is known for its abundant catfish and striped bass, making it a great destination for fishing enthusiasts.
  • Attend a concert or sporting event at the Choctaw Casino & Resort. This popular entertainment venue hosts a variety of high-profile performers and events throughout the year.

Week-Long Itinerary for Durant

If you’re planning a week-long visit to Durant, here’s an itinerary that will help you make the most of your time:

  • Day 1: Visit the Choctaw Cultural Center and explore the rich history and culture of the Choctaw Nation.
  • Day 2: Hike the Choctaw Nation Trail and take in the picturesque views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Day 3: Head to Lake Texoma for a day of fishing and boating on the water.
  • Day 4: Explore the Downtown Durant Historic District and enjoy a meal at one of the local restaurants.
  • Day 5: Take a guided tour of the Three Valley Museum and learn about the early history of the city.
  • Day 6: Visit the Fort Washita Historic Site and discover the role it played in the region’s early history.
  • Day 7: Attend a concert or event at the Choctaw Casino & Resort to cap off your week in style.

Food and Drink Scene of Durant

Durant offers a variety of delicious cuisine and drinks for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the local favorites:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Catfish: This staple of Southern cuisine is a must-try when you’re in Durant.
  • Indian Tacos: A local specialty that features frybread, taco meat, cheese, and other toppings.
  • BBQ Brisket: Tender and flavorful brisket is a favorite at many of Durant’s BBQ joints.

Local Beverages

  • Kiamichi IPA: A locally brewed beer with a hoppy and citrusy flavor.
  • Native American Herbal Tea: This unique tea incorporates indigenous plants and herbs for a distinct flavor.
  • Oklahoma Wines: Several wineries in the area offer tastings of locally produced wines.

Where to Stay in Durant: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Durant has a range of accommodation options to suit any budget and preference. Here are some of the best places to stay in the city:

  • Choctaw Casino & Resort: This luxurious resort features a variety of dining options, entertainment venues, and a casino.
  • La Quinta Inn & Suites: This affordable hotel features comfortable rooms and convenient amenities, including a fitness center and outdoor pool.
  • Durant / Choctaw KOA: This campground offers RV and tent sites, as well as cabins and lodges.

What to Buy in Durant: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs or locally made products, Durant has plenty to offer. Here are some ideas for what to buy:

  • Choctaw pottery and crafts: These handmade items showcase the heritage and culture of the Choctaw Nation.
  • Locally made jams, jellies, and honey: Many farms and orchards in the area produce delicious preserves and honey that make great gifts.
  • Oklahoma souvenirs: T-shirts, hats, and other items that feature the state’s iconic symbols are popular souvenirs for visitors.

Local Culture and Customs of Durant

Durant has a unique blend of cultures and traditions, ranging from Native American heritage to Southern hospitality. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and follow these guidelines:

  • Dress appropriately for cultural landmarks and events.
  • Show respect for the traditions and customs of the Choctaw Nation.
  • Embrace the laid-back and friendly culture of the city.

Best Time to Visit Durant

The best time to visit Durant is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and snowy.

Getting to Durant: Transportation Options and Logistics

Durant is easily accessible by car or bus, and there are several transportation options available for visitors. Here are some tips for getting to and around the city:

The best way to get around Durant is by car, as the city is spread out and not easily walkable. Rental cars are available at the airport or in town, and there are also several transportation services that offer rides.
