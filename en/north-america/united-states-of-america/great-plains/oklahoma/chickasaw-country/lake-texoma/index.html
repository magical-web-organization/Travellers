Introduction to Lake Texoma

Located in Chickasaw Country, in Oklahoma, in the Great Plains of the United States of America, Lake Texoma is a sprawling reservoir formed by the Denison Dam on the Red River. With over 89,000 acres of surface area, it is the largest lake in Oklahoma and boasts a variety of attractions and activities for visitors of all ages. Whether you’re looking for a weekend getaway or a longer vacation, Lake Texoma is the perfect destination for those who appreciate rugged outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Lake Texoma

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Eisenhower Birthplace State Historic Site: This museum is located in Denison, Texas, just across the border from Oklahoma. It was once the home of Dwight D. Eisenhower, the 34th President of the United States, and is now a museum dedicated to his life and presidency.

  • Fort Washita Historic Site: Fort Washita was established by the United States Army in 1842 to protect Native American tribes in the area. Today, visitors can explore the fort’s restored buildings and learn about its history.

  • Chickasaw Cultural Center: This center showcases the history and culture of the Chickasaw Nation through exhibits, demonstrations, and programs. Visitors can participate in traditional dances, learn about tribal customs, and even try traditional Chickasaw foods.

Natural Attractions of Lake Texoma: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cross Timbers Hiking Trail: This trail stretches for over 14 miles along the southern shore of Lake Texoma and offers spectacular views of the lake and surrounding wilderness areas. With easy-to-moderate difficulty, it’s perfect for hikers of all skill levels.

  • Lake Texoma State Park: This state park offers a variety of outdoor activities, including fishing, boating, hiking, and camping. With over 1,000 acres of rolling hills and pristine shoreline, there’s plenty of room to explore and enjoy the great outdoors.

  • Hagerman National Wildlife Refuge: This 11,300-acre wildlife refuge is located on the Big Mineral Arm of Lake Texoma and is home to over 300 species of birds, as well as a variety of other wildlife. Visitors can hike or bike on the refuge’s trails, go fishing or boating on the lake, or simply relax and take in the scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lake Texoma

Lake Texoma is a popular destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to explore the area:

  • Cross Timbers Hiking Trail: This trail is a must-visit for hikers who want to experience the natural beauty of Lake Texoma. It is 14.5 miles long and follows the heavily wooded shoreline of the lake.

  • Juniper Point Trail: This easy 0.8-mile trail offers stunning views of the blue waters of Lake Texoma and is perfect for family hikes.

  • Tanglewood Trail: This 1.4-mile trail winds through hilly terrain and offers scenic views of the lake. It is popular with both hikers and mountain bikers.

Best Things to Do in Lake Texoma: Activities and Tours

Lake Texoma offers a wide variety of activities and tours to keep visitors entertained. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Fishing: Lake Texoma is home to a variety of fish species, including striped bass, largemouth bass, catfish, and crappie. Visitors can charter a fishing boat or rent equipment to try their hand at catching one of these trophy-sized fish.

  • Boating: With over 89,000 acres of water, Lake Texoma is a boater’s paradise. Visitors can rent a pontoon boat, kayak, or jet ski to explore the lake and its many coves and inlets.

  • Golfing: Lake Texoma is home to several golf courses, including the Chickasaw Pointe Golf Club, which offers stunning views of the lake and challenging fairways.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lake Texoma

Day 1: Visit the Chickasaw Cultural Center and learn about the history and traditions of the Chickasaw people.

Day 2: Take a fishing charter out on Lake Texoma and try to catch your dinner.

Day 3: Explore the Cross Timbers Hiking Trail and take in the stunning views of the lake.

Day 4: Rent a boat and spend the day exploring the coves and inlets of Lake Texoma.

Day 5: Visit Fort Washita Historic Site and learn about the history of the area.

Day 6: Play a round of golf at the Chickasaw Pointe Golf Club.

Day 7: Relax on the shoreline and take in the stunning sunsets over the lake.

Food and Drink Scene of Lake Texoma

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tex-Mex: With its close proximity to Texas, Lake Texoma boasts a variety of Tex-Mex restaurants serving up spicy dishes like fajitas and enchiladas.

  • Barbecue: Oklahoma is known for its barbecue, and Lake Texoma has no shortage of delicious options. Try some pulled pork or brisket from one of the many local barbecue restaurants.

  • Fried Catfish: Lake Texoma is famous for its catfish, and fried catfish dinners can be found at many restaurants around the lake.

Local Beverages

  • Oklahoma Craft Beer: Oklahoma has a growing craft beer scene, and Lake Texoma is home to several breweries. Be sure to try some local beers from places like Rusty Nail Brewing Company and Island Nation Brewing.

  • Wine: The Lake Texoma area is home to several wineries, including Homestead Winery and Vineyards. Visitors can enjoy tastings or take a bottle home to enjoy later.

Where to Stay in Lake Texoma: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lake Texoma has a variety of accommodation options, from campgrounds to luxury hotels. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Lake Texoma RV Resort: This campground offers RV sites as well as cabins and tent camping options. It also has a swimming pool, playground, and mini-golf course.

  • Tanglewood Resort and Conference Center: This resort offers a range of rooms, including suites and villas. Guests can enjoy golfing, spa treatments, and lake activities on-site.

  • Marina Place: This lakeside hotel offers comfortable rooms and suites, as well as boat rentals and a marina.

What to Buy in Lake Texoma: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Lake Texoma Apparel: Show off your love for Lake Texoma with t-shirts, sweatshirts, and hats sporting the lake’s name.

  • Chickasaw Nation Arts and Crafts: The Chickasaw Nation is known for its beautiful pottery, beadwork, and basketry. Visitors can purchase these items at the Chickasaw Cultural Center or at various markets and stores around the area.

  • Oklahoma Wine: Take home a bottle of wine from one of the many wineries in the area as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Lake Texoma

The area around Lake Texoma is heavily influenced by the Chickasaw Nation, and visitors should be respectful of the tribe’s traditions and customs. One important custom is to ask for permission before taking photos of tribal members or their possessions.

Best Time to Visit Lake Texoma

The best time to visit Lake Texoma is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and the crowds are thinner. Summer can be hot and crowded, while winter can be chilly and quiet.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lake Texoma

Do:

  • Do take advantage of all the outdoor activities Lake Texoma has to offer, from fishing to hiking to boating.

  • Do try the local food and drink scene, including Tex-Mex, barbecue, and local craft beer and wine.

  • Do respect the traditions and customs of the Chickasaw Nation.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash behind in the parks or on the lake.

  • Don’t trespass on private property.

  • Don’t disturb the wildlife or local ecosystems.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lake Texoma

Lake Texoma is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should always take precautions to ensure their safety. Some things to keep in mind:

  • Always wear a life jacket while on the water.

  • Be aware of changing weather conditions.

  • Use sunscreen and insect repellent to protect yourself from the sun and bugs.

Health in Lake Texoma

Lake Texoma is not a known hotspot for any infectious diseases, but visitors should still take precautions to protect their health. Make sure to drink plenty of water to stay hydrated, especially during the hot summer months.

Getting to Lake Texoma: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lake Texoma

The closest major airport to Lake Texoma is Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport, which is about an hour’s drive away. Smaller regional airports in the area include the Ardmore Municipal Airport and the Gainesville Municipal Airport.

Bus Stations Near Lake Texoma

Greyhound buses stop in several towns near Lake Texoma, including Gainesville, Texas, and Ardmore, Oklahoma.

Other Options

Some visitors choose to arrive by boat or seaplane, as Lake Texoma has several marinas and seaplane landing areas.

How to Get Around in Lake Texoma

The best way to get around Lake Texoma is by car, as many of the area’s attractions are spread out. Some visitors also choose to hike or bike between attractions.

Where to Go Next from Lake Texoma: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Broken Bow Lake: Located in southeastern Oklahoma, Broken Bow Lake is a popular destination for boating, fishing, and camping.

  • Turner Falls: This natural attraction is located in the Arbuckle Mountains and features a 77-foot waterfall and natural swimming pools.

  • Chickasaw National Recreation Area: This park in south-central Oklahoma is known for its natural springs and hiking trails.

Conclusion

Lake Texoma is a natural wonderland that offers something for everyone. With its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural history, and wide variety of outdoor activities, it’s the perfect destination for a weekend getaway or longer vacation. Be sure to plan your trip in advance to make the most of your time at this beautiful destination.
