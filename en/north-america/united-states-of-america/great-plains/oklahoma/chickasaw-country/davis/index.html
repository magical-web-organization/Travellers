Introduction to Davis

Welcome to Davis! Located in Chickasaw Country, Davis is a hidden gem in the Great Plains of Oklahoma. Its rich Native American history, scenic beauty, and warm hospitality make it an ideal getaway destination for nature lovers, adventure enthusiasts, history buffs, and cultural travelers alike.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and what to eat in Davis. So, let’s start exploring!

List of Places to Visit in Davis

Here are some must-visit places in Davis:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chickasaw Cultural Center: This beautiful center offers an immersive experience of Chickasaw culture through interactive exhibits, traditional art, dance performances, and language classes.
  • Turner Falls Park: Named after Mazeppa Thomas Turner, this 77-foot waterfall is the largest in Oklahoma. You can swim under the falls, hike to the top, or enjoy scenic views from a distance.
  • Healdton Oil Museum: Learn about the history and evolution of the oil industry in Oklahoma at this fascinating museum.
  • Artesian Hotel: This stunning 81-room hotel was built in 1906 and has played an important role in the history of the town. It’s been beautifully restored and features a casino, spa, and multiple dining options.

Natural Attractions of Davis: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Arbuckle Wilderness Park: This 200-acre wildlife preserve is home to more than 400 exotic animals from 80 species. You can drive your car through the park, feed the animals, and enjoy a picnic.
  • Lake of the Arbuckles: This serene lake offers multiple water activities like boating, fishing, swimming, and hiking.
  • Chickasaw National Recreation Area: This park offers an array of outdoor activities including hiking, fishing, swimming, and camping. The area is rich with natural springs and scenic landscapes.
  • Cross Bar Ranch: This 6,500-acre ranch has multiple hiking and biking trails, fishing ponds, and wildlife to explore.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Davis

  • Bromide Hill Trail: This 1.5-mile trail offers scenic views of the town and the Arbuckle Mountains.
  • Little Niagara Trail: This short trail leads to a scenic waterfall and a natural swimming hole.
  • Guy Sandy Trail: This moderate 2.5-mile loop trail offers beautiful views of the Cross Bar Ranch.

Best Things to Do in Davis: Activities and Tours

  • Soar above Davis: Take a hot air balloon ride and enjoy a bird’s eye view of the beautiful landscape.
  • Go fishing: Davis has many pristine lakes and ponds where you can fish or enjoy other water sports.
  • Outdoor adventures: Go zip-lining, horseback riding, ATV riding, or rent a kayak and explore the lakes and rivers.
  • Arbuckle Historical Society Museum: Explore the history of Arbuckle Mountains and Davis through artifacts and exhibits.

Food and Drink Scene of Davis

Davis has a vibrant food scene, with many local restaurants serving a mix of American and Native American specialties.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried catfish
  • Chickasaw tacos
  • Bison burgers
  • Burnt ends

Local Beverages

  • Blackberry wine
  • Sassafras tea
  • Chickasaw coffee

Where to Stay in Davis: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Artesian Hotel: This luxurious 81-room hotel offers vintage-style rooms, a spa, casino, multiple dining options, and great views of Chickasaw National Recreation Area.
  • Chickasaw Retreat and Conference Center: A perfect mix of comfort, convenience, and natural beauty, this center offers a range of accommodation options, conference facilities, swimming pool, and other amenities.
  • Chickasaw RV Park: Perfect for those traveling with RVs, this park provides all the necessary facilities like hookups, restrooms, showers, laundry, and Wi-fi.
  • Local Bed and Breakfasts: There are several cozy and charming B&Bs in and around Davis that offer personalized service, home-cooked food, and a warm atmosphere.

What to Buy in Davis: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Chickasaw Nation Souvenirs: You can buy Chickasaw Indian-made products like pottery, baskets, blankets, jewelry, and more from the souvenir shops in Davis.
  • Native American Art: The Chickasaw Cultural Center has a gift shop selling traditional Chickasaw art, crafts, and clothing.
  • Arbuckles Coffee: This award-winning local coffee has been roasted in the area for over 150 years and is a must-buy for coffee lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of Davis

Davis has a rich Native American culture and a strong sense of community. Respect for nature and the elders, love for music and dance, and hospitality are some of the core values of the locals.

Best Time to Visit Davis

The best time to visit Davis is between March to November when the weather is pleasant, and most of the outdoor activities are open. However, the spring and fall months offer the best weather and avoid the summer crowd.

Do’s and Don’ts of Davis

Do:

  • Respect the Native American culture and customs.
  • Enjoy the natural beauty of the region but remember to take only memories and leave only footprints.
  • Try the local food and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the environment in any way.
  • Ignore the safety guidelines while indulging in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Davis

Davis is a safe place to visit. However, it’s recommended to take general safety precautions like keeping an eye on your belongings and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Davis

The healthcare system in Davis is excellent with several hospitals and medical facilities nearby. Visitors are advised to carry necessary medical and travel insurance.

Getting to Davis: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Davis

  • Will Rogers World Airport (OKC): Located in Oklahoma City, about 80 miles from Davis, this airport is the nearest and most convenient international airport.
  • Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport (DFW): Located in Dallas, about 136 miles from Davis, this airport is another option for international travelers.

Bus Stations Near Davis

  • Ardmore Station: This bus station is located about 18 miles from Davis and serves Greyhound buses.
  • Gainesville Bus Station: This station is located about 40 miles from Davis and serves transportation companies like Bonham Stage Lines and others.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: You can rent a car from the nearby airports or bus stations to reach Davis or explore the area.
  • Private Car Services: Several private car services offer direct and comfortable transportation from the airports or other nearby cities to Davis.

How to Get Around in Davis

Driving is the best way to explore Davis and its natural surroundings. However, there are also several taxis, shuttle services, and car rental options available.

Where to Go Next from Davis: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Sulphur: This charming town is located just 10 miles south of Davis and offers a range of outdoor and cultural activities.
  • Turner Falls Park: This beautiful park is located about 20 miles north of Davis and is a great natural attraction with hiking trails and waterfalls.
  • Oklahoma City: Located about 80 miles north of Davis, this vibrant city has several museums, attractions, and historic sites worth exploring.

Conclusion

Davis is a beautiful hidden gem in the Great Plains of Oklahoma with a rich history, vibrant culture, and abundant natural beauty. Plan your trip with this comprehensive travel guide to discover the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and what to eat in Davis. Be prepared to be mesmerized by the stunning landscapes, warm hospitality, and unique experiences that Davis has to offer.
