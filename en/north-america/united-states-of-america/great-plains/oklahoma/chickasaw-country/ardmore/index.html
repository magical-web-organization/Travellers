Introduction to Ardmore

Located in Chickasaw Country, Oklahoma, Ardmore is a charming city that sits at the foothills of the Arbuckle Mountains. Known for its rich cultural heritage, historic landmarks, and natural beauty, Ardmore is a popular destination for travelers seeking a balance between outdoor adventures and cultural experiences.

List of Places to Visit in Ardmore

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Greater Southwest Historical Museum: The museum is dedicated to preserving and promoting the cultural history of the Southwest. The exhibits showcase the lives and livelihoods of the early settlers, pioneers, and Native Americans who inhabited the region.

  • Eliza Cruce Hall Doll Museum: Located on the campus of the Ardmore Convention Center, the museum features over 1,000 antique dolls, including rare and hard-to-find pieces from around the world.

  • Ardmore Historic Downtown: The downtown area of Ardmore is filled with charming buildings and cultural landmarks that offer a glimpse into the city’s rich history. From art galleries to antique shops, downtown Ardmore offers something for everyone.

Natural Attractions of Ardmore: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Turner Falls Park: Located just a short drive from Ardmore, this park is home to one of the tallest waterfalls in Oklahoma. The park offers plenty of hiking trails, swimming areas, and camping facilities for visitors.

  • Lake Murray State Park: The park boasts over 12,000 acres of beautiful landscapes, including a pristine lake that’s perfect for fishing and water activities. Visitors can also enjoy hiking, biking, and horseback riding on the numerous trails.

  • The Chickasaw National Recreation Area: This stunning national park offers visitors the chance to explore hundreds of acres of scenic landscapes, natural springs, and forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ardmore

Ardmore offers some of the best hiking and biking routes in Oklahoma. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Lake Murray Nature Trail: This scenic trail runs along the banks of the lake and showcases the natural beauty of the area. The trail is suitable for both hiking and biking.

  • Ardmore Central Park: The park features a paved walking trail that’s perfect for a leisurely stroll or a bike ride.

  • Lake Murray Dam: A popular spot for hikers, the dam provides excellent views of the surrounding area and the lake.

Best Things to Do in Ardmore: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Ardmore Emporium: A popular attraction for travelers seeking unique souvenirs, the Ardmore Emporium is a treasure trove of local art, antiques, and handmade crafts.

  • Take a Brewery Tour: Ardmore is home to several award-winning microbreweries, including the Ardmore Brewing Company and the Red River Brewery. Taking a brewery tour is a great way to sample some of the best craft beers in the area.

  • Go Fishing in Lake Murray: Known for its clear waters and abundant fish populations, Lake Murray is a popular destination for anglers. Visitors can rent boats or kayaks, or fish from the banks.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ardmore

Day 1-2: Explore the cultural landmarks of Ardmore, including the Greater Southwest Historical Museum and the Eliza Cruce Hall Doll Museum.

Day 3-4: Take a hike on one of the many scenic trails in the area, such as the Lake Murray Nature Trail or the Turner Falls Park Trail.

Day 5: Visit the local breweries and sample some of the best craft beers in the area.

Day 6: Enjoy a day of fishing or water activities at Lake Murray.

Day 7: Take a day trip to the Chickasaw National Recreation Area and explore the stunning landscapes and natural springs.

Food and Drink Scene of Ardmore

Must-Try Dishes

  • Southern-Style BBQ: Oklahoma is known for its excellent barbecue, and Ardmore is no exception. Make sure to try the pulled pork, brisket, and smoked sausage at popular spots like Dickey’s Barbecue Pit and Smokey M’s BBQ.

  • Chicken Fried Steak: A classic Southern comfort food, the chicken fried steak at Prairie Kitchen is a must-try for visitors in Ardmore.

  • Fried Catfish: Fresh from the lake, the fried catfish at the Fisherman’s Cove Restaurant is a local favorite.

Local Beverages

  • Local Craft Beer: Ardmore is home to several award-winning microbreweries, including Ardmore Brewing Company and Red River Brewery. Visitors can sample some of the best craft beers in Oklahoma on a brewery tour.

  • Sweet Tea: A popular beverage in the South, sweet tea can be found at most restaurants and cafes in Ardmore.

Where to Stay in Ardmore: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Springhill Suites Ardmore: This stylish hotel offers comfortable rooms and suites with top-notch amenities, including an indoor pool and fitness center.

  • La Quinta Inn and Suites Ardmore: Located just a short drive from downtown Ardmore, this hotel offers spacious rooms and suites, free breakfast, and a heated pool.

  • Magnuson Hotel Ardmore: This budget-friendly hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms, free Wi-Fi, and a complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Ardmore: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Art and Crafts: The Ardmore Emporium is a must-visit for travelers seeking unique local products, including handmade art and crafts.

  • Oklahoma-made Food Products: From locally-made honey to pecan brittle, there are plenty of Oklahoma-made food products to bring home from Ardmore. Try the gourmet popcorn at Bedré Fine Chocolate or the homemade jams at Hilltop Ranch.

Local Culture and Customs of Ardmore

Ardmore’s culture is heavily influenced by its Native American history, which is reflected in the local art, music, and cuisine. The city is also known for its love of sports, particularly football and basketball.

Best Time to Visit Ardmore

The best time to visit Ardmore is during the fall and spring months, when the temperatures are mild and the weather is perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ardmore

Do:

  • Do visit the cultural landmarks and scenic attractions in Ardmore.

  • Do try the local cuisine, including Southern-style BBQ, fried catfish, and chicken fried steak.

  • Do take a brewery tour to sample some of the best craft beer in Oklahoma.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to pack sunscreen and bug spray if you plan to spend time outdoors.

  • Don’t visit during the summer months, when temperatures can soar well above 100 degrees.

  • Don’t forget to respect the local customs and traditions of Ardmore’s Native American community.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ardmore

Ardmore is a generally safe city, but visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety. Avoid walking alone at night and keep valuables out of sight to prevent theft.

Health in Ardmore

Ardmore has several medical facilities, including Mercy Hospital Ardmore and Healthcare Express Urgent Care, should visitors require medical attention.

Getting to Ardmore: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ardmore

The nearest airport to Ardmore is the Will Rogers World Airport in Oklahoma City, located approximately 95 miles northeast of the city.

Bus Stations Near Ardmore

Greyhound operates a bus station in Ardmore, making it easy to travel to and from the city via bus.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi to get around Ardmore and the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Ardmore

Visitors can get around Ardmore by car, taxi, or public transportation.

Where to Go Next from Ardmore: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Chickasaw Cultural Center: This cultural center showcases the history and heritage of the indigenous Chickasaw people and is located just a short drive from Ardmore.

  • Arbuckle Wilderness Park: Just a 30-minute drive from Ardmore, this wildlife park offers visitors the chance to see exotic animals up close.

  • Lake Texoma: Located on the Texas-Oklahoma border, Lake Texoma is a popular destination for boating, fishing, and water sports.

Conclusion

Ardmore offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural landmarks that make it a must-visit destination for any traveler. From hiking and biking to local cuisine and craft beer, there’s something for everyone in this charming city. Plan your visit today and discover all that Ardmore has to offer!
