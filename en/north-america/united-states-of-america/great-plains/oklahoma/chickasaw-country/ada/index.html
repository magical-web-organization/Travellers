Introduction to Ada

Located in Chickasaw Country, Ada is a charming town in Oklahoma that offers visitors a unique blend of cultural and natural attractions. Set amidst the rolling hills of the Great Plains, Ada is known for its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and warm hospitality. Whether you are a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or simply looking for a relaxing getaway, Ada has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Ada

There are a number of places to explore in Ada that offer a range of experiences:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chickasaw Cultural Center: This center showcases the history, culture, and traditions of the Chickasaw Nation, with interactive exhibits, films, and artwork.
  • Pontotoc County Historical and Genealogical Society Museum: The museum features exhibits on the history of the region, including displays on the Civil War, ranching, and early medicine.
  • Byrds Mill Spring: This spring is known for its picturesque beauty and historical significance, as it was a major water source for early settlers.
  • Barber Vintage Motorsports Museum: This museum features an extensive collection of vintage motorcycles and racing cars.

Natural Attractions of Ada: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Chickasaw National Recreation Area: This area comprises of natural springs, lakes, and trails offering ample opportunities for outdoor activities such as hiking, bird-watching, swimming, and fishing.
  • Redbud Park: A popular picnic spot, with a playground, a skate park, and scenic views.
  • Wintersmith Park: A scenic park with walking trails, picnic areas, and a fishing pond.
  • Fittstown Nature Preserve: A hidden gem of Ada, offering stunning vistas and a diverse range of plant and animal life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ada

Ada offers several excellent hiking and biking routes with breathtaking scenery, from rugged trails to scenic roads:

  • Veterans Memorial Lake Trail: A two-mile trail around Veterans Memorial Lake, offering peaceful views and opportunities for bird-watching and fishing.
  • Glenwood Trail: A moderate three-mile hike through the Chickasaw National Recreation Area, featuring beautiful waterfalls and wildflowers.
  • Travertine Creek Trail: A scenic 1.8-mile hiking trail through the Chickasaw National Recreation Area, with cool swimming holes and stunning rock formations.
  • Stevens Gap Trail: A moderate three-mile hike through the Chickasaw National Recreation Area, offering panoramic views and excellent photo opportunities.

Best Things to Do in Ada: Activities and Tours

Ada offers plenty of activities to enjoy, catering to every taste and budget, from guided tours to adventurous excursions:

  • Amish Country Tour: A unique opportunity to explore Amish country and learn about their unique way of life.
  • River Road Park: A perfect spot for swimming, kayaking, and canoeing.
  • Cow Pasture Golf Course: A beautiful 18-hole golf course surrounded by rolling hills and lush greenery.
  • Oak Hills Winery: A local winery, offering tasting sessions and tours of their vineyards.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ada

Here is an example of a week-long itinerary for Ada:

Food and Drink Scene of Ada

Ada has unique and delicious local cuisine that offers a diverse palate of flavors:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried pies: A local favorite, these sweet or savory pies are made with a flaky pastry crust and a flavorful filling.
  • Fried okra: Fresh, crunchy and perfect for a snack, these okras are fried to perfection.
  • Frito chili pie: Made with Fritos, chili, cheese, onions, and jalapenos, this dish is a filling and delicious treat.
  • Biscuits and gravy: A Southern classic, this dish features homemade biscuits topped with a rich sausage gravy.

Local Beverages

  • Tornado Alley: A cocktail made with equal parts of peach schnapps, Southern Comfort, and cranberry juice. A perfect drink for a hot and humid day.
  • Choctaw beer: A local craft beer brewed in Oklahoma with a unique mixture of hops and malts.

Where to Stay in Ada: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ada has a range of accommodations that suit every budget and preference:

  • Hampton Inn & Suites Ada: A modern and comfortable hotel, with a business center, fitness room, and free breakfast.
  • La Quinta Inn & Suites Ada: A budget-friendly hotel, with comfortable rooms and free breakfast.
  • Stone Creek Bed and Breakfast: A cozy and romantic retreat, featuring luxurious rooms and personalized service.
  • Ardmore Inn Bed and Breakfast: A charming B&B, known for its warm hospitality and delicious breakfast.

What to Buy in Ada: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ada offers a range of unique and memorable souvenirs that showcase the local culture and flavors:

  • Chickasaw Nation gift shop: A perfect place to buy Chickasaw-themed souvenirs, including jewelry, artwork, and authentic apparel.
  • Chantrois Farm: A lovely farmstead, where you can buy locally-produced honey, vegetables, and eggs.
  • Oklahoma Market Place: A wonderful farmer’s market, featuring a range of handmade items such as candles, soap, and pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of Ada

Ada is a town with a rich cultural heritage and a strong sense of community. Visitors are often struck by the warmth and friendliness of the locals, who are always ready to share their stories and traditions. It is important to be respectful of the traditions and customs of the Chickasaw Nation and to learn more about their history and culture.

Best Time to Visit Ada

The best time to visit Ada is during the spring (March-May) and fall (September-November) when the weather is pleasant and the foliage is at its most vibrant. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be quite chilly, so it’s best to plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ada

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Ada:

Do:

  • Respect and learn about the traditions and customs of the Chickasaw Nation.
  • Embrace the local culture and cuisine and try new foods.
  • Get out and explore the natural beauty of the area.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect or disregard the traditions and customs of the Chickasaw Nation.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Ignore the signs and warnings posted in recreational areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ada

Ada is a relatively safe town, with low crime rates. Visitors should take standard precautions to ensure their safety, such as locking their cars and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Ada

Ada has a range of medical facilities and services, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. Visitors should ensure they have adequate medical insurance and should take any necessary precautions, such as getting vaccines and medications before traveling.

Getting to Ada: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ada

Ada is serviced by the Ada Municipal Airport, which offers limited direct flights to Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport. The closest major airports are Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport and Will Rogers World Airport in Oklahoma City.

Bus Stations Near Ada

Greyhound operates a bus service that stops at the Ada Bus Station, which is conveniently located in the town center.

Other Options

There are several car rental companies in Ada, including Enterprise, Hertz, and Avis. Taxis and rideshare services such as Uber and Lyft are also available.

How to Get Around in Ada

Ada is a small town, and many of the attractions are located in close proximity to each other. Walking, biking, and driving are the most convenient ways to get around.

Where to Go Next from Ada: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Sulphur: A popular spa town, home to the Chickasaw National Recreation Area and the Chickasaw Cultural Center.
  • Davis: A charming town, with several attractions such as Turner Falls, Arbuckle Wilderness, and Bedre Fine Chocolate.
  • Oklahoma City: The state capital of Oklahoma, featuring cultural attractions such as the Oklahoma City National Memorial and Museum and the National Cowboy and Western Heritage Museum.

Conclusion

Ada is a hidden gem of Oklahoma, offering a delightful mix of natural beauty, rich history, and warm hospitality. With a range of attractions and activities to suit every taste and budget, Ada is a perfect destination for a relaxing and enjoyable vacation. Plan your trip today and discover the magic of Ada!
