Chickasaw Country, located in the Great Plains of Oklahoma, is a destination that offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage. From outdoor adventures and scenic vistas to museums and historical landmarks, Chickasaw Country has something for everyone. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the top attractions, must-try food and drink, where to stay, and much more. So, let’s get started!

Introduction to Chickasaw Country

Named after the Chickasaw Nation Native American tribe, Chickasaw Country is a region that covers 7,648 square miles within the state of Oklahoma. With its stunning landscapes, rich history, and vibrant culture, Chickasaw Country is a destination that should be on every traveler’s bucket list.

List of Places to Visit in Chickasaw Country

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chickasaw Cultural Center: A living history museum that showcases the Chickasaw culture through exhibits, demonstrations, and interactive experiences.
  • The Chickasaw Nation Capitol Building: A historic landmark that served as the seat of government for the Chickasaw Nation until Oklahoma became a state in 1907.
  • Fort Washita Historic Site: A restored 19th-century military fort that played a crucial role in protecting the Chickasaw Nation during the Civil War era.
  • Chickasaw National Recreation Area: A lush, natural park that features freshwater springs, hiking trails, and camping sites.

Natural Attractions of Chickasaw Country: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Arbuckle Mountains: A breathtaking mountain range that offers hiking, camping, and rock-climbing opportunities.
  • Lake Murray State Park: A recreational oasis with 5,700 acres of clear water for boating, swimming, and fishing.
  • Turner Falls Park: A scenic destination with a 77-foot waterfall, natural swimming pools, and hiking trails.
  • Chickasaw National Wildlife Refuge: A protected wilderness area with over 10,000 acres of wetlands, forests, and prairies teeming with wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chickasaw Country

  • Ouachita Trail: A 223-mile long trail that includes some of the most scenic sections of the Ouachita Mountains.
  • Chickasaw National Recreation Area: A system of trails that offer various levels of difficulty and scenic views of the park’s natural wonders.
  • Ardmore Rails-to-Trails: A scenic paved trail that follows the abandoned railway line between Ardmore and Springer.

Best Things to Do in Chickasaw Country: Activities and Tours

  • WinStar World Casino and Resort: A world-class gambling and entertainment destination that features over 800,000 square feet of gaming space, live shows, and gourmet dining options.
  • Rusty Nail Winery: A local winery that offers vineyard tours, wine tastings, and live music events.
  • Cross Bar Ranch: An off-road adventure park that features over 6,500 acres of trails, mud pits, and challenging terrain to explore.
  • Bedre Fine Chocolate: A chocolate factory and store that offers tours, tastings, and a wide range of delicious sweet treats.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chickasaw Country

Day 1: Arrive in Ardmore, check into your hotel and explore the historic downtown area.

Day 2-3: Visit the Chickasaw Cultural Center and other historical landmarks in the area.

Day 4-5: Enjoy outdoor adventures, such as hiking, kayaking, and fishing in the Arbuckle Mountains and Lake Murray State Park.

Day 6-7: Wind down with some wine tastings, live music, and shopping in the quaint towns of Davis and Sulphur.

Food and Drink Scene of Chickasaw Country

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried catfish: A regional staple, served crispy and seasoned with spices.
  • Chicken-fried steak: A hearty dish made with tenderized beef, breaded, and fried to perfection.
  • Biscuits and gravy: A classic breakfast dish that features fluffy biscuits and a savory gravy made from bacon or sausage drippings.
  • Indian tacos: A unique spin on the classic dish that features fry bread topped with ground beef, lettuce, tomatoes, and cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Oaklahoma craft beer: From local breweries such as COOP Ale Works, Prairie Artisan Ales, and Roughtail Brewing Company.
  • Medicine Stone Coffee: A locally-roasted blend that is enjoyed at many of the region’s cafes and restaurants.

Where to Stay in Chickasaw Country: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Artesian Hotel, Casino, and Spa: A luxurious hotel in Sulphur that offers elegant rooms, a full-service spa, and a casino.
  • Lake Murray Floating Cabins: Unique and fully-furnished cabins that float on Lake Murray.
  • Pointe Vista Development: A collection of spacious villas and condos located on the shores of Lake Texoma.

What to Buy in Chickasaw Country: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Chickasaw-made crafts: Authentic Native American artwork, jewelry, and pottery make for unique and meaningful souvenirs.
  • Bedre Chocolates: A variety of decadent, handmade chocolates available at the Bedre Fine Chocolate store.
  • Oklahoma-themed apparel: From t-shirts, caps, and hoodies to coffee mugs and fridge magnets, you’ll find something special for everyone on your list.

Local Culture and Customs of Chickasaw Country

The Chickasaw Nation has a rich and storied history that is celebrated in various cultural events such as powwows, festivals, and arts and crafts fairs. Visitors are encouraged to learn about the traditions and customs of the Chickasaw people and to treat the region’s natural and cultural resources with respect and reverence.

Best Time to Visit Chickasaw Country

The best time to visit Chickasaw Country largely depends on your preferences. Spring and fall are the most temperate and offer pleasant weather for outdoor activities. Summer is the busiest season and offers the most festivals and events. Winter is the least crowded but can be chilly and unpredictable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chickasaw Country

Do:

  • Respect the cultural heritage and traditions of the Chickasaw Nation.
  • Take time to explore the natural wonders and scenic vistas of the region.
  • Sample the local cuisine and savor the flavors of Chickasaw Country.
  • Be open-minded and embrace the unique experiences offered in this vibrant destination.

Don’t:

  • Damage or remove any natural or cultural resources during your stay.
  • Disrespect the beliefs or customs of the Chickasaw Nation or other local communities.
  • Litter or pollute the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chickasaw Country

Chickasaw Country is a relatively safe destination, with low crime rates and a friendly community. However, visitors should take precautions while hiking or engaging in outdoor activities, such as bringing appropriate gear and supplies and staying on designated trails.

Health in Chickasaw Country

The region has access to medical facilities and services, but visitors should bring any necessary prescription medications. It is also recommended to drink plenty of water and wear sunscreen while participating in outdoor activities.

Getting to Chickasaw Country: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chickasaw Country

Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport (DFW) and Will Rogers World Airport (OKC) are two of the nearest international airports to Chickasaw Country, serving domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Chickasaw Country

Greyhound offers bus service to Ardmore, located in the heart of Chickasaw Country.

Other Options

Renting a car or driving your own vehicle is a convenient way to explore Chickasaw Country, with well-maintained highways and roads connecting the major attractions and towns.

How to Get Around in Chickasaw Country

Driving is the most common way of getting around Chickasaw Country. Taxis and ride-sharing apps such as Uber and Lyft are also available in some parts of the region.

Where to Go Next from Chickasaw Country: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Dallas, Texas: Located just a few hours south of Chickasaw Country, Dallas offers a cosmopolitan atmosphere, historic landmarks, and world-class dining and entertainment options.
  • Tulsa, Oklahoma: A vibrant city known for its art deco architecture, cultural festivals, and top-rated museums.
  • Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge: A magnificent natural area with spectacular views of granite peaks, prairies, and wildlife.

Conclusion

Chickasaw Country is a unique and vibrant destination that offers a blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. From hiking trails and scenic vistas to museums and historical landmarks, visitors have plenty of reasons to fall in love with this region. We hope this travel guide has inspired you to start planning your next adventure to Chickasaw Country!
