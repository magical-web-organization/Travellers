Introduction to El Reno

Located in Frontier Country, in Oklahoma, El Reno is a charming city in the Great Plains of the United States of America. El Reno is not only known for its historic landmarks and delicious food, but also for being the birthplace of the famous onion burger. Whether you are a history buff, nature lover, or foodie, El Reno has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in El Reno

If you are planning a trip to El Reno, don’t miss these top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

El Reno is home to several museums and historical places that showcase the city’s rich history. Some of the must-visit attractions include:

  • Fort Reno - Built in 1875, Fort Reno played a significant role in the Indian Wars. Today, visitors can tour the historic site and learn about the fort’s past.
  • Canadian County Historical Museum - Located in a beautiful historic mansion, the museum houses a vast collection of artifacts that celebrates the history of Canadian County.
  • Cherokee Strip Museum - The museum features exhibits that depict the early pioneer days and the Land Run of 1893.

Natural Attractions of El Reno: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

El Reno is also known for its beautiful natural attractions and outdoor activities. Some of the top spots include:

  • Lake El Reno - The lake is a popular spot for fishing, boating, and picnicking. Visitors can also enjoy the scenic views and wildlife that call the lake home.
  • Redlands Community College Arboretum - The arboretum is home to various plant species, walking trails, and a butterfly garden.
  • Lake Overholser - This 1,500-acre lake features fishing, kayaking, and hiking trails, and is a great place to enjoy Oklahoma’s beautiful sunsets.
  • Lucky Star Casino - If you’re feeling lucky, El Reno has a local casino. Lucky Star Casino offers gaming and entertainment options for adults.

Hiking/Biking Routes of El Reno

El Reno offers a great opportunity for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The Lake El Reno Trail is a beautiful 1.5-mile walkway that offers scenic views of the lake and its surroundings. The trail is paved, making it great for both walkers and cyclists.

Best Things to Do in El Reno: Activities and Tours

El Reno offers a variety of activities and tours to make your trip memorable. Here are some of the best things to do when in El Reno:

  • Braum’s Dairy Farm - Take a tour of the Braum’s dairy farm and ice cream production plant. Visitors can see how ice cream and other dairy products are made and sample their delicious ice cream.
  • Onion Festival - If you’re in town in May, don’t miss the El Reno’s Onion Festival. Enjoy live music, onion burgers, and other onion-inspired treats.
  • El Reno Carnegie Library - This celebrated library was the first Carnegie Library in Oklahoma and is listed on the National Register of Historic Places. The library’s architecture is beautiful, and it’s a must-see for history and architecture enthusiasts.

Week-Long Itinerary for El Reno

Day 1-2: Visit Fort Reno, Canadian County Historical Museum, and the El Reno Carnegie Library.

Day 3-4: Spend time outdoors by visiting Lake El Reno, Redlands Community College Arboretum, and Lake Overholser.

Day 5-6: Take a tour of Braum’s Dairy Farm, enjoy the Onion Festival if visiting in May, and go gaming or see an event at Lucky Star Casino.

Food and Drink Scene of El Reno

El Reno may be small, but the city is famous for its delicious food. Make sure you try these local favorites:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Onion Burger - The famous onion burger is a must-try when visiting El Reno. It’s a classic American cheeseburger with a twist of grilled onions inside the patty.
  • Frybread - This Native American bread made of fried dough is a local delicacy and is often served with meat or beans.
  • Chicken Fried Steak - A southern classic, the chicken fried steak served in El Reno is crispy and delicious.

Local Beverages

  • Choc Beer - This chocolate-flavored beer is brewed locally in Oklahoma and is a favorite among locals.
  • Adams Hall Wine - Located in El Reno, Adams Hall Winery offers a variety of wine distilled from locally harvested grapes.

Where to Stay in El Reno: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

El Reno has a variety of accommodation options suitable for different preferences, budgets, and travel styles.

  • Best Western Hensley’s - This budget-friendly hotel is situated near Fort Reno and offers comfortable rooms and amenities.
  • Holiday Inn Express El Reno - This mid-range hotel offers modern rooms, fitness center, and indoor pool for guests.
  • El Reno Motor Inn - Situated near the city center and Lucky Star Casino, El Reno Motor Inn offers clean and straightforward accommodation for travelers on a budget.

What to Buy in El Reno: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for authentic souvenirs or local products to take back home, consider the following options:

  • El Reno Onion Burger T-Shirt - A fun and quirky t-shirt that showcases the famous onion burger.
  • Braum’s Dairy Farm Products - Braum’s Dairy is locally owned and operated and offers dairy products such as milk, ice cream, and cheese, making great gifts.
  • Adams Hall Wine - This local winery offers wine tastings and bottles for purchase, making it an excellent souvenir to take back home.

Local Culture and Customs of El Reno

El Reno is known for its friendly, welcoming locals and a laid-back lifestyle. When visiting, it’s important to respect the local culture and customs, including:

  • Tipping - Tipping is customary in El Reno, especially in restaurants and bars. It is generally expected to tip around 15-20% of the total bill.
  • Friendliness - El Reno locals are known for their friendly and welcoming nature, and it’s always polite to smile and say hello when passing by.

Best Time to Visit El Reno

The best time to visit El Reno is in the summer months, from June to August, when temperatures are mild and it’s the perfect time to enjoy outdoor activities and festivals. However, if you’re looking to avoid the crowds, visiting during the winter months can be pleasant as well.

Do’s and Don’ts of El Reno

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine - As mentioned above, the food is one of the main attractions of El Reno, so make sure to try some of the local dishes when visiting.
  • Take a hike or go biking - El Reno offers lovely walking and biking trails to appreciate the beautiful scenery.
  • Explore the local museums - El Reno is a city rich in history, and its museums are among its best attractions.

Don’t:

  • Forget to tip - As previously mentioned, tipping is customary in El Reno, and it’s essential to show appreciation for good service.
  • Neglect to dress appropriately - When visiting El Reno, it’s essential to dress appropriately for the weather and activities you plan on doing.

Safety and Health

Safety in El Reno

El Reno is considered a relatively safe city, but visitors should always take precautions when traveling. Be aware of pickpockets in busy tourist areas, and keep an eye on your belongings.

Health in El Reno

El Reno has several health facilities, including hospitals and doctor’s offices to offer medical treatment and health checkups. Keep in mind that El Reno is in a remote location, and medical facilities can be limited.

Getting to El Reno: Transportation Options and Logistics

El Reno is a remote location, but several transportation options can make your trip more comfortable.

Airports Near El Reno

The closest airport to El Reno, OK is Oklahoma City Will Rogers World Airport, approximately 30 miles away.

Bus Stations Near El Reno

Greyhound Lines has many bus stations near El Reno, making it an excellent option for those traveling closer to public transport.

Other Options

Driving to El Reno is also a great option and allows you the added flexibility of having your own vehicle while in town. With the price of gas much cheaper than a plane ticket, this option can also be budget-friendly.

How to Get Around in El Reno

Getting around in El Reno is easiest with a car, and many rental car options are available in the city. Taxis and ride-sharing apps are also available, but they can be limited.

Where to Go Next from El Reno: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Oklahoma City - Oklahoma City is only 30 minutes away from El Reno and is home to various museums, nightlife, and foodie scene.
  • Lake Hefner - Just north of Oklahoma City, Lake Hefner offers hiking trails, bike paths, and wildlife viewing opportunities.
  • Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge - This beautiful wildlife refuge is about an hour and a half southwest of El Reno and offers hiking, nature trail walks, and wildlife viewing.

Conclusion

El Reno is a charming, small city with a rich history, friendly locals, and delicious food. Whether you’re interested in hiking, biking, or just enjoying the city’s famed onion burger, El Reno offers something for everyone. With this comprehensive guide, you should be equipped and ready to explore all the best places to visit, eat, stay, and explore in El Reno, Oklahoma.
