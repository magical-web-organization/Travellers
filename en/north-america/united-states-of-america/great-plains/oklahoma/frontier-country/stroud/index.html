Introduction to Stroud

Located in Frontier Country, in the heart of Oklahoma, Stroud is a charming town that boasts a rich history and has a range of exciting attractions and things to do. From scenic hikes through breathtaking natural wonders to exploring local museums and historical landmarks, there’s something for everyone in this vibrant and captivating destination.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore the best things to do and see while visiting Stroud — from culturally significant museums and historical places to outdoor adventures, food and drink scene, and do’s and don’ts for a safe and enjoyable trip.

List of Places to Visit in Stroud

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Stroud Historical Museum

The Stroud Historical Museum is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in learning more about the town’s rich history and culture. The museum features a collection of historical artifacts and photographs, and visitors can learn about notable events, people and places that have helped to shape Stroud into what it is today.

Rock Café

Built in the 1930s, the Rock Café is a historic diner that has been serving up delicious food for almost a century. The café is a popular destination not only for its tasty menu but also its connection to the Pixar movie ‘Cars’, which was inspired the building’s unique architecture.

Natural Attractions of Stroud: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Wild Horse Park

Wild Horse Park is a beautiful urban park that covers over 400 acres of land and is home to a variety of wildlife species, including bison, deer, and wild turkeys. Visitors can enjoy fishing in the park’s ponds, hiking through its scenic trails, or having a picnic under the shade of its trees.

Sacred Heart Cemetery

For hikers and nature lovers, Sacred Heart Cemetery is the perfect destination offering a picturesque and tranquil escape. The cemetery offers a network of trails suitable for hikers of all skill levels. Visitors enjoy the breathtaking views of nature walks lined with century-old oak trees.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Stroud

Route 66 Interpretive Trail

The Route 66 Interpretive Trail is an excellent destination for nature enthusiasts. The trail winds through beautiful scenic areas with breathtaking views of the Oklahoma landscape. One of the highlights is the trail’s connection to the historic US Route 66, a 2,451-mile historic highway that stretches from Chicago, Illinois to Santa Monica, California.

Stroud Lake Trail

Stroud Lake Trail is a 1.5-mile hiking trail that surrounds the beautiful Stroud Lake, which is a popular destination for fishing enthusiasts. The trail is suitable for hikers of all skill levels, and visitors can enjoy incredible views of the surrounding nature.

Best Things to Do in Stroud: Activities and Tours

Stroud Antiques District

Stroud Antiques District is home to a vast array of antique shops, making it the perfect destination for antique collectors. Visitors can explore the district’s various antique shops and galleries and discover unique and rare finds.

Rock Creek Ranch Resort

The Rock Creek Ranch Resort is an excellent destination for family vacations. The resort offers a range of activities and amenities, including an outdoor pool, basketball court, and mini-golf, making it the perfect destination for fun and relaxation.

Week-Long Itinerary for Stroud

Day 1: Visit the Stroud Historical Museum and the Rock Café to learn more about the town’s history and enjoy a delicious meal.

Day 2: Explore the Wild Horse Park and enjoy a picnic in the serene surroundings.

Day 3: Hike around Sacred Heart Cemetery, taking in the views of the century-old oak trees and tranquil surroundings.

Day 4: Enjoy a scenic drive along the historic Route 66 Interpetive Trail with lunch at a roadside diner.

Day 5: Take a guided tour of the Stroud Antiques District and discover rare finds.

Day 6: Relax and unwind at the Rock Creek Ranch Resort.

Day 7: Soak in the 4th of July Fireworks show in Stroud.

Food and Drink Scene of Stroud

Must-Try Dishes

Chicken Fried Steak

A true southern delicacy, chicken-fried steak is a must-try dish in Stroud. It’s usually served with mashed potatoes and gravy and is a hearty meal that will keep you going for hours.

Pecan Pie

End your meal at the Rock Café with a slice of their famous pecan pie, a sweet and nutty dessert that’s perfect with a cup of coffee.

Local Beverages

Oklahoma Wine

Stroud is home to various wineries that are known for their distinctive blend of locally-grown grapes. Visit Nuyaka Creek Wines, Whispering Meadows Vineyard, or Bear Creek Wines to sample some of the area’s best local wines.

Margaritas

No visit to Stroud is complete without enjoying a cold, refreshing margarita. Visit Los Arcos Mexican Restaurant, which serves up some of the best margaritas in town.

Where to Stay in Stroud: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Stroud Motor Lodge

The Stroud Motor Lodge is a family-friendly hotel that offers comfortable and affordable accommodations. The hotel offers a range of amenities, including an outdoor pool, free WiFi, and complimentary breakfast.

Budget-friendly Accommodations

If you are looking for budget-friendly accommodation, then you might want to consider the Budget Inn Stroud, which offers clean and comfortable lodging at a reasonable price.

What to Buy in Stroud: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Rock Café Souvenirs

The Rock Café gift shop offers an excellent selection of souvenirs, including t-shirts, hats, mugs, and other unique items that represent the town’s culture and history.

Nuyaka Creek Wines

Bring home a bottle of Nuyaka Creek’s locally made wine. Their wines are made from locally grown grapes and are incredibly delicious.

Local Culture and Customs of Stroud

The town of Stroud takes immense pride in its rich cultural heritage, and its residents do their best to preserve it. Visitors should expect to be welcomed with warm hospitality, and it’s customary to reciprocate with a friendly greeting. Respectful behavior is much appreciated in Stroud, so be sure to be mindful of the local customs while visiting.

Best Time to Visit Stroud

The best time to visit Stroud is during the spring and the fall months as the weather is mild, and the outdoor activities are more enjoyable during these times.

Do’s and Don’ts of Stroud

Do:

  • Do visit the quaint souvenir stores and museums
  • Do explore the natural beauty of the town
  • Do try the local cuisine
  • Do greet the friendly locals

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to carry sunscreen lotion if you are traveling during the summer months
  • Don’t litter in public areas
  • Don’t be rude to the locals

Safety and Health

Safety in Stroud

Stroud is considered to be a safe town with relatively low crime rates. Visitors should practice common sense while traveling, such as keeping valuables in a safe place and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Stroud

Stroud has a well-equipped medical center and a range of specialist doctors if needed.

Getting to Stroud: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Stroud

Tulsa International Airport is the closest airport to Stroud, which is located approximately 50 miles to the northeast of Stroud.

Bus Stations Near Stroud

The Jefferson Lines and Greyhound bus services offer connections to Stroud, departing from Tulsa and connecting through Oklahoma City.

Other Options

Visitors that arrive in Oklahoma City can rent a car and drive to Stroud in approximately an hour.

How to Get Around in Stroud

Visitors to Stroud can rent a car or use a taxi or ride-sharing service to get around town. Bicycles are also available for rent, and visitors can explore the town at their leisure.

Where to Go Next from Stroud: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Oklahoma City

Oklahoma City is an excellent destination for visitors to explore and experience the state’s rich history and cultural heritage. The city is home to several tourist destinations, including the Oklahoma City National Memorial & Museum, the Myriad Botanical Gardens, and the Oklahoma City Museum of Art.

Tulsa

Tulsa is an ideal destination for visitors interested in the arts, music, and cultural attractions. The city is home to various museums, galleries, and music halls, including the Philbrook Museum of Art, the Woody Guthrie Center, and the BOK Center.

Conclusion

Stroud is a charming town that’s full of surprises and offers a range of exciting attractions and activities for visitors to enjoy. From its rich history and cultural heritage to the scenic landscapes and mouth-watering cuisine, Stroud is the perfect destination for an adventurous and unforgettable holiday.
