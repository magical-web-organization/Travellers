Introduction to Midwest City

Midwest City is a charming urban hub located in the heart of Greater Oklahoma City, on the eastern edge of the Great Plains region in the United States of America. Established in 1941, Midwest City is known for its aircraft industry, proud military history, and vibrant cultural scene. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Midwest City.

List of Places to Visit in Midwest City

If you’re planning a trip to Midwest City, don’t miss out on these top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tinker Air Force Base: One of the largest military bases in the world, with an impressive collection of aircraft and military memorabilia.
  • Oklahoma City Museum of Art: Featuring a world-class collection of contemporary and historical art, as well as special exhibitions and events.
  • Oklahoma History Center: Discover Oklahoma’s rich history and culture through interactive exhibits, artifacts, and documents.
  • National Cowboy & Western Heritage Museum: Located just a short drive from Midwest City, this museum features an extensive collection of Western art, Native American artifacts, and cowboy memorabilia.

Natural Attractions of Midwest City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Stanley Draper: A popular destination for fishing, boating, and outdoor recreation.
  • Trosper Park: This scenic park offers hiking trails, playgrounds, and picnic areas.
  • Midwest City Dog Park: A fun and safe off-leash dog park for furry friends and their owners.
  • Lake Thunderbird State Park: Just a short drive from Midwest City, this expansive park features hiking trails, campgrounds, and lake activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Midwest City

Explore the great outdoors with these hiking and biking routes in and around Midwest City:

  • Midwest City Gibson Park Trail: A 1.6-mile loop trail that’s good for all skill levels and features scenic views.
  • Lake Hefner Trail: A 9.1-mile trail that loops around Lake Hefner and offers stunning views of the lake and city skyline.
  • Oklahoma City River Trail: A 9.5-mile trail that follows the Oklahoma River and connects to several parks and attractions.
  • Oklahoma River Trails: A network of paved and natural trails that run along the Oklahoma River, showcasing scenic landscapes and wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Midwest City: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re into history, sports, or adventure, there’s something for everyone in Midwest City. Check out these fun activities and tours:

  • Midwest City Farmer’s Market: A weekly market that offers fresh produce, handmade crafts, and live music.
  • Midwest City Sports Complex: A multi-purpose sports complex that hosts recreational and competitive leagues.
  • iFLY Indoor Skydiving: Experience the thrill of skydiving without jumping from an airplane at this indoor skydiving facility.
  • Riversport Adventure Park: A premier destination for white water rafting, zip lining, and other outdoor adventures.

Week-Long Itinerary for Midwest City

If you have a week to spend in Midwest City, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Midwest City

Midwest City’s food and drink scene features a diverse range of local and international flavors. Don’t miss out on these must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken Fried Steak: A favorite Midwest comfort food, chicken fried steak is a breaded and fried beef dish served with mashed potatoes and gravy.
  • Fried Catfish: Freshly caught catfish is battered and fried to crispy perfection, and served with hushpuppies and coleslaw.
  • Bison Burgers: A lean and flavorful alternative to beef burgers, bison burgers are a popular menu item at local restaurants.
  • BBQ: Midwest City is home to some of the best BBQ in Oklahoma, featuring slow-smoked meat, savory sauces, and sides like baked beans and cornbread.

Local Beverages

  • Prairie Wolf Vodka: A popular local distillery that produces award-winning spirits, including vodka, gin, and whiskey.
  • Roughtail Brewing Co.: This local craft brewery offers a wide range of beers, from hoppy IPAs to rich stouts and porters.
  • Iron Monk Brewing Co.: A local brewery that combines traditional brewing methods with cutting-edge technology to create unique and flavorful beers.
  • Oklahoma-made wines: Sample local wines made from Oklahoma-grown grapes at wineries like Canadian River Vineyards and Winery and StableRidge Vineyards.

Where to Stay in Midwest City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Midwest City offers a range of accommodations to suit every budget and travel style. Here are some options to consider:

  • La Quinta Inn & Suites by Wyndham Midwest City/OKC: A budget-friendly hotel located near Tinker Air Force Base and other attractions.
  • Sheraton Midwest City Hotel at the Reed Conference Center: A modern and stylish hotel with easy access to downtown Oklahoma City.

What to Buy in Midwest City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a unique souvenir or gift to bring back from Midwest City? Check out these local shops and products:

  • Route 66 Marketplace: A gift shop that offers a wide selection of nostalgic Route 66-themed souvenirs, as well as local products from Oklahoma.
  • Okie Crowe: A handmade soap and skincare company that uses natural, locally sourced ingredients.
  • Green Acres Market: A local health food store that offers a wide selection of organic and natural products, including local honey and produce.
  • Midwest City Farmer’s Market: A great place to pick up fresh and locally grown produce, handmade crafts, and other unique souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Midwest City

Midwest City residents are friendly, welcoming, and proud of their community and heritage. Here are some local customs and traditions to keep in mind:

  • Respect for military: Given the city’s close ties to the military, it’s important to show respect for veterans and active duty personnel.
  • Hospitality: Southern hospitality is alive and well in Midwest City, and visitors can expect to be warmly welcomed wherever they go.
  • Sports culture: Midwest City is home to a strong sports culture, with a particular love for football and basketball.
  • Festivals and events: Throughout the year, Midwest City hosts a variety of community events and festivals, including the Fourth of July Celebration and Holiday Lights Spectacular.

Best Time to Visit Midwest City

Midwest City can be enjoyed year-round, with each season offering its own unique activities and attractions. The best time to visit depends on your interests and preferences:

  • Spring (March - May): Ideal for hiking, biking, and enjoying the outdoors.
  • Summer (June - August): Perfect for lake activities, festivals, and outdoor events.
  • Fall (September - November): The best time to experience fall foliage, seasonal foods, and cultural events.
  • Winter (December - February): Enjoy holiday lights, indoor activities, and cozying up with warm drinks.

Do’s and Don’ts of Midwest City

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Midwest City:

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, including BBQ, bison burgers, and chicken fried steak.
  • Do explore the city’s outdoor attractions, including Lake Stanley Draper and Oklahoma River Trials.
  • Do show respect for the military and veterans.
  • Do attend local festivals and events, such as the Fourth of July Celebration and Holiday Lights Spectacular.

Don’t:

  • Don’t underestimate the Oklahoma weather - bring appropriate clothing for all seasons.
  • Don’t litter - Midwest City residents take pride in their clean and beautiful community.
  • Don’t ignore local customs and traditions - embrace the friendly and hospitable culture of the region.
  • Don’t miss out on local breweries, wineries, and distilleries - Oklahoma offers a range of unique and flavorful beverages.

Safety and Health

Midwest City is a safe and healthy travel destination, but it’s always important to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings. Here are some tips for staying safe and healthy while visiting:

Safety in Midwest City

  • Use caution when driving, particularly during severe weather.
  • Be aware of your surroundings, and avoid walking alone at night.
  • Keep valuables locked in your car or hotel room.
  • Follow all safety guidelines at outdoor attractions and activities.

Health in Midwest City

  • Stay hydrated, particularly during hot summer months.
  • Practice good hand hygiene, and carry hand sanitizer.
  • Wear sunscreen and bug spray when spending time outdoors.
  • Be prepared for allergies and seasonal illnesses, and bring any necessary medications.

Getting to Midwest City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Midwest City is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Midwest City

  • Will Rogers World Airport: Located about 20 minutes from Midwest City, this airport offers domestic and international flights.
  • Tulsa International Airport: Located about 1.5 hours from Midwest City, this airport offers domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Midwest City

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in downtown Oklahoma City, about 15 minutes from Midwest City.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Rent a car at one of the airports or in downtown Oklahoma City.
  • Shuttle service: Several shuttle services and private car services are available in the area.

How to Get Around in Midwest City

While a car is the most convenient way to get around Midwest City, there are also several other transportation options available:

  • Public transit: The EMBARK system serves Midwest City and Oklahoma City, with bus routes and a downtown streetcar.
  • Bike share: Oklahoma City has a bike share program called Spokies, with rental stations throughout the city.
  • Ride share: Uber and Lyft are both available in Midwest City and the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Midwest City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Midwest City is a great starting point for exploring other destinations in Oklahoma and beyond. Here are a few nearby options:

  • Oklahoma City: Just a short drive from Midwest City, Oklahoma City offers a wealth of attractions and activities, including Bricktown, the Oklahoma City Zoo, and the National Cowboy & Western Heritage Museum.
  • Tulsa: About 1.5 hours from Midwest City, Tulsa is a vibrant city that’s home to the Philbrook Museum of Art, Tulsa Zoo, and Route 66 landmarks.
  • Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge: Located about 2 hours from Midwest City, this stunning wildlife refuge features scenic landscapes, hiking trails, and wildlife viewing opportunities.

Conclusion

Midwest City is a hidden gem in the heart of Oklahoma, offering a unique blend of history, culture, and outdoor recreation. Whether you’re visiting for a weekend or a week, there’s plenty to see and do in the area. With this comprehensive tour guide, you’re sure to make the most of your time in Midwest City and the surrounding region.
