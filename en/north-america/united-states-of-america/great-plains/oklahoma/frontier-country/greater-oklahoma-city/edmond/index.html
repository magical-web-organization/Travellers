Introduction to Edmond

Nestled in the Frontier Country region of Oklahoma City, Edmond is a vibrant city with a rich history and stunning natural beauty. Home to a number of cultural attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures, Edmond makes for an ideal destination for travelers seeking to connect with nature and soak in the local culture. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you on a journey to explore all that Edmond has to offer, including the best places to visit, hiking routes, food and drink scene, where to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Edmond

Whether you’re interested in exploring the history of the city or taking in its natural wonders, there’s something for everyone in Edmond. Here are some of the top places to visit during your time here:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Edmond Historical Museum: This museum offers a glimpse into the city’s rich past, with exhibits highlighting the early pioneers, businesses, and cultural evolution of Edmond.

  2. Armstrong Auditorium: This stunning theatre is home to the Armstrong International Cultural Foundation and hosts performances ranging from classical music to ballet to jazz.

  3. Kickingbird Golf Club: Home to a pristine golf course, this club is perfect for golf enthusiasts and novices alike.

Natural Attractions of Edmond: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Mitch Park: This expansive park is a popular spot for outdoor activities and features amenities such as playgrounds, sports fields, and walking trails.

  2. Arcadia Lake: This picturesque lake is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts, offering opportunities for boating, camping, and fishing.

  3. Martin Park Nature Center: This stunning nature center is a must-visit destination for those who love the great outdoors, offering scenic trails, wildlife viewing, and educational programs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Edmond

Edmond boasts a number of scenic hiking and biking trails perfect for nature enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  1. Lake Trail at Arcadia Lake: A 3.5-mile trail that meanders alongside the shimmering waters of Arcadia Lake.

  2. Spring Creek Trail: This 5-mile trail is perfect for both hiking and biking, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape as it winds through the heart of Edmond.

  3. Hafer Park Trail: This 1.4-mile trail is an excellent option for those looking for a shorter trek, with options for both walking and biking.

Best Things to Do in Edmond: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and exploring the local cultural attractions, there are a number of other activities and tours to check out in Edmond.

  1. Visit the Farmer’s Market: Held regularly at the Festival Market Place, the farmer’s market is a great place to sample the local cuisine and take in the vibrant atmosphere.

  2. Go Horseback Riding: Edmond is home to a number of horseback riding facilities, offering visitors the opportunity to explore the local landscape on horseback.

  3. Take a Winery Tour: A visit to one of the local wineries offers a unique glimpse into the art of winemaking, with tours that include tastings and insights into the local industry.

Week-Long Itinerary for Edmond

If you’re planning to spend a week in Edmond, here’s a suggested itinerary to help you plan your trip:

Day Activity
1 Explore the Edmond Historical Museum and Armstrong Auditorium
2 Hike the Lake Trail at Arcadia Lake and visit Mitch Park
3 Take a horseback riding tour and sample the local cuisine at the farmer’s market
4 Visit Martin Park Nature Center and take the Spring Creek Trail
5 Relax and enjoy a round of golf at Kickingbird Golf Club
6 Take a winery tour and enjoy a glass of local wine
7 Visit the historic downtown area and enjoy shopping and dining at local establishments

Food and Drink Scene of Edmond

Edmond has a rich food and drink scene, with a wide variety of local dishes and craft beverages to try. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Chicken Fried Steak: A Southern classic, this dish features a breaded and fried steak served with gravy and a side of mashed potatoes.

  2. Barbecue Ribs: Edmond is home to a number of barbecue joints, with fall-off-the-bone ribs being a local favorite.

  3. Fried Catfish: Another Southern classic, fried catfish is a staple of Oklahoma cuisine, with a crispy exterior and tender, flaky fish inside.

Local Beverages

  1. Oklahoma Craft Beer: Edmond is home to a number of local breweries, offering visitors the chance to sample unique craft beers inspired by the local landscape.

  2. Oklahoma Wine: With a climate perfect for grapes, Oklahoma is home to a burgeoning wine industry, with local wineries showcasing the rich flavors of the region.

Where to Stay in Edmond: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxurious hotel or a cozy guesthouse, Edmond has a range of options to suit all budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay in town:

  1. Fairfield Inn and Suites: Located in the heart of Edmond, this charming hotel offers comfortable accommodations and easy access to the downtown area.

  2. The Grandison at Maney Park: This stunning bed and breakfast offers an elegant and luxurious experience, with beautifully appointed rooms and top-notch amenities.

  3. Hostelling International Oklahoma City: For travelers on a budget, this hostel offers clean and comfortable dorm rooms and private rooms at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Edmond: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For those looking to bring home a piece of the local culture, there are a number of souvenirs and local products to choose from. Here are some ideas:

  1. Local Wines: Bring home a bottle of wine from one of the local wineries to enjoy and share with friends and family.

  2. Artisanal Foods: Edmond is home to a number of artisanal food producers, offering unique and flavorful products such as preserves, baked goods, and candies.

Local Culture and Customs of Edmond

Edmond has a rich cultural heritage, with a history shaped by Native American history and the westward expansion of the United States. Visitors can explore this heritage through the local museums and historical landmarks, as well as by experiencing the warmth and hospitality of the local community.

Best Time to Visit Edmond

The best time to visit Edmond is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the natural beauty of the region is on full display. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters are relatively mild with occasional snowfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Edmond

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Edmond:

Do:

  • Do take the time to explore the natural beauty of the region, including the many parks and hiking trails.

  • Do sample the local cuisine and craft beverages.

  • Do engage with the local community and learn about the rich history and culture of the region.

Don’t:

  • Don’t underestimate the importance of sun protection during the summer months.

  • Don’t litter or leave any trace in the natural areas you visit.

  • Don’t forget to bring comfortable shoes for all the walking and hiking opportunities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Edmond

Edmond is a safe city, with low crime rates and a welcoming community. Visitors should exercise common sense and take basic precautions, such as securing valuables and not leaving personal items unattended.

Health in Edmond

Edmond has a variety of healthcare facilities available to visitors. Visitors should ensure they have adequate travel insurance before traveling, and should take any necessary precautions based on their personal health needs.

Getting to Edmond: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Edmond

The nearest airport to Edmond is Will Rogers World Airport, which is located approximately 25 miles to the south in Oklahoma City.

Bus Stations Near Edmond

Greyhound Bus Lines operates a station in Oklahoma City, located approximately 15 miles south of Edmond.

Other Options

Visitors to Edmond may choose to rent a car, use ride-sharing services such as Uber or Lyft, or take a taxi to get around.

How to Get Around in Edmond

Edmond is a relatively small city, and many of the attractions and amenities are located within easy walking or biking distance. Visitors may also choose to rent a car, use ride-sharing services, or take a taxi.

Where to Go Next from Edmond: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’ve finished exploring Edmond and are looking for nearby destinations to explore, there are a number of options within easy driving distance. Here are a few suggestions:

  1. Oklahoma City: Just a short drive south of Edmond, Oklahoma City is home to a variety of cultural attractions and outdoor adventures.

  2. Tulsa: Located to the northeast of Edmond, Tulsa is a vibrant and dynamic city with a rich history and unique culture.

  3. Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge: Located to the west of Edmond, this stunning wildlife preserve offers opportunities for hiking, camping, and wildlife viewing.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in exploring the local culture or immersing yourself in the natural beauty of the region, Edmond offers something for travelers of all interests. From hiking and biking to exploring the local museums and artisans, there are endless opportunities to discover this vibrant and welcoming city.
