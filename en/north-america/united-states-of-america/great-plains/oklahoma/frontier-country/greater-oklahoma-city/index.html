Introduction to Greater Oklahoma City

Welcome to Greater Oklahoma City, a vibrant and lively city located in the heart of Frontier Country, Oklahoma. The city offers a unique mix of culture, history, and natural beauty, making it an ideal destination for travelers interested in exploration, adventure, and discovery.

Boasting a diverse array of attractions, experience the very best of Greater Oklahoma City, from exploring its many museums and historical sites to hiking along scenic trails. This comprehensive travel guide will provide you with everything you need to plan your trip to this remarkable city.

Week-Long Itinerary for Greater Oklahoma City

Day 1-2: Explore downtown Oklahoma City attractions such as the Oklahoma City Museum of Art, the Oklahoma City National Memorial & Museum, and the Myriad Botanical Gardens.

Day 3: Hike at Martin Park Nature Center or take a bike ride on the Bluff Creek Trail, followed by an evening out in the Bricktown Entertainment District.

Day 4: Take a trip to the Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge and take in the stunning views from Mount Scott.

Day 5: Visit the National Cowboy and Western Heritage Museum, followed by an evening at the Chesapeake Energy Arena to watch the Thunder basketball team play.

Day 6: Take a day trip to the Lake Texoma area for boating, swimming and fishing.

Day 7: Visit the Science Museum Oklahoma and enjoy the interactive exhibits before heading to Frontier City theme park in the afternoon.

Local Culture and Customs of Greater Oklahoma City

Greater Oklahoma City is known for its friendly locals, cowboy culture, and love of sports. Visitors are encouraged to embrace the city’s laid-back vibe, and to show respect for Native American traditions and heritage.

Best Time to Visit Greater Oklahoma City

The best time to visit Greater Oklahoma City is during the spring (March to May) and fall (September to November) when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter can bring cold temperatures and snow and ice storms.

Do’s and Don’ts of Greater Oklahoma City

Do:

  • Visit the Oklahoma City National Memorial & Museum to pay respects to those lost in the Oklahoma City bombing.
  • Take in a game at the Chesapeake Energy Arena.
  • Hike or bike the many trails in the area, including the Bluff Creek Trail and Lake Hefner Trail.

Don’t:

  • Don’t underestimate the weather - be prepared for hot summers and cold winters.
  • Don’t miss out on the opportunity to try some local dishes, such as chicken fried steak or bison burgers.

Safety and Health

Safety in Greater Oklahoma City

Greater Oklahoma City is a relatively safe destination, but visitors should take normal precautions to keep themselves and their belongings safe. Downtown areas can be busy and should be avoided late at night.

Health in Greater Oklahoma City

The healthcare system in Greater Oklahoma City is good, with several hospitals and clinics available to visitors.

How to Get Around in Greater Oklahoma City

Getting around Greater Oklahoma City is easy with several transportation options, including the Oklahoma City Streetcar, the EMBARK bus service, and bike-sharing programs.
