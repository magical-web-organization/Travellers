Introduction to Purcell

Located in the heart of Oklahoma’s Frontier Country, Purcell is a charming small town well-known for its rich history, natural beauty, and warm hospitality. Its proximity to Oklahoma City makes it a popular destination for day-trippers and vacationers alike.

List of Places to Visit in Purcell

There are numerous attractions in Purcell catering to all types of travelers. Here are some of the highlights:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • McClain County Historical and Genealogical Society Museum: This museum displays the region’s cultural heritage, from Native American artifacts to Civil War-era memorabilia and beyond.

  • The Santa Fe Depot Museum: This restored 1908 depot houses a fascinating collection of photographs, documents, and train memorabilia.

Natural Attractions of Purcell: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Purcell Lake: This picturesque lake offers fishing, boating, and hiking opportunities, as well as stunning views.

  • Ardmore Arboretum: This beautiful park is home to a variety of native fauna, including deer, coyotes, and wildflowers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Purcell

Purcell is known for its scenic hiking and biking trails, including:

  • Purcell Lake Trail: A flat, 2-mile trail that winds around the lake and offers beautiful views of the water.

  • Lake Thunderbird State Park Trails: Located 10 miles from Purcell, these trails offer stunning views of Lake Thunderbird and the surrounding forests.

Best Things to Do in Purcell: Activities and Tours

  • Purcell Art Walk: Take a leisurely stroll through downtown Purcell and explore the local art galleries and studios.

  • Scenic Train Rides: Hop aboard the Heartland Flyer train and travel through the stunning Oklahoma countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Purcell

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Purcell:

Day Activity
1 Visit the McClain County Historical and Genealogical Society Museum and the Santa Fe Depot Museum
2 Hike the Purcell Lake Trail and enjoy a picnic by the lake
3 Take a scenic train ride on the Heartland Flyer
4 Explore the Ardmore Arboretum and enjoy an afternoon of bird-watching
5 Visit Lake Thunderbird State Park and go fishing or boating
6 Join the Purcell Art Walk and meet local artists
7 Relax and enjoy a day at a local spa

Food and Drink Scene of Purcell

Purcell has a surprisingly diverse culinary scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Smokestack BBQ: This popular eatery is known for its delicious smoked brisket and pulled pork.

  • Cupcakes to Go-Go: This charming bakery serves up cupcakes, cookies, and other sweet treats.

Local Beverages

  • COOP Ale Works: Located in nearby Oklahoma City, this award-winning craft brewery offers beer tastings and tours.

Where to Stay in Purcell: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Purcell has a variety of lodging options, including:

  • Best Western Plus: This modern hotel offers comfortable rooms and amenities such as a fitness center and an indoor pool.

  • Purcell Inn: This budget-friendly motel offers clean and cozy rooms as well as complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Purcell: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Thunderbird Supply Co.: This store sells a variety of locally-made artisanal products, from pottery to jewelry.

  • The Pink Door Boutique: This trendy clothing store offers stylish pieces made by local designers.

Local Culture and Customs of Purcell

Purcell is a friendly and welcoming town with a relaxed atmosphere. Visitors are expected to be respectful of local customs, such as saying “hello” and “thank you” to locals and observing local laws and regulations.

Best Time to Visit Purcell

The best time to visit Purcell is during the spring and fall when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Purcell

Do:

  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

  • Try new foods and drinks in local restaurants and bars.

Don’t:

  • Be rude or disrespectful to locals.

  • Ignore local laws and regulations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Purcell

Purcell is a safe and friendly town, but visitors should always be aware of their surroundings and take normal precautions, such as locking cars and securing valuables. In case of an emergency, call 911.

Health in Purcell

Visitors should have all necessary vaccinations up to date and carry adequate health insurance. It’s important to stay hydrated and protected from the sun during hot weather.

Getting to Purcell: Transportation Options and Logistics

Purcell is easily accessible by plane, car, or bus. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Purcell

  • Will Rogers World Airport: Located in Oklahoma City, this airport is the nearest major airport to Purcell.

  • Tulsa International Airport: Located about 90 miles from Purcell, Tulsa International Airport is another option for travelers.

Bus Stations Near Purcell

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in Oklahoma City, this bus station offers connections to various destinations in the region.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Renting a car is a convenient way to explore Purcell and the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Purcell

The best way to get around Purcell is by car or bike. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Purcell: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Purcell is a great base for exploring nearby attractions, such as:

  • Oklahoma City: Just a short drive away, Oklahoma City offers a variety of cultural and entertainment options.

  • Chickasaw National Recreation Area: This beautiful park features stunning natural landscapes and a variety of outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Purcell is a hidden gem in the Oklahoma countryside, offering visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and warm hospitality. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or simply a relaxing getaway, Purcell has something for everyone.
