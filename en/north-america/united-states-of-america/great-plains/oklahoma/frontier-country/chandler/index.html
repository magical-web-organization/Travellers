Introduction to Chandler

Chandler, located in the heart of Oklahoma, is a small town with a rich history and scenic landscapes. Known for its beautiful lake, friendly locals, and a range of outdoor activities, a trip to Chandler promises to be a fun-filled and adventurous experience for all types of travelers.

In this guide, we’ll take you through the must-see sights and sounds of Chandler and help you plan your itinerary for an unforgettable trip.

List of Places to Visit in Chandler

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Chandler is home to a number of culturally significant attractions - from museums to historical sites. A few must-visit places include:

  • Lincoln County Museum of Pioneer History: This museum showcases the history of the pioneer community in Lincoln County and features exhibits, objects, and artifacts that reflect the history of the area.

  • Route 66 Interpretive Center: A museum that explores the history of the historic Route 66, which passes through Chandler.

  • Chandler Round Barn: The only round barn in Oklahoma and one of only a few in the United States, the Chandler Round Barn is a National Historic Landmark and a popular attraction for tourists.

Natural Attractions of Chandler: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those who love the great outdoors, Chandler has plenty of natural attractions to explore. These include:

  • Bell Cow Lake: With over 1300 acres of water and surrounded by 2300 acres of parkland, Bell Cow Lake is the perfect destination for hiking, camping, fishing, and boating.

  • Chandler Park: With nearly 100 acres of park space, Chandler Park offers plenty of opportunities for hiking, picnicking, and enjoying the scenic views.

  • Tandem Bike Tours: For those looking for a unique outdoor experience, take a tandem bike tour of Chandler and explore the town in a fun and memorable way.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chandler

Chandler offers some of the best hiking and biking trails in the area. Some popular routes include:

  • Pawnee Bill Hiking Trail: This scenic trail is perfect for hiking enthusiasts and offers stunning views of the Chandler landscape.

  • Heartland of America Bike Route: The Heartland of America Bike Route passes through Chandler and offers cyclists a scenic and challenging ride through the area.

  • Chandler Trail System: This system of interconnected trails is perfect for hikers and bikers of all skill levels and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Chandler: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Chandler offers a range of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. A few must-try experiences include:

  • Chandler Adventure Tour: This guided tour takes visitors through the town and offers a range of fun and exciting activities, including rock climbing, zip-lining, and more.

  • Kickingbird Cinema: Catch a movie at Kickingbird Cinema, a local favorite and a great way to unwind after a day of exploring.

  • Chandler Rodeo: Experience the thrill of the rodeo first-hand and see some of the best rodeo performers in action at the annual Chandler Rodeo.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chandler

For those planning a week-long trip to Chandler, here’s a sample itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the Lincoln County Museum of Pioneer History and the Chandler Round Barn
  • Day 2: Hike the Pawnee Bill Hiking Trail and explore Chandler Park
  • Day 3: Take a tandem bike tour of Chandler and enjoy the scenic views
  • Day 4: Go fishing and boating at Bell Cow Lake
  • Day 5: Experience the Chandler Adventure Tour and enjoy some rock climbing and zip-lining
  • Day 6: Explore the Route 66 Interpretive Center and catch a movie at Kickingbird Cinema
  • Day 7: Attend the annual Chandler Rodeo and experience the thrill of the rodeo first-hand

Food and Drink Scene of Chandler

No trip to Chandler is complete without enjoying some of the local food and drink. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken fried steak: A local favorite, try this southern classic dish at one of Chandler’s many restaurants.

  • Fried catfish: Freshly caught and fried to perfection, fried catfish is a must-try dish for seafood lovers.

Local Beverages

  • Route 66 Root Beer: Made from pure cane sugar and carbonated water, this local root beer is a refreshing drink for all ages.

  • Cimarron Red: A local beer brewed in Oklahoma, Cimarron Red is a favorite among beer enthusiasts.

Where to Stay in Chandler: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chandler offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. A few popular places to stay include:

  • Hampton Inn & Suites: A comfortable and luxurious hotel that offers all the amenities for a relaxing stay.

  • Nights Inn: A budget-friendly option that offers clean and comfortable rooms for a great price.

  • Chandler Lake RV Park: For those traveling with an RV, this RV park offers stunning views of Bell Cow Lake and a range of amenities for a comfortable stay.

What to Buy in Chandler: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Make sure to stop by some local shops and vendors to pick up some souvenirs and local products to bring home. A few popular items include:

  • Handmade pottery and ceramics: Many local artists offer handmade pottery and ceramics that make great souvenirs and gifts.

  • Route 66 memorabilia: Chandler is known for its Route 66 connection, so make sure to stop by a local shop to pick up some Route 66 memorabilia.

Local Culture and Customs of Chandler

Chandler is known for its friendly locals and tight-knit community. Visitors are often welcomed with open arms and encouraged to explore the town and its attractions.

Best Time to Visit Chandler

The best time to visit Chandler is in the spring or fall when the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter can be chilly and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chandler

Do:

  • Do ask the locals for recommendations on where to eat, shop and explore.

  • Do respect the local culture and customs and be mindful of the environment.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage any natural attractions or public property.

  • Don’t be disrespectful towards the locals or their culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chandler

Chandler is a safe town with a low crime rate. However, visitors should be mindful of their surroundings and avoid leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Chandler

Chandler has several medical facilities and pharmacies to cater to any medical needs. Visitors should make sure to have their medical insurance in place before traveling.

Getting to Chandler: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chandler

The nearest airports to Chandler are Will Rogers World Airport in Oklahoma City and Tulsa International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Chandler

Greyhound Bus Lines operates a station in Shawnee, which is a 20-minute drive from Chandler.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Chandler from Oklahoma City or Tulsa.

How to Get Around in Chandler

Getting around in Chandler is easy using a car or a bike. Many attractions are within walking distance, and there are several bike rental shops in town.

Where to Go Next from Chandler: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursions to explore after visiting Chandler, including:

  • Oklahoma City: The biggest city in Oklahoma and home to a range of attractions, including the National Cowboy & Western Heritage Museum and the Oklahoma City Zoo.

  • Tulsa: A vibrant city with a rich arts scene and plenty of attractions, such as the Philbrook Museum of Art and the Tulsa Zoo.

Conclusion

Chandler is a town with a rich history, natural beauty and friendly locals. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences or simply some rest and relaxation, a trip to Chandler promises to be a memorable and enjoyable one.
