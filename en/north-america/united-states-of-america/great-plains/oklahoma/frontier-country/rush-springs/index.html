Introduction to Rush Springs

Nestled in the heart of Frontier Country in Oklahoma, Rush Springs is a small town that packs a big punch. This charming community is known for its friendly locals, rich culture, and stunning natural beauty. Rush Springs is home to a variety of local attractions and activities that cater to a diverse range of interests. From hiking and biking through scenic trails to exploring the town’s rich history, there is never a dull moment in Rush Springs.

In this comprehensive travel guide to Rush Springs, we will be highlighting the best local attractions, food and drink scene, lodging options, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Rush Springs

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Rush Springs Historical Museum: This museum is home to a vast collection of artifacts and exhibits that showcase the town’s rich history from prehistoric times to modern-day.
  • Medicine Park: This historic resort town features picturesque buildings and unique shops that are sure to charm any visitor.
  • Fort Sill National Historic Landmark & Museum: Established in 1869, this historic fort is home to a museum that showcases the area’s military history.
  • Chickasaw Cultural Center: This center is dedicated to preserving and celebrating the Chickasaw Nation’s history and culture.

Natural Attractions of Rush Springs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Chickasaw National Recreation Area: This breathtaking park features multiple natural springs, hiking trails, swimming holes, and stunning scenery.
  • Lake Murray State Park: This park is home to the state’s largest lake and provides opportunities for boating, fishing, and hiking.
  • Turner Falls Park: This scenic park is home to the state’s largest waterfall and offers swimming, hiking, and camping opportunities.
  • Cross Timbers Hiking Trail: This scenic trail runs through the beautiful Arbuckle Mountains and offers breathtaking views of the landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rush Springs

  • Chickasaw National Recreation Area Trails: This park features over 30 miles of multi-use trails that are perfect for hiking, cycling, and horseback riding.
  • Cross Timbers Hiking Trail: This trail stretches for 14 miles through the Arbuckle Mountains and offers stunning views of the surrounding scenery.
  • Lake Murray State Park Trails: This park features over 12 miles of trails that are perfect for hiking and cycling.

Best Things to Do in Rush Springs: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Rush Springs Watermelon Festival: This annual festival is held in August and features a parade, carnival rides, live music, and of course, plenty of watermelon.
  • Take a Horse-Drawn Carriage Ride: This charming activity is perfect for couples or families who want to experience the town’s historic charm and scenic beauty.
  • Explore the Antique Shops: Rush Springs is home to a variety of unique antique shops that are perfect for treasure hunters and collectors.
  • Tour the Chickasaw Cultural Center: This center offers guided tours that provide an in-depth look at the Chickasaw Nation’s history and culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Rush Springs

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rush Springs Watermelon: This juicy and sweet watermelon is famous throughout the region and is a must-try for any visitor.
  • Fried Okra: This traditional Southern dish is a staple in Rush Springs and is available at many local restaurants.
  • Bison Burger: This lean and flavorful burger is a great alternative to traditional beef and can be found at many local eateries.
  • Catfish: This Southern classic is a popular menu item at many local restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Beer from the Choc Beer Company: This local brewery offers a variety of craft beers that are brewed using traditional methods and locally sourced ingredients.
  • Sweet Tea: This refreshing Southern drink is a staple at many local restaurants and cafes.
  • Chickasaw Nation Wines: This winery offers a variety of locally produced wines that are perfect for wine lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of Rush Springs

Rush Springs is a town that is steeped in tradition and culture. The locals are known for their friendly and welcoming nature, and visitors should be sure to embrace the town’s unique customs and traditions. The town is also home to a significant Native American population, and visitors should be respectful of their culture and customs.

Best Time to Visit Rush Springs

The best time to visit Rush Springs is during the summer months when the weather is warm and the town is bustling with activity. The Rush Springs Watermelon Festival is also held during the summer and is a must-see event for any visitor.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rush Springs

Do:

  • Embrace the town’s unique culture and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine, including watermelon and bison burgers.
  • Take advantage of the town’s natural beauty and scenic landscapes.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the town’s local customs and traditions.
  • Litter or damage the town’s natural resources.
  • Ignore the town’s history and culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rush Springs

Rush Springs is a relatively safe community, but visitors should still exercise caution and be aware of their surroundings. The town is located in Tornado Alley, and visitors should be prepared for severe weather conditions during the spring and summer months.

Health in Rush Springs

Rush Springs is home to several medical clinics and pharmacies, but visitors with serious medical conditions should seek treatment in nearby Lawton or Oklahoma City.

Getting to Rush Springs: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Rush Springs

  • Lawton-Fort Sill Regional Airport (LAW): This airport is located approximately 30 minutes from Rush Springs and offers flights to major regional hubs like Dallas-Fort Worth and Denver.
  • Will Rogers World Airport (OKC): This airport is located approximately 70 miles from Rush Springs and offers flights to major national and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Rush Springs

  • Greyhound Bus Station (Lawton): This bus station is located approximately 30 minutes from Rush Springs and offers bus service to major regional destinations.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or arrange for a shuttle service from one of the nearby airports or bus stations.

How to Get Around in Rush Springs

The best way to get around Rush Springs is by car. Visitors can rent a car at one of the nearby airports or in Lawton.

Where to Go Next from Rush Springs: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Oklahoma City: This vibrant city is located approximately 70 miles from Rush Springs and is home to a variety of attractions, including the Oklahoma City National Memorial and Museum and the Bricktown Entertainment District.
  • Dallas, Texas: This bustling city is located approximately 180 miles from Rush Springs and offers a variety of attractions, including the Dallas Arboretum and Botanical Garden and the Sixth Floor Museum at Dealey Plaza.
  • Fort Worth, Texas: This historic city is located approximately 150 miles from Rush Springs and is home to the Fort Worth Stockyards National Historic District and the Kimbell Art Museum.
