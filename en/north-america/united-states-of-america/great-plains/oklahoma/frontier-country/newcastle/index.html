Newcastle, Oklahoma is a charming town located in Frontier Country in the Great Plains region of the United States of America. Known for its rich history, natural beauty, and exciting activities, Newcastle is a must-visit destination for travelers who want to experience the best of Oklahoma. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, food to eat, and local customs and traditions of Newcastle.

Introduction to Newcastle

Newcastle is a small town located just outside of Oklahoma City, making it an ideal destination for travelers who want to experience the best of both worlds. The town has a rich history that dates back to the early 1900s when it was founded as a farming community. Today, Newcastle is a thriving community with a strong sense of pride in its heritage and a commitment to preserving its natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Newcastle

Whether you are interested in history, outdoor adventures, or just exploring new places, Newcastle has something for everyone. Here is a list of some of the top places to visit in Newcastle:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Newcastle Historical Museum: This museum is located in the former Newcastle State Bank building and showcases the history of the town and its people.

  • McClain County Historical Museum: This museum is located in nearby Purcell and showcases the history of the county and its people.

  • Pioneer Library System: This library system serves Newcastle and surrounding towns and is a great place to learn about the history and culture of the area.

Natural Attractions of Newcastle: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Newcastle Casino Bike Trail: This 4-mile bike trail is located at the Newcastle Casino and offers scenic views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Chisholm Trail Park: This park features a playground, picnic tables, and a sports complex.

  • Blanchard Springs Park: This park is located just a few miles outside of Newcastle and features a swimming pool, walking trails, and a playground.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Newcastle

Newcastle is home to some of the best hiking and biking trails in Oklahoma. Here are a few trails that are worth checking out:

  • Newcastle Casino Bike Trail: This 4-mile trail winds through the beautiful countryside surrounding Newcastle and is perfect for bikers of all skill levels.

  • Redbud Park Trail: This 1.3-mile trail is located in nearby Bethany and offers scenic views of the nearby lake.

  • Earlywine Park Trail: This 1.2-mile trail is located in Oklahoma City and offers great views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Newcastle: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of exciting activities and tours to enjoy in Newcastle. Here are a few ideas:

  • Visit the Newcastle Gaming Center: Whether you’re a seasoned gambler or just looking to try your luck, the Newcastle Gaming Center is the perfect place to spend an evening.

  • Take a guided tour of the Newcastle Historical Museum: Learn about the history of the town and its people on a guided tour of the Newcastle Historical Museum.

  • Explore the local parks: Newcastle is home to several beautiful parks, including Chisholm Trail Park and Blanchard Springs Park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Newcastle

If you’re planning to spend a week in Newcastle, here’s an itinerary that will help you make the most of your time:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Newcastle and check in to your hotel. Spend the afternoon exploring the town and visiting local attractions, such as the Newcastle Historical Museum.

  • Days 2-3: Spend these days exploring the local parks and hiking/biking trails.

  • Days 4-5: Take a guided tour of the Oklahoma City National Memorial & Museum and explore the city’s other attractions, such as the Myriad Botanical Gardens and the Oklahoma City Museum of Art.

  • Days 6-7: Spend the last few days of your trip relaxing in Newcastle and enjoying the local food and drink scene.

Food and Drink Scene of Newcastle

Newcastle may be a small town, but it’s home to some fantastic restaurants and bars. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to enjoy:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken Fried Steak: This classic southern dish is a staple of the local cuisine.

  • Oklahoma-style BBQ: For a taste of true Oklahoma flavor, be sure to try the local BBQ.

  • Fried Catfish: Another southern favorite, fried catfish can be found on many menus in Newcastle.

Local Beverages

  • Oklahoma beer: The state is home to several fantastic craft breweries, including COOP Ale Works and Prairie Artisan Ales.

What to Buy in Newcastle: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a unique souvenir to remember your trip to Newcastle? Here are a few ideas:

  • Local honey: Oklahoma is known for its delicious honey, and you can find it for sale at many local markets.

  • Native American crafts: Oklahoma has a strong Native American heritage, and you can find a wide variety of crafts and artwork for sale in local shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Newcastle

Newcastle is a friendly and welcoming community with a strong sense of pride in its heritage and traditions. Visitors are advised to be respectful of local customs and traditions, and to ask questions if they are unsure about anything.

Best Time to Visit Newcastle

The best time to visit Newcastle is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summer can be quite hot, and winters can be cold and snowy.

  • Do try the local food and drink.

  • Do be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

Safety and Health

Safety in Newcastle

Newcastle is a safe and friendly community, but visitors are advised to take normal precautions such as locking their car doors and not leaving valuables in plain sight.

Health in Newcastle

Visitors to Newcastle should take normal precautions to stay healthy, such as washing their hands frequently and keeping their vaccinations up-to-date.

Getting to Newcastle: Transportation Options and Logistics

Newcastle is located just outside of Oklahoma City and is easily accessible by car or bus. Here are a few transportation options:

Airports Near Newcastle

  • Will Rogers World Airport (OKC): This airport is located in Oklahoma City and is the largest airport in the state.

Bus Stations Near Newcastle

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: Greyhound operates several buses a day to and from Oklahoma City.

Other Options

  • Rental cars: Rental cars are available at the airport and in Oklahoma City.

How to Get Around in Newcastle

The best way to get around Newcastle is by car, as many of the attractions are spread out across the town.
