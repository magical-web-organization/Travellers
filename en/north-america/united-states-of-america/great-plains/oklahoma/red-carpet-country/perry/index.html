Introduction to Perry

Located in Red Carpet Country, Oklahoma, Perry is a charming city that offers visitors a blend of natural beauty, cultural landmarks, and outdoor adventure. Founded in the 1890s, the city has a rich history that is reflected in its distinctive architecture, museums, and historical landmarks. If you’re planning to visit Perry, this comprehensive travel guide will help you discover the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re looking to explore the cultural landmarks of Perry, here are some must-visit places:

  • Cherokee Strip Museum - This museum offers an insight into the history of the region and artifacts from the Cherokee Strip Land Run.
  • Marland’s Grand Home - A national historic landmark, this mansion was built in 1916 and is a fine example of French architecture.
  • Grant County Courthouse - This courthouse was built in 1930 and is a part of the National Register of Historic Places.
  • Noble County Courthouse - Built in 1916 and designed in the Beaux Arts style, this courthouse is a stunning landmark.
  • The Vintage House - A bed and breakfast, this 1930s home is a charming destination that offers dining, shopping, and events.

Natural Attractions of Perry: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Perry offers visitors a range of natural attractions that boast breathtaking views and outdoor adventures. Some favorites include:

  • Lake Perry - This 2,000-acre lake offers boating, fishing, hiking, camping, and swimming activities.
  • McMurtry Wildlife Refuge - Located near Stillwater, this refuge covers over 4,000-acres and is a great place for bird-watching and wildlife spotting.
  • Perry Lake Park - This park offers campgrounds, picnic areas, hiking trails, fishing docks, and more.
  • Seven Clans Paradise Casino - This casino offers gaming and resort amenities along with a stunning view of the lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Perry

Perry offers some great hikes and biking trails that wind through the natural beauty of the area. Some options include:

  • Cherokee Strip Trail - This trail is a part of the Transamerica Trail and stretches for 115 miles, offering scenic views and natural landscapes.
  • Perry City Lake Trail - This trail is a favorite spot for hiking, biking, and horseback riding.
  • The Great Plains Trail of Oklahoma - A 1,600-mile stretch of trails that offers a mix of rural and urban landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Perry: Activities and Tours

Perry offers a range of activities and tours that cater to different interests and ages. Some popular options include:

  • Perry Golf and Country Club - A beautiful golf course that offers a challenging game and stunning vistas.
  • Perry Wine Trail - This trail covers some of the best wineries and vineyards in Oklahoma and offers a chance to sample local wines.
  • Cherokee Strip Golf Classic - An annual golf tournament held in Perry that attracts visitors from across the state.
  • Day Trips - There are several day trips that you can take from Perry, including to the Tallgrass Prairie Preserve, Tulsa, Oklahoma City, and more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Perry

If you’re planning a week-long stay in Perry, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1 - Visit the Cherokee Strip Museum and Marland’s Grand Home.
  • Day 2 - Explore the McMurtry Wildlife Refuge and enjoy hiking, bird-watching, and wildlife spotting.
  • Day 3 - Take the Cherokee Strip Wine Trail and sample local wines.
  • Day 4 - Play a round of golf at the Perry Golf and Country Club.
  • Day 5 - Visit the Perry Lake Park and enjoy a picnic and hiking.
  • Day 6 - Take a day trip to the Tallgrass Prairie Preserve and experience the natural beauty of Oklahoma.
  • Day 7 - Visit the Seven Clans Paradise Casino and try your luck at the gaming tables and enjoy the view of Lake Perry.

Best Time to Visit Perry

Perry enjoys a mild climate, with warm summers and mild winters. The best time to visit is from March to May and September to November when the weather is pleasant and the crowds are smaller. However, if you’re into outdoor activities like hiking and fishing, the summer months may be more suitable for you.

Getting to Perry: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Perry

The nearest airport to Perry is Stillwater Regional Airport, which is about 21 miles away. Other options include Tulsa International Airport and Will Rogers World Airport in Oklahoma City.

Bus Stations Near Perry

There are several Greyhound bus stations near Perry, including ones in Stillwater, Oklahoma City, and Tulsa.

Other Options

Driving is a popular option for getting to Perry, with major highways like I-35 and US-77 connecting the city to other major destinations in the state.

How to Get Around in Perry

Perry offers several options for getting around, including rental cars, taxis, and public buses. Biking and walking are also great ways to explore the city and its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Perry: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
