Introduction to Red Carpet Country

Red Carpet Country is a hidden gem in the Great Plains of Oklahoma, USA. It is a region of rolling hills, vast prairies, and charming towns that offer a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. From the historical landmarks of Route 66 to the stunning vistas of the Great Salt Plains State Park, this region has something for everyone. Whether you are a history buff, a nature lover, or an adventure seeker, Red Carpet Country is a must-visit destination.

List of Places to Visit in Red Carpet Country

Here are some of the top attractions and destinations to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Cherokee Strip Land Rush Museum

The Cherokee Strip Land Rush Museum in Enid tells the story of the famous land run of 1893. You can explore exhibits and artifacts that depict what life was like for the settlers who raced to claim their share of Cherokee land.

National Route 66 Museum

The National Route 66 Museum in Elk City is a fascinating tribute to the Mother Road. You can learn about the history and culture of Route 66, explore classic cars and authentic memorabilia, and even take a virtual road trip along the highway.

Oklahoma State Capitol

The Oklahoma State Capitol in Oklahoma City is a stunning example of Art Deco architecture. You can take a guided tour to discover the history and significance of this iconic building and see the impressive murals that adorn its interiors.

Natural Attractions of Red Carpet Country: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Great Salt Plains State Park

The Great Salt Plains State Park in Jet is an otherworldly landscape of salt flats, shallow lakes, and sand dunes. You can hike or bike along the trails, go fishing or kayaking, and even dig for selenite crystals.

Little Sahara State Park

The Little Sahara State Park in Waynoka is a playground of sand dunes that stretch over 1,600 acres. You can rent a sandboard or ATV to explore the dunes, or camp under the stars for a one-of-a-kind experience.

Alabaster Caverns State Park

The Alabaster Caverns State Park in Freedom is home to the largest natural gypsum cave in the world. You can take a guided tour to see the stunning formations and learn about the history and geology of the caves.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Red Carpet Country

Red Carpet Country offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor enthusiasts to explore its scenic trails. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the region:

  • Roman Nose State Park Trail System: This network of trails in Watonga winds through rugged terrain, forests, and rock formations.
  • Gloss Mountain State Park Trail: This short but scenic trail in Fairview offers stunning views of the surrounding red sandstone mesas.
  • Bluff Creek Mountain Bike Trail: This challenging trail in Woodward is a favorite among mountain bikers for its technical features and breathtaking scenery.

Best Things to Do in Red Carpet Country: Activities and Tours

If you are looking for unique experiences in Red Carpet Country, here are some of the top things to do:

  • Play golf at one of the many championship courses, such as the Perry Golf and Country Club or the Boiling Springs Golf Course.
  • Attend a rodeo at the Woodward Elks Rodeo, the largest outdoor rodeo in Oklahoma.
  • Take a hot air balloon ride over the Great Salt Plains or the Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge.
  • Join a ghost tour of the haunted towns of Guthrie or Enid.
  • Try your luck at one of the casinos, such as the Lucky Star Casino in Canton or the Riverwind Casino in Norman.

Week-Long Itinerary for Red Carpet Country

If you have a week to spend in Red Carpet Country, here is a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1-2: Explore the museums and historic sites of Oklahoma City, such as the Oklahoma City National Memorial and Museum, the National Cowboy and Western Heritage Museum, and the Oklahoma History Center.
  • Day 3: Visit the Great Salt Plains State Park for hiking, birdwatching, and crystal digging.
  • Day 4: Take a scenic drive on Route 66 to Elk City and visit the National Route 66 Museum.
  • Day 5: Go sandboarding or ATV riding at the Little Sahara State Park.
  • Day 6: Explore the natural wonders of the Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge, such as Mount Scott, the Bison Range, and the Medicine Park Historic District.
  • Day 7: Visit the Alabaster Caverns State Park for a guided cave tour and enjoy a picnic in the park.

Food and Drink Scene of Red Carpet Country

Red Carpet Country has a rich culinary scene that reflects its blend of cultures and traditions. Here are some of the top dishes and beverages to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken-fried steak: This Southern classic is a staple in many Red Carpet Country restaurants, such as the Eischen’s Bar in Okarche.
  • Fried catfish: Freshwater catfish is a popular dish in many dining places, such as the Catfish Round Up in Tonkawa.
  • Barbecue ribs: Slow-cooked ribs are a must-try in the region, especially at Smokehouse BBQ in Enid.

Local Beverages

  • Prairie Wolf Vodka: This award-winning vodka is distilled in Guthrie using locally sourced wheat and corn.
  • Cimarron Breeze Winery: This family-owned winery in Drumright produces a variety of wines from Oklahoma-grown grapes.
  • Coop Ale Works: This craft brewery in Oklahoma City offers a range of flavorful beers, such as the F5 IPA and the DNR Belgian Strong Ale.

Where to Stay in Red Carpet Country: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Red Carpet Country offers a range of accommodations for all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the top options:

  • 21c Museum Hotel in Oklahoma City: This boutique hotel features contemporary art exhibits and luxurious amenities.
  • Hotel Indigo in Downtown Tulsa: This stylish hotel is housed in a historic building and offers a vibrant nightlife scene.
  • Hostelling International in Tulsa: This budget-friendly hostel is perfect for backpackers and solo travelers who want to meet fellow adventurers.
  • The Pollard Inn in Guthrie: This historic bed and breakfast offers comfortable rooms and a home-cooked breakfast.

What to Buy in Red Carpet Country: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to bring a piece of Red Carpet Country home with you, here are some of the best souvenirs and products to buy:

  • Native American crafts: You can find a variety of handmade items, such as jewelry, pottery, and textiles, at the Cherokee Heritage Center in Tahlequah.
  • Route 66 memorabilia: Many gift shops and antique stores along Route 66 offer vintage signs, postcards, and other collectibles.
  • Oklahoma-made products: From gourmet food to artisanal crafts, you can find a wide range of locally made items at the Made In Oklahoma store in Oklahoma City.

Local Culture and Customs of Red Carpet Country

Red Carpet Country has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its art, music, and traditions. Here are some of the key aspects of the local culture:

  • Native American heritage: The region is home to several tribes, such as the Cherokee, Osage, and Kiowa, who have preserved their traditions and customs to this day.
  • Western lifestyle: The cowboy culture is alive and well in Red Carpet Country, with rodeos, horse shows, and country music concerts happening throughout the year.
  • Food and hospitality: People in the region are known for their love of good food and warm hospitality, and you will often find yourself invited to share a meal or a conversation with the locals.

Best Time to Visit Red Carpet Country

The best time to visit Red Carpet Country is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the landscape is vibrant with foliage or wildflowers. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and snowy, so plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Red Carpet Country

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Red Carpet Country:

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Learn about the local history and culture.
  • Be friendly and polite to the locals.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural sites.
  • Speed or drive recklessly on the roads.
  • Disrespect the Native American traditions and customs.
  • Be rude or confrontational to the locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Red Carpet Country

Red Carpet Country is generally a safe and welcoming destination, but as in any place, there are some precautions you should take:

  • Watch out for severe weather conditions, such as tornadoes and hailstorms.
  • Be aware of the risks of outdoor activities, such as hiking, swimming, or boating.
  • Avoid remote or unfamiliar areas at night.
  • Keep your valuables secure and lock your car and hotel room.

Health in Red Carpet Country

Red Carpet Country has good healthcare facilities, but it is always a good idea to have travel insurance and bring any necessary medication or medical supplies. In case of emergency, dial 911 or go to the nearest hospital or clinic.

How to Get Around in Red Carpet Country

The best way to explore Red Carpet Country is by car, as the region is vast and many attractions are located in remote areas. You can rent a car at the airports or in the major cities, or bring your own vehicle. There are also some public transportation options, such as buses and shuttles, but they may not be available in all areas.

Where to Go Next from Red Carpet Country: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to extend your trip from Red Carpet Country, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Tulsa: This vibrant city is known for its Art Deco architecture, world-class museums, and lively music scene.
  • Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge: This stunning natural preserve is home to bison, elk, prairie dogs, and a variety of wildlife.
  • Bartlesville: This charming town has a rich oil heritage and features the impressive Frank Lloyd Wright-designed Price Tower.
  • Crystal Bridges Museum of American Art: This world-class museum in Bentonville, Arkansas, features a collection of American art from colonial times to the present.

Conclusion

Red Carpet Country is a unique and diverse destination that offers plenty of opportunities for adventure, culture, and relaxation. By following this guide and exploring the many attractions and experiences of the region, you can create memories that will last a lifetime.
