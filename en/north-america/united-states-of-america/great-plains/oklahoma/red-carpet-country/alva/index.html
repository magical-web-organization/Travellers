Introduction to Alva

Welcome to Alva, a beautiful city situated in Red Carpet Country, in Oklahoma’s Great Plains. Known for its rich history, beautiful architecture, and stunning natural beauty, Alva offers travelers an array of cultural, natural, and outdoor attractions to explore.

Located just two hours north of Oklahoma City and near the Kansas border, this charming city is easily accessible from all parts of Oklahoma and the surrounding states. Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or foodie, Alva has something for everyone.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll help you discover the best things to do in Alva, from visiting its cultural and natural attractions, trying out local cuisine, hiking and biking routes, and staying in the best accommodation options. So, let’s start exploring!

List of Places to Visit in Alva

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For history buffs or anyone interested in cultural attractions, Alva has a lot to offer. Here are some of the must-visit museums and historical places in Alva:

  • Cherokee Strip Museum: This museum is dedicated to preserving the history of the Cherokee Strip Land Run, which was a significant historical event that helped shape Oklahoma’s political and social history.
  • Graceful Arts Gallery & Studio: This premier art center showcases local artists from the Alva area. Visitors can enjoy unique art exhibits, art classes and workshops, and shop for handmade goods and souvenirs.
  • Alva’s College Hill Historic District: This is a National Register of Historic Places neighborhood in Alva, Oklahoma. It features a variety of architectural styles, including Queen Anne, Prairie, Bungalow, and Colonial Revival.
  • Little Sahara State Park: This park offers outdoor activities for visitors to enjoy all year-round, including hiking, camping, picnicking, and sand dune riding. It’s ideal for adventure lovers and nature enthusiasts.

Natural Attractions of Alva: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Alva is a paradise for nature lovers, with its outdoor adventures and scenic views. Here are some of the best outdoor attractions that visitors should add to their travel itinerary:

  • Salt Plains National Wildlife Refuge: This beautiful wildlife refuge is home to almost 200 species of birds and is an ideal spot for bird-watching. Visitors can also enjoy hiking trails, fishing, camping, and nature photography.
  • Lake Alva: A beautiful lake located right in the heart of Alva, it is an ideal recreational spot for visitors and locals alike. Activities include fishing, boating, and hiking trails.
  • Great Salt Plains State Park: This park features hiking trails, fishing, and camping sites, where visitors can relax and enjoy the beautiful scenery of the park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alva

Alva has an abundance of hiking and biking routes for visitors who wish to explore the city’s beautiful natural surroundings. Some of the best hiking and biking routes include:

  • Alva Trail System: The Alva Trail System offers visitors a variety of trails that pass by lakes and streams. These trails are ideal for hiking, biking, or running.
  • Salt Plains Wilderness Trail: This trail passes through the Salt Plains National Wildlife Refuge and offers visitors stunning scenery of the salt flats and wildlife.
  • Little Sahara Sand Dunes Trail: Visitors can hike or bike on the sand dunes in Little Sahara State Park, which provides a unique experience for adventure enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Alva: Activities and Tours

Apart from its natural and cultural attractions, Alva offers visitors several exciting activities and tours to participate in. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Attend Alva’s Annual Christmas Tours: Alva’s Christmas Tours are famous as they showcase the best-decorated houses in the city. Visitors can enjoy the festive lights, music, and local treats around the town.
  • Visit Alabaster Caverns: This is a unique cave system located in the northwest part of Oklahoma. Visitors can explore the beautiful alabaster caverns and take a tour of the cave system.
  • Attend the Freedom Fest: Held annually in honor of Independence day, this festival features live music, food vendors, and a spectacular fireworks display.

Week-Long Itinerary for Alva

A week-long itinerary is the perfect way to explore and appreciate everything that the city of Alva has to offer. Here’s a sample week-long itinerary:

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore Alva’s downtown area, visit Graceful Arts Gallery & Studio, and try local cuisine at The Station.
Day 2 Experience the natural beauty of Salt Plains National Wildlife Refuge and enjoy bird watching, hiking, and photography.
Day 3 Enjoy hiking and camping at Great Salt Plains State Park
Day 4 Attend a live performance at Northwestern Oklahoma State University or Alva’s Heritage Center

Food and Drink Scene of Alva

No trip is complete without trying out the local cuisine, and Alva has plenty of delicious food and drinks for visitors to experience.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken Fried Steak: A true Southern staple, the Chicken Fried Steak is one of the most popular dishes in the Alva area. This dish features a chicken steak that is breaded and fried and served with a side of mixed vegetables or mashed potatoes.
  • Fried Okra: Fried okra is another Southern dish that is a must-try when visiting Alva. Made with freshly picked okra, dipped in cornmeal, and fried to perfection, this dish is a delicious side that goes well with any main course.
  • Braum’s Ice Cream: Braum’s is a popular ice cream chain that originated in Tuttle, Oklahoma. They offer a variety of flavors and toppings, including their specialty flavor, Pecan Praline.

Local Beverages

  • Prairie Beerworks: A local brewery located in downtown Alva, Prairie Beerworks offers visitors a variety of craft beers and ales that are made using local ingredients. Visitors can enjoy a beer flight, a pint, or take growlers home.
  • Sonic Drive-In: Known as the “First fast-food chain in America,” Sonic Drive-in originated in Oklahoma deep in the heart of the Midwest. Visitors can try their refreshing slushies or milkshakes, which come in a wide variety of flavors.

Where to Stay in Alva: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Alva has several accommodation options that cater to every budget, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels, and everything in between.

  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites Alva: This hotel features an outdoor pool, fitness center, 24-hour business center, and complimentary breakfast.
  • Yellow House Bed and Breakfast: This cozy bed and breakfast is located in a charming 1919 home and features comfortable guest rooms and a delicious breakfast served daily.
  • Alva Comfort Inn & Suites: This hotel features a fitness center, free Wi-Fi, and complimentary breakfast, and is located just a few minutes away from downtown Alva.

What to Buy in Alva: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Alva can bring back lovely souvenirs as a reminder of their trip to the city. Here are some of the must-buy souvenirs and local products:

  • Local Jewelry: Visitors can buy beautiful jewelry made from locally-mined alabaster at Alabaster Caverns Gift Shop and other shops around Alva.
  • Souvenir T-Shirts: Visitors can take home a T-Shirt with Alva’s famous slogan, “The Little Town That Could.”
  • Scented Candles: Alva boasts many local candle makers that produce scented candles with unique flavors such as lavender, Pineapple Orange, and Gingerbread.

Local Culture and Customs of Alva

Alva boasts of a rich culture and traditions that visitors should try and experience while in the city. Here are some customs and cultural events that visitors should look out for:

  • Northwestern Oklahoma State University Rodeo: An annual event that is popular with visitors and locals alike. This rodeo features bull riding, steer wrestling, and barrel racing, among other events.
  • Native American Folklore: Alva is located within the traditional homeland of the Cheyenne and Arapaho Tribes. Visitors should look out for cultural events where they can experience traditional drumming, dancing, and storytelling.
  • Harvest Festival: Alva celebrates this festival annually in mid-August with fun events like cow-chip tossing, a parade, and games for children.

Best Time to Visit Alva

The best time to visit Alva is from April to September, as this is when the weather is pleasant, and visitors can enjoy outdoor activities. The temperature hits its peak in July, so visitors should expect high temperatures during this month.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alva

Do:

  • Do try some of the local cuisine, including chicken-fried steak, fried okra, and Braum’s ice cream.
  • Do attend cultural events and immerse yourself in the local culture and traditions.
  • Do explore the natural beauty of Alva and enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and bird-watching.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage historical and natural sites.
  • Don’t visit sensitive areas without permission, such as archaeological sites or private property.
  • Don’t miss out on the opportunity to interact with the friendly locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Alva

Alva is generally safe for tourists, but as it’s always the case in any new destination, visitors should always exercise caution and keep their valuables close while exploring the city. Visitors should also take care while driving on the highways of Alva, especially during unfavorable weather conditions.

Health in Alva

Alva has several health care facilities, including the Share Medical Center, located in the heart of Alva. Visitors should also familiarize themselves with the weather patterns and take precautions as necessary.

Getting to Alva: Transportation Options and Logistics

Alva is easily accessible by air, car, or bus.

Airports Near Alva

The closest airport to Alva is the Will Rogers World Airport, which is located two hours south of Alva. Visitors can also consider flying to Tulsa International Airport located 150 miles east of Alva. Both airports offer car rental services.

Bus Stations Near Alva

Greyhound bus operates daily services between Oklahoma City and Waynoka, which is located just 20 miles west of Alva. Visitors can then take a taxi or rent a car to Alva.

Other Options

Visitors can drive or take a private shuttle service to Alva. The city offers ample parking, and visitors can also try biking or walking to explore the city.

How to Get Around in Alva

Alva has ample parking facilities, and visitors can easily drive around the city. Visitors can also rent bikes from Northwestern Oklahoma State University or go on guided bike tours. Taxis and rental car services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Alva: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Alva can extend their trip and explore nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some ideas:

  • Woodward: This small city is located 45 miles east of Alva and offers several outdoor activities such as hiking, golfing, and fishing.
  • Enid: Enid is located about 65 miles south of Alva and offers visitors several cultural attractions such as museums, art galleries, and theaters.
  • Medicine Park: This charming town is located about 100 miles south of Alva and is known for its cobblestone buildings, scenic trails, and waterfalls.

Conclusion

Alva is a charming city that has a lot to offer visitors, from exploring cultural and historic sites, hiking and biking adventures, trying out local food, and experiencing traditional events. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can easily plan their trip to Alva, discovering the best of Oklahoma’s Great Plains. Plan your next trip to Alva today and explore the city’s hidden gems.
