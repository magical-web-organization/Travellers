Introduction to Elk City

Elk City is a charming town located in Great Plains Country in western Oklahoma, United States. This small community of just over 12,000 people offers visitors a unique glimpse into the rural way of life in Oklahoma. Elk City is known for its rich history, natural beauty, charming downtown area, and friendly locals. There are plenty of things to see and do in Elk City, making it a worthwhile travel destination.

List of Places to Visit in Elk City

Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor adventures, or just soaking up the local culture, Elk City has something for everyone. Here are some of the top places to visit in Elk City:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Route 66 Museum - This museum is dedicated to the iconic Route 66 highway and its impact on American culture. The museum features a collection of vintage cars, exhibits on the history of Route 66, and plenty of photo opportunities.
  • Old Town Museum Complex - This living history museum features over a dozen historic buildings, including a schoolhouse, jail, and blacksmith shop. Visitors can experience what life was like in Elk City during the early 1900s.
  • Washita Battlefield National Historic Site - This sacred site commemorates the Battle of Washita, where General Custer and the U.S. Army attacked a Cheyenne village in 1868. Visitors can explore the site’s exhibits and learn about the history of the site.

Natural Attractions of Elk City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Elk City Lake - This beautiful lake is perfect for fishing, boating, and swimming. Visitors can rent boats, kayaks, and jet skis at the lake’s marina.
  • Great Plains State Park - This state park offers visitors plenty of hiking and biking trails, picnic areas, and campsites. The park is home to a diverse array of wildlife, including bison, elk, and pronghorn.
  • Ackley Park - This lovely park in downtown Elk City is home to a beautiful Japanese garden, a playground, and several picnic areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Elk City

For those looking for outdoor adventure, Elk City offers plenty of hiking and biking opportunities. Here are some of the top routes in the area:

  • Elk City Lake Trail - This trail circles the perimeter of Elk City Lake, offering stunning views of the water and surrounding landscape. The trail is approximately six miles long and is suitable for all skill levels.
  • Black Kettle Trail - This challenging trail is located within the Great Plains State Park and offers visitors the chance to hike through a variety of terrains, including grasslands, forests, and canyons. The trail is approximately 11 miles long and is suitable for experienced hikers.
  • Butterfield Overland Trail - This historic trail follows the same route that early pioneers took as they traveled westward. Visitors can hike along the trail and learn about the history of the region.

Best Things to Do in Elk City: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities to enjoy in Elk City. Here are some of the best things to do in the area:

  • Visit a local farm - Elk City is surrounded by farmland and visitors can take a tour of a local farm to experience rural Oklahoma life. Some of the farms offer activities such as hayrides and pumpkin patches.
  • Go antiquing - Elk City has a vibrant antique shopping scene. Visitors can explore the town’s many antique shops and find unique treasures.
  • Take a guided tour - Local tour operators offer tours of Elk City and the surrounding area. Visitors can choose from guided walking tours, trolley tours, or even helicopter tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Elk City

For visitors who have a week to explore Elk City and the surrounding area, here is a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Visit the National Route 66 Museum and Old Town Museum Complex

Food and Drink Scene of Elk City

No trip to Elk City is complete without sampling some of the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken Fried Steak - A classic Southern dish, chicken fried steak is a breaded and fried steak served with mashed potatoes and gravy.
  • BBQ - Oklahoma is known for its BBQ, and Elk City has some great options. Look for local BBQ joints for a taste of Oklahoma’s best brisket and pulled pork.
  • Fried Okra - A Southern favorite, fried okra is a crispy and crunchy side dish that pairs well with any meal.

Local Beverages

  • Prairie Wolf Vodka - This small-batch vodka is made in Guthrie, Oklahoma, and is known for its smooth taste.
  • Mariposa Coffee Roastery - This local coffee roaster offers a variety of coffee blends made from beans sourced from around the world.
  • Peach Wine - The Oklahoma Peach Wine Company makes a delicious peach wine that is perfect for sipping on a warm Oklahoma evening.

Where to Stay in Elk City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Elk City offers a variety of accommodations for visitors. From budget-friendly options to luxurious hotels, there is something for every traveler. Here are some of the top accommodation options in Elk City:

  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites Elk City - This modern hotel offers a variety of amenities, including a fitness center, breakfast buffet, and outdoor pool.
  • Motel 6 Elk City - A budget-friendly option, this motel offers clean and comfortable rooms at an affordable price.
  • Whippoorwill Wood Lodge - This charming bed and breakfast is located just outside of Elk City and offers a peaceful retreat from the hustle and bustle of town.

What to Buy in Elk City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for a unique souvenir or gift to bring back home, Elk City has plenty of options. Here are some of the top souvenirs and local products to look for:

  • Route 66 Memorabilia - Elk City is located along the historic Route 66 highway, and there are plenty of shops that sell Route 66 souvenirs and memorabilia.
  • Local Artwork - Elk City has a vibrant arts community, and visitors can find a variety of local artwork for sale in galleries and shops around town.
  • Oklahoma-made Products - Look for local Oklahoma-made products, such as handmade soap, locally roasted coffee, and artisanal cheese.

Local Culture and Customs of Elk City

Elk City is a friendly and welcoming town, and visitors should expect to be greeted with Southern hospitality. The local culture is deeply rooted in Oklahoma’s rural history, with a focus on family, community, and hard work. Visitors should take the time to learn about the town’s history and traditions, and respect the local way of life.

Best Time to Visit Elk City

The best time to visit Elk City is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the crowds are fewer. Summer in Oklahoma can be hot and humid, and winter can be cold and unpredictable. That being said, Elk City has plenty to offer visitors year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Elk City

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Elk City:

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Do take the time to learn about the town’s history and culture
  • Do be respectful to the local community and their way of life

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment
  • Don’t be rude or disrespectful to locals
  • Don’t leave valuables in your car while parked

Safety and Health

Elk City is a safe and welcoming town, but visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take basic precautions. Here are some tips to stay safe and healthy while in Elk City:

Safety in Elk City

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night
  • Lock your car and keep valuables out of sight
  • Follow all traffic laws while driving

Health in Elk City

  • Bring sunscreen and a hat to protect yourself from the sun
  • Drink plenty of water, as Oklahoma summers can be hot and humid
  • Bring any necessary medications and first aid supplies

Getting to Elk City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Elk City is located approximately 110 miles west of Oklahoma City and is easily accessible by car or bus. Here are some transportation options for getting to Elk City:

Airports Near Elk City

  • Will Rogers World Airport (OKC) - Approximately two hours by car

Bus Stations Near Elk City

  • Greyhound Bus Station - Elk City, OK

Other Options

  • Rental Car - There are several rental car agencies located in Oklahoma City
  • Taxi/Uber/Lyft - Limited taxi service is available in Elk City, and ride-sharing services are not yet available.

How to Get Around in Elk City

It is recommended to have a car or bike while visiting Elk City as public transportation options are limited. Visitors can rent a car, bike, or book a tour through a local tour operator.

Where to Go Next from Elk City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Elk City is located in a stunning region of Oklahoma with plenty of nearby destinations worth visiting. Here are some of the nearby places to add to your itinerary:

  • Oklahoma City - The state’s capital city is just a two-hour drive east of Elk City and offers a variety of cultural attractions, shopping and dining options, and outdoor adventures.
  • Great Salt Plains State Park - Located just over an hour north of Elk City, this state park is known for its salt flats, fishing, and birdwatching opportunities.
  • Black Mesa State Park and Nature Preserve - Located in the panhandle of Oklahoma, this park offers visitors the chance to hike to the highest point in Oklahoma and explore a rugged landscape of mesas and canyons.

Conclusion

Elk City is a charming town that offers visitors a unique glimpse into the rural way of life in Oklahoma. With its rich history, natural beauty, charming downtown area, and friendly locals, there are plenty of things to see and do in Elk City. Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor adventure, or just soaking up the local culture, Elk City is a worthwhile travel destination.
