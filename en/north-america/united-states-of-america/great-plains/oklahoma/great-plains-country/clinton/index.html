Introduction to Clinton

Clinton is a vibrant city located in Great Plains Country, Oklahoma, known for its rich cultural heritage and natural attractions. This charming city offers a wide range of exciting activities and breathtaking sights to tourists from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Clinton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Clinton’s rich history and diverse culture can be best explored at some of the best museums and historical places that the city has to offer. Here are some must-visit places:

  • Oklahoma Route 66 Museum: This museum showcases the history of the Mother Road of America and offers visitors a chance to experience life as it was in the 1950s.
  • Washita Battlefield National Historic Site: This site offers a deep insight into the Battle of Washita, which occurred in 1868, through a variety of exhibits, tours, and special events.
  • Stafford Air & Space Museum: This museum is home to a large collection of aircraft, spacecraft, and artifacts, including a full-scale replica of the Wright Brothers’ 1903 Wright Flyer.

Natural Attractions of Clinton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Clinton’s natural beauty and breathtaking landscapes are a nature lover’s paradise. Here are some of the best natural attractions to explore:

  • Foss State Park: This park is a popular destination for camping, hiking, fishing, and boating, with picturesque views of Foss Lake.
  • Red Rock Canyon Adventure Park: This park is known for its beautiful rock formations, hiking trails, and the opportunity to rappel down the canyon walls.
  • Water-Zoo Indoor Water Park: This park is great for families, offering numerous thrilling water slides, a large wave pool, and an indoor lazy river.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Clinton

Clinton offers some of the most beautiful hiking and biking trails in the Great Plains.

  • Acme Road Trail System: This trail system offers around 20 miles of mountain biking and hiking trails, allowing visitors to enjoy the scenic views of the surrounding area.
  • Clinton Lake Trail: This trail is perfect for hiking and biking enthusiasts who love the great outdoors and want to explore the remarkable Clinton Lake area.
  • Red Rock Canyon Adventure Park Trail System: This trail system features 5 miles of hiking trails, 8 miles of mountain bike trails, and a 110-foot rappelling tower.

Best Things to Do in Clinton: Activities and Tours

Clinton offers a range of fun and exciting activities for every age and interest. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Attend a rodeo at the Oklahoma Horseshoe Arena
  • Tour the Route 66 Museum and dine at the nearby Country Cupboard
  • Visit the Lucky Star Casino to have some fun and win big
  • Participate in the Annual Watermelon Festival, held every August

Food and Drink Scene of Clinton

Clinton is known for its unique blend of flavors and locally sourced produce. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken Fried Steak: This dish is a classic staple found in almost every restaurant in the area.
  • Barbecue Ribs: The slow-smoked ribs are a must-try and are served with a variety of homemade sauces.
  • Fried Catfish: You can’t go wrong with the local fried catfish, a popular dish in Oklahoma.

Local Beverages

  • COOP Ale Works Beer: This local brewery offers some of the best beer in the area.
  • Turner Falls Marie: This refreshing drink, named after the famous Turner Falls in the Arbuckle Mountains, is a must-try.

Where to Stay in Clinton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Clinton offers a wide range of lodging options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Hampton Inn Clinton: This hotel offers comfortable rooms, a heated indoor pool, a fitness center, and a complimentary breakfast.
  • Holiday Inn Express & Suites Clinton: This hotel is conveniently located near the Oklahoma Route 66 Museum and offers a variety of amenities, including a fitness center, free breakfast, and an indoor pool.
  • Motel 6 Clinton: This budget-friendly hotel is perfect for travelers on a tight budget and offers clean rooms and basic amenities.

What to Buy in Clinton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Clinton offers a variety of unique souvenirs and locally sourced products for tourists to take home. Here are some of the best items to buy:

  • T-shirts and hats with Route 66 logos
  • Homemade barbecue sauce and locally sourced honey
  • Pottery and handmade crafts from local artisans

Local Culture and Customs of Clinton

Clinton has a diverse cultural heritage that’s deeply rooted in its history and traditions. Here are some customs and cultural practices you may encounter:

  • Oklahoma is known for its love for American football, so expect to see this sport played and revered throughout the state.
  • Most Oklahomans are warm, friendly, and welcoming to visitors, so don’t be surprised if you’re greeted with a smile and a chat.
  • The state has a deep history tied to Native American cultures, and visitors can experience these cultural influences at various events and festivals.

Best Time to Visit Clinton

The best time to visit Clinton is during the spring and fall seasons when the weather is mild, and the crowds are fewer. However, visitors should note that this area is prone to tornadoes, so be sure to stay updated on the weather forecast during your stay.

Safety and Health

Safety in Clinton

Clinton is a safe city for tourists, but visitors should be aware of the risks of natural disasters such as tornadoes. When hiking or participating in outdoor activities, always take necessary safety precautions. Follow safety guidelines when visiting areas like Foss State Park and other natural attractions.

Health in Clinton

Clinton has several medical facilities, including Great Plains Regional Medical Center, for any medical emergencies or concerns.

Getting to Clinton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Clinton is accessible primarily by road with several transportation options. Here’s how to get to Clinton:

Airports Near Clinton

The closest airport to Clinton is the Will Rogers World Airport (OKC) in Oklahoma City, about an hour and a half away. Several rental car companies operate out of the airport, making it easy to explore the area.

Bus Stations Near Clinton

Greyhound operates a daily bus service that connects Clinton to other cities in Oklahoma and beyond. The bus stop is located at 107 S. 25th St.

Other Options

For those who prefer a more private mode of transportation, renting a car is recommended. Alternatively, taxis and ride-sharing services are available for moving around the city.

How to Get Around in Clinton

Clinton is a small city that’s easily walkable, and several bike rental shops offer bicycle rental opportunities. Visitors can also opt for taxis and ride-sharing services if they want to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Clinton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Clinton is surrounded by several exciting destinations that tourists can visit, including:

  • Elk City: A vibrant city located 30 miles east of Clinton, known for its rich history and natural attractions.
  • Oklahoma City: The capital city, located about 83 miles east of Clinton, offering a range of activities and attractions.
  • Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge: A beautiful nature preserve, known for its unique landscape and natural attractions, located 70 miles south of Clinton.

Conclusion

Clinton is a unique city with a rich history and diverse range of landscapes, activities, and attractions. Whether you’re a nature lover or a history buff, there’s something for everyone in Clinton. From the scenic hiking trails to the delicious food and drink scene, visitors are sure to have an unforgettable experience in this charming city.
