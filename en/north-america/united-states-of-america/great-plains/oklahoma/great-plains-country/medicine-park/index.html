Introduction to Medicine Park

Nestled in the picturesque Wichita Mountains of Oklahoma, Medicine Park is a charming town steeped in history and natural beauty. Known as the “Jewel of the Great Plains”, Medicine Park is known for its unique cobblestone architecture, crystal-clear lakes, and breathtaking hiking trails. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural landmarks, or simply a relaxing retreat, Medicine Park is the perfect destination for you.

List of Places to Visit in Medicine Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cobblestone Community Museum: This museum highlights the unique architecture of Medicine Park, with exhibits on the construction of the cobblestone buildings and the history of the town.
  • The Old Plantation Restaurant: Housed in a historic building, this restaurant offers a taste of southern cuisine, with dishes such as fried chicken, catfish, and pecan pie.
  • The Mattie Beal Home: This beautiful Victorian home is a local landmark and offers guided tours of the historic property.
  • The Holy City: This outdoor amphitheater tells the story of the life and death of Jesus through an annual Easter pageant.

Natural Attractions of Medicine Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Lawtonka: This popular lake is perfect for swimming, fishing, and boating, and offers stunning views of the Wichita Mountains.
  • The Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge: This wildlife refuge is home to bison, elk, prairie dogs, and other native animals, and offers various hiking trails and scenic drives.
  • Mount Scott: The highest peak in the Wichita Mountains, Mount Scott offers panoramic views of the surrounding scenery, and is a favorite spot for hiking and picnicking.
  • Bath Lake: This spring-fed lake is believed to have healing properties, and visitors can still take a dip in the refreshing waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Medicine Park

Medicine Park is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with several beautiful trails to explore:

  • Eagle Mountain Trail: A challenging 5-mile loop that offers stunning views of the Wichita Mountains and Medicine Creek.
  • Sunset Trail: A 2-mile trail that leads to the top of Mount Scott, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding area.
  • Elmer Thomas Trail: An easy 1.5-mile trail that winds through the Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge, with several scenic overlooks along the way.

Best Things to Do in Medicine Park: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking and canoeing: Visitors can rent kayaks and canoes to explore Medicine Creek, which winds through the heart of Medicine Park.
  • Fishing: Lake Lawtonka is a popular spot for fishing, with several species of fish, including bass, crappie, and catfish.
  • Horseback riding: The Wichita Mountains offer several horseback riding tours, with trails ranging from beginner to advanced.
  • Ghost Tours: Experience the eerie side of Medicine Park with a guided ghost tour through the town’s historic buildings and cemeteries.
  • Art Walks: Medicine Park is home to several galleries and studios, and visitors can take a self-guided tour of the local art scene.

Food and Drink Scene of Medicine Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken fried steak: A traditional southern dish, chicken fried steak is a must-try at any of Medicine Park’s local restaurants.
  • Fried catfish: Another southern favorite, fried catfish is a staple at many of Medicine Park’s seafood restaurants.
  • Pecan pie: End your meal with a slice of delicious pecan pie, a regional specialty.

Local Beverages

  • Oklahoma beer: Try a local brew from Oklahoma breweries such as Prairie Artisanal Ales or Anthem Brewing Company.
  • Cattleman’s Bloody Mary: This signature drink from the Cattleman’s Steakhouse at Fort Sill includes a shot of whiskey and beef jerky.

Where to Stay in Medicine Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Cobblestone Inn and Suites: This charming hotel is located in the heart of Historic Downtown Medicine Park, and offers comfortable rooms and suites with unique decor.
  • Medicine Park Camping Resort: For those who prefer camping, this resort offers tent and RV sites, as well as cabins for rent.
  • Park Hill Motel: This budget-friendly motel offers comfortable rooms and is located just a short drive from Medicine Park.

What to Buy in Medicine Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cobblestone-themed souvenirs: Take home a piece of Medicine Park’s unique architecture with cobblestone-themed souvenirs such as coasters, magnets, or postcards.
  • Local honey: Pick up a jar of Oklahoma honey from a local beekeeper.
  • Native American crafts: Medicine Park is located near several Native American reservations, and visitors can find unique arts and crafts at gift shops and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Medicine Park

Medicine Park’s rich history and unique architecture are a testament to the town’s cultural heritage. Visitors are encouraged to respect the town’s historic buildings and support local businesses to help preserve the town’s charm and character.

Best Time to Visit Medicine Park

The best time to visit Medicine Park is in the fall or spring, when the weather is mild and the scenery is at its most beautiful. However, the town’s unique attractions and activities make it an enjoyable destination year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Medicine Park

Do:

  • Respect the historic buildings and cobblestone architecture of Medicine Park.
  • Support local businesses and artisans.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor adventures and natural beauty of the Wichita Mountains.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter on hiking trails or in public areas.
  • Disturb or damage the natural habitats of the wildlife in the surroundings areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Medicine Park

Medicine Park is generally a safe place to visit, but it’s always best to take basic safety precautions, such as locking car doors and avoiding walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Medicine Park

Visitors should drink plenty of water and wear sunscreen while enjoying outdoor activities in the summer months. Be aware of seasonal allergies and insect bites.

Getting to Medicine Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Medicine Park

The closest airport to Medicine Park is Lawton-Fort Sill Regional Airport, which is about 15 miles away. Other nearby airports include Oklahoma City and Wichita Falls.

Bus Stations Near Medicine Park

Greyhound Bus Lines has a station in Lawton, which is about 10 miles from Medicine Park.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Medicine Park or rent a car from nearby rental agencies.

How to Get Around in Medicine Park

Many of the attractions and landmarks in Medicine Park are within walking distance, but visitors can also rent bicycles or kayaks to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Medicine Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Fort Sill Military Reservation: Located just a short drive from Medicine Park, Fort Sill offers tours of historic buildings and military sites, as well as a museum and visitor center.
  • Oklahoma City: Oklahoma City is about two hours away from Medicine Park and offers attractions such as the National Cowboy Museum and the Oklahoma City Zoo.
  • Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge: Although technically part of Medicine Park, the Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge offers enough attractions and activities to warrant a separate visit.

Conclusion

Medicine Park, Oklahoma is a hidden gem in the Great Plains Country, offering natural beauty, cultural landmarks, and outdoor adventures for visitors of all ages. With its unique cobblestone architecture and charming small-town vibe, Medicine Park is the perfect destination for a weekend getaway or a longer vacation.
