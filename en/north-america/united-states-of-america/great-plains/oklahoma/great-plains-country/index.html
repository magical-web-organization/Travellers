Introduction to Great Plains Country

Located in the heart of Oklahoma, Great Plains Country is a vast and beautiful region that boasts an array of outdoor adventures, culturally significant attractions, and unique local experiences. Spanning across the Great Plains region of the United States, Great Plains Country offers travelers a chance to experience the rich history, traditions, and natural wonders of this part of the country. With a variety of destinations to explore, Great Plains Country is the perfect vacation spot for anyone looking for a truly unique and memorable travel experience.

List of Places to Visit in Great Plains Country

If you’re planning a trip to Great Plains Country, there are plenty of must-visit destinations to add to your itinerary. Here are some of the top places to visit in the region:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Cowboy & Western Heritage Museum: Located in Oklahoma City, this museum is dedicated to preserving the history and traditions of the American West.
  • Will Rogers Memorial Museum: This museum in Claremore honors the life and legacy of Will Rogers, one of America’s most beloved entertainers.
  • Pawnee Bill Ranch and Museum: This historic ranch in Pawnee offers a glimpse into the life of one of Oklahoma’s most famous cowboys.

Natural Attractions of Great Plains Country: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge: This 59,000-acre refuge in Lawton is home to a variety of wildlife, including bison, elk, and longhorn cattle. Visitors can enjoy hiking, biking, and scenic drives through the park.
  • Alabaster Caverns State Park: Located in Freedom, this park is home to the largest gypsum cave in the world.
  • Red Rock Canyon Adventure Park: This scenic park in Hinton is known for its towering red rock formations and offers hiking, rock climbing, and rappelling opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Great Plains Country

Great Plains Country is a hiker and biker’s paradise, with countless trails and routes to explore. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in the region:

  • Black Kettle National Grassland Trail: This 14-mile trail in Cheyenne is known for its stunning views of the surrounding grasslands and rocky bluffs.
  • Ouachita National Trail: This 223-mile trail runs through Great Plains Country, offering hikers and bikers the chance to explore the region’s diverse landscapes and wildlife.
  • Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge Trails: With over 15 miles of hiking and biking trails, the Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge offers some of the best outdoor adventures in Great Plains Country.

Best Things to Do in Great Plains Country: Activities and Tours

From rodeos to wine tastings, Great Plains Country offers a variety of fun, unique activities and tours. Here are some of the best things to do in the region:

  • Rodeos: Great Plains Country is known for its rodeos, which offer visitors a chance to experience the excitement and traditions of the American West. Some of the most popular rodeos in the region include the Will Rogers Stampede Rodeo in Claremore and the Elk City Rodeo of Champions.
  • Wine Tastings: Oklahoma may not be the first place that comes to mind when you think of wineries, but Great Plains Country is home to some excellent vineyards. The Canadian River Vineyards & Winery in Lexington and the Sparks Vineyard & Winery in Sparks are both renowned for their award-winning wines.
  • Route 66 Tour: Great Plains Country is home to a portion of the famous Route 66, which offers travelers a chance to experience some of the most iconic sights and landmarks of the American West.

Week-Long Itinerary for Great Plains Country

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Great Plains Country, here’s a suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your time in the region:

  • Day 1: National Cowboy & Western Heritage Museum in Oklahoma City
  • Day 2: Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge for hiking and wildlife watching
  • Day 3: Pawnee Bill Ranch and Museum in Pawnee
  • Day 4: Red Rock Canyon Adventure Park for hiking and rock climbing
  • Day 5: Route 66 Tour through Tulsa and Claremore
  • Day 6: Will Rogers Memorial Museum and J.M. Davis Arms and Historical Museum in Claremore
  • Day 7: Alabaster Caverns State Park in Freedom

Food and Drink Scene of Great Plains Country

No trip to Great Plains Country is complete without trying some of the region’s delicious food and drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken Fried Steak: This classic Southern dish, made with a breaded and fried steak, is a staple in Great Plains Country.
  • Biscuits and Gravy: A hearty breakfast dish that’s perfect fuel for a day of hiking or exploring.
  • Fried Catfish: A popular dish in Oklahoma, fried catfish is a must-try for seafood lovers.

Local Beverages

  • Choctaw IPA: A locally brewed beer made with Choctaw hops and barley.
  • Prairie Wolf Vodka: Made with locally sourced wheat, Prairie Wolf Vodka is known for its smooth and clean taste.
  • Turner Falls White Wine: A crisp, refreshing white wine made with grapes grown at Turner Falls Vineyard.

Where to Stay in Great Plains Country: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxurious hotel or a budget-friendly hostel, there are plenty of accommodation options to choose from in Great Plains Country. Here are some top picks:

  • Ambassador Hotel Tulsa, Autograph Collection: This stylish boutique hotel in Tulsa offers luxurious amenities and a convenient location near many of the city’s top attractions.
  • The Mayo Hotel: Located in downtown Tulsa, The Mayo Hotel is a historic landmark known for its elegant architecture and upscale amenities.
  • HI Oklahoma City Hostel: This budget-friendly hostel in Oklahoma City offers clean, comfortable rooms and a convenient location near many of the city’s top attractions.

What to Buy in Great Plains Country: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs to bring home from Great Plains Country, here are some top picks:

  • Native American Crafts: Great Plains Country is home to many Native American communities, which offer a variety of handmade crafts and art.
  • Cowboy Boots: A classic Western accessory, cowboy boots are available at many boutiques and stores throughout the region.
  • Turner Falls Honey: Made with honey from local bees, Turner Falls Honey is a sweet and natural treat that makes a great souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Great Plains Country

Great Plains Country is steeped in rich cultural traditions and customs that reflect the region’s history and heritage. Some key aspects of local culture and customs include:

  • Native American Heritage: Great Plains Country is home to many Native American tribes, such as the Cheyenne and Arapaho, who have a deep and rich history in the region.
  • Western Heritage: Great Plains Country has a strong cowboy culture, with rodeos, livestock shows, and other Western-themed events taking place throughout the year.
  • Religion: The region has a strong Christian heritage, with many churches and religious sites throughout the area.

Best Time to Visit Great Plains Country

The best time to visit Great Plains Country is in the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and comfortable for outdoor activities. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and snowy. However, some travelers might prefer to visit during the winter months to take advantage of winter sports and activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Great Plains Country

Before you visit Great Plains Country, here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect the region’s cultural traditions and customs.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and any outdoor activities.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Be courteous and polite to locals and other travelers.

Don’t:

  • Ignore safety precautions when hiking or participating in other outdoor activities.
  • Disrespect Native American culture and customs.
  • Drive off-road or damage the region’s natural habitats.
  • Litter or leave garbage behind.

Safety and Health

Great Plains Country is generally a safe and healthy destination, but visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety and well-being while traveling in the region.

Safety in Great Plains Country

  • Always carry a map, compass, or GPS device when hiking or exploring the outdoors.
  • Be aware of the weather and any potential hazards, such as flash floods or wildfires.
  • Follow all posted signs and warnings in parks and natural areas.
  • Lock your car and secure any valuables when parked in tourist areas.

Health in Great Plains Country

  • Stay hydrated and protect yourself from the sun when spending time outdoors.
  • Use insect repellent to prevent mosquito bites and tick-borne illnesses.
  • Seek medical attention if you experience any illness or injury while traveling in the region.

Getting to Great Plains Country: Transportation Options and Logistics

Great Plains Country is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here are some transportation options and logistics to keep in mind:

Airports Near Great Plains Country

  • Will Rogers World Airport in Oklahoma City
  • Tulsa International Airport in Tulsa

Bus Stations Near Great Plains Country

  • Greyhound Bus Lines in Oklahoma City
  • Jefferson Lines in Tulsa

Other Options

  • Amtrak serves several cities in Oklahoma, including Oklahoma City and Tulsa.
  • Car rentals are available at all major airports and in many cities throughout the region.

How to Get Around in Great Plains Country

Great Plains Country is best explored by car, as many of the top attractions are spread out in remote areas. However, there are also plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking in the region.

Where to Go Next from Great Plains Country: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’ve already explored Great Plains Country and are looking for your next adventure, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Ozark National Forest in Arkansas: A stunning natural area that offers hiking, camping, and rock climbing.
  • Dallas, Texas: A vibrant city with a lively arts scene, excellent food, and plenty of attractions and activities.
  • Carlsbad Caverns National Park in New Mexico: A breathtaking network of caves and underground passageways that is home to a variety of unique wildlife.

Conclusion

Great Plains Country is a truly unique and unforgettable travel destination that offers a range of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and local experiences. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, there’s something for everyone in this beautiful and diverse region of Oklahoma. So why not start planning your trip today and discover all that Great Plains Country has to offer?
