A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Texola, Oklahoma

Texola is a small town in Beckham County, Oklahoma, located in the western part of the state. Despite its size, Texola is a great place for travelers to explore due to its rich history, natural beauty, and friendly small-town charm. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventure, cultural experiences, or just a relaxing getaway, Texola has something to offer.

Introduction to Texola

Texola was founded as a railway town in the early 1900s and has a long history of hosting travelers passing through. Its location on Route 66 makes it a popular stop for road trippers and history buffs. Today, Texola has a population of under 50 people and has maintained its small-town feel. Visitors to Texola can enjoy its many historic buildings and unique attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Texola

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Texola Historical Museum: This museum showcases the history of Texola and its importance as a stop on Route 66. Visitors can see artifacts from the town’s past, including its days as a railway stop and its connection to the development of the American West.
  • The Pioneer Cemetery: This historic cemetery is the final resting place of many of Texola’s early settlers. Visitors can see unique headstones and learn about the town’s early days.
  • The Old Texola Jail: This historic jailhouse is a popular spot for tourists to take photos and learn about the town’s history.

Natural Attractions of Texola: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Black Kettle National Grasslands: This preserved area of grassland and woodland offers visitors a chance to explore the natural beauty of Texola. Visitors can hike and bike through the preserve and enjoy scenic views of the surrounding landscape.
  • The Washita River: The Washita River runs through the heart of Texola and offers visitors a chance to fish, swim, and enjoy water sports.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Texola

Texola and the surrounding areas offer visitors a variety of hiking and biking opportunities. Some popular routes include:

  • Black Kettle National Grasslands Hiking Trails: This system of trails offers visitors a chance to explore the diverse landscape of Black Kettle National Grasslands.
  • Washita River Bike Trail: This 8-mile trail runs along the Washita River and offers scenic views of the water and surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Texola: Activities and Tours

  • Take a guided historic walking tour of the town: Visitors can learn about the history and architecture of Texola on a guided walking tour of the town.
  • Visit the Texola Trading Post: This historic trading post is a great place to pick up souvenirs and learn about local history.
  • Attend the annual Texola Old Settlers Reunion: This event, held each year in September, celebrates the town’s history and features live music, food, and entertainment.

Food and Drink Scene of Texola

Must-Try Dishes

  • Texoma Chili: This classic dish is a hearty and spicy chili featuring beef, beans, and a variety of spices.
  • BBQ Ribs: Smoked and grilled to perfection, Texola’s BBQ ribs are a must-try for meat lovers.

Local Beverages

  • Oklahoma Beer: Local craft breweries in Oklahoma produce some delicious beers that should not be missed.
  • Texola Sweet Tea: A southern classic, this refreshing and sweet iced tea is a perfect way to beat the heat.

Where to Stay in Texola: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Texola Inn: This historic inn offers comfortable accommodations and easy access to all of Texola’s attractions.
  • The Washita Motel: This motel features clean and affordable rooms and is a great option for budget-conscious travelers.

What to Buy in Texola: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Texola Trading Post: This historic trading post offers a variety of souvenirs and local products, including handmade crafts, jewelry, and clothing.
  • Route 66 Memorabilia: Texola is a popular stop on Route 66, and visitors can find a variety of Route 66 memorabilia, including t-shirts, hats, and postcards.

Local Culture and Customs of Texola

Texola embodies the friendly and welcoming culture of small-town America. Visitors should expect to be greeted with a smile and treated like family during their stay.

Best Time to Visit Texola

The best time to visit Texola is in the spring or fall when the weather is mild and comfortable for outdoor activities. However, Texola is a great year-round destination for travelers looking to experience small-town charm and historic attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Texola

Do:

  • Explore the town’s historic buildings and attractions.
  • Take a scenic drive on Route 66.
  • Enjoy the natural beauty of Black Kettle National Grasslands.

Don’t:

  • Forget to respect local customs and culture.
  • Leave trash behind in natural areas.
  • Speed on the town’s streets.

Safety and Health

Safety in Texola

Texola is a safe small town, but visitors should take standard safety precautions, such as locking doors and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Texola

Visitors should bring any necessary medications with them, as medical facilities in Texola are limited. Travelers should also take standard health precautions, such as washing hands regularly and staying hydrated.

Getting to Texola: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Texola

The closest major airport to Texola is Oklahoma City Will Rogers World Airport, located about 120 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Texola

The closest major bus station to Texola is in Elk City, about 20 miles away.

Other Options

Texola is a small town and does not have public transportation. Visitors should plan to rent a car or use a ride-sharing service to get around.

How to Get Around in Texola

Texola is a small town and is easy to get around on foot or by bike. Visitors who want to explore the surrounding areas should plan to rent a car or use a ride-sharing service.

Where to Go Next from Texola: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Black Mesa State Park: This state park, located about 75 miles from Texola, offers visitors a chance to hike to the highest point in Oklahoma and enjoy stunning views.
  • Route 66 Museum in Clinton: This museum, located about 30 miles from Texola, is a great place to learn more about the history of Route 66.
  • Oklahoma City: The state capital is about 120 miles from Texola and offers a variety of cultural attractions and activities.

Conclusion

Texola may be a small town, but it has a big heart and plenty to offer visitors. From historic attractions to natural beauty to friendly locals, Texola is a great destination for travelers looking for a unique and off-the-beaten-path experience.
