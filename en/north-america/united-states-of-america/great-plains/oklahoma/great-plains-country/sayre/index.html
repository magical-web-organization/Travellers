Introduction to Sayre

Sayre is a small town located in the heart of Great Plains Country, in Oklahoma, a state in the central region of the United States. With a population of under 5,000 people, Sayre is a charming, peaceful place where visitors can experience authentic Midwestern hospitality and get a glimpse into rural American life.

Despite its small size, Sayre has plenty to offer visitors who are looking for a break from the hustle and bustle of city life. From natural attractions and outdoor adventures to cultural landmarks and historical sites, there’s something for everyone in this charming town.

List of Places to Visit in Sayre

Here’s a comprehensive list of places to visit in Sayre, categorized by their respective attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sayre Pioneer Museum: This museum showcases the history of Sayre and the surrounding area, including exhibits on pioneer life, Native American culture, and the Dust Bowl era.
  • Beckham County Courthouse: Listed on the National Register of Historic Places, this beautiful courthouse was built in 1911 and features classic Renaissance revival architecture.
  • Route 66 Museum: Located just north of Sayre in nearby Clinton, this museum is dedicated to preserving the history of the iconic Route 66, which runs through Oklahoma.

Natural Attractions of Sayre: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Quartz Mountain Nature Park: This stunning natural area is located just south of Sayre and features hiking trails, rock-climbing opportunities, and breathtaking views of Lake Altus-Lugert and the Wichita Mountains.
  • Washita National Wildlife Refuge: This sprawling wildlife refuge covers over 8,000 acres and is home to a variety of animals, including eagles, deer, and bison. Visitors can hike, bird-watch, fish, and hunt in designated areas of the park.
  • Sayre City Park: This lovely park is a great spot for a picnic, a game of Frisbee, or just a leisurely stroll. It features playgrounds, picnic tables, and a walking trail that winds around the perimeter of the park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sayre

Sayre and its surrounding area offer plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to check out:

  • Quartz Mountain Nature Park Hiking Trails: With over 15 miles of hiking trails, Quartz Mountain Nature Park is a must-visit destination for hikers of all levels. The trail difficulty ranges from easy to moderate, and the views are simply stunning.
  • Sayre City Park Trail: This easy, 1-mile loop trail is perfect for a leisurely walk or bike ride. It winds through the picturesque Sayre City Park and is suitable for all ages and skill levels.
  • Washita River Trails: These hiking and biking trails run along the scenic Washita River and offer a peaceful respite from the hustle and bustle of city life.

Best Things to Do in Sayre: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for things to do in Sayre that aren’t related to the outdoors, don’t worry – there’s plenty to see and do in town. Here are some top-rated activities and tours to consider:

  • Sayre Pioneer Museum Tour: Take a guided tour of the Sayre Pioneer Museum and learn about the rich history of the town and its surrounding area.
  • Shopping in Downtown Sayre: Take a stroll down Main Street in Sayre and browse the quaint shops and boutiques for unique souvenirs and gifts.
  • Sayre Wind Energy Tour: Oklahoma is known for being a national leader in wind energy production, and Sayre is no exception. Take a guided tour of the Sayre Wind Energy Center and learn about this fascinating industry.

If you’re planning a visit to Sayre and want to make the most of your time, here’s a suggested week-long itinerary to follow:

Food and Drink Scene of Sayre

Sayre may be a small town, but it’s home to some excellent restaurants and bars. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to seek out:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken Fried Steak: A Southern classic, chicken fried steak is a must-try dish in Sayre. For the best chicken fried steak in town, head to Hoochie’s Cafe.
  • Fried Okra: Another Southern staple, fried okra is a delicious side dish that’s perfect for sharing. You can find it at many local restaurants, including the Sayre Cafe.
  • Apple Butter: Made with fresh apples and spices, apple butter is a local favorite in Sayre. You can buy it at the Sayre Farmers Market or at The Attic antique store.

Local Beverages

  • Prairie Wolf Vodka: This award-winning vodka is made right here in Oklahoma and can be found at bars and liquor stores throughout Sayre.
  • Choc Beer: Brewed in nearby Krebs, Oklahoma, Choc Beer is a local favorite and can be found at many bars and restaurants in Sayre.
  • Homemade Sweet Tea: In the South, sweet tea is practically a religion, and Sayre is no exception. Order a glass of refreshing homemade sweet tea at any local restaurant or cafe.

Where to Stay in Sayre: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Despite its small size, Sayre offers plenty of options for accommodations. Here are some top-rated hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in the area:

  • AmericInn by Wyndham Sayre: This cozy hotel offers clean, comfortable amenities and a continental breakfast for guests.
  • Redneck Yacht Club: A unique, quirky option, the Redneck Yacht Club is a hostel located in nearby Elk City. It offers affordable, no-frills accommodations for budget travelers.
  • Airbnb: There are plenty of Airbnb options in Sayre and the surrounding area, ranging from cozy cabins to spacious family homes.

What to Buy in Sayre: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

No trip to Sayre is complete without taking home a few souvenirs and local products. Here are some great options to consider:

  • Handmade Quilts: Oklahoma is known for its rich quilting tradition, and you can find beautiful handmade quilts for sale at the Sayre Farmers Market.
  • Coyote Hills Casting: This local business creates unique, handcrafted jewelry using natural and found materials. You can purchase their one-of-a-kind pieces at The Attic antique store.
  • Sayre Wind Energy Center Souvenirs: The Sayre Wind Energy Center gift shop sells souvenirs and merchandise related to the wind energy industry.

Local Culture and Customs of Sayre

Sayre is a small, tight-knit community with a strong sense of Midwestern hospitality. Visitors are sure to feel welcomed and at home in this charming town. However, it’s important to remember that Sayre is a conservative community with traditional values. Visitors should dress modestly and be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Sayre

The best time to visit Sayre is in the spring (March to May) or fall (September to November), when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter can be cold and snowy.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sayre

Sayre is generally a safe and peaceful town, but visitors should exercise caution when traveling alone or at night. Always lock your car and keep your valuables out of sight, and be aware of your surroundings when walking or biking.

Health in Sayre

There are no major health concerns in Sayre, but visitors should be aware of the risks associated with outdoor activities in the area, such as heat exhaustion, sunburn, and snake bites. Wear appropriate clothing and use sunscreen, bug spray, and other protective measures when spending time outdoors.

Getting to Sayre: Transportation Options and Logistics

Sayre is located in the central region of Oklahoma and is easily accessible by car or bus. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Sayre

  • Will Rogers World Airport (OKC): located 135 miles east of Sayre; the largest airport in Oklahoma
  • Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport (DFW): located 240 miles south of Sayre; a major hub for domestic and international flights

Bus Stations Near Sayre

  • Greyhound Bus Station: located in Elk City, just 16 miles east of Sayre
  • Jefferson Lines Bus Station: located in Clinton, 25 miles north of Sayre

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: Several major car rental companies operate in Oklahoma and can be found at airports and in major cities.
  • Private Car Service: Taxis and private car services are available in major cities and can be arranged in advance.

How to Get Around in Sayre

Sayre is a small town, and most attractions and amenities are within walking or biking distance. However, if you prefer to drive, rental cars are available in nearby cities and towns.

Where to Go Next from Sayre: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Oklahoma or the surrounding region, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Oklahoma City (135 miles): The capital city of Oklahoma, Oklahoma City offers a variety of attractions, including museums, parks, and nightlife.
  • Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge (90 miles): Located just south of Lawton, Oklahoma, the Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge is a stunning natural area that’s home to herds of bison, elk, and other wildlife.
  • Palo Duro Canyon State Park (150 miles): Located just across the border in Texas, Palo Duro Canyon State Park is known for its towering red rock formations and sweeping vistas.

Conclusion

Sayre may be a small town, but it offers plenty of attractions and activities for visitors who are looking for a break from the city. From hiking and biking to shopping and cultural landmarks, there’s something for everyone in this charming town in the heart of Great Plains Country.
