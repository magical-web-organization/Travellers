Introduction to Hobart

Hobart is a small town located in Great Plains Country, Oklahoma, that offers a variety of attractions for travelers looking to explore the region’s natural beauty, local culture, and historical significance. This hidden gem is a great destination for outdoor adventure enthusiasts, history buffs, and foodies alike.

List of Places to Visit in Hobart

When visiting Hobart, there’s plenty to see and do. Here’s a list of the top places to visit while you’re in town:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hobart is home to several museums and historical sites that are worth a visit. Here are some of the must-see attractions:

  • General Tommy Franks Museum: This museum tells the story of the inspirational life of General Tommy Franks, a retired four-star general in the United States Army and rose to fame by being the commander of the United States Central Command. Located on the campus of Frederick High School, this museum is a testament to the bravery and sacrifice of the American soldiers who served during the Gulf War.

  • Kiowa County Museum: Take a step back in time by visiting the Kiowa County Museum, which features exhibits about the region’s history and culture, including Native American artifacts, early farming equipment, and the railroad that helped shape the area.

Natural Attractions of Hobart: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those who love the great outdoors, Hobart has no shortage of natural attractions to explore. Here are some of the highlights:

  • Quartz Mountain Nature Park: This 3,000-acre park is a nature lover’s paradise, with miles of hiking trails, sparkling lakes, and stunning mountain vistas. Visitors can enjoy activities like fishing, camping, and paddle boating, as well as take in the natural beauty of the area.

  • Lugert-Altus Irrigation Lake: Another beautiful body of water, Lugert-Altus Irrigation Lake is a popular spot for fishing, boating, and water sports. The lake is surrounded by lush forests and hills, making it a picturesque spot for a picnic or hike.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hobart

There are plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore in Hobart. Here are some of the best routes to take:

  • Quartz Mountain Nature Park Trail: This four-mile hiking trail takes you through the stunning scenery of the park, including scenic lookouts and waterfalls.

  • Red Rock Canyon Trail: This 15-mile bike trail takes you through the rugged terrain of the Red Rock Canyon, with stunning views along the way.

Best Things to Do in Hobart: Activities and Tours

Aside from outdoor adventures, Hobart offers a variety of activities and tours. Here are some of the best options:

  • Kiowa Tribe Cultural Tours: Learn about the Native American heritage of the region by taking a guided tour of the Kiowa Tribe’s cultural center and museum.

  • Downtown Hobart Art Walk: Hobart’s downtown boasts a variety of art galleries and public art installations. Take a walking tour of the area to soak up the local culture and artistic offerings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hobart

To make the most of your time in Hobart, we’ve put together a week-long itinerary that includes all the best sights and activities. Here’s what your week might look like:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Hobart and visit the General Tommy Franks Museum and Welcome Center.

  • Day 2: Explore Quartz Mountain Nature Park, hiking and boating along the way.

  • Day 3: Take a guided tour of the Kiowa Tribe Cultural Center and Museum.

  • Day 4: Go biking along the Red Rock Canyon Trail.

  • Day 5: Take a scenic drive to the town of Medicine Park, known for its charming architecture and unique shops.

  • Day 6: Visit the Kiowa County Museum to learn about the region’s history and culture.

  • Day 7: Complete your trip with a leisurely stroll around downtown Hobart, taking in the public art installations and local galleries.

Food and Drink Scene of Hobart

Hobart has a thriving food and drink scene that’s worth exploring. Here’s what to try while you’re in town:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fried Catfish: This classic Southern dish is a local favorite, and you can find it on many restaurant menus around town.

  • Chickasaw Fry Bread Tacos: Made with traditional Native American fry bread, these tacos are a delicious and unique treat you won’t want to miss.

Local Beverages

  • Prairie Wolf Spirits: This local distillery creates award-winning vodka and gin, using only the finest Oklahoma grains.

Where to Stay in Hobart: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Hobart, from hotels to guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Quartz Mountain Resort: This upscale resort offers stunning views of Quartz Mountain and its amenities include a golf course, swimming pool, and spa.

  • The Downtown Hobart Guesthouse: This charming bed and breakfast is located in the heart of downtown Hobart, making it a convenient option for exploring the area.

What to Buy in Hobart: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts to bring home, you’ll find plenty of options in Hobart. Here are some of the best items to consider:

  • Native American Jewelry: The Kiowa Tribe is known for their intricate and beautiful jewelry, which makes for a unique and meaningful gift.

  • Prairie Wolf Spirits: Bring home a bottle of the award-winning vodka or gin made locally by Prairie Wolf Spirits.

Local Culture and Customs of Hobart

Hobart is a small town with a friendly, welcoming culture that values community and tradition. Visitors should be respectful of the town’s cultural heritage and traditions, including the customs of the Kiowa Tribe.

Best Time to Visit Hobart

The best time to visit Hobart is during the spring or fall when the weather is mild and comfortable for outdoor activities. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold and icy, limiting outdoor options.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hobart

Do:

  • Explore the natural beauty of Quartz Mountain Nature Park.

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Trespass on private land or disturb wildlife.

  • Ignore safety guidelines and warning signs in outdoor areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hobart

Hobart is a relatively safe town, but visitors should be cautious and aware of their surroundings, especially when exploring outdoor areas.

Health in Hobart

Hobart has a small hospital and medical center, but visitors should come prepared with any necessary medications or medical supplies.

Getting to Hobart: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hobart

The closest major airport to Hobart is Will Rogers World Airport in Oklahoma City, which is about two hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Hobart

Greyhound buses stop in nearby cities like Wichita Falls, Texas, and Lawton, Oklahoma, each about an hour away from Hobart.

Other Options

Driving to Hobart is the most convenient option, but there are also car rental options available in nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Hobart

The best way to get around Hobart is by car or bike, as the town is small and easily navigable. Walking is also a great option, particularly in downtown Hobart.

Where to Go Next from Hobart: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of the Great Plains region, consider visiting these nearby destinations:

  • Red Rock Canyon State Park: Boasting a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, camping, and rock climbing, Red Rock Canyon is just a short drive from Hobart and offers stunning natural beauty.

  • Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge: Located about an hour away from Hobart, this protected wildlife area is home to bison, elk, and other wildlife, as well as hiking and biking trails.

Conclusion

Hobart is a small town with plenty to offer visitors, from outdoor adventures to cultural attractions. Whether you’re looking to hike, bike, or simply soak up the local culture, Hobart is a hidden gem worth exploring on your next trip to Oklahoma.
