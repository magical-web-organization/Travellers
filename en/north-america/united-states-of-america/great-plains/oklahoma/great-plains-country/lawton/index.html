Introduction to Lawton

Lawton, a charming city located in Great Plains Country, Oklahoma, is a hidden gem that’s worth considering for your next vacation. It offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, history, and culture. The city is surrounded by the Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge, which is home to several outdoor activities, including rock climbing, hiking, and fishing.

List of Places to Visit in Lawton

Lawton has a lot of places to visit and things to do, and it can be overwhelming for first-time visitors. Here’s a list of some of the most popular attractions that should be on your must-visit list.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lawton is steeped in history, and it has several museums and historical places to explore.

Museum of the Great Plains

The Museum of the Great Plains offers visitors a chance to explore the exhibits that document the history of the Great Plains. It also has a planetarium that showcases the wonders of the universe.

Fort Sill National Historic Landmark and Museum

Fort Sill was founded in 1869 and was used as a military installation during the Indian Wars. The museum showcases the history of the fort and its role in US History.

Natural Attractions of Lawton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge

The Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge, located just outside of Lawton, is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts. It offers opportunities for hiking, rock climbing, fishing, and wildlife viewing. Be sure to check out the bison herd that calls the refuge home.

Lake Lawtonka

Lake Lawtonka is located just minutes from Lawton and offers visitors a chance to enjoy water-based activities like boating, kayaking, and fishing. There are also several hiking trails around the lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lawton

Lawton has several hiking and biking trails that offer scenic routes for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are a few trails to explore:

Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge Trails

The Wildlife Refuge has several hiking trails that range from easy to difficult. The Bison Trail and Elk Mountain Trail are the most popular.

Lake Elmer Thomas Recreation Area Trail

This trail is a 6.6-mile loop that goes around Lake Elmer Thomas and offers hikers and bikers panoramic views of the lake and the Wichita Mountains.

Best Things to Do in Lawton: Activities and Tours

Historic Trolley Tours

The Historic Trolley Tours take visitors on an informative ride through the historic district of Lawton. The tour guides share interesting facts about the city’s history and the landmarks that make up the district.

Rock Climbing

The Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge is a popular destination for rock climbers thanks to its unique geological features that offer challenging climbs for climbers of different experience levels.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lawton

For those planning an extended stay in Lawton, here’s a week-long itinerary that covers some of the city’s best attractions.

  • Day 1: Explore the Museum of the Great Plains and visit the planetarium
  • Day 2: Visit Fort Sill National Historic Landmark and Museum
  • Day 3: Hike the Bison Trail in the Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge
  • Day 4: Take a scenic drive through the Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge
  • Day 5: Rock climb in the Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge
  • Day 6: Enjoy water-based activities at Lake Lawtonka
  • Day 7: Take a Historic Trolley Tour of the city

Local Culture and Customs of Lawton

Lawton has a rich history and culture that’s worth exploring. Here are some interesting facts about the city’s customs:

  • The Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge is home to several Native American tribes. Visitors can learn about the tribes through the artwork displayed at the Visitors Center.
  • Lawton’s public art program encourages local artists to display their work throughout the city.

Best Time to Visit Lawton

The best time to visit Lawton is in the spring and fall. The weather is pleasant, and the crowds are thinner.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lawton

Do:

  • Respect the natural beauty of the Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge.
  • Explore the city’s historic district.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public spaces.
  • Take photos of Native American tribes without permission.
  • Trespass on private property.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lawton

Lawton is generally safe, but travelers should take the usual precautions when traveling.

Health in Lawton

Visitors to Lawton should ensure they are up to date on routine vaccinations, as well as the flu vaccine during flu season.

Getting to Lawton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lawton

The closest airport to Lawton is Lawton-Fort Sill Regional Airport.

Bus Stations Near Lawton

The Greyhound Bus Station is located in Lawton.

Other Options

Lawton is easily accessible by car, and visitors can rent a car from major car rental companies.

How to Get Around in Lawton

Lawton is a compact city, and most attractions are within walking distance. Taxis and car rental services are also available for those who want to explore at their own pace.

Conclusion
