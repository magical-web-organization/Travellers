Introduction to Erick

Located in the heart of Great Plains Country, Erick is a hidden gem of Oklahoma that offers visitors a chance to experience the region’s unique culture and natural beauty. With a population of less than 1,000, Erick is a small town with big personality. As you explore its streets, you’ll quickly discover its rich history, lively arts scene, and friendly locals.

List of Places to Visit in Erick

Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Erick has something for everyone. Here is a list of must-see attractions in and around town:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Roger Miller Museum: This museum celebrates the life and music of legendary country singer Roger Miller, who grew up in Erick. Learn about his rise to fame and see artifacts from his career, including his Grammy award.
  • Washita Battlefield National Historic Site: Located just outside of town, this site commemorates the Battle of Washita, a key event in the history of the Cheyenne and Arapaho tribes and their interactions with the US Army.
  • Old Town Museum: Discover life in the early 1900s through this living history museum. You’ll see authentic buildings and artifacts from the town’s past, including a schoolhouse, saloon, and blacksmith’s shop.

Natural Attractions of Erick: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Quartz Mountain Nature Park: Just a short drive from Erick, this park offers hiking, fishing, and boating opportunities against the stunning backdrop of Quartz Mountain.
  • Little Sahara State Park: For an adrenaline-filled adventure, head to this park to experience the thrill of sandboarding and off-roading through the picturesque sand dunes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Erick

Erick is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of options for hiking and biking. Here are some top routes to explore:

  • Washita River Trail: This scenic trail follows the winding path of the Washita River, offering stunning views of the surrounding plains and bluffs.
  • Quartz Mountain Trail: Located in Quartz Mountain Nature Park, this trail offers a challenging hike with rewarding vistas of the park’s unique landscape.
  • Black Kettle National Grassland: With over 30 miles of trails, this grassland offers a variety of options for hiking, biking, and horseback riding.

Best Things to Do in Erick: Activities and Tours

  • Route 66 Roadside Attraction Tour: Take a tour of Erick’s quirky and colorful roadside attractions, including the world’s largest totem pole and the sandhills pyramids.
  • Rockin’ Route 66 Festival: This annual festival celebrates Erick’s connection to the Mother Road with live music, classic car shows, and food vendors.
  • Cheyenne & Arapaho Filmmakers Short Film Festival: Held each November at the Quartz Mountain Nature Park, this festival celebrates Native American filmmaking and culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Erick

Day 1: Visit the Roger Miller Museum and explore Erick’s Main Street. Day 2: See the historic Washita Battlefield and hit the trails at Quartz Mountain Nature Park. Day 3: Take a day trip to the Little Sahara Sand Dunes and try sandboarding or off-roading. Day 4: Tour the Old Town Museum and enjoy local cuisine at the nearby eateries. Day 5: Explore the Black Kettle National Grassland and hike one of its scenic trails. Day 6: Take in the local arts scene with a visit to the Sayre Theatre and Gallery, or catch a live performance at the Rodeo Opry. Day 7: Relax and unwind with a day at the Red Rock Canyon State Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Erick

No trip to Erick is complete without trying the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken fried steak: A classic Oklahoma comfort food, this dish features a tenderized steak coated in seasoned flour and fried until golden brown.
  • Fried catfish: Fresh from the nearby Washita River, this crispy and flavorful dish is a favorite among locals.
  • Indian taco: A unique twist on a classic dish, this taco features traditional taco toppings served on fry bread, a staple of Native American cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Choc Beer: A local favorite, this beer is brewed in nearby Krebs and is named after the Choc family, who have been brewing beer for over 100 years.
  • Red Dirt Red: This award-winning wine is made from grapes grown in Oklahoma and is known for its rich, full-bodied flavor.
  • Sweet tea: A Southern staple, this refreshing beverage is a must-try while in Oklahoma.

Where to Stay in Erick: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

While Erick is a small town, there are still plenty of options for where to stay. From cozy cabins to budget-friendly motels, here are some top choices:

  • Quartz Mountain Resort: Treat yourself to a luxurious stay at this resort, which features a golf course, spa, and stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Green Garage Cabins: These charming cabins are located near Erick’s Main Street and offer a cozy and quiet retreat.
  • Sunset Motel: This vintage-style motel is a budget-friendly option with a retro feel.

What to Buy in Erick: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to gift shopping, Erick has plenty of options for unique and locally made souvenirs. Here are some top picks:

  • Route 66 memorabilia: Pick up a classic Route 66 sign or magnet, or get creative with a handmade piece of jewelry or art.
  • Native American crafts: Erick is located near several tribes, including the Cheyenne and Arapaho, and showcases a vibrant local Indigenous culture.
  • Choc beer: Bring a taste of Oklahoma home with you by picking up a six-pack of the local brew.

Local Culture and Customs of Erick

Erick is proud of its rich history and unique culture. When visiting, remember to:

  • Respect Indigenous customs and traditions, including giving thanks for the land and water.
  • Take time to explore the local arts scene, including music, theater, and visual arts.
  • Be friendly and open to conversation with locals, who are known for their warm hospitality.

Best Time to Visit Erick

Erick is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the scenery is at its most beautiful. Summer can be hot and humid, while winter can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Erick

Do:

  • Take advantage of the many outdoor adventures that Erick offers, including hiking, fishing, and sandboarding.
  • Try the local cuisine, from chicken fried steak to Indian tacos.
  • Explore the local arts and culture scene, including the Roger Miller Museum and the Sayre Theatre and Gallery.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or disrespect natural sites and areas.
  • Don’t underestimate the weather – come prepared for hot summers and cold winters.
  • Don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation with the friendly locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Erick

Erick is a safe town with a low crime rate. However, it’s always important to take precautions when traveling, such as:

  • Storing valuables safely in your accommodation
  • Being aware of your surroundings when exploring the town at night

Health in Erick

Erick has a small hospital, as well as several nearby medical clinics. It’s important to have travel insurance and consult a healthcare provider before traveling.

Getting to Erick: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Erick

The closest major airport to Erick is Will Rogers World Airport (OKC), located approximately 130 miles away in Oklahoma City.

Bus Stations Near Erick

Greyhound Bus Lines has a station located in Elk City, just 18 miles from Erick.

Other Options

A rental car is the best option for getting around Erick and exploring the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Erick

Erick is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. A rental car or taxi may be necessary for day trips to nearby parks and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Erick: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Oklahoma City: Just a few hours’ drive from Erick, this bustling city offers a vibrant arts scene, historic landmarks, and delicious cuisine.
  • Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge: Located in nearby Lawton, this refuge offers hiking, wildlife viewing, and stunning views of the mountains.
  • Route 66: Follow the iconic Mother Road to see more of small-town America, from New Mexico to Illinois.

Conclusion

Erick, Oklahoma may be a small town, but it offers big adventures and unique experiences for travelers. From its rich history and vibrant arts scene to its beautiful natural landscapes and friendly locals, Erick is a hidden gem of the Great Plains Country that is waiting to be discovered.
