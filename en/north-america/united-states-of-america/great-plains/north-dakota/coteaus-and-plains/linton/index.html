Introduction to Linton

Nestled in the heart of North Dakota’s Great Plains, Linton is a charming town full of natural beauty and rich history. With a population of just over 1,000 people, this small town packs a big punch when it comes to attractions and activities. From outdoor adventures to cultural experiences, there’s something for everyone in Linton.

List of Places to Visit in Linton

If you’re planning a trip to Linton, here are some of the top places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Emmons County Museum: This museum offers a glimpse into the history and culture of Emmons County, which includes Linton. Check out the exhibits and displays showcasing everything from Native American artifacts to farming equipment.

  • Strasburg Heritage Association’s Pioneer Hjemkomst Interpretive Center: Just a short drive from Linton, this museum features exhibits that showcase the lives of the German-Russian pioneers who settled in the area.

  • Fort Abraham Lincoln State Park: This historic site brings visitors back to the days of the Great Plains Indian Wars. Explore the reconstructed buildings and learn about the frontier life of soldiers and civilians.

Natural Attractions of Linton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Beaver Lake State Park: Located just outside of Linton, this state park boasts a 968-acre lake and miles of hiking and biking trails. Fishing, swimming, and boating are also popular activities at this scenic spot.

  • Medicine Rock State Historic Site: This natural attraction features unique rock formations that were once used by Native Americans for spiritual purposes.

  • The Badlands: Although it’s a bit of a drive to get there, the Badlands are well worth the trip. Witness towering rock formations, sweeping prairie landscapes, and breathtaking views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Linton

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, Linton has a lot to offer. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in the area include:

  • The Beaver Lake Nature Trail: This 1.5-mile loop trail winds through a variety of habitats, including wooded areas, prairies, and wetlands.

  • The Backway Trail: This popular hiking and biking trail follows the route of an old railroad, offering stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  • The Glacial Lake Trail: This trail spans over 7 miles and passes through scenic woodlands and prairies.

Best Things to Do in Linton: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other fun activities to enjoy in Linton. Some top picks include:

  • Fishing: With multiple lakes and ponds in the area, Linton is a great destination for anglers. Common fish species include walleye, pike, bass, and panfish.

  • Golfing: Linton Golf Course is a beautiful 9-hole course that’s perfect for golfers of all skill levels.

  • Tours: For a unique experience, consider taking a tour of a local farm or ranch. You’ll learn about the agriculture industry and see how the locals live.

Week-Long Itinerary for Linton

Here’s an example itinerary for a week-long trip to Linton:

1 Explore Linton’s downtown area and visit the Emmons County Museum

6 Play a round of golf at Linton Golf Course

Food and Drink Scene of Linton

No trip to Linton would be complete without trying some of the delicious local cuisine. Some must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Knoephla Soup: This creamy soup is a North Dakota staple, made with dumplings and potatoes.

  • German Sausage: Linton is known for its German-Russian heritage, and one of the most popular dishes is German sausage. Try it grilled or smoked for a hearty meal.

  • Rhubarb dessert: Linton is home to some of the best rhubarb desserts around. Try a slice of rhubarb pie or crisp for a sweet treat.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: North Dakota is home to a growing craft beer scene, and Linton is no exception. Check out local breweries like Stonehome Brewing Company or Linton Beer Store and Tap Room for a taste of the region.

  • Prairie Winds Wine: Made from North Dakota-grown grapes, Prairie Winds wine is a must-try for wine lovers.

Where to Stay in Linton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

While Linton is a small town, there are still plenty of options when it comes to accommodations. Some top picks include:

  • Linton Motor Inn: This cozy motel offers comfortable rooms and easy access to downtown Linton.

  • The First and Last Chance: This charming bed and breakfast is located just a short drive from Linton and features comfortable, antique-decorated rooms.

  • Campgrounds: If you prefer to stay close to nature, consider camping at Beaver Lake State Park or other nearby campsites.

What to Buy in Linton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a unique souvenir to bring home from Linton? Consider these local products:

  • German sausage: As mentioned before, Linton is known for its delicious German sausage. Pick up a package to bring home at one of the local meat markets.

  • Prairie Winds Wine: If you’re a wine lover, be sure to bring home a bottle (or two) of local Prairie Winds wine.

  • Native American Crafts: Linton is located in an area with a rich Native American history. Browse the local shops for unique Native American crafts and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Linton

North Dakota has a strong rural culture, and Linton is no exception. Visitors to Linton can expect to experience traditional Midwest hospitality and friendly locals. The area is also home to a significant German-Russian population, so visitors may notice this influence throughout the community.

Best Time to Visit Linton

The best time to visit Linton depends on what you’re interested in. If outdoor activities are your priority, consider visiting in the summer or early fall when temperatures are mild and the foliage is beautiful. If you’re interested in local festivals and events, plan your visit around Oktoberfest in September or Linton’s Independence Day celebration in July.

Do’s and Don’ts of Linton

Do:

  • Be respectful of the locals and their rural way of life.
  • Try some of the local cuisine, including German sausage and Knoephla soup.
  • Explore the natural beauty of the area and take advantage of the hiking and biking trails.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter in the parks or natural areas.
  • Assume that everyone in the area is of German-Russian heritage.
  • Drive off designated roads or trails.

Safety and Health

Safety in Linton

Linton is a safe and friendly community, but visitors should always take precautions when exploring natural areas. Be sure to bring plenty of water and wear comfortable clothing and footwear when hiking or biking. Leave valuables locked up in your hotel room or car and be aware of your surroundings at all times.

Health in Linton

The area around Linton is relatively rural, so visitors should be prepared to travel to nearby towns or cities for medical care if necessary. Be sure to bring any necessary medications or medical supplies with you on your trip.

Getting to Linton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Linton is a bit off the beaten path, but there are still several transportation options available.

Airports Near Linton

The nearest airport to Linton is the Bismarck Municipal Airport, which is about 100 miles away. From there, visitors can rent a car or take a shuttle to Linton.

Bus Stations Near Linton

There are no major bus stations near Linton, but visitors can take a bus to nearby cities like Bismarck and rent a car from there.

Other Options

Driving is the most popular way to get to Linton. The town is located just off of Highway 83, which runs north to south through the state.

How to Get Around in Linton

The best way to get around in Linton is by car. While the town is small enough to walk around, visitors will need a car to explore the surrounding areas and nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Linton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of North Dakota, here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Bismarck: North Dakota’s capital city is just a two-hour drive from Linton and features a variety of attractions, including museums, parks, and restaurants.

  • Fargo: Located about four hours from Linton, Fargo is another great option for visitors looking for a taste of North Dakota city life.

  • Theodore Roosevelt National Park: This scenic national park is located just a few hours west of Linton and boasts stunning badlands scenery and a variety of hiking trails.

Conclusion

Linton is a hidden gem in North Dakota’s Great Plains region. From natural attractions to cultural experiences, there’s something for everyone in this charming small town. Whether you’re looking to explore the great outdoors or immerse yourself in local culture, Linton won’t disappoint.
