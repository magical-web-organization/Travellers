Introduction to Steele

Welcome to Steele, a charming town located in the heart of North Dakota. Situated in Coteaus and Plains, in the Great Plains of the United States of America, Steele is known for its scenic beauty, rich history, and friendly locals. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or simply a relaxing getaway, Steele has something for everyone. In this guide, we’ll take you on a journey through the best of Steele, from top attractions to hidden gems and insider tips.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Steele is home to several museums and historical places that offer a glimpse into the town’s rich past and cultural heritage. The Kidder County Historical Society and Museum is a must-visit attraction that features exhibits on the history of Kidder County, including the Native American era, settlement, agriculture, and transportation. Another popular attraction is the Double Ditch Indian Village, an archaeological site that showcases the remnants of a Mandan Indian village dating back to the 1500s. Be sure to also visit the Fort Clark State Historic Site, which was once a key military outpost during the 19th century.

Natural Attractions of Steele: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Steele is surrounded by breathtaking natural beauty, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The Harmon Lake Recreation Area is a popular spot for boating, fishing, and swimming, and offers scenic trails for hiking and biking. For those looking for a less strenuous activity, Village Pointe Commons Park and Playground is perfect for a picnic or casual walk. The Cottonwood Golf Course is an excellent destination for golfers, and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Steele

Steele offers a variety of hiking and biking trails that are perfect for both novice and experienced outdoor enthusiasts. Some popular routes include the Harmon Lake Nature Trail, which is a 1.8-mile loop that offers scenic views of the lake and surrounding woods, and the Steele-Sand Creek Trail, which is a 6.7-mile trail that runs between Steele and Dawson. The Heritage Park Trail is another popular destination, with its 1.5-mile loop that winds through the town’s historic district.

Best Things to Do in Steele: Activities and Tours

Steele offers a variety of activities and tours that cater to different interests. The McClusky Canal Boat Club provides opportunities to boat on a historic canal, while the Steele Community Pool and Park offers a perfect way to beat the summer heat. If you’re interested in agricultural history, take a tour of the Beans Elevator and learn about the important role the railroad played in Steele’s history.

Food and Drink Scene of Steele

Steele has a variety of dining options that cater to different tastes and budgets. Some must-try dishes include the local specialty - kuchen, a German pastry typically filled with fruit or custard, and the North Dakota staple - knoephla soup, a thick, creamy soup made with potatoes, dumplings and chicken. The local cafes and diners are perfect for a hearty breakfast or lunch, and the town also has a few upscale dining options for a romantic dinner.

Where to Stay in Steele: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Steele offers a variety of lodging options that cater to different budgets and preferences. There are several hotels and motels in the town, as well as guesthouses and private rentals. For those looking for a more rustic experience, there are also camping and RV options available at the Harmon Lake Recreation Area.

What to Buy in Steele: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Steele has several unique shops that offer a variety of souvenirs and local products. The Kidder County Market offers a selection of locally made crafts and gifts, while the Beans Elevator is a great place to find antique items and collectibles. For those interested in agriculture, there are several stores that sell locally sourced produce and meats.

Local Culture and Customs of Steele

Steele is a friendly and welcoming town, and visitors are encouraged to explore the area and interact with the locals. North Dakota has a strong agricultural heritage, and the town celebrates this with annual events such as the Kidder County Fair and the Steele Watermelon Festival. Visitors should also be aware of local customs, such as removing shoes when entering someone’s home, and tipping at restaurants and cafes.

Best Time to Visit Steele

Steele can be visited year-round, but the best time to visit is during the summer months when the weather is mild and outdoor activities are in full swing. The fall season also offers beautiful foliage and cooler temperatures, while the winter season provides ample opportunities for snow-based activities.

Safety and Health

Steele is a safe and friendly destination, but visitors should take precautions to stay healthy and safe during their stay.

Safety in Steele: Visitors should be aware of the local weather conditions, especially during the winter when snow and ice can make driving and outdoor activities hazardous. Visitors should also take precautions when hiking or biking on unfamiliar trails.

Health in Steele: Visitors should bring any necessary medications and be aware of any allergies they may have. The town has a medical clinic for emergencies, but visitors should make sure they have health insurance that covers them in North Dakota.

Getting to Steele: Transportation Options and Logistics

Steele is easily accessible by road, rail, and air.

Airports Near Steele: The closest major airport is in Bismarck, which is about 60 miles away. There are also smaller regional airports in nearby towns such as Jamestown and Minot.

Bus Stations Near Steele: There are several bus stations in nearby towns such as Jamestown and Valley City.

Other Options: Visitors can also reach Steele by car, with major highways such as I-94 and US-83 passing through the town.

Steele is a small town and can be easily explored on foot or by bicycle. Rental cars are also available for those who want to explore the surrounding areas.
