Introduction to Bismarck

Bismarck is the capital city of North Dakota, located along the eastern bank of the Missouri River. The city boasts of a vibrant history that dates back to the Native American tribes that once roamed the Great Plains. Visitors to Bismarck will find a perfect blend of modernity and old-world ambiance, with numerous outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and a lively food scene. Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or simply seeking an exciting destination with plenty of attractions, Bismarck is a perfect choice.

List of Places to Visit in Bismarck

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bismarck is home to several museums and historical sites that highlight its unique history, culture, and heritage. These include:

  • North Dakota Heritage Center and State Museum: offers a rich display of exhibits that explore the state’s geology, paleontology, anthropology, and more.
  • Former Governor’s Mansion: a 19th-century mansion showcases Victorian architecture and the history of the state’s governors.
  • Camp Hancock State Historic Site: a restored military post built-in 1872 and provides insight into the frontier army barracks.
  • Bismarck Art and Galleries Association: provides art exhibitions, live music, and performing arts in the historic Hirsch building.

Natural Attractions of Bismarck: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bismarck is surrounded by stunning natural beauty, making it a haven for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some must-visit nature and outdoor attractions in Bismarck:

  • Fort Abraham Lincoln State Park: the park comprises trails, camping sites, and a reconstructed village of the Mandan Indian Tribe and the former home of Lt. Col. George Armstrong Custer.
  • Dakota Zoo: provides a fun and educational experience for visitors, featuring over 600 animals from more than 125 species.
  • Missouri River: allows visitors to enjoy various recreational activities, such as canoeing, kayaking, and fishing.
  • Sertoma Park: a park offers walking trails, picnic areas, and a Japanese garden.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bismarck

Bismarck offers several hiking and biking trails that provide breathtaking views of the city and its surrounding areas. Some popular trails include:

  • Bismarck Trail: a multi-use trail that stretches 13 miles, from Riverwood Golf Course to the State Capitol Grounds.
  • Riverfront Bike Trail: a paved trail along the Missouri River, providing spectacular views of the river and its surrounding wildlife.
  • Huff Hills Trail: an excellent mountain biking trail located in Mandan’s rugged terrain, offering a challenging ride.

Best Things to Do in Bismarck: Activities and Tours

Bismarck offers numerous activities and tours for visitors of all ages, including:

  • Gateway to Science: provides hands-on experiences, interactive exhibits, and events that are ideal for families with children.
  • Bismarck Event Center: hosts concerts, theatrical performances, and other cultural events throughout the year, making it a great place to catch a show.
  • Lewis and Clark Riverboat: a paddle wheeler boat that offers guided tours of the Missouri River, showcasing the scenic views of the waterway and the wildlife it supports.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bismarck

Visiting Bismarck for a week gives you the opportunity to explore the city’s numerous attractions, including:

  • Day 1-2: Visit cultural destinations such as the North Dakota Heritage Center, Fort Abraham Lincoln State Park, and the Former Governor’s Mansion.
  • Day 3-4: Enjoy outdoor adventures at Riverfront Bike Trail, Missouri River, and Sertoma Park.
  • Day 5-6: Embark on group tours such as Gateway to Science and Lewis and Clark Riverboat.
  • Day 7: Dine at one of the city’s premier restaurants, shop for souvenirs, and enjoy a night out at a local bar.

Food and Drink Scene of Bismarck

Must-Try Dishes

Bismarck’s culinary scene reflects its rich agricultural heritage and multicultural history. Here are some of the top must-try dishes:

  • Knoephla Soup: a creamy chicken and dumpling soup that is a North Dakota staple.
  • Bison Burger: a juicy burger made from locally raised bison meat - a healthier and tastier alternative to beef.
  • Fleischkuechle: a savory meat turnover with a mix of ground beef, onions, and spices found at the annual German Russian Food Festival.

Local Beverages

Bismarck is home to several breweries and wineries renowned for their handcrafted beverages, including:

  • Laughing Sun Brewing Co: offers a wide range of unique craft beers made from locally sourced ingredients.
  • 4e Winery: produces small-batch wines from fruits and berries grown within North Dakota farms.

Where to Stay in Bismarck: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bismarck provides a range of accommodation options to meet every traveler’s needs. Some top-rated options include:

  • Radisson Hotel Bismarck: provides a luxurious stay with stunning views of the Missouri River and the State Capitol.
  • My Place Hotel: features modern and spacious rooms, a kitchenette, and laundry facilities, perfect for extended stays.
  • MainStay Suites: offers free Wi-Fi and a complimentary breakfast buffet, ideal for budget-conscious travelers.

What to Buy in Bismarck: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bismarck abounds in locally made gifts and souvenirs, including:

  • Prairie Rose Meadery: supplies mead produced from North Dakota honey, fermented and aged to perfection.
  • Prairie Fire Pottery: produces handcrafted stoneware pottery and is located in the historic Belle Mehus Auditorium building downtown.

Local Culture and Customs of Bismarck

North Dakota’s cultural and social norms are conservative. Visitors are advised to dress modestly and behave graciously and fit into the local community.

Best Time to Visit Bismarck

The best time to visit Bismarck is during spring and fall when the weather is mild and pleasant, ideal for outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bismarck

Bismarck is a safe city, with a low crime rate. Visitors are advised to take normal precautionary measures such as not leaving valuables unattended, locking doors and windows, and using ATM machines from reputable banks or locations.

Health in Bismarck

North Dakota state provides excellent healthcare systems, and Bismarck has many healthcare facilities. However, visitors are required to have health insurance coverage before leaving their home country.

Getting to Bismarck: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bismarck is accessible by road, air, and bus transportation modes.

Airports Near Bismarck

  • Bismarck Municipal Airport (BIS)
  • Hector International Airport (FAR)
  • Minot International Airport (MOT)

Bus Stations Near Bismarck

  • Greyhound Bus Lines
  • Jefferson Lines

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Cars are available for rent from major car rental agencies at major airports near Bismarck.
  • Taxi and Shuttle Services: Several businesses provide taxi and shuttle services to and from Bismarck.

How to Get Around in Bismarck

Bismarck is a relatively small city that is easy to navigate via the city’s bus service, taxi, or rideshare services like Uber.

Where to Go Next from Bismarck: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Several destinations near Bismarck are worth exploring, including:

  • Theodore Roosevelt National Park: A vast park located in the North Dakota Badlands, providing stunning scenery of rugged terrain, prairies, rivers, and wildlife.
  • Medora: A charming town offering beautiful views, local restaurants, fantastic shopping, and musical theatre.
  • Fargo: The largest city in North Dakota, providing extensive opportunities for shopping, dining, and nightlife.

Conclusion

Bismarck is an excellent destination that offers visitors a unique mix of culture, cuisine, and outdoor activities. Whether you’re looking for an authentic cultural experience, outdoor adventures or a fun family vacation, Bismarck is an ideal place to visit. With this in-depth travel guide, you will be well-equipped to make the most of your trip to Bismarck.
