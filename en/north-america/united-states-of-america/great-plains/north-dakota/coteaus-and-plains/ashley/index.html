Introduction to Ashley

Nestled in the heart of Great Plains, Ashley is a charming town in North Dakota known for its rich cultural heritage, scenic beauty, and friendly locals. This hidden gem of the Midwest is fast becoming a popular destination among nature lovers, history buffs, and adventure seekers alike. Whether you’re planning a romantic getaway, a family vacation, or a solo trip, Ashley has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Ashley

Here’s a list of must-see attractions in Ashley that will leave you spellbound:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. McIntosh County Heritage Center: This museum showcases the history and culture of the German-Russian settlers who founded Ashley in the late 19th century. Explore the exhibits and rare artifacts that transport you back in time.

  2. Ashley Depot: Built in 1910, this historic train station has been painstakingly restored to its former glory. Take a guided tour and learn more about its intriguing past and importance in the town’s growth.

  3. St. Mary’s Church: A beautiful example of Gothic Revival architecture, this church is a testament to the enduring faith and close-knit community of Ashley’s residents.

Natural Attractions of Ashley: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Ashley Lake: This breathtakingly beautiful lake is perfect for fishing, kayaking, and hiking. Take a stroll along its tranquil shores and soak in the serene ambiance.

  2. Long Lake National Wildlife Refuge: Experience the wonder of nature at this scenic sanctuary, where you can spot rare migratory birds, wild animals, and diverse vegetation.

  3. Ashley Country Club: Tee off at this 9-hole golf course surrounded by pristine greenery and rolling hills.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ashley

If you’re looking for some outdoor adventure, Ashley offers an abundance of hiking and biking trails that cater to all skill levels. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Coalmine Trail: A 2.5-mile loop that takes you through lush forests and open prairies.
  • Birding Trail: A 2-mile trail that allows you to observe rare bird species up close.
  • Post Office Hill Hike: A moderate 1-mile hike that leads to stunning panoramic views of the town and surrounding countryside.
  • Ft. Ransom State Park Trail: An 11-mile hike that showcases the rugged beauty of North Dakota’s wilderness.

Best Things to Do in Ashley: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, Ashley offers a range of other fun activities and tours that you don’t want to miss:

  • Fishing in Ashley Lake: Catch trout, bass, and walleye in the crystal-clear waters of this picturesque lake.
  • Hunting: Venture into the wilds and experience the thrill of hunting pheasants, deer, and other game animals.
  • McIntosh County Fair: Join the locals and celebrate the rich agricultural heritage of Ashley with live music, food, and livestock exhibits.
  • Prairie Pioneer Days: An annual celebration of Ashley’s pioneer history featuring live reenactments, old-fashioned games, and a parade.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ashley

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Ashley:

Food and Drink Scene of Ashley

No trip to Ashley is complete without sampling its mouth-watering local cuisine and drink. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Knoephla Soup: A hearty soup made with dumplings, bacon, and vegetables.
  • Chislic: A popular dish made of grilled cubes of lamb or beef, served with a side of garlic bread.
  • Fleischkuekle: A savory pastry filled with ground beef or pork, onions, and spices.
  • Kuchen: A scrumptious cake-like pastry with a sweet filling of fruit or custard, dusted with powdered sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Schell’s Beer: This award-winning brewery in nearby New Ulm produces a range of flavorful beers that pair well with almost any dish.
  • Prairie Rose Meadery: Taste the nectar of the gods at this family-owned meadery that uses locally-sourced honey and fruits to make their fine meads.

Where to Stay in Ashley: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ashley offers a variety of accommodation options that cater to all budgets and preferences. Here are some suggestions:

  • Country View Lodge: A cozy lodge located on the outskirts of town, with stunning views of the countryside.
  • Ashley Motel: A budget-friendly motel with clean and comfortable rooms, ideal for families and solo travelers.
  • Ketterling Country House: A charming guesthouse with tastefully decorated rooms, a fully-equipped kitchen, and a garden.

What to Buy in Ashley: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Ashley with you by purchasing some of these unique souvenirs and local products:

  • Ashley T-shirts and caps: Show your love for Ashley with these stylish and affordable accessories.
  • German-Russian cookbooks: Learn how to make traditional German-Russian dishes with these authentic cookbooks.
  • Artisanal honey and jams: Made from the finest locally-grown fruits and honey, these jams and honey make for great gifts.
  • Hand-crafted pottery and jewelry: Browse through the work of local artists and buy some unique hand-crafted pottery or jewelry as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Ashley

Ashley has a rich cultural heritage that reflects the traditions and customs of its German-Russian ancestors. The locals take pride in their strong community spirit, love for the outdoors, and culinary prowess. You’ll notice that people greet each other with warm smiles and a friendly wave, and are always willing to strike up a conversation about the town’s history or offer tips on local attractions.

Best Time to Visit Ashley

The best time to visit Ashley is during the spring and summer months, from April to September. The weather during this time is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities such as hiking, fishing, and golfing. The fall season, from October to November, is another great time to visit, as the foliage transforms into a stunning tapestry of golden and red hues. The winter months, from December to March, can be bitterly cold, so it’s best to come prepared with warm clothing and gear if you plan to visit during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ashley

Do:

  • Be respectful of the locals’ customs and traditions.
  • Explore the outdoors and take in the scenic beauty.
  • Try the local cuisine and beer.
  • Attend the town’s cultural events and festivals.

Don’t:

  • litter or damage the natural environment.
  • disregard posted signs and regulations while hiking or fishing.
  • criticize the locals or their way of life.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ashley

Ashley is a safe and friendly town with a low crime rate. However, it’s always advisable to take necessary precautions, such as locking your doors and avoiding unfamiliar areas at night.

Health in Ashley

Ashley has a reputable healthcare system, with a local hospital and medical clinic. It’s always recommended to have travel insurance and any necessary medications on hand in case of emergencies.

Getting to Ashley: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ashley is easily accessible by road and air. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Ashley

  • Bismarck Airport (BIS): Located approximately 100 miles west of Ashley, BIS is the closest major airport.

Bus Stations Near Ashley

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: The nearest bus station is in Aberdeen, SD, about 70 miles southeast of Ashley.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car is a popular option for travelers who prefer the flexibility and freedom to explore the region at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Ashley

Ashley is a small town that can be explored easily on foot or bicycle. Car rental is another option for those who want to venture further afield.

Where to Go Next from Ashley: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can explore after Ashley:

  • Fort Totten State Historic Site: This former military fort located in Fort Totten, ND, offers a glimpse into the region’s rich military history and culture.
  • Lake Sakakawea: North Dakota’s largest lake offers a wealth of outdoor activities such as fishing, boating, and hiking, and is located approximately 120 miles west of Ashley.
  • Fargo: This bustling city offers a vibrant arts and culture scene, excellent dining and entertainment options, and is located approximately 160 miles north of Ashley.

Conclusion

Ashley is a hidden gem that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty that make for an unforgettable travel experience. Whether you’re seeking adventure in the outdoors, soaking in the local culture, or simply enjoying some downtime with loved ones, Ashley has something to offer for everyone. So pack your bags, book your trip, and get ready to discover all that this charming town has to offer!
