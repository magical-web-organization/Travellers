Introduction to Strasburg

Nestled in the heart of North Dakota’s Great Plains region, Strasburg is a charming small town known for its welcoming locals, rich history, and gorgeous natural scenery. Founded by German immigrants in the late 19th century, Strasburg retains much of its Old World charm to this day. From exploring historical attractions to hiking scenic trails, there’s no shortage of ways to fill your days in this delightful destination.

List of Places to Visit in Strasburg

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Strasburg is home to a wealth of historical and cultural attractions that are sure to fascinate visitors of all ages. Here are some of the top places to check out:

  • Emmons County Museum: This fantastic museum is dedicated to preserving the history of Emmons County, and features exhibits covering everything from pioneer life to local agriculture.
  • Sts. Peter and Paul Catholic Church: This stunning church is a must-visit for architecture and history buffs alike. The church’s interior is adorned with beautiful stained glass windows, murals, and a hand-carved altar.
  • Guten Tag Haus Museum: Fun fact: Strasburg is known as the “Cradle of the Germans from Russia” due to its large population of German immigrants. The Guten Tag Haus Museum celebrates this heritage with exhibits on local history and German culture.
  • Old Overland Trail Historic Marker: This marker commemorates the Overland Trail, which once connected Fort Snelling in Minnesota to Fort Pierre in South Dakota. Interpretive signs explain the trail’s significance and history.

Natural Attractions of Strasburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re looking to get outside and soak up some natural beauty during your visit to Strasburg, you’re in luck. The town and surrounding areas offer plenty of opportunities for hiking, birdwatching, and more. Here are some of the top natural attractions to see:

  • Sand Lake National Wildlife Refuge: Located just a short drive from Strasburg, this beautiful refuge is a haven for migratory birds and other wildlife. Visitors can hike one of the refuge’s many trails, or simply relax and take in the stunning vistas.
  • Bismarck Lake: Take a dip or enjoy a picnic at this lovely lake, which is just a short drive from Strasburg.
  • Turtle Mountain State Forest: Located about three hours north of Strasburg, this massive forest is a must-visit for nature lovers. Visitors can hike, bike, and even cross-country ski on the forest’s trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Strasburg

The natural beauty of Strasburg and its surrounding areas makes it an ideal destination for hikers and bikers. Here are some of the top routes to check out during your visit:

  • Heart River State Trail: This 20-mile trail begins in Mandan, North Dakota (about an hour and a half from Strasburg) and passes through heart-stopping views of the Heart River, providing a wonderfully scenic route for bikers and hikers alike.
  • Cross Ranch State Park: Cross Ranch State Park, located about an hour north of Strasburg, boasts over 16 miles of hiking and biking trails along the stunning Missouri River. Visitors will enjoy the tranquility of the natural surroundings while still getting some exercise in.

Best Things to Do in Strasburg: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for a laid-back afternoon or an active adventure, Strasburg has plenty of activities to suit every interest. Here are some of the best things to do during your visit:

  • Take a stroll down Main Street: This charming thoroughfare is lined with historic buildings and businesses that are worth exploring.
  • Attend the annual Strasburg Corn Festival: This fun, family-friendly festival celebrates the town’s agricultural roots with games, live music, and, of course, corn.
  • Go fishing: The area rivers are home to a variety of fish species, including walleye and perch. Local guides can help you find the best fishing spots.
  • Visit the local winery: North Dakota may not be the first place that springs to mind when you think of wine, but the James River Winery produces delicious reds and whites sure to impress any oenophile.

Week-Long Itinerary for Strasburg

Day 1: Visit Emmons County Museum and Sts. Peter and Paul Catholic Church.

Day 2: Hike or bike the Heart River State Trail.

Day 3: Explore the Guten Tag Haus Museum and Old Overland Trail Historic Marker.

Day 4: Take a day trip to Sand Lake National Wildlife Refuge.

Day 5: Go fishing or attend the local winery for a tasting.

Day 6: Explore Main Street and enjoy the local shops.

Day 7: Relax and take in the natural beauty of Strasburg at Bismarck Lake.

Food and Drink Scene of Strasburg

Must-Try Dishes

Strasburg’s German heritage is reflected in its delicious cuisine. Here are some of the top dishes to try during your visit:

  • Knöpfle: This homemade German pasta is the perfect comfort food on a chilly day.
  • Knoephla soup: A creamy North Dakota staple soup made with potatoes, dumplings, and just a touch of bacon.
  • Sauerbraten: A dish of marinated and slow-cooked beef, served with a rich, tangy sauce.

Local Beverages

Beer lovers will enjoy the local brews on offer in Strasburg. 13 Stripes Brewery and Last Turn Saloon are popular spots serving up a variety of beers and cocktails.

Where to Stay in Strasburg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Though Strasburg is a small town, there are plenty of accommodation options for visitors. Here are a few of the best places to stay:

  • Strasburg Super 8: This hotel offers comfortable rooms and convenient amenities like a continental breakfast and free Wi-Fi.
  • Country View Inn & Suites: This cozy inn features charming rooms with modern amenities, as well as a hot breakfast each morning.
  • The Treehouse at Moose Ridge: For a unique and memorable stay, book this charming treehouse on Airbnb. The treehouse is nestled in the countryside, just a short drive from Strasburg.

What to Buy in Strasburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect memento of your trip to Strasburg? Here are some local products to consider:

  • German candies and treats: Satisfy your sweet tooth with a bag of gummy candies or a box of chocolate truffles from a local shop.
  • Handmade crafts: Check out local boutiques and specialty stores for handmade jewelry, pottery, and other artisanal goods.
  • Local artwork: Creations by a local artist are the most memorable souvenir that you can take back from Strasburg.

Local Culture and Customs of Strasburg

Strasburg is a friendly and welcoming town, but there are a few customs and etiquette rules to keep in mind during your visit. Greeting people with a friendly “hello” or “howdy” is always appreciated and expected. Drinking is typically a big part of socializing in the area, but it is important to never drive under the influence. And of course, always ask for permission before taking photos of people or their property.

Best Time to Visit Strasburg

The best time to visit Strasburg is during the warmer months of May through September, when outdoor activities and festivals are in full swing. Fall is also a beautiful time of year to visit, with colorful foliage and crisp air.

Do’s and Don’ts of Strasburg

Do:

  • Explore the local history and culture by visiting museums and historical landmarks.
  • Take the opportunity to experience the great outdoors by hiking or biking through the area’s natural beauty.
  • Try some of the delicious German-inspired cuisine that Strasburg is known for.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to respect local customs and etiquette.
  • Don’t litter or damage the natural beauty of the area.
  • Don’t forget to always be friendly and say hello!

Safety and Health

Safety in Strasburg

Strasburg is a safe place for tourists to visit, but it is always important to be aware of your surroundings. Keep your valuables in a secure location and take caution when driving on local roads, as some are unpaved and bumpy.

Health in Strasburg

No major health concerns are present in Strasburg, but it is always a good idea to bring sunscreen and stay hydrated to avoid heat exhaustion during the hot summer months.

Getting to Strasburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Strasburg

The closest major airport to Strasburg is Bismarck Airport, which is about an hour and a half away. From there, options include renting a car, using a rideshare service, or taking a taxi to reach Strasburg.

Bus Stations Near Strasburg

Greyhound Bus Lines has a station in Bismarck, about an hour and a half from Strasburg. From there, visitors can rent a car or use a rideshare service to reach the town.

Other Options

One of the easiest ways to get around Strasburg and the surrounding areas is by renting a car. This option allows you to explore at your own pace and make the most of your trip.

How to Get Around in Strasburg

If you prefer not to rent a car, you can still get around Strasburg and the surrounding areas by using local taxi services, Uber, or Lyft.

Where to Go Next from Strasburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip beyond Strasburg, there are plenty of nearby destinations to consider. Here are a few ideas:

  • Bismarck: North Dakota’s capital city is just an hour and a half away and boasts a wealth of cultural attractions and outdoor adventures.
  • Medora: This charming town is home to the Theodore Roosevelt National Park and a variety of historical and cultural attractions.
  • Fargo: About four hours away from Strasburg, Fargo is the state’s largest city and offers plenty of entertainment options, from museums to theater performances.

Conclusion

Whether you’re drawn to the historic landmarks, natural beauty, or delicious cuisine, Strasburg is an ideal destination for anyone looking to get away from it all. Plan your trip today and experience the best that this charming North Dakota town has to offer!
