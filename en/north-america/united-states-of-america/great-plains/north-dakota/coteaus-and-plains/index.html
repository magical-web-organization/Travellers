Introduction to Coteaus and Plains

Welcome to Coteaus and Plains, a region of North Dakota that boasts stunning natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and friendly locals who are eager to share their history, arts, and traditions with visitors. Located in the Great Plains of the United States, this region is often overlooked by tourists but offers a unique and authentic travel experience.

List of Places to Visit in Coteaus and Plains

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Mandan - a reconstruction of the Lewis and Clark Expedition winter camp that has become a symbolic representation of the historic adventure and military fortification in Western North Dakota.
  • Knife River Indian Villages National Historic Site - a tribute to Native American life and culture, a place of meditation and learning, and a site of spiritual and ceremonial significance.
  • Dakota Dinosaur Museum - an impressive display of dinosaur fossils, minerals, and geology in the heart of the Bakken oil fields.
  • North Dakota Heritage Center & State Museum - a collection of more than two million artifacts and specimens that offer an immersive and interactive experience into North Dakota’s rich heritage and ecosystems.

Natural Attractions of Coteaus and Plains: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Theodore Roosevelt National Park - a natural wonderland boasting rugged Badlands, grassy prairies, and herds of bison and wild horses in a serene backdrop.
  • Lake Sakakawea - a 368,000-acre reservoir that offers endless opportunities for fishing, swimming, boating, and camping.
  • Sheyenne National Grasslands - a vast expanse of rolling hills, lush meadows, and pristine forests that offers solitude and inspiration.
  • Maah Daah Hey Trail - a 144-mile-long trail that winds through the rugged Badlands of North Dakota, offering jaw-dropping scenery and outdoor adventure.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Coteaus and Plains

  • North Country National Scenic Trail - a 165-mile stretch of trail through the North Dakota wilderness, offering serene views of prairies, forests, streams, and wildlife.
  • McClusky Canal Shoreline Trail - a lakeside trail that offers scenic views of Lake Sakakawea and abundant opportunities for fishing, boating, and picnicking.
  • Maah Daah Hey Trail - a multi-day trek through the rugged terrain of the Badlands, offering a challenging and rewarding hiking or biking experience.

Best Things to Do in Coteaus and Plains: Activities and Tours

  • Horseback Riding Tours - explore the beauty of the North Dakota landscape on horseback, taking in the vast scenery and chatting with knowledgeable local cowboys.
  • Wildlife Viewing Tours - experience the natural splendor of North Dakota’s rich wildlife and ecosystems, from majestic bison and wild horses to migratory birds and native plants.
  • Fishing and Boating Tours - enjoy a day out on the lake or river, casting a line and reeling in walleye, bass, trout, and other fish.

Week-Long Itinerary for Coteaus and Plains

Day 1: Arrive in Bismarck, ND, and check-in at a hotel. Visit North Dakota Heritage Center & State Museum to learn about the state’s history and culture.

Day 2: Take a scenic drive to Knife River Indian Villages National Historic Site, followed by a visit to Fort Mandan. Spend the night in Minot.

Day 3-4: Explore Minot’s historic downtown, University of North Dakota, and Scandinavian Heritage Center. Hike or bike in the Minot State University Wildlife Sanctuary. Spend a night in a cozy bed-and-breakfast.

Day 5: Check out Dakota Dinosaur Museum in Dickinson, ND, and then head to Theodore Roosevelt National Park for a scenic drive and wildlife viewing opportunities. Spend the night in a campground or hotel near the park.

Day 6: Enjoy a guided horseback riding tour in the park and then take a dip in Lake Sakakawea. Spend the night in a lakeside cabin.

Day 7: Drive to Maah Daah Hey Trail, and enjoy a full-day hike or bike ride through the Badlands, admiring the stunning scenery. Head back to Bismarck and catch a flight or bus home.

Food and Drink Scene of Coteaus and Plains

Must-Try Dishes

  • Knoephla Soup - a creamy and hearty soup made with dumplings and potatoes, served with white or wheat bread.
  • Fry Bread - a Native American staple, made by frying a dough of flour, salt, water, and yeast until golden brown and crispy.
  • Meatballs with Mashed Potatoes - a classic Scandinavian dish made with spiced meatballs and creamy mashed potatoes.
  • Barbecue - a smoky and tangy dish featuring slow-cooked meats, such as beef brisket, pork ribs, or chicken, accompanied by traditional sides such as coleslaw and baked beans.

Local Beverages

  • Chokecherry Wine - a flavorful wine made from the chokecherry fruit, which grows abundantly in North Dakota.
  • Bakken Brew - a locally crafted beer that showcases North Dakota’s hops and barley.
  • Prairie Fire - a fiery cocktail made with North Dakota-produced vodka or whiskey and infused with hot peppers.

Where to Stay in Coteaus and Plains: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Radisson Hotel Bismarck - a luxurious hotel in downtown Bismarck that offers stunning views of the Missouri River and easy access to local attractions.
  • Streetcar Hotel - a charming and budget-friendly hostel in Minot that offers cozy rooms in a historic setting.
  • The Cross Ranch Lodge - a rustic and charming lodge located on the banks of the Missouri River that provides a unique and tranquil atmosphere.

What to Buy in Coteaus and Plains: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Native American Crafts - Choose from traditional beadwork, dreamcatchers, pottery, and paintings made by local Native American artisans.
  • Bakken Oil Field Memorabilia - Take home an oil rig model, T-shirts, hats, or magnets that showcase North Dakota’s oil exploration industry.
  • North Dakota Food Products - Indulge in locally produced jams, jellies, honey, and chocolates, made with homegrown ingredients.

Local Culture and Customs of Coteaus and Plains

North Dakotans are proud of their heritage and enjoy sharing it with visitors. The people are generally warm and welcoming, and it is common to strike up a conversation with strangers. Being respectful of Native American traditions and customs is essential when visiting historical sites or attending cultural events.

Best Time to Visit Coteaus and Plains

The best time to visit Coteaus and Plains is from May to October when the weather is ideal for outdoor activities and festivals. The peak tourist season is June to August, so be prepared for crowds and higher prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Coteaus and Plains

Do:

  • Dress in layers since temperatures can change dramatically throughout the day.
  • Rent a car to explore the region since public transportation is limited.
  • Observe local customs and traditions when visiting cultural sites or attending events.

Don’t:

  • Trespass on private lands or disturb wildlife.
  • Litter or damage natural resources in the parks or wilderness areas.
  • Use disrespectful language or behavior towards locals or Native Americans.

Safety and Health

Safety in Coteaus and Plains

Coteaus and Plains is generally safe, but visitors should exercise caution when engaging in outdoor activities in remote areas. Wildlife encounters can occur, so keep a safe distance, and follow park rules. When hiking or biking, be prepared with plenty of water, sunscreen, and insect repellant.

Health in Coteaus and Plains

North Dakota is a low-risk area for infectious diseases. However, it is essential to have comprehensive travel insurance and get medical advice before traveling. Bring any necessary medications with you, and use insect repellant to avoid tick bites.

Getting to Coteaus and Plains: Transportation Options and Logistics

The two primary airports nearby are Bismarck Municipal Airport and Minot International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Coteaus and Plains

Other Options

Driving to Coteaus and Plains is the best way to explore the region fully. Consider renting a car or camper to experience all the hidden gems the area has to offer.

How to Get Around in Coteaus and Plains

Driving is the most popular way to get around Coteaus and Plains, and visitors can rent a car or a camper from regional agencies. Cyclists will appreciate the scenic bike routes, especially in summer, but it’s essential to be cautious because traffic laws are more flexible in rural areas.

Where to Go Next from Coteaus and Plains: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
