Introduction to Binford

Welcome to Binford, a small town in the Coteaus and Plains region of North Dakota, lined with green spaces and scenic views. Nestled in the heart of the Great Plains region, this charming town is known for its cultural attractions, outdoor activities, and friendly locals. Whether you are a history buff, nature enthusiast, or an adventure seeker, Binford has something in store for every kind of traveler. In this comprehensive guide, we will take you through the must-visit places, best things to do, local food and drink scene, accommodation options, and travel tips to make your visit to Binford an unforgettable experience.

List of Places to Visit in Binford

Binford boasts a plethora of interesting places to explore, from culturally significant attractions to natural wonders. Here are some of the must-visit destinations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Binford has a rich history and is home to a number of prominent historical sites and museums. Here are the top attractions:

  • Binford Historical Society Museum: This museum showcases the rich history and heritage of Binford and its surrounding areas, with a collection of artifacts, photographs, and documents that date back to the early 1900s.
  • McLean Steam Threshers Association: This is a fascinating museum that houses an impressive collection of steam-powered machines, including tractors and threshers. The museum also organizes an annual threshing show, which attracts thousands of visitors from across the country.
  • St. Mary’s Catholic Church: This is one of Binford’s most prominent landmarks and is listed on the National Register of Historic Places. The historic church was built in 1911 and features stunning stained glass windows.

Natural Attractions of Binford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Binford is surrounded by lush green spaces, hills, and valleys that make for fantastic outdoor activities and scenic views. Here are the top natural attractions:

  • Binford Wildlife Management Area: This is a great place to go if you are an animal lover or a bird watcher. The management area provides habitat for a variety of wildlife species, including mule deer, pronghorn antelope, and bald eagles.
  • Binford Dam: This is a great spot for fishing and boating, with scenic views of the surrounding hills and valleys. The dam also serves as a popular picnic spot for families and friends.
  • Saddle Butte: This is a popular hiking and biking trail that offers stunning panoramic views of Binford and the surrounding areas. The trail is suitable for all levels of fitness and offers a great workout with its steep inclines and declines.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Binford

Binford has a number of hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes. Here are the top routes:

  • Saddle Butte Trail: This 2.5-mile trail is a favorite among hikers and mountain bikers, with steep inclines and declines that provide a great workout. The trail offers panoramic views of Binford and the surrounding hills.
  • McClusky Canal Trail: This 8-mile trail follows the route of the historic McClusky Canal and offers stunning views of the countryside. The trail is suitable for hikers and mountain bikers of all levels.
  • Binford Dam Trail: This 1.8-mile trail is a great spot for family-friendly hiking and biking. The trail follows the shore of the Binford Dam and offers scenic views of the water and surrounding hills.

Best Things to Do in Binford: Activities and Tours

Binford offers a range of activities and tours that will keep you entertained throughout your stay. Here are the top things to do:

  • Fishing: Head to the Binford Dam or one of the nearby lakes for some of the best fishing in the region. You can expect to catch bass, walleye, and pike, among other fish species.
  • Horseback Riding: Take a scenic horseback ride through the hills and valleys around Binford for a unique perspective of the region. Local outfitters provide guided tours for riders of all levels.
  • Threshing Show: Plan your visit around the annual McLean Steam Threshers Association Threshing Show, a popular event that features vintage steam machinery demonstrations, live music, and food vendors.

Food and Drink Scene of Binford

Binford is known for its hearty, farm-fresh food that will fill you up for a day of outdoor activities. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Knoephla Soup: This is a hearty, creamy soup made with potato dumplings, chicken, and vegetables. Perfect for a chilly day in Binford.
  • Walleye Sandwich: A Binford classic, this sandwich is made with deep-fried walleye fillet and served with tartar sauce and a side of fries.
  • Lefse: A traditional Norwegian flatbread that is served with butter or jam, perfect for a quick snack.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Binford is home to a number of microbreweries that produce some of the best craft beer in the region. Some of the top breweries include Binford Brewing Company and Prairie Brothers Brewing Company.
  • Buick: A local favorite, this drink is made with orange soda and ice cream and is perfect for a hot day in Binford.
  • Honey Wine: Binford is home to a number of beekeepers, and honey wine is a popular local beverage made from local honey.

  • Binford Inn and Suites: This is a comfortable hotel with modern amenities and a great location in the heart of Binford.
  • Prairie Guest House: This is a cozy guesthouse with a relaxed atmosphere and comfortable rooms. The property also has a lovely garden and outdoor seating area.
  • Airbnb: There are a number of great Airbnb listings in Binford, ranging from private rooms to entire houses that can accommodate larger groups or families.

What to Buy in Binford: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Binford is home to a number of local artisans and farmers who sell their products at farmers’ markets and craft fairs. Here are some of the top products to bring home as souvenirs:

  • Honey and Honey Products: Binford is known for its local honey, and you can find a range of honey-based products, including honey soap, lotion, and candles.
  • Craft Beer: Binford has a number of microbreweries that produce some of the best craft beer in the region. Pick up a few bottles to bring home and share with friends and family.
  • Local Crafts: Binford is home to a number of talented artisans who make everything from pottery to jewelry. Check out local craft fairs and farmers’ markets to pick up unique, locally made souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Binford

Binford is a friendly, welcoming community, and visitors are encouraged to get to know the locals and immerse themselves in the local culture. Here are some of the customs and traditions you can expect to encounter in Binford:

  • Community Gatherings: Binford is a tight-knit community that values community gatherings and events. Look out for local fairs, festivals, and other events where you can meet locals and learn about local culture.
  • Farming and Agriculture: Binford is an agricultural town, and visitors can learn about local farming practices and the importance of agriculture to the local economy.
  • Religion: Binford has a strong religious tradition, with several prominent churches and religious institutions in the town.

Best Time to Visit Binford

The best time to visit Binford is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the outdoor activities are in full swing. The fall months, from September to November, are also a great time to visit, with beautiful fall foliage and cooler temperatures that are perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Binford

Here are some of the do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Binford:

  • Do try the local food and drink scene: Binford is known for its hearty, farm-fresh food and craft beer, so make sure to indulge in the local cuisine and support local businesses.

Safety and Health

Safety in Binford

Binford is a safe town, with a low crime rate and friendly, welcoming locals. However, visitors should still take basic safety precautions, such as keeping their belongings close and being aware of their surroundings when walking alone at night.

Health in Binford

Binford has a small hospital and a handful of medical clinics that can handle minor medical issues. However, visitors with more serious health concerns should seek medical attention in nearby cities such as Minot or Bismarck.

Getting to Binford: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Binford

The closest airports to Binford are Minot International Airport, which is about 90 miles away, and Hector International Airport in Fargo, which is about 200 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Binford

Greyhound buses stop at the nearby cities of Devils Lake (25 miles away) and Minot (90 miles away). From there, visitors can take a cab or rent a car to get to Binford.

Other Options

Driving is the most popular way to get around Binford and the surrounding areas. Visitors can rent a car at the nearby airports or major cities such as Fargo or Bismarck.

How to Get Around in Binford

Binford is a small town, and many of the top attractions are located within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also rent bicycles to explore the countryside or use a car to get around more quickly.

Where to Go Next from Binford: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Binford is located in the heart of the Great Plains region and is surrounded by a number of interesting nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some great suggestions:

  • Devils Lake: This charming town is just 25 miles away from Binford and is known for its scenic lake, hiking trails, and fishing opportunities.
  • Minot: This bustling city is just 90 miles away and is home to a number of cultural attractions, including the Scandinavian Heritage Park and the Minot State University.
  • Theodore Roosevelt National Park: This gorgeous national park is about a three-hour drive from Binford and is home to some of the most stunning landscapes in the region, including the Badlands and the Little Missouri River.
