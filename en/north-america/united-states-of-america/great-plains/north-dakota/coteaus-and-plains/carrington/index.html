Introduction to Carrington

Nestled in the heart of North Dakota’s Great Plains, Carrington offers a unique blend of attractions that cater to outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and cultural aficionados. With its small-town charm, stunning scenery, and rich heritage, Carrington is a destination worth exploring.

List of Places to Visit in Carrington

If you’re thinking of visiting Carrington, here are some of the must-visit attractions to include in your itinerary.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Foster County Historical Society: Learn about the history of Carrington and its neighboring communities at this museum, which houses a collection of artifacts, photographs, and documents from the region.
  • Grace City Historic Depot: Discover the era of steam trains and railroads at this museum, which features a restored depot, vintage railway cars, and a collection of railroad memorabilia.
  • Carrington City Auditorium: This historic landmark, built in 1935, is home to the Carrington Community Arts Center and hosts various cultural events throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Carrington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Arrowwood Wildlife Refuge: Explore the beauty of the North Dakota wilderness at this wildlife park, which offers hiking trails, birdwatching opportunities, and fantastic views of the countryside.
  • Pipestem Dam and Reservoir: Enjoy a day of boating, fishing, and swimming at this picturesque reservoir which offers stunning views of the surrounding hills.
  • Carrington Municipal Golf Course: Tee off at this 18-hole golf course, which boasts lush greens, scenic surroundings, and a relaxed atmosphere.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Carrington

Carrington is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, and it offers plenty of trails and routes for hiking and biking. Here are some of the best places to hit the trails.

  • Arrowwood Wildlife Refuge: The refuge features more than 15 miles of hiking trails, which offer a chance to explore the wilderness and spot wildlife.
  • Pipestem Dam and Reservoir: The area around the reservoir offers miles of scenic hiking and biking trails with varying levels of difficulty.

Best Things to Do in Carrington: Activities and Tours

Carrington offers plenty of activities and tours that cater to various interests and preferences. Here are some of the best things to do in Carrington:

  • Kayaking and Canoeing: Explore the waterways of the Arrowwood Wildlife Refuge and Pipestem Dam and Reservoir by kayaking or canoeing.
  • Historic Walking Tour: Take a stroll through downtown Carrington and explore its historic landmarks, architectural gems, and charming boutiques.
  • Carrington Street Fair: Join the festivities at this annual event, which takes place in July and features live music, arts and crafts, food vendors, and much more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Carrington

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Carrington, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your stay:

Food and Drink Scene of Carrington

Carrington boasts a vibrant food and drink scene that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re looking for a quick bite or an elaborate dinner, Carrington has plenty of options to choose from.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Knoephla Soup: This creamy soup is a North Dakota specialty, made with dumplings, potatoes, and vegetables.
  • Pasties: These savory pastries are filled with meat, vegetables, and spices and are a staple of North Dakota cuisine.
  • Walleye: This freshwater fish is a favorite among anglers and seafood lovers alike and is commonly served fried or grilled.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: North Dakota has a thriving craft beer scene, and Carrington is no exception. Check out the local breweries to sample some of the best beers in the region.
  • Chokecherry Juice: This North Dakota favorite is made from the berries of the chokecherry tree and is often used as a sweetener in various dishes and beverages.

What to Buy in Carrington: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Carrington is known for its local crafts, handmade products, and unique souvenirs. Here are some of the best things to buy in Carrington:

  • Handmade Pottery: Visit the local pottery studios to buy handcrafted pieces made by local artisans.
  • Lefse: This traditional Norwegian flatbread is a favorite in North Dakota and is sold in local bakeries and markets.
  • Honey and Jams: North Dakota’s fertile lands produce some of the best honey and jams, which are sold in local markets and gift shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Carrington

Carrington is known for its friendly people, welcoming community, and rich cultural heritage. The town celebrates its heritage through various cultural events, festivals, and traditions throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Carrington

The best time to visit Carrington is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is warm, and the outdoor activities are in full swing. However, if you’re looking to avoid the crowds and enjoy lower prices, consider visiting during the shoulder seasons (May and September).

Getting to Carrington: Transportation Options and Logistics

Carrington is located in the heart of North Dakota and is easily accessible by road, air, or rail.

  • Bismarck Municipal Airport in Bismarck, North Dakota (120 miles).

Bus Stations Near Carrington

  • Jefferson Bus Lines in Carrington, North Dakota.

Other Options

If you’re driving to Carrington, the town is easily accessible from the major highways:

  • Interstate 94 connects Carrington to Fargo and Bismarck, North Dakota.
  • US Route 281 connects Carrington to Jamestown, North Dakota, and Aberdeen, South Dakota.

How to Get Around in Carrington

Carrington is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by bike. If you’re looking for a more convenient option, consider renting a car or using a local taxi service.

Where to Go Next from Carrington: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Carrington is a great base to explore the surrounding areas of North Dakota. Here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions that you can add to your itinerary:

  • Devils Lake: Enjoy hiking, fishing, and boating at one of North Dakota’s largest natural lakes (60 miles).
  • Medora: Discover the beauty of the Badlands and visit the Theodore Roosevelt National Park (170 miles).
