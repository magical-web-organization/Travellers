Introduction to Hazen

Located in Western North Dakota, Hazen is a charming town that offers a range of cultural and natural attractions. With its rich history, scenic landscapes, and small-town vibe, Hazen is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking an authentic North Dakota experience. In this guide, we’ll take you through the top things to see and do in Hazen, along with practical information on transportation, accommodation, and local culture.

List of Places to Visit in Hazen

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Dakota Queen: Explore the history of Hazen’s riverboat transportation with a visit to the Dakota Queen, situated on the shores of the Missouri River.
  • Lewis and Clark Interpretive Center: Discover the legacy of Lewis and Clark’s famous expedition with a trip to this interactive museum, featuring exhibits on early American exploration and Native American culture.
  • Mercer County Museum: Step back in time at this local historical museum, which showcases the pioneer era of Mercer County and Hazen.

Natural Attractions of Hazen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Beaver Lake State Park: Enjoy swimming, boating, and picnicking at this picturesque state park, located just a few miles south of Hazen. Hike the trails and enjoy stunning views of the Coal Country landscape.
  • Hazen Bay Recreation Area: Get outdoors and go fishing, kayaking, or paddling at Hazen Bay. This is also a great spot for camping and hiking.
  • Knife River Indian Villages National Historic Site: Immerse yourself in the culture and history of the Mandan, Hidatsa, and Arikara tribes, with a visit to this National Historic Site. In addition to the museum and visitor center, you can take a guided tour of the archeological sites here.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hazen

  • Beaver Lake State Park Trail: This 2.5-mile hiking trail winds through the park’s lush forest and offers scenic views of Beaver Lake.
  • Killdeer Mountain Four Bears Scenic Byway: Explore the rolling hills and winding roads of western North Dakota, with this picturesque 71-mile drive from Elbowoods to Killdeer.
  • Lake Sakakawea State Park Trail: Discover the beauty of the Missouri River Valley with a hike along the 5-mile trail that loops through Lake Sakakawea State Park.

Best Things to Do in Hazen: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing Excursions: Whether you prefer lake or river fishing, Hazen offers excellent opportunities to cast your line. Many local outfitters offer guided tours and equipment rentals.
  • Wilstem Wildlife Park: Get up close and personal with exotic animals, including zebras, camels, and ostriches, at this family-friendly park.
  • Hiking and Camping: With its array of state parks, wildlife areas, and riverfront parks, Hazen provides ample opportunities for adventurers to explore the great outdoors.

Food and Drink Scene of Hazen

Must-Try Dishes

  • Knoephla Soup: This hearty soup is a North Dakota staple, made with potato dumplings and vegetables.
  • Pheasant Sandwich: Savor the taste of this truly local sandwich, featuring tender pheasant breast served on a bun with toppings like lettuce, tomato, and mayo.
  • Chokecherry Syrup: This syrup is made from chokecherries, a wild fruit that grows throughout North Dakota. It can be used on pancakes or waffles, or mixed with seltzer water to make a refreshing drink.

Local Beverages

  • Prairie Rose Meadery: Sample locally-made mead at this charming winery, located just a few miles south of Hazen.
  • Nodak Brewing Company: Enjoy craft beer brewed in North Dakota at this popular local brewery.
  • Dakota Prairie Winery: Sip on fine wines made from North Dakota grapes and fruits at this expansive winery, located in nearby Anamoose.

Where to Stay in Hazen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Beaver Lake State Park Campgrounds: Enjoy a true camping experience with lakeside campsites and modern amenities.
  • Cobblestone Inn & Suites: This modern hotel features comfortable guest rooms, free breakfast, and an on-site fitness center.
  • Turtle Lake Motel: This affordable hotel is located just a short drive from Hazen, and features clean and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Hazen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Coal Country Coffee: This local coffee roaster features a variety of blends made from beans sourced throughout the world.
  • Bakken Rocks Cookbooks: Get creative in the kitchen with these cookbooks featuring local recipes and culinary traditions.
  • Bakken Oil Rush Hats and T-shirts: Show your support for the local oil industry with a stylish hat or T-shirt.

Local Culture and Customs of Hazen

Hazen is a friendly community with a strong sense of local pride. Visitors are encouraged to respect the area’s natural beauty and local traditions, including a deep appreciation for agriculture and rural life. Hazen is known for its annual Coal Country Fair, featuring carnival rides, live music, and a rodeo. The event draws visitors from across North Dakota and beyond.

Best Time to Visit Hazen

The best time to visit Hazen is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and outdoor activities are in full swing. Fall is also a beautiful time to visit, as the leaves change color and the weather is mild. However, temperatures can drop quickly, so be sure to bring warm clothing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hazen

Do:

  • Do take time to visit the local museums and historical sites to learn about Hazen’s rich history and culture.
  • Do explore the great outdoors, with opportunities for fishing, hiking, and camping abound.
  • Do try local cuisine, such as Knoephla soup and the Pheasant Sandwich.
  • Do attend the annual Coal Country Fair for a true taste of local culture.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage natural areas, and be respectful of local wildlife and their habitat.
  • Don’t ignore local customs or traditions, and be sure to ask locals for advice and recommendations on where to go and what to see.
  • Don’t overindulge in alcohol or drugs, and always follow local laws and regulations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hazen

Hazen is generally a safe and welcoming community, but visitors should take standard precautions to ensure their safety. Be aware of your surroundings, and avoid walking alone at night. Be cautious while driving on rural roads, especially during winter months when snow and ice can make driving hazardous.

Health in Hazen

Hazen is served by several medical clinics and a local hospital, providing basic healthcare services. Visitors should ensure that they have comprehensive travel insurance to cover any unforeseen health issues.

Getting to Hazen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hazen

  • Bismarck Airport (BIS): 55 miles south of Hazen
  • Minot International Airport (MOT): 70 miles north of Hazen

Bus Stations Near Hazen

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Bismarck, ND (55 miles south of Hazen)

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Several car rental companies operate in Bismarck and Minot, providing convenient access to Hazen and surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Hazen

The best way to get around Hazen is by car, as many of the attractions are located outside of the town center. Bicycles and walking are also good options for exploring the town, particularly during the summer months. Visitors should exercise caution while driving on rural roads, and be aware of changing weather conditions.

Where to Go Next from Hazen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lake Sakakawea: Head north to visit this stunning reservoir on the Missouri River, which offers excellent fishing, boating, and hiking opportunities.
  • Theodore Roosevelt National Park: Drive west to explore the rugged beauty of this national park, featuring bison, elk, and other wildlife, as well as scenic drives and hiking trails.
  • Bismarck: The capital of North Dakota is just a short drive from Hazen and offers cultural attractions like the North Dakota Heritage Center and State Capitol.

Conclusion

Hazen, North Dakota is a gem of the Great Plains, offering visitors a chance to experience local culture and rich natural beauty. From hiking and camping to fishing and local cuisine, there is something for everyone in Hazen. With this guide, you can plan your trip with confidence and make the most of your time in this charming town.
