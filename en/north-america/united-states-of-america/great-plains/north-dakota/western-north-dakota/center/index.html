Introduction to Center

Center is located in the heart of North Dakota’s Great Plains region. The town was established in 1903 and is known for its rich history, arts, culture, and outdoor recreation. While the town itself is small, it boasts a wide range of attractions and activities that make it a great vacation spot for families, adventurers, and history enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Center

Whether you’re interested in culture or nature, the following attractions are worth visiting in Center.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Oliver County Historical Society Museum - This museum is dedicated to preserving the history and culture of Oliver County. Exhibits include displays of early pioneer life, transportation, and military artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Center: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Sakakawea - This artificial lake is the state’s largest and is formed by the Garrison Dam on the Missouri River. Visitors can enjoy fishing, boating, water sports, and picnicking.
  • Cross Ranch State Park - This 5,000-acre park offers a variety of recreational activities, including hiking, birdwatching, and canoeing. Visitors can also learn about the park’s natural history at the interpretive center.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Center

Outdoor enthusiasts can enjoy hiking and biking on the trails at Cross Ranch State Park and Lake Sakakawea.

Best Things to Do in Center: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing on Lake Sakakawea - The lake is home to a variety of fish species, including walleye, northern pike, and smallmouth bass.
  • Canoeing at Cross Ranch State Park - The park features several miles of the Missouri River, perfect for exploring by canoe.
  • Touring Oliver County Historical Society Museum - Take a guided tour of the museum to learn about the history and culture of Oliver County.

Food and Drink Scene of Center

When it comes to food and drink, Center offers a variety of options.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hotdish - A classic North Dakota casserole made with hamburger, potatoes, and cream of mushroom soup.
  • Knoephla Soup - A hearty soup made with dumplings, chicken, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Grain Belt Beer - Brewed in Minnesota, Grain Belt Beer is a popular choice among North Dakota locals.
  • Karl’s Pure North Dakota Honey - A popular local honey that can be enjoyed in tea or on toast.

Where to Stay in Center: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are a few options for accommodation in Center, depending on your budget and needs.

  • Center Homestay - A cozy guesthouse with comfortable rooms and friendly hosts.
  • Sleepy Hollow RV Park and Campground - RV and tent camping sites, with picnic tables and grills.
  • Ramada by Wyndham Bismarck - A nearby hotel option located in Bismarck.

What to Buy in Center: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Oliver County Historical Society Cookbook - A collection of recipes from local residents, featuring classic North Dakota dishes.
  • North Dakota State Souvenir - A perfect memento of your trip to North Dakota. You can find these in local gift shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Center

The people of North Dakota are known for their friendly and welcoming nature. If you’re visiting, be sure to say hello to locals and strike up a conversation.

Best Time to Visit Center

The best time to visit Center is in the summer months, from June to August. During this time, the weather is warm and ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Center

  • Do travel responsibly and be mindful of the environment.

Don’t:

Safety and Health

Safety in Center

Center is a safe town, but visitors should take proper precautions when spending time outdoors. Stay aware of wildlife, use caution when swimming or boating, and always follow safety guidelines when participating in outdoor activities.

Health in Center

If you’re visiting Center, be sure to pack sunscreen, insect repellent and other necessities for outdoor activities. Drink plenty of water and be mindful of the sun and heat.

Where to Go Next from Center: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of North Dakota, consider the following nearby destinations:

  • Theodore Roosevelt National Park - Located about 100 miles from Center, this beautiful national park features rugged badlands, prairies, and hiking trails.
  • Fort Mandan - Located about 25 miles from Center, this reconstructed fort is where Lewis and Clark spent the winter of 1804-1805.
