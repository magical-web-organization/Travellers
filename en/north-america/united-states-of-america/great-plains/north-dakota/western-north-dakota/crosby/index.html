Introduction to Crosby

Nestled in Western North Dakota, Crosby is a charming town with a soulful mixture of history and scenic beauty. The town has a population of around 900 people and offers visitors a wide range of cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and recreational activities. Crosby boasts of being a hub for outdoor enthusiasts of all kinds, from hikers to bikers to anglers.

List of Places to Visit in Crosby

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history buff, Crosby will surprise you with its fascinating stories of the past. The Divide County Museum displays thousands of artifacts highlighting the early pioneer life in the area. The Pioneer Village Museum transports visitors back in time with its authentic pioneer cabin, blacksmith shop, and one-room schoolhouse. The Boyd-Robertson Mansion is a stunning example of Queen Anne-style architecture.

Natural Attractions of Crosby: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Crosby has vast areas of natural beauty, making it an excellent destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Scenic drives on the Des Lacs National Wildlife Refuge offer fantastic views of the rolling hills, wetlands, and grasslands. Bowbells Wildlife Management Area and Lewis and Clark State Park offer visitors fishing, canoeing, and other water activities. Crosby Country Club provides visitors with an excellent opportunity to experience challenging golf courses and rolling hills.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Crosby

Crosby’s rugged terrain offers plenty of opportunities for explorers to venture out on foot or by bike. The Crosby Trail, which runs for 5.6 miles past the city’s natural prairies and wildlife, is a perfect spot for hiking or biking. The Skyline trail, located east of Crosby, offers breathtaking views from the top of the hill. Birdwatchers will encounter owls, hawks, waterfowl, and songbirds along the route.

Best Things to Do in Crosby: Activities and Tours

Crosby offers visitors a wide range of events and activities throughout the year, no matter the weather. In summer, visitors can take part in the Divide County Fair, held every August. In winter, the town transforms with snow-filled activities like snowmobiling on the groomed trails of the area. Visitors can also go hunting, fishing, or take part in a photo safari to capture the stunning scenery.

Week-Long Itinerary for Crosby

Visitors can settle down in Crosby for a week and get to experience the town’s full range of activities. They can start the week by exploring the Divide County Museum and the Boyd-Robertson Mansion, then move on to check out the Des Lacs National Wildlife Refuge. The following days can include hiking the Skyline Trail, fishing at Bowbells Wireless Management Area, and a tour of the local parks.

Food and Drink Scene of Crosby

Must-Try Dishes

Crosby’s food scene is a blend of old and new with comfort food and innovative dining options in equal measure. The town’s most popular dish is the bison burger; visitors can get it at many of the town’s eateries. Another must-try is the sour cream raisin pie, a staple in local diners.

Local Beverages

Crosby has a growing beer scene. Local brewpubs serve up refreshing, locally brewed beer, and trendy cocktails and wine are also available for visitors. The area is known for its wheat and barley, with local distilleries using these grains in their products.

Where to Stay in Crosby: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Crosby offers lodgings to suit different tastes and budget options. Visitors can choose to stay in a guesthouse, hostel, or hotel based on their preferences. The Grandview Motel is a popular choice for B&B enthusiasts, while the Divide County Fairgrounds RV Park is ideal for campers.

What to Buy in Crosby: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can bring home locally made products that serve as unique souvenirs of their trip to Crosby. Products like locally made jams, jellies, honey, pickles, and wine make for perfect gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Crosby

Crosby has a rich history, and the locals are proud of it. Visitors can expect a warm and welcoming atmosphere. They can also explore the unique customs and traditions of the town through the many festivals and cultural celebrations throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Crosby

The best time to visit Crosby is during the summer months, from June to September. The weather is mild, and visitors can enjoy outdoor activities like hiking, fishing, and camping. The town is also a top destination during the winter months for snowmobiling and winter sports enthusiasts.

Do’s and Don’ts of Crosby

Do:

  • Enjoy the natural beauty of the area
  • Take part in the many festivals and cultural celebrations
  • Explore the town’s history by visiting its museums and historical sites

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Ignore the local customs and traditions
  • Be disrespectful to the locals or their property

Safety and Health

Safety in Crosby

Crosby is a safe town, and visitors don’t have to worry about their safety. However, visitors should take necessary precautions while hiking, biking, or participating in other outdoor activities.

Health in Crosby

Crosby has a medical center with highly trained medical staff and necessary equipment. Visitors can also find pharmacies and clinics that can take care of minor medical issues.

Getting to Crosby: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Crosby

Visitors can fly into Minot International Airport, which is around 95 miles from Crosby. The airport has daily flights to major cities in the US.

Bus Stations Near Crosby

Visitors can use the Greyhound bus service to reach Minot, located 95 miles from Crosby.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car to reach Crosby. This option gives them more flexibility in exploring the area.

How to Get Around in Crosby

Visitors can use their vehicles or rent a car to get around Crosby. Biking and hiking are also popular options.

Where to Go Next from Crosby: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can include nearby destinations like Minot, Bismarck, and Theodore Roosevelt National Park in their itinerary. These places offer visitors further adventures and explorations.

Conclusion

Crosby is a charming destination for those looking for outdoor adventure, cultural exploration, and great food and drink. The town’s rich history, natural beauty, and recreational activities make it an excellent choice for visitors of all kinds.
