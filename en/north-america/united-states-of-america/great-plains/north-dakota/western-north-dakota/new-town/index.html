New Town Travel Guide

New Town is a small city located in Western North Dakota, USA. Known for its rich cultural heritage and stunning natural attractions, New Town is a popular travel destination for both tourists and adventure seekers. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures or just a relaxing trip away from the city bustle, New Town is the perfect destination for you.

List of Places to Visit in New Town

New Town offers a wide range of places to visit and things to do. Here’s a list of must-visit attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Four Bears Bridge: This historic bridge spanning Lake Sakakawea offers stunning views of the water and surrounding hills.
  • Three Affiliated Tribes Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of the Mandan, Hidatsa, and Arikara Nation.
  • Van Hook Territorial Park: The park offers a glimpse into the region’s rich heritage with reconstructed historical buildings.
  • St. Anthony Church: This iconic church, built in 1914, is a prime example of Gothic Revival architecture and is often considered one of the major attractions in the area.

Natural Attractions of New Town: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lund’s Landing: A popular fishing spot on Lake Sakakawea, known for its spectacular sunsets.
  • Crow Flies High Butte: This scenic butte offers panoramic views of the countryside and is a great spot for a picnic lunch.
  • New Town Nature Trail: A perfect spot for a leisurely walk, bike ride, or an afternoon picnic with family and friends.
  • Lostwood National Wildlife Refuge: Home to many prairie species and a wildlife playground for nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of New Town

If you’re looking for outdoor recreation, hiking and biking in New Town is a must. Here’s a list of popular routes:

  • Roughrider Trail: This trail runs 19 miles from Mandaree to New Town, offering breathtaking views of the river, flatland and rolling hills.
  • Barrel Springs Trail: A well-marked trail through beautiful badlands, featuring natural springs and stunning rock formations.
  • Maah Daah Hey Trail: A challenging trail covering more than 140 miles of unspoiled grasslands and badlands.

Best Things to Do in New Town: Activities and Tours

Beyond outdoor recreation, New Town has lots to offer. Here are the best things to do:

  • New Town Rockin on the River: A music festival held annually on the river banks that features local artists.
  • Fort Union Trading Post: A National Historic Site with great history lessons and almost 150 years old.
  • Eagle Ridge Golf Club: A challenging golf course with scenic views that will delight all golfers.
  • Twin Buttes Dam: A great spot for boating, fishing, hunting and recreational activities and is merely an hour away from New Town.

Week-Long Itinerary for New Town

Here’s a sample itinerary of things you can do in New Town during a week-long vacation:

Day Activity
1 Arrive and explore the Three Affiliated Tribes Museum
2 Take the Roughrider Trail for a biking and hiking route
3 Experience the local culture with a visit to St. Anthony Church and Van Hook Park

Food and Drink Scene of New Town

New Town offers a variety of eating and drinking options that cater to both locals and tourists. Here are some highlights:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Buffalo burger: A locally sourced Bison burger with plenty of delicious toppings.
  • Fried bread tacos: A unique take on taco, where the traditional taco ingredients are served on a fried bread instead of a tortilla.
  • Kuchen: A famous German dessert, which is made with a sweet pastry dough and filled with fruit, cream or nuts.

Local Beverages

  • Tatanka Beer: A locally brewed beer made with buffalo grass and herbs.
  • Chokecherry wine: A sweet wine made from chokecherries, a local fruit.

Where to Stay in New Town: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

New Town has a range of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best options:

  • Little Missouri Inn & Suites: A comfortable hotel with great amenities like free breakfast and Wi-Fi.
  • Northern Lights Inn: Another option that features comfortable rooms and a fitness center.
  • Skillings Lodge: A guesthouse with cozy rooms and a welcoming atmosphere.

What to Buy in New Town: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to take home some souvenirs, New Town offers a range of locally made products. Here are some to consider:

  • Quillwork: Traditional Native American craft made with porcupine quills, used to decorate items like moccasins, jackets or handbags.
  • Beadwork: Similar to quillwork, but using small beads to create intricate designs on bags, belts, shirts, jackets, and pouches.
  • Dreamcatchers: A Native American symbol meant to catch and ward off bad dreams.

Local Culture and Customs of New Town

New Town has a rich cultural heritage that you should keep in mind when visiting. The region is home to the Mandan, Hidatsa, and Arikara Nation, and the local customs reflect unique aspects of their heritage. Take time to learn the history and customs of the community, and show respect for traditional practices.

Best Time to Visit New Town

The best time to visit New Town is during the summer months, between June and August. The weather is warm and sunny, perfect for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of New Town

Do:

  • Respect the native customs.
  • Keep a photo ID on you.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or vandalize.
  • Don’t smoke or drink in prohibited areas.

Safety and Health

New Town is a safe place for visitors, but it’s always wise to take common-sense precautions. Here are some tips on staying safe:

Safety in New Town

  • Keep your valuables safe and do not allow strangers to have access to them.
  • Lock your car and hotel room doors.
  • Be aware that wildlife could pose some risks during outdoor activities.

Health in New Town

  • Stay hydrated during outdoor activities with plenty of fluids to prevent dehydration.
  • Wear sunscreen, a hat, and protective clothing to avoid sunburns.

Getting to New Town: Transportation Options and Logistics

New Town has several transportation options to help visitors get around. Here are some of the options available:

Airports Near New Town

  • Minot International Airport - Located about 90 miles east of New Town.
  • Bismarck Municipal Airport - Located about 120 miles south of New Town.

Bus Stations Near New Town

  • Greyhound Bus Lines - Located in nearby Minot, ND

Other Options

  • Rental cars – Available upon arrival at the airport or at rental car locations.

How to Get Around in New Town

The best way to get around New Town is by car. Rental cars are readily available, and you’ll have the freedom to explore without the need to rely on public transportation.

Where to Go Next from New Town: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

New Town is located in the heart of Western North Dakota surrounded by plenty of nearby attractions. Here are some options for extending your stay:

  • Theodore Roosevelt National Park: A few hours’ drive south of New Town, this park offers a glimpse of the stunning North Dakota Badlands.
  • Lewis and Clark Trail State Park: A State Park with a visitor center highlighting the area’s Lewis and Clark heritage.
  • Medora: A historic town near the entrance of Theodore Roosevelt National Park, offering several restaurants, shops, and entertainment.

Conclusion

New Town is an excellent destination for travelers looking for a blend of culture, history, and outdoor adventure. With stunning landscapes, exciting outdoor activities, and a rich cultural heritage, New Town is the perfect location to experience the Midwest. With this guide, you’re ready to start planning your trip.
