Introduction to Buffalo

Buffalo is a charming town located in the heart of North Dakota’s Great Plains region. Situated in the Red River Valley, Buffalo boasts a rich history and unique culture that draws visitors from across the globe. Whether you’re an avid history buff, nature lover or outdoor enthusiast, Buffalo has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Buffalo

Here are some of the most notable places to visit in Buffalo:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Ransom State Park: This historical gem showcases the remnants of a military fort, which was once used to protect travelers from Native American raiders during the late 19th century.
  • St. Boniface Catholic Church: This charming church is one of the oldest still-standing buildings in Buffalo and is known for its stunning Gothic architecture and beautiful stained-glass windows.
  • Buffalo Museum: This museum offers a look into the history of the town and the surrounding region, complete with exhibits on early pioneers, Native American artifacts, and more.

Natural Attractions of Buffalo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sheyenne National Grassland: This stunning natural preserve offers breathtaking views of North Dakota’s rolling hills and vibrant wildflowers, as well as opportunities for hiking and picnicking.
  • Lake Tobiason: This tranquil lake is ideal for fishing, kayaking and boating. A perfect spot to unwind and take in the beauty of the region.
  • Turtle River State Park: This state park offers excellent opportunities for hiking and birdwatching, as well as a chance to take in the stunning beauty of the Red River Valley.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Buffalo

Buffalo is home to a variety of spectacular hiking and biking routes. Here are some of the most popular:

  • Chahinkapa Park River Walk: This scenic trail winds through the picturesque Chahinkapa Park and offers stunning views of the nearby river.
  • Fort Ransom State Park Trail: This 14-mile trail features varying terrain and is a perfect spot for hiking and mountain biking.
  • Maah Daah Hey Trail: This 144-mile trail passes through some of North Dakota’s most stunning landscapes, offering awe-inspiring views around every bend.

Best Things to Do in Buffalo: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures, cultural experiences or simply relaxing and unwinding, here are some of the best things to do in Buffalo:

  • Go fishing or boating on Lake Tobiason
  • Check out the stunning views at Sheyenne National Grassland
  • Tour Fort Ransom State Park
  • Explore the exhibits at the Buffalo Museum
  • Enjoy a picnic or hike at Turtle River State Park

Week-Long Itinerary for Buffalo

For those looking to make the most of their time in Buffalo, here is a suggested week-long itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Buffalo

When it comes to dining and drinking in Buffalo, there are plenty of local favorites to try. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Knoephla Soup: This creamy soup is a North Dakotan staple and is made with flour dumplings, potatoes, and vegetables.
  • Lefse: This Norwegian flatbread is a beloved local treat and is often enjoyed with butter and sugar.
  • Walleye: This freshwater fish is a regional favorite and can be found on menus throughout the state.

Local Beverages

  • Rhubarb Wine: Made from locally grown rhubarb, this sweet wine is unique to North Dakota and is a must-try for wine lovers.
  • Dakota Dry Beer: This local brew is made with pure Red River Valley water and is a refreshing way to cool off after a long day of exploring.

Where to Stay in Buffalo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxurious getaway or a more budget-friendly option, there are plenty of accommodation options in Buffalo:

  • Buffalo Motel: A budget-friendly motel that offers comfortable rooms and easy access to nearby attractions.
  • Souris Valley Suites: This stylish hotel offers spacious suites with modern amenities and is close to restaurants and shops.
  • Chieftain Conference Center and Campground: For those who enjoy the great outdoors, this campground offers a variety of camping options, as well as conference facilities for events.

What to Buy in Buffalo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home from your trip to Buffalo:

  • Prairie Fire Pottery: Handcrafted pottery made from Red River Valley clay.
  • North Dakota Made Products: From jams and jellies to handmade crafts, there are plenty of locally made products to choose from.
  • Dakota Roots Apparel: Show off your love for North Dakota with clothing from this local brand.

Local Culture and Customs of Buffalo

North Dakota’s culture is deeply rooted in its Scandinavian heritage, and this is reflected in everything from its local cuisine to its traditional festivals. Visitors should be prepared for long, snowy winters and warm, welcoming locals.

Best Time to Visit Buffalo

The best time to visit Buffalo is during the summer months when the weather is warm and the landscapes are in full bloom. However, those looking to experience the snowy beauty of North Dakota during wintertime should plan their visit for December through February.

Do’s and Don’ts of Buffalo

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Buffalo:

Do:

  • Embrace the local culture and try the regional cuisine.
  • Take time to explore the region’s stunning natural landscapes.
  • Bring warm clothing and be prepared for inclement weather.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful to locals or their customs.
  • Ignore safety warnings when exploring outdoor areas.
  • Leave without trying some of the local food and drinks.

Safety and Health

Visitors to Buffalo should take precautions to ensure their safety and well-being during their trip.

Safety in Buffalo

  • Be aware of the local wildlife and never approach wild animals.
  • Take care when exploring outdoor areas and always follow safety guidelines when hiking or camping.
  • Be cautious when traveling on the region’s rural roads and highways.

Health in Buffalo

  • Visitors should be up-to-date on their routine vaccinations before visiting North Dakota.
  • Tick-borne diseases, particularly Lyme disease, are a concern in some areas, so be sure to take precautions to prevent tick bites.
  • Be prepared for hot weather during the summer months and stay hydrated.

Getting to Buffalo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Buffalo is located in a rural area, but there are still plenty of transportation options to get you there.

Airports Near Buffalo

  • Hector International Airport (FAR)
  • Grand Forks International Airport (GFK)

Bus Stations Near Buffalo

  • Greyhound Bus Station, Fargo, ND

Other Options

  • Renting a car is a good option for those looking to explore the region on their own.
  • Taxis, Uber and Lyft are also available in the area.

How to Get Around in Buffalo

As a small town, walking or biking is often the best way to get around Buffalo. Rental cars are also available for those who prefer to drive.

Where to Go Next from Buffalo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Buffalo is located in the heart of North Dakota’s Great Plains region, making it a perfect home base for further exploration. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Fargo, ND: Just over 70 miles east of Buffalo, Fargo offers a lively city scene with plenty of restaurants, shops, and attractions.
  • Theodore Roosevelt National Park: Located approximately 4 hours west of Buffalo, this park offers an unforgettable wilderness experience.
  • The Enchanted Highway: This unique stretch of highway features a series of gigantic sculptures that make for a fun and memorable day trip.

Conclusion

With its stunning natural landscapes, rich history and unique cultural offerings, Buffalo is an ideal destination for anyone looking to experience the beauty and charm of the North Dakota Great Plains region. From outdoor adventures to cultural experiences, there is something for everyone in this charming town, making it a must-visit destination for anyone planning a trip to North Dakota.
