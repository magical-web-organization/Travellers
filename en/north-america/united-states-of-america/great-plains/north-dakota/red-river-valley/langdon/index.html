Introduction to Langdon

Langdon is a small city located in the Red River Valley of North Dakota, in the Great Plains region of the United States. It is a peaceful community that features a mix of historical and modern attractions, scenic views, and outdoor adventures.

Tourists can explore the town’s rich history by visiting museums and historical places or by hiking or biking on the trails that trace the area’s stunning natural landscape. Visitors can also enjoy a variety of activities, such as golfing, fishing, and bird watching. Additionally, the food, drink, and souvenirs available in Langdon highlight the region’s distinct local flavor.

This comprehensive guide offers essential information for anyone planning to visit Langdon, including the best places to visit, hiking and biking routes, activities, and tours, among other things.

List of Places to Visit in Langdon

Below are some of the key attractions and destinations to visit in Langdon:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Roxy Theatre: This historic theater was built in 1936 and still operates as a cinema. It plays the latest movies as well as classic films, and the building features a beautiful art deco facade.
  • Cavalier County Museum: The museum features exhibits and artifacts that showcase the town’s history and cultural heritage. Visitors can view period costumes, household items, and farming implements used in the region.
  • The Langdon Area Heritage Center: A museum celebrating the history and heritage of the Langdon area. It includes exhibits on local agriculture, business, sports, and music.

Natural Attractions of Langdon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Icelandic State Park: The park spans over 900 scenic acres and offers visitors over 20 miles of hiking and biking trails. The park also features a marina, picnic grounds, and a swimming beach.
  • Arrowwood National Wildlife Refuge: This wildlife refuge is a haven for a variety of migratory birds. It is a must-visit destination for birding enthusiasts and anyone looking to enjoy the natural beauty of the region.
  • Grahams Island State Park: Located on the south edge of Devils Lake, Grahams Island State Park offers visitors scenic views and outdoor activities. Activities that visitors can enjoy include fishing, boating, and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Langdon

Langdon has a rich and diverse landscape and is a haven for hikers and mountain bikers alike. Here are some of the notable routes:

  • Icelandic State Park Trails: These trails cover over 20 miles. The park has three main trails and several shorter ones that visitors can choose from.
  • Fort Ransom State Park: The park features hiking trails that are perfect for beginners and more experienced hikers. The views on the trail include prairies, hills, and river valleys.
  • Pembina Gorge: Located just an hour’s drive from Langdon, the area offers several excellent hiking and mountain biking trails.

Best Things to Do in Langdon: Activities and Tours

Langdon has lots of fun and exciting activities that visitors can enjoy. Below are some of the best things to do in Langdon:

  • Fish in a nearby lake: Surrounding Langdon are many lakes that are open for fishing. The nearby Devils Lake is a hotspot for fishing walleye, northern, perch, and white bass.
  • Hit the links: Area golf courses are in abundance with two golf courses nearby, Langdon Country Club and Frost Fire Park, offering challenging 9-hole and 18-hole courses.
  • Go bird watching: Arrowwood National Wildlife Refuge is known for its legendary birding and nature programs that visitors can take advantage of with advance registration.

Additionally, there are many tours available, including the Devils Lake Beer Tours that visitors can join in on, along with Chartier Cuisine tours, which take visitors through some of the area’s most renowned breweries and wineries.

Week-Long Itinerary for Langdon

If you’re planning on spending a week in Langdon, here is a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Visit Icelandic State Park and hike the Beaver Pond Trail.

Day 2: Explore the town’s rich history and visit the Cavalier County Museum and Roxy Theatre.

Day 3: Take a day trip to Pembina Gorge for outdoor adventures.

Day 4: Visit Grahams Island State Park and fish.

Day 5: Take a tour of the area’s craft breweries and wineries.

Day 6: Go bird watching at Arrowwood National Wildlife Refuge.

Day 7: Relax and dine at downtown Langdon’s charming restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Langdon

Langdon’s food and drink scene highlights the region’s distinct local flavor. Below are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages to try while in town:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Knoephla Soup: A beloved local dish made of dumplings and chicken broth.
  • Fried Walleye: Langdon’s close proximity to Devils Lake makes it home to great fish and chips, with walleye an area delicacy.
  • Sconnie Burger: A triple cheeseburger topped with bacon and mac n’ cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Whiteface Woodfire Bourbon Whiskey: A unique whiskey made in northeast North Dakota, that is perfectly matched by smoked meats and hearty meal options.
  • Wild Terra Cider: North Dakota’s first urban cider house offers a diverse range of ciders that perfectly accompany area dishes.

Where to Stay in Langdon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Langdon has several options for visitors looking for accommodation. Here are some of the top places to stay in town:

  • Cobblestone Inn & Suites: A modern hotel located in the heart of downtown Langdon.
  • Northern Lights Inn: A welcoming, pet-friendly hotel conveniently located near the town’s attractions.
  • White Fox Retreat and Guesthouse: A cozy and unique guesthouse with a beautiful view of the countryside.

What to Buy in Langdon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Langdon will want to bring back some unique souvenirs and local products. Here are some of the best items to look out for:

  • Icelandic State Park Apparel: Visitors can buy some memorabilia that highlights their time spent at Icelandic State Park.
  • Bowling Green Farm Products: Purchase meats, cheeses, and jellies made from local farms and craft breweries.
  • Lutheran Sunset Home Soaps: Shop a variety of artisanal soaps produced by a local nursing home.

Local Culture and Customs of Langdon

Langdon is a small town that values its history, farms, and community. Visitors can enjoy attending a variety of community events, such as fairs, concerts, and rodeos. It’s essential not to miss out on the local activities in Langdon, as these activities give you a chance to experience the town’s customs and culture.

Best Time to Visit Langdon

Langdon has an average temperature range, with the warmest times being July and August, and during the winter, temperatures can drop to -10 degrees Fahrenheit. The best time to visit Langdon is during the summer months of June through August when many of the outdoor activities are open.

Do’s and Don’ts of Langdon

Do:

  • Do explore the area’s nature trails and scenic views.
  • Do embrace the local culture and customs of Langdon.
  • Do indulge in the town’s local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in the parks and natural attractions.
  • Don’t forget to bring sunscreen and insect repellent when exploring the outdoors.
  • Don’t hesitate to ask locals for information or directions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Langdon

Langdon is a generally safe community with low crime rates. However, visitors should ensure they take safety precautions such as locking their cars and not keeping valuables lying around in plain view.

Health in Langdon

Langdon has excellent healthcare facilities, including a hospital with a 24-hour emergency department. Visitors should ensure that they have adequate travel insurance to cater to any medical emergencies.

Getting to Langdon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Langdon

The closest airport to Langdon is the Grand Forks International Airport (GFK), approximately 80 miles south of Langdon.

Bus Stations Near Langdon

Several bus services connect Langdon to nearby towns and other attractions such as Fargo and Grand Forks.

Other Options

Car rentals are available from the airport and Langdon, which gives visitors the freedom to explore the town and surrounding area at their leisure.

How to Get Around in Langdon

Langdon is small enough to get around on foot, but visitors may also rent a car to explore nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Langdon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Langdon can explore other neighboring areas of North Dakota on short day trips, such as Grand Forks and Fargo. Additionally, there are several exciting attractions in Manitoba, Canada, including Winnipeg, the capital and largest city, which are only a few hours` drive north from Langdon.

Conclusion

Langdon offers a unique and enjoyable experience to anyone inspired to learn and explore the town’s rich history and natural landscape. Visitors can enjoy a range of activities such as exploring the town’s museums and historical places, hiking or biking its scenic trails, bird watching at the Arrowwood National Wildlife Refuge, or trying unique local cuisine and beverages. Ensure you plan accordingly, whether it’s on a weeklong itinerary or a day trip, it’s important to explore and experience Langdon’s unique charm.
