Introduction to Barney

Barney is an off-the-beaten-path destination tucked away in the unspoiled Red River Valley of North Dakota’s Great Plains. This charming town boasts a rich cultural heritage and breathtaking natural beauty, making it an ideal destination for travelers looking for an authentic and unique experience. In this guide, we’ll take you on a journey through Barney’s top attractions and things to do, giving you a comprehensive look at this hidden gem of North Dakota.

List of Places to Visit in Barney

Here is a list of places to visit in Barney:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Sodbusters Museum - This museum features exhibits detailing the history of agriculture and settlement in the Red River Valley. Exhibits include a working blacksmith shop, a one-room schoolhouse, and a pioneer home that has been fully restored.
  • Fort Abercrombie State Historic Site - This historic site features a restored 1860s fort with barracks, a hospital, and a blockhouse.
  • Bonanzaville USA - This museum complex in nearby West Fargo features over 40 historic buildings, including a saloon, a church, a schoolhouse, and a train depot.

Natural Attractions of Barney: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sheyenne River Valley Scenic Byway - This 63-mile drive takes visitors through picturesque rural scenery along the Sheyenne River. The route includes stops at several historic sites and natural attractions.
  • The Great Northern Bike Trail - This 275-mile trail stretches through the heart of North Dakota, passing through Barney and several other charming small towns along the way.
  • Maple River State Wildlife Management Area - This 28,000-acre wildlife management area is home to a variety of native North Dakota species, including white-tailed deer and wild turkeys. Visitors can enjoy hiking, hunting, and wildlife viewing opportunities in this secluded natural area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Barney

Barney offers several fantastic hiking and biking routes to experience the stunning natural beauty of the Red River Valley. Here are some of the top routes:

  • Barnes County Scenic Trail - This flat, 10-mile trail travels through the scenic countryside west of Barney and offers stunning views of the surrounding farmland.
  • Sheyenne River Valley National Scenic Byway Trail - This multi-use trail follows the Sheyenne River along the scenic byway of the same name, offering 27 miles of picturesque North Dakota landscape.
  • Fort Ransom State Park Trails - A series of hiking trails in Fort Ransom State Park that range from easy to difficult, with distances from 1 to 8 miles.

Best Things to Do in Barney: Activities and Tours

In addition to the hiking and biking trails, Barney offers a variety of other fun and unique activities that visitors can enjoy. Here are some top things to do in Barney:

  • Take a horse-drawn carriage ride - Several local companies offer horse-drawn carriage rides through the scenic countryside around Barney, giving visitors a taste of old-fashioned transportation and beautiful scenery.
  • Visit the local wineries - The Red River Valley is home to several fantastic wineries, where visitors can get a taste of North Dakota’s stunning natural beauty and delicious wines.
  • Attend the annual Sunflower Festival - This festival celebrates North Dakota’s most iconic flower and features live music, food vendors, and plenty of family-friendly activities.

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for Barney, allowing visitors to experience all the top attractions and get a true feel for this charming town:

Food and Drink Scene of Barney

Barney is home to several great restaurants, cafes, and bars, offering a range of delicious dishes and locally-brewed beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lefse - A traditional Scandinavian flatbread made from potatoes, flour, and cream, often served with butter or jam.
  • Knoephla Soup - A hearty soup made from potatoes, dumplings, and ham.
  • Fried Walleye - A popular dish featuring North Dakota’s state fish, usually deep-fried and served with potatoes and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Buffalo Commons Brewery - This local brewery offers a range of artisanal beers made from locally-sourced ingredients.
  • Casselton Cider Company - This small-batch cidery uses locally-grown apples to create delicious and refreshing hard ciders.

Where to Stay in Barney: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Barney offers several accommodation options for visitors, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the top options:

  • Barney Inn - A charming bed and breakfast located in downtown Barney, featuring cozy rooms and a complimentary breakfast.
  • Lakeside Motel - A family-owned motel located on the shores of a nearby lake, offering affordable rooms and stunning views.
  • Cottonwood Campground - A budget-friendly option for visitors looking to camp in the countryside, offering tent and RV sites and basic amenities.

What to Buy in Barney: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Barney can bring home a taste of North Dakota’s unique culture and heritage with these souvenir and local product options:

  • Lefse-making kit - A kit that includes all the ingredients you need to make this traditional Scandinavian flatbread.
  • Bison Jerky - A delicious snack made from locally-raised bison meat.
  • North Dakota-made pottery - A range of artisanal pottery made by local artists and featuring North Dakota-inspired designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Barney

Barney is proud of its Scandinavian heritage, which influences much of the local culture and customs. Visitors should expect to encounter friendly and welcoming locals who are proud of their history and eager to share it with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Barney

The best time to visit Barney is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and the natural beauty of the Red River Valley is at its peak. Visitors can enjoy outdoor activities like hiking and biking, as well as attend local festivals and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Barney

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Barney:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs, which are deeply tied to Scandinavian heritage.
  • Spend time outdoors, exploring the stunning natural beauty of the Red River Valley.
  • Try the local cuisine, including traditional dishes like knoephla soup and lefse.

Don’t:

  • Don’t underestimate the importance of warm clothing if visiting during the winter months, when temperatures can be quite chilly.
  • Don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation with locals, who are often friendly and eager to share their knowledge of the area.

Safety and Health

Visitors to Barney should take precautions to ensure their safety and health during their visit.

Safety in Barney

  • Be aware of local weather conditions and any potential hazards associated with outdoor activities.
  • Use caution when driving on rural roads, which may be less well-maintained than those in more urban areas.
  • Use caution when exploring potentially hazardous areas like rivers or lakes.

Health in Barney

  • Be aware of local dangers associated with ticks and other insects, which can carry diseases like Lyme disease and West Nile Virus.
  • Use sunscreen and take other measures to protect yourself from the sun during outdoor activities.
  • Use caution when consuming local food and drink, especially if you have any food allergies or sensitivities.

Getting to Barney: Transportation Options and Logistics

Barney is relatively isolated and can be challenging to get to, but there are several transportation options available for visitors.

Airports Near Barney

  • Hector International Airport - Located in Fargo, about an hour’s drive from Barney, this airport offers several daily flights to major regional hubs.

Bus Stations Near Barney

  • Greyhound Bus Terminal - Located in Fargo, about an hour’s drive from Barney, this bus station offers daily bus service to cities throughout the region.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars - Visitors can rent a car from the Hector International Airport in Fargo and drive to Barney.

How to Get Around in Barney

Most visitors to Barney will want to have their own transportation in order to explore the town and surrounding areas. Rental cars are available at the Fargo airport, and several local companies offer horse-drawn carriage rides through the countryside.

Where to Go Next from Barney: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Barney is the perfect jumping-off point for exploring the surrounding area, which is full of natural beauty and cultural attractions. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider once you’ve explored everything Barney has to offer:

  • Fargo - The largest city in North Dakota, located about an hour’s drive from Barney. Fargo offers a range of cultural attractions, including museums, theaters, and art galleries.
  • Medora - Located in the rugged Badlands of North Dakota, about a 4-hour drive from Barney. Medora features stunning natural scenery and cultural attractions like the famous Medora Musical.
  • Devils Lake State Park - Located about 3 hours’ drive from Barney, this state park features stunning scenery and a range of outdoor activities like hiking, fishing, and boating.

Conclusion

Barney is a hidden gem of North Dakota, offering visitors a range of unique cultural attractions, stunning natural scenery, and outdoor activities. Whether you’re looking to explore the historic downtown, sample traditional North Dakota cuisine, or go hiking or biking through the countryside, Barney has something to offer everyone. So why wait?
