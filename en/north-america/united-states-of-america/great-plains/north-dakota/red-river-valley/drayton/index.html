Introduction to Drayton

Drayton is a quaint little town located in the Red River Valley of North Dakota, in the heart of the Great Plains. Known for its scenic landscape, rich history, and friendly small-town charm, Drayton is a hidden gem that offers visitors a chance to experience rural America at its finest.

Whether you’re interested in exploring the town’s museums and historical sites, hiking through the beautiful natural landscapes, indulging in local cuisine, or simply relaxing in the great outdoors, Drayton has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll show you the best places to stay, things to do, and sights to see in Drayton.

List of Places to Visit in Drayton

Here is a list of the must-visit places in Drayton:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kittson County Historical Society Museum: This museum showcases the history of Kittson County and the Red River Valley through exhibits and artifacts from the 19th and 20th centuries. Visitors can learn about the area’s pioneering settlers, early industries, Native American culture, and more.

  • Historic Fort Pembina: Fort Pembina is a historic military fort that dates back to the mid-19th century. Visitors can explore the restored fort buildings, learn about the daily life of soldiers and their families, and see exhibits on the history of the fur trade and the town of Pembina.

  • Sons of Norway Lodge: The Sons of Norway Lodge is a historic Norwegian-American cultural center that hosts events, exhibits, and classes on Scandinavian culture, traditions, and cuisine.

Natural Attractions of Drayton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pembina Gorge State Recreation Area: This 2,800-acre park offers hiking trails, campgrounds, picnic areas, and scenic overlooks. Visitors can explore the park’s rugged terrain, which features forests, prairies, and cliffs overlooking the Pembina River.

  • Red River State Recreation Area: This 36-acre park on the banks of the Red River offers hiking trails, a boat ramp, and picnic areas. Visitors can enjoy a peaceful walk along the river, or go fishing or boating in its waters.

  • North Dakota Wildlife Management Area: This 2,330-acre nature reserve is home to a diverse range of plants and animals, including whitetail deer, waterfowl, and songbirds. Visitors can enjoy bird-watching, hiking, and wildlife photography in this natural paradise.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Drayton

  • Pembina Gorge Trail: This 30-mile trail winds through the scenic Pembina Gorge State Recreation Area, offering hikers and bikers stunning views of the valley and the Pembina River.

  • Red River State Recreation Area Trail: This 2-mile trail follows the banks of the Red River, offering hikers a peaceful stroll through the woods and along the river.

  • North Dakota Wildlife Management Area Trail: This 5-mile trail takes hikers through the diverse landscape of the North Dakota Wildlife Management Area, where they can see a variety of wildlife and scenic vistas.

Best Things to Do in Drayton: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking on the Red River: Rent a kayak from a local outfitter and explore the serene waters of the Red River. See the picturesque landscapes from a new angle and enjoy the calm waters of this tranquil river.

  • Take a Guided Fishing Tour: Drayton is known for its great fishing opportunities. Take a guided fishing tour and spend the day fishing for walleye, northern pike, and other native species.

  • Visit an Amish Farm: Take a tour of an Amish farm and learn about their way of life, traditions and beliefs. Taste their produce which is fresh and organic.

Week-Long Itinerary for Drayton

Day 1: Visit the Kittson County Historical Society Museum, take a relaxing stroll along the Red River State Recreation Area Trail, and enjoy dinner at a local restaurant.

Day 2: Explore the unique Scandinavian culture at the Sons of Norway Lodge, go kayaking on the Red River, and visit a local farm for a farm-to-table meal.

Day 3: Hike the Pembina Gorge Trail, go fishing on the Pembina River, and enjoy a picnic in the park.

Day 4: Take a guided fishing tour, and visit the historic Fort Pembina.

Day 5: Visit the North Dakota Wildlife Management Area for a scenic hike, and enjoy a sunset stroll along the banks of the Red River.

Day 6: Explore nearby towns and visit the nearby Woodland Resort and Campground for a day of relaxation and picnic.

Day 7: Finish your trip with a peaceful morning walk in nearby natural destinations, like the Park River State Nature Preserve or Agassiz National Wildlife Refuge.

Food and Drink Scene of Drayton

  • Must-Try Dishes

  • Lefse: A traditional Norwegian flatbread made with potatoes, flour, and cream. It is typically served with butter and sugar, and is a favorite snack of locals.

  • Knoephla Soup: This hearty soup is made with potatoes, cream, dumplings, and chunks of chicken or beef. It’s the perfect meal to warm up with on a chilly day.

  • Walleye: Freshwater fish that is highly prized for its sweet, delicate flavor. It is typically fried or baked and served with potatoes or a side of vegetables.

  • Local Beverages

  • Schell’s Beer: A local brewery’s beer made in New Ulm, Minnesota. Be sure to taste this classic craft beer during your trip.

  • Winehaven Winery: Located a short drive from Drayton, Winehaven Winery is a family-owned winery that produces a variety of award-winning wines from locally grown grapes.

Local Culture and Customs of Drayton

Drayton has a rich history and culture that is reflected in its cuisine, architecture, and traditions. Norwegian, Finnish, and German settlers brought their unique cultures and customs to the area, which can still be seen in the town’s vibrant community events, festivals, and local attractions.

Best Time to Visit Drayton

The best time to visit Drayton is during the summer when the weather is warm and sunny, and the town comes alive with festivals, fairs, and outdoor events. Fall and winter can also be a great time to visit Drayton, when the landscapes are transformed by autumn leaves or snowdrifts.

Do’s and Don’ts of Drayton

Safety and Health

  • Safety in Drayton

Drayton is a very safe town with low crime rates, however, always be careful while exploring the natural landscapes. Make sure to follow all safety measures, especially if you’re kayaking or hiking.

  • Health in Drayton

Drayton has one of the best health care facilities in the region with CHI St. Alexius Medical Center being the nearest hospital located 43 miles away in Grafton, North Dakota.
