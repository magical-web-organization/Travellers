Introduction to Pembina

Pembina is a small town located in North Dakota’s beautiful Red River Valley. Situated in the Great Plains region of the United States of America, Pembina is a charming destination that offers a mix of historical significance and natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Pembina

Whether you’re looking to explore the town’s cultural heritage or venture out into the great outdoors, Pembina has something for everyone. Here are some top places to visit in Pembina:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Pembina State Historic Site: Explore the history of this former military fort, which played a critical role in the fur trade and the settlement of the area.
  • Pembina State Museum: Discover the story of the town’s early settlers and Native American heritage at the state museum, located across the street from Fort Pembina.
  • United States/Canada Border Crossing: Visit the Peace Garden State Park to see the only 24-hour port of entry between the United States and Canada.

Natural Attractions of Pembina: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pembina Gorge State Recreation Area: Go hiking, biking, horseback riding, or hunting in the gorgeous Pembina Gorge, which features 180-foot limestone cliffs and forested river bottoms.
  • Tetrault Woods State Forest: Experience the beauty of North Dakota’s forests and wildlife habitats at this state forest, which offers camping, hiking, and hunting opportunities.
  • Arvilla Wildlife Management Area: Take a nature walk through this lush wetland area, home to a variety of bird species and other wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pembina

For outdoor enthusiasts, Pembina offers a variety of hiking and biking trails, ranging from easy nature walks to challenging mountain bike loops. Some popular routes include:

  • Pembina Gorge Trailhead: Starting at the trailhead in Walhalla, this 12-mile trail takes you through the Gorge’s stunning limestone cliffs and river views.
  • Lake Renwick Trailhead: This 7-mile loop is perfect for an afternoon bike ride, with scenic views of Lake Renwick and the surrounding wilderness.
  • Riverview Trailhead: Starting in the town of Pembina, this 8-mile trail combines natural scenery with cultural landmarks, including the United States/Canada Border Crossing and the historic downtown area.

Best Things to Do in Pembina: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of things to do in Pembina, whether you’re looking for adventure or relaxation. Here are some top activities and tours to consider:

  • Horseback Riding at Bear Den Ranch: Take a guided trail ride through the scenic Pembina Gorge and surrounding countryside.
  • Guided Fishing Tour: Join a local guide and try your hand at catching some of the area’s abundant fish species, including walleye, northern pike, and bass.
  • Historic Walking Tour: Stroll through the charming downtown area and learn about the town’s rich history, from its fur trade roots to its early settlement by European immigrants.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pembina

To make the most of your trip to Pembina, consider this week-long itinerary, which combines the town’s top attractions with some off-the-beaten-path gems:

Day Activity
1 Explore Fort Pembina State Historic Site and Pembina State Museum, then head to Tetrault Woods State Forest for a hike and picnic lunch.
2 Take a guided fishing tour or horseback ride in the morning, then visit the United States/Canada Border Crossing and Peace Garden State Park in the afternoon.
3 Drive to Walhalla and hike the Pembina Gorge Trailhead, stopping for lunch at one of the area’s restaurants.
4 Check out the historic downtown area on a guided walking tour, then visit the Arvilla Wildlife Management Area for a nature walk.
5 Go on a day trip to Grand Forks, just 70 miles south of Pembina, and explore the city’s museums, parks, and restaurants.
6 Spend the day at Bear Den Ranch, horseback riding, hiking, and enjoying nature.
7 Relax and unwind at your hotel or guesthouse, or take a scenic drive through the countryside and visit some of the area’s artisanal shops and farms.

Food and Drink Scene of Pembina

While Pembina may be a small town, it’s big on flavor. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lefse: A Norwegian flatbread made with potatoes and flour, often served with butter or jelly.
  • Walleye: A freshwater fish native to the area, often served fried, grilled, or baked.
  • Knoephla Soup: A German-inspired soup made with chicken, potatoes, and dumplings.

Local Beverages

  • Canad Inns Destination Center: Stop by the hotel’s lounge for a craft beer or cocktail, featuring local North Dakota spirits and brews.
  • Pembina Hills Vineyard and Winery: Sample some of the winery’s award-winning wines, made from locally grown grapes and fruits.

Where to Stay in Pembina: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxurious hotel or a cozy guesthouse, Pembina has a range of accommodations to suit any budget. Some top options include:

  • Canad Inns Destination Center: This hotel features 101 guest rooms, an indoor water park, and a pub with craft beer and cocktails.
  • Pembina River Inn: A family-owned hotel located on the main street of downtown Pembina, offering comfortable rooms and free breakfast.
  • Bigfoot Motel: A budget-friendly motel with clean rooms and a convenient location just off Highway 81.

What to Buy in Pembina: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

While in Pembina, consider picking up some local products and souvenirs to take home with you. Some popular items include:

  • Pottery from Wren’s Nest: This artisanal studio sells one-of-a-kind pottery pieces, featuring North Dakota-inspired designs and motifs.
  • Honey from the Bee’s Knees: Stock up on all-natural honey and beeswax products, made by a local beekeeper.
  • Jams and Jellies from Pebbledash Farm: This family-run farm sells homemade jams and jellies, made from locally sourced fruits and berries.

Local Culture and Customs of Pembina

Pembina is a friendly and welcoming town, with a rich cultural heritage that reflects the area’s diverse history. Some local customs and traditions include:

  • Ojibwe and Metis Heritage: Many residents of Pembina are of Native American or mixed heritage, and the town celebrates this heritage with events and festivals throughout the year.
  • Fur Trade Legacy: Pembina played an important role in the early fur trade, and this legacy is still evident in the town’s museums, historic sites, and cultural events.
  • Norwegian and German Influences: Many of Pembina’s early settlers were of Norwegian or German descent, and the town celebrates this heritage through music, food, and dance.

Best Time to Visit Pembina

The best time to visit Pembina depends on your interests and preferences. Summer and fall are peak seasons for outdoor activities and festivals, while winter offers opportunities for snowmobiling, cross-country skiing, and ice fishing. Spring is a good time to visit for bird watching and wildflower hikes.

  • Take advantage of the many outdoor activities and natural beauty in the area.
  • Bring comfortable shoes and clothing for hiking and exploring.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful to locals or other travelers.

Safety and Health

Before traveling to Pembina, it’s important to be aware of any safety or health concerns. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Pembina

  • Take precautions when engaging in outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, hunting, and fishing. Be aware of potential wildlife hazards, including snakes, bears, and cougars.
  • Follow local regulations and safety guidelines when driving, particularly during winter months when roads may be icy or snow-packed.
  • Be aware of potential weather hazards, including severe thunderstorms and tornadoes.

Health in Pembina

  • Be prepared for extreme temperatures and varying weather conditions, particularly during winter months when temperatures can drop well below freezing.
  • Remember to stay hydrated during outdoor activities, and protect your skin from sun and wind damage.
  • Take any necessary precautions to prevent mosquito-borne illnesses, particularly during summer months when mosquito populations are high.

Getting to Pembina: Transportation Options and Logistics

Pembina is located about 90 miles north of Grand Forks, North Dakota, and about 75 miles south of Winnipeg, Manitoba. The town is easily accessible by car, with several major highways running through the area. Here are some transportation options to consider when traveling to Pembina:

Airports Near Pembina

  • Grand Forks International Airport in Grand Forks, North Dakota, is about 90 miles south of Pembina.
  • Winnipeg James Armstrong Richardson International Airport in Winnipeg, Manitoba, is about 75 miles north of Pembina.

Bus Stations Near Pembina

  • Jefferson Lines offers bus service to several nearby towns and cities, with connections to major regional and national bus lines.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: Several major car rental companies operate in the Pembina area, with rental cars available at both airports and nearby cities.
  • Taxi and Ride-Sharing Services: Local taxi and ride-sharing services may be available in the Pembina area.

How to Get Around in Pembina

While in Pembina, renting a car is the easiest way to get around the town and surrounding areas. However, the town is also walkable, with several attractions and restaurants located within walking distance of each other.

Where to Go Next from Pembina: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip beyond Pembina, consider visiting one of these nearby destinations:

  • Winnipeg: Just 75 miles to the north, Winnipeg is a vibrant city with a rich cultural scene, museums, galleries, and restaurants.
  • Turtle Mountain Provincial Park: Located about 100 miles west of Pembina, this park offers hiking, camping, and wildlife watching opportunities.
  • Lake of the Woods: About 60 miles east of Pembina, this scenic lake offers fishing, boating, and other water activities.
