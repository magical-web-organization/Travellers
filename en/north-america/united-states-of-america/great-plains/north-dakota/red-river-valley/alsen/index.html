Introduction to Alsen

Located in the heart of Red River Valley, in North Dakota, Alsen is a charming small town that offers a peaceful retreat for nature lovers and history buffs. With a population of around 50 people, the town features spectacular natural scenery and historical landmarks that provide visitors with a unique experience. In this travel guide, you will discover everything you need to know about planning an unforgettable trip to Alsen.

List of Places to Visit in Alsen

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Alsen Depot Museum

If you’re interested in history, Alsen Depot Museum is a must-visit destination. This historic depot built-in 1902 has been turned into a museum that displays artifacts from the town’s past. From traditional farming equipment to old household items, the museum provides an insight into the life of early settlers in Alsen.

Saint Olaf Lutheran Church

Founded in 1906, the Saint Olaf Lutheran Church is an important landmark of the town. With its beautiful interior decor, stained-glass windows, and peaceful ambiance, the church provides visitors with a glimpse of the religious and cultural traditions of early settlers.

Natural Attractions of Alsen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Alsen Wildlife Management Area

Covering an area of 1,300 acres, the Alsen Wildlife Management Area offers visitors an opportunity to explore the natural beauty of North Dakota. The area features a mix of grassland, wetland, and woodland habitats and is home to a variety of wildlife including coyotes, beavers, and migratory birds. Visitors can hike, bike, or birdwatch on the trails that crisscross the area.

North Fork of the Turtle River

If you’re looking for a serene spot to relax or fish, the North Fork of the Turtle River is the perfect place to visit. The clear waters of the river flow through the flat plains of North Dakota creating a picturesque landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alsen

One of the best ways to explore the natural beauty of Alsen is by hiking or biking. Here are some of the top routes:

  • Turtle River State Park Trails
  • Kellys Slough National Wildlife Refuge Trails
  • Icelandic State Park Trails

Best Things to Do in Alsen: Activities and Tours

Horseback Riding

For a unique experience, visitors can take a horseback riding tour of the North Dakota countryside. Several ranches in the area offer guided tours that take visitors through scenic trails and provide an opportunity to learn about horse husbandry.

Fairdale Roller Rink

For some old-fashioned fun, visitors can lace-up their skates and visit the Fairdale Roller Rink. Built-in 1942, the rink offers a nostalgic experience where visitors can relive their childhood memories and skate to classic tunes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Alsen

Day 1: Arrive in Alsen, check-in to accommodation, visit Saint Olaf Lutheran Church.

Day 2: Explore Alsen Depot Museum, take a nature walk around Alsen Lake.

Day 3: Drive to Turtle River State Park, hike one of the trails, have a picnic lunch.

Day 4: Visit Icelandic State Park and take a guided horseback riding tour.

Day 5: Birdwatching at Kellys Slough National Wildlife Refuge, based on the migratory season visit.

Day 6: Take a day trip to the nearby towns of Grafton or Drayton, explore local shops and restaurants.

Day 7: Spend a day relaxing at Alsen Wildlife Management Area, fishing or enjoying nature.

Food and Drink Scene of Alsen

Must-Try Dishes

Knephla Soup

A traditional North Dakota soup made with dumplings and chicken, this dish is hearty and comforting.

Knoephla Kuchen

A savory pastry made with potatoes, onions, and cream, this dish is a staple in German cuisine.

Local Beverages

Dakota Harvest Wheat Beer

A local microbrewery that creates unique and flavorful beers, visit the Dakota Harvest Brewery for a sample of their award-winning wheat beer.

Where to Stay in Alsen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Alsen Guest House

A charming bed and breakfast that features comfortable rooms and homemade breakfast, the Alsen Guest House is perfect for a cozy getaway.

Icelandic State Park Campground

For a more outdoorsy experience, visitors can camp at the Icelandic State Park Campground. Featuring campsites with stunning views of Lake Renwick and the Pembina Gorge, this is a popular option for families and groups.

What to Buy in Alsen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Dakota Soaps

Handmade in North Dakota, Dakota Soaps are made with organic and natural ingredients and feature scents inspired by the local landscape.

Red River Valley Honey

Produced in the region, Red River Valley Honey is delicious and high in nutritional value.

Local Culture and Customs of Alsen

North Dakota is known for its traditional German and Scandinavian heritage, which is reflected in the local customs and traditions. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local way of life and learn about the history and cultural significance of the town’s landmarks.

Best Time to Visit Alsen

The best time to visit Alsen is during the summer months from May to September when the weather is mild and pleasant. The autumn months from September to November, when the fall foliage is at its peak, is also a great time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alsen

Do:

  • Respect the local way of life and traditions.
  • Take time to learn about the history and cultural significance of the town’s landmarks.
  • Bring sturdy shoes and comfortable clothing for hiking and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Ignore safety precautions when participating in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Alsen

Alsen is a safe town with a low crime rate. Visitors should exercise common sense and caution when participating in outdoor activities and follow safety guidelines provided by guides.

Health in Alsen

It is recommended to carry insect repellent as the area is known to have mosquitoes and ticks. Visitors should also bring sunscreen and apply it liberally as the sun can be harsh.

Getting to Alsen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Alsen

  • Grand Forks International Airport (GFK) - 55 miles
  • Hector International Airport (FAR) - 87 miles

Bus Stations Near Alsen

  • Grafton Bus Station - 18 miles
  • Cavalier Bus Station - 30 miles

Other Options

  • Rental cars are available at both Grand Forks International and Hector International airports and in nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Alsen

The best way to get around in Alsen is by car or bike. Bikes are available for rent in nearby towns, and there are several car rental agencies at the airports.

Where to Go Next from Alsen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Pembina Gorge State Recreation Area

With its stunning views and abundant wildlife, Pembina Gorge State Recreation Area is a must-visit destination for nature lovers. Visitors can hike or bike the trails, go fishing, or take a guided tour of the area.

Icelandic State Park

Located on the shore of Lake Renwick, Icelandic State Park is a scenic park that offers visitors an opportunity to explore the great outdoors. The park features hiking and cross-country ski trails, campsites, and fishing spots.

Conclusion

Alsen, North Dakota, is a hidden gem that provides visitors with an opportunity to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and enjoy the natural beauty and local culture of the region. From hiking and biking to cultural attractions and local cuisine, Alsen has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and discover the charm of the North Dakota countryside.
