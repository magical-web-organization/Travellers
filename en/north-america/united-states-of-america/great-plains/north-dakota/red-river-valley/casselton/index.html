Introduction to Casselton

Located in the bustling Red River Valley of North Dakota, Casselton is a hidden gem that’s known for its outdoor adventures, rich history, and friendly locals. This charming town is the perfect destination for nature enthusiasts, history buffs, and anyone who wants to escape the hustle and bustle of the city. In this Casselton travel guide, we’ve rounded up the top attractions, activities, and must-try dishes to help you plan your trip to this peaceful and picturesque town.

List of Places to Visit in Casselton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Casselton has a rich history, which can be seen in the numerous museums and historical sites scattered throughout the town. The George Soeffker Museum is a must-visit for history buffs. This museum features exhibits that showcase the history of the town, from its founding to its role in the Civil War. Another notable museum is the Casselton Heritage Center, which is a volunteer-run museum that offers visitors a glimpse into the town’s past through various exhibits and hands-on activities.

Natural Attractions of Casselton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Casselton is home to some of the most picturesque natural attractions in North Dakota. The Sheyenne River Valley Scenic Byway is a popular spot for nature lovers and offers stunning views of the surrounding grasslands and forest. The Central Valley Golf Course is another popular attraction that offers visitors a chance to unwind while playing a round of golf. For those who love hiking and biking, the North Country National Scenic Trail is a must-visit. This trail spans across 4,600 miles, offering plenty of opportunities to explore the great outdoors.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Casselton

Hiking and biking are the perfect way to explore the natural beauty of Casselton. The North Country National Scenic Trail is a great option for hikers who want to take in the scenic views. For something more challenging, the Sheyenne River Valley Scenic Byway offers hikers and bikers a chance to explore the rugged terrain and abundant wildlife. The Central Valley Golf Course is another popular spot for those looking for a leisurely stroll, while enjoying the picturesque views of the golf course.

Best Things to Do in Casselton: Activities and Tours

Casselton has a wide range of activities and tours that cater to all types of travelers. The Boondocks Trail is a popular spot for horseback riding and offers visitors a chance to explore the area’s stunning views and wildlife. For something more interactive, the Fargo Air Museum offers visitors a chance to see vintage aircraft, including World War II planes and replicas. Another popular activity is taking a guided tour of the city, which is a great way to learn about Casselton’s rich history and culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Casselton

Day 1: Visit the George Soeffker Museum and learn about the town’s history. Day 2: Hike or bike along the North Country National Scenic Trail. Day 3: Explore the Central Valley Golf Course before heading out to the Sheyenne River Valley Scenic Byway. Day 4: Take a guided tour of the city and learn about Casselton’s culture and customs. Day 5: Visit the Fargo Air Museum and see vintage aircraft. Day 6: Horseback ride along the Boondocks Trail. Day 7: Spend the day at the Casselton Heritage Center and learn about the town’s past.

Food and Drink Scene of Casselton

Must-Try Dishes

Casselton is known for its hearty and flavorful cuisine. One of the most popular dishes is the German sausage, which can be found at numerous restaurants and cafes around town. Another must-try dish is the hotdish, which is a classic comfort food made with meat, vegetables, and a creamy sauce. For something sweet, try the lefse, a traditional Scandinavian flatbread that is often served with butter and brown sugar.

Local Beverages

North Dakota is known for its craft beer, and Casselton is no exception. Several breweries and taprooms, such as Kilstone Brewing and Sterlyng’s Beer, offer visitors a chance to sample some of the region’s best beers. For those who prefer wine, the Red Trail Vineyard is a great option. Here, visitors can sample a variety of wines, including locally produced reds and whites.

Where to Stay in Casselton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Casselton offers a range of options for travelers looking for a place to stay. For those who prefer hotels, the Casselton Inn and the Days Inn by Wyndham are both popular choices. For budget travelers, the Maple River Bed & Breakfast is a cozy and comfortable option, while the Casselton RV Park is perfect for those traveling with an RV or campervan.

What to Buy in Casselton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Casselton is known for its locally made products, such as crafts, maple syrup, and honey. The Casselton Farmers Market is a great place to find these items, as well as fresh produce and baked goods. Another popular souvenir is the Casselton-branded merchandise, which can be found at many of the town’s gift shops and boutiques.

Local Culture and Customs of Casselton

Casselton is a small town that is deeply rooted in its traditions and customs. Visitors are encouraged to respect the town’s history and culture, and to take part in local events and festivals. Casselton is known for its strong sense of community, and visitors will often find themselves welcomed with open arms.

Best Time to Visit Casselton

Casselton is an all-season destination, with each season offering a unique experience. Spring and summer are the best times to enjoy the outdoor activities and farmer’s markets. Fall is when the town comes alive with colorful foliage and harvest festivals, while winter is perfect for skiing, snowshoeing, and other winter sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Casselton

Do:

  • Respect the town’s history and culture
  • Try the German sausage and other local dishes
  • Take part in local events and festivals
  • Visit the farmers market

Don’t:

  • Disrespect other people’s beliefs or customs
  • Litter or damage the natural attractions
  • Ignore the town’s safety guidelines

Safety and Health

Safety in Casselton

Casselton is a safe town, but visitors are advised to take precautions and stay alert. Always be aware of your surroundings, and keep an eye on your belongings. Drink responsibly and never leave your drink unattended. If you’re traveling alone or at night, it’s best to stick to well-lit and popular areas.

Health in Casselton

North Dakota is known for its harsh winters, so it’s important to pack warm clothes and stay hydrated. Visitors should also be aware of ticks during the warmer months and take appropriate precautions, such as wearing long-sleeved clothing and using bug spray.

Getting to Casselton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Casselton

The nearest airport to Casselton is Hector International Airport in Fargo, which offers several non-stop flights to major cities in the United States.

Bus Stations Near Casselton

Greyhound Bus Lines has a station in Fargo, which is a 20-minute drive from Casselton.

Other Options

Renting a car is a good option for travelers who want to explore the surrounding areas. Taxi services are available in Casselton, but it’s best to book in advance.

How to Get Around in Casselton

Casselton is a small town, and many of the attractions are within walking distance. For those who prefer to drive, renting a car or using a taxi service is an option.

Where to Go Next from Casselton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Casselton is a great starting point for exploring the surrounding areas, such as Fargo and Grand Forks. Those looking for outdoor activities can head to the Sheyenne National Grassland, while history buffs can visit the nearby Fort Abercrombie State Historic Site.

Conclusion

Casselton is a small town that packs a big punch when it comes to natural beauty, history, and culture. Whether you’re a hiker, a foodie, or a history buff, there’s something for everyone in this charming town. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip and make the most of your visit to Casselton.
