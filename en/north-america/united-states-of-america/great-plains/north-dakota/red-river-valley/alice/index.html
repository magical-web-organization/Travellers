A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Alice, North Dakota

If you are looking for a peaceful vacation destination in the United States, then Alice in North Dakota is the perfect place for you. Situated in the Great Plains region of North America, Alice is a small town with a rich history, natural beauty, and a unique culture.

List of Places to Visit in Alice

Alice may seem small, but it has plenty of exciting attractions to explore. Here are some of the absolute must-visit places in and around Alice:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Totten State Historic Site: This historical site located at the southern end of Devil’s Lake offers an excellent insight into the life of soldiers and Native Americans in the 19th century.
  • Spirit Lake Casino and Resort: A perfect place to try your luck, this casino also has an excellent restaurant and hotel.
  • Historic Prairie Village: A museum complex with a variety of exhibits exploring the area’s history and culture. It includes a general store, a school, and a chapel.

Natural Attractions of Alice: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Devil’s Lake State Park: The largest natural lake in North Dakota, Devil’s Lake is a prominent attraction, with its clear waters and breathtaking views. Perfect for fishing, boating, and camping.
  • Sullys Hill National Game Preserve: The Preserve is home to an incredible range of wildlife, including bison, pronghorns, and even some rare bird species. The multiple hiking trails offer great opportunities to explore the natural beauty of the park.
  • Turtle River State Park: Spread over 784 acres, this park offers excellent camping facilities, hiking trails, and wildlife viewing opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alice

Alice is an incredible destination for hikers and bikers with plenty of beautiful trails to explore. Here are some of the best:

  • The Greenway Trail: A perfect trail for bikers and hikers, this paved trail runs along the Red River and offers spectacular views.
  • Grand Forks Park: This paved trail circles the city’s outskirts and provides easy walking, running, or biking opportunities. The goal of this trail is to promote physical activities and lead a healthy lifestyle.

Best Things to Do in Alice: Activities and Tours

  • Go Fishing: There are several places in and around Alice to fish, including Devils Lake, Stump Lake, and Red River.
  • Attend Traditional Powwow: Held in the months of July and August, the traditional powwow is a great way to learn more about the region’s culture and traditions.
  • Try Your Luck: The Spirit Lake Casino and Resort offer visitors excellent opportunities to try their luck. You’ll find electronic gaming machines, blackjack, and poker.

Food and Drink Scene of Alice

Alice has no shortage of fantastic places to eat, from casual diners to upscale restaurants. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lefse: A traditional Norwegian flatbread made from potatoes, flour, and cream that can be eaten plain or with butter and cinnamon sugar.
  • Knoephla Soup: A filling soup with dumplings and vegetables in a creamy broth.

Local Beverages

  • Dakota Sunflower Wine: Known for its light and fruity flavor, this wine is a must-try.
  • Lakota Coffee: Rich, smooth, and invigorating, this coffee is made with locally grown beans.

Where to Stay in Alice: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Alice offers several accommodation options, including modern hotels and guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Holiday Inn Express Devils Lake: An excellent option that offers comfortable rooms, great amenities, and a complimentary breakfast.
  • Woodland Resort: A budget-friendly option located on the shores of Devil’s Lake, offering camping sites and cabins for rent.
  • Lakeview Lodge: A cozy lodge with beautiful balconies overlooking the scenic views of Devils Lake.

What to Buy in Alice: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique gifts to bring back home, Alice offers plenty of options:

  • Turtle Mountain Chippewa Crafts: A great place to buy handmade traditional arts and crafts created by the Native American community in North Dakota.
  • Lewis and Clark Interpretive Center Gift Shop: Go here for some interesting books, maps, art, and souvenirs about the Lewis and Clark expedition.
  • Northwoods Candy Emporium: Buy premium chocolates, fudge, and candies created from original recipes in this fun little store

Local Culture and Customs of Alice

The Native American Reservations in the area have shaped the development of Alice’s culture and customs. Visitors should respect and appreciate diversity in this community. Musical flourishing, art, and dance are integral parts of the Stump Lake and Spirit Lake native tribes. Always have a friendly approach and learn more about the culture.

Best Time to Visit Alice

The best time to visit Alice is in the summer (June to August), when the weather is warm, and the outdoor activities are plentiful. This is the peak season, so be sure to book your accommodation in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alice

Here are some critical do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Alice:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Wear comfortable shoes; there’s plenty of walking and hiking to do!
  • Bring sunscreen and insect repellent, since some of Alice’s best attractions are outdoors.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter.
  • Don’t smoke in public places without checking the local laws.
  • Don’t take pictures without asking permission in the Native American community areas.

Safety and Health

Alice is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, it’s always best to be cautious and take necessary precautions. Here are some tips:

Safety in Alice

  • Be aware of your surroundings when walking or hiking.
  • Keep your valuables securely locked away.
  • Stay in well-lit areas, especially during the evenings.

Health in Alice

  • Bring insect repellent, because insects can be a problem in the area.
  • Drink plenty of water to avoid dehydration, particularly when engaging in outdoor activities.
  • If you’re allergic, be careful when stepping on dead buffalo grass stalks, which are present in the region.
