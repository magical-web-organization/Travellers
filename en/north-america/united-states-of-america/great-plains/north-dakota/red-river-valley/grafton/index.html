Introduction to Grafton

Grafton is a picturesque city located in the Red River Valley, in North Dakota, in the Great Plains, in the United States of America. It is a welcoming community with a rich history and a vibrant culture that offers plenty of attractions for visitors of all ages and interests. Whether you are looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or delicious food and drink, Grafton has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take a closer look at the top places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and other helpful tips to make your trip to Grafton a memorable one.

List of Places to Visit in Grafton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Leistikow Park – A historic park that features statues and monuments commemorating the lives of local heroes. The park is also a popular spot for picnics and outdoor activities.
  • Historical District – This charming district showcases the architecture and history of Grafton’s early settlers, with several buildings that have been preserved and restored to their original condition.
  • Grafton Pioneer Village – A must-visit for history buffs, this village offers a glimpse into Grafton’s past with a collection of original buildings, artifacts, and exhibits.
  • United States Custom House – Located on the banks of the Park River, this historic building once served as a customs office and post office, and today houses the Grafton Chamber of Commerce.
  • International Peace Garden – Just a short drive from Grafton, this 2,339-acre garden is a symbol of peace and friendship between the United States and Canada. It features spectacular gardens, outdoor sculptures, and a chapel.

Natural Attractions of Grafton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Leaf Mountain – A popular spot for hiking and birdwatching, this natural area features stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Park River – This scenic river is perfect for fishing, kayaking, and canoeing, or simply relaxing by the water’s edge.
  • Frost Fire Park – This winter wonderland is home to a ski resort, snow tubing hill, and snowmobile trails. During the summer months, visitors can enjoy hiking, biking, and scenic chairlift rides.
  • Grafton Dam – This manmade lake is a popular spot for fishing and boating, with several picnic areas and campsites available.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Grafton

  • Pembina Gorge State Recreation Area – A great spot for hiking and biking, with miles of trails that wind through dense forests and scenic valleys.
  • Grafton Lions Park – This park offers a 1.5-mile walking and biking trail that loops around the park and provides beautiful views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Leaf Mountain – Home to several hiking trails, this natural area offers stunning views of the rolling hills and forests.

Best Things to Do in Grafton: Activities and Tours

  • Grafton Summerfest – This annual festival celebrates the local culture with live music, food vendors, and fun activities for all ages.
  • Birding – Grafton is a birdwatcher’s paradise, with over 300 species of birds that call the area home. The Pembina Gorge State Recreation Area is a great spot to start.
  • Grafton Speedway – For racing fans, this quarter-mile dirt track offers a thrilling experience with regular races throughout the summer.
  • Farmers Market – Held on Saturdays from June through September, this market offers fresh produce, baked goods, and crafts from local vendors.
  • Hunting and Fishing – Grafton’s natural beauty and abundance of wildlife make it a popular destination for hunting and fishing enthusiasts.

Food and Drink Scene of Grafton

Must-Try Dishes

  • Walleye – A popular freshwater fish that is often served pan-fried or grilled.
  • Kuchen – A traditional German pastry that is a local favorite in Grafton.
  • Bison Burger – A healthier alternative to beef, bison burgers are a staple of the American Prairie.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer – Grafton is home to several microbreweries that offer a variety of delicious craft beers.
  • Local Wine – The fertile river valley surrounding Grafton is perfect for growing grapes, and several wineries offer tastings and tours.

Where to Stay in Grafton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Walsh County Historical Society Guest House – This historic guesthouse offers comfortable accommodations in the heart of the Historical District.
  • Evergreen Motel – A budget-friendly option that offers clean and comfortable rooms and is located near the city center.
  • Cobblestone Inn & Suites – A modern hotel that features comfortable rooms, a fitness center, and a complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Grafton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pottery – Grafton is home to several talented potters who create beautiful and unique pieces.
  • Jams and Jellies – Many local farmers sell homemade jams and jellies made with fresh fruits and berries.
  • Handmade Crafts – The farmers market and local boutiques offer a variety of handmade crafts that make great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Grafton

Grafton is a friendly and welcoming community that values its history and traditions. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and embrace the unique culture of the area. Don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation with a local and learn more about life in Grafton.

Best Time to Visit Grafton

The best time to visit Grafton is during the summer months, when the weather is pleasant and many outdoor activities and events take place. Fall is also a beautiful time to visit, with vibrant foliage and many local festivals and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Grafton

Do:

  • Embrace the local culture and customs.
  • Enjoy the natural beauty and outdoor activities.
  • Try the local cuisine and craft beer.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.
  • Litter or damage natural areas.
  • Ignore safety guidelines.

Safety and Health

Safety in Grafton

Grafton is a safe and welcoming community, but visitors should be aware of potential hazards while enjoying outdoor activities. Take proper precautions while hiking, biking, or fishing, and follow local rules and regulations. In case of emergency, dial 911.

Health in Grafton

Grafton has several healthcare providers, including a hospital and clinics. Visitors should ensure they have adequate medical insurance coverage during their stay.

Getting to Grafton: Transportation Options and Logistics

  • Airports Near Grafton – The closest major airport is Hector International Airport (FAR) in Fargo, North Dakota, about 80 miles away. Another option is Grand Forks International Airport (GFK), about 50 miles away.
  • Bus Stations Near Grafton – Greyhound Bus Lines operates in Grand Forks, which is the closest bus station to Grafton.
  • Other Options – Renting a car is the most convenient way to get around and explore Grafton and the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Grafton

Grafton is easily walkable, and many attractions are within walking distance of each other. Biking and driving are also popular options, and car rentals are available at the airport.

Where to Go Next from Grafton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Grand Forks – Just a short drive from Grafton, this college town offers plenty of dining, shopping, and entertainment options.
  • Winnipeg – Located just over the border in Canada, this charming city is full of museums, galleries, and great restaurants.
  • Devils Lake – A popular destination for fishing and boating, this scenic lake is just an hour’s drive from Grafton.

Conclusion

Grafton is a beautiful and welcoming destination that offers something for everyone. From historic landmarks and cultural experiences to outdoor adventures and delicious food and drink, there’s no shortage of things to do and see in this charming city. So come explore Grafton and discover all that this unique destination has to offer.
