Introduction to Mylo

Mylo is a small town located in the Turtle Mountains of North Dakota, in the Great Plains region of the United States. Nestled in a beautiful natural setting, it is a great destination for travelers seeking outdoor adventures, scenic views, and a peaceful escape from the city. The town’s cultural richness and warm hospitality of its residents also make it a must-visit location.

List of Places to Visit in Mylo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museums: Visit the International Peace Garden located on the border of the US and Canada, which features beautifully landscaped gardens, a conservatory, and a tribute to peace. Here, you will also find the Historical and Cultural Society of North Dakota, which boasts historical artifacts, exhibits, and programs.
  • Historical places: Explore the authentically preserved 1885 St. John’s Catholic Church, and the Pierce County Courthouse.

Natural Attractions of Mylo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hiking and biking: Turtle Mountains offers an extensive network of trails for hiking and biking. Bear Butte State Park, located around an hour’s drive from Mylo, features stunning Black Hills native vegetation and unique geological formations.
  • Scenic views: The International Peace Garden is home to the Turtle River Waterfall, numerous gardens, and spectacular viewpoints.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mylo

  • Turtle Mountain Scenic Byway: A 63-mile drive from Mylo leads you to the Turtle Mountain Scenic Byway, renowned for its picturesque landscapes, vibrant autumn colors, and wildlife sightings.
  • Bear Butte State Park: Take the Bear Butte State Park Trail, a 3.5-mile moderate hike in the park, for the most stunning views.

Best Things to Do in Mylo: Activities and Tours

  • International Peace Garden: Enjoy a guided tour of the gardens and learn about the history of the location.
  • Fishing: North Dakota’s lakes are a favorite for fishing enthusiasts. Lake Metigoshe State Park, located around 20 minutes from Mylo, is a popular fishing spot for walleye, northern pike, and perch.
  • Kayaking and paddleboarding: Lake Darling, located roughly an hour from Mylo, offers rentals of kayaks and paddleboards for an adventurous trip out on the water.

Food and Drink Scene of Mylo

Must-Try Dishes

  • Knoephla soup: A creamy soup made with chewy dumplings, potatoes, and vegetables.
  • Hot dish: A casserole-style dish made with meat, vegetables, and pasta or rice.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: A variety of craft beers from microbreweries are available in the region.
  • Cider: Apple cider is a popular choice.

Where to Stay in Mylo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Lake Metigoshe State Park Cabins: Offers cabins in a serene setting with modern amenities.
  • Dakota Rose Inn: A cozy bed and breakfast with comfortable rooms and a delicious breakfast.

What to Buy in Mylo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • North Dakota-made wares: Shop for locally-made products at Dakota Sun Gardens and Winery.
  • International Peace Garden souvenirs: You can buy souvenirs at the International Peace Garden gift shop.

Local Culture and Customs of Mylo

  • Friendliness: Locals in Mylo are known to be friendly and welcoming, so don’t be surprised if people strike up a conversation with you.
  • Etiquette: Dressing modestly and removing your shoes before entering someone’s home are common practices.

Best Time to Visit Mylo

The best time to visit Mylo is during the summer months of June through August when the weather is at its best, and local festivals are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mylo

Do:

  • Pack proper attire: Be sure to pack hiking shoes, rain gear, and warm clothing for the cooler nights.
  • Bring bug repellent: Mosquitoes are prevalent in the summer months, so bring some repellent.
  • Explore the outdoors: Mylo offers plenty of outdoor activities like hiking, biking, fishing, and kayaking, so take advantage of them.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Mylo takes great pride in its natural surroundings, so please respect it by taking your waste with you.
  • Trespass: Some areas of the Turtle Mountains are private property, so seek permission if you wish to explore them.
  • Ignore safety warnings: Always obey safety precautions at hiking trails, water bodies, and other outdoor areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mylo

Mylo is a safe town with low instances of crime. However, visitors should exercise standard safety precautions like avoiding isolated areas at night, locking their car doors, and keeping valuables in a secure place.

Health in Mylo

Visitors should take precautions to prevent tick bites; a common carrier of Lyme disease. Applying bug repellent and wearing long-sleeved clothes, particularly in the forested areas, is recommended.

Getting to Mylo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mylo

  • Minot International Airport (MOT): About two hours’ drive from Mylo.

Bus Stations Near Mylo

  • Greyhound Station Minot: About two hours’ drive from Mylo.

Other Options

A rental car from Minot international airport is the most convenient option to reach Mylo.

How to Get Around in Mylo

Driving or riding a bike is the preferred mode of transportation within Mylo.

Where to Go Next from Mylo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bismarck: The state capital of North Dakota is a two-hour drive from Mylo and offers a variety of museums, galleries, and landmarks to explore.
  • Theodore Roosevelt National Park: Located about two and a half hours from Mylo, this park is famous for its rugged Badlands, bison herds, and beautiful rock formations.

Conclusion

Mylo presents an ideal destination for travelers looking for a peaceful retreat, outdoor adventure, and unique cultural experiences. With plenty of natural and historical attractions and a warm welcoming community, it guarantees you an unforgettable trip.
