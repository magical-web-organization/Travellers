Introduction to Standing Rock

Welcome to Standing Rock, a stunning destination nestled in the rolling hills of North Dakota that attracts visitors from all over the world. Standing Rock is situated in the heart of Great Plains and is home to a vibrant culture, breathtaking natural landscapes, and a variety of outdoor adventures.

With its unique blend of Native American traditions and modern attractions, Standing Rock offers something for everyone, from history buffs and nature lovers to thrill-seekers looking for an adrenaline rush. This comprehensive guide will give you all the information you need to plan your trip to Standing Rock.

List of Places to Visit in Standing Rock

No matter what kind of traveler you are, Standing Rock has something to offer. Here are some must-visit places and attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sitting Bull College Heritage Center: Learn about the legacy of Sitting Bull, the famous Lakota Sioux warrior and spiritual leader, at this museum and cultural center.
  • Fort Yates: Visit the site of the infamous Fort Yates, which played a significant role during the Sioux Wars.
  • Standing Rock Sioux Tribal Museum: Discover the rich history and traditions of the Standing Rock Sioux tribe through exhibits and artifacts at this museum.

Natural Attractions of Standing Rock: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cannonball River: Go fishing or kayaking on the Cannonball River, which runs through Standing Rock and offers great views of the surrounding hills.
  • Prairie Knights Casino and Resort: Enjoy gaming, dining, and entertainment at this popular resort, which also offers scenic views of the Missouri River.
  • Blacktail Trail: Hiking enthusiasts will love this challenging trail that winds through stunning natural landscapes and offers breathtaking views from the top.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Standing Rock

Standing Rock is an outdoor enthusiast’s paradise, with plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the best:

  • Standing Rock Trail: This 16-mile trail offers hikers and bikers stunning views of the landscape, including rock formations and prairies.
  • Fort Yates Trail: A shorter, more leisurely trail that winds through oak woodlands and offers views of nearby farms.
  • Fort Lincoln State Park Trail: This 30-mile trail passes through dense forests, grasslands, and rocky terrain, offering scenic views of the Missouri River and surrounding hills.

Best Things to Do in Standing Rock: Activities and Tours

Looking for things to do in Standing Rock? Here are some top-rated activities and tours:

  • Horseback Riding: Experience the beauty of the Great Plains from horseback with a guided trail ride.
  • Cultural Tours: Take a guided tour to learn about the history, traditions, and culture of the Standing Rock Sioux tribe.
  • Water Sports: From fishing and kayaking to swimming and tubing, there are plenty of ways to enjoy the water in Standing Rock.

Week-Long Itinerary for Standing Rock

If you’re planning a weeklong trip to Standing Rock, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day 1: Explore museums and historical sites, including the Sitting Bull College Heritage Center and the Standing Rock Sioux Tribal Museum.

Day 2: Go hiking on one of the scenic trails, such as the Standing Rock Trail or Fort Yates Trail.

Day 3: Enjoy water sports, such as fishing or kayaking on the Cannonball River.

Day 4: Try your luck at Prairie Knights Casino and Resort, or take a cultural tour of the area.

Day 5: Take a horseback riding tour and experience the beauty of the Great Plains from a different perspective.

Day 6: Do some souvenir shopping and explore the local food and drink scene.

Day 7: Relax and soak up the scenery before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Standing Rock

No trip to Standing Rock is complete without experiencing the local food and drink scene. Here are some top picks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Indian Tacos: A delicious and filling delicacy, Indian Tacos feature fry bread topped with seasoned ground beef, lettuce, tomatoes, and cheese.
  • Bison Burger: Taste the heartland of America with a juicy bison burger.
  • Chokecherry Jam: This sweet and tangy jam is made from local berries and is the perfect condiment for toast or biscuits.

Local Beverages

  • Mint Tea: Made from fresh mint leaves, this refreshing beverage is a popular drink among Standing Rock Sioux tribe members.
  • Coffee: Taste the bold and robust coffee that is grown locally and roasted in-house at coffee shops in the area.
  • Prairie Berry Wine: Try some of the local wine made from the berries that grow on the rolling hills of Standing Rock.

Where to Stay in Standing Rock: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for upscale amenities or rustic accommodations, there are plenty of options to choose from in Standing Rock. Here are some top picks:

  • Prairie Knights Casino and Resort: This popular resort offers modern amenities, gaming, dining, and entertainment.
  • Standing Rock Lodge: This cozy lodge offers rustic cabins that are perfect for a relaxing getaway.
  • Sitting Bull Lodge: This charming guesthouse is located in a historic building and offers comfortable accommodations and easy access to local attractions.

What to Buy in Standing Rock: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for a unique souvenir or gift? Here are some local products to consider:

  • Native American Art: Choose from a variety of handmade traditional crafts and artworks created by local artisans.
  • Bison Jerky: Taste the local specialty and bring home some bison jerky, which is lean, healthy, and packed with protein.
  • Native American Jewelry: Beaded necklaces, bracelets, and earrings are popular souvenirs that showcase the intricate artistry of Native American jewelry-making traditions.

Local Culture and Customs of Standing Rock

To respect the local culture and customs of Standing Rock, visitors should be aware of the following:

  • Dress modestly, covering knees and shoulders while visiting sacred sites.
  • Seek permission before photographing or filming any people or events.
  • Do not touch any artifacts or items in museums or cultural centers without permission.

Best Time to Visit Standing Rock

The best time to visit Standing Rock is during the summer months, when temperatures are warm and outdoor activities are in full swing. Spring and fall offer milder temperatures and fewer crowds, while winter can be cold and snowy but also beautiful and peaceful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Standing Rock

Here are some important tips to keep in mind when visiting Standing Rock:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Bring appropriate clothing and footwear for the weather and activity.
  • Stay on marked trails and respect the natural environment.

Don’t:

  • Trespass on private property.
  • Take anything from natural or cultural sites.
  • Litter or leave trash behind.

Safety and Health

While Standing Rock is a safe and welcoming place to visit, visitors should be aware of basic safety and health guidelines.

Safety in Standing Rock

  • Carry a map, cellphone, and water while hiking or exploring the outdoors.
  • Follow posted signs and warnings.
  • Contact law enforcement or park officials for emergencies.

Health in Standing Rock

  • Protect yourself from sunburn and insect bites.
  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated.
  • Seek medical attention at the first sign of illness or injury.

  • Fargo Hector International Airport

Bus Stations Near Standing Rock

  • Trailways Bus Terminal

Other Options

  • Uber and Lyft are available in larger cities.

How to Get Around in Standing Rock

The easiest way to get around Standing Rock is by car. However, several tour companies offer guided tours of the area, which can be a convenient and informative way to explore the attractions.

Where to Go Next from Standing Rock: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time, consider visiting one of these nearby destinations:

  • Theodore Roosevelt National Park: Located just a few hours from Standing Rock, this park is home to breathtaking canyons, wildlife, and hiking trails.
  • Spearfish Canyon: Located in South Dakota, this scenic canyon offers hiking, biking, and rock climbing opportunities.
  • Fort Mandan: Located near Bismarck, this site marks the spot of Lewis and Clark’s winter encampment during their famous expedition.

Conclusion

Standing Rock offers a rich cultural experience, breathtaking natural beauty, and a variety of outdoor adventures that make it a must-visit destination for any traveler. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your trip, experience the best attractions and activities, and enjoy the local food and drink scene.
