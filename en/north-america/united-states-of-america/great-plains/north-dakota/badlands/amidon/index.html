Amidon Travel Guide - A Comprehensive Tour Guide for Amidon, North Dakota

Welcome to Amidon, a charming town situated in the heart of the Great Plains in North Dakota. Amidon is surrounded by picturesque Badlands offering both natural beauty and rich culture. In this guide, we will take you on a journey through the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, how to get there and what to see in Amidon.

List of Places to Visit in Amidon

Here are the best places to visit in Amidon.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Dakota Dinosaur Museum
  • Pioneer Trails Regional Museum
  • Medora Musical
  • Amidon Historic Site

    Natural Attractions of Amidon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Little Missouri National Grassland
  • Theodore Roosevelt National Park
  • Magpie Creek Nature Walk
  • Sentinel Butte Trailhead

Hiking/Biking Routes of Amidon

If you are a hiking and biking enthusiast, then Amidon has some fantastic routes for you. With its scenic beauty and rugged terrain, hiking in Amidon will take you through some of the most incredible views. For bikers, the town has some challenging routes that offer the perfect opportunity to explore the town’s natural wonder.

  • Maah Daah Hey Trail
  • Painted Canyon Trail
  • Sunset Trailhead
  • Square Butte

Best Things to Do in Amidon: Activities and Tours

From hiking trails to museums, there’s plenty to do in Amidon. Here are some of the must-visit sights and activities for everyone.

  • Horseback Riding and Rodeo
  • Badlands Overlook
  • Tour of the Medora Musical

Week-Long Itinerary for Amidon

If you’re planning to visit Amidon for a week, here’s a suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your time.

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Amidon Historic Site and Dakota Dinosaur Museum
Day 2 Take a tour of Sentinel Butte Trailhead and Maah Daah Hey Trail
Day 3 Explore the Little Missouri National Grassland
Day 4 Head to Theodore Roosevelt National Park
Day 5 Join a horseback riding trip or attend a rodeo
Day 6 Visit the Pioneer Trails Regional Museum and grab a bite at the local diner
Day 7 Take the Magpie Creek Nature Walk and relax while enjoying the sunset at the Painted Canyon Trail

Local Culture and Customs of Amidon

Amidon is known for its rich culture and tradition. If you want to get a feel for the local culture, make sure to check out these events like Rodeos, Dakota Nights Astronomy festival, and the Roughrider Days.

Best Time to Visit Amidon

The best time to visit Amidon is from May to September, especially during late spring and early summer, as the weather will be comfortable and perfect for outdoor activities. Also, September is an excellent time to visit the town as the fall colors start to appear.

Do’s and Don’ts of Amidon

Do:

  • Respect the Local Culture and Tradition.
  • Enjoy the Local Beer and Dishes.
  • Leave No Trace.
  • Wear Comfortable shoes for hiking and biking

    Don’t:

  • Don’t Forget Sunscreen and hats for Hiking
  • Don’t litter or harm the natural environment.
  • Don’t climb the hills or touch animals in the grasslands.

Safety and Health

Safety in Amidon

Overall Amidon is a safe destination, but still, visitors must take precautions, especially while hiking or camping in remote areas. Moreover, it is recommended to be aware of the wild animals while hiking.

Health in Amidon

There are limited medical facilities in Amidon, and visitors should carry their medication and necessary medical supplies.

Getting to Amidon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Amidon is located in the Great Plains region in North Dakota. The town is near to major cities and easily accessible by air, road, and rail.

Airports Near Amidon

  • Bismarck Municipal Airport
  • Dickinson Theodore Roosevelt Regional Airport

    Bus Stations Near Amidon

  • Greyhound bus station in Bowman, ND
  • Jefferson Lines station in Dickinson, ND

    Other Options

  • Drive - The town is easily accessible by car via the I-94 and US-85 highways.
  • Train - Amtrak stations are located nearby in Williston, Watford City, and Glendive, MT.

How to Get Around in Amidon

The best way to get around in Amidon is by car, bike or on foot. Rental cars and bikes are available at the nearby airports, and visitors can also join a guided tour.

  • Medora

  • Bismarck

Conclusion

Amidon is a small town with lots of character and charm. From its rich history and culture to spectacular natural sights, there is something for everyone. We hope this guide has helped you plan your next trip to Amidon.
