Introduction to Watford City

Located in the heart of the Badlands in North Dakota, Watford City is a small town in the Great Plains region of the United States. The town was originally founded in 1914 as a temporary settlement for oil workers and has since grown to become a hub for outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and natural beauty. With its friendly residents, stunning landscapes, and rich history, Watford City provides a unique and memorable experience for travelers visiting North Dakota.

List of Places to Visit in Watford City

Watford City offers numerous attractions for visitors of all ages and interests. Here are some of the best places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Long X Trading Post - A National Historic Site, the Long X Trading Post was founded in 1864 and played a vital role in the development of the western frontier. Visitors can explore the restored buildings and artifacts, and learn about the history of the fur trade and the settlement of the west.

  • McKenzie County Heritage Park - This outdoor museum features several historic buildings, including a schoolhouse, church, and pioneer home. The site also has a prairie garden and a collection of antique farming equipment.

Natural Attractions of Watford City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Theodore Roosevelt National Park - Located just 30 miles south of Watford City, this national park is a must-see attraction for any nature lover. Visitors can explore the park’s rugged badlands, scenic drives, hiking trails, and wildlife sightings.

  • Lake Sakakawea - The largest man-made lake in North Dakota, Lake Sakakawea offers fishing, boating, swimming, and camping opportunities. Visitors can also enjoy scenic driving routes and hikes along the lake’s shoreline.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Watford City

Watford City and the surrounding area offer numerous hiking and biking trails for visitors to explore. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Maah Daah Hey Trail - This 144-mile trail passes through the North Dakota Badlands and offers stunning views of the rugged landscape. The trail is open to hikers, bikers, and horseback riders.

  • CCC Trail - Located in the Theodore Roosevelt National Park, the CCC Trail is a 3.5-mile route that offers stunning views of the park’s badlands and wildlife sightings.

Best Things to Do in Watford City: Activities and Tours

Watford City offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors to explore. Here are some of the best:

  • Horseback Riding - Visitors can explore the rugged terrain of the Badlands on horseback with local outfitters.

  • Riverboat Tours - Visitors can take a scenic riverboat tour along the Missouri River and enjoy views of the countryside and wildlife.

Week-Long Itinerary for Watford City

If you’re planning to visit Watford City for a week, here’s a sample itinerary:

Day 1: Visit Long X Trading Post and McKenzie County Heritage Park.

Day 2: Explore Theodore Roosevelt National Park and hike the CCC Trail.

Day 3: go Horseback Riding and explore outdoor wanderings.

Day 4: Visit the museum and historical places of Watford City.

Day 5: Take a riverboat tour of the Missouri River.

Day 6-7: Relax at Lake Sakakawea and enjoy camping, fishing, and boating opportunities.

Food and Drink Scene of Watford City

Watford City offers a variety of dining options to suit every taste. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bison Burger - A North Dakota specialty, the bison burger is a must-try for meat lovers.

  • Frybread Tacos - This Native American specialty features frybread topped with taco ingredients.

Local Beverages

  • Lemon Kuchen - A sweet and refreshing beverage made with lemon juice and soda.

  • Sarsaparilla - A classic soda made with sassafras and other herbs and spices.

Best Time to Visit Watford City

The best time to visit Watford City is from May to September, when the weather is mild and the outdoor activities are at their best. The autumn months also offer beautiful foliage and wildlife sightings in the Badlands.
