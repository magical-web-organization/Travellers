A Comprehensive Tour Guide to Little Missouri National Grasslands

Located in North Dakota, Little Missouri National Grasslands is a must-visit destination for nature lovers, outdoor enthusiasts, and history buffs. This scenic destination showcases the Great Plains of the United States and offers visitors a chance to explore its natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll cover the top attractions, activities, food, lodging, and logistics for an unforgettable trip to Little Missouri National Grasslands.

Introduction to Little Missouri National Grasslands

Little Missouri National Grasslands is a 1.03 million-acre Grassland located in the Badlands region of western North Dakota. This vast area consists of rolling hills, deep canyons, and rugged badlands terrain. The grasslands are home to a diverse range of wildlife, including elk, bighorn sheep, pronghorn, and bison. Visitors can also experience North Dakota’s rich history, which includes Native American cultures, cowboys, explorers, and pioneers.

List of Places to Visit in Little Missouri National Grasslands

When visiting Little Missouri National Grasslands, there are several places that you won’t want to miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chateau de Mores - This historic home was built by the French aristocrat, Marquis de Mores. The 26-room chateau has been restored and furnished in the style of the late 1800s and is open for tours.
  • North Dakota Cowboy Hall of Fame – This museum is dedicated to preserving the history and culture of the North Dakota cowboy.
  • Knife River Indian Villages National Historic Site - This site is home to the ancestral homeland of the Hidatsa people. Visitors can take a guided tour to learn about the customs, traditions, and culture of the Hidatsa people.

Natural Attractions of Little Missouri National Grasslands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Theodore Roosevelt National Park – This national park is adjacent to Little Missouri National Grasslands and offers visitors a chance to explore scenic loop drives and hiking trails. Visitors can also see the famous bison herd and other wildlife.
  • The Maah Daah Hey Trail – This 144-mile long trail runs through Little Missouri National Grasslands and is a popular hiking and biking route.
  • Little Missouri River – This river runs through Little Missouri National Grasslands and is a popular spot for fishing, kayaking, and canoeing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Little Missouri National Grasslands

Little Missouri National Grasslands has a variety of hiking and biking trails suitable for both beginners and experienced hikers/bikers. Some of the popular trails include:

  • Caprock Coulee Trail – This 3.5-mile trail offers visitors scenic views of the surrounding badlands, including the colorful layers of sedimentary rocks.
  • Sperati Point Trail – This 3-mile trail is a moderately strenuous uphill hike that rewards visitors with panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Long X Trail – This 14-mile trail is for experienced hikers and bikers and offers a chance to see the unique rock formations and canyons in Little Missouri National Grasslands.

Best Things to Do in Little Missouri National Grasslands: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities to do and tours to take when visiting Little Missouri National Grasslands:

  • Horseback Riding – There are several outfitters that offer guided horseback tours of Little Missouri National Grasslands.
  • Fishing - Little Missouri River is a top spot for trout fishing.
  • Photography - Little Missouri National Grasslands offers incredible opportunities for landscape and wildlife photography.

Week-Long Itinerary for Little Missouri National Grasslands

Here’s a sample itinerary to help you plan your week-long trip to Little Missouri National Grasslands:

Day 1: Explore the Chateau de Mores and take a guided tour of the North Dakota Cowboy Hall of Fame.

Day 2: Visit Theodore Roosevelt National Park, explore scenic drive routes, and see the buffalo herd.

Day 3: Take a guided tour of the Knife River Indian Villages National Historic Site and hike the Caprock Coulee Trail.

Day 4: Take a horseback tour of the Little Missouri National Grasslands and go fishing in Little Missouri River.

Day 5: Hike the Sperati Point Trail and enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.

Day 6: Bike the Long X Trail and explore the rugged canyons and rock formations of the Grasslands.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the scenic views of Little Missouri National Grasslands.

Food and Drink Scene of Little Missouri National Grasslands

North Dakota is known for its hearty cuisine, and Little Missouri National Grasslands has several dishes that you won’t want to miss:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bison Burger – This is a North Dakota specialty and a must-try for meat lovers.
  • Knoephla Soup – This is a creamy soup made with potatoes, dumplings, and chicken.
  • Chokecherry Jelly – This is a sweet and tangy jelly made with chokecherries, a North Dakota native fruit.

Local Beverages

  • Prairie Rose Meadery – This meadery offers a variety of honey wines, including their signature Mead Royale.
  • Laughing Sun Brewing Co. – This microbrewery offers a variety of craft beers, including their popular Farmhouse Ale.

Where to Stay in Little Missouri National Grasslands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Little Missouri National Grasslands has several options for lodging, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses:

  • Medora - This historic town is the gateway to Little Missouri National Grasslands and has several lodging options, including the historic Rough Riders Hotel.
  • Watford City - This town has several lodging options, including hotels and motels.
  • Camping – There are several campsites available in Little Missouri National Grasslands for visitors who enjoy camping.

What to Buy in Little Missouri National Grasslands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products to bring home from your trip to Little Missouri National Grasslands:

  • Native American Crafts – Little Missouri National Grasslands is home to several Native American tribes, and visitors can find authentic crafts and artwork.
  • North Dakota Prairie Fire Hot Sauce – This is a hot sauce made with peppers grown in North Dakota and is a must-try for hot sauce lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of Little Missouri National Grasslands

North Dakota has a rich cultural history, and when visiting Little Missouri National Grasslands, it’s essential to respect the local customs and traditions. North Dakotans are friendly and hospitable, and visitors should always be courteous and respectful towards locals.

Best Time to Visit Little Missouri National Grasslands

The best time to visit Little Missouri National Grasslands is in the summer months of June through September. The weather is warm, and the grasslands are lush and green during this time. However, visitors should be prepared for sudden changes in weather and storms.
