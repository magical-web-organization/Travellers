Introduction to Bowman

Bowman is a small city located in the Badlands area of North Dakota in the Great Plains of the United States of America. Surrounded by stunning badlands, rolling hills, and wide-open grasslands, Bowman is a hidden gem that is often overlooked by travelers visiting North Dakota. The city is a perfect destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts who want to explore the beauty of the American Midwest through hiking, biking, and scenic drives.

List of Places to Visit in Bowman

Here are some of the must-visit places and attractions in Bowman:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bowman County Museum: Discover the history of Bowman and the surrounding areas through interactive exhibits and displays of artifacts, photographs, and documents.
  • Pioneer Trails Regional Museum: Learn about the history and culture of the Great Plains through exhibits and events displaying the lifestyle, religion, and customs of the pioneers and settlers of North Dakota.

Natural Attractions of Bowman: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Little Missouri Grasslands: Explore the vast wilderness of North Dakota through hiking or biking. The Little Missouri Grasslands is a beautiful area with unique geological formations, canyons, and stunning views of rolling hills and prairies.
  • Theodore Roosevelt National Park: Discover the natural beauty and ruggedness of the North Dakota Badlands through camping, hiking, and scenic drives. The park has three distinct areas- the North Unit, South Unit, and Elkhorn Ranch Unit- each offering different opportunities to explore.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bowman

Bowman is a paradise for hikers and bikers. Here are some of the best trails for outdoor adventures:

  • Cottonwood Trail: A scenic hiking trail that offers views of rolling hills and the Little Missouri River.
  • Oxbow Overlook Trail: A moderate hiking trail that passes through grasslands and is ideal for bird-watching.
  • The Maah Daah Hey Trail: One of the longest single-track biking trails in the United States, stretching over 100 miles through the Badlands and grasslands of North Dakota.

Best Things to Do in Bowman: Activities and Tours

  • Badlands Scenic Drive: A breathtaking drive that takes you through the rugged beauty of the North Dakota Badlands, offering stunning views of canyons, rolling hills, and unique geologic formations.
  • Horseback Riding: Experience the beauty of North Dakota’s landscapes on horseback with a guided tour.
  • Fishing: The Little Missouri River is an excellent spot for fishing for trout and walleye.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bowman

Here is a recommended week-long itinerary for exploring Bowman and its surrounding areas:

Day 1-2 Explore Bowman and visit the Bowman County Museum and Pioneer Trails Regional Museum
Day 3 Hike the Cottonwood Trail and visit the Little Missouri Grasslands
Day 4 Drive the Badlands Scenic Drive and hike the Oxbow Overlook Trail
Day 5 Visit Theodore Roosevelt National Park and camp at one of the campgrounds
Day 6 Horseback riding tour
Day 7 Fishing trip on the Little Missouri River

Food and Drink Scene of Bowman

Must-Try Dishes

  • Steak: North Dakota is known for its quality beef, and steak is a must-try dish in Bowman. The prime rib and sirloin steaks at the Bowman Lodge and Convention Center are highly recommended.
  • Walleye: As the state fish of North Dakota, walleye is a staple in the diet of many North Dakotans. Try the walleye sandwich at Long X Saloon.

Local Beverages

  • Ale Works: A craft brewery offering a rotating selection of beers brewed onsite.

Where to Stay in Bowman: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Bowman Lodge and Convention Center: A comfortable hotel with excellent amenities, including an indoor pool and hot tub, fitness center, and free breakfast.
  • Western 6 Motel: A budget-friendly option with clean rooms and friendly staff.

What to Buy in Bowman: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Western Wear: Bowman is located in cattle country, so buying local western wear, including cowboy hats, boots, and belts, is a great way to bring a piece of North Dakota home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Bowman

North Dakota is famous for its traditional ranching culture, and Bowman is no exception. Visitors should expect polite and courteous behavior from locals. The state’s cowboy culture and deep roots in agriculture are symbols of the region’s heritage. Visitors should respect the local customs and always ask before taking photographs of people and their property.

Best Time to Visit Bowman

The best time to visit Bowman is from June to October when the weather is mild and pleasant. July and August are the busiest months, while September and October offer cooler temperatures, spectacular fall colors, and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bowman

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Wear comfortable shoes for hiking and exploring the outdoors.
  • Try the local cuisine, especially the steak and walleye.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Take photographs of people and their property without permission.
  • Ignore wildlife safety rules in natural areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bowman

Bowman is a safe city, but visitors should always take precautions in unfamiliar areas. It is important to lock your car, keep your valuables safe, and not leave them in plain sight.

Health in Bowman

Visitors should stay hydrated during outdoor activities and use proper sun protection. It is recommended to carry bug spray and avoid areas where there are high levels of ticks and mosquitoes.

Getting to Bowman: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bowman

The closest airport to Bowman is the Dickinson Theodore Roosevelt Regional Airport (DIK), located about 70 miles to the northwest. The airport offers flights from Denver, CO, and Minneapolis, MN.

Bus Stations Near Bowman

The nearest bus station is located in Dickinson, ND, about 70 miles northwest of Bowman.

Other Options

Bowman is best accessed by car or RV. The main highway, Highway 85, runs through Bowman and connects to other major interstates and highways in North Dakota.

How to Get Around in Bowman

Bowman is a small city, and most attractions are within walking distance. However, renting a car or bringing your own car is recommended to explore the surrounding areas and natural attractions.

Where to Go Next from Bowman: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lewis and Clark Trail: Follow the trail of the famous explorers through North Dakota and Montana.
  • Devil’s Lake: Visit the largest natural body of water in North Dakota, with fishing, hiking, and camping opportunities.

Conclusion

Bowman, North Dakota, is a hidden gem in the Great Plains, offering incredible natural beauty and outdoor experiences. From hiking and biking to exploring museums and trying local cuisine, there is something for everyone in Bowman. Whether you’re seeking adventure or a peaceful escape, Bowman should be on your list of must-visit destinations in the United States.
