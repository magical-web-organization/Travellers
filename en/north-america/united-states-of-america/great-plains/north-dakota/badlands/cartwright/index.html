Introduction to Cartwright

Nestled in the heart of the rugged Badlands of North Dakota, Cartwright is a little-known gem that you absolutely must visit. This charming town has something for everyone - from history buffs to nature enthusiasts to adventure seekers. Explore its fascinating museums, hike its scenic trails, and marvel at its breath-taking vistas. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, things to do, and local culture of Cartwright.

List of Places to Visit in Cartwright

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

North Dakota Cowboy Hall of Fame

The North Dakota Cowboy Hall of Fame is a must-visit for anyone interested in the rich history of the American West. This museum honors the legendary cowboys, cowgirls, and rodeo stars who have helped shape this iconic era. As you wander through the exhibits, you’ll marvel at the amazing artifacts, art, and interactive displays that capture the spirit of the Wild West.

Wannagan Creek

If you’re looking for a peaceful spot to relax and unwind, head to Wannagan Creek. This hidden gem lies just outside the town of Cartwright and offers stunning views of the surrounding natural scenery. Take a stroll along the creek, have a picnic, or just sit and meditate as you listen to the tranquil sounds of nature.

Natural Attractions of Cartwright: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Theodore Roosevelt National Park

Just a short drive from Cartwright lies one of the crown jewels of North Dakota - Theodore Roosevelt National Park. This breathtaking park is named after the 26th President of the United States, who was an avid outdoorsman and conservationist. Roam the park’s scenic drives or hike its many trails to witness the diverse wildlife and stunning geological formations of the Badlands. Don’t forget your camera - you’ll want to snap a picture of the park’s iconic bison, prairie dogs, and horses!

Red Banks

Red Banks is another natural wonder that you simply can’t miss during your visit to Cartwright. This area features towering cliffs that are made of red sandstone and clay - a sight that is truly remarkable. Hikers and rock climbers alike will appreciate the challenging terrain that Red Banks offers. Take some time to explore this unique geological formation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cartwright

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, then you’ll be delighted to know that Cartwright has dozens of amazing hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the best ones:

Maah Dey Hey Trail

The Maah Dey Hey Trail is a 144-mile-long hiking trail that winds its way through the Badlands of North Dakota. If you’re up for a long-distance hike, you won’t find a more rewarding one than this. Trek through rugged terrain, across wild rivers, and along steep ridges as you marvel at the stunning natural beauty of this rugged landscape.

Jones Creek Trail

The Jones Creek Trail is a great option if you’re looking for a more moderate hike. This trail is just over six miles long and takes you through an idyllic forested area that is filled with wildlife. Keep an eye out for the many birds, deer, and other critters that call this area home.

Best Things to Do in Cartwright: Activities and Tours

Take a Guided Tour of Theodore Roosevelt National Park

To fully appreciate the beauty and wonder of the Theodore Roosevelt National Park, we highly recommend taking a guided tour. Experienced guides will help you navigate the park’s many trails while providing insights and information about the park’s unique history and wildlife.

Try Your Hand at Horseback Riding

North Dakota is cowboy country, and there’s no better way to experience this rich heritage than by going horseback riding. Local ranches offer a variety of riding experiences - from easy trail rides to full-day excursions. This is a great way to get up-close-and-personal with the stunning natural scenery of the Badlands.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cartwright

Day 1: Arrive in Cartwright and check in to your hotel Day 2: Visit the North Dakota Cowboy Hall of Fame and explore the town Day 3: Hike the Maah Dey Hey Trail Day 4: Take a guided tour of Theodore Roosevelt National Park Day 5: Horseback riding excursion Day 6: Visit Wannagan Creek and Red Banks Day 7: Depart Cartwright

Food and Drink Scene of Cartwright

Must-Try Dishes

If you’re visiting North Dakota, you have to try one of its most famous dishes - the knoephla soup. This rich and creamy soup has a reputation as the ultimate comfort food. Other local specialties include Indian tacos, bison burgers, and walleye - a type of fish that is caught locally.

Local Beverages

North Dakota is known for its microbreweries, and Cartwright has a fantastic one called Black Leg Brewing. This brewery is housed in a historic building and features a huge selection of craft beers. Try one of their unique brews, such as the Dakotan Summer Wheat or the Badlands Red Ale.

Where to Stay in Cartwright: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Theodore Roosevelt Inn

The Theodore Roosevelt Inn is a cozy and comfortable hotel that offers great value for your money. The rooms are spacious and clean, and many of them feature stunning views of the nearby Badlands. Plus, the staff is friendly and accommodating.

Airbnb

For a more authentic experience, consider staying in an Airbnb. There are many options available in and around Cartwright that offer a true taste of local life. You’ll have the chance to meet friendly locals and experience the area’s unique culture up close.

What to Buy in Cartwright: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

North Dakota Souvenirs

If you’re looking for a unique souvenir to bring home, check out the North Dakota Heritage Center & State Museum gift shop. They have a wide range of local products and souvenirs - from handmade jewelry to locally-grown foods.

Handcrafted Pottery

North Dakota is famous for its pottery, and you can find some great pieces at the Little Missouri Gallery in Medora. Pick up a beautiful piece of handcrafted pottery to remind you of your visit to the area.

Local Culture and Customs of Cartwright

North Dakota has a rich cultural heritage that is heavily influenced by its Native American and cowboy heritage. We recommend taking the time to learn about the history of the area and the customs of the local people. Respectfully engage with local traditions and try to learn as much as you can.

Best Time to Visit Cartwright

The best time to visit Cartwright is during the summer months when the weather is warm and sunny. However, keep in mind that this is peak tourist season, so crowds and prices may be higher. If you’re looking for a quieter experience, consider visiting in the spring or fall when the weather is still pleasant but there are fewer tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cartwright

Do:

  • Respect the natural scenery and wildlife
  • Support local businesses and artisans
  • Take the time to learn about the area’s rich cultural heritage

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash in the park
  • Disrupt local wildlife or their natural habitats
  • Ignore safety recommendations from park rangers or other officials

Safety and Health

Safety in Cartwright

Overall, Cartwright is a safe place to visit. However, it’s always a good idea to take some basic precautions, such as locking your car doors and being aware of your surroundings. When hiking or participating in outdoor activities, make sure you have ample water, maps, and emergency supplies.

Health in Cartwright

North Dakota has a low risk of contagious diseases, but it’s still a good idea to take some basic health precautions. Make sure you have all necessary medications and know where the nearest clinic or hospital is in case of an emergency.

Getting to Cartwright: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cartwright

The closest major airport to Cartwright is the Theodore Roosevelt Regional Airport in Dickinson, North Dakota. From there, you can rent a car or take a taxi to Cartwright.

Bus Stations Near Cartwright

Greyhound has a stop in Belfield, North Dakota, which is about 30 minutes away from Cartwright.

Other Options

You can also drive to Cartwright, which is a great option if you want to have your own transportation during your visit. From the east, take I-94 to State Highway 22 North and follow it to Cartwright.

How to Get Around in Cartwright

The best way to get around Cartwright is by car, as public transportation options are limited. You can rent a car at the nearby airport or in Dickinson, North Dakota.

Where to Go Next from Cartwright: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Medora, North Dakota

Medora is a charming town that lies just 30 minutes east of Cartwright. This town is famous for its musical productions, wild west reenactments, and unique cultural offerings. Explore its fascinating museums, hike in the nearby Theodore Roosevelt National Park, or just relax at one of its many restaurants and cafes.

South Dakota

If you’re up for a longer road trip, consider heading south to South Dakota. This state is home to many iconic American landmarks, such as Mount Rushmore and the Crazy Horse Memorial. You’ll also find stunning natural attractions like Badlands National Park and the Black Hills.

Conclusion

Cartwright is a hidden gem that is just waiting to be discovered. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, or just a peaceful getaway, this town has something for everyone. So pack your bags and come experience the natural beauty and rich history of Cartwright, North Dakota.
