Introduction to Belfield

Belfield is a charming small town located in the Badlands region of North Dakota, in the United States of America. The town is situated in the heart of the Great Plains, offering visitors a unique mix of cultural and natural attractions. Belfield is famous for its beautiful landscapes, hiking trails, and world-renowned museums. Whether you’re here for outdoor adventure or cultural exploration, Belfield is a perfect destination for all.

List of Places to Visit in Belfield

Here is the list of must-visit places in Belfield:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • North Dakota Cowboy Hall of Fame: This museum offers a deep understanding of the history and culture of cowboys and ranching in North Dakota.

  • Pioneer Trails Regional Museum: This museum showcases the prairie heritage of the region through exhibits, programs, and research.

Natural Attractions of Belfield: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Theodore Roosevelt National Park: This national park offers breathtaking views of badlands, canyons, and prairies and is home to bison, deer, elk, and prairie dogs.

  • Painted Canyon Visitor Center: The visitor center offers an observation deck with stunning views of Painted Canyon and the Badlands.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Belfield

Belfield offers various hiking and biking routes for adventure lovers. Some of the popular routes include:

  • The Petrified Forest Loop Trail: A 3.8-mile trail that takes you through a petrified forest that’s over 50 million years old.

  • Maah Daah Hey Trail: A 144-mile trail running from North Dakota to South Dakota, passing through Theodore Roosevelt National Park.

Best Things to Do in Belfield: Activities and Tours

  • Horseback Riding: Take a horseback ride through the Badlands and discover the beauty of the region.

  • Fishing: Discover great fishing spots like the Heart Butte Reservoir, where anglers can catch walleye, northern pike, and yellow perch.

Week-Long Itinerary for Belfield

Here is a recommended itinerary for a week in Belfield:

Day 1: Visit Theodore Roosevelt National Park and explore the Painted Canyon Visitor Center.

Day 2: Hike the Petrified Forest Loop Trail and visit North Dakota Cowboy Hall of Fame.

Day 3: Go horseback riding and explore the Maah Daah Hey Trail.

Day 4: Visit Pioneer Trails Regional Museum and enjoy fishing in Heart Butte Reservoir.

Day 5: Take a scenic drive through the Badlands and visit the historic town of Medora.

Day 6: Explore the Giant Sloth exhibit at Pioneer Trails Regional Museum and visit Buffalo Gap Trail.

Day 7: Spend the day at The Buffalo Gap Guest Ranch, enjoying horseback riding and outdoor sports.

Food and Drink Scene of Belfield

Belfield offers a unique culinary experience to its visitors. Don’t forget to try these must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bison Burger: A unique delicacy made from bison meat.

  • Knoephla Soup: A creamy chicken and potato soup, usually served with dumplings.

Local Beverages

  • Prairie Fire: A local cocktail made with vodka, grenadine, and Tabasco sauce.

  • Red Beer: A beer mixed with tomato juice, Worcestershire sauce, and Tabasco sauce.

Where to Stay in Belfield: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Belfield offers various accommodation options to suit different preferences and budgets. Some of the best places to stay in Belfield include:

  • The Buffalo Gap Guest Ranch: A guest ranch offering lodging, horseback riding, and outdoor sports.

  • Medora Campground: A serene camping spot in the heart of the Badlands, with an outdoor pool and hot tub.

What to Buy in Belfield: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t forget to bring home these unique souvenirs and local products from Belfield:

  • Bison meat: Bring home some bison meat and try making a bison burger at home.

  • Badlands souvenirs: Bring home souvenirs, memorabilia, and artwork inspired by the Badlands.

Local Culture and Customs of Belfield

Belfield is a welcoming town that values community and connection. During your visit, remember to embrace the Western hospitality, and engage with the locals.

Best Time to Visit Belfield

The best time to visit Belfield is between May to September when the weather is warm and pleasant, and the outdoor attractions are fully operational.
