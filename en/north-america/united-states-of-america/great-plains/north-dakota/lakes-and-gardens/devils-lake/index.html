Introduction to Devils Lake

Devils Lake is a beautiful city located in Lakes and Gardens, North Dakota, Great Plains, United States of America. It is named after the nearby body of water, Devils Lake, which is the largest natural body of water in the state. The city is a popular tourist destination because of its beautiful natural landscapes, cultural attractions, and wide range of outdoor activities. In this travel guide, we will provide you with everything you need to know about visiting Devils Lake.

List of Places to Visit in Devils Lake

Devils Lake offers a wide range of attractions for tourists. Here is a list of places you must visit when in Devils Lake:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Totten State Historic Site: The fort was built by the US Army in the 19th century to protect the settlers and is now a museum and National Historic Landmark.
  • Quentin N. Burdick Sports Arena and Engelstad Arena: These stadiums host many sports events and are popular tourist spots.
  • Spirit Lake Casino & Resort: This resort offers live entertainment, casino gaming, and an excellent hotel facility to tourists visiting Devils Lake.

Natural Attractions of Devils Lake: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sullys Hill National Game Preserve: A wildlife sanctuary with hiking trails, camping grounds, and water activities like fishing and boating.
  • Grahams Island State Park: The park offers a beautiful beach on the shore of the Devils Lake and has camping and picnic facilities.
  • Old Oak Trail: A scenic driving route that has fantastic views of the Missouri River and the nearby landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Devils Lake

Devils Lake offers several hiking and biking routes for adventurers. Some of the best routes include

  • Channel A Trail: A 2.5-mile-long route ideal for hiking or mountain biking.
  • Devils Lake State Park Trails: A network of trails of varying difficulty levels that offer fantastic views of the lake and surrounding areas.

Best Things to Do in Devils Lake: Activities and Tours

Devils Lake caters to all tourists, and there is an array of exciting activities and tours available. Here are a few suggestions:

  • Fort Totten Days: An annual celebration where the history and traditions of the Dakota people are celebrated.
  • Devil’s Run: A classic car show that brings people from all over the country to Devils Lake.
  • Fishing: Devils Lake is a haven for fishing enthusiasts as it offers year-round fishing opportunities.

Week-Long Itinerary for Devils Lake

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Devils Lake:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Sullys Hill National Game Preserve, go on a nature hike.
Day 2 Explore the Old Oak Trail.
Day 3 Visit Spirit Lake Casino and Resort, enjoy live entertainment and gaming.
Day 4 Go on a fishing excursion on the Devils Lake.
Day 5 Visit Fort Totten State Historic Site, learn about the local history.
Day 6 Go on a guided hunting trip.
Day 7 Visit Devils Lake State Park and enjoy the natural beauty of the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Devils Lake

When in Devils Lake, you must try the local cuisine and beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Knoephla Soup: A creamy soup made with potato dumplings.
  • Beef and Cabbage Hotdish: A hearty meal made with ground beef, cabbage, rice, and cream of mushroom soup.
  • Walleye: A freshwater fish famous in the region.

Local Beverages

  • Prairie Rose: A dry wine made from local grapes.
  • Ice wine: A sweet dessert wine made from grapes that have been frozen on the vine.
  • Devil’s Lake Wheat: A locally brewed beer that has earned accolades among beer enthusiasts.

Where to Stay in Devils Lake: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Devils Lake offers a wide range of accommodation options. Some popular places to stay include

  • Lakeview Lodge Hostel: A budget-friendly option offering basic amenities for backpackers and solo travelers.
  • Fireside Inn & Suites: A mid-range hotel providing excellent amenities like a pool, free breakfast, and Wi-Fi.
  • The Cove: A luxury resort providing lake view rooms and a wide range of activities and amenities.

What to Buy in Devils Lake: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some suggested souvenirs to buy when in Devils Lake:

  • Handmade Crafts: The locals are known for their exceptional handmade crafts, including pottery, jewelry, and knitted products.
  • Syrup: The region’s maple and birch syrups are some of the best in the world.
  • Art: Explore the local galleries and take home a piece of exclusive Native American art.

Local Culture and Customs of Devils Lake

The region is home to the Dakota Native Americans who have lived in the area for centuries. The customs and traditions of the Dakota people are still prevalent, and visitors should respect them. The region has a rich history, and visitors should learn about the local culture during their stay.

Best Time to Visit Devils Lake

The region is beautiful year-round, but the best time to visit is during the summer, between June and August. The temperature is mild, and the outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Devils Lake

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs of the Dakota people.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Plan your trip to coincide with local celebrations and events.

Don’t:

  • Leave litter in public spaces.
  • Disturb the natural habitat of the wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Devils Lake

Devils Lake is generally a very safe place to visit. However, tourists should take normal precautions to ensure their safety. Visitors should ensure they have the proper gear and safety equipment for any adventure activities they engage in.

Health in Devils Lake

The region has excellent health facilities in case of emergencies. Visitors should ensure they have proper medical insurance before traveling.

Getting to Devils Lake: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Devils Lake

The nearest airport is the Devils Lake Regional Airport, which has flights from major US cities. Other airports in the region include the Grand Forks International Airport and Fargo’s Hector International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Devils Lake

The nearest bus stations are in Grand Forks and Fargo and connect the area with many cities in the region and the country.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Devils Lake, and the area is well-connected to the neighboring cities through a network of highways.

How to Get Around in Devils Lake

Visitors can use rental cars, taxis, or public transport to get around the area. Biking and walking are also popular modes of transportation for tourists.

Where to Go Next from Devils Lake: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Devils Lake is surrounded by many other beautiful destinations that are worth exploring. Some of the nearby must-visit places include:

  • Theodore Roosevelt National Park: A national park known for its wildlife, hiking trails, and scenic landscapes.
  • Fargo: A vibrant city with a rich culture and history located about 140 miles east of Devils Lake.
  • Bismarck: The capital of North Dakota, home to important historical attractions and situated about 195 miles south of Devils Lake.

Conclusion

Devils Lake is an excellent destination for all types of visitors, whether you want to relax in nature, explore the local culture, or engage in adventure activities.
