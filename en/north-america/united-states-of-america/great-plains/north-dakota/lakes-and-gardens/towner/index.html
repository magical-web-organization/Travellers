Introduction to Towner

Towner is a small town located in the region of Lakes and Gardens in North Dakota, United States of America. It is a peaceful and friendly community that offers a variety of exciting activities and attractions for travelers of all ages and interests. The town is surrounded by beautiful natural landscapes, including hills, forests, and lakes, providing a perfect backdrop for outdoor adventures. Whether you are looking to explore the local culture, take a stroll in beautiful parks, or simply relax and unwind, Towner is an ideal travel destination.

List of Places to Visit in Towner

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Towner is steeped in history, and visitors can explore its rich heritage by visiting the following museums and historical places:

  • Rolette County Museum - This museum showcases the history of Rolette County, including exhibits tracing the area’s settlement by the Chippewa Indians. Visitors can also explore artifacts of early settlers and local military veterans.
  • Heritage Park - This living history park has several historical buildings, including a one-room schoolhouse, a church, a railway station, and an early settler’s home.
  • Elevation Station - This former church has now been renovated to become an art gallery, and gift shop showcasing local artists.

Natural Attractions of Towner: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Towner has plenty of natural attractions suitable for those who love outdoor adventures and scenic views. Here are a few must-visit locations:

  • Cobblestone Lake - This scenic lake is located a few miles north of Towner, providing visitors with an opportunity for fishing, boating, and swimming. It is surrounded by a beautiful campground with picnic areas and hiking trails.
  • Rural Route 5 - A scenic drive that starts in Towner and takes visitors through the picturesque rolling hills of North Dakota.
  • International Peace Gardens - Located 30 miles northeast of Towner, the International Peace Gardens is an extensive botanical garden with multiple trails that showcase a plethora of beautiful plant life. Visitors can also participate in bird watching and attend musical events held here.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Towner

Towner is an ideal destination for hiking and biking. It offers several trails suitable for both beginners and advanced adventurers. Here are some notable hiking and biking routes:

  • Doug Thorson Trail - This trail runs 11 miles east of town and is ideal for hikers, walkers, and bikers. It offers magnificent views of rolling hills and agricultural landscapes, a perfect opportunity to see the North Dakota countryside.
  • Rudd Lake Trail - This trail is located southwest of Towner and runs through the beautiful countryside. It is ideal for nature enthusiasts looking to discover local wildlife and birdlife.
  • Trinity Lakes Trail - This trail is located to the north of town and is ideal for hikers who want a more challenging terrain.

Best Things to Do in Towner: Activities and Tours

Towner offers several exciting activities for travelers looking to enjoy their time in the town. Some must-do activities include:

  • Fishing - Visitors can enjoy excellent fishing at Cobblestone Lake, Rudd Lake, and Trinity Lakes. These lakes are stocked with walleye, northern pike, and bass, making them ideal for anglers.
  • Hunting - Towner is an ideal destination for hunting enthusiasts, with a variety of game birds and animals.
  • Golfing - The town has multiple golf courses catering to all skill levels, making it an ideal destination for golf enthusiasts.
  • Horseback Riding - Towner offers multiple horseback riding options for visitors, including trail rides and guided tours.

Food and Drink Scene of Towner

Towner offers some local dishes and drinks that every visitor should try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lefse - Lefse is a traditional Norwegian flatbread made with potatoes, flour, and butter. It is a popular snack in Towner.
  • Knoephla Soup - This creamy soup is a staple in restaurants in Towner. It is made with dumplings and potatoes.
  • Wild Game Sausages - Visitors who love wild game should try sausage made from elk, deer, beef, and bison.

Local Beverages

  • Craft Beer - North Dakota has several microbreweries that produce delicious craft beers. Towner has a few bars that serve local beers.
  • Honey Wine - Towner is known for its honey wine, also known as mead, which is made from honey and water.

Where to Stay in Towner: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Towner has several accommodation options suitable for every type of traveler. Here are some recommendations:

  • Kerma Hotel - The Kerma Hotel is a historic hotel in Towner, North Dakota, famous for hosting Theodore Roosevelt during his visit to the area.

What to Buy in Towner: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Travelers can buy souvenirs and local products to take home as mementos of their trip to Towner. Some recommended items include:

  • Local Artwork - Towner has a thriving local art scene, with several galleries showcasing the work of local artists.

  • Towner T-Shirts - T-Shirts with phrases like “I Love Towner” are popular souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Towner

The people of Towner are known for their friendly nature and welcoming attitude towards visitors. Visitors should dress appropriately and behave respectfully in places of worship, museums, and cemeteries. Tipping is customary in restaurants, hotels, and other service-oriented businesses.

Best Time to Visit Towner

Towner has a continental climate with cold winters and hot summers. The best time to visit Towner is during the spring and fall when the weather is pleasant and the foliage is beautiful to look at.

  • Enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and fishing.

  • Take plenty of pictures of scenic views.

Safety and Health

Safety in Towner

Towner is a safe town to visit, and crime rates are low. Visitors should take precautions while exploring natural areas, such as hiking and hunting. They should carry a first-aid kit, dress appropriately, and carry water and snacks for long hikes or outdoor activities.

Health in Towner

Towner has a few medical facilities that offer basic medical services. Visitors should carry their necessary medications and report any health concerns to a medical professional as soon as possible.
