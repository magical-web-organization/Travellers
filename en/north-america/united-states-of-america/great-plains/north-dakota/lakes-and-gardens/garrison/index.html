Introduction to Garrison

Nestled in the Lakes and Gardens region of North Dakota, Garrison is a charming town that offers a plethora of activities for visitors. From outdoor adventures to cultural attractions, Garrison has something for everyone. Whether you’re looking for a weekend getaway or a week-long vacation, Garrison should definitely be on your list of must-visit destinations. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take a closer look at the top attractions, hiking/biking routes, food and drink scene, accommodations, local culture and customs, safety and health, and transportation options in Garrison.

List of Places to Visit in Garrison

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Garrison offers a wealth of cultural and historical attractions that are worth exploring. One of the must-visit places is the Fort Stevenson State Park, which is located just a short drive from the town center. This park is home to several historical sites, such as Fort Seward and the Gordon House, which offer a glimpse into North Dakota’s past. Other popular cultural attractions in the area include the Garrison Dam Visitor Center, which provides an overview of the history and construction of the Garrison Dam, and the Earth Lodges at the Knife River Indian Villages National Historic Site, which offer an insight into the lifestyle of the Native American tribes that once inhabited the area.

Natural Attractions of Garrison: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Garrison is surrounded by natural beauty, which makes it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The Lake Sakakawea State Park is one of the top spots for outdoor activities, including fishing, boating, and hiking. The park covers a total of 1,964 acres and has several campsites that offer stunning views of the lake. Another outdoor attraction that’s worth checking out is the Audubon National Wildlife Refuge, which is home to a variety of wildlife species and several hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Garrison

If hiking or biking is your thing, Garrison has plenty of options for you. The Lewis and Clark Interpretive Trail is a popular hiking route that offers breathtaking views of the Missouri River. The trail is located in the Fort Stevenson State Park and covers a distance of approximately 5 miles, making it a perfect spot for a day hike. Another option for hikers is the Sully Creek State Recreation Area, which has several well-marked hiking trails that wind through the hills and valleys of the park.

Best Things to Do in Garrison: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and biking, Garrison has plenty of other activities and tours to offer. One of the most popular activities is boating on Lake Sakakawea. Several boat rental companies operate in the area, allowing visitors to explore the lake’s numerous bays and coves. Another popular activity is fishing, with the lake offering an abundance of species such as walleye, northern pike, and smallmouth bass. Visitors can also book guided fishing tours, which provide a unique experience and insight into the local fishing scene.

Week-Long Itinerary for Garrison

Day 1 - Visit the Fort Stevenson State Park and explore its historical sites. Day 2 - Spend the day fishing or boating on Lake Sakakawea. Day 3 - Take a guided fishing tour or explore the hiking trails at the Sully Creek State Recreation Area. Day 4 - Visit the Earth Lodges at the Knife River Indian Villages National Historic Site. Day 5 - Go on a scenic drive and explore the rugged beauty of the North Dakota countryside. Day 6 - Check out the Audubon National Wildlife Refuge and discover the area’s rich wildlife. Day 7 - Relax and unwind by the lake, or book a spa day at one of the local resorts.

Food and Drink Scene of Garrison

Must-Try Dishes

Garrison boasts of a vibrant food scene, with several local restaurants and cafes that serve up delicious cuisine. One of the must-try dishes is the famous North Dakota knoephla soup, which is available at several restaurants in the area. This hearty soup is made from dumplings, potatoes, and cream, and is the perfect dish for a chilly evening. Another local favorite is the hot beef sandwich, which features tender roast beef served on mashed potatoes with gravy.

Local Beverages

Visitors to Garrison should also try the local brews, which are renowned for their quality and flavor. One of the top breweries in the area is the Garrison Brewing Company, which offers a range of craft beers and ales. Another popular option for beer lovers is the Souris River Brewing Company, which serves up an impressive selection of beers and ciders.

Where to Stay in Garrison: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Garrison offers a range of accommodation options for visitors, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the top-rated hotels in the area include the Lakeview Lodge and the Garrison Inn. Both of these hotels offer comfortable rooms, excellent amenities, and stunning views of the lake. Visitors on a budget can also find several affordable hostels and guesthouses in the area, such as the Bearcreek Lodge and the Maumee Bay Lodge.

What to Buy in Garrison: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Garrison can also take home some unique souvenirs and local products. One of the top items to buy is the North Dakota-made pottery, which is renowned for its quality and craftsmanship. The local shops also sell several types of hand-woven textiles and Native American-inspired jewelry, which make for excellent gifts and souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Garrison

As part of the Great Plains region, Garrison has a rich cultural heritage that’s worth exploring. Visitors can learn about the area’s history and its Native American tribes by visiting the local museums and cultural centers. The area is also known for its agricultural heritage, with several farms and ranches that produce local crops such as wheat and sunflowers.

Best Time to Visit Garrison

The best time to visit Garrison is between June and September, when the weather is warm and pleasant. The summer months are ideal for outdoor activities, such as boating, fishing, and hiking, while the fall months offer stunning views of the changing leaves and vibrant sunsets. Visitors should be aware that winters in Garrison can be harsh, with heavy snowfall and cold temperatures.

  • Take in the stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Safety and Health

Safety in Garrison

Overall, Garrison is a safe destination for visitors. However, visitors should be aware of potential hazards, such as inclement weather and wildlife encounters. It’s also important to follow safety guidelines while engaging in outdoor activities, such as boating and hiking.

Health in Garrison

Visitors to Garrison should be aware of the potential health risks, such as tick-borne illnesses and sunburn. It’s important to protect yourself by wearing proper clothing and using bug spray and sunscreen.

Getting to Garrison: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Garrison

The nearest airport to Garrison is the Bismarck Municipal Airport (BIS), which is located approximately 65 miles from the town center. Visitors can rent a car or book a shuttle service to get to Garrison.

Bus Stations Near Garrison

There are no bus stations in Garrison, but visitors can take a bus to the nearby town of Minot and rent a car or take a taxi to Garrison.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Garrison using the Interstate 94, which passes through North Dakota. The state is easily accessible by road from neighboring states such as Minnesota and Montana.

How to Get Around in Garrison

The town of Garrison is small and walkable, making it easy to explore on foot. Visitors can also rent a bike or car to explore the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Garrison: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Garrison is located in the Lakes and Gardens region, which is home to several other charming towns and attractions. Visitors can explore the nearby town of Minot and its attractions, such as the Roosevelt Park Zoo and the Scandinavian Heritage Center. Another option is to take a scenic drive along the Missouri River, which offers stunning views and several outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Garrison is a hidden gem in the heart of North Dakota, offering visitors a perfect blend of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and scenic beauty. Whether you’re looking for a weekend getaway or a week-long vacation, Garrison is a destination that’s sure to leave you with unforgettable memories. With this comprehensive tour guide, you can explore the best of Garrison and discover all that this charming town has to offer.
