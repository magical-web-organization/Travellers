Introduction to Burlington

Welcome to Burlington, a charming city located in Lakes and Gardens, North Dakota, in Great Plains, United States of America. Its picturesque views, cultural significance, and natural landscapes make it a popular destination among travelers. Burlington is a small town, but with plenty of adventures to offer, from museums and historical sites to outdoor activities and world-class dining options.

List of Places to Visit in Burlington

Whether it’s natural landscapes or historical sites that interest you, Burlington has an array of attractions for you to explore. Here’s a list of the top places you must-visit while in the city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lewis and Clark Interpretive Center: This museum is a must-visit for history and nature buffs, telling the story of the famous Lewis and Clark Expedition through interactive exhibits.

  • Antelope Creek State Wildlife Area: Covering over 17,000 acres of grassland and rivulet systems, the Antelope Creek State Wildlife Area is a perfect place to spot wild animals, ride horses, or hike along the peaceful trails in the natural reserve.

  • Old Town Hall Theater: This historical landmark also operates as a live theater. It was built in 1915 and is an excellent example of architectural beauty.

Natural Attractions of Burlington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sakakawea Lake: Located near the city, this man-made lake was created in the 1950s by damming the Missouri River. Sakakawea Lake offers activities such as boating, fishing, swimming, and beach-going during the summer season.

  • Big Muddy Wildlife Refuge: With a vast diversity of wildlife, including bighorn sheep, mountain lions, and beavers, this park provides a unique opportunity for nature enthusiasts to observe wildlife in their natural habitat.

  • Schramm Park State Recreation Area: Located in the Missouri River, this stunning park includes miles of trails winding through woodlands and along a scenic trout stream.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Burlington

Burlington is an ideal destination for hikers and bikers, with numerous trails to discover. Some of the popular trails include:

  • Marten Creek Trail: This 5.2-mile trail is moderate in difficulty, making it suitable for beginners, and features scenic views of wildlife and waterfalls.

  • Lostwood National Wildlife Refuge: This 49,000-acre park includes six hiking trails, ranging from 0.5 miles to 7.3 miles. It is well known for its diverse bird species and flowers.

  • Missouri River Valley: This trail provides scenic views of the Missouri River and is an excellent option for hikers of all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Burlington: Activities and Tours

Aside from the natural and cultural attractions, Burlington offers an array of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy:

  • Fishing: With a wide variety of fish species, such as walleye, catfish, and salmon, fishing on Sakakawea Lake is a popular activity among visitors.

  • Boating: Explore the beauty of the lake through boating, kayaking, or jet-skiing.

  • Golfing: The city has several golf courses for visitors to enjoy and practice their swings.

  • Hunting: The Big Muddy Wildlife Refuge offers hunting opportunities for waterfowl, upland game, and big game.

If you have a week to spare, here’s a suggested itinerary for the top attractions in Burlington:

Food and Drink Scene of Burlington

Burlington’s food and drink scene offers a blend of traditional, regional, and international cuisines; here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Knoephla Soup: This creamy chicken and dumplings soup is a traditional dish that is popular among locals.

  • Frybread: A Native American staple, frybread is a fried dough topped with either sweet or savory toppings.

  • Walleye: A local fish, which is often prepared battered and fried, is a must-try for seafood lovers.

Local Beverages

  • Wild Prairie Winery: The winery offers a vast range of red, white, and fruit wines made locally.

  • Prairie Rose Coffee House & Gift Shop: This cozy coffee house offers patrons a variety of locally roasted and crafted coffees, along with specialty drinks.

  • Driftwood bar: A hallmark of Burlington’s nightlife scene, this bar serves both local and international beer and spirits.

Where to Stay in Burlington: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Burlington has a range of accommodations to choose from, depending on your budget and preferences. Here are some options:

  • Cobblestone Inn and Suites: This hotel is well-situated near attractions like the Lewis and Clark Interpretive Center and offers excellent amenities like free Wi-Fi, a fitness center, and a complimentary breakfast.

  • Little Missouri Inn and Suites: This hotel provides guests with pet-friendly accommodations, free breakfast, pool access, meeting rooms, and a fitness center.

  • The Lodge at Little Missouri State Park: For travelers seeking a more rustic experience, this lodge is located in a state park and offers elegant guest rooms and suites.

What to Buy in Burlington: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for memorable souvenirs and local products to bring back from your trip? Here are some options:

  • Burlington-branded Apparel: A perfect souvenir, featuring Burlington’s name in different fonts and sizes, printed on t-shirts, jackets, and hats.

  • Hand-woven Rugs: A unique local gift that depicts the native culture of North Dakota.

  • Heritage Homemade Soaps: Made with locally sourced ingredients, these hand-made soaps are specialized with their specific scents.

Local Culture and Customs of Burlington

Native American culture influences the local customs and lifestyles of the Burlington residents. Visitors are advised to be mindful and respectful of the local culture and its heritage, including rituals and customs.

Best Time to Visit Burlington

The best time to visit Burlington is during the summer months, from June to September, as the weather is mild and sunny, providing ample opportunities for outdoor activities and adventures. Winter months from November to March, are cold and snowy and are best for indoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Burlington

To make the most of your trip to Burlington, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and the residents’ privacy.

  • Explore the local natural wonders of the city.

  • Sample local cuisine at the area’s top-rated dining spots.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture, customs, and religious beliefs.

  • Litter or damage the natural scenery in the city.

  • Engage in illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Burlington has low crime rates, and visitors can have a safe and enjoyable experience. However, visitors are advised to take precautions to protect themselves and their possessions.

Safety in Burlington

  • Keep an emergency contact number on hand.

  • Avoid unmarked and/or deserted locations at night.

  • Follow the road-safety rules while driving or biking.

Health in Burlington

  • Carry medications for allergies and other medical conditions.

  • Apply insect repellent when exploring natural sites to avoid tick and mosquito bites.

  • Stay hydrated and apply sunscreen to avoid dehydration and sunburn.

Getting to Burlington: Transportation Options and Logistics

Burlington can be accessed by air or ground transportation, depending on your preference.

Airports Near Burlington

  • Bismarck-Mandan Municipal Airport: The airport is 28 miles from Burlington and serves several domestic flights throughout the United States.

Bus Stations Near Burlington

  • Greyhound Bus Station: Located in Bismarck, the station is 26 miles from Burlington and connects to several neighboring cities.

Other Options

  • Drive: Visitors can travel to Burlington via highways, especially from the nearby city of Bismarck. Rental cars are available at the airport and other car rental agencies.

  • Cab Service: The city has cab services available for transportation to and from locations within the city.

How to Get Around in Burlington

Burlington is a small city, and it is fairly easy to navigate it on foot or by car. Visitors can also rent bikes to explore the city’s beautiful scenic routes.

Where to Go Next from Burlington: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time to explore after visiting Burlington, here are some suggestions for nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Bismarck: Located just a few miles away from Burlington, this city is home to several top-notch museums, galleries, and theatres.

  • Theodore Roosevelt National Park: This national park is located approximately 30 miles from Burlington and offers stunning natural landscapes, including canyons, badlands, and grasslands.

  • Jamestown: This nearby city offers visitors a glimpse of life on the Northern Great Plains, complete with buffalo, bison, and prairie dog herds.

Conclusion

Burlington offers visitors a selection of attractions and activities, making it an ideal destination for travelers seeking natural beauty, charming culture, and adventure. Plan your trip using this comprehensive travel guide and take advantage of the city’s many treasures. Enjoy the unique experience that only Burlington can offer!
