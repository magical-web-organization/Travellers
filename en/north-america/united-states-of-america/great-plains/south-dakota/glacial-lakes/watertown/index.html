Introduction to Watertown

Nestled in the heart of Glacial Lakes in South Dakota, USA, Watertown is a small city that has something for everyone. From rich historical attractions to thrilling outdoor adventures, this charming city offers a wide range of activities and attractions for travelers of all ages.

Whether you’re a history buff, an adventure seeker, or a food enthusiast, Watertown won’t disappoint. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll cover the best things to do, places to visit, and local culture of this beautiful city.

List of Places to Visit in Watertown

  • Redlin Art Center: The world-renowned painting collection of Terry Redlin, known to be a master of natural settings created with lighting, is displayed here.
  • Codington County Heritage Museum: A historical museum that showcases the history, culture, and traditions of Codington County and South Dakota.
  • Bramble Park Zoo: A haven to an array of fauna ranging from exotic birds to monkeys to polar bears, Bramble Park Zoo provides entertainment for all ages.
  • Lake Kampeska: One of South Dakota’s largest lakes offering activities such as boating, fishing, birdwatching, and more.
  • Historic Uptown Watertown: A downtown area that highlights the traditional architecture of the region with a range of unique shops, dining experiences, and lodging options.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

When in Watertown, immerse yourself in its rich history and culture by visiting its museums and historical landmarks.

Redlin Art Center

The Redlin Art Center is a showcase of Terry Redlin’s remarkable talent where he captures his experiences and memories of the American rural experience and its culture in his paintings. This 52,000 square foot space is a perfect place for art lovers and a perfect place for some relaxation and rejuvenation.

Codington County Heritage Museum

This museum is considered as one of South Dakota’s best-kept secrets. The museum takes you on a drive through the history of the county and has interactive exhibits of the historical buildings and places of South Dakota from the 1800s to the present day.

Natural Attractions of Watertown: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Surrounded by beautiful landscapes, Watertown is a great destination for outdoor activities and adventures.

Lake Kampeska

One of the largest lakes in South Dakota, Lake Kampeska offers multiple recreation activities like boating, fishing, bird watching, camping, and more. It is a perfect place for a family picnic and an enjoyable day with friends.

Pelican Lake

Pelican Lake lies in the heart of the Glacial Lakes region in Northeastern South Dakota. The lake provides excellent fishing opportunities for walleye, northern pike, perch and more. The crystal-clear water surrounded by oak trees makes it a picture-perfect location for photography.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Watertown

Hiking and biking enthusiasts have several options when in Watertown, here are some of the popular routes.

Terry Redlin Art Center Trail

The Terry Redlin Art Center Trail is a self-guided tour that takes visitors to every artwork of Terry Redlin. The 7-acre outdoor space provides engaging artwork for a pleasant walk with a sprinkle of history.

Prairie Winds Trail

This 3.8 miles trail is perfect for cycling, walking or running. It runs around the lake and offers picturesque scenic views that will soothe your body and mind.

Best Things to Do in Watertown: Activities and Tours

Bramble Park Zoo

Bramble Park Zoo is one of the prominent wildlife destinations in the region, providing shelter to hundreds of exotic animals. The zoo also offers educational programs and activities for people of all ages.

Watertown Family Aquatic Center

This year-round water-centred recreational facility offers entertainment for people of all ages with several swimming pools, whirlpool, water slides, and more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Watertown

Day 1: Terry Redlin Art Center Trail, Prairie Winds Trail Day 2: Bramble Park Zoo, Lake Kampeska Day 3: South Dakota Agricultural Heritage Museum, Pelican Lake Day 4: Thunder Road, Historic Uptown Watertown Day 5: Codington County Heritage Museum, Goss Opera House Day 6: Watertown Family Aquatic Center, Sandy Shore State Park Day 7: Golf Course

Food and Drink Scene of Watertown

Must-Try Dishes

When in Watertown, do not miss the traditional food such as the Pheasant sandwiches, walleye, and freshly baked goods.

Local Beverages

South Dakota has a strong preference for locally crafted beer. Discover the local breweries in and around the city offering some of the best brews in the area.

When in Watertown, do not miss South Dakota’s unique products, including homemade soaps, locally owned boutiques and handcrafted jewellery.

Local Culture and Customs of Watertown

South Dakota has a colourful history and is famous for its cowboys, rodeos, and farming culture. When in Watertown, take part in the local events such as performances and theatre festivals to understand the community’s culture.

Best Time to Visit Watertown

Watertown experiences all four seasons, with warm summers, brisk winters, and mild weather in spring and autumn. Summer months are best to visit for outdoor activities and to soak in the cultural heritage of the region.

Safety and Health

Safety in Watertown

Watertown is a safe and welcoming community, with a low crime rate and a friendly atmosphere. But, as in any unfamiliar place, it is essential to take necessary precautions, like locking your vehicle, securing valuables, and staying vigilant.

Health in Watertown

Medical facilities in the city provide adequate care. In case of a medical emergency, reach out to the local hospital and healthcare centers.
