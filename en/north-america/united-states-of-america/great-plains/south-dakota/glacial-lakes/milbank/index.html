Milbank is a hidden gem tucked away in Glacial Lakes, South Dakota, found in the Great Plains region of the United States of America. With breathtaking views of nature and a vibrant culture, Milbank is a must-visit destination for any traveler. In this ultimate Milbank travel guide, we’ll explore all the best things to do, places to stay, and local customs that will make your trip unforgettable.

Introduction to Milbank

Milbank is a charming community situated on the banks of the Big Stone Lake, with a population of around 3,311 people. It is a historical town with a thriving economy, which you can explore through its numerous museums, galleries, and local shops that sell everything from artisanal crafts to rustic antiques.

The people of Milbank are welcoming and proud of their community, which is readily evident in the numerous festivals and events that take place throughout the year.

List of Places to Visit in Milbank

When in Milbank, you’ll never run out of exciting spots to explore. Here are some of the most popular places you should visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Milbank has a rich and exciting history, and you can explore it through its numerous museums and historical sites. The Grant County Courthouse Museum is one such site. It houses a wealth of artifacts, articles, and exhibits that tell the stories of Milbank’s past. The museum has guided tours, and the knowledgeable staff makes learning about Milbank’s history fascinating.

Another ought-to-visit attraction is the Milbank Historic District, which comprises 47 unique sites that date back to the late 1800s and early 1900s. The district is listed on the National Register of Historic Places.

Natural Attractions of Milbank: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Milbank is full of scenic views and outdoor activities that will get your adrenaline pumping. Here are a few recommended spots:

Big Stone Lake, Milbank’s largest and most prominent lake, spans over 26 miles and is an exceptional fishing destination. The lake offers a variety of fish species all year round, including walleye, northern pike, crappie, and bullhead. If fishing is not your thing, you can take a leisurely stroll or go kayaking on the lake shore to get closer to nature.

For more outdoor experiences, head over to the nearby Horseshoe Lake, a peaceful spot where visitors can swim, enjoy a picnic or hike along the scenic nature trail.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Milbank

For avid hikers and bikers, check out the city’s bike trails, which run a total length of 7 miles and connect several city parks and the town’s commercial district.

The trails provide excellent rides for bikers of all levels, and hikers can expect to see the beautiful countryside that surrounds Milbank.

Best Things to Do in Milbank: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best activities and tours you can take while in Milbank:

1. Visit the Milbank Area Farmer’s Market:

Operational during the summer months, the Farmer’s market offers a fantastic shopping opportunity for fresh local produce, baked goods, jams and jellies, and handmade products.

2. Take a guided fishing tour on Big Stone Lake:

Hire a guide for a unique and unforgettable fishing experience on the Big Stone Lake. You’ll get a chance to catch some of the area’s popular fish species.

3. Explore the Grant County Courthouse Museum:

Take a tour of the museum, which houses significant historical artifacts and fascinating exhibits.

Food and Drink Scene of Milbank

Milbank has something to offer everyone, including food lovers. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

1. Blarney Stone Pub & Grill:

A local Irish-themed restaurant, the Blarney Stone Pub offers excellent fish and chips, bangers and mash, and shepherd’s pie. It also has a variety of beers on tap.

2. Pizza Ranch:

A family-friendly restaurant, serving both traditional and unique pizza variations, salads, chicken wings, and other delicious eats.

Local Beverages

1. Big Stone Brewing:

Milbank’s only brewery that offers an excellent selection of craft beers and a cozy ambiance.

2. Bottom’s Up Brewery:

A craft brewery in nearby Corona, Bottom’s up is known for its delightful range of beers, including ales and stouts.

Where to Stay in Milbank: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Milbank has different lodging options, including hotels, motels, and guesthouses. Here are a few good options:

1. Super 8 by Wyndham Milbank:

This is an affordable and comfortable option, with complimentary continental breakfast, Wi-Fi, and ample free parking. The hotel provides basic amenities, such as air conditioning and cable TV.

2. Lantern Inn:

This is a historic bed and breakfast that provides a cozy ambiance, elegant guestrooms, and complimentary breakfast. The inn is ideal for lovebirds, offering romance packages that include wine and complementary chocolates.

What to Buy in Milbank: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Milbank is an excellent place to shop for souvenirs as the local shops offer unique and creative products that embody Milbank’s essence. Consider buying artisanal crafts, locally grown produce, and rustic antiques.

Local Culture and Customs of Milbank

Milbank has a rich cultural heritage, which visitors can learn about from its numerous museums, galleries, and festivals. The people of Milbank are warm and hospitable, and taking the time to learn and appreciate their culture can make your visit even more rewarding.

Best Time to Visit Milbank

The best time to visit Milbank is during the summer (between June and September) as the weather is mild, and most of the local attractions and events are operational. The fall season (October to November) is also a delightful time to visit as the autumn colors make the city picturesque.

Safety and Health

Safety in Milbank

Milbank is a safe destination but as a visitor, it is always best to take precautions. Avoid walking alone in unlit areas at odd hours, especially if you’re unfamiliar with the neighborhood.

Health in Milbank

Milbank has excellent medical facilities, including hospitals, clinics, rehabilitation facilities, and long-term care. The city also has several pharmacies where visitors can purchase prescription medicines.

How to Get Around in Milbank

Milbank is a small community with easy-to-navigate roads. The best way to get around is by car, but visitors can also rent a bike to explore the city’s cycling trails.
