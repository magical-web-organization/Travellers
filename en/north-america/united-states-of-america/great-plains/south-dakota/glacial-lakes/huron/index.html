Introduction to Huron

Nestled in the heart of South Dakota’s Glacial Lakes region, Huron is a small and vibrant city that offers outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and breathtaking scenic views that will leave every visitor spellbound.

Huron derives its name from the Huron Indians who were the first inhabitants of the area. The town is home to a diverse community and lively events that celebrate Huron’s small-town charm and local culture.

Whether you’re a history buff, nature enthusiast, foodie, or adventure seeker, Huron promises an unforgettable experience. In this travel guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan your next trip to Huron.

List of Places to Visit in Huron

Huron is a treasure trove of history, culture, and natural beauty. Here’s a list of can’t-miss attractions and must-see destinations in Huron:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • South Dakota State Fair: A 10-day annual event held in August and September, showcasing the state’s livestock, agriculture, crafts, and entertainment.

  • Dakotaland Museum: A history museum that showcases artifacts, exhibits, and interactive displays that depict the history of Huron and the surrounding area.

  • Historic Huron State Hospital Museum: The museum is housed in a former asylum built-in 1883, and provides insights into the history of psychiatry and the lives of the patients who were treated here.

Natural Attractions of Huron: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Winter Sports Park: A winter wonderland that offers ice skating, sledding, cross-country skiing, and snowmobiling during the winter season.

  • Kain Park: A scenic park that offers walking trails, biking trails, fishing, and a playground.

  • Broadway Avenue Historic District: A National Register of Historic Places site that features beautifully restored historic buildings and houses.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Huron

Take a break from the hustle and bustle of the city and explore the great outdoors of Huron.

  • Dakota Nature Park: A 150-acre nature park that offers hiking trails, bird watching, and outdoor activities all year round.

  • Beaver Creek Nature Area: A 195-acre nature area offering hiking trails, bird watching, and fishing.

Best Things to Do in Huron: Activities and Tours

  • Huron Community Campus: A multi-use facility that offers a wide range of sports activities, fitness classes, and events.

  • Huron Area Aquatic Center: A family-friendly water park that features water slides, a lazy river, and a splash pad.

  • Huron Butterfly Garden: A beautiful garden full of vibrant flowers and fluttering butterflies.

Week-Long Itinerary for Huron

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Huron:

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore downtown Huron and visit the Dakotaland Museum.
Day 2 Hike or bike on Dakota Nature Park trails and visit the Huron Butterfly Garden.
Day 3 Visit the Historic Huron State Hospital Museum and enjoy a picnic at Kain Park.
Day 4 Head to the Winter Sports Park for ice-skating and sledding.
Day 5 Explore the Broadway Avenue Historic District and enjoy a shopping spree.
Day 6 Enjoy water fun at the Huron Area Aquatic Center and take a stroll down the Huron Riverwalk.
Day 7 Visit the South Dakota State Fair and enjoy the carnival rides, entertainment, and food.

Food and Drink Scene of Huron

Huron offers a diverse culinary scene that caters to every taste preference. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to indulge in during your trip:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pheasant: A classic South Dakota dish, served at Pheasant Cafe, that offers a tasty twist on this local game bird.

  • Chislic: A regional delicacy of cubes of meat skewered and fried or grilled, served with garlic toast, and dipping sauce.

  • Fry Bread Tacos: A Native American dish of deep-fried bread stuffed with ground beef, grated cheese, lettuce, and tomatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Dakota Thunder: A refreshing beer, brewed locally at Aces & 8s Brewing Co.

  • South Dakota Sunshine: A light and fruity wine, served at the Prairie Berry Winery tasting room.

Where to Stay in Huron: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Huron offers a variety of options for accommodations, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels.

  • Crossroads Hotel & Huron Event Center: A modern hotel with spacious rooms, indoor swimming pool, and fitness center.

  • The SureStay Hotel by Best Western: A newly renovated hotel that offers free breakfast, high-speed internet, and on-site laundry.

  • Huron Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel offering shared and private rooms, a communal kitchen, and bathroom facilities.

What to Buy in Huron: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t leave Huron empty-handed, shop for souvenirs and local products at the following places:

  • Main Street Marketplace: A charming store that sells unique and locally made gifts, home decor, and art.

  • Becker’s Sundries: A classic soda fountain and drugstore that offers vintage candies, gifts, and souvenirs.

  • Elegance on Dakota: A boutique store that sells beautiful and stylish clothing, accessories, and gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Huron

Huron has a rich and diverse cultural heritage that is celebrated through the town’s festivals, events, and traditions. The town also has a strong agricultural culture, which is reflected in its cuisine, art, and way of life.

When interacting with locals, it’s vital to be respectful and courteous. The people of Huron are friendly and hospitable and will appreciate visitors who embrace the local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Huron

The best time to visit Huron is during the summer months between June and August. The weather is mild, and the outdoor activities are in full swing.

If you’re a fan of winter sports, plan your trip between December and February when the Winter Sports Park is open.

Do’s and Don’ts of Huron

Do:

  • Embrace the local culture and traditions

  • Respect the natural beauty of Huron

  • Be courteous and friendly to locals

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment

  • Disrespect local customs and beliefs

  • Engage in illegal activities

Safety and Health

Safety in Huron

Huron is a safe city; however, visitors are advised to take precautions like locking car doors and securing valuables.

Health in Huron

Visit Huron with peace of mind, knowing that the city offers quality health care services at the Huron Regional Medical Center.

Getting to Huron: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Huron

The closest airports to Huron are:

  • Huron Regional Airport

  • Watertown Regional Airport

Bus Stations Near Huron

Greyhound offers bus service to Huron from several surrounding cities.

Other Options

Rent a car or use a ride-sharing service to reach Huron easily.

How to Get Around in Huron

Huron has a well-established public transportation system, with buses running through the city’s major destinations and sights.

Taxis, rental cars, and ride-sharing services are also readily available.

Where to Go Next from Huron: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Huron is located in the heart of South Dakota and is surrounded by many picturesque destinations, including:

  • De Smet: A small town that offers unique history and culture experiences about pioneer life and homesteading genres.

  • Pierre: The state’s capital offers a unique cultural and historic experience, quaint shops, and lovely restaurants.

  • Sioux Falls: The largest city in South Dakota offers a wide range of cultural attractions, outdoor activities, and shopping.

Conclusion

Huron may be a small city, but it’s packed with natural beauty, cultural experiences, and local charm that will leave you with unforgettable memories.

From hiking and biking to historic tours and local cuisine, Huron has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your next adventure to South Dakota’s enchanting Glacial Lakes region.
