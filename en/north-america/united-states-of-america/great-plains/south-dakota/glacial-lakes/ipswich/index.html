Introduction to Ipswich

Nestled in Glacial Lakes region of South Dakota, Ipswich is a charming small town that boasts of a rich cultural heritage and breathtaking natural beauty. Surrounded by serene lakes, rolling hills, and vast grasslands, Ipswich promises visitors a unique and unforgettable taste of South Dakota.

Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, a history buff, or a food lover, Ipswich has something for everyone. In this travel guide, we’ll highlight the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay to help you make the most out of your trip to Ipswich.

List of Places to Visit in Ipswich

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Edmunds County Museum: This museum offers a fascinating look at the history of Edmunds County with exhibits on agriculture, Native Americans, and early pioneers. Visitors can explore the town’s first pharmacy, doctor’s office, and the Depot, which shows the importance of railways to the town.
  • St. Joseph’s Catholic Church: Built-in 1914, St. Joseph’s Catholic Church showcases stunning architecture and handmade stained-glass windows that illustrate the life of the church’s patron saint. It is also the highest point in Ipswich with breathtaking views of the town and countryside.
  • Hutterite Colonies: The Hutterite Colonies, located near Ipswich, are an integral part of the region’s history. Visitors can learn about their unique way of life and purchase handmade crafts and baked goods produced in the colony.

Natural Attractions of Ipswich: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Oahe: Situated on the Missouri River, Lake Oahe is one of the largest reservoir systems in the United States and offers excellent opportunities for boating, fishing, and swimming. Visitors can also hike the nearby trails or go birdwatching.
  • Split Rock Creek State Park: With its scenic views and abundant wildlife, Split Rock Creek State Park is a favorite among nature lovers. Visitors can hike the trails, ride bikes, canoe, or fish in Big Stone Lake.
  • Ipswich Recreation Area: Perfect for a family picnic, the Ipswich Recreation Area has a playground, picnic shelters, and a swimming pool. Visitors can also partake in boating and fishing on one of the park’s four lakes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ipswich

  • The OC Trail: A 12-mile trail used for both hiking and biking. The OC Trail traverses Edmunds County from the town on Ipswich to the nearby city of Oakes, North Dakota.
  • Lake Louise Recreational Trail: A 1.5-mile trail surrounding the stunning Lake Louise. The trail boasts of scenic views and is especially popular among bird watchers.

Best Things to Do in Ipswich: Activities and Tours

  • Tea and Tour: The Edmunds County Historical Society organizes Tea and Tour where visitors can sample homemade treats while getting an in-depth knowledge of Ipswich’s history.
  • Hunting and Fishing: With numerous lakes and rivers, Ipswich provides excellent hunting and fishing opportunities. Visitors can experience fly fishing on the nearby James River or hunt for pheasants, turkey, and deer in the area’s many hunting preserves.
  • Ipswich’s Winter Wonderland: A festival that takes place during the holiday season. Special events include live music performances, light displays, hay rides, and a visit with Santa.

Food and Drink Scene of Ipswich

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pheasant: As the state bird of South Dakota, pheasant is a popular dish in Ipswich. This game bird is often served roasted or pan-seared with wild rice or mashed potatoes.
  • Kuchen: A German pastry, kuchen, is a favorite among Ipswich’s residents. This sweet treat is made with custard and is topped with fruit or streusel.

Local Beverages

  • Coteau Red: Produced by Schade Vineyard, Coteau Red is a unique blend of Frontenac and DeChaunac grapes that produces a flavorful red wine with a fruity fragrance.
  • Flasher Red Ale: Brewed by Ipswich’s own Foxtail Brewing Company, Flasher Red Ale is a bold hoppy ale with rich, malty flavors.

Where to Stay in Ipswich: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Ipswich Inn: A cozy and comfortable place with 20 rooms all with quality bedding and other amenities, and a full-service restaurant serving breakfast, lunch, and dinner in a homely ambience.
  • Paisley Pod:- A modern and affordable option located in the heart of Ipswich. It offers guests a choice of well-appointed rooms and capsule-style rooms.
  • Benoit’s Lodge & Cabins: For a more rustic experience, Benoit’s Lodge & Cabins offer cozy cabins and hunting lodges with modern amenities, including fully equipped kitchens and comfortable bedding.

What to Buy in Ipswich: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Native American Crafts: The area has a thriving Native American culture, and visitors can purchase traditional crafts, including beaded jewelry and pottery, at the Edmunds County Museum gift shop.
  • Hutterite Baked Goods and Crafts: Hutterite colonies are known for producing quality hand-made crafts and baked goods, including canned preserves, artisanal bread, and honey.

Local Culture and Customs of Ipswich

Ipswich is primarily a farming community, with residents relying on agriculture and hunting for their livelihood. It’s known for its Hutterite colonies, which operate much like the Amish communities.

Visitors are recommended to respect the local culture by dressing modestly and being mindful of cultural customs. Visiting the Hutterite colonies is a unique experience, and visitors are expected to show respect for their privacy and way of life.

Best Time to Visit Ipswich

The best time to visit Ipswich is during the summer months, June to August, when the temperatures are warm and the weather is ideal for outdoor activities. The winter months, from December to February, can be bitterly cold and best avoided if you don’t enjoy frigid temperatures. Additionally, in the fall months, September to November, the area is also fantastic due to the changing colors of the leaves.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ipswich

Do:

  • Bring comfortable, sturdy walking or hiking shoes.
  • Layer your clothing, as weather conditions can change quickly.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing. Dress modestly.
  • Interfere with local wildlife or damage the natural environment.
  • Leave valuables in your car or unattended when out exploring

Safety and Health

Safety in Ipswich

Ipswich is a safe and welcoming town. Visitors should take standard safety precautions, such as not leaving valuables in their vehicals, locking their doors, and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Ipswich

Ipswich has an excellent medical facility, the Avera St. Luke’s Hospital. Visitors with medical concerns can seek care at the hospital or nearby clinics.

Getting to Ipswich: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ipswich

Aberdeen Regional Airport is the nearest airport served by major airlines and frequent flights. The distance between Aberdeen and Ipswich is approximately 25 miles.

Bus Stations Near Ipswich

The nearest bus station is in Aberdeen, served by Jefferson Lines. IPSWICH customers can arrange pickups with Jefferson Lines in Aberdeen by calling 605-225-4801 or stops in nearby towns.

Other Options

Ipswich has a public transportation system that is primarily served by local taxis and ride-sharing services.

How to Get Around in Ipswich

One of the best ways to explore Ipswich is on foot or by bicycle. Local car rentals are available for visitors who prefer to drive. You can also opt for locally available ride-sharing and taxi services for your transportation needs.

Where to Go Next from Ipswich: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Aberdeen: Located approximately 25 miles from Ipswich, Aberdeen offers numerous shops, restaurants, and attractions, including the Dacotah Prairie Museum and Storybook Land.
  • Watertown: A one-hour drive from Ipswich, Watertown, boasts of fantastic fishing and hunting as well as outdoor recreational activities such as hiking and biking.

Conclusion

Ipswich is a charming small town with fabulous indoor and outdoor attractions for visitors of all ages. Be sure to pack comfortable shoes and outdoor clothing essentials, along with an open mind to experience the town’s unique culture and customs. With our comprehensive travel guide, you’ll surely make unforgettable memories that you’ll cherish for years to come.
