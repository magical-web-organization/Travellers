Introduction to De Smet

De Smet is a charming small town nestled in the heart of Glacial Lakes region, in South Dakota, United States. The town has a rich history, beauty, and peace that offer a delightful getaway experience. It’s known as the childhood home of Laura Ingalls Wilder, whose novels inspired the popular TV series “Little House on the Prairie.”

List of Places to Visit in De Smet

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

De Smet is home to many significant historical landmarks and museums. Be sure to visit:

  • Laura Ingalls Wilder Historic Home & Museum
  • De Smet Cemetery & Memorial Marker
  • The 1880 Flour Mill
  • Kingsbury County Courthouse

Natural Attractions of De Smet: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

De Smet is a paradise for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. Check out these attractions:

  • Lake Thompson: The second-largest natural lake in South Dakota and popular for fishing, boating, and camping.
  • Ingalls Homestead: A historical family farm where you can experience the life of pioneers through various activities, including wagon rides, farm chores, and more.
  • De Smet Wildlife Refuge: This natural preserve is home to a wide variety of animals, including deer, foxes, beavers, and more.

Hiking/Biking Routes of De Smet

De Smet has an extensive network of hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking views, natural surroundings, and a variety of terrains. Check out these popular trails:

  • Prairie Hill Trail: A 2.2-mile trail that takes you through the beautiful prairies and hills of De Smet.
  • Lake Thompson Trail: A 3.1-mile trail that runs along the shore of Lake Thompson.
  • Lake Hendricks State Park Trail: A 3.5-mile loop trail that provides picturesque views of Lake Hendricks and its surroundings.

Best Things to Do in De Smet: Activities and Tours

There are many fun things to do in De Smet, whether you’re traveling solo, with friends, or family. Here are some of the best activities and tours:

  • Horse-drawn carriage tour: Learn about the history and architecture of De Smet while riding on a horse-drawn carriage.
  • Fishing: Lake Thompson is known for its abundant variety of fish species, including walleye, northern pike, and crappie.
  • Shopping: De Smet has a range of unique shops that offer art, antiques, and locally made products.

Week-Long Itinerary for De Smet

Day 1: Visit the Laura Ingalls Wilder Historic Home & Museum. Day 2: Explore the De Smet Cemetery & Memorial Marker, and Kingsbury County Courthouse. Day 3: Go fishing or camping at Lake Thompson. Day 4: Discover the Ingalls Homestead and experience the life of pioneers. Day 5: Hike or bike the Lake Hendricks State Park Trail. Day 6: Take a horse-drawn carriage tour, and enjoy shopping for souvenirs. Day 7: Relax and enjoy the natural beauty of De Smet.

Food and Drink Scene of De Smet

Must-Try Dishes

De Smet offers plenty of delicious local food options. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Wild game: Try the locally sourced deer or pheasant for a unique culinary experience.
  • Kuchen: A traditional German pastry filled with fruit or custard.
  • Chislic: A regional dish of bite-sized pieces of meat, grilled or deep-fried and served on skewers.

Local Beverages

De Smet has a burgeoning craft beer scene, with various breweries and pubs. Make sure to try the local brews, including:

  • Lake Thompson Lager: A refreshing and crisp brew made with locally grown grains.
  • Ingalls Red Ale: A medium-bodied beer with a caramel and toffee note.
  • De Smet Wheat Beer: A light-bodied beer with a citrusy and refreshing taste.

Where to Stay in De Smet: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

De Smet has several lodging options to suit every budget and preference. Consider these options:

  • Prairie Inn: A comfortable and affordable hotel in the heart of De Smet.
  • Cedar Shore Resort: A luxurious lakefront resort close to Lake Thompson.
  • The Loft Above: A charming and cozy guesthouse that’s perfect for a romantic getaway.

What to Buy in De Smet: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

De Smet has many unique local products that make for perfect souvenirs. Here are some ideas:

  • Ingalls family memorabilia: The Laura Ingalls Wilder Historic Home & Museum has a gift shop that offers original manuscripts, books, and souvenirs related to the Ingalls family.
  • Handmade quilts: The local quilt shops offer beautiful handmade quilts that are perfect for a cozy home.
  • Wild game meat: De Smet has several shops that offer locally sourced deer, pheasant, and other wild game.

Local Culture and Customs of De Smet

De Smet has a friendly and welcoming community that treasures its cultural heritage. The local culture and customs reflect the pioneering spirit and values of the American Midwest.

Best Time to Visit De Smet

The best time to visit De Smet is from May to October when the weather is pleasant and comfortable.

Getting to De Smet: Transportation Options and Logistics

De Smet is easily accessible via various transportation options.

Bus Stations Near De Smet

The nearest bus station to De Smet is in Watertown.
