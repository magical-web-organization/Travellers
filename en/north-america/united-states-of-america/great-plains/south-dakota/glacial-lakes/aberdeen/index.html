Introduction to Aberdeen

Aberdeen is a charming city located in the Glacial Lakes region of South Dakota, in the heart of Great Plains, USA. Known for its historic architectural landmarks, cultural attractions, and natural beauty, Aberdeen is the perfect destination for travelers looking for a unique and authentic experience.

Whether you’re interested in exploring the rich local history, hiking along scenic trails, or trying local cuisine, Aberdeen has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan your trip to Aberdeen.

List of Places to Visit in Aberdeen

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Aberdeen has a rich cultural history, and visitors can explore this through a number of museums and historical landmarks.

  • The Dacotah Prairie Museum: A state-of-the-art museum showcasing South Dakota’s history, culture, and heritage.

  • The Brown County Courthouse Museum: Housed in an elegant building dating back to 1904, the museum features exhibits on local history, agriculture, and industry.

  • The Storybook Land Castle: A magical fairy-tale-inspired castle that offers fun for both kids and adults.

  • The Wylie Park Campground: Set in a stunning natural environment, Wylie Park features hiking and biking trails, fishing spots, and camping facilities.

Natural Attractions of Aberdeen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Aberdeen is home to a number of beautiful parks and scenic areas, perfect for outdoor enthusiasts.

  • Richmond Lake Recreation Area: A stunning park with over 1,100 acres of space, including a pristine lake, hiking and biking trails, and campsites.

  • Mina Lake Recreation Area: An excellent place for fishing and boating, with a marina, boat ramp, and fishing pier.

  • Sand Lake National Wildlife Refuge: A bird watcher’s paradise, with over 400 species of birds visiting seasonally, and numerous hiking trails.

  • Storybook Land: A family-friendly attraction featuring a variety of fairy tale and nursery rhyme displays, perfect for kids and adults.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aberdeen

Aberdeen has several great hiking and biking paths that pass through stunning natural areas, offering incredible scenic views.

  • The South Dakota Discovery Trail: A 97-mile trail route, perfect for hiking, biking, or cross-country skiing.

  • The Prairie Trail: A concrete walking and biking trail, winding through scenic areas of Aberdeen, connecting parks and shopping centers.

  • The Aberdeen Recreation and Cultural Center: Featuring an indoor track and a full fitness center, with a variety of activities and fitness classes.

Best Things to Do in Aberdeen: Activities and Tours

In addition to its scenic beauty and rich history, Aberdeen offers numerous activities and tours for visitors.

  • The Aberdeen Downtown Association Historic Trolley Tour: Experience the history and beauty of Aberdeen in a trolley tour of the city.

  • The Sand Lake Refuge Visitor Center: Featuring a variety of exhibits showcasing the local wildlife and natural habitats.

  • The Aberdeen Mall: A shopping and entertainment destination, featuring a variety of stores, dining options, and a movie theater.

  • The Storybook Land Express: A magical train ride through Storybook Land, showcasing a variety of fairy tale displays, perfect for kids and adults.

Week-Long Itinerary for Aberdeen

Day 1: Visit the Dacotah Prairie Museum, followed by lunch at Max & Erma’s. Camp overnight at the Wylie Park Campground.

Day 2: Explore the Brown County Courthouse Museum, followed by a stroll through historic downtown. Attend an Aberdeen Wingnuts baseball game in the evening.

Day 3: Hike or bike along the South Dakota Discovery Trail, followed by lunch at Fisherman’s Cafe & Steakhouse. Spend the evening at the Storybook Land Castle.

Day 4: Visit the Sand Lake Refuge Visitor Center, followed by lunch at the Aberdeen Mall food court. Relax in the evening at Mina Lake Recreation Area.

Day 5: Take the Aberdeen Downtown Association Historic Trolley Tour and explore the local shops and restaurants in downtown. End the day with dinner at Red Rooster Coffee House.

Day 6: Visit Richmond Lake Recreation Area and spend the day fishing, boating or hiking.

Day 7: Spend the day at the Aberdeen Aquatic Center or playing a round of miniature golf, followed by dinner at Mavericks Steak & Cocktails.

Food and Drink Scene of Aberdeen

Must-Try Dishes

Aberdeen boasts a diverse culinary scene, with everything from classic American fare to international cuisine.

  • The Bullpen Sports Bar & Grill: Featuring classic pub grub, as well as steaks and seafood.

  • Mavericks Steak & Cocktails: Offering a fine-dining experience with premium steaks, chops and a variety of cocktails.

  • The Beef O’Brady’s Family Sports Pub: A family-friendly eatery, offering wings, burgers, sandwiches, and a variety of beer and other drinks.

  • Roma’s Ristorante & Lounge: Serving delicious Italian cuisine, featuring fresh ingredients and homemade sauces.

Local Beverages

  • The Aberdeen Brewing Company: Featuring a selection of craft beers, including seasonal and limited-edition flavors.

  • Lager’s Inn: A popular local watering hole, featuring a variety of beer and wine options.

Where to Stay in Aberdeen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Aberdeen has a variety of accommodation options to suit any budget.

  • My Place Hotel-Aberdeen: A comfortable and affordable option, with spacious rooms and all necessary amenities.

  • AmericInn Lodge & Suites Aberdeen: Featuring a swimming pool and hot tub, as well as a complimentary breakfast.

  • Quality Inn: Located near the airport, with pet-friendly rooms and free breakfast.

What to Buy in Aberdeen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Aberdeen can find a variety of unique souvenirs and local products to take home as a souvenir.

  • Prairie Winds Gifts: Offering a selection of South Dakota-themed souvenirs, including clothing, jewelry, and accessories.

  • Aberdeen Meat & Grocery: Featuring local meats, cheeses, and other gourmet food items.

Local Culture and Customs of Aberdeen

Aberdeen has a rich cultural identity, with a number of unique customs and traditions.

  • The Aberdeen Scottish Games: An annual event held in June, featuring Highland games and traditional Scottish music and food.

  • The Storybook Land Festival: A family-friendly event held annually in June, featuring live entertainment, games, and food vendors.

Best Time to Visit Aberdeen

The best time to visit Aberdeen is in the summer, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and many outdoor events and festivals are taking place.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aberdeen

Do:

  • Visit local museums and historical sites to learn about the city’s rich cultural heritage.

  • Explore local parks and hiking trails to experience the natural beauty of Aberdeen.

  • Try the local cuisine, including South Dakota’s famous beef and traditional Scottish dishes.

  • Attend local festivals and events to experience the city’s vibrant cultural scene.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to bring warm clothing, as the weather in Aberdeen can be unpredictable, even during the summer.

  • Don’t litter or pollute the natural environment, and respect local wildlife.

  • Don’t over-pack for your trip, as many amenities, such as towels and toiletries, are provided at hotels and other accommodations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aberdeen

Aberdeen is a safe and friendly city, with low crime rates and a welcoming community.

Visitors should take appropriate precautions when exploring natural areas, such as bringing appropriate equipment and clothing for hiking or biking.

Health in Aberdeen

Aberdeen has excellent medical facilities, including Avera St. Luke’s Hospital and Sanford Aberdeen Medical Center.

Visitors should stay hydrated and protected from the sun during outdoor activities, and should consult their doctor before traveling if they have any health concerns.

Getting to Aberdeen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Aberdeen

The Aberdeen Regional Airport is located approximately five miles east of downtown Aberdeen, and offers daily flights to and from Minneapolis and Denver.

Bus Stations Near Aberdeen

Jefferson Lines operates bus service to Aberdeen, with stops at the Aberdeen transportation center.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive into Aberdeen, as the city is conveniently located near several major highways.

How to Get Around in Aberdeen

Aberdeen has a number of transportation options for visitors, including taxis, local buses, and rental cars.

The city also has several bike paths and walking trails, making it easy to explore on foot or by bike.

Where to Go Next from Aberdeen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have additional time in South Dakota, consider taking a short trip to one of these nearby destinations:

  • The Badlands National Park: A stunning natural wonderland featuring unique rock formations and scenic vistas.

  • Mount Rushmore National Memorial: A world-famous monument featuring the faces of four American presidents.

  • The Black Hills: A beautiful mountain range, offering hiking, camping, and other outdoor activities.

  • Custer State Park: A popular natural attraction, featuring scenic drives, hiking trails, and wildlife watching.

Conclusion

If you’re looking for a unique and authentic American travel experience, Aberdeen has it all - from stunning natural beauty and rich cultural history to diverse cuisine and exciting activities and attractions.

With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re now ready to plan your trip to Aberdeen and make lasting memories in this beautiful and welcoming city.
