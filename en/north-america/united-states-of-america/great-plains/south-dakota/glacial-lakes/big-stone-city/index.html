Introduction to Big Stone City

Located in the heart of Glacial Lakes in South Dakota, Big Stone City is a hidden gem in the Great Plains of the United States of America. This small town has a rich history dating back to the late 19th century and offers a peaceful retreat for visitors seeking adventure and natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Big Stone City

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Big Stone County Historical Society – This museum showcases the history of the region through exhibits and artifacts.

  2. Ortonville Municipal Airport – This airport served as a training facility for World War II pilots and now features a museum with aircraft on display.

Natural Attractions of Big Stone City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Big Stone Lake – This lake spans over 26 miles and offers opportunities for boating, fishing, and swimming.

  2. Big Stone National Wildlife Refuge – This protected area is home to diverse wildlife and offers hiking trails and scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Big Stone City

  1. Big Stone Lake State Park Trail – This 1-mile trail leads to the top of the Stone Lookout Tower, offering panoramic views of the surrounding area.

  2. Old Railroad Trail – This 5-mile trail follows the former path of a railroad and offers scenic views of marshes and forests.

Best Things to Do in Big Stone City: Activities and Tours

  1. Big Stone County Fair – This annual event features live music, carnival rides, and agricultural exhibits.

  2. Fishing and Hunting – Big Stone City offers some of the best fishing and hunting opportunities in the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Big Stone City

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Jell-O Salad – This traditional Midwestern dish is made with gelatin, fruit, and sometimes vegetables.

  2. Hot Dish – This casserole-style dish typically contains meat, vegetables, and starches such as potatoes or pasta.

Local Beverages

  1. South Dakota Craft Beer – The state boasts a growing craft beer scene, with breweries such as Dakota Brickhouse and Wooden Legs Brewing Company.

  2. Wine – The region’s climate is ideal for growing grapes, and local wineries such as Schade Vineyard and Twisted Vine offer tastings and tours.

Where to Stay in Big Stone City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Eagle View Lodge – This family-owned lodge features comfortable rooms and stunning views of Big Stone Lake.

  2. Ortonville Municipal Airport RV Park – This RV park offers affordable camping with easy access to the airport museum and nearby attractions.

What to Buy in Big Stone City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Prairie Grass Candle – This hand-poured candle features a unique blend of grass and wildflower scents.

  2. Stone Birthstones – These jewelry pieces are made from locally sourced stones and are believed to bring good luck and health.

Local Culture and Customs of Big Stone City

Residents of Big Stone City value hard work, family, and the natural beauty of the region. Visitors should respect these values and take time to appreciate the area’s unique history and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Big Stone City

The best time to visit Big Stone City is between May and September, when the weather is mild and outdoor activities are at their peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Big Stone City

Do:

  1. Take time to explore downtown Big Stone City.

  2. Respect the natural beauty of the region by practicing Leave No Trace principles while hiking and camping.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or damage the natural environment.

  2. Be loud or disruptive, especially in residential areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Big Stone City

Big Stone City is a relatively safe destination, but visitors should take standard precautions such as locking car doors and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Big Stone City

Medical facilities are limited in Big Stone City, so visitors should bring necessary medications and seek medical attention in nearby communities if needed.

Getting to Big Stone City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Big Stone City

  1. Watertown Regional Airport (ART) – 67 miles from Big Stone City.

  2. Aberdeen Regional Airport (ABR) – 126 miles from Big Stone City.

Bus Stations Near Big Stone City

  1. Jefferson Lines – This bus company operates routes throughout the region, with a stop in nearby Ortonville.

  2. Greyhound – This bus company operates routes throughout the United States, with a stop in nearby Milbank.

Other Options

  1. Car rental – Many major car rental companies operate in the region, with pick-up and drop-off options in nearby communities.

  2. Private transportation – Taxis and rideshare services such as Uber and Lyft are not available in the area, but private transportation options are available.

How to Get Around in Big Stone City

While a car is the easiest way to get around Big Stone City and the surrounding area, there are also options for biking and walking.

Where to Go Next from Big Stone City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Sisseton, SD – This small town offers opportunities for hiking, fishing, and exploring local history.

  2. Fargo, ND – This city is a 3-hour drive from Big Stone City and offers a thriving art and culture scene, as well as unique dining and shopping options.

Conclusion

Big Stone City offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and small-town charm. Whether you’re seeking fishing and hunting opportunities, scenic hiking trails, or a peaceful retreat, you’re sure to find something to love in this hidden gem of the Great Plains.
