Introduction to Sioux Falls

Sioux Falls is a vibrant and picturesque city located in Southeast South Dakota, at the confluence of the Big Sioux River and the Dakota Dunes. It is the largest city in South Dakota, and a popular destination for travelers due to its rich history, scenic beauty, and cultural attractions.

Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just a relaxing getaway, Sioux Falls has something to offer. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to eat and stay, and how to get around Sioux Falls.

List of Places to Visit in Sioux Falls

Sioux Falls is home to a variety of attractions and landmarks, which cater to every taste and preference. Here’s a list of some of the top places to visit in Sioux Falls:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Pettigrew Home and Museum: This historic home-turned-museum was once the residence of South Dakota’s first senator, Richard Pettigrew, and offers an insight into the life and times of an influential politician and businessman.

  • SculptureWalk Sioux Falls: This outdoor art exhibit, featuring over 50 sculptures by local and international artists, is a must-visit for art lovers and culture enthusiasts.

  • Washington Pavilion: This multi-purpose center houses a performance venue, art exhibits, science center, and a cinema, making it a great choice for families with children.

  • Old Courthouse Museum: This 19th-century courthouse has been converted into a history museum that tells the story of the city’s past through exhibits and artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Sioux Falls: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Falls Park: This 123-acre park is one of the most popular attractions in Sioux Falls, offering breathtaking views of the Big Sioux River and its stunning waterfalls. Visitors can hike, bike, or simply relax and enjoy the scenery.

  • Great Bear Recreation Park: This ski and tubing park is a great family getaway during the winter months, when visitors can enjoy skiing, snowboarding, and other winter sports.

  • Sioux Falls Bike Trail: This 20-mile trail is a great way to explore the city on two wheels, taking visitors on a scenic journey past the city’s parks and river.

  • Sertoma Butterfly House & Marine Cove: This unique attraction features indoor and outdoor butterfly exhibits, as well as a coral reef aquarium and other marine life exhibits.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sioux Falls

If you’re looking for hiking or biking trails in Sioux Falls, you’re in luck. The city has a number of scenic routes that offer great outdoor adventures for visitors of all ages and experience levels.

Sioux Falls Bike Trail

The Sioux Falls Bike Trail is a popular 20-mile trail that takes visitors on a scenic journey through the heart of the city. The trail connects several parks, including Falls Park, Fawick Park, and Spencer Park, and offers a chance to explore the city by bike or on foot.

Big Sioux River Greenway

The Big Sioux River Greenway is a 26-mile trail that runs along the banks of the Big Sioux River, offering stunning views of the river and its surroundings. Visitors can hike, bike, or enjoy other outdoor activities along the trail.

Best Things to Do in Sioux Falls: Activities and Tours

Sioux Falls is a city that offers a wide range of activities and tours, no matter what your interests may be. Here are some of the best things to do in Sioux Falls:

  • Wild Water West: This water park offers a variety of water rides and attractions, making it a great choice for families with children.

  • Skyzone Trampoline Park: This trampoline park offers a fun and exhilarating experience for visitors of all ages, with wall-to-wall trampolines and other attractions.

  • Sioux Falls Indoor Aquatic Center: This indoor aquatic center features several water slides, splash pads, and other water attractions, making it a great destination for aquatic fun.

  • Great Plains Zoo and Delbridge Museum of Natural History: This popular zoo features over 1,000 animals from around the world, as well as an adjacent museum that showcases natural history exhibits.

Food and Drink Scene of Sioux Falls

Sioux Falls is home to a diverse and exciting food and drink scene, with something to offer every palate. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Sioux Falls:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chislic: This popular South Dakota dish features cubed meat, usually lamb or beef, that is seasoned and grilled on a skewer. It is typically served with garlic bread and a side of fries.

  • Kuchen: This German dessert is a pastry crust topped with a sweet custard filling and baked to perfection. It is often served with fresh fruit or whipped cream.

Local Beverages

  • Custer State Park Buffalo Bourdon: This bourbon, made from South Dakota corn, is aged in oak barrels and has a rich, smooth flavor.

  • Cedar Shore Red: This South Dakota-made wine is a blend of locally-grown grapes, with a fruity and full-bodied taste.

Where to Stay in Sioux Falls: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sioux Falls has a wide range of accommodation options for visitors to choose from, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Here are some of the top choices:

  • Hilton Garden Inn Sioux Falls Downtown: This modern and stylish hotel is located in the heart of downtown Sioux Falls, making it a great choice for visitors who want to be close to the city’s attractions.

  • Sheraton Sioux Falls & Convention Center: This upscale hotel offers luxurious rooms and suites, as well as conference facilities and a fitness center.

  • Quality Inn & Suites: This budget-friendly hotel offers comfortable rooms and convenient amenities, such as free breakfast and Wi-Fi.

What to Buy in Sioux Falls: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home from Sioux Falls, here are some great options:

  • Sioux Falls T-Shirts and Sweatshirts: These make great souvenirs of your trip, and you can find them at local gift shops and stores.

  • Local Art and Crafts: Sioux Falls is home to many talented artists and craftspeople, and you can find unique and handmade items at local galleries and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Sioux Falls

Sioux Falls has a rich and diverse culture that reflects its history and traditions. Visitors can enjoy local festivals, events, and cultural experiences throughout the year, such as:

  • Festival of Cultures: This annual event celebrates Sioux Falls’ diverse cultural heritage, with food, music, and dance performances representing different cultures from around the world.

Best Time to Visit Sioux Falls

The best time to visit Sioux Falls depends on your preferences and interests. The city experiences four distinct seasons, with hot summers and cold winters, so it’s important to plan accordingly.

If you’re looking for outdoor activities and scenic views, the summer months (June-August) are the best time to visit, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, if you’re interested in cultural events and festivals, spring and fall may be the best times to visit, when the weather is mild and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sioux Falls

To make the most of your visit to Sioux Falls, here are some dos and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Do explore Falls Park and its surroundings, as its one of the must-see attractions in Sioux Falls.

  • Do try some local cuisine, like chislic and kuchen.

  • Do visit the Great Plains Zoo and Delbridge Museum for a unique wildlife experience.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or disturb the natural environment.

  • Don’t go hiking or biking without proper safety gear, especially during extreme weather conditions.

  • Don’t forget to tip your servers after dining out.

Safety and Health

As a tourist destination, Sioux Falls is generally safe and welcoming to visitors. However, it’s important to take basic safety precautions and be mindful of your surroundings.

Safety in Sioux Falls

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially when walking in unfamiliar areas.

  • Keep valuables out of sight and lock your car doors when parked.

  • Use caution when driving, especially in winter when roads can be icy.

Health in Sioux Falls

Sioux Falls has good healthcare facilities, but it’s still important to take basic health precautions when traveling, such as:

  • Wash your hands frequently to prevent the spread of germs.

  • Apply sunscreen and stay hydrated during the summer months.

  • Seek medical attention if you experience any health issues during your trip.

Getting to Sioux Falls: Transportation Options and Logistics

Sioux Falls is easily accessible by air, bus, or car, making it a convenient destination for visitors.

Airports Near Sioux Falls

  • Sioux Falls Regional Airport: This airport is located just five miles from downtown Sioux Falls, and serves several major airlines.

  • Joe Foss Field Airport: This smaller airport is located in Sioux Falls and serves general aviation.

Bus Stations Near Sioux Falls

  • Jefferson Lines: This regional bus service offers connections to several cities in the Midwest and Rocky Mountain regions.

  • Greyhound: This national bus service connects Sioux Falls to destinations throughout the United States.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: Several car rental agencies operate in Sioux Falls, making it easy to rent a car and explore the city at your own pace.

  • Taxis and Ride-Sharing Services: Several taxi companies and ride-sharing services, such as Uber and Lyft, operate in Sioux Falls.

How to Get Around in Sioux Falls

Sioux Falls is a relatively small and walkable city, especially in the downtown area. However, if you’re looking to explore further afield, there are several transportation options available:

  • Buses: The Sioux Falls Metro offers a bus service that covers the city and surrounding areas.

  • Bikes: Several bike rental companies operate in Sioux Falls, and the city has a number of bike trails and bike-friendly roads.

  • Taxis and Ride-Sharing Services: Several taxi companies and ride-sharing services, such as Uber and Lyft, operate in Sioux Falls.

Where to Go Next from Sioux Falls: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sioux Falls is located in the heart of the Midwest, making it a great starting point for exploring the region. Here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions that are worth considering:

  • Badlands National Park: This stunning national park is located just a few hours’ drive from Sioux Falls, and features otherworldly rock formations and other natural wonders.

  • Mount Rushmore: This iconic national monument, featuring the faces of four former U.S. presidents carved into a mountainside, is located in the Black Hills region of South Dakota.

Conclusion

Sioux Falls is a vibrant and welcoming city that offers a wide range of attractions and activities for visitors of all ages and interests. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just a relaxing getaway, Sioux Falls has something to offer. With this tour guide, you can plan your trip and make the most of your stay in this beautiful city.
