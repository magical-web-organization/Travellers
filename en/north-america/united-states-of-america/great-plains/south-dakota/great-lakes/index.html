Introduction to Great Lakes

Located in the Great Plains region of the United States of America, in South Dakota, the Great Lakes is a unique destination that boasts remarkable natural features, rich culture, history, and various outdoor adventures. The Great Lakes region houses a chain of glacial lakes, including Big Stone Lake, Lake Kampeska, Lake Poinsett, and Lake Thompson, surrounded by vast prairies and woodlands, and historic towns offer countless possibilities for exploration and relaxation.

In this travel guide, we have compiled a comprehensive list of all the best places to visit, things to do, sights to see, and hidden gems to discover, to make your trip to Great Lakes unforgettable.

List of Places to Visit in Great Lakes

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Granary Rural Cultural Center: Located in Aberdeen, this museum provides a glimpse into the history and culture of the Great Plains region through art, exhibits, and hands-on activities.
  • South Dakota Agricultural Heritage Museum: Explore the state’s agricultural past and present via interactive exhibits, hands-on activities, and special events in the heart of Brookings.
  • South Dakota State Capitol: Visit the South Dakota State Capitol building in Pierre, where you can learn more about the state’s rich history, government, and get a stunning view of the surrounding area.
  • Laura Ingalls Wilder Museum: Located in De Smet, this museum celebrates the life and legacy of the famous author of “Little House on the Prairie” and offers visitors a chance to experience pioneer life in the late 19th century.

Natural Attractions of Great Lakes: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Coteau des Prairies Lodge: This stunning lodge, located near Sisseton, offers guests a chance to stay in luxury cabin accommodations, with access to miles of hiking and biking trails, hunting, fishing, and outdoor activities available on-site.
  • Lake Poinsett State Park: Located near Arlington, this park is home to a large recreational lake, picnic areas, and secluded campgrounds.
  • Glacial Lakes State Park: This park is known for its crystal clear waters and offers visitors an opportunity to enjoy the outdoors while taking a swim, boating, or enjoying a lakeside picnic.
  • Oahe Dam: This scenic spot, located in Pierre, is one of the largest dams in the U.S. and offers breathtaking views of the Missouri River and the surrounding area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Great Lakes

  • The Mickelson Trail: This 109-mile trail offers access to some of the most scenic views in the Great Lakes region, including pine forests, rocky cliffs, sparkling streams, and prairies.
  • The Fort Sisseton Historic Trail: This 7.5-mile trail follows the beautiful Lake Cochrane shoreline and explores the historic sites of Fort Sisseton.
  • The Deep Valley Trail: This 2-mile trail, located in Ft. Pierre National Grassland, takes visitors through rugged limestone cliffs and sprawling prairies and is perfect for day hikes.

Best Things to Do in Great Lakes: Activities and Tours

  • Take a Boat Tour on Lake Oahe: Join a guided boat tour for a unique look at the stunning landscapes, rock formations, and beaches surrounding Lake Oahe.
  • Visit the South Dakota Discovery Center: Located in Pierre, this interactive science learning center provides hands-on exhibits and educational programs for children and adults.
  • Take a Hot Air Balloon Ride: For a bird’s eye view of Great Lakes, embark on a hot air balloon ride to experience the breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Take a Fishing trip: With several lakes and rivers in the region, Great Lakes offers some of the best fishing experiences in the country. Book a guided fishing trip to catch some trophy fish or enjoy a relaxing day by the water.

Week-Long Itinerary for Great Lakes

If you have a week to spend in Great Lakes, here’s a perfect itinerary to make the most of your time:

Day 1: Arrive in Aberdeen and visit the Granary Rural Cultural Center. Day 2: Head to Lake Thompson State Park for a full day of outdoor activities and wildlife viewing. Day 3: Visit the South Dakota State Capitol in Pierre and explore the adjacent Oahe Dam. Day 4: Spend the day at the Coteau des Prairies Lodge, indulging in hiking and biking trails. Day 5: Take a bat tour on the beautiful Lake Poinsett and enjoy the prairies along the way. Day 6: Travel to De Smet and admire the historic Laura Ingalls Wilder Museum. Day 7: Head back to Aberdeen and stop at the South Dakota Agricultural Heritage Museum.

Food and Drink Scene of Great Lakes

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chislic: This popular South Dakota dish is made of bitesize chunks of lamb or beef, skewered and grilled or fried.
  • Knoephla Soup: Traditionally made by German immigrants, this soup is a hearty combination of chicken, potatoes, and dumplings.
  • Lefse: This is a traditional Norwegian flatbread made of potatoes, flour, milk, and cream.

Local Beverages

  • Pheasant Ridge: This award-winning winery in Brookings offers wine tastings and tours with panoramic views of the surrounding area.
  • Wooden Legs Brewery: This popular brewery in Brookings offers creative, craft beers and specialty pizzas.

Where to Stay in Great Lakes: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Coteau des Prairies Lodge: This lodge offers luxury cabin accommodations with access to miles of hiking and biking trails, hunting, fishing, and outdoor activities on-site.
  • Candlewood Suites: Located in Aberdeen, this hotel offers clean, spacious accommodations and is conveniently located near the Granary Rural Cultural Center and other attractions.
  • Super 8: Offering clean, budget-friendly accommodations in Pierre, this hotel is a great choice for travelers on a budget.

What to Buy in Great Lakes: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Dakota provisions: Beef jerky, summer sausage, and other smoked meats are a local favorite and make a great souvenir or gift.
  • Pheasant Feathers: South Dakota is famous for its pheasant hunting, and pheasant feathers make for a unique and beautiful souvenir to bring home.
  • Decorative Pottery: Pottery crafted by local artisans is a great way to take home a piece of South Dakota’s rich cultural heritage.

Local Culture and Customs of Great Lakes

South Dakota is a state with strong cultural roots, with a rich history influenced by Native American tribes. People are friendly and hospitable.

Best Time to Visit Great Lakes

The best time to visit Great Lakes is from May to October when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities are in full swing. In the summer months, it can get hot, so make sure to pack light clothing and plenty of sunscreen.

Do’s and Don’ts of Great Lakes

Do:

  • Embrace the outdoor lifestyle and enjoy the fresh air and natural beauty of the Great Lakes region.
  • Plan your trip early to get the best deals on hotel bookings, rental cars, and airfare.
  • Respect the rules, customs and laws of the land.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave your trash outside.
  • Ignore the warnings or signs along the trails or at park entrances.
  • Trespass on private property without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Great Lakes

Great Lakes is a safe place to travel to; however, be sure to take necessary precautions to ensure your safety, such as locking your vehicle doors at night or in unattended areas, and being aware of your surroundings when walking alone, especially at night.

Health in Great Lakes

Be aware of the health risks associated with outdoor activities, including sun exposure and insect bites. It’s essential to pack sunscreen, bug spray, and any necessary medication for your trip.

Getting to Great Lakes: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Great Lakes

The nearest airport is Aberdeen Regional Airport, followed by Watertown Regional Airport.

Bus Stations Near Great Lakes

Jefferson Lines, the major bus line in the area, serves the Aberdeen, Pierre and Watertown areas.

Other Options

Car rentals are available at all airports serviced and train options from other cities are available via Amtrak.

How to Get Around in Great Lakes

The best way to get around Great Lakes is by car. Car rentals are available at all major airports in the area.

Where to Go Next from Great Lakes: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • The Badlands National Park: Located just three hours from Great Lakes, the Badlands are home to stunning landscapes and unique rock formations.
  • Mount Rushmore and Crazy Horse Memorial: Both located within driving distance from Great Lakes, these monuments are iconic symbols of American history and culture.
  • Black Hills National Forest: This forest is famous for its scenic drives, hiking trails, and wildlife.

Conclusion

Great Lakes, South Dakota, is a must-visit destination for outdoor enthusiasts and history buffs alike. With its breathtaking natural scenery, rich cultural history, and endless outdoor adventures, Great Lakes offers an experience that is unique and unforgettable. Use this guide to plan your next adventure in Great Lakes and discover all the hidden gems this region has to offer.
