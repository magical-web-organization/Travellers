Introduction to Selby

Welcome to Selby, a quaint town located in the Great Plains of South Dakota. Selby offers visitors a unique blend of cultural and natural attractions, making it a must-visit destination in the United States of America.

List of Places to Visit in Selby

Selby has something for every type of traveler; here’s an overview of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Selby Area Historical Society Museum: This museum showcases Selby’s rich history with exhibits on everything from Native American artifacts to pioneer history and agriculture. Join a guided tour to learn even more about the town.
  • Old Southside School Museum: Another historical attraction, the Old Southside School Museum is dedicated to preserving the history of education in South Dakota. Here, you can find artifacts from the early 1900s and experience what school was like back then.
  • Youth Center and Water Tower: The Youth Center and Water Tower is a landmark of Selby and is the only remaining water tower of its kind in the state. Be sure to snap a photo of this iconic structure during your visit.

Natural Attractions of Selby: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Hiddenwood Recreation Area: A popular spot for outdoor recreation, Lake Hiddenwood offers fishing, boating, hiking, and wildlife viewing opportunities. Be sure to pack a picnic and spend the day taking in the natural beauty of the lake.
  • Hiddenwood State Wildlife Preserve: This protected area is home to an array of prairie wildlife, such as buffalo, elk, and pronghorn. Hike or bike the trails, and take in the scenic views of the prairies and rolling hills.
  • Bridges of South Dakota Scenic Byway: Take a scenic drive on the Bridges of South Dakota Scenic Byway, which passes through Selby. This drive offers stunning views of the countryside and takes you over several historic bridges.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Selby

Hiking and biking enthusiasts are spoiled for choice with the following options in Selby:

  • Lake Hiddenwood Recreation Area Hiking Trail: This scenic trail winds around the perimeter of Lake Hiddenwood and is an easy hike suitable for all skill levels.
  • Hiddenwood State Wildlife Preserve Hiking and Biking Trail: Choose from several trails ranging from easy to difficult in the Hiddenwood State Wildlife Preserve. Biking is also allowed on some of the trails.

Best Things to Do in Selby: Activities and Tours

In addition to the attractions mentioned above, check out these unique activities and tours in Selby:

  • Go Buffalo Tour: Join a guided tour and learn about the history of buffalo in South Dakota. You will get up close and personal with these majestic creatures on this unforgettable tour.
  • Selby Sizzlers Car Club Show and Shine: Car enthusiasts won’t want to miss the annual Selby Sizzlers Car Club Show and Shine. Held every summer, this event showcases some of the best classic and muscle cars in the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Selby

To make the most of your visit to Selby, follow this week-long itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Selby Area Historical Society Museum and Old Southside School Museum
2 Spend the day fishing and picnicking at Lake Hiddenwood Recreation Area
3 Take the Bridges of South Dakota Scenic Byway and stop at the Youth Center and Water Tower
4 Explore the Hiddenwood State Wildlife Preserve
5 Join a guided Go Buffalo Tour
6 Attend the annual Selby Sizzlers Car Club Show and Shine
7 Shop for souvenirs and relax before heading out of Selby

Food and Drink Scene of Selby

Sample the local cuisine and drinks in Selby:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kuchen: A German dessert that’s popular in South Dakota, Kuchen is a sweet pastry featuring fruit and custard.
  • Chislic: A South Dakota specialty, Chislic is a dish consisting of fried or grilled meat, typically served with garlic or other seasonings.

Local Beverages

  • Schwans beer: Brewed in South Dakota, Schwans beer is a favorite among locals and is best enjoyed with a view of the prairies.

Where to Stay in Selby: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

While Selby is a small town, there are a few options for accommodations:

  • Selby Motel: Offering basic but comfortable rooms, the Selby Motel is the only motel in town.
  • Hiddenwood Guest Ranch: This guest ranch offers a unique experience, with overnight accommodations in a historic bunkhouse and horseback riding and other outdoor activities.

What to Buy in Selby: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t leave Selby without bringing home a few of these souvenirs and locally-made products:

  • Bison wool products: Shop for hats, gloves, and other products made from bison wool at local shops in Selby.
  • Handmade pottery: Selby has several artisans who create beautiful handcrafted pottery that makes for a unique and meaningful souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Selby

Selby’s culture is heavily shaped by its pioneer history and agricultural roots. Visitors can learn about this heritage through the town’s museums and historical attractions.

Best Time to Visit Selby

The best time to visit Selby is during the summer months, between June and August, when the weather is warm and the outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Selby

Here are a few things to keep in mind during your visit to Selby:

Do:

  • Explore the natural attractions and outdoor activities in Selby.
  • Learn about the town’s history at the museums and historical attractions.

Don’t:

  • Trespass on private property or disturb the wildlife in the state wildlife preserve.
  • Ignore safety warnings while participating in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

As with any travel destination, it’s important to prioritize your safety and health while visiting Selby.

Safety in Selby

Selby is a safe town, but visitors should still be aware of their surroundings, especially when participating in outdoor activities.

Health in Selby

Be sure to pack any medications and personal hygiene items you might need during your stay in Selby. The town has a small medical clinic, but for any serious medical issues, you should plan to seek treatment in a larger nearby city.

Getting to Selby: Transportation Options and Logistics

Selby is located in a remote part of South Dakota, so it can be challenging to get there. However, there are a few options:

Airports Near Selby

  • Aberdeen Regional Airport: Located about 80 miles from Selby, Aberdeen Regional Airport is the closest airport to Selby.

Bus Stations Near Selby

  • Greyhound Bus Lines: Greyhound offers service to Aberdeen, located about 80 miles from Selby.

Other Options

  • Rental Car: Renting a car is the best option for exploring Selby and the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Selby

Because Selby is a small town, it’s easy to get around on foot. Visitors can also rent a car to explore the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Selby: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Selby’s remote location means that other South Dakota destinations are a bit of a drive away. However, here are a few nearby destinations worth considering:

  • Aberdeen: Located about 80 miles from Selby, Aberdeen is a larger town with more accommodations and restaurants. Check out the Dacotah Prairie Museum and Wylie Park while you’re there.
  • Pierre: The capital of South Dakota, Pierre is about 160 miles from Selby. Visit the South Dakota State Capitol and explore the Oahe Dam and Lake.

Conclusion

Selby’s unique blend of culture and nature make it a must-visit town in South Dakota. We hope this comprehensive guide helps you plan your visit to Selby and enjoy all the town has to offer.
