Introduction to Pierre

Nestled in the heart of the Great Plains, Pierre is the capital city of South Dakota, located in the Midwestern region of the United States of America. This charming city is surrounded by natural beauty, rich history, and cultural heritage that make it an ideal destination for travelers seeking an unforgettable experience.

List of Places to Visit in Pierre

Pierre offers plenty of attractions and activities for every type of traveler. Here’s a list of must-visit places on your visit to Pierre:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Pierre is steeped in history, and you can learn a lot about the city’s past by visiting its museums and historical places. Some of the famous cultural attractions of Pierre are:

  • South Dakota Cultural Heritage Center: This museum features exhibits on the history of South Dakota from prehistoric times to the present day.
  • South Dakota State Capitol: This stunning building is a symbol of the state’s political history and governance. It also houses the offices of the Governor and the Legislature.
  • Verendrye Museum: This museum showcases the exhibits related to the Lewis and Clark Expedition, the fur trade, the history of the Dakota, Lakota, and Nakota people, and more.

Natural Attractions of Pierre: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pierre is home to some of the most beautiful natural attractions in the Great Plains. Here are some outdoor adventures and scenic views to check out in Pierre:

  • Oahe Dam: Located on the Missouri River, the Oahe Dam is one of the largest earthen dams in the world.
  • Farm Island Recreation Area: Spread over 2,200 acres, this park offers hiking and biking trails, fishing, boating, and camping facilities.
  • La Framboise Island Nature Area: This serene island is a great place to take a stroll and enjoy the natural beauty surrounding the Missouri River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pierre

Pierre is a great destination for hiking and mountain biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes to explore in Pierre:

  • Capitol Lake: The Capitol Lake Loop Trail is a picturesque walking trail that takes you around the State Capitol building and offers stunning views of the lake.
  • Steamboat Park: This park has a network of trails that cover over 4 miles and offer excellent opportunities for mountain biking and hiking.
  • Mickelson Trail: This 109-mile long trail stretches from Edgemont to Deadwood and features stunning scenery, diverse wildlife, and unique historic sites along the route.

Best Things to Do in Pierre: Activities and Tours

Pierre offers a wide range of activities and tours for tourists. Here are some fun things to do in Pierre:

  • Pierre Trappers Baseball: If you’re a sports fan, make sure to attend a Pierre Trappers baseball game at Hyde Stadium.
  • Missouri River Kayak and Canoe Tours: Enjoy a kayak or canoe tour of the Missouri River with local outfitters that provide everything from equipment to transportation.
  • Prairie Pages Booksellers: This bookshop offers a cozy, welcoming atmosphere and a wide selection of books to browse and purchase.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pierre

A perfect week-long itinerary for Pierre would include:

Day 1: Visit the South Dakota Cultural Heritage Center and the State Capitol building. Day 2: Spend the day on the water at Farm Island Recreation Area. Day 3: Hike or bike the Steamboat Park trails. Day 4: Take a kayak tour on the Missouri River. Day 5: Explore the City of Pierre and dine at some of the local restaurants. Day 6: Head to La Framboise Island Nature Area for a tranquil day in nature. Day 7: Shop at Prairie Pages Booksellers and enjoy a Pierre Trappers baseball game at Hyde Stadium.

Food and Drink Scene of Pierre

Pierre is famous for its unique blend of American and European cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages while in Pierre:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Knoephla Soup: This hearty soup is made of dumplings, potatoes, and chicken or ham broth.
  • Chislic: These bite-sized pieces of mutton or beef are a popular local appetizer.
  • Kuchen: This traditional dessert is a cake made with sweet dough and filled with fruit, custard, or jam.

Local Beverages

  • Prairie Berry Winery: This winery features South Dakota’s only tasting room, where you can sample a selection of award-winning wines.
  • Dakota Territory Brewing Company: This microbrewery serves a variety of handcrafted beers, including some of its own unique recipes.
  • Crow Peak Brewing Company: Located in nearby Spearfish, this brewery is well-known for its IPA’s and stouts.

Where to Stay in Pierre: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pierre offers a variety of options for accommodation. Here are some of the best places to stay in Pierre:

  • Clubhouse Hotel & Suites: This hotel offers a variety of amenities, including an indoor pool, fitness center, and on-site dining.
  • Days Inn by Wyndham: This hotel provides affordable and comfortable rooms and is situated in an excellent location near local attractions.
  • The Roam Inn: This cozy bed and breakfast offers 6 unique guest rooms, each with its own theme and decor.

What to Buy in Pierre: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some popular souvenirs and local products to take home from Pierre are:

  • Native American Crafts: From handmade beadwork to unique pottery, Pierre is a great place to buy Authentic Native American crafts.
  • Harrisburg Cheese Store: This store sells a variety of locally produced cheeses, such as Colby, gouda, and cheddar.
  • Prairie Pages Booksellers: This bookshop offers a wide selection of books on South Dakota and the surrounding region.

Local Culture and Customs of Pierre

Pierre is a welcoming small town with a relaxed and friendly atmosphere. Here are some local customs to keep in mind:

  • Respect for Nature: Pierre’s residents have a deep appreciation for nature, so make sure to take care of the environment.
  • Support Local Businesses: Pierre has a vibrant small business community, so try to support local business whenever possible.

Best Time to Visit Pierre

The best time to visit Pierre is between May and September when the weather is warm and ideal for outdoor activities. June is an especially busy month in Pierre, with many festivals and events taking place during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pierre

Do:

  • Do attend a sporting event or concert at the local venues.
  • Do take a tour on the Missouri River.
  • Do explore the local museums and historical sites.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t forget to try the local food and drinks.
  • Don’t forget to pick up some souvenirs from the local shops.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pierre

Pierre is a relatively safe city, but it’s always important to take precautions while traveling. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid walking alone at night in unlit areas.
  • Keep your valuables, such as passports and money, secure.
  • Always lock your vehicle and use a car alarm if available.

Health in Pierre

Pierre has good medical facilities like Avera St. Mary’s Hospital, which provides 24-hour emergency care. However, it’s essential to have travel insurance in case of unexpected medical emergencies.

Getting to Pierre: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pierre

The nearest airport to Pierre is the Pierre Regional Airport. This airport offers direct flights to Denver and Minneapolis.

Bus Stations Near Pierre

Jefferson Lines offers daily bus service from Pierre to various destinations in the region, including Sioux Falls and Rapid City.

Other Options

Driving to Pierre is also an option, and Interstate 90 and US Highways 14, 83, and 212 offer easy access. Once in Pierre, renting a car is a convenient option for getting around the city and surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Pierre

Pierre is a small town and relatively easy to get around. Visitors can easily use their own vehicles, rental cars, or bikes to explore the city and surrounding areas. Taxi and ride-sharing services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Pierre: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Pierre is a great starting point for exploring South Dakota and the surrounding region. Here are some nearby destinations that you can add to your travel itinerary:

  • Badlands National Park: Located in southwestern South Dakota, the Badlands National Park is home to a variety of unique land formations and diverse wildlife.
  • Mount Rushmore National Monument: Just a few hours’ drive from Pierre, you can visit the iconic Mount Rushmore National Monument, the famous sculpture of American Presidents George Washington, Thomas Jefferson, Theodore Roosevelt, and Abraham Lincoln.
  • Custer State Park: This is South Dakota’s first and largest state park, offering a wide range of outdoor activities such as hiking, fishing, wildlife viewing, and scenic drives.

Conclusion

Pierre is a charming town with a rich history, cultural heritage, and a welcoming atmosphere. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, or local culinary experiences, Pierre has something for everyone. With this comprehensive guide, you can plan your visit to Pierre and explore the best that this charming city has to offer.
