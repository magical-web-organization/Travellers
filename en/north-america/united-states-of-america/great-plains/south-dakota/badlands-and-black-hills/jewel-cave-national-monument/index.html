Introduction to Jewel Cave National Monument

Jewel Cave National Monument is a stunning natural attraction that features a maze of more than 200 miles of mapped and explored underground passageways. This National Monument is named for its jewel-like calcite crystals found in the cave that sparkle in the darkness. It is a unique opportunity to explore the hidden treasures below the surface, with a variety of tours and activities offered throughout the year. The cave is open to the public year-round, with guided tours available from Memorial Day weekend to Labor Day.

List of Places to Visit in Jewel Cave National Monument

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The monument comprises over 1,200 acres of the rugged Black Hills terrain, where you can find historical artifacts and remnants of ancient cultures. Some notable cultural attractions include:

  • Visitor Center: The Jewel Cave Visitor Center features exhibits detailing the history, geology, and biology of the area.
  • Historic Buildings: The Thoen Stone building was constructed by Scandinavian immigrants in the 1890s, and you can still see it today in the Visitor Center.
  • Forest and Hills: Jewel Cave National Monument is located within the Black Hills National Forest.

Natural Attractions of Jewel Cave National Monument: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Jewel Cave National Monument is a fantastic place to enjoy outdoor activities and spectacular views. With more than 1,200 acres of rugged terrain, the Monument has a plethora of outdoor adventure options, such as:

  • Hiking trails: From a short and easy nature trail to a challenging backcountry trek, various hiking trails offer visitors a chance to explore the natural beauty of the Monument.
  • Wildlife Watching: You can spot various wildlife species at the monument, including deer, elk, turkey, and mountain lions.
  • Overlooks and Scenic Drives: You can enjoy a picturesque view of the hills on the Scenic Drive Loop, which is located in the southern portion of the Monument.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jewel Cave National Monument

Jewel Cave National Monument is a treasure trove of adventure for hikers and bikers. There are miles of trails, including:

  • The Canyon Trail: A 1.5-mile trail leads to a high overlook of Hell Canyon.
  • The Roof Trail: A ¾-mile trail that takes visitors to the highest point of Jewel Cave National Monument.
  • Centennial Trail: An 111-mile trail that runs through the Black Hills.

Best Things to Do in Jewel Cave National Monument: Activities and Tours

Jewel Cave National Monument offers a variety of tours and activities that cater to visitors of all ages and interests. Some of the best things to do are:

  • Cave Tours: Jewel Cave National Monument offers several cave tour options, ranging in duration, distance, and difficulty level.
  • Wildlife Observation: Visitors can spot a variety of wildlife species at the monument, including deer, elk, turkey, and mountain lions.
  • Ranger-Led Programs: The “Explore with a Ranger” program offers park visitors the opportunity to learn more about the area’s history, geology, and biology.

Food and Drink Scene of Jewel Cave National Monument

Must-Try Dishes

The cuisine of South Dakota is a rich and hearty blend of regional and international flavors. If you are a foodie, you must try these dishes:

  • Chislic: a local favorite, chislic, is a dish that is considered a true delicacy. It is simply seasoned fried cubes of beef or lamb.
  • Kuchen: a cake-like dessert with a sweet and buttery crust and a custard-like filling.

Local Beverages

South Dakota has a thriving craft beer scene, and the region is home to many breweries that make delicious beer. The most popular drinks in the region include the following:

  • Crow Peak Pile O Dirt Porter: a rich and hearty beer made with chocolate and coffee.
  • Firehouse Brewing Co. Raspberry Wheat: a fruity and light beer made with raspberries.
