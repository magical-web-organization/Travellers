Swett, located in Badlands and Black Hills, in South Dakota, in Great Plains, in the United States of America, is a hidden gem waiting to be explored by outdoor and history lovers. This small town might not have major tourist attractions, but it boasts stunning natural landscapes, a rich history, and a welcoming community that makes visitors feel at home. Pack your bags, and let’s go on a journey to discover the best of Swett.

Introduction to Swett

Swett is a small town with a population of around 2 people, but despite its size, it has a lot to offer. The town was founded in 1932 and was initially a hub for railroad operations. Today, it is a peaceful community that still has remnants of its rich history.

List of Places to Visit in Swett

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bunk House Museum: This museum showcases the unique history of Swett and the surrounding areas. It has a collection of artifacts and photographs that document the town’s history, including its railroad operations, agriculture, and early homesteaders.

  • Wall Drug Store: Just a few miles from Swett, Wall Drug Store is a famous roadside attraction that has been a stop for road-trippers since 1931. It has 76,000 square feet of shopping, dining, and entertainment.

  • Prairie Homestead: This homestead is a preserved example of an early 1900s sod house. It showcases the challenges early settlers faced and how they made a home in the prairies.

  • Black Hills Wild Horse Sanctuary: A non-profit organization that is home to over 600 wild mustangs that roam free in 11,000 acres of vast greenery. Visitors get to tour the sanctuary in a vehicle where you’ll see these beautiful creatures in their natural habitat.

Natural Attractions of Swett: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Badlands National Park: Just 56 miles from Swett is this natural wonderland featuring beautiful layered rock formations, stark canyons, and miles and miles of hiking trails. Take a drive and marvel at the stunning geological structures and watch wildlife like bison and bighorn sheep roaming in their natural habitat.

  • Black Hills National Forest: This national forest is known for its towering ponderosa pine trees, breathtaking scenic drives, and crystal-clear lakes. Enjoy outdoor adventures activities like hiking, camping, fishing, and hunting.

  • Bear Butte State Park: This majestic butte is a sacred site to Native Americans. Visitors can hike and see stunning views of the Black Hills and the prairies. Visitors can also experience animal sightings like pronghorns, bighorn sheep, and other wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Swett

  • Castle Trail: This 10-mile trail is Badlands National Park’s longest trail. It has stunning views of the park’s geological features and wildlife sightings. The trail is also accessible to hiking, running and biking.

  • Mickelson Trail: This 109-mile trail runs through the Black Hills from Deadwood to Edgemont. It offers distinctive views of the hills, forests, and wildlife sightings.

  • Black Elk Peak Trail: Located in Black Hills National Forest, it’s South Dakota’s highest point. The trail is eight miles round trip and features beautiful views of pine forests, granite peaks, and lovely meadows.

Best Things to Do in Swett: Activities and Tours

  • Cycling Tours: Experience the beauty of the surrounding areas with the pedals. There are various cycling tours that take visitors through scenic routes like Badlands National Park and Black Hills National Forest.

  • ATV Off-Road Adventures: Off-road in the impressive landscape of the Black Hills National Forest. With beautiful views and exciting drops, this thrilling experience will be unforgettable.

  • Horseback Riding: Horseback riding offers the opportunity to immerse yourself in the history of the west. Travel through authentic western terrain and see the beauty of Swett like never before.

Food and Drink Scene of Swett

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chislic: Cubes of seasoned lamb or beef served on toothpicks are a popular snack across South Dakota. A must-try dish while traveling through this area.

  • Kuchen: A traditional German dessert made up of sweet bread and fruit. It’s delicious warm with ice cream or fresh cream.

  • Corn on the cob: South Dakotan’s really know how to prepare corn on the cob and it’s served with butter, salt and pepper as a staple.

Local Beverages

  • South Dakota wine: A great selection of Nebraska’s best wines can be found at the winery in Martin, South Dakota. Enjoy a glass or bottle on a picnic bench overlooking the massive landscape.

  • Craft Beer: A mixture of local brew and well-known regional and national brewing companies can be found in the downtown district surrounding Swett.

  • Fresh Brewed Coffee: The coffee scene is widely popular, and you will find a variety of coffee houses around South Dakota serving up specialty brews.

Where to Stay in Swett: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Maverick Motel: This family-owned motel offers comfortable and budget-friendly accommodations. Amenities include a free continental breakfast, swimming pool, and 24-hour reception.

  • Badlands Budget Host: This hostel offers private rooms and dormitories for backpackers and budget-conscious travelers. It also has a shared kitchen, lounge area, and a barbecue facility.

  • Prairie View Bed and Breakfast: Provides a farm stay experience surrounded by stunning hills and historic homesteads. Enjoy an included breakfast and a unique venture into rural history of the state.

What to Buy in Swett: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Homemade Jams/Jellies: Discover the local taste and purchase homemade Jams/Jellies, which are easily available in nearby stores.

  • Antiques: Tourists with an eye for antique items and furniture will have a vast collection of choices and rare finds in Swett.

  • Souvenirs: Get yourself a souvenir or gifts for family and friends with memorable gifts available in various souvenir shops around town.

Local Culture and Customs of Swett

South Dakota has a deep rich history in western culture, and locals will share their personal tales. Expect an open and friendly culture from all people during your visit. Respectful behavior when visiting places is important, and equal respect for the native population and their traditions should be taken.

Best Time to Visit Swett

The best time to visit Swett is from May to September, where the weather is mild, and you can enjoy the outdoor activities that Swett has to offer. Summer is also when most of the festivals and events take place, so there is a lively atmosphere throughout the town.

Do’s and Don’ts of Swett

Do:

  • Be respectful to the locals and the native population of South Dakota.
  • Keep the city clean and don’t litter.
  • Try some local foods like chislic and kuchen.

Don’t:

  • Do not touch the exhibits or artifacts in the museums and historical places.
  • Do not take photographs of the wildlife- it distracts and frightens them.
  • Do not smoke in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Swett

Swett is a safe and peaceful community, but always be aware of your surroundings. Practice caution while sight-seeing on the hiking trails and preserve nature by respecting animals in their natural habitats.

Health in Swett

Swett has a limited medical facility, and it’s best to carry personal first-aid. Wear comfortable shoes and clothes when hiking. Stay hydrated and bring sunscreen.

Getting to Swett: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Swett

The nearest airport to Swett is Rapid City Regional Airport, around a 45-minute drive away. Alternatively, if you are arriving from a nearby state, you could use Sioux Falls Regional Airport, Fargo’s Hector International, or Billings Logan International.

Bus Stations Near Swett

A variety of public transportation options are available such as Rapid Transit Bus that provides transport to nearby areas.

Other Options

Driving is the most convenient option for getting around Swett, as it offers easy access to nearby attractions and scenic routes.

How to Get Around in Swett

Renting a car would be a wise choice to move around and explore the small farming town, but alternatively, bikes could be rented or cycling tours organized.

Where to Go Next from Swett: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Deadwood: 70 miles from Swett, it was once famous for wild west and gold rush, and now the town offers casinos, museums and scenic outdoor trails.

  • Mt. Rushmore: A significant national monument located in the Black Hills, around 50 miles from Swett. The monument is a must-visit for history lovers, American culture enthusiasts, and those interested in art.

  • Custer State Park: 70 miles from Swett is a state park that offers stunning scenery and wildlife. Visitors can enjoy hiking trails, scenic drives, and wildlife sightings.
