Introduction to Sturgis

Sturgis, located in the Badlands and Black Hills region of South Dakota, is a hidden gem in the Great Plains. Known worldwide for hosting one of the largest motorcycle rallies in the world, Sturgis is an underrated destination for tourists who appreciate scenic beauty, outdoor activities, and local culture. With a population of around 7,000 people, this charming town is a perfect escape from the hustle-bustle of city life. In this travel guide, we will explore everything that Sturgis has to offer for tourists.

List of Places to Visit in Sturgis

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sturgis Motorcycle Museum and Hall of Fame is a must-visit attraction for motorcycle enthusiasts. The museum showcases the evolution of motorcycling through interactive exhibits and displays. Admission fee for adults is $15.
  • Fort Meade Cavalry Museum is located in the nearby Fort Meade military base. The museum displays the history of the US cavalry through various artifacts and memorabilia. Admission to the museum is free, but visitors need to obtain a pass from the Fort Meade gate.

Natural Attractions of Sturgis: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bear Butte State Park is a sacred mountain for the Lakota people and a popular hiking destination for tourists. The 3.5-mile trail takes visitors to the summit with breathtaking views of the surrounding hills and plains.
  • Spearfish Canyon Scenic Byway is a 22-mile drive through the Black Hills’ high cliffs, waterfalls, and colorful forest. The drive also passes the historic mining town of Lead and the majestically carved Mount Rushmore National Memorial.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sturgis

Hiking and biking are some of the most popular activities for tourists in Sturgis. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Centennial Trail is a 111-mile trail that crosses the Black Hills National Forest and Custer State Park. The trail offers stunning views of wildlife, mountains, and lakes.
  • Bear Butte State Park trail, mentioned earlier, is another popular hiking route for visitors.
  • Michelson Trail is a 109-mile rail trail that passes through the heart of the Black Hills.

Best Things to Do in Sturgis: Activities and Tours

  • Sturgis Motorcycle Rally is the town’s biggest annual event, attracting thousands of bikers from all over the world. The rally usually takes place in the first week of August and includes live music, entertainment, and motorcycle races.
  • Guided ATV and Jeep Tours are a great way to explore the off-road terrain of the Black Hills. Several tour companies offer various packages for tourists.
  • Horseback Riding is another popular activity in Sturgis. Visitors can ride through the scenic Black Hills trails with experienced guides.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sturgis

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for visitors to explore Sturgis and its surrounding areas:

Food and Drink Scene of Sturgis

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bison Burger is a popular dish in Sturgis, made from locally raised bison meat. Several restaurants, including Loud American Roadhouse and The Knuckle Saloon, serve bison burgers.
  • Chislic is another local delicacy, made with cubed beef or lamb that is deep-fried and seasoned with garlic salt.

Local Beverages

  • Prairie Berry Winery is a family-owned winery in Hill City, producing award-winning wines from local fruits and berries.
  • Crow Peak Brewing Company and Sturgis Brewing Company are two local craft breweries that serve a variety of beers on tap.

Where to Stay in Sturgis: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sturgis has several accommodation options for visitors, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels:

  • Hotel Sturgis is a historic hotel that dates back to 1908, located in the center of downtown Sturgis.
  • Days Inn by Wyndham Sturgis is a budget-friendly hotel located on the outskirts of the town.
  • Iron Horse Inn Bed & Breakfast is a cozy guesthouse that offers a unique experience to visitors.

What to Buy in Sturgis: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sturgis Motorcycle Rally Merchandise is a popular souvenir among visitors. The rally features various vendors selling t-shirts, leather jackets, and other memorabilia.
  • Black Hills Gold Jewelry is a unique type of jewelry that originated in the Black Hills region and is made from green and pink gold.
  • Prairie Dust is a popular seasoning blend made from dried herbs and spices and is available in several stores in Sturgis.

Local Culture and Customs of Sturgis

Sturgis has a rich cultural history, and visitors should respect the town’s customs and traditions. Here are some of the things to keep in mind while visiting Sturgis:

  • The town has a strong motorcycle culture, and visitors should be aware of the noise from motorcycles during the rally.
  • The Lakota people consider Bear Butte State Park as a sacred place, and visitors should respect the park’s cultural and religious significance.
  • The official language spoken in Sturgis is English, but visitors might find people speaking in French or Spanish.

Best Time to Visit Sturgis

The best time to visit Sturgis depends on the visitors’ preferences and interests. Here are some of the factors to consider:

  • The Sturgis Motorcycle Rally, usually held in August, is the town’s biggest annual event and attracts thousands of visitors.
  • Summer (June-August) is the peak tourist season, with pleasant weather and several outdoor activities.
  • Fall (September-November) is a quieter season, with fewer crowds and scenic fall foliage.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

Safety and Health

  • Sturgis is a relatively safe town for tourists, but visitors should take basic precautions to avoid theft and other crimes.

Health in Sturgis

  • Visitors should carry necessary medication and take precautions (including sunscreen and insect repellent) while exploring the natural attractions of the town.

Where to Go Next from Sturgis: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
