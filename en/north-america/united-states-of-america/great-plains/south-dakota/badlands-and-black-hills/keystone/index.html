Introduction to Keystone

Keystone, a small town in the Black Hills of South Dakota, captures the essence of Western frontier towns with its charming buildings and scenic views. The town is located just minutes from Mount Rushmore National Memorial and offers a variety of activities for visitors.

List of Places to Visit in Keystone

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Keystone has a rich history, and visitors can learn about it by visiting some of the town’s cultural attractions. The following museums and historical places are must-visit spots.

  • Keystone Historical Museum: The Keystone Historical Museum documents the history of Keystone and the surrounding Black Hills. The museum features exhibits on the town’s mining history, Native American culture, and famous visitors to the area.
  • Mount Rushmore National Memorial: Just a few minutes’ drive from Keystone, the Mount Rushmore National Memorial features the iconic faces of four American presidents carved into a mountainside. Visitors can take a ranger-led tour, watch a film about the memorial’s creation, or attend a special event at the memorial’s amphitheater.

Natural Attractions of Keystone: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Black Hills and Badlands region surrounding Keystone is known for its scenic views and outdoor adventures. Visitors to Keystone can explore the following natural attractions and activities.

  • Black Hills National Forest: The Black Hills National Forest covers over 1.2 million acres of land and has dozens of trails for hiking and biking. One popular trail near Keystone is the Iron Mountain Road, which is known for its tunnels that perfectly frame views of Mount Rushmore.
  • Custer State Park: Custer State Park is one of the largest state parks in the US and offers visitors opportunities for hiking, camping, and wildlife viewing. Visitors can see bison, elk, pronghorns, and bighorn sheep in their natural habitats.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Keystone

Visitors to Keystone can enjoy the natural beauty of the Black Hills by walking or biking the area’s scenic trails. The following are some of the top hiking and biking routes in Keystone.

  • George S. Mickelson Trail: The George S. Mickelson Trail is a 109-mile trail that runs through the heart of the Black Hills. The trail is open to hikers, bikers, and horseback riders and offers stunning vistas of the surrounding mountains.
  • Mount Rushmore Walkway: The Mount Rushmore Walkway is a paved trail that takes visitors on a leisurely walk through the park’s attractions. Visitors can see Mount Rushmore, the presidential trail, and the Avenue of Flags.

Best Things to Do in Keystone: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Keystone has a variety of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. The following are some of the best things to do in Keystone.

  • Rushmore Tramway Adventures: Rushmore Tramway Adventures offers visitors a chance to see Keystone and the Black Hills from the air. Visitors can take a chairlift up the mountain and then zipline or ride a roller coaster down.
  • Ride to Paradise: Ride to Paradise is a tour company that offers visitors a chance to take a scenic drive through the Black Hills and see attractions like Mount Rushmore, Crazy Horse Memorial, and Custer State Park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Keystone

Keystone and the surrounding Black Hills area offer enough activities and attractions to fill a week-long vacation. The following is a sample itinerary to help visitors plan their week in Keystone.

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Keystone Historical Museum and explore the town’s shops and restaurants.
Day 2 See Mount Rushmore in the morning and then hike the Black Elk Peak Trail in the afternoon.
Day 3 Spend the day at Custer State Park. Take a scenic drive through Wildlife Loop Road and end the day with a hike to the top of Harney Peak.
Day 4 Visit Crazy Horse Memorial and then take a horseback ride through the Black Hills.
Day 5 Tour the Badlands National Park and end the day with a visit to Wall Drug.
Day 6 Take the Rushmore Tramway Adventures and zipline down the mountain. Spend the afternoon shopping in Keystone.
Day 7 Take a Ride to Paradise and see the sights of the Black Hills. End the day with a concert at the amphitheater at Mount Rushmore.

Food and Drink Scene of Keystone

Must-Try Dishes

Keystone has a variety of restaurants and cafes that serve up classic Western fare. The following dishes are must-try items for visitors to Keystone.

  • Bison Burger: Many restaurants in Keystone offer bison burgers, which are made from the lean meat of bison and served with classic toppings like cheese, bacon, and lettuce.
  • Fry Bread: Fry bread is a Native American staple and is made by frying dough until it is light and crispy. Fry bread is often served with berries, honey, or savory toppings like beef and cheese.

Local Beverages

South Dakota is known for its craft beer scene, and Keystone has a few breweries that visitors should check out. The following are some of the best breweries and beers in Keystone.

  • Crow Peak Brewing Company: Crow Peak Brewing Company is located in Spearfish, South Dakota, and serves up a variety of craft beers. Visitors should try the Pile O’Dirt Porter, which has won several awards at national beer competitions.
  • Hay Camp Brewing Company: Hay Camp Brewing Company is located in Rapid City, South Dakota, and offers a variety of craft beers. Visitors should try the Stovepipe Porter, which is brewed with locally roasted coffee.

Where to Stay in Keystone: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Keystone has a variety of options for accommodations, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. The following are some of the best places to stay in Keystone.

  • K Bar S Lodge: K Bar S Lodge is a rustic lodge located just minutes from Mount Rushmore. The lodge offers comfortable rooms and suites and has a variety of amenities, including an indoor pool and hot tub.
  • Roosevelt Inn: Roosevelt Inn is a cozy bed and breakfast located in the heart of Keystone. The inn offers comfortable rooms and suites and a complimentary breakfast each morning.

What to Buy in Keystone: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Keystone can take home a variety of souvenirs and local products to remember their trip. The following are some of the best souvenirs to buy in Keystone.

  • Black Hills Gold Jewelry: Black Hills Gold Jewelry is a traditional style of jewelry that originated in South Dakota in the late 1800s. Visitors can find Black Hills Gold Jewelry in many shops in Keystone.
  • Buffalo Leather Products: Buffalo leather products are made from the hides of bison and are known for their durability and unique texture. Visitors can find bags, wallets, and other leather products made from buffalo leather in Keystone.

Local Culture and Customs of Keystone

South Dakota has a rich Native American history, and visitors to Keystone can learn about the area’s culture and customs by attending powwows and cultural events. The following are some of the cultural events that visitors to Keystone should check out.

  • Black Hills Powwow: The Black Hills Powwow is held in October and celebrates the Native American culture of the area. The powwow features tribal dancing and music and arts and crafts vendors selling traditional Native American items.
  • Hot Harley Nights: Hot Harley Nights is a motorcycle rally that takes place in Rapid City in July. The event features a parade of motorcycles and a variety of entertainment options, including live music and food vendors.

Best Time to Visit Keystone

The best time to visit Keystone is from late spring to early fall, when the weather is mild and the area’s outdoor attractions are open. The following are some of the best times to visit Keystone.

  • July: July is a busy time in Keystone, with many events and activities taking place. Visitors can attend the Hot Harley Nights rally and see fireworks displays at Mount Rushmore on the Fourth of July.
  • September: September is a quiet time in Keystone, but the weather is still mild, and visitors can enjoy the area’s attractions without the crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Keystone

Do:

  • Do take time to explore the natural beauty and outdoor activities of the Black Hills.
  • Do try local dishes like bison burgers and fry bread.
  • Do attend cultural events and powwows to learn about the area’s history.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the area’s natural attractions.
  • Don’t feed or approach wildlife in the area.
  • Don’t ignore safety guidelines for activities like hiking and biking.

Safety and Health

Safety in Keystone

Keystone and the surrounding Black Hills region are generally safe for visitors. The following are some safety tips for visitors to Keystone.

  • Use caution while hiking on steep trails; wear appropriate footwear and stay on marked trails.
  • Respect wildlife and keep a safe distance from animals.
  • Be mindful of weather conditions, especially during lightning storms.

Health in Keystone

Keystone does not have any major health concerns, but visitors should take precautions to avoid illness or injury. The following are some health tips for visitors to Keystone.

  • Use bug spray to avoid tick bites, which can carry Lyme disease.
  • Drink plenty of water to avoid dehydration, especially during outdoor activities.
  • Wear sunscreen to avoid sunburn.

Getting to Keystone: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Keystone

The nearest major airport to Keystone is Rapid City Regional Airport, which is located about 23 miles from the town. The airport has several daily flights from major US cities.

Bus Stations Near Keystone

Greyhound offers bus service to Rapid City, which is located about 23 miles from Keystone. Visitors can rent a car or take a bus or shuttle service to get to Keystone from Rapid City.

Other Options

Visitors to Keystone can also drive to the town from other parts of the US or take a train into nearby cities like Rapid City or Sioux Falls.

How to Get Around in Keystone

The best way to get around Keystone is by car or by bike. Many of the town’s attractions are located within walking distance of one another, and visitors can park their cars and explore on foot.

Where to Go Next from Keystone: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Keystone can find plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to round out their trip. The following are some of the best places to visit after Keystone.

  • Deadwood: Deadwood is a historic gold-mining town located about 50 miles from Keystone. Visitors can explore the town’s casinos, museums, and historic buildings.
  • Badlands National Park: Badlands National Park is located about 90 miles from Keystone and offers stunning views of the area’s unique geology and wildlife.

Conclusion

Keystone is a charming town with plenty of attractions and activities for visitors to enjoy. Whether visitors are interested in learning about the history of the area, exploring the natural beauty of the Black Hills, or simply relaxing and enjoying the local cuisine, Keystone offers something for everyone.
