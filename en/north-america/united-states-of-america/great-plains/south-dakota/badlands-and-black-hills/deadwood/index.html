Introduction to Deadwood

Deadwood, located in the Badlands and Black Hills area of South Dakota, is rich in history and culture. Known for its gold rush history and connection to Wild Bill Hickok, Deadwood is now a popular tourist destination. With natural beauty and plenty of things to see and do, Deadwood is a must-visit for any traveler exploring the Great Plains.

List of Places to Visit in Deadwood

If you’re planning a trip to Deadwood, be sure to check out these top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Adams Museum & House: This museum showcases Deadwood’s history, including Native American artifacts, the gold rush, and early pioneer life.
  2. Mount Moriah Cemetery: The resting place of Wild Bill Hickok and Calamity Jane, this cemetery is a must-see for Western history buffs.
  3. Deadwood History & Information Center: This center offers exhibits and tours of the town’s historic buildings and highlights the many famous figures who passed through Deadwood during its early days.
  4. Days of ‘76 Museum: This museum celebrates the Days of ‘76 rodeo, which has been held in Deadwood since 1924.

Natural Attractions of Deadwood: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Spearfish Canyon: A short drive from Deadwood, this canyon is known for its stunning waterfalls, hiking trails, and beautiful foliage.
  2. Mount Rushmore: A national icon, Mount Rushmore is a must-visit attraction when in the area.
  3. Custer State Park: Located just south of Deadwood, this park offers beautiful landscapes, wildlife viewing opportunities, and hiking trails.
  4. Mickelson Trail: A former railroad route turned hiking/biking trail, the Mickelson Trail offers scenic views of the Black Hills.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Deadwood

For those outdoors enthusiasts, Deadwood offers some of the best hiking and biking opportunities in the area. Here are some of the top routes:

  1. Old Baldy Trail: This 6.1-mile trail offers scenic views of the Black Hills and the Deadwood skyline.
  2. Mickelson Trail: With 109 miles of trail to explore, the Mickelson Trail offers a range of difficulty levels and scenic vistas.
  3. Spearfish Canyon: The area offers numerous hiking trails ranging from easy to difficult, and most offer stunning views of the canyon and its waterfalls.
  4. George S. Mickelson Trail: Named after the former South Dakota governor, this trail offers over 100 miles of hiking/biking with picturesque views along the way.

Best Things to Do in Deadwood: Activities and Tours

  1. Saloon No. 10: This historic saloon was the location of Wild Bill Hickok’s infamous death. Today, visitors can watch a reenactment of the shooting and enjoy a drink at the bar.
  2. Kevin Costner’s Original Deadwood Tour: Get a behind-the-scenes look at the making of “Dances with Wolves” and other Costner movies filmed in the area.
  3. Dakota Discovery Museum: A unique museum showcasing Native American culture and artifacts, including traditional art and crafts.
  4. Gold Mine Tour: Take a tour of one of Deadwood’s many mines and learn about the town’s gold rush history.

Week-Long Itinerary for Deadwood

Day 1: Arrival and check-in at one of Deadwood’s lodging options. Day 2: Visit Mount Rushmore and Custer State Park. Day 3: Explore downtown Deadwood, including the Adams Museum and Mount Moriah Cemetery. Day 4: Take a hike on the Old Baldy Trail. Day 5: Enjoy a bike ride on the Mickelson Trail. Day 6: Take a mine tour or visit the Dakota Discovery Museum. Day 7: Departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Deadwood

Deadwood offers a range of dining options to satisfy any palate. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Buffalo burgers: These burgers feature lean bison meat and are a staple of the South Dakota food scene.
  2. Chislic: A popular South Dakota dish, chislic consists of small pieces of deep-fried meat served with crackers.
  3. Kolache: A pastry filled with fruit or cheese and topped with powdered sugar, kolaches are a popular breakfast food in Deadwood.
  4. Kuchen: A German dessert, kuchen is a sweet cake with a crumbly topping.

Local Beverages

  1. Prairie Berry Winery: A local winery, Prairie Berry offers a range of fruit wines made from locally grown ingredients.
  2. Miner Brewing Company: This brewery offers a range of craft beers with unique flavors and ingredients.
  3. Crow Peak Brewing: A popular brewery, Crow Peak offers a range of beers from light to dark and everything in between.
  4. Deadwood Distillery: This distillery offers a range of handcrafted spirits, including vodka, gin, and bourbon.

Where to Stay in Deadwood: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From historic hotels to modern lodgings, Deadwood offers a range of accommodations to fit any budget. Some popular options include:

  1. The Franklin Hotel: A historic property with modern amenities, the Franklin offers comfortable rooms and is located in the heart of downtown Deadwood.
  2. SpringHill Suites: A modern hotel, the SpringHill Suites offers spacious rooms, free breakfast, and a pool and fitness center.
  3. Deadwood Mountain Grand: This hotel and casino features luxurious rooms and suites with beautiful views of the Black Hills.
  4. Cedar Wood Inn: A budget-friendly option, the Cedar Wood Inn offers comfortable rooms and is located just minutes from downtown Deadwood.

What to Buy in Deadwood: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Deadwood offers a range of souvenirs and local products to take home as a reminder of your trip. Some popular items include:

  1. Black Hills Gold Jewelry: This unique jewelry is made with a mix of gold and other metals and features intricate designs.
  2. South Dakota Memorabilia: T-shirts, hats, and other items with the South Dakota flag or state name are popular souvenirs.
  3. Western Wear: Boots, hats, and other items of cowboy clothing can be found at many of Deadwood’s shops.
  4. Hutterite Quilts: Made by a local religious community, these handcrafted quilts are a unique gift and a nod to South Dakota’s pioneer history.

Local Culture and Customs of Deadwood

Deadwood has a strong Western culture and is proud of its gold rush history. Visitors should expect to see locals dressed in cowboy boots and hats, and many attractions are centered around Western history. Additionally, visitors should be respectful of Native American culture and traditions, as this is an important part of the area’s history and present-day culture.

Best Time to Visit Deadwood

The best time to visit Deadwood depends on personal preference. Summers are the most popular time to visit, with warm weather and a range of outdoor activities available. Fall is also a beautiful time to visit, with the changing foliage making for stunning scenery. Winter can be cold and snowy, but the town is decorated for the holidays, offering a cozy atmosphere.

Do’s and Don’ts of Deadwood

Do:

  1. Do try the local cuisine, including buffalo burgers and chislic.
  2. Do take a tour of one of Deadwood’s many mines to learn about the town’s gold rush history.
  3. Do visit Mount Rushmore and Custer State Park for stunning views and outdoor activities.
  4. Do be respectful of Native American culture and traditions.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t forget to dress in layers, as the weather can be unpredictable.
  2. Don’t leave Deadwood without trying a local beverage, such as a fruit wine or craft beer.
  3. Don’t miss out on the town’s many cultural attractions, including the Adams Museum and Deadwood History & Information Center.
  4. Don’t litter or damage the natural beauty of the area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Deadwood

Deadwood is generally safe for visitors, but travelers should exercise common sense and take normal safety precautions, such as locking car doors and not leaving valuables in plain sight. Visitors should also be cautious when traversing hiking trails and other outdoor areas.

Health in Deadwood

Visitors to Deadwood should be aware of the high altitude and the potential for altitude sickness, especially if coming from lower elevations. It is important to stay hydrated and avoid strenuous activity until acclimated to the elevation. Additionally, visitors should be aware of ticks and take proper precautions when hiking or spending time outdoors.

Getting to Deadwood: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Deadwood

  1. Rapid City Regional Airport (RAP): Located in Rapid City, about a 45-minute drive from Deadwood, RAP offers daily flights to major cities.
  2. Black Hills Airport/Clyde Ice Field (SPF): Located in Spearfish, about a 20-minute drive from Deadwood, SPF offers daily flights to Denver.

Bus Stations Near Deadwood

  1. Spearfish Greyhound Bus Station: Located in Spearfish, about a 20-minute drive from Deadwood, the Spearfish Greyhound Bus Station serves as the closest bus station to Deadwood.

Other Options

Many visitors choose to rent a car when visiting Deadwood, as this offers the most flexibility for exploring the area. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available in the area.

How to Get Around in Deadwood

Walking is the most popular and convenient way to get around Deadwood, as the town is relatively small and many attractions are within walking distance. However, visitors may also choose to rent a car or take advantage of the public transportation options available.

Where to Go Next from Deadwood: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Black Hills National Forest: A beautiful forest with numerous hiking trails and scenic vistas, located just south of Deadwood.
  2. Badlands National Park: Known for its unique rock formations and stunning scenery, Badlands National Park is located about a two-hour drive from Deadwood.
  3. Devils Tower National Monument: A unique natural wonder located in northeastern Wyoming, Devils Tower is about a three-hour drive from Deadwood.

Conclusion

Deadwood offers a blend of history, culture, and natural beauty, making it a must-visit destination for any traveler exploring the Great Plains. Whether you’re interested in Western history, outdoor adventures, or just a relaxing getaway in a beautiful area, Deadwood has something to offer. With a range of attractions, accommodations, and dining options, Deadwood is sure to please any traveler’s preferences.
