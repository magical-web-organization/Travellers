Introduction to Flores

Flores Island, the westernmost point of Europe, is part of the Azores archipelago and one of the nine islands that make up this unique region. Flores Island is known for its lush vegetation, volcanic landscapes, and pristine natural environment. This island boasts countless natural wonders, including hidden valleys, waterfalls, geysers, hot springs, and rocky coastlines. This travel guide will provide you with all the information you need to plan your dream vacation to Flores Island.

List of Places to Visit in Flores

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Flores Island has a rich cultural heritage and history. The following are among the must-see cultural attractions:

  • Museum of Flores: This museum is located in the town of Santa Cruz and showcases the history and culture of Flores Island.
  • Sao Boaventura Cultural Center: A cultural center dedicated to promoting local Flores culture.
  • Caveiro Church: This historic church, located in Lajes, is a great example of Flores’ unique architecture.

Natural Attractions of Flores: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Flores Island is famous for its untouched wilderness and natural beauty. The following are the top natural attractions of Flores:

  • Fajazinha Lakes: These stunning lakes are one of the most beautiful natural wonders in Flores. They are situated on a plateau and are surrounded by dense vegetation.
  • Rocha dos Bordoes: Rocha dos Bordoes is made up of a group of vertical basalt columns which stand up to twenty meters tall. This beautiful natural formation is a popular tourist attraction.
  • Morro Alto: Standing at an altitude of 914 meters, Morro Alto is the highest peak on Flores Island.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Flores

Flores Island is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. The following are the best hikes and bike trails on the island:

  • Caldeira Funda: This is a moderate hike that takes approximately two hours to complete. The trail takes you through beautiful forests and ends at the Caldeira Funda crater lake.
  • Ponta Delgada to Lomba Loop Trail: This is a difficult trail that takes around five hours to complete. You will be rewarded with stunning views of the island and the Atlantic Ocean.
  • Lajedo to Fazenda: This is an easy trail that is suitable for all levels of hikers. The trail takes you along the coast and finishes at Fajazinha.

Best Things to Do in Flores: Activities and Tours

Flores Island offers a wide range of activities and tours. Here are the top things to do:

  • Whale watching: Flores Island is known for its whale watching tours. You can book a tour to see the different species of whales that pass through the island’s waters.
  • Water sports: Flores Island offers a wide range of water sports, including kayaking and snorkeling.
  • Horseback riding: You can go horseback riding and explore the island’s wild interior and coastline.

Week-Long Itinerary for Flores

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long stay in Flores Island:

Food and Drink Scene of Flores

Must-Try Dishes

Flores Island is known for its fresh seafood and delicious traditional dishes. Here are a few must-try dishes:

  • Lapas: This dish consists of grilled limpets and is served with garlic butter and lemon.
  • Alcatra: A traditional meat dish made with beef, potatoes, and spices.
  • Queijadas: A local pastry that is filled with custard and cinnamon.

Local Beverages

Flores Island has its own unique drinks, including:

  • Vinho de cheiro: A local wine that is made from grapes grown in the Azores.
  • Cerveja Especial: A special beer that is brewed on the island.

Where to Stay in Flores: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Flores Island has a range of accommodation options, including:

  • Hotel Ocidental: This hotel is situated in Santa Cruz and offers stunning views of the surrounding ocean.
  • Pocinho Bay Lodge: This lodge is located on the western coast of Flores and offers comfortable accommodation with beautiful views of the sea.
  • Aldeia da Cuada: A unique and charming village that has been converted into guesthouses and rental properties.

What to Buy in Flores: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The following are the best souvenirs and local products to buy in Flores:

  • Handmade products: Flores is famous for its handmade pottery and textiles.
  • Cheeses: The Azores is known for its cheese industry, and Flores produces high-quality cheese.
  • Wine: The Azores is also known for its wine, and a bottle of Vinho de cheiro makes a great souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Flores

Flores Island has a unique and rich culture, and it is important to respect its customs and traditions. One of the island’s most important customs is the Espirito Santo festivals, which are celebrated in May or June. You may also come across traditional dances, music, and crafts produced by the locals.

Best Time to Visit Flores

The best time to visit Flores Island is between May and September when the weather is warm and dry. The high season runs from July to August when many tourists visit the island. The shoulder season (May-June, September) can be a great time to visit as temperatures are still pleasant and there are fewer tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of Flores

Do:

  • Respect the island’s natural environment and wildlife.
  • Learn some basic Portuguese phrases before arriving on the island.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter in the local environment.
  • Visit protected places (e.g., nature reserves) without an authorized guide.
  • Ignore the instructions of local authorities during crises or emergency situations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Flores

Flores Island is generally a safe destination, with low crime rates. However, as with any destination, it is important to keep your belongings secure and avoid wandering around deserted areas alone. Be aware of the ocean’s strong currents and follow guidelines when swimming.

Health in Flores

The health facilities on Flores Island are limited, so it is advisable to have travel insurance that covers medical care. Ensure that you have the necessary vaccinations before traveling to the Azores.

Getting to Flores: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Flores

There are two airports near Flores:

  • Flores Airport: This is the best airport to fly into on Flores.
  • Horta Airport: Located on the neighboring island of Faial, this airport offers connections to Flores.

Bus Stations Near Flores

There are no bus stations on Flores Island, but there are several car rental companies offering car and bike rentals.

Other Options

The best way to travel around Flores is by car or bike. Taxis are also available and can be booked through local taxi firms.

How to Get Around in Flores

The best way to get around Flores Island is by car or bike. Car and bike rentals are available throughout the island, and some hotels also offer bikes for rent. Taxis are also available and can be booked through local taxi firms.

Where to Go Next from Flores: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The following destinations are neighboring Flores and offer great excursions:

  • Corvo: A small and remote island that can only be reached by small boats or planes.
  • Faial: An attractive island with great beaches, architecture, and culture.
  • Pico: An island with a famous volcano, beaches, and vineyards.

Conclusion

Flores Island is a hidden gem in the Azores archipelago with breathtaking scenery, a rich culture, and unique traditions. Plan your dream vacation to this beautiful island today and discover the magic of the westernmost point of Europe.
