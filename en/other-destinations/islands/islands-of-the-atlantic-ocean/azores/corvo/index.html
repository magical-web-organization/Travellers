Introduction to Corvo

Corvo, a small volcanic island in the Azores archipelago, is renowned for its untouched natural beauty and rugged cliffs that drop hundreds of feet into the Atlantic Ocean. This remote and stunning island measures just 6.5km in length and 4km in width, making it the smallest island in the Azores. In addition to being the ideal destination for nature lovers, Corvo is also known for its rich cultural heritage, charming villages, and friendly locals.

List of Places to Visit in Corvo

From cultural landmarks to unreal natural beauty, Corvo boasts plenty of fascinating places to visit. Here are some of the must-visit attractions that you shouldn’t miss during your trip to this hidden paradise:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Caldeirão - Also known as the “big pot,” the Caldeirão is a volcanic crater filled with crystal-clear water. Visitors can enjoy the views of this stunning natural wonder from the surrounding hiking trails.

  • Nossa Senhora dos Milagres Church - The only church on the island of Corvo, Nossa Senhora dos Milagres is a beautiful construction that dates back to the 16th century and is also said to be the oldest of all the Azorean churches.

  • Fort of the Island of Corvo - Built in the 17th century to protect the island from pirates, the Fort of the Island of Corvo is a popular destination for history buffs, offering stunning panoramic views of the surrounding ocean.

Natural Attractions of Corvo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Monte Gordo - Corvo’s highest point at 720 meters above sea level, Monte Gordo is a challenging hike but offers the most beautiful views of the island, the ocean, and the neighboring islands of Flores and Faial.

  • The Santa Cruz Lighthouse - Perched on the rugged cliffs of Corvo’s easternmost point, the Santa Cruz Lighthouse offers a stunning panoramic view of the island’s rocky coastline and the Atlantic Ocean beyond.

  • Corvo’s coastline - One of the most striking features of Corvo is its rugged coastline, marked by towering cliffs, deep ravines, and rocky coves. Head to the coast to capture some stunning photos and explore the various hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Corvo

The island’s limited size makes it ideal for exploring on foot or by bicycle. Here are some of the most popular hiking/biking routes:

  • The Circular Route of Ponta Delgada - A leisurely walk that takes you through the village of Ponta Delgada and offers views of the island’s flora and volcanic landscapes.

  • Toledo Trail - A moderate hike that takes you through a series of breathtaking landscapes, including dense forests, volcanic craters, and stunning coastline vistas.

  • Mount Gordo Trail - One of the most popular hiking routes on Corvo, the Mount Gordo Trail takes you to the top of the island’s highest peak through forests of Laurissilva vegetation and panoramic views.

Best Things to Do in Corvo: Activities and Tours

Despite its rural setting, there’s no shortage of things to do on the island of Corvo:

  • Whale Watching Tours - Corvo is located on the migratory route of many cetacean species, including minke whales, sperm whales, and bottlenose dolphins. A whale watching tour is an unforgettable experience.

  • Bird Watching - With its rich and rare birdlife, including the barn owl, the Cory’s shearwater, and the Eurasian sparrowhawk, Corvo is a birdwatcher’s paradise.

  • Rock Climbing - Corvo’s unique volcanic landscape and rugged cliffs make it perfect for rock climbing enthusiasts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Corvo

For those planning to spend a week in Corvo, here’s a recommended itinerary:

  • Day 1-2: Explore the island’s charming villages and beaches, including Vila do Corvo and Praia da Areia.

  • Day 3: Take a whale watching tour and spot some of the stunning marine life that surrounds the island.

  • Day 4: Visit the Caldeirão and hike its surrounding trails.

  • Day 5: Discover the island’s fascinating history and culture by visiting museums and historical landmarks.

  • Day 6: Hike to the top of Monte Gordo and take in the spectacular views of the island.

  • Day 7: Enjoy some leisure time and soak up the island’s laid-back vibe.

Food and Drink Scene of Corvo

Corvo is a haven for foodies, with its traditional local cuisine, farm-to-table offerings, and fresh seafood. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cozido das Furnas - A hearty stew consisting of various meats and vegetables, simmered in volcanic steam for several hours. It’s a traditional Azorian dish that highlights the island’s unique geothermal landscape

  • Lapas - A type of shellfish that’s popular in the Azores, Lapas can be enjoyed grilled or served with garlic butter.

  • Queijo do Pico - A type of cheese made from the milk of grass-fed cows that roam the pastures of Corvo. It typically accompanies any meal and is a popular souvenir to bring back home.

Local Beverages

  • Vinho Verde - A light white wine that’s ideal for sipping while enjoying the island’s beautiful sunsets.

  • Cafezinho - A type of coffee that’s unique to the Azores, cafezinho is a delicious and potent shot of espresso that’s enjoyed after a meal.

  • Queimada - A type of liquor that’s made from agricultural rum, cinnamon, sugar, and lemon. It’s usually served with a set of rituals, involving creating flaming effects while pouring and mixing the drink.

Where to Stay in Corvo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

If you’re planning to spend the night on the island, you’ll find plenty of options ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious resorts.

  • Residencial Corvo Mar - A comfortable hotel located close to the city center, the Residencial Corvo Mar offers spacious rooms and stunning sea views.

  • Casa das Flores - One of the best-rated guesthouses on Corvo, Casa das Flores boasts a beautiful garden and a terrace where visitors can relax and enjoy the tranquility of the island.

  • Casa do Boqueirão - A small, family-run hotel located in the heart of the island’s volcanic landscape, Casa do Boqueirão offers cozy accommodations and delicious homemade breakfasts.

What to Buy in Corvo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts to bring back home, you’ll find a wide variety of traditional handicrafts and local products in Corvo. Here are some of the most sought-after items:

  • Embroidery - The island is renowned for its beautiful embroidery, with intricate patterns and colorful designs.

  • Laurissilva Honey - A type of honey made from the nectar of the island’s Laurissilva vegetation, and it’s considered one of the best in the world.

  • Handmade pottery - You’ll find various unique pieces of pottery made by local artisans, such as plates, mugs, and bowls with hand-painted designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Corvo

Corvo is a traditional island with a strong sense of community and culture. Some of the unique customs and traditions of the island include:

  • The Procession of Senhor Jesus dos Milagres - A religious procession that takes place every year in August, where the statue of Senhor Jesus dos Milagres is carried throughout the streets of the island.

  • The Festival of the Divine Holy Spirit - Held in June, this festival celebrates the island’s rural heritage and features parades, live music, and traditional dances.

Best Time to Visit Corvo

Corvo’s mild climate makes it a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is during the summer months, from June to August. These months offer the warmest temperatures, the most pleasant weather, and longer daylight hours for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Corvo

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when traveling to Corvo:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions of the islanders.

  • Take care of the natural environment and follow the Leave No Trace principles.

  • Support local businesses by purchasing local products and souvenirs.

Don’t:

  • Do not disturb the natural environment or wildlife.

  • Avoid making noise or causing disturbances during the night.

  • Do not litter the island.

Safety and Health

Safety in Corvo

Corvo is a safe destination for tourists. However, visitors should take the usual safety precautions, such as keeping valuable items in a safe place, being aware of their surroundings, and avoiding dark alleys at night.

Health in Corvo

Corvo has a well-equipped medical center that offers general medical services. Visitors should ensure they have comprehensive travel insurance that covers medical expenses and emergency evacuation.

Getting to Corvo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Corvo is accessible by air or sea, and there are no direct flights from the mainland. Here are the transportation options and logistics:

Airports Near Corvo

Corvo Airport is located on the island and offers daily flights to other islands in the Azores, such as Flores and Faial, and the mainland.

Bus Stations Near Corvo

There are no buses on Corvo, but taxis and rental cars are available. Visitors should book in advance to avoid disappointment, especially during the high season.

Other Options

Ferries and boats depart from Flores and Horta to Corvo, but the schedules vary and are susceptible to weather conditions.

How to Get Around in Corvo

The island is compact and can be easily explored by foot or by bicycle. Visitors can also rent a car or a taxi to explore the island’s more remote areas.

Where to Go Next from Corvo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

In addition to exploring the other picturesque Azorean islands, such as Sao Jorge and Pico, visitors can also indulge in the following excursions:

  • Scuba Diving - Explore the underwater world of Corvo with a scuba diving excursion to the nearby waters.

  • Horseback Riding - Discover the island’s rugged terrain and stunning coastline while horseback riding under the supervision of professional guides.

  • Whale and Dolphin Watching - Take a boat tour from the island to see some of the ocean’s most majestic creatures in their natural habitat.

Conclusion

From its stunning natural landscapes to its rich cultural heritage and charming villages, Corvo is a hidden gem that must be experienced to be believed. Explore this unique destination, and you’ll see why it’s one of the most exciting tourist destinations in the Azores.
