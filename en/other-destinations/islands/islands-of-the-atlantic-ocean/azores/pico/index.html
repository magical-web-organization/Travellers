Introduction to Pico

Pico is a stunning island located in the Azores archipelago, in the middle of the Atlantic Ocean. It’s known for its volcanic landscapes, rich marine life, and traditional vineyards. The island offers plenty of outdoor activities, such as hiking, whale watching, and scuba diving. Moreover, Pico has a unique culture and history that are worth exploring.

List of Places to Visit in Pico

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Whaling Museum - learn about the island’s whaling history and see a collection of whale hunting tools.
  • São Roque Museum - discover the island’s religious art and see a replica of the whaleboats used for whaling.
  • Pico Island Vineyard Culture - explore the vineyards that have been classified as UNESCO World Heritage Sites.
  • Gruta das Torres - visit one of the longest lava tunnels in the Azores.

Natural Attractions of Pico: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Pico - hike to the top of this mountain, which is the highest in Portugal, for breathtaking views of the island and the sea.
  • Furnas do Enxofre - marvel at the fumaroles and volcanic vents that emit hot sulfuric gases.
  • Baía de Canas - swim in the natural pools formed by volcanic rocks on the coast.
  • Lajes do Pico - go scuba diving to see manta rays, barracudas, and other marine life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pico

  • Pico Mountain Trail - a challenging hike to the peak of Mount Pico.
  • Caminho das Voltas - a circular hike that starts and ends in the village of São Roque do Pico, passing through vineyards, forests, and natural swimming pools.
  • Lagoa do Capitão Trail - a short hike that leads to a scenic lake surrounded by volcanic formations.
  • Caminho da Costa - a coastal trail that offers views of cliffs, coves, and small fishing villages.

Best Things to Do in Pico: Activities and Tours

  • Whale Watching Tour - spot and learn about the different species of whales, dolphins, and porpoises that visit the waters around Pico.
  • Wine Tasting Tour - visit local vineyards and taste the unique Verdelho wine.
  • Scuba Diving Trip - explore the underwater world and see shipwrecks, caves, and colorful marine life.
  • Horseback Riding Adventure - ride a Lusitano horse through the countryside and enjoy the views.

Food and Drink Scene of Pico

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lapas - grilled limpets, a type of sea snail, served with garlic and butter.
  • Cacoila - slow-cooked pork marinated in wine and garlic.
  • Alcatra - beef stewed with onions, tomatoes, and red wine.
  • Fofas da Povoação - small sweet rolls filled with custard and topped with sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Verdelho wine - a dry white wine made from a unique grape variety grown in Pico’s vineyards.
  • Licores dos Açores - liqueurs made from local fruits and herbs, such as passion fruit, pineapple, and anise.
  • Espresso with a shot of local brandy - a popular way to end a meal in Pico.

Where to Stay in Pico: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Aldeia da Fonte Hotel - a boutique hotel with a spa, a sea-water pool, and ocean-view rooms.
  • Pousada de Juventude do Pico - a youth hostel with affordable rooms and a prime location in Madalena.
  • Pensão da Rocha - a cozy guesthouse with a patio, a garden, and a restaurant serving homemade food.

What to Buy in Pico: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Verdelho wine - a bottle of this unique white wine makes a great souvenir or gift.
  • Black basalt handicrafts - objects made from the island’s volcanic rock, such as bowls, vases, and sculptures.
  • Cheese and honey - try local varieties of cheese, such as São Jorge or Pico, and honey from the island’s beekeepers.

Local Culture and Customs of Pico

  • Whale hunting tradition - Pico was once a major whaling center, and its culture and history are linked to this activity.
  • Religious celebrations - the island’s calendar is marked by festivals and processions honoring saints, such as São Roque and São João.
  • Traditional crafts - locals still practice handicrafts such as lace making, basket weaving, and embroidery.

Best Time to Visit Pico

The best time to visit Pico is from May to September, when the weather is mild and sunny, and most outdoor activities are available. However, the island’s beauty and charm can be enjoyed year-round, as long as visitors are prepared for occasional rain and wind.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pico

Do:

  • Respect the island’s natural beauty and conservation efforts.
  • Sample local food and drinks, such as seafood, cheese, and wine.
  • Participate in cultural activities, such as festivals and fado concerts.
  • Support local businesses and craftspeople.

Don’t:

  • Harm, hunt, or disturb marine life.
  • Trespass on private property or protected areas.
  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pico

Pico is a safe destination, but visitors should follow normal precautions, such as locking their accommodation and not leaving valuables unattended. Moreover, outdoor activities such as hiking and diving require proper gear and preparation.

Health in Pico

Pico has good medical facilities, but visitors should have travel insurance, wear sunscreen, and protect themselves from mosquito bites. Tap water is safe to drink, but bottled water is widely available.

Getting to Pico: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pico

Pico Airport (PIX) is the island’s main airport, with flights to other Azores islands and mainland Portugal. Visitors can also fly to Horta Airport (HOR) in Faial and take a ferry to Pico.

Bus Stations Near Pico

Rodoviária dos Açores operates several bus routes on the island, connecting its main cities and villages. Timetables and fares are available online.

Other Options

Car rental, taxi, and private transfer services are also available on the island.

How to Get Around in Pico

The best way to explore Pico is by car or bike, as public transportation is limited. Visitors can rent a car or bike at Pico Airport or in the main cities. Taxis and private transfer services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Pico: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Faial Island - take a ferry to visit the charming city of Horta and explore the island’s volcano and coastline.
  • Terceira Island - fly or ferry to Terceira to discover its historical capital, Angra do Heroísmo, and its lush natural parks.
  • São Miguel Island - take a flight to the largest Azores island to see its geothermal wonders, such as Furnas and the Sete Cidades crater.

Conclusion

Pico is a mesmerizing island that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural richness, and outdoor adventures. From its volcanic landscapes to its unique wine and cheese, Pico is a destination that will linger in your memory long after your trip.
