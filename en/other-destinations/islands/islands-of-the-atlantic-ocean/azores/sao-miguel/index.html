Introduction to São Miguel

Located in the middle of the Atlantic Ocean, São Miguel is the largest and most populous island in the Azores. Known for its lush greenery, stunning landscapes, and a rich history, São Miguel has become one of the most popular destinations for nature lovers and adventurous travelers.

List of Places to Visit in São Miguel

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you enjoy learning about history, São Miguel has several museums and historical sites that are worth a visit.

  • Museu Carlos Machado: It is one of the oldest museums in Portugal, featuring a collection of art, science, and history.
  • Santuário do Senhor Santo Cristo dos Milagres: This church is the site of one of the most important religious events in Azores, the annual pilgrimage that takes place on the fifth Sunday after Easter.
  • Fortaleza de São Brás: Built in the 16th century, this fort used to protect the island from pirates and other invaders.

Natural Attractions of São Miguel: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

From hiking and biking to swimming and whale watching, São Miguel offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures.

  • Sete Cidades: This stunning volcanic crater is a popular spot for hiking and relaxation, featuring two beautiful lakes, Lagoa Azul and Lagoa Verde.
  • Furnas: Known for its hot springs, geysers, and fumaroles, this village is a great place to enjoy a relaxing bath and explore the surrounding nature.
  • Lagoa do Fogo: This remote lake is a hidden gem for nature lovers, surrounded by lush vegetation and breathtaking views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of São Miguel

São Miguel has a variety of hiking and biking trails that offer different levels of difficulty and scenic views.

  • Trilho dos Burros: This easy trail takes you through fields, forests, and a medieval bridge, ending at the beautiful secluded beach of Ribeira Quente.
  • Caldeira Velha: A moderate hike that takes you through a lush forest to a hot waterfall and geothermal pool, perfect for a refreshing swim.
  • Salto do Prego: This challenging hike leads to a stunning waterfall with crystal clear water, surrounded by beautiful flora and fauna.

Best Things to Do in São Miguel: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, São Miguel offers plenty of other activities and tours that allow you to explore the island’s flora, fauna, and cultural heritage.

  • Whale and Dolphin watching: São Miguel is one of the best places in the world to spot whales and dolphins in their natural habitat.
  • Horse Riding: Explore the island’s stunning countryside on horseback, and enjoy the peace and quiet of nature.
  • Azorean Cooking Class: Discover the secrets of Azorean cuisine and learn how to cook delicious traditional dishes using fresh local ingredients.

Week-Long Itinerary for São Miguel

Day 1: Arrive in Ponta Delgada, the capital of São Miguel, and explore the city center. Day 2: Visit Sete Cidades and enjoy a relaxing picnic in the park. Day 3: Hike to Caldeira Velha and enjoy a dip in the hot waterfall and geothermal pool. Day 4: Visit the hot springs in Furnas and try the famous cozido, a traditional meat and vegetable stew cooked underground. Day 5: Explore the northeastern part of the island, including the villages of Nordeste and Povoação, and the beautiful viewpoint of Salto do Cavalo. Day 6: Take a whale watching tour and see the amazing marine life of São Miguel. Day 7: Visit São Miguel’s oldest and most beautiful gardens, the Jardim Antonio Borges, and relax in the lush greenery.

Food and Drink Scene of São Miguel

Must-Try Dishes

São Miguel has a rich culinary tradition, with a focus on fresh, seasonal, and local ingredients. Some of the must-try dishes are:

  • Cozido: A traditional meat and vegetable stew, cooked underground using the natural heat from the hot springs in Furnas.
  • Bolo Lêvedo: A sweet bread that is perfect for breakfast or as a snack, often served with butter and cheese.
  • Espetada: Grilled cubes of beef, often served with bread and potatoes.

Local Beverages

São Miguel is also known for its local beverages, including:

  • Vinho Verde: A light, refreshing white wine that pairs perfectly with seafood and fish.
  • Liqueur: Made with local fruits and herbs, such as passion fruit, pineapple, and mint, these liqueurs are perfect after-dinner drinks.
  • Kima: A fizzy, fruity soda made with the local orange and pineapple, perfect for a hot summer day.

Where to Stay in São Miguel: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

São Miguel offers a variety of accommodation options for every budget, from luxurious hotels to cozy guesthouses and hostels.

  • Grand Hotel Açores Atlântico: A five-star hotel located in the heart of Ponta Delgada, offering luxurious rooms and suites, a spa, and a rooftop pool.
  • Rua Direita Guesthouse: A charming guesthouse located in the historic center of Ponta Delgada, offering comfortable rooms and a cozy atmosphere.
  • YHA Hostel Ponta Delgada: A budget-friendly hostel located in the center of Ponta Delgada, offering dorms and private rooms, a communal kitchen, and free Wi-Fi.

What to Buy in São Miguel: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For those looking for the perfect souvenir to bring back home, some of the best local products are:

  • Azorean Cheese: Made with local milk from cows, sheep, and goats, this cheese is known for its rich and creamy flavor.
  • Azores Tea: Grown on the island of São Miguel, this tea is best enjoyed with a dash of milk and sugar.
  • Ceramics: From traditional plates and bowls to modern pieces and art, São Miguel has a rich history of pottery and ceramic crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of São Miguel

São Miguel has a rich cultural heritage, with a mix of Portuguese, Azorean, and local traditions.

  • Religion: The island is deeply religious, with several important religious events and festivals throughout the year.
  • Folklore: São Miguel has a rich tradition of folklore, with music, dance, and costumes representing its unique diversity.
  • Hospitality: The people of São Miguel are known for their warm and welcoming hospitality, making visitors feel at home.

Best Time to Visit São Miguel

The best time to visit São Miguel is from May to October, when the weather is warm and sunny, and most of the outdoor activities and tours are available.

Do’s and Don’ts of São Miguel

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Learn some basic Portuguese.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in religious sites.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Talk loudly in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in São Miguel

São Miguel is generally a safe and peaceful island, with low crime rates and friendly people. However, it is always important to take basic safety precautions, such as:

  • Lock your doors and windows.
  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables.
  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night.

Health in São Miguel

São Miguel has a modern and efficient healthcare system, with several public and private hospitals and clinics. Visitors from the EU can use their EHIC card to access free or discounted medical treatment. It is also recommended to have a travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to São Miguel: Transportation Options and Logistics

São Miguel is accessible by air and by sea, with several options for transportation within the island.

Airports Near São Miguel

  • João Paulo II Airport: Located in Ponta Delgada, this is the main airport in São Miguel, with daily flights to and from major European cities.

Bus Stations Near São Miguel

  • Ponta Delgada Bus Terminal: This is the main bus terminal in São Miguel, with connections to most of the island’s villages and towns.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car is one of the best ways to explore São Miguel on your own, with several rental companies available at the airport and in Ponta Delgada.
  • Taxi: Taxis are also available in São Miguel, with fixed fares for most destinations.

How to Get Around in São Miguel

São Miguel has a good network of roads and highways, with several options for transportation.

  • Car: Renting a car is the best option for exploring the island at your own pace, with several rental companies available at the airport and in Ponta Delgada.
  • Bus: The local buses are a budget-friendly option, with connections to most of the island’s villages and towns.
  • Taxi: Taxis are available in São Miguel, with fixed fares for most destinations.

Where to Go Next from São Miguel: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

São Miguel is surrounded by several smaller islands, known for their stunning landscapes and unique cultures.

  • Santa Maria: Known for its beautiful beaches and sunny weather, Santa Maria is a great place to relax and unwind after exploring São Miguel.
  • Terceira: One of the most beautiful islands in Azores, Terceira is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, with stunning architecture and a rich cultural heritage.

Conclusion

São Miguel is a unique destination, offering a blend of nature, culture, and adventure. With its stunning landscapes, rich history, and warm hospitality, São Miguel has something to offer to every type of traveler. Whether you are looking for a relaxing holiday or an adrenaline-pumping adventure, São Miguel is the perfect place to explore.
