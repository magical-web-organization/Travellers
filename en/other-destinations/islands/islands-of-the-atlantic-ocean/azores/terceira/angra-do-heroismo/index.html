Introduction to Angra do Heroísmo

Angra do Heroísmo is a city located on the island of Terceira, in the Azores archipelago. It was the first city established in the Azores and played a strategic role in the Portuguese Empire’s maritime expansion in the 15th and 16th centuries. In 1983, the city was named a UNESCO World Heritage Site due to its historical and cultural significance.

The city is known for its colorful streets, charming architecture, and stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean. Visitors can explore the city’s museums, historical buildings, and natural attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Angra do Heroísmo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Angra do Heroísmo Museum

The Angra do Heroísmo Museum is housed in the former Convent of São Francisco, which was built during the 16th century. The museum showcases the history and culture of the Azores, including art, archaeology, and ethnography.

Fortaleza de São João Batista

Located on the eastern side of Angra do Heroísmo Bay, the fortress of São João Batista was built in the 16th century to protect the city from pirates and other invaders. Visitors can explore the fortress and its museums, which showcase the history of Angra do Heroísmo and the Azores.

Sé Cathedral

The Sé Cathedral is a beautiful Baroque-style church dating back to the 16th century. The cathedral is located in the heart of the city and features stunning artwork, including a painted ceiling and a 17th-century wooden altar.

Natural Attractions of Angra do Heroísmo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Monte Brasil

Monte Brasil is a volcanic cone that rises above the city. It offers panoramic views of Angra do Heroísmo Bay and the surrounding landscape. Visitors can hike to the summit or explore the park’s hiking trails, which offer stunning views.

Algar do Carvão

Algar do Carvão is a unique volcanic cave located near Angra do Heroísmo. Visitors can explore the cave’s lava formations and underground lake, which are illuminated by electric lights.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Angra do Heroísmo

Monte Brasil Trail

The Monte Brasil Trail is a hiking trail that leads to the summit of Monte Brasil. The trail starts near the city center and passes through the forested slopes of the volcano, offering stunning views of Angra do Heroísmo Bay.

Ecovia do Litoral

The Ecovia do Litoral is a coastal trail that runs along the south coast of Terceira Island. The trail passes through vineyards, coastal cliffs, and traditional villages. Visitors can hike or bike the trail, which offers stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean.

Best Things to Do in Angra do Heroísmo: Activities and Tours

Whale Watching

Whale watching tours are a popular activity in Angra do Heroísmo. Visitors can spot several species of whales, including sperm whales, pilot whales, and humpback whales, in the waters surrounding Terceira Island.

Caving Tours

Caving tours are a unique way to explore the underground caves and lava tubes of Terceira Island. Visitors can explore caves such as Algar do Carvão and learn about the island’s volcanic history.

Food and Drink Scene of Angra do Heroísmo

Must-Try Dishes

Cozido das Furnas

Cozido das Furnas is a traditional Portuguese stew that is cooked for hours in the volcanic soil of Furnas in São Miguel Island. The stew contains several meats, vegetables, and legumes and is served with bread.

Lapas

Lapas are a popular seafood dish in the Azores. They are grilled limpets and are often served with garlic, butter, and Portuguese spices.

Local Beverages

Vinho Verdelho

Vinho Verdelho is a white wine produced in the Azores. It has a crisp flavor and pairs well with local seafood dishes.

Cerveja Especial

Cerveja Especial is a local beer that is brewed in Terceira Island. It has a crisp, refreshing taste and is often served with local snacks.

Where to Stay in Angra do Heroísmo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Terceira Mar Hotel

The Terceira Mar Hotel is a modern hotel located on the beachfront of Angra do Heroísmo Bay. It features a swimming pool, spa, and restaurant.

Quinta das Merces

Quinta das Merces is a historic guesthouse located in a former 17th-century convent. It features a beautiful garden and traditional Azorean architecture.

What to Buy in Angra do Heroísmo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Azorean Cheese

Azorean cheese is a must-try when visiting the Azores. The cheese is made from cow’s milk and has a variety of flavors, from mild to strong.

Azorean Pineapple

Azorean pineapple is a unique type of pineapple that is grown on the island. It has a sweet, succulent flavor and is often used in local desserts.

Local Culture and Customs of Angra do Heroísmo

Angra do Heroísmo has a rich cultural heritage. Traditional Azorean festivals and events are held throughout the year, including the Sanjoaninas Festival in June and the Angra do Heroísmo International Jazz Festival in September. The Azorean culture is also reflected in the island’s cuisine, music, and handicrafts.

Best Time to Visit Angra do Heroísmo

The best time to visit Angra do Heroísmo is from May to September when the weather is warm and sunny. The city can be visited year-round, but some attractions may be closed during the winter months.

Safety and Health

Safety in Angra do Heroísmo

Angra do Heroísmo is a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should take the usual precautions when traveling, such as locking doors and windows and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Angra do Heroísmo

The healthcare system in the Azores is of a high standard. However, visitors should have travel insurance before arrival to the Azores.

Getting to Angra do Heroísmo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Angra do Heroísmo

The closest airport to Angra do Heroísmo is Terceira Island Airport. It has connections with mainland Portugal and other destinations.

Bus Stations Near Angra do Heroísmo

The Angra do Heroísmo bus station is located in the city center and has connections with other cities on Terceira Island.

Other Options

Ferries connect Angra do Heroísmo with other islands in the Azores.

How to Get Around in Angra do Heroísmo

The best way to explore Angra do Heroísmo is on foot. However, visitors can also rent a car or bike to explore the island.

Where to Go Next from Angra do Heroísmo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

São Miguel Island

São Miguel Island is the largest island in the Azores and is known for its stunning natural scenery and geothermal hot springs.

Pico Island

Pico Island is known for its volcanic landscape and wine production. Visitors can hike to the summit of Pico Mountain, the highest point in Portugal.

Conclusion

Angra do Heroísmo is a city rich in history and culture, offering a variety of activities and attractions. Visitors can explore the city’s museums, natural attractions, and enjoy local food and drink. Visitors can also use Angra do Heroísmo as a base to explore other nearby islands in the Azores.
