Introduction to Funchal

Funchal is a beautiful port city located in Madeira, an archipelago of Portugal situated in the Atlantic Ocean. This charming city serves as the capital of Madeira and has become a popular destination due to its stunning landscapes, historical sites, and thriving culinary scene. Funchal offers a unique blend of modernity and tradition, making it an ideal destination for all kinds of travelers. In this guide, we will take a closer look at the top attractions, best things to do, where to stay, and much more in Funchal.

List of Places to Visit in Funchal

Funchal offers an array of choices for travelers looking to explore its diverse array of attractions. Here’s a list of must-visit places when in Funchal:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Visit the Funchal Cathedral located in the old town area. Built in the 15th century, the cathedral boasts stunning Gothic architecture and is home to an impressive collection of Portuguese religious art.
  • Check out the Madeira Story Centre, which showcases the history and culture of Madeira, including its unique flora and fauna.
  • Go on a tour of the Blandy’s Wine Lodge and learn about the city’s famous Madeira wine, which has been a staple for over 200 years.

Natural Attractions of Funchal: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Take a cable car ride to Monte Palace Tropical Garden, known for its botanical gardens and stunning views of the city.
  • Explore the Laurissilva Forest, a UNESCO World Heritage site known for its diverse range of flora and fauna.
  • Take a dip in the natural swimming pools at Porto Moniz, located on the northwestern coast of the island.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Funchal

Funchal offers plenty of options for outdoor enthusiasts, from easy strolls to challenging hikes, all of which offer stunning vistas of the island’s natural beauty. Here are some of the best trails to explore:

  • Levada do Caldeirão Verde: This is a popular trail that winds through the dense forests along the Levada, passing through tunnels and over gushing waterfalls.
  • Pico Ruivo: This is the highest mountain in Madeira, and the peak offers stunning panoramic views of the island.
  • Ponta de São Lourenço: This is a scenic hike that takes you through the easternmost point of Madeira, offering breathtaking views of the Atlantic Ocean.

Best Things to Do in Funchal: Activities and Tours

Funchal has plenty of activities and tours on offer to keep travelers entertained throughout their stay. Here are a few popular options:

  • Take a dolphin and whale watching tour to see these majestic creatures up close.
  • Go on a food tour of Funchal, and sample the local cuisine, which is heavily influenced by Portuguese and African flavors.
  • Explore the famous Madeira Botanical Garden, home to an extensive variety of plant species, including indigenous and exotic plants.

Week-Long Itinerary for Funchal

To fully experience Funchal’s diverse attractions, we recommend spending at least a week in the city. Here’s an itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day Activities
1 Explore the Funchal Old Town and visit the Funchal Cathedral
2 Visit the Laurissilva Forest
3 Take a cable car ride to Monte Palace Tropical Garden
4 Go on a Levada hike
5 Take a dolphin and whale watching tour
6 Explore the Blandy’s Wine Lodge
7 Relax at Porto Moniz natural swimming pools

Food and Drink Scene of Funchal

Funchal’s food scene is a melting pot of African, Portuguese, and Brazilian influences, creating a unique culinary experience. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages when in Funchal:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Espetada: Grilled meat skewers served with a side of milho frito (fried polenta) and salad are a staple in Madeira.
  • Bacalhau: This traditional Portuguese salted cod dish is a popular delicacy in Funchal.
  • Bolo do Caco: A flatbread made with sweet potatoes and typically served with garlic butter or cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Poncha: This is Funchal’s signature drink, made with aguardente de cana (sugar cane brandy), honey, sugar, and lemon juice.
  • Madeira wine: Madeira wine has a unique flavor and aroma, and you can taste it at various wineries and wine lodges across Funchal.

Where to Stay in Funchal: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Funchal has a range of accommodation options to suit any travel budget, with several hotels, hostels, and guesthouses available. Here are some of the best options when in Funchal:

  • Belmond Reid’s Palace: One of the most iconic hotels in Funchal, offering stunning sea views and luxurious amenities.
  • Madeira Panoramico Hotel: This hotel is perfect for budget travelers, located on a hill offering panoramic views of Funchal.
  • Estalagem do Vale: A serene hotel located in a peaceful valley outside Funchal, offering guests a peaceful and tranquil retreat.

What to Buy in Funchal: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Funchal, be sure to check out the local markets for souvenirs, crafts, and local products. Here’s what you should look out for:

  • Embroidery: Funchal is known for its high-quality embroidery, and you can find it at local markets or at the Madeira Embroidery Museum.
  • Madeira wine: Bring home a bottle or two of the famous Madeira wine, a unique and flavorful souvenir.
  • Traditional crafts: Look out for local crafts such as wicker baskets, pottery, and lacework.

Local Culture and Customs of Funchal

Funchal has a rich history and culture, heavily influenced by its Portuguese heritage, African roots, and scenic surroundings. Here are a few customs and traditions you should know before visiting Funchal:

  • Religion plays an important role in the city, with many religious festivals and events held throughout the year, such as the Funchal Flower Festival and Madeira Wine Festival.
  • Funchal has a rich artistic heritage, with a range of events and exhibitions taking place throughout the year, showcasing local talent and creativity.
  • Madeira is famous for its floral displays, and you can see some of the most impressive blooms at the Madeira Botanical Garden.

Best Time to Visit Funchal

Funchal has a mild climate year-round, making it a popular destination throughout the year. However, the best time to visit Funchal is during the summer months from June to September when the weather is warm and sunny.

Do’s and Don’ts of Funchal

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Funchal:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages, including the poncha and Madeira wine.
  • Respect religious festivals and traditions when in Funchal.
  • Take time to explore the city’s natural beauty, including the Laurissilva Forest and Madeira Botanical Garden.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment when exploring Funchal’s natural attractions.
  • Don’t leave valuables unattended or carry large sums of money when in crowded areas.

Safety and Health

When traveling to Funchal, it’s important to consider both safety and health precautions to ensure a comfortable and safe trip.

Safety in Funchal

Funchal is generally a safe city, but it’s always wise to exercise caution when traveling. Here are a few safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Keep valuables out of sight and only carry essentials when exploring the city.
  • Stick to well-lit areas at night and avoid walking alone in unfamiliar areas.
  • Beware of pickpockets and keep an eye on your belongings when in crowded areas.

Health in Funchal

Funchal is a healthy destination, but it’s always recommended to take precautions during travel. Here are a few health tips to keep in mind:

  • Stay hydrated throughout your trip, especially during the summer months.
  • Apply sunscreen regularly to avoid sunburn and protect your skin from UV rays.
  • Be aware of your food and water sources and follow standard food-handling procedures.

Getting to Funchal: Transportation Options and Logistics

Funchal is easily accessible through multiple modes of transportation.

Airports Near Funchal

The Madeira International Airport (FNC) is located about 20 minutes from the city center, and several international airlines offer flights to this airport.

Bus Stations Near Funchal

Funchal has an extensive bus system that connects to several popular destinations across Madeira, including Porto Moniz, Camara De Lobos, and Santana.

Other Options

Taxis and car rentals are also available for travelers looking for more convenience and flexibility when exploring the city.

How to Get Around in Funchal

Funchal is a pedestrian-friendly city, and many of the popular attractions are within walking distance. Taxis, buses, and car rentals are also available for travelers looking for more convenience and flexibility when exploring the city.

Where to Go Next from Funchal: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Funchal’s proximity to other popular destinations in Madeira makes it an ideal base for exploring the island. Here are some nearby destinations to check out:

  • Camara De Lobos: A picturesque fishing village known for its scenic beauty and charming atmosphere.
  • Porto Moniz: A village located on the northwestern coast of Madeira, offering natural swimming pools and stunning coastal views.
  • Santana: A small town known for its unique triangular houses and traditional Madeiran architecture.

Conclusion

Funchal is a beautiful city with something to offer for all kinds of travelers and budgets. From its rich cultural heritage to its stunning natural landscapes, Funchal has earned its reputation as one of the top destinations in Portugal. With this comprehensive travel guide, we hope you’re inspired to plan your trip to this charming city soon.
