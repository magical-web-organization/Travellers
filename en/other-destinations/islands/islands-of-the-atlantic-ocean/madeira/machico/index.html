Introduction to Machico

Located on the East coast of Madeira, Machico is often overlooked by tourists in favour of more popular destinations like Funchal and Porto Santo. However, this charming town has a rich history and stunning natural beauty that makes it well worth a visit.

Founded in the 15th century, Machico was the first capital of Madeira and has many cultural attractions that reflect its long and fascinating history. From beautiful beaches to rugged mountain landscapes, Machico offers visitors the perfect blend of outdoor adventure and cultural exploration.

List of Places to Visit in Machico

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • São Roque Chapel: Built in the 15th century, the chapel is a fine example of Gothic architecture and is home to some beautiful 16th century paintings.
  • Engenho Velho Museum: This museum is dedicated to the history of sugar production on the island and gives visitors an insight into what life was like on Madeira during the 19th century.
  • Nossa Senhora do Amparo Church: Built in the 18th century, this church is considered one of the most important religious sites on the island.

Natural Attractions of Machico: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pico do Facho: This 320-metre high peak offers stunning views of the town and the surrounding coastline. The hike to the top takes around 30 minutes and is well worth the effort for the views alone.
  • Machico Beach: With golden sand and crystal-clear waters, Machico Beach is a popular spot for swimming and sunbathing. There are also plenty of cafes and restaurants nearby.
  • Parque Ribeira Primeira: This beautiful park is home to several walking trails that take visitors through the lush forest and along the river.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Machico

Machico has some of the most breathtaking hikes and bike tours in Madeira. These routes are suitable for all levels of expertise and are a great way to explore the island’s rugged and dramatic landscapes.

  • Vereda do Larano: This 11-kilometre trail takes hikers through the Laurissilva forest, a UNESCO World Heritage site. The trail takes approximately five hours to complete and offers stunning views of the island’s highest peaks.
  • Bike day tours: These guided tours take visitors through the stunning valley of Serra d’Agua. Visitors will cycle through the Laurissilva forest and take in the breathtaking views of the island’s highest peaks.

Best Things to Do in Machico: Activities and Tours

  • Levada Walks: Machico has several levadas (water channels) that provide excellent walking opportunities. The gentle slopes and beautiful views make it perfect for walkers of all levels.
  • Dolphin and Whale watching: Rachico offers some of the best whale and dolphin watching experiences in the world. With a range of tours and options to choose from, visitors can witness the beauty and grace of these incredible creatures in their natural habitat.
  • Guided Tours: Exploring Machico with a local guide is the perfect way to discover the hidden gems of the town. From cultural landmarks to secret hiking trails, the knowledgeable guides can offer visitors a memorable experience and a deeper understanding of the local way of life.

Week-Long Itinerary for Machico

Here’s a week-long itinerary that will allow you to make the most of your stay in Machico:

Day 1: Arrive in Machico and spend the day exploring the town. Visit São Roque Chapel and the old town.

Day 2: Take a levada walk along Vereda do Larano and enjoy the stunning views.

Day 3: Explore the cultural sites of Machico including the Engenho Velho Museum and Nossa Senhora do Amparo Church.

Day 4: Head to Pico do Facho and enjoy the breathtaking views.

Day 5: Take a guided tour of the hidden gems of Machico.

Day 6: Go whale watching and see these majestic creatures up close.

Day 7: Relax on Machico Beach and soak up the sun.

Food and Drink Scene of Machico

Must-Try Dishes

  • Espetada: A traditional Madeiran dish consisting of skewered grilled beef and served with garlic bread.
  • Black Scabbardfish: Locally caught black scabbardfish is a staple in Machico. Typically served with potato and salad.

Local Beverages

  • Poncha: A popular alcoholic drink made with Madeira’s local rum, honey and lemon juice.
  • Coral Beer: Made in Madeira Island since 1924, this refreshing beer is a favourite among locals and visitors alike.

Where to Stay in Machico: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Dom Pedro Madeira: This four-star hotel is located just a stone’s throw from Machico Beach and has stunning ocean views from many of its rooms.
  • Residencial Amparo: A cosy guesthouse located in the heart of Machico’s old town. It is a perfect base for exploring the cultural attractions of Machico.
  • Hotel Rural A Quinta: Located on the outskirts of Machico, this charming hotel is surrounded by lush greenery and offers visitors a tranquil escape from the hustle and bustle of town.

What to Buy in Machico: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Traditional Embroidery: Madeira is famous for its beautiful embroidery, and many shops in Machico sell these products.
  • Madeira Wine: Madeira wine is a unique product of the region and is a must-try for visitors.

Local Culture and Customs of Machico

Like other parts of Madeira, Machico has a unique culture and customs. Music and dance play a big role in the local festivals and carnivals.

Best Time to Visit Machico

The best time to visit Machico is between June and September when the weather is warm and the skies are blue. Visitors can also experience the many festivals and cultural celebrations that take place during this period.

Do’s and Don’ts of Machico

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and culture.
  • Take a guided tour to learn more about Machico’s history and way of life.
  • Visit during the annual festivals and carnivals.

Don’t:

  • Leave your valuables unattended on the beach.
  • Ignore safety warnings on hiking trails.

Safety and Health

Safety in Machico

Machico is a safe place to visit, and crime rates are very low. However, visitors should still exercise caution and keep an eye on their belongings.

Health in Machico

Machico has several medical centres and pharmacies, and visitors can access medical care if needed. Visitors should also be aware of the risks of sunburn and dehydration and take appropriate precautions.

Getting to Machico: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Machico

Madeira International Airport is the main airport serving Madeira and is located approximately 10km from Machico. Visitors can take a taxi or bus from the airport to Machico.

Bus Stations Near Machico

Machico Bus Station is located in the centre of town and offers connections to other parts of Madeira.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi to Machico from other parts of the island.

How to Get Around in Machico

Machico is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance. Visitors can also take a taxi or rent a car to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Machico: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Funchal: Madeira’s capital and largest city, just 22km from Machico.
  • Porto Santo: A small island off the coast of Madeira that is known for its beautiful beaches.
  • Santana: A picturesque village known for its traditional triangular-shaped houses.

Conclusion

Machico is a hidden gem in Madeira, offering visitors a perfect blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. From stunning beaches to rugged mountain landscapes, Machico has something for everyone. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventure or cultural exploration, Machico is a destination that should be on your travel list. So pack your bags and head to Machico for an unforgettable experience.
