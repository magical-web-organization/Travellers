Introduction to Santa Cruz

Santa Cruz is a charming town located on the eastern coast of Madeira Island in the Atlantic Ocean. Known for its warm weather, breathtaking natural beauty, and rich cultural heritage, Santa Cruz is a popular destination for travelers seeking a relaxing and enjoyable holiday.

This comprehensive tour guide is designed to help you make the most of your visit to Santa Cruz. From a list of places to visit and things to do, to the best places to eat and stay, and everything in between, we have got you covered. So, without further ado, let’s dive right in!

List of Places to Visit in Santa Cruz

Whether you’re a history buff, an adventure seeker, or just someone looking to relax and soak up the stunning scenery, Santa Cruz has plenty of exciting places to explore. Here are some of the must-visit attractions in Santa Cruz:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Santa Cruz Church: This beautiful 16th-century church is one of the most notable examples of Manueline architecture in Madeira.
  • Quinta do Santo da Serra: An 18th-century estate that houses a museum showcasing the history and culture of Santa Cruz.
  • Forte de São José: Built in the 17th century, this fort is a reminder of Santa Cruz’s colonial past.

Natural Attractions of Santa Cruz: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Reis Magos Beach: A stunning black sand beach with crystal clear waters.
  • Machico Marina: A picturesque marina where you can enjoy a variety of water sports, including diving, sailing, and fishing.
  • Pico do Facho: A popular hiking trail that offers breathtaking views of the entire Santa Cruz region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Santa Cruz

Santa Cruz boasts a variety of fantastic hiking and biking trails that will take you through some of the most beautiful natural landscapes on the island. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Levada do Caniçal: This is a peaceful and easy walk along an old water channel.
  • Vereda dos Balcões: This hiking trail offers stunning views of the Ribeiro Frio valley and its surrounding mountains.
  • Bike Trail from Santo da Serra to Machico: This is an exciting mountain bike trail that provides breathtaking views of the Machico area.

Best Things to Do in Santa Cruz: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for a relaxing day out or an adrenaline-packed adventure, Santa Cruz has something for everyone. Here are some of the best activities and tours to do in Santa Cruz:

  • Dolphin and Whale Watching Tour: A boat trip that takes you out into the Atlantic to spot dolphins and whales in their natural habitat.
  • Madeira Wine Tasting Tour: A guided tour of the island’s famous wine cellars, where you can sample the varied and delicious Madeira wines.
  • Santa Cruz Swimming Pool: A fantastic place to unwind and relax, with stunning sea views and crystal-clear water.

Week-Long Itinerary for Santa Cruz

To help you make the most of your visit to Santa Cruz, we’ve put together an itinerary that covers some of the best attractions, activities, and experiences the town has to offer. Here’s what you could do during your week-long stay:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Santa Cruz Church and Museum, then head to Reis Magos Beach in the afternoon
Day 2 Explore Pico do Facho, followed by a visit to Machico Marina
Day 3 Go on a Dolphin and Whale Watching Tour in the morning. In the afternoon, take a stroll through the town center and visit local shops and markets.
Day 4 Spend the day at Santa Cruz Swimming Pool, followed by an evening wine tasting tour
Day 5 Walk along Levada do Caniçal, followed by a visit to Quinta do Santo da Serra
Day 6 Cycle down from Santo da Serra to Machico in the morning, then enjoy some downtime in Machico in the afternoon
Day 7 Go on a farewell hike along Vereda dos Balcões and enjoy the stunning views of Ribeiro Frio valley and its surroundings

Food and Drink Scene of Santa Cruz

When it comes to food and drink, Santa Cruz offers a wealth of delicious local cuisine and drinks that you won’t want to miss. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Espetada: A traditional Madeiran dish of skewered meat, usually beef or pork.
  • Black Scabbardfish: A fish dish that is unique to Madeira and is served with banana or passion fruit sauce.
  • Bolo do Caco: A type of bread made with sweet potato and garlic butter.

Local Beverages

  • Poncha: A traditional Madeiran drink made with honey, sugar, lemon juice and rum.
  • Madeira wine: A sweet, fortified wine unique to Madeira, and one of the island’s most famous exports.
  • Coral Beer: A locally brewed beer that is crisp, refreshing, and perfect for a warm day.

Where to Stay in Santa Cruz: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Santa Cruz offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets, from luxurious hotels to cozy guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in Santa Cruz:

  • Porto Bay Serra Golf: A luxurious hotel with stunning views of the surrounding mountains and the Atlantic Ocean.
  • Quinta do Santo: A cozy guesthouse set in beautiful wooded grounds.
  • Vila Gale Santa Cruz: A modern hotel with excellent amenities, including a swimming pool and spa.

What to Buy in Santa Cruz: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to souvenirs and local products, Santa Cruz offers plenty of options that reflect the town’s cultural heritage and natural beauty. Here are some of the best souvenirs to bring back home:

  • Honey cake: A sweet cake made with honey, sugar, eggs, and flour, and decorated with sugar or almonds.
  • Embroidered linens: Madeira is well-known for its intricate and beautiful embroidered linens.
  • Madeira wine: A bottle of Madeira wine is the perfect way to bring a taste of the island home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Santa Cruz

Santa Cruz has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its architecture, cuisine, and traditions. Some of the unique cultural customs and practices in Santa Cruz include:

  • Festa da Flor: This annual festival celebrates the blooming of the island’s flowers and includes parades, dancing, and lots of food.
  • Semana Santa: Holy Week is a significant event in the Madeiran calendar, and Santa Cruz is renowned for its religious processions and rituals during this time.
  • Madeiran Folklore Group: A group dedicated to preserving and promoting Madeiran folk music, dance, and costumes.

Best Time to Visit Santa Cruz

Santa Cruz has a relatively mild and pleasant climate year-round, with temperatures ranging from 18°C to 25°C. However, the best time to visit Santa Cruz is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is warm and sunny, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Santa Cruz

To ensure that you have an enjoyable and stress-free visit to Santa Cruz, it’s essential to be aware of some of the local customs and practices. Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife.
  • Learn some Portuguese words and phrases to communicate with locals.

Don’t:

  • Wear beachwear outside the beach or pool areas.
  • Litter or vandalize the local attractions.
  • Take photos of people without their consent.

Safety and Health

While Santa Cruz is a safe place to visit, it’s always essential to take some precautions when traveling. Here’s what you need to know:

Safety in Santa Cruz

  • Keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas.
  • Avoid walking alone at night and in remote areas.
  • Use licensed taxis and transportation services.

Health in Santa Cruz

  • Make sure to have comprehensive travel insurance.
  • Ensure that you’re up to date on routine vaccinations.
  • Take precautions to protect yourself against mosquito bites.

Getting to Santa Cruz: Transportation Options and Logistics

Santa Cruz is easily accessible by air and sea. Here are some transportation options and logistics to consider when planning your trip:

Airports Near Santa Cruz

  • Madeira Airport: Located just 5 km from Santa Cruz, Madeira Airport is the primary gateway to the island.

Bus Stations Near Santa Cruz

  • Santa Cruz Bus Station: This is the main bus station in Santa Cruz, with connections to other parts of Madeira.

Other Options

  • Taxis: Taxis are widely available in Santa Cruz and can be hailed from the street or booked in advance.
  • Rental Cars: If you prefer to explore the island at your own pace, renting a car is a great option.

How to Get Around in Santa Cruz

Santa Cruz is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. However, if you’re looking to venture further afield, there are several transportation options.

  • Buses: Buses in Santa Cruz run regularly and connect to other parts of Madeira.
  • Taxis: Taxis are widely available in Santa Cruz and can be hailed from the street or booked in advance.
  • Rental Cars: If you prefer to explore the island at your own pace, renting a car is a great option.

Where to Go Next from Santa Cruz: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Madeira Island is packed with exciting destinations and attractions. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you might want to consider:

  • Funchal: Madeira’s vibrant capital city, just a 20-minute drive from Santa Cruz.
  • Porto Santo: An island located off the coast of Madeira that boasts a stunning 9-kilometer, golden sand beach.
  • Levada Walks: Madeira is home to over 2,000 kilometers of levadas, narrow water channels that run through the island’s mountainous terrain. There are numerous levada walks around the island that offer stunning views of the landscape.

Conclusion

Santa Cruz is a town that has something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or just soaking up the sun on a beautiful beach, Santa Cruz has got you covered. We hope that this comprehensive tour guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan an unforgettable trip to this charming town.
