Calheta is a charming coastal town in the southwestern region of Madeira, known for its natural beauty, rich history, and warm hospitality. If you’re looking for a tranquil getaway where you can relax and recharge amidst stunning scenery, Calheta is the perfect destination for you. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll show you the best things to do, places to visit, and tips to make the most of your trip to Calheta, Madeira.

Introduction to Calheta

Calheta is a quaint town located on the western coast of Madeira, surrounded by lush greenery and crystal-clear waters. The town is known for its picturesque beaches, rugged cliffs, and stunning sunsets, which make it a popular destination for tourists from all over the world. Calheta is also home to several historical landmarks and natural attractions, such as museums, hiking trails, and scenic viewpoints.

List of Places to Visit in Calheta

Calheta has a lot to offer visitors, from culturally rich historical sites to breathtaking natural beauty. Here is a list of the must-visit destinations in Calheta:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Casa das Mudas Arts Center: This modern art museum is housed in a stunning contemporary building and features a wide range of works by local and international artists.
  • Engenho da Calheta: A traditional sugar cane factory that has been producing sugar cane honey and rum for more than a century.
  • Parish Church of Calheta: A beautiful baroque church adorned with intricate carvings and frescoes.

Natural Attractions of Calheta: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Levada Nova Trail: A scenic hiking trail in the mountains that offers breathtaking views of the valley and the ocean.
  • Praia da Calheta: A beautiful sandy beach with crystal-clear waters that is perfect for swimming and sunbathing.
  • Pico da Urze: A scenic viewpoint on the top of a mountain that offers panoramic views of the town and the ocean.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Calheta

Calheta is home to some of the most scenic hiking and biking trails in Madeira, offering visitors a unique opportunity to explore the island’s natural beauty. Here are some popular routes:

  • 25 Fontes: A challenging but rewarding trail that takes you through a lush forest and leads to a beautiful waterfall.
  • Calheta Coastal Promenade: A stunning walkway that offers picturesque views of the coastline, beaches, and cliffs.
  • Bica da Cana: A breathtaking viewpoint located at the top of a mountain, which can be accessed via a scenic hiking trail.

Best Things to Do in Calheta: Activities and Tours

Calheta offers a diverse range of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy, from water sports to cultural experiences. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • Whale and dolphin watching: Take a boat tour to see these magnificent creatures up close and learn about their habitats and behaviors.
  • Tuk Tuk Tour: An exciting and unique way to explore the town and learn about its rich history and culture.
  • Stand-Up Paddleboarding (SUP): A fun and thrilling water sport that allows you to explore the coastline and beaches from a unique perspective.

Week-Long Itinerary for Calheta

Day 1: Visit Praia da Calheta and Engenho da Calheta. Day 2: Go hiking on the Levada Nova Trail and visit Casa das Mudas Arts Center. Day 3: Take a boat tour to see whales and dolphins and visit Pico da Urze. Day 4: Explore the town on a Tuk Tuk tour and visit the Parish Church of Calheta. Day 5: Try stand-up paddleboarding at Praia da Calheta and visit Bica da Cana. Day 6: Go hiking on the 25 Fontes trail and enjoy a picnic lunch surrounded by natural beauty. Day 7: Relax on the beach and enjoy the beautiful sunset.

Food and Drink Scene of Calheta

One of the highlights of visiting Calheta is sampling the local cuisine and beverages. The town is known for its fresh seafood, traditional dishes, and sweet desserts. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Espetada: Skewered meat cooked over an open flame and served with garlic bread and fried maize.
  • Bolo do caco: A round bread made with sweet potato flour and served warm with garlic butter.
  • Espada com banana: A traditional dish made with scabbard fish and served with fried bananas and other vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Poncha: A traditional alcoholic beverage made with distilled sugar cane, honey, and lemon juice.
  • Nikita: A refreshing non-alcoholic drink made with beer, ice cream, and pineapple juice.
  • Madeira Wine: A sweet fortified wine made exclusively in Madeira.

Where to Stay in Calheta: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Calheta offers many different types of accommodations, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • Savoy Calheta Beach: A beautiful beachfront hotel that offers stunning views and top-notch amenities.
  • Villa Calheta: A cozy guesthouse with spacious rooms and friendly staff.
  • Hotel Jardim Atlantico: A modern hotel with a spa, pool, and panoramic views of the ocean.

What to Buy in Calheta: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Calheta is known for its traditional crafts and local products, which make great souvenirs or gifts to bring back home. Some of the popular items include:

  • Traditional Wicker Baskets: Hand-woven baskets made from local materials.
  • Embroidered Linen: Beautifully crafted tablecloths, napkins, and other linen products.
  • Sugar Cane Honey and Rum: Locally produced sweeteners and spirits.

Local Culture and Customs of Calheta

Calheta is a friendly and welcoming town that celebrates its history, traditions, and local culture. Visitors are encouraged to participate in the town’s festivals and events, such as the Carnival, Wine Festival, and Folklore Festival. It’s also important to respect local customs, such as dressing modestly when visiting religious sites and using appropriate etiquette when interacting with locals.

Best Time to Visit Calheta

The best time to visit Calheta is from April to October, when the weather is warm and sunny. The town can get crowded during peak season, so consider visiting in the shoulder season (April to June or September to October) for a more relaxed experience.

Do’s and Don’ts of Calheta

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Bring sunscreen, a hat, and comfortable shoes for outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural sites.
  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Drink and drive or engage in other unsafe behaviors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Calheta

Calheta is a safe town but it’s important to take basic safety precautions, such as locking valuables in a safe, being aware of your surroundings, and avoiding isolated areas at night. In case of an emergency, dial 112, the European Union emergency number.

Health in Calheta

Calheta has a medical center and a pharmacy, but for serious health issues, it’s recommended to go to the Hospital Dr. Nélio Mendonça in Funchal, which is about a 30-minute drive from Calheta. It’s also important to have travel insurance to cover unexpected health expenses.

Getting to Calheta: Transportation Options and Logistics

Calheta is located on the southwestern coast of Madeira and can be easily accessed by different modes of transportation. Here are some options:

Airports Near Calheta:

  • Madeira International Airport (FNC): Located about 40 km from Calheta.

Bus Stations Near Calheta:

  • Funchal Bus Station: Located about 30 km from Calheta.
  • Calheta Bus Terminal: Located in the town, with connections to other parts of the island.

Other Options:

  • Taxi: Taxis are available at the airport and in Funchal for a fixed rate.
  • Rental Car: Rental cars can be booked in advance or at the airport and offer a convenient way to explore the island.

How to Get Around in Calheta

Calheta is a small town that can be easily explored on foot, but for longer distances, it’s recommended to take a bus or rent a car. Taxis are also available but can be expensive. There are several hiking trails in the area that offer scenic views and can be accessed by foot or bike.

Where to Go Next from Calheta: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Madeira is a beautiful island with many exciting destinations and activities to offer. Some popular nearby places to visit include:

  • Funchal: The capital city of Madeira, known for its colorful markets, scenic gardens, and rich culture.
  • Ribeira Brava: A charming town located about 10 km north of Calheta, with stunning views and historical landmarks.
  • Porto Moniz: A seaside town located on the northern coast of Madeira, famous for its natural swimming pools and scenic views.

Conclusion

Calheta is a hidden gem in Madeira that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural richness, and warm hospitality. From sandy beaches to rugged cliffs, from traditional crafts to modern art, from hiking trails to water sports, Calheta has something for everyone to enjoy. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has inspired you to plan a memorable trip to Calheta, Madeira, and explore all that this beautiful town has to offer.
