Introduction to Saint Helena Island

Hidden in the middle of the Atlantic Ocean, Saint Helena Island is a true hidden gem. As part of the Saint Helena, Ascension and Tristan da Cunha British Overseas Territory, this island has a unique charm that will enchant you. From its stunning natural scenery to its rich cultural history, there is something for everyone on Saint Helena Island.

List of Places to Visit in Saint Helena Island

If you are planning a trip to Saint Helena Island, make sure you don’t miss out on these must-see attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Museum of Saint Helena: Home to an impressive collection of artifacts documenting the island’s geological history, Napoleon’s exile, and the island’s role as a British outpost.
  • High Knoll Fort: Built by the British in the 19th century, the fort is now a historical monument offering impressive views of the island.
  • Longwood House: The former residence of Napoleon Bonaparte during his exile, it is now a museum offering a glimpse into the life of this famous figure.
  • Plantation House: The official residence of the Governor of Saint Helena and home to a unique and endearing population of giant tortoises.

Natural Attractions of Saint Helena Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Diana’s Peak National Park: This pristine natural reserve offers some of the most stunning landscapes on the island, including hiking trails, waterfalls, and panoramic views.
  • Jacob’s Ladder: A 699-step staircase that ranks as one of the world’s steepest and provides panoramic views of the island at the top.
  • Sandy Bay Beach: This secluded beach is a hidden treasure, known for its crystal-clear waters and pristine white sand.
  • Jamestown: The island’s capital is a UNESCO World Heritage Site characterized by beautiful Georgian architecture.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saint Helena Island

Saint Helena Island has a vast network of hiking trails that offer stunning views of the island’s natural beauty. Some of the most popular hikes include the Heart-Shaped Waterfall Hike, Lot’s Wife’s Ponds, and the Flagstaff Hill Hike. For those who prefer biking, the island offers a range of exciting routes through the mountains and along the spectacular coastline.

Best Things to Do in Saint Helena Island: Activities and Tours

In addition to the fantastic hiking and biking routes, Saint Helena Island offers a range of activities and tours for visitors. Here are just some of the best:

  • Scuba diving: Explore the island’s incredible marine life and see shipwrecks from the past.
  • Fishing: Fish in the deep waters and try to catch some of the most delicious fish the island has to offer like tuna, wahoo, and marlin.
  • Stargazing: The clear night skies of Saint Helena Island make it the perfect place for stargazing.
  • Boat tours: Take a tour around the island’s stunning coastline by boat to get a unique view of the island.

Week-Long Itinerary for Saint Helena Island

With so much to see and do on Saint Helena Island, it can be challenging to know where to start. Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week’s visit:

Day Activity
1 Explore the island’s capital, Jamestown, including the Museum of Saint Helena and Longwood House.
2 Hike to Diana’s Peak National Park. Enjoy the views and check out the Heart-Shaped Waterfall.
3 Scuba dive and explore the marine life around the island.
4 Walk up Jacob’s Ladder for a stunning panoramic view of the island.
5 Travel to Sandy Bay Beach for some sunbathing and swimming.
6 Take a boat tour around the coast of the island.
7 Relax, shop for souvenirs, and enjoy the island’s delicious cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Saint Helena Island

Saint Helena Island has a unique cuisine influenced by British and South African traditions with a splash of local flavors. Some delicious dishes you must try include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • St Helena fishcakes: Made from mashed spuds, spiced fish and parsley, are a local favourite.
  • Plo: A traditional lamb or beef stew with cabbage, potato, and sweet potato.
  • Coffee cake: A dessert made with locally grown coffee.
  • Pumpkin Fritters: This sweet and savory treat is crispy on the outside and soft on the inside.

Local Beverages

  • Run: The South African wine-based spirit, Amarula, is a popular drink on the island, especially after dinner.
  • Saint Helena Coffee: An Arabica coffee grown on the island that has become famous worldwide.
  • Tungi Spirit: A locally made spirit with a unique hot and spicy taste, it’s always a good idea to enjoy some with friends.

Where to Stay in Saint Helena Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Saint Helena Island’s accommodations offer many options for all types of travelers, from hotels and guesthouses to lodges and backpacker hostels. Some of the best options include:

  • Mantis St Helena: A four-star hotel located in the island’s capital, Jamestown.
  • Briars B&B: A guest house in the countryside offering cozy accommodation.
  • Farm Lodge Country House Hotel: A 10-room hotel in the historic Plantation House area of the island.

What to Buy in Saint Helena Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Saint Helena Island’s local market and stores offer a wide range of unique products, including:

  • Saint Helena coffee: Made from beans that grow in the island’s unique soil, this coffee has a unique aroma and taste that is unforgettable.
  • Handicrafts made from flax: Made by Saint Helena craftspeople, flax handicrafts are unique souvenirs.
  • Embroidery: Saint Helena has a long embroidery tradition, and you can find it in many shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Saint Helena Island

Saint Helena has a unique culture that is a mix of British, African, and Portuguese traditions. Some customs you should be aware of include:

  • Respect for elders: Saint Helena is a traditional society where respect for elders is highly valued.
  • Dress code: Modest clothing is recommended, and beachwear should be reserved for the beach.
  • Politeness: Saint Helenians value politeness and civility and expect their guests to do the same.

Best Time to Visit Saint Helena Island

The climate of Saint Helena Island is tropical and moderate throughout the year, making it a perfect destination any time of the year. However, the best time to visit is between October and June, when the weather is typically mild and dry.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saint Helena Island

Do:

  • Respect the culture and customs of Saint Helena.
  • Embrace the island’s nature and outdoor activities.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Saint Helena Island is a pristine environment, and littering is not tolerated.
  • Disrespect locals: The people of Saint Helena are proud of their heritage, so be respectful.
  • Disturb wildlife: The island’s flora and fauna are essential to the ecology and economy of Saint Helena.

Safety and Health

Safety in Saint Helena Island

Saint Helena is one of the safest places in the world with a low crime rate. Visitors should still take precautions, such as avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Saint Helena Island

Saint Helena has an excellent healthcare system, so there is no need to worry about any emergencies. However, visitors should be aware that malaria is not present on the island, so they should bring their medication if traveling from a malaria endemic area.

Getting to Saint Helena Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Travel to Saint Helena Island involves a bit of planning, but it’s well worth it. Some transportation options include:

Airports Near Saint Helena Island

The only way to get to Saint Helena Island is by air, with flights from Johannesburg or Cape Town in South Africa.

How to Get Around in Saint Helena Island

The island is relatively small and easy to navigate. Most visitors rent a car, but taxis are also available.

Where to Go Next from Saint Helena Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Saint Helena is an excellent base for exploring other parts of the Saint Helena, Ascension and Tristan da Cunha Overseas British Territory, including Ascension Island and Tristan da Cunha.

Conclusion

Saint Helena Island is a hidden gem that should be on every traveler’s itinerary. With its pristine natural scenery, rich cultural history, and unique cuisine, there’s something for everyone on this beautiful island.
