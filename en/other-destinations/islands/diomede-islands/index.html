Introduction to Diomede Islands

The Diomede Islands are a pair of islands located in the Bering Strait, separating Russia and the United States. Big Diomede Island belongs to Russia, while Little Diomede is part of the United States. These two islands, separated by 2.4 miles of water, have a fascinating history and unique culture.

List of Places to Visit in Diomede Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Saint Innocent Church: This church was built in 1930 and is located on Little Diomede. It is famous for its beautiful stained glass windows.
  • Diomede Village: This is the only inhabited settlement on Little Diomede and is home to the Inupiaq people. Here, visitors can learn about the Inupiaq culture and history.
  • Big Diomede Island: This island is currently abandoned but was once home to Russian military forces. Visitors can see some remnants of the former military presence, including abandoned buildings and rusting equipment.

Natural Attractions of Diomede Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cape Dezhnev: This is the easternmost point of the mainland, and a popular spot for bird-watching.
  • Bering Land Bridge National Preserve: This beautiful park is part of the National Park System and features a variety of outdoor activities such as hiking, fishing, and wildlife observation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Diomede Islands

  • Little Diomede Island Trail: This is an easy 2.5 mile trail that takes visitors around the island and provides stunning views of the Bering Strait and surrounding mountains.
  • Big Diomede Island Hiking Trail: This is a moderate 4-mile hike that takes visitors through the abandoned Russian military base and offers scenic views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Diomede Islands: Activities and Tours

  • Whale Watching Tour: Visitors can book a tour and go on a boat to watch whales in their natural habitat.
  • Northern Lights Tour: Between September and April, visitors can witness the breathtaking Aurora Borealis, visible from both islands.

Food and Drink Scene of Diomede Islands

Must-Try Dishes

  • Muktuk: A traditional Inupiaq dish made from whale skin and blubber.
  • Akutaq: Also known as “Eskimo ice cream,” this dessert is made from whipped fat mixed with berries.

Local Beverages

  • Chaga Tea: Made from the chaga mushroom, which is found in the forests of the region.
  • Sourdough Beer: A type of beer made with sourdough starter instead of yeast.

Where to Stay in Diomede Islands

  • Little Diomede Lodge: This hotel offers comfortable rooms and stunning views of the Bering Strait.
  • Big Diomede Island Guesthouse: Basic accommodation is available on Big Diomede Island for visitors who are interested in exploring the abandoned Russian military base.

What to Buy in Diomede Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ivory carvings: Carvings made from walrus tusks are a popular souvenir.
  • Fur clothing: Visitors can purchase traditional fur clothing made from animal hides.

Local Culture and Customs of Diomede Islands

The Inupiaq people, who live on Little Diomede Island, have a rich culture and history. Visitors should be respectful of their traditions and customs, including their subsistence lifestyle and reliance on hunting and fishing for survival.

Best Time to Visit Diomede Islands

The peak tourist season is between June and August when the weather is milder, and outdoor activities are available. However, visitors interested in witnessing the Northern Lights should visit between September and April.

Do’s and Don’ts of Diomede Islands

Do:

  • Respect the Inupiaq people and their customs.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather.
  • Be mindful of your impact on the environment.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the wildlife or natural habitats on the islands.
  • Take any souvenirs or artifacts from the islands.
  • Disrespect any historical or cultural sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Diomede Islands

Visitors should be aware of the unpredictable weather conditions, with temperatures dropping rapidly, and strong winds picking up quickly. They should also be aware of any wildlife in the area, such as polar bears or walruses, and take the necessary precautions to avoid any encounters.

Health in Diomede Islands

There are no medical facilities on the islands, and visitors should ensure that they bring any necessary medication and emergency supplies.

Getting to Diomede Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Diomede Islands

There are no airports on either of the Diomede Islands. The closest airports are in Nome, Alaska, and Anadyr, Russia.

Bus Stations Near Diomede Islands

There are no bus stations near the islands.

Other Options

Visitors can book a boat or helicopter tour to reach the islands.

How to Get Around in Diomede Islands

Visitors can explore both islands on foot, but for those who prefer, there are also snowmobiles and bicycles available for rent.

Where to Go Next from Diomede Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore other areas of Alaska, such as Nome, or head north to the Arctic Circle. In Russia, visitors can explore the Kamchatka Peninsula or head to Moscow.

Conclusion

The Diomede Islands offer visitors a unique and fascinating experience. From exploring Inupiaq culture and history to witnessing the Northern Lights, the islands have something to offer for everyone. Visitors should respect the local customs and wildlife and be prepared for the unpredictable weather conditions.
