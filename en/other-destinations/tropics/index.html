Introduction to Tropics

Located in the tropical zone, Tropics is a region known for its lush rainforests, turquoise waters, stunning beaches, and vibrant culture. The region is vast and covers a large part of the world, including parts of Asia, Africa, Australia, and South America. A trip to Tropics promises to be an unforgettable adventure with endless opportunities to explore, relax, and immerse yourself in the local culture.

List of Places to Visit in Tropics

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are interested in culture and history, you will be pleased with the range of museums and historical places to visit in Tropics. Some of the must-visit places include:

  • Smithsonian National Museum of Natural History in Washington D.C. - showcasing an extensive collection of objects and artifacts related to natural history and anthropology.
  • The Kumasi Fort and Military Museum in Ghana - telling the stories of the Ashanti Kingdom’s ruling family and the influence of their leadership in the region.
  • The National Museum of Tanzania in Dar es Salaam - featuring a range of exhibits that showcase Tanzania’s history and culture, including archaeology and anthropology sections.

Natural Attractions of Tropics: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tropics is an ideal destination to enjoy nature and its wonders. The region boasts incredible biodiversity comprising hundreds of endemic species of plants and animals, many of which can be seen while exploring its natural attractions. Here are some of the best natural attractions to check out in Tropics:

  • The Amazon Rainforest in Brazil - the world’s largest tropical rainforest and home to millions of species of plants, animals, insects, and birds. The Amazon River runs through it, and you can go on various tours to explore the rainforest.
  • The Great Barrier Reef in Australia - the world’s largest coral reef system is home to countless species of marine life and has some of the best diving and snorkeling sites in Tropics.
  • Table Mountain in Cape Town, South Africa - a natural heritage site with incredible views of the city and the Atlantic Ocean.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tropics

Tropics is an excellent destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts looking for varying terrain and scenic views. The following routes are some of the best to explore:

  • The Inca Trail in Peru - a famous hiking trail that attracts visitors worldwide, offering stunning views of the Andes and the ancient Inca ruins.
  • The Cape Peninsula in South Africa - a scenic coastal road that provides breathtaking views of the Atlantic Ocean and the Cape of Good Hope.
  • The Kokoda Track in Papua New Guinea - a challenging 96-kilometer trek with 12 significant river crossings, passing through remote villages and dense jungle terrain.

Best Things to Do in Tropics: Activities and Tours

Tropics offers endless opportunities for adventure and relaxation, making it a perfect destination for all types of travelers. Some of the best things to do in Tropics include:

  • Snorkeling and scuba diving in the Great Barrier Reef in Australia.
  • Zip-lining in Costa Rica’s rainforests.
  • Safari tours in the Serengeti in Tanzania.
  • Exploring the underground river in Palawan, Philippines.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tropics

If you have a week to spare, you can explore some of the best destinations in Tropics with the following itinerary:

  • Day 1-3: Explore the temples and ancient cities of Cambodia, including Angkor Wat, Angkor Thom, and Ta Prohm.
  • Day 4-5: Head to Bali in Indonesia, known for its stunning beaches, rice fields, and temples.
  • Day 6-7: Take a safari tour in Kruger National Park in South Africa, known for its unique flora and fauna.

Food and Drink Scene of Tropics

Must-Try Dishes

Tropics is a food lover’s paradise, with a wide variety of dishes unique to each region. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Pupusas in El Salvador - a traditional thick tortilla filled with cheese, beans, and pork.
  • Pad Thai in Thailand - stir-fried rice noodles with peanuts, vegetables, and meat.
  • Churrasco in Brazil - grilled meat served with rice, beans, and salad.

Local Beverages

To wash down your food, try some of the local beverages. Some of the popular options include:

  • Coconut water in the Philippines - a refreshing drink made from fresh coconuts.
  • Caipirinha in Brazil - a cocktail made with sugarcane liquor, lime, and sugar.
  • Rooibos tea in South Africa - a caffeine-free tea made from the red bush native to the region.

Where to Stay in Tropics: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tropics offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • Shangri-La Hotel in Singapore - a luxurious hotel with stunning views of the city and Marina Bay.
  • Hostal Casa Verde in Guatemala - a budget-friendly option in Antigua with a rooftop terrace and views of the nearby volcanoes.
  • Tambuli Beach Resort in the Philippines - a beachfront resort with excellent facilities and a range of activities to enjoy.

What to Buy in Tropics: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tropics offers a range of souvenirs and local products to take back home. Some of the best options include:

  • Handcrafted textiles from Peru.
  • Coffee beans from Costa Rica.
  • Hand-carved masks from West Africa.

Local Culture and Customs of Tropics

Tropics is a diverse region, with each country boasting its unique culture and customs. However, some common threads run through the different cultures, such as respect for elders and hospitality to guests.

Best Time to Visit Tropics

The best time to visit Tropics depends on the region you plan to visit. However, as a general rule, the dry season (winter) is a favorable time to go. Keep in mind that the peak seasons may attract more tourists and higher prices, so plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tropics

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly in places of worship.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing or inappropriate clothing.
  • Photograph people without their permission.
  • Litter or damage the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tropics

Tropics, like any other region in the world, has its safety concerns. Exercise caution and common sense when traveling, avoid carrying large sums of cash, and keep your valuables hidden. Consult your government travel advisory before traveling to any Tropics destination and take note of any safety warnings.

Health in Tropics

Tropics is home to many endemic diseases and illnesses, such as malaria, dengue fever, and yellow fever. Consult a doctor or travel clinic before traveling to Tropics and get vaccinated as necessary.

Getting to Tropics: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports in Tropics

Tropics has many international airports, making it easy to access different regions. Some of the major airports include:

  • Suvarnabhumi Airport in Bangkok.
  • Jomo Kenyatta International Airport in Nairobi.
  • Guarulhos International Airport in Sao Paulo.

Bus Stations in Tropics

Bus travel in Tropics is a great way to see the country and experience the local culture. Some major bus stations include:

  • South Terminal Bus Station in Bangkok.
  • Bung Karno Bus Terminal in Jakarta.
  • Mbezi Luis Bus Terminal in Dar es Salaam.

Other Options

Other transportation options include trains, taxis, and car hire services.

How to Get Around in Tropics

Tropics offers various modes of transport for getting around, including buses, trains, ferries, and taxis. In some areas, motorbikes and scooters are a popular option. Ensure that you check the safety measures concerning each mode of transport before you decide.

Where to Go Next from Tropics: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tropics is full of nearby destinations to explore once your trip is over. Some of the best destinations to visit after exploring Tropics include:

  • New Zealand - a beautiful country with stunning landscapes and unique culture.
  • Bali – A paradise for beach lovers wanting to relax and indulge in peaceful surroundings.
  • Australia – The land of the Kangaroos and home to the Great Barrier Reef.

Conclusion

Tropics is a vast and diverse region with plenty of natural wonders, cultural gems, and delicious cuisine. With excellent transport infrastructure and a wide range of accommodation options, it is easy to plan a trip that meets your budget and preferences. Whether you’re seeking adventure, relaxation, or culture, you’re sure to have an unforgettable experience in one of the world’s most beautiful regions.
